ingredients:
- "162 grams dry cake mix (vanilla or funfetti) toasted"
- "450 grams full-fat cream cheese softened"
- "4 tablespoons assorted sprinkles, divided"
- "85 grams white chocolate chips or candy melts (four colors)"
- "Optional: 2 teaspoons vanilla pudding mix (sub for dry cake mix)"
- "Optional: 1 tablespoon non-dairy creamer (to adjust texture)"
instructions:
- "Preheat dry cake mix. Put 162 grams dry cake mix in medium heat-safe bowl. Microwave in 3 intervals of 20 seconds, stirring well each time. Watch for faint toasty smell and slightly darker hue. Let cool fully; critical to avoid grainy clumps mixing with cream cheese."
- "Beat softened cream cheese in medium bowl with handheld mixer. Use medium-high speed, whip 90 to 110 seconds until utterly lump-free, fluffy peaks start to form. Scrape bowl edges to maintain uniform texture. If lumpy, keep mixing rather than adding liquids. Too warm cream cheese breaks structure."
- "Fold in cooled dry cake mix and 4 tablespoons sprinkles gently. Only mix until just combined; overdoing aerates and will make coating uneven. Mixture should have thick, pliable feel, not sticky or gooey."
- "Cover tightly with plastic wrap. Refrigerate 2 hours minimum to solidify dough and let flavors meld. Too short chilling results in sticky balls that lose shape while coating."
- "Prep baking sheet with parchment paper. Use for portioning and resting bites. Scoop a level tablespoon-sized dollop using small cookie scoop. Roll by hand into firm balls."
- "Divide white chocolate or candy melts into 4 small microwave-safe bowls. Melt separately in increments of 25 seconds, stirring vigorously between intervals to ensure even melting without scorching. Watch carefully. Should be fluid but thick enough to cling to bites."
- "Dip cheesecake balls into one candy color at a time. Use fork to lift out and tap gently to remove excess coating. If patches remain, spoon extra melted chocolate over bare spots, don't double dip or candy shell becomes too thick and cracks."
- "Sprinkle with dedicated unicorn sprinkles immediately while coating is wet. Toothpick helps to place delicate bites back on lined sheet without cracking."
- "Repeat dipping, tapping, and sprinkling with remaining colors until all bites are coated. Let cool fully at room temperature or refrigerate briefly to harden shells before serving."
- "If coating thickens too much while working, reheat in short bursts again. Avoid overheating melts; will seize or lose shine."
- "Store leftovers in airtight container refrigerated up to 3 days. Bring to near room temp before serving for best mouthfeel."
