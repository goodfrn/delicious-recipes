ingredients:
- "1 tbsp unsalted butter"
- "1 cup chopped asparagus tips and stems, about 1/4 inch pieces"
- "1/3 cup finely diced shallot or substitute with mild leek whites"
- "4 large eggs"
- "1 1/4 cup milk whole or half-and-half for richer custard"
- "1 tsp fine sea salt"
- "1/2 tsp ground black pepper"
- "1/4 tsp garlic powder or substitute with 1 minced garlic clove"
- "1 cup grated Swiss cheese substitute Gruyere or sharp Cheddar for twist"
- "9-inch refrigerated or homemade pie crust"
- "Optional garnish asparagus spears for design on top"
instructions:
- "Set oven to 350 F but watch for golden edges on crust and custard set, not wiggle under finger."
- "Heat butter in medium skillet over moderate heat. Toss in asparagus bits and shallot chunks. Saute until bright green, just tender but not mushy around 4-6 minutes depending on size. Softening onions/similar aromatics help balance asparagus’s green bite."
- "Off the heat, let the mixture cool briefly or eggs scramble later. Moisture balance matters here."
- "Whisk eggs in a medium bowl vigorously to combine yolk and white evenly. Add milk, salt, pepper and garlic powder. Mix thoroughly. Season to taste — bland custard ruins impact."
- "Fold grated cheese and sautéed veggies into egg mix gently. Distribute without overmixing to avoid dense texture."
- "Place pie crust in 9-inch pie plate. Spray lightly with oil to prevent sticking, especially if using homemade or butter crust prone to adherence."
- "Pour custard blend into crust. Arrange asparagus spears in a pattern if desired—adds rustic charm and even cooking on top."
- "Bake in center rack for about 38-43 minutes. Listen for gentle bubbling sound from filling. Edges of crust should be golden to medium brown; top custard set but slightly jiggly center."
- "Remove from oven and cool minimum 5 minutes on wire rack or counter. Filling firms up with residual heat; slicing too hot means runny slices."
- "Slice with serrated knife or thin-bladed pie server for clean cuts. Serve warm or room temp."
- "Tweak timing by visual cues: undercooked is loose and shiny, overbaked turns dry and cracks."
- "If lacking Swiss, a combo of mozzarella and Parmesan works but changes flavor and texture dynamic."
