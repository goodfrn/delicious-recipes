ingredients:
- "10 tbsp unsalted butter softened close to room temp"
- "1 1/4 cups granulated sugar"
- "3 large eggs"
- "1 tsp pure vanilla extract"
- "1 cup full fat Greek yogurt substitute for sour cream"
- "2 3/4 cups cake flour sifted (or 2 1/2 cups all purpose minus 2 tbsp cornstarch)"
- "2 tsp baking powder"
- "1/2 tsp baking soda"
- "1/2 tsp kosher salt"
- "streusel==="
- "3/4 cup light brown sugar packed"
- "1/3 cup all purpose flour"
- "1 tsp ground cinnamon"
- "1/4 tsp fine salt"
- "6 tbsp cold unsalted butter diced"
- "glaze==="
- "1 cup powdered sugar sifted"
- "3 tbsp pure maple syrup"
- "water or milk, dropped by drops to thin"
instructions:
- "warming oven to 345 F. Tubed pan sprayed and dusted lightly with flour. Avoid wet spray, flour sticks better there."
- "beat butter alone 4-5 minutes. Color brightens, texture fluffy but dense — this traps air better than when sugar added early."
- "sugar goes in gradually. Cream 2 more minutes. You want the mix velvety but not grainy or greasy. Butter grain is a sign sugar wasn’t fully creamed."
- "eggs one by one. Each should disappear fully before next. If batter looks curdled, you can add a spoon of dry ingredients now to save it."
- "vanilla and yogurt fold in slowly, mix just till uniform. Yogurt makes crumb tender and adds subtle tang."
- "whisk dry: cake flour, baking powder, soda, salt. Add in 3 parts. Fold with big gentle strokes. Batter will be thick but smooth. No overmixing — batter streaks visible but no flour clumps."
- "streusel time. Mash cold butter into dry mix with fork till pebble sized lumps form. Refrigerate till needed. Butter must stay cold or topping will melt into batter."
- "half batter spooned in pan, tapped. Look — batter should settle flat but not liquidy or sticky at edges."
- "spinkle 3/4 cup streusel over batter layer. Then rest of batter, spread gently to cover streusel. Last streusel sprinkled on top, pressed lightly to stick."
- "bake 50-65 minutes. Visual: golden top with firm edges. Toothpick inserted near center should come out with moist crumbs but no raw batter."
- "cool at least 35 minutes on rack. Hot streusel falls apart if you remove too early. Flip out carefully streusel side up to keep clean look."
- "whisk glaze — powdered sugar, maple, thin with water/milk dropwise till pourable but not runny. Drizzle over cooled cake with fork or spoon for webs of sweet shine."
- "serve at room temp. Keeps well covered 2 days. Reheat slices briefly if cold."
