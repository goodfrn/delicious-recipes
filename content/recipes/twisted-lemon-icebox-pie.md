ingredients:
- "1-1/2 cups crushed shortbread cookies ; use a food processor or pound tightly sealed in zip bag"
- "6 tablespoons browned butter, melted"
- "4 large eggs, separated"
- "1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk ; substitute coconut cream for lactose-free"
- "3/4 cup fresh lemon juice ; preferably Meyer lemons or a mix of regular"
- "1/4 teaspoon cream of tartar"
- "1/8 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "1/2 cup granulated sugar"
- "1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder ; optional, for meringue color twist"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 320°F. Toast shortbread crumbs lightly on dry pan if not using browned butter ; enhances nutty aroma."
- "Mix crumbs thoroughly with warm browned butter until evenly moistened. Firmly press into the base and sides of a 9-inch deep pie dish. Best to press with bottom of glass or measuring cup; avoids uneven spots and crumbling edges."
- "Beat egg yolks briskly in medium bowl. Pour in condensed milk slowly, whisk until thickened appearance. Add lemon juice last, whisk just to incorporate ; don’t overmix or yellow’ll dull."
- "Pour filling into crust. Bake 28 minutes or until edges just set but center jiggles slightly when nudged. Remove from oven."
- "In very clean metal bowl — key for stable egg whites — whisk egg whites on low to medium speed until frothy and loose peaks form. Add cream of tartar and salt; speeds up and stabilizes foam."
- "Gradually stream in sugar while continuing to whip at medium-high speed. Add turmeric now if using for a warm sandlewood tone. Beat until firm, shiny peaks form that bend without breaking — classic meringue texture. Gritty sugar means underbeaten or coarse sugar used."
- "Spread meringue thick and even over hot lemon filling. Seal edges to the crust all around, prevents shrinkage and weeping."
- "Return pie to 325°F oven. Bake another 18 minutes or until meringue tips start to color golden brown. If in doubt, use broiler at low distance for 30 seconds bursts to avoid burning."
- "Cool pie on rack to room temp, then chill minimum 2 hours. Slicing cold ensures clean wedges. Leftovers keep well covered, meringue may soften after a day or two."
- "If lemon juice runs low, blending in some orange juice reduces acidity without sacrificing citrus punch. For a dairy-free crust, swap browned butter with coconut oil, mindful it can soften crust texture slightly."
