ingredients:
- "1 lb ground beef"
- "1 large egg beaten"
- "1/2 cup panko breadcrumbs"
- "2 small shallots minced"
- "1 tsp garlic powder"
- "1 tsp kosher salt"
- "1/3 cup light brown sugar packed"
- "2 tbsp tomato paste"
- "2 tbsp rice vinegar"
- "1/4 cup low sodium soy sauce"
- "1/2 tsp ground ginger"
- "1 1/2 cups cold water divided"
- "1 tbsp arrowroot powder"
- "1 tbsp honey"
- "black sesame seeds for garnish"
- "2 tbsp sliced fresh chives"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 345°F. Spray a 9x13 glass or ceramic dish well. Air circulation prevents soggy bottoms; avoid dark pans which bake faster on outsides but risk burning meatballs."
- "In bowl, crumble ground beef fine with fingers. Add beaten egg, panko, minced shallots, garlic powder, salt. Mix gently; overmix toughens texture."
- "Form into balls about 1 3/4 inches. Should get around 18. Fewer means bigger pies, more means smaller bites, affects baking time."
- "Nestle balls spaced in pan. Give air room; crowding steams, no caramel crust. Bake 22 minutes. Look for light golden edges and firm touch but not dry. Juices should bubble slightly under crust."
- "While baking, start glaze: whisk brown sugar, tomato paste, rice vinegar, soy sauce, ground ginger, honey, and 1 1/4 cups cold water in saucepan. Stir until tomato paste dissolves fully, no lumps."
- "Bring to gentle simmer. Watch carefully; bubbling should be low, not roaring. This lets sugar melt gradually. Shiny bubbles form when thickening starts."
- "Mix arrowroot powder in remaining 1/4 cup water until no lumps. Whisk into simmering glaze. Keep stirring until sauce thickens, coats back of spoon like a glossy cast. Should deepen in color but no burnt smell. About 3-5 minutes here."
- "Drain baked meatballs from any rendered juices and fat carefully—excess dilutes glaze, loses punch. Add meatballs to sauce pan."
- "Simmer on lowest heat with lid slightly ajar. Stir every 6 minutes to stop sticking and ensure even glaze coverage. Meatballs soak in sweet-tart gloss. Texture firms but stays juicy inside. About 27 minutes total simmer time."
- "Final check: poke a meatball gently. Juices run clear, no pink. Sauce is thick, clings well with shine."
- "Plate and shower black sesame seeds and fresh chives. Adds mild crunch and herbaceous bite. Don’t skip garnishes; they wake the dish."
