ingredients:
- "2 cups crushed cookies; shortbread or gingersnaps work well"
- "3 tablespoons melted coconut oil, or substitute with mild vegetable oil"
- "1/4 to 1/3 cup whole milk or unsweetened almond milk"
- "2 tablespoons brown sugar, packed"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon"
- "1/4 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "1/4 cup chopped toasted pecans or walnuts, optional twist for crunch"
instructions:
- "=== Blend Base ==="
- "Dump cookie crumbs, brown sugar, cinnamon, salt in a food processor bowl. Pulse a few times until mixed and crumbly but no large bits remain."
- "Add melted coconut oil. Turn processor on and blend slowly. Watch texture carefully—should become pasty but not oily. Scrape sides once or twice."
- "=== Adjust Consistency ==="
- "Pour milk slowly while running blender on medium speed. Stop as soon as paste starts to look creamy and moves easily. Too much milk makes liquidy mess. Feel spread between finger gaps for texture."
- "=== Add Crunch and Final Touch ==="
- "If using nuts, toss them in and pulse just once or twice to distribute but keep pieces intact."
- "Taste test. Add pinch more salt or cinnamon if it needs brightness."
- "=== Storing and Serving ==="
- "Transfer cookie butter to airtight container. Smooth top with spatula. Use right away if you want spreadable softness."
- "Refrigerate to firm it up or freeze small jars for longer storage. Room temp leftovers soften over time, may separate oil—stir gently before serving."

ingredientsNote: "Cookies need to be dry, brittle, not chewy. Digestive biscuits, gingersnaps, or shortbread all lend different profiles—choose wisely based on flavor. Coconut oil is preferred for richness and a neutral coconut scent; vegetable or canola oil are fine substitutes but lose that subtle aroma. Whole milk adds creaminess and aids emulsification but can be swapped with almond or oat milk if dairy-free; be wary of thin plant-based milks making spread runny. Brown sugar adds molasses notes and moisture—adjust for sweetness. Ground cinnamon is simple but flexible; try cardamom or nutmeg for twists. Nuts optional but improve texture contrast—always toast them; raw nuts lack punch. Salt isn’t optional—to balance sweet and bring out nuanced flavors."
