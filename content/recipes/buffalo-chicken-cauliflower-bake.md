ingredients:
- "1 bag (about 12oz) riced cauliflower, steamed"
- "1 cup heavy cream, or plain Greek yogurt as substitute"
- "4oz cream cheese, softened"
- "3/4 cup buffalo hot sauce"
- "1 teaspoon sriracha, optional for extra heat"
- "1 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "1/2 teaspoon cracked black pepper"
- "1/2 teaspoon chipotle powder, smoked paprika if unavailable"
- "2 cups shredded Colby jack cheese, divided"
- "3/4 cup crumbled blue cheese, divided"
- "3 cups shredded rotisserie chicken"
- "Nonstick cooking spray"
- "2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley"
instructions:
- "Start by heating riced cauliflower in microwave about 4 to 6 minutes—don’t rush. Bag will steam inside; let it sit 3 minutes before opening to finish softening. Texture should be tender but not mushy. Avoid watery mess, drain if needed."
- "Meanwhile, in a heavy-bottom saucepan—choose thick base to avoid scorching—combine cream and softened cream cheese. Medium-low heat, constant stirring crucial. Cream should just begin to simmer, cheese fully melted. Watch for glossy texture, no lumps. About 5-6 minutes here."
- "Add buffalo sauce and sriracha. Then salt, pepper, and chipotle powder. Don’t dump all at once—whisk steadily, taste for balance between heat and salt. Adjust as needed, but no overpowering spices."
- "Lower heat slightly. Incorporate 1 1/2 cups Colby jack and 3/4 cup blue cheese crumbles gradually. Stir constantly. Cheese must melt fully and create a thick, velvety sauce. If sauce separates or grains form, temperature too high."
- "Preheat oven to 345°F. Different ovens vary—target slightly lower temp for even baking, helps prevent cheese burning on top."
- "Combine shredded chicken and steamed cauliflower into cheese sauce. Mix thoroughly, ensure chicken evenly coated. Transfer mixture to a sprayed 9 by 9-inch baking dish. Spread out evenly. Sprinkle remaining 1/2 cup Colby jack on top. Avoid piling too thick, will bake unevenly."
- "Bake 22 to 28 minutes. Visual cues key: bubbling edges, lightly toasted cheese crust, and golden spots on top. Don't rely exclusively on timer. Let rest at least 5 minutes before serving—sets the filling, easier to portion."
- "Sprinkle with remaining blue cheese and fresh parsley just before serving. Adds sharpness and brightness against creamy, spicy base. Serve warm."
