ingredients:
- "3 tablespoons unsalted butter, browned and still warm"
- "2 tablespoons pure maple syrup"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1 teaspoon chai spice blend (cinnamon, cardamom, clove, ginger - no nutmeg)"
- "8 cups freshly popped plain popcorn"
- "1 cup dried pear chips (substitute dried cranberries or apple chips)"
- "Pinch of sea salt"
instructions:
- "==="
- "Melt butter in a small pan over low heat - watch closely. Swirl as it foams and turns golden brown, nutty aroma will hit in seconds. Avoid burning or black specks. Remove from heat immediately once browned, you want warm not hot."
- "Stir in maple syrup, vanilla, and chai spices. Mix briskly to emulsify. Expect thick, glossy liquid that lightly bubbles at edges. If it looks too runny, no worries - will coat popcorn better anyway."
- "Pour warm mixture over popcorn in a large mixing bowl. Toss quickly but gently with a spatula or two spoons, making sure every kernel flashes with a thin glaze. Too slow and syrup cools, causing clumps."
- "Add the dried pear chips and a pinch of sea salt last. Toss once more to combine. The pear chips bring a subtle sweetness and crunch contrast."
- "Serve right away for crunch. If you stash leftovers airtight, popcorn texture softens but flavor stays. To revive crispness, spread on baking sheet 5 minutes in 300-degree oven - watch carefully to prevent burning."
