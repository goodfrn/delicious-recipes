ingredients:
- "1 1/4 cups digestive biscuits or graham crackers crushed"
- "1/3 cup light brown sugar packed"
- "6 tablespoons melted unsalted butter"
- "1 teaspoon ground cinnamon"
- "3 cups chopped firm apples (preferably Granny Smith or Fuji)"
- "2 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "1/4 cup raw cane or demerara sugar"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground allspice"
- "1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract"
- "6 ounces cream cheese softened"
- "3/4 cup full fat Greek yogurt or sour cream"
- "1/3 cup heavy cream"
- "3/4 cup granulated sugar"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "3 large eggs at room temperature"
- "For streusel:"
- "1/2 cup all purpose flour"
- "1/3 cup brown sugar"
- "1/4 cup chopped pecans or walnuts"
- "1 teaspoon cinnamon"
- "3 tablespoons cold unsalted butter cubed"
instructions:
- "==="
- "Make crust first. Preheat oven 340F instead of 350 to compensate for longer bake. Line 9-inch springform pan with parchment bottom, grease sides lightly or line sides with parchment strips for easy release. Blitz biscuits, brown sugar, cinnamon, and melted butter in food processor until coarse crumbs stick together when pinched. Press crumbs firmly into bottom and about 1 inch up the sides. Use bottom of a flat glass or measuring cup for smooth even crust. Bake 10-12 minutes. Will smell toasty, edges turn golden. Cool crust completely. This step prevents soggy crust later."
- "==="
- "Make apples. Melt butter in sauté pan over medium heat until it just foams. Add chopped apples, sugar, vanilla, and spices. Stir often, apples should soften and release juice after 3-5 minutes. Cook until tender but not mushy - test by piercing one with a fork. Critical: drain excess liquid through fine mesh or gently press with paper towels. Too much moisture ruins texture. Set apples aside to cool. If apples are watery, pat dry again before topping cheesecake."
- "==="
- "Mix streusel. In a bowl, combine flour, brown sugar, cinnamon, nuts. Cut in cold butter with fingers or fork until crumbly clusters form. Should resemble coarse sand or wet gravel. Set aside."
- "==="
- "Prepare cheesecake filling. In large bowl, beat cream cheese on medium speed about 2 minutes until creamy and lump-free. Add sugars and beat again. Stir in Greek yogurt (or sour cream), heavy cream, vanilla, and cinnamon. Mix just to combine—don't overbeat or air bubbles form. Lower speed, add eggs one at a time, mixing gently after each until just blended. Overmixing eggs causes cracks. Scrape sides with rubber spatula to incorporate all."
- "==="
- "Wrap springform pan sides with foil to avoid water bath leaks and overbrowning if using one. Pour filling into cooled crust. Spoon drained apple mixture evenly over filling. Sprinkle streusel evenly on top, not too thick or will mask apples."
- "==="
- "Bake cheesecake at 340F for 65-75 minutes. Done when edges set firmly and center jiggles slightly when pan is shaken. Avoid overbaking; filling will firm as it cools. Turn oven off, leave cheesecake inside for 1 hour to slowly finish cooking and reduce cracks."
- "==="
- "Remove from oven, cool at room temperature about 1 hour. Chilling overnight or at least 6 hours crucial to set filling completely. Run knife around sides before unlocking springform rim. Optional: drizzle caramel sauce over top just before serving."
- "==="
- "Slice with sharp knife dipped in hot water then wiped dry between cuts for clean slices. Store covered in refrigerator up to 3 days. If crust softens, defend by serving chilled or reheat briefly in toaster oven at low heat to crisp crumbs."
