ingredients:
- "2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1/3 cup granulated sugar"
- "1 tablespoon ground cinnamon"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1/4 teaspoon salt"
- "1/3 cup cold unsalted butter, diced"
- "3/4 cup plain Greek yogurt"
- "1 large egg, beaten"
- "1 teaspoon almond extract"
- "1 cup peeled and chopped tart apple"
- "1/2 cup chopped pecans"
- "1/4 cup packed brown sugar"
- "3 tablespoons melted unsalted butter"
instructions:
- "Line a 12-cup muffin tin with paper liners or grease very well. Using liners helps with cleanup but isn’t essential if you grease thoroughly—watch out for sticking."
- "In a large bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, cinnamon, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. The chemical leaveners keep crumb light alongside wet ingredients."
- "Cut cold butter into flour mix using a pastry blender or two forks. Some bits of butter visible in dry mix—important for flakiness so do not overwork here."
- "In separate bowl, stir Greek yogurt, beaten egg, and almond extract until combined. Greek yogurt offers tang and moisture; plain yogurt is a fine substitute but expect slightly thinner batter."
- "Add wet mix and chopped apple to flour-butter base. Fold gently to moisten everything. Avoid overmixing—the batter should be lumpy but no dry pockets remain. Overmix causes dense muffins, underdeveloped rise."
- "Spoon batter evenly into muffin cups, filling each about three-quarters full. That’s room for rise but avoids overflow."
- "Mix pecans, brown sugar, and melted butter with fork until crumbly but moist enough to hold clumps. Sprinkle topping generously over batter without pressing down—it crisps up during bake."
- "Bake at 375 degrees F on middle rack. Listen for tin sounds—muffled but firm means muffins setting. Edges turn golden and pull away slightly from pan when done. Test by inserting toothpick in center—should emerge with few moist crumbs, no wet batter."
- "Cool muffins at least 10 minutes in pan before removing to wire rack. Warm muffins sensitive—removing too soon breaks structure and causes crumbles."
- "Store baked muffins in airtight container at room temp up to 2 days or freeze individually wrapped for longer life. Reheat wrapped in microwave 15 seconds for soft, fresh feel."
