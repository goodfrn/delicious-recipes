ingredients:
- "4 pounds russet potatoes peeled and sliced 1 3/16 inch thick or thinner"
- "3 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "3 tablespoons all-purpose flour"
- "3 cups whole milk warmed slightly"
- "1 bay leaf"
- "1 teaspoon salt plus extra for seasoning"
- "1/2 teaspoon black pepper freshly ground"
- "1/2 teaspoon onion powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "1 1/2 cups shredded sharp gouda cheese"
- "1 cup shredded white cheddar cheese"
- "1/2 cup extra white cheddar cheese for topping"
- "cooking spray"
- "optional garnish bacon bits scallions chives"
instructions:
- "Set oven to 355 degrees Fahrenheit. Grease a 3 quart casserole with spray to prevent sticking."
- "Melt butter in small saucepan over medium heat until foamy but not browned. Stir in flour quickly to form thick paste. Keep stirring for about 1 minute to cook raw flour taste away but don’t let it brown."
- "Gradually whisk milk into butter-flour paste. Do it steadily to avoid lumps. Expect mixture to loosen and then thicken as you stir. Add bay leaf salt pepper onion powder garlic powder. Bring to gentle simmer, bubbles just breaking surface, not rolling boil. Simmer 4-5 minutes until slightly thickened."
- "Remove sauce from heat immediately once thickened and bay leaf infused. Stir in gouda and white cheddar, 2 1/2 cups combined. Cheese will slowly melt and thicken sauce to creamy consistency. If sauce feels thin let sit off heat for a few minutes it will thicken more."
- "Drain sliced potatoes thoroughly if soaked in cold water. Soaking stops browning but remove moisture well or sauce thins."
- "Discard bay leaf. Layer half potatoes in baking dish, overlapping slightly like shingles for even bake."
- "Pour half cheese sauce evenly over potatoes pushing around to fill gaps."
- "Top with rest of potato slices, then pour remaining cheese sauce over that. Cover tightly with aluminum foil sealed well to trap steam. This steams potatoes soft and melds flavors."
- "Bake for roughly 65 to 70 minutes. Look for bubbly sauce along edges and potatoes easily pierced with fork but not collapsing."
- "Remove foil carefully to avoid steam burns."
- "Sprinkle remaining 1/2 cup shredded cheddar over top. Increase oven temp to 375°F to get golden crispy crust. Return uncovered to oven for 15-20 minutes. Watch closely so cheese melts then browns nicely without burning."
- "Let rest 15 minutes before cutting. Resting lets sauce set and cool slightly so slices hold shape."
- "Optional: scatter bacon bits chopped scallions or chives on top for added texture flavor."
- "If sauce is too runny next time use less milk or cook sauce longer before cheese. If top browns too fast tent with foil. Uniform thin slices mean more even cooking time. Mandoline preferred for consistent thickness."
