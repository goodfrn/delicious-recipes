ingredients:
- "1 rack pork ribs, membrane removed"
- "2 tbsp light brown sugar"
- "3 tbsp dark brown sugar"
- "2 tsp onion powder"
- "2 tsp kosher salt"
- "1 tbsp smoked chipotle powder"
- "1 tsp black pepper"
- "1 tsp ground mustard"
- "1 tsp ground cumin"
- "1 tsp cayenne pepper"
- "½ tsp ground cinnamon"
- "1 dash ground nutmeg"
- "½ cup barbecue sauce"
- "3 tbsp molasses"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven just shy of 300°F, set to 295°F – gives a slower cook, less drying. Let heat settle while prepping ribs."
- "Mix sugars brown and dark, onion powder, kosher salt, smoked chipotle powder, black pepper, ground mustard, cumin, cayenne, cinnamon, and nutmeg in a small bowl. Whisk aggressively to break lumps, spread evenly later."
- "Membrane removal crucial. Slide small sharp knife just under membrane edge at ribs’ narrow end. Wiggle knife gently, lift, then pull with fingers. Don’t skip this step—keeps rub and smoke into meat better, not rubbery."
- "Heavy-duty foil, length enough for wrapping full rack plus excess for sealing. Lay foil on baking sheet for stability, stops drips and clamps heat inside."
- "Place ribs bone side up on foil. Keeps meat on foil surface for better rub contact. Meaty side exposed after rub application."
- "Dry rub: Spread one-third on bones, push rub into crevices. Flip ribs. Rub remaining mixture into meat side fully. Coat thick, no bare spots, rub in like you mean it."
- "Combine foil ends, crimp well with no gaps. Roll edges tight. Locks moisture, steams ribs in own juices. Prevents drying out, creates tender texture."
- "Slide pan into oven rack center. Bake for 2 hours 10 mins. Smell sugar and spices deepening. Meat softens, little shrinkage at bone edges signals progress."
- "Remove ribs carefully (steam hot). Crank oven to broil, let it heat 5 minutes while mixing sauce."
- "In bowl, whisk barbecue sauce and molasses. The molasses adds dark richness, deeper caramelization. Best with thick sauce, balanced sweet and tang."
- "Open foil cautiously, steam escape loud and fast. Spoon sauce over ribs thickly but don't drown them. Every rib gets glossy coat, clings well to rough meat surface."
- "Place ribs back under broiler 3-4 minutes. Watch closely—should bubble, darken, not burn. Sweet sauce starts to caramelize and bubble, edges crisp and tacky."
- "Remove from oven, let ribs rest on foil for 10 minutes. Juices settle, meat firms just enough to slice clean. Helps ribs separate easily without shredding."
- "Slice between bones with sharp knife. Serve warm or room temp. Ribs flexible but hold shape, resistant to drying out even reheated."
- "If ribs too chewy, likely membrane left on or undercooked. Overly dry? Foil not sealed tightly or oven temp too high."
- "Substitutions: Swap cumin with smoked paprika for smoky hit without cumin flavor. Use maple syrup instead of molasses for lighter sweetness but less caramel depth. Brown sugar quantities adjusted to reduce sweetness slightly but keep balance."
- "If ribs need faster cook, raise oven to 310°F but reduce time by 10-15min, watch for drying. Longer cook at lower temp produces tender pull-apart texture. Texture shifts noticeable in cooking – slight bounce back under pressure means done."
- "Dry rub doubles as a seasoning for other cuts: chicken thighs, pork shoulder. Rubbing well and allowing time for penetration key before baking."
- "Use heavy gloves handling foil after baking, hot steam lethal. Opening foil too fast nearly guaranteed burns. Let rest inside closed foil if needed up to 15 min more."
