ingredients:
- "1 box chocolate supreme brownie mix"
- "1 syrup packet included with mix"
- "¾ cup all-purpose flour"
- "¼ cup whole wheat flour"
- "⅓ cup melted butter (instead of canola oil)"
- "2 large eggs"
- "3 tablespoons water"
- "½ cup semi-sweet chocolate chips"
- "½ cup chopped toasted pecans (replace part of chips)"
instructions:
- "1. Dump brownie mix, syrup from packet, both flours, melted butter, eggs, and water into big bowl. Use handheld mixer low speed about 50 seconds. Scrape down sides. Batter thick, shaggy, chocolate aroma rises."
- "2. Fold in chocolate chips and toasted pecans with spatula. Don’t overmix or nuts lose crunch."
- "3. Cover bowl tightly with plastic wrap. Chill minimum 45 minutes—keeps dough firm, flavor melds, easier to handle."
- "4. Heat oven to 355°F. Line baking sheet with parchment paper to avoid sticking and uneven browning."
- "5. Use 1½ inch cookie scoop (approx 2 tablespoons) to portion dough balls. Place 10–12 per tray, spaced well for spread."
- "6. Bake 11–12 minutes. Look for tops firming up, crackle webs on surface, edges slightly pulling away. Avoid overbake or dry crumb."
- "7. Rest cookies on pan 12 minutes before transferring to rack. They feel fragile hot; firm up on cooling."
- "8. Repeat scooping, baking with remaining dough. Store cookies airtight for days; warm slightly before serving for melty gluten breakfast feel."
