ingredients:
- "6 boneless skinless chicken thighs, patted dry"
- "8 slices thick-cut bacon"
- "1 large yellow onion, sliced thin"
- "1⁄3 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1 1⁄2 cups homemade chicken velouté (or substitute condensed cream of celery soup)"
- "1 can (12 oz) water"
- "1 1⁄2 cups smoked gouda cheese, shredded"
- "Salt and freshly ground black pepper"
- "2 tablespoons vegetable oil (if needed)"
- "Hot mashed potatoes or grits for serving"
instructions:
- "Start with bacon—medium heat skillet. Sizzle until edges crisp, not burnt. Remove to paper towels. Keep rendered fat in skillet—it’s flavor gold."
- "Once bacon cool, crumble it by hand. Don’t rush this; uneven pieces add texture contrast later."
- "Preheat oven to 355°F. Spray a 9x13-inch baking dish with nonstick spray. Don’t forget this step or stick mess awaits."
- "Season thighs generously with salt and pepper. Dust lightly with flour—shake off excess. Flour coating binds sauce and improves browning."
- "If bacon fat is skimpy, add a splash of vegetable oil. Brown thighs in bacon drippings, medium heat. Get a golden crust—don’t crowd pan or they steam. Flip after 3–4 minutes when crust resists sticking."
- "Transfer browned thighs to baking dish, spacing evenly. Don’t stack. Crowding clogs heat circulation."
- "Into the skillet go thin onion slices. Stir gently. Cook 6–7 minutes till translucent then hitting light brown, edges curling—a sweet aroma will fill kitchen. Avoid burning; onions develop bitter off-flavors fast."
- "Spread onions over thighs evenly. This layer adds moisture and flavor base beneath sauce."
- "Mix velouté with water in bowl. Pour over onions and chicken, ensuring all coated. Velouté lighter than cream soup—less heavy but still rich."
- "Slide dish into oven uncovered. Bake 27–32 minutes. Chicken should register 165°F internally or juices run clear when pierced. Sauce will bubble and thicken slightly."
- "Pull dish out, scatter shredded smoked gouda atop hot chicken and onions. Sprinkle crumbled bacon on cheese. Return to oven—just long enough, 6–8 minutes—for cheese to melt, bubble, and develop faint golden spots."
- "Serve immediately. Ladle over hot mashed potatoes or creamy grits. Expect a contrast of melty cheese, smoky bacon crunch, and tender chicken dripping with velouté."
