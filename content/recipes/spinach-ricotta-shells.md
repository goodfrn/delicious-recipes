ingredients:
- "12 jumbo pasta shells"
- "12 ounces ricotta cheese"
- "3 cups shredded mozzarella cheese divided"
- "1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese"
- "1 large egg"
- "1 tablespoon Italian seasoning"
- "6 cups fresh spinach leaves"
- "3 cloves garlic minced"
- "3 tablespoons olive oil divided"
- "3 cups marinara sauce"
- "Salt and freshly ground black pepper"
- "Cooking spray"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 380F. Grease 9×13 inch baking pan with cooking spray."
- "Bring large pot salted water to rapid boil. Drop in jumbo shells, stirring gently immediately. Cook till shells hold shape but teeth feel slight resistance, about 10 minutes. Drain. Toss lightly with 2 teaspoons olive oil to prevent sticking."
- "Toast minced garlic in 2 tablespoons olive oil over medium heat. Watch for fragrant aroma, about 1 minute. Add fresh spinach. Stir constantly till all leaves collapse, moisture bubbles, about 4 minutes. Scoop spinach onto paper towels. Press firmly to remove liquid. Chop roughly once cooled enough to handle."
- "Spread 2 cups marinara evenly in bottom of baking dish."
- "In large mixing bowl combine ricotta, chopped spinach, 2 1/2 cups mozzarella, Parmesan, egg, Italian seasoning, and pinch salt and black pepper. Fold gently until uniform but not overmixed."
- "Fill each shell with 2 heaping tablespoons of filling. Nestle shells carefully on sauce in pan, seams facing up to hold filling. If necessary, leave small gaps for sauce circulation but keep compact."
- "Pour remaining 1 cup marinara over shells. Sprinkle remaining 1/2 cup mozzarella evenly on top."
- "Cover pan tightly with foil. Bake 25 minutes. Listen for bubbling sauce edges and cheese softening through foil. Remove foil carefully."
- "Continue baking uncovered another 7 minutes or until cheese topping is blistered lightly and golden in spots. Edges of shells will firm up. Internal stitching feel of shells remains tender but not mushy."
- "Once out of oven, rest 10 minutes to let filling set. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley or basil if desired."
- "Serve with garlic bread or simple salad. Store leftovers covered tightly in fridge. Reheat gently to avoid drying pasta."
