ingredients:
- "10 ounces white chocolate chunks (substitute 8 ounces white chocolate chips + 2 ounces cream cheese for smoother finish)"
- "14 ounces sweetened condensed milk (try half evaporated milk + half honey for lighter sweetness)"
- "3 tablespoons molasses"
- "1/3 cup dark brown sugar (instead of white sugar for deeper caramel notes)"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon"
- "1/4 teaspoon ground ginger"
- "1/8 teaspoon ground cloves"
- "1/8 teaspoon ground cardamom (twist for complexity)"
- "Pinch salt"
- "Crushed ginger snap cookies (about 1/4 cup) for topping"
- "Seasonal nonpareil sprinkles to decorate"
- "Nonstick spray to coat pan"
instructions:
- "Lightly grease an 8x8 pan with nonstick spray."
- "Dump white chocolate and sweetened condensed milk in a microwave-safe bowl."
- "Heat in 20-second bursts, stirring thoroughly each time. The mix stiffens midway; stir patiently till fully melted and glossy with errant chunks gone."
- "Once melted, stir in molasses and dark brown sugar until color shifts to deep caramel and texture velvety. Don’t rush here; molasses thickens but dissolves with enough warm stirring."
- "Mix in cinnamon, ginger, cloves, cardamom, and salt. Spices bloom aroma and flavor at this stage; stir briskly to avoid clump."
- "Pour fudge batter into the prepped pan."
- "Dampen hands lightly and press fudge evenly to corners and edges. This tactile method smooths surface better than spatulas and avoids disruptive swirls or air pockets."
- "Sprinkle crushed ginger snaps and nonpareils quickly on still-tacky fudge. Gently press crumbs and sprinkles to set without embedding them too deep. Visual contrast and crunchy accents."
- "Chill fudge in refrigerator at least 1 hour 55 minutes (range 1 hour 50 min to 2 hours) until edges firm but center slightly soft to touch when pressed lightly."
- "Remove from fridge 10 minutes before serving to soften for best bite. Cut into 24 squares with sharp knife wiping blade clean between cuts."
- "Leftover fudge hangs well wrapped in airtight container up to 1 week in fridge or frozen for 2 months. Thaw slowly in fridge to avoid condensation spots."

instructionsNote: "Microwave melting demands attention. 20-second bursts, not one long blast, prevents scorching white chocolate which burns easily. Stir vigorously after each burst, scrape sides and bottom to homogenize. Mixture stiffens halfway, that’s molasses and sugar locking in—stir patiently, keep heat low, don’t add sugar upfront or it clumps dry. Stir spices in once fudge is molten, heat activates aromas—no need to toast separately. Spread fudge with clean damp hands, better grip and even spread than utensil, plus prevents scratching pan.
