ingredients:
- "1 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1/2 cup oat flour"
- "2 tsp arrowroot powder"
- "1/2 tsp baking soda"
- "1/2 tsp baking powder"
- "1/2 tsp fine sea salt"
- "1 tsp ground ginger"
- "1 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon"
- "1/4 tsp ground cloves"
- "1/4 tsp freshly ground black pepper"
- "7 tbsp unsalted browned butter chilled"
- "1/4 cup packed dark brown sugar"
- "1/4 cup granulated white sugar"
- "5 tbsp molasses"
- "1 large egg"
- "1 large egg yolk"
- "1 tsp vanilla extract"
- "1/4 cup turbinado sugar for rolling"
- "1/3 cup white chocolate chips"
- "1 tsp coconut oil"
- "Festive sprinkles optional"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 370°F. Lightly grease or line baking sheets with parchment."
- "In a large bowl whisk together all dry ingredients except sugars and molasses: flours, arrowroot powder, baking soda, baking powder, salt, spices, black pepper. Keep spices fine ground to avoid uneven flavor bursts."
- "Brown butter in a saucepan over medium heat stirring constantly, watch for nutty aroma and brown bits on bottom. Remove from heat and chill briefly until just warm."
- "Fit stand mixer with paddle blade. Cream browned butter and both sugars on medium speed 3-4 minutes until mixture appears lighter and visibly aerated. Stop mixer to scrape sides with a rubber spatula to avoid uneven creaming."
- "Add molasses carefully (mixer off) to avoid splatters, then resume mixing to combine. Beat in egg and egg yolk one at a time followed by vanilla. Scrape sides occasionally. Dough should shine and feel a bit tacky but not wet."
- "Gradually add dry mix in thirds, mixing on low speed until just combined. Avoid over mixing; too much will develop gluten and toughen cookies."
- "If dough sticky, chill 15 minutes or dust hands lightly with flour. Form balls about 2 tbsp each, roll in turbinado sugar for crisp exterior texture."
- "Arrange cookies spaced evenly on sheets. Bake 12-17 minutes watching for cracked tops and edges firm to touch but centers soft and slightly puffed — this guarantees chewiness inside."
- "Cool on sheet for 5 minutes then transfer to wire rack. Wait at least 10 minutes before decorating."
- "Melt white chocolate chips with coconut oil in microwave in 20 second bursts stirring well each time until silky smooth. Dip half the cookie or drizzle. Sprinkle immediately if using decorations."
- "Store fully cooled cookies in an airtight container at room temp. They stay soft and chewy for up to seven days. Can freeze dough balls up to one month wrapped tightly."
