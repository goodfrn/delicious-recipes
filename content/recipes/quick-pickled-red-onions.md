ingredients:
- "3 cups water"
- "2 medium red onions sliced thin"
- "1 cup white wine vinegar"
- "1 tablespoon honey"
- "1 teaspoon kosher salt"
instructions:
- "=== Pickling Prep ==="
- "Fill a saucepan halfway with water. Bring it to a steady boil; watch steam swirl. When bubbles break surface, no gentle simmer now."
- "Add sliced onions to boiling water. Watch them soften, shift color—edges translucent, layers pliable but hold form."
- "Pour in white wine vinegar and honey immediately. Swirling liquid shifts tone, tiny bubbles cling to onion pieces."
- "Sprinkle kosher salt evenly. Salt draws out moisture, balances sweet acid; don’t skip or uneven dissolution can leave harsh spots."
- "Lower heat to medium-low. Simmer gently, hearing steady small bubbles, not aggressive rolling boil. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking or uneven cooking."
- "Test onions after about 15 minutes. They should be tender when poked but not falling apart. Texture is key—soft enough to bend, firm enough to snap slightly."
- "Turn off heat. Carefully drain contents into colander. Let onions drain well; excess brine dilutes flavor and ruins crunch later."
- "Cool to room temp before using. Store in airtight container in fridge up to 10 days. Flavor deepens over time but shriveling signals past prime."
- "=== Use Notes ==="
- "Serve on tacos, burgers, or chopped in salads. Substitute red wine vinegar for a darker hue and earthier taste. Raw sugar can replace honey but dissolve first in warm vinegar before adding to pan."
