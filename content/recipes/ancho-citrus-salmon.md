ingredients:
- "4 salmon fillets, skin on or off, about 5-6 ounces each"
- "2 tablespoons fresh lime juice, replaces lemon juice"
- "1 tablespoon honey, swaps agave nectar"
- "1 teaspoon smoked paprika, stands in for ancho chili powder"
- "2 tablespoons grapefruit juice, replaces orange juice concentrate"
- "1 tablespoon olive oil"
- "Cooking spray or a light brush of oil for pan"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 375 degrees. Use a vented broiler pan or a wire rack set over rimmed baking sheet sprayed lightly with oil. Rinse salmon under cold water and pat dry. The vented pan allows heat to circulate around all sides, prevents steaming and soggy skin or bottom crust."
- "Combine lime juice, honey, smoked paprika, grapefruit juice, and olive oil in small saucepan. Warm on medium heat until honey dissolves fully and liquids meld. Keep warm but do not simmer, to keep glaze syrupy and sticky."
- "Lay fillets on the pan, evenly spaced. Brush a generous coat of glaze on first side only. Place in oven for 6-7 minutes, letting glaze start to caramelize and salmon begin to firm around edges. Look for translucence fading, surface bubbling slightly."
- "Flip fillets carefully. Baste this side, then return to oven for another 5 minutes. The glaze should thicken and cling, fish getting opaque and flaky at edges but still juicy. Check flakiness by gently pressing with fork at thickest part."
- "Give one last baste, place back for 3-4 minutes if needed. Cooking time depends on thickness - thinner pieces may need less. Don’t overcook or flesh dries and toughens. Center should just barely flake, still moist and shiny inside."
- "Remove pan, let fish rest 3 minutes. Resting lets fibers relax, redistributes juices. Makes leftovers less dry too. Drizzle residual glaze from pan over fish or serve extra on side for dipping."
- "Serve with simple sides - grilled veggies, a green salad, or roasted potatoes. If you do not have vented pan, raise fish on wire rack inside sheet tray to mimic airflow. No lime? Lemon or even a splash of vinegar works with honey and paprika. Grapefruit juice is bright and acidic; substitute with fresh orange or a splash of tart white grape juice if needed. Smoked paprika adds complexity and warmth—don’t skip or replace with plain paprika or chili powder only."
