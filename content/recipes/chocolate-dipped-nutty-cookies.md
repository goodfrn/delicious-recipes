ingredients:
- "3/4 cup unsalted butter softened"
- "1/2 cup powdered sugar sifted"
- "1 teaspoon pure almond extract"
- "1 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1/2 cup finely ground almond flour"
- "1/4 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "1/3 cup coconut oil solid"
- "1 cup dark chocolate chips"
- "1/2 cup toasted chopped almonds"
instructions:
- "Heat oven 340˚F. Spray 2 cookie sheets lightly with baking spray; parchment optional for cleanup."
- "Whip softened butter and powdered sugar in large bowl on medium speed. Look for pale, light volume, texture starts airy. Takes about 3 minutes. Scrape sides mid-process so no lumps hide."
- "Add almond extract. Drop in flours and salt. Mix slowly to avoid flour cloud. Must form soft dough - sticky, not crumbly. If too loose add slight flour pinch, too dry a few drops milk."
- "Shape dough into logs 2-inch long by 1/2-inch thick or roll into little mounds for round shapes. Space at least 2 inches apart on sheets."
- "Press fork tines gently across each log almost full length but leave one tip alone. This vents steam, adds texture. No pressing on tip keeps areas for chocolate dip."
- "Bake near center rack 16 to 19 minutes. Watch edges. When edges gleam golden brown and centers just firm under light touch, pull out. Let cool on wire racks fully before chocolate step."
- "In microwave safe bowl, combine coconut oil and chocolate chips. Heat in 30-second bursts, stir between, repeat till melted and glossy. Avoid burning chocolate—burnt chocolate bitter, grainy."
- "Dip each cookie tip into chocolate, rotate so tip fully coated. Rest on plate. Immediately sprinkle chopped toasted almonds. Let set at room temp until firm or chill for 7 minutes."
- "Serve or store airtight. Cookies hold crisp texture several days but dip works best fresh."
