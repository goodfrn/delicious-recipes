ingredients:
- "25 Ritz style crackers"
- "3⁄4 cup creamy peanut butter powder"
- "1 cup almond flour"
- "2 1⁄4 cups white chocolate chips, divided"
- "1⁄2 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "Pinch salt"
instructions:
- "Line a half-sheet pan with parchment paper. Dry surface prevents sticking; don’t skip parchment."
- "In a small bowl, combine peanut butter powder, almond flour, pinch salt, and vanilla. Stir with spoon or small spatula until crumbly but cohesive. The almond flour replaces powdered sugar, bringing dryer texture and nuttiness."
- "Arrange 25 crackers, face down, evenly spaced on the lined pan. This sets the base before filling."
- "Drop about 1 rounded tablespoon peanut butter mix onto each cracker, center only. Avoid edge—too thin topping, leaks when pressed."
- "Top each with another cracker, face up. Press gently but firmly until filling nears edges without spilling out. Overpressing breaks crackers; underpressing makes loose sandwiches."
- "Refrigerate on pan for 35 to 50 minutes. Filling firms, crackers settle; watch for condensation, wipe if needed to keep coating dry later."
- "Melt 1 3⁄4 cups white chocolate chips in microwave-safe bowl. Heat 25 seconds, stir, repeat short bursts until mostly melted, not burnt. Residual heat finishes melting—avoid overheating, seizing risks."
- "Add remaining 1⁄2 cup chips and stir vigorously until velvety, no lumps. Use vanilla-infused chips if possible, adds depth."
- "Remove sandwiches from fridge. One by one, submerge in chocolate, use fork to flip carefully. Coat fully. Tap fork on bowl edges to shed excess drip; prevents thick blobs that crack when set."
- "Place coated sandwiches back on parchment. Work quickly; chocolate begins to harden, get a matte finish, shiny means too warm."
- "Chill covered for 25 to 35 minutes. Chocolate sets, peanuts meld with cracker crunch. Store airtight at room temp for up to 2 days or fridge for longer to prevent melting."
- "Serve cool, not cold. Plain peanut butter or almond butter powder both work; sub with cashew powder for different nut note. Use graham crackers if no Ritz available. If chocolate thickens, stir in a teaspoon of vegetable oil; thins for better dip consistency."
