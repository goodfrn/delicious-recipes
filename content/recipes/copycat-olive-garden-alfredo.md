ingredients:
- "12 ounces fettuccine pasta"
- "3 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "4 cloves garlic minced"
- "1/4 teaspoon salt"
- "1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg"
- "2 tablespoons all-purpose flour"
- "1 cup heavy cream"
- "1/2 cup whole milk"
- "1/2 cup low sodium chicken broth"
- "3/4 cup freshly grated parmesan cheese"
- "1/4 cup freshly grated pecorino romano cheese"
- "2 tablespoons chopped scallions"
- "Fresh parsley chopped for garnish"
instructions:
- "Hot water rolling boil. Salt it like the sea to season pasta internally. Add fettuccine, stir once to separate. Cook pasta stirring occasionally until just shy of al dente. Drain and toss lightly with drizzle olive oil or neutral oil so it doesn’t stick. Set aside."
- "Large skillet, medium flame. Drop butter in, let melt till bubbling edges appear. Add minced garlic and salt immediately. Stir gently, smell aroma rising - garlic soft but not colored. Nutmeg dusted in now replaces garlic powder giving subtle warmth."
- "Sprinkle flour evenly over butter garlic mix. Whisk constantly for 1-2 minutes, no browning. This cooks raw flour taste out. Slowly pour in cream, milk, and chicken broth in thin streams while whisking vigorously. Watch sauce thicken, tiny bubbles form on edges, audible simmer starts. Lower heat to maintain gentle simmer, not boil to avoid curdling. Simmer about 5 minutes, whisk often, sauce should coat back of spoon with veil thin enough to run but sticky."
- "Off heat, sprinkle parmesan and pecorino cheeses. Whisk briskly till melted, sauce smooth, glossy. If too thick add splash more broth or milk, whisk. Taste and adjust salt. Sauce must be silky, slightly clingy, not gluey."
- "Add pasta back into skillet. Use tongs to toss, coating strands thoroughly but delicately to avoid breaking noodles. Pasta will soak some sauce as it rests a minute off heat."
- "Serve immediately, scattered scallions for crunch and fresh parsley for color. Optional cracked black pepper."
- "Common Notes: Butter quality matters; salted vs unsalted affects final salt balance. If sauce splits, a teaspoon cold water whisked quickly may bring back together. garlic browning ruins smooth sauce, watch flames. Flour lumps? Whisk flour into butter fully before liquids. No heavy cream? Add more milk and butter but sauce thinner. Fresh cheeses melt better than pregrated powdered types."
