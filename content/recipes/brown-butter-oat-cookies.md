ingredients:
- "1 1/4 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1/2 cup spelt flour"
- "1/2 cup old fashioned oats"
- "1 teaspoon arrowroot powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1/4 teaspoon baking powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "3/4 cup packed light brown sugar"
- "1/4 cup granulated sugar"
- "1/2 cup unsalted butter, browned and cooled"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract"
- "3/4 cup butterscotch chips"
- "3/4 cup mini semi-sweet chocolate chips"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon"
- "Flaky sea salt for sprinkle"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 380F. Line two baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone mats. Set aside."
- "Brown butter in a light skillet over medium heat, swirling often. Watch for foam bubbling followed by nutty brown specks and aroma hinting toasted nuts. Remove immediately to cool 10-15 min off heat. This step adds depth and complexity not found in plain melted butter."
- "Whisk all dry ingredients — all-purpose flour, spelt flour, oats, arrowroot powder, baking soda, baking powder, kosher salt, and cinnamon — in a medium bowl. This blending ensures leavening distributes evenly. Set aside."
- "In separate bowl, whisk sugars with cooled browned butter until smooth and glossy. Warm butter helps sugars dissolve better, preventing gritty texture. Add eggs one at a time, fully incorporating each for stable emulsion. Add vanilla extract last."
- "Fold in dry mix carefully until barely combined. Overmixing develops gluten, toughening cookies—avoid. Then stir in butterscotch and chocolate chips gently, just enough so dough looks speckled."
- "Scooping dough can feel sticky. Use a cookie scoop for consistent shapes—about 10-12 on your trays. Space them apart; they spread. Refrigerate dough 5-10 minutes if too soft, especially in warm kitchens."
- "Bake 8-13 minutes, watching edges, not just time. Edges gain light gold tone, centers still look set with faint shine and slightly puffed. Underbaking leads to gooey centers that collapse; overbaking dries cookies out."
- "Remove from oven. While warm, sprinkle flaky sea salt over the tops. Salt balances sweetness and cuts fat heaviness, vital in brown butter recipes. Optionally add extra chips on top for show."
- "Cool 5 minutes on sheets before transferring. Centers finish setting but stay tender. Store airtight after fully cooled to retain chewiness."
