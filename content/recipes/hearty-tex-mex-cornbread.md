ingredients:
- "1 tablespoon avocado oil"
- "1 pound lean ground turkey"
- "1 medium yellow onion, finely chopped"
- "1 cup cornmeal"
- "1 cup buttermilk"
- "1 can (14 ounces) creamed corn"
- "1 can (7 ounces) whole kernel corn, drained"
- "3 large eggs, beaten"
- "1/2 cup pickled green chilies, chopped (optional)"
- "1 1/2 cups shredded sharp white cheddar cheese"
- "Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 360 degrees Fahrenheit. Coat bottom of a 10-inch cast iron skillet with avocado oil. Set skillet in oven while heating—listen for faint sizzle when hot enough."
- "Meanwhile, brown ground turkey in a skillet over medium-high heat with onions until no pink remains and onions soften. Season lightly with salt and pepper. Drain any excess liquid to avoid soggy cornbread layers."
- "Combine cornmeal, buttermilk, creamed corn, drained whole kernel corn, and beaten eggs in a large bowl. Mix until combined but still slightly lumpy—overmixing toughens cornbread."
- "Sprinkle a pinch of cornmeal in the hot skillet; it should immediately sizzle and pop—that's your signal the pan is ready. Pour roughly half the batter evenly across the skillet base."
- "Scatter browned turkey mixture evenly over batter. Distribute pickled green chilies on top if using. Then, spread shredded cheddar cheese over the filling. Finish layering with remaining batter, smoothing gently to seal edges."
- "Bake in the preheated oven. After about 45 minutes, check surface for deep golden color and edges pulling slightly away from skillet. Insert toothpick in center; it should come out with moist crumbs but no raw batter."
- "Remove skillet carefully—handle will be very hot. Let cornbread rest in pan 10 minutes to set and firm up for easier slicing. Run a thin spatula along edges to loosen before flipping onto serving plate if preferred."
- "Cut into wedges. Serve warm, ideally with a swipe of butter or a dollop of sour cream. Store leftovers tightly wrapped at room temperature for up to two days. Reheat gently to revive crisp crust."
