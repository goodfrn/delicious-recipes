ingredients:
- "2 tablespoons bacon grease or bacon fat substitute such as rendered duck fat"
- "1 cup diced yellow onion"
- "3/4 cup diced green bell pepper"
- "3/4 cup diced celery stalk"
- "4 cloves garlic minced fine"
- "8 ounces lean ground turkey"
- "6 ounces diced smoked Andouille sausage"
- "1/2 teaspoon Creole seasoning mix plus more to taste"
- "1 cup uncooked long grain white rice"
- "2 1/4 cups chicken broth"
- "1 small bay leaf"
- "1/2 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves"
- "Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste"
- "2 tablespoons sliced green onions for garnish"
instructions:
- "Preheat large heavy-bottomed skillet or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add bacon grease—listen for gentle sizzle, no smoking. Swirl to coat surface thoroughly."
- "Add diced onion, bell pepper, celery. Stir frequently; vegetables should soften until translucent, no caramelizing. Watch color change—about 4 minutes. Toss in minced garlic last; stir 30 seconds until fragrant, never browned."
- "Add ground turkey and Andouille sausage pieces. Sprinkle 1/2 teaspoon Creole seasoning evenly over meats. Increase heat to medium-high. Use wooden spoon to break meat apart immediately; ensure even contact against pan for browning. Stir and break apart every minute, render fat gradually. Look for edges turning golden, juices evaporating. Approximately 9 minutes here. Drain excess fat carefully into heatproof container if too greasy."
- "Stir in rice thoroughly, coating grains in fat and flavor bits stuck to pan. Toast rice grains by stirring repeatedly—look for faint nutty aroma and slight dulling of grain surface. Takes about 6 minutes. Avoid letting rice brown too dark; goal is gentle toasting, not frying."
- "Pour in chicken broth cautiously, deglazing pan bottom, loosening stuck bits. Add bay leaf and fresh thyme leaves. Add salt and black pepper sparingly at this stage to build layers gradually. Raise heat till liquid bubbles vigorously—a rolling boil."
- "Immediately reduce heat to low, cover with tight-fitting lid to trap steam. Simmer gently—steam condensation on lid signals proper cook rate. Resist temptation to stir often; rice cooks best undisturbed. After 22 minutes check gently with fork for tenderness and moisture level. Add additional Creole seasoning or adjust salt/pepper now if needed."
- "Remove bay leaf before serving. Fluff rice lightly with fork for separate grains, avoid mashing. Sprinkle sliced green onions over dish for sharp contrast and fresh flavor punch."
- "Serve hot. Great alongside crisp salad or pickled vegetables to cut richness."
