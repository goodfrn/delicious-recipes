ingredients:
- "2 tablespoons olive oil"
- "10 ounces kielbasa sausage sliced thick"
- "1 medium red onion diced"
- "3 cloves garlic minced"
- "1 1/2 cups low sodium chicken stock"
- "1 14-ounce can fire-roasted diced tomatoes undrained"
- "8 ounces penne pasta"
- "1/2 cup coconut milk full fat"
- "1 teaspoon potato starch"
- "3 cups fresh baby spinach rinsed"
- "1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese"
- "Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste"
instructions:
- "Heat oil in large skillet over medium heat. Add sliced kielbasa and diced onion. Stir occasionally, listen for gentle sizzle. Onions soften and edges start translucence – about 6 minutes. Avoid browning sausage too fast; want gentle caramelization only."
- "Add minced garlic. Stir quickly. Garlic aroma will bloom in about 45 seconds. Watch closely; burns fast and bitter. Immediately pour chicken stock, fire-roasted tomatoes with juices, and dry penne pasta straight in. Don’t rinse pasta beforehand. Stir well to combine."
- "Cover skillet and reduce heat to low-simmer. Bubbles should barely break surface. Stir every 3 minutes so pasta cooks evenly, releases starch, and for sauce to thicken gradually. Pasta tender but with slight bite usually 12-17 minutes. Pull one out and bite test. Undercooked pasta crunch feels off; mushy means overcooked."
- "While pasta cooks, whisk potato starch into coconut milk until smooth. When penne hits desired texture, quickly pour coconut milk mixture into skillet. Stir vigorously. Sauce thickens visibly in 2 to 3 minutes. Potato starch holds better than cornstarch and coconut milk adds subtle richness with less dairy heaviness."
- "Toss in fresh spinach leaves. They look bulky but will quickly shrink down. Stir continuously until wilted but still bright green, about 1 to 2 minutes. Season with salt and freshly cracked pepper now to taste. Important – salt earlier means dry noodles; later is more controlled."
- "Sprinkle shredded sharp cheddar evenly over top. Cover skillet again and remove from heat. Residual warmth melts cheese in 3-4 minutes. Don’t rush with direct heat; cheese melts smoother from steam than from direct pan contact. Serve piping hot."
