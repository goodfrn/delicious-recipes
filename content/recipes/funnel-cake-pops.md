ingredients:
- "1 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1/3 cup rice flour"
- "1/2 teaspoon salt"
- "1 teaspoon baking powder"
- "2 tablespoons granulated sugar"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1/2 cup half-and-half"
- "1/4 cup whole milk"
- "1/2 teaspoon almond extract"
- "2 tablespoons melted unsalted butter, cooled"
- "1 tablespoon vegetable oil (for batter)"
- "Shortening or canola oil, enough for 3 inches depth in pot"
- "Powdered sugar, for dusting"
instructions:
- "Fill a heavy-bottomed Dutch oven or deep frying pan with at least 3 inches of oil—canola or shortening. Attach a candy or deep-fry thermometer. Heat over medium to medium-high, letting oil come to 370°F—watch closely. 375°F too aggressive, burns outside before inside cooks through."
- "While oil warms, whisk together flours, salt, baking powder, sugar, and cinnamon in a bowl. Mixing dry ingredients first ensures even rising and keeps sugar from sinking to bottom during frying."
- "In separate bowl, beat eggs until frothy—not stiff. Add half-and-half, whole milk, almond extract, melted butter, and tablespoon of oil. The extra oil adds a slight crisp and prevents sticking in the pan."
- "Fold dry mixture into wet ingredients gently—don't overmix. Lumps are fine. Over-beating develops gluten—chewy bites, not the light puffs we want."
- "Use a ladle or tablespoon to drop batter into the hot oil. Expect immediate bubbling and puffing. Batter balls should sizzle and start browning on bottom in about 40 seconds; look for golden edges before flipping carefully with slotted spoon."
- "Flip once bottom is firm and golden, cook another 30 seconds or so until uniformly browned. The color is your doneness cue—too pale means greasy, too dark means bitter."
- "Remove cooked bites with slotted spoon, drain on paper towels. Keep batches small to avoid temperature drops. If oil temperature falls, bites soak more oil—heavy, greasy mess."
- "Once slightly cooled but still warm, dust generously with powdered sugar. Serve right away for crunch contrast against soft interior."
- "Leftovers re-crisp well in 350°F oven for 5 minutes. Avoid microwaving—turns soggy. If batter is too thick, thin lightly with milk, too runny means bites won’t hold shape in oil."
- "If a burnt smell or smoking oil occurs, lower heat immediately. Using a neutral-tasting oil with high smoke point essential. Vegetable or canola best choices."
