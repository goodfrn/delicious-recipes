ingredients:
- "2 large chicken breasts cut into 3/4 inch strips"
- "2 large eggs"
- "2 tablespoons Dijon mustard"
- "2 tablespoons oat flour"
- "1 teaspoon smoked paprika"
- "1 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "1 teaspoon onion powder"
- "3/4 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "1 cup panko breadcrumbs"
- "1 teaspoon sea salt"
- "1 teaspoon dried Italian herbs"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 425F. Line a large rimmed baking sheet with parchment. Set aside."
- "Slice chicken into strips about 3/4 inch thick. Uniformity matters to keep cook times even. Larger pieces cook unevenly—a rookie mistake. Pat dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture for better adhesion."
- "In a medium bowl, whisk eggs and Dijon mustard. Dijon adds a subtle tang and helps emulsify binder. Stir in oat flour, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, and kosher salt until mixture thickens into a loose paste. Too thin—crumbs won’t stick; too thick—coating gets gummy. Test by dipping a strip."
- "Separate shallow dish for crumb mixture. Combine panko, sea salt, and dried Italian herbs. Shake or toss to blend evenly. Herbs replace everything seasoning for a herbal note; sea salt replaces plain salt for texture boost."
- "Dredge each chicken strip first in the wet mixture, coating entirely. Press into the breadcrumb mix firmly, ensuring crumbs adhere without clumping. Tap off excess—excess leads to burned bits and uneven crust. Place on prepared baking sheet leaving space between strips for even airflow."
- "Bake for about 9 minutes. Look for golden edges starting to crisp and juices pooling slightly. Flip carefully with tongs to avoid knocking off coating. Continue baking 8-9 minutes more until the chicken registers 165F internal temp or juices run clear when pierced. Crust will be crunchy, not burnt, and chicken firm but juicy inside."
- "Remove from oven and let rest 5 minutes on wire rack or plate. Resting seals the juices internal. Avoid cutting too soon—moisture will escape and texture becomes dry."
- "Serve with mustard-based dipping sauce or aioli for extra cred."
