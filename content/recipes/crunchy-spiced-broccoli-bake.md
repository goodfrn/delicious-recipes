ingredients:
- "1 1/2 pounds broccoli florets, patted dry"
- "2 tablespoons olive oil, divided"
- "3 ounces sliced pepperoni, roughly chopped"
- "2 tablespoons finely minced shallot"
- "1 cup Japanese style panko"
- "1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika"
- "1 teaspoon coarse kosher salt, divided"
- "Freshly ground black pepper to taste"
- "1 tablespoon Dijon mustard"
instructions:
- "Start with preheating oven 430 degrees Fahrenheit. Get broccoli dry. Moisture kills crisp. Toss broccoli florets in 1 1/2 tablespoons olive oil liberally; coat every crease."
- "Spread broccoli flat on rimmed pan. Single layer means even char. Roast for about 12 minutes until edges blacken slightly, smell deepening. Listen for faint crackles."
- "While broccoli roasts, blitz pepperoni, shallot, paprika, and 1/4 teaspoon salt in small food processor. Pulse to coarse crumb size, not paste. Should be varied but no large slices."
- "Add panko and a couple twists of pepper. Pulse just to integrate. Avoid over processing to retain texture."
- "Heat remaining 1/2 tablespoon olive oil in skillet medium heat. Add panko-pepperoni mix. Stir often for 4-6 minutes until toasted golden and clumps firm. Oil should be absorbed, no slick residue."
- "In a large bowl, smear Dijon mustard around sides. Scrape broccoli out to bowl while still warm. Toss broccoli gently with mustard coating each piece; mustard stops clumping, distributes flavor evenly."
- "Add toasted crumb mixture. Toss lightly until broccoli evenly dotted with crunchy bits. Avoid breaking broccoli florets."
- "Return broccoli mixture back to baking sheet. Roast additional 8-10 minutes. Listen for popping crumb sounds. Visual: crumbs deepen in color and broccoli tender but firm. Avoid overbaking or crumb burns."
- "Remove and serve immediately. Crunch softens fast, best hot."
- "If oven heats unevenly, rotate pan halfway through each roast phase. If broccoli steams instead of roasting, dry better and ensure single layer."
