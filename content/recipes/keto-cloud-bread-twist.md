ingredients:
- "4 large eggs separated"
- "2 tablespoons erythritol (granulated sweetener)"
- "4 tablespoons mascarpone cheese softened"
- "1/4 teaspoon cream of tartar"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 290 degrees F. Line two baking sheets with parchment and lightly oil or spray them. Avoid metal trays without lining; cloud breads stick if not careful."
- "Separate eggs cleanly. Any yolk wrecks the whites. Use your hands or a clean separator. Whites must be pristine to aerate properly."
- "Whisk yolks, erythritol, and mascarpone together until no lumps. Expect a slight creaminess; mascarpone carries more fat than cream cheese—adds silkiness and richer mouthfeel."
- "Add cream of tartar to whites. Beat on high speed. Listen for a shredding, crisp sound when lifting the beaters. Whites should form firm, glossy peaks that hold shape but aren’t dry or broken."
- "Gently fold yolk mixture into whites. Cut through air bubbles vertically, then gently swirl just until combined. Overmix and you’ll lose volume—pancake flat. Under mix and streaks remain."
- "Spoon or scoop dollops—big mounds. Space them evenly, leaving room for slight spreading but not merging. Wet your spoon for cleaner scoops, less sticky mess."
- "Bake around 27 to 30 minutes. Watch for golden tops and dry edges. Tap lightly with fingertip; firm but not hard. Soft but set inside. If top browns too fast, drop temp slightly or shield with foil."
- "Cool on racks to air dry. Immediately they’re crisp like meringues. Store airtight after cooled to let them soften into that bread texture. Refrigerate or room temp is fine but texture evolves overnight."
