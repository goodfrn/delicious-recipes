ingredients:
- "1 cup unsalted butter softened"
- "3/4 cup packed light brown sugar"
- "3/4 cup granulated sugar"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1 tsp almond extract"
- "2 tbsp milk"
- "2 cups cake flour sifted"
- "2 tsp cornstarch"
- "1 tsp baking soda"
- "1 tsp fine sea salt"
- "1 1/2 cups chopped bittersweet chocolate or chocolate chunks"
instructions:
- "=== Dough prep ==="
- "In a large bowl or stand mixer with paddle, mix butter and both sugars until fluffyish about 1-2 minutes. Don’t go too far or’ll be greasy. Use softened butter, not melted — texture difference huge here."
- "Add eggs one at a time then almond extract and milk. Blend just enough to combine. Avoid foaming; too much air equals cakey cookies."
- "In another bowl sift cake flour, cornstarch, baking soda, salt. Cake flour lighter, helps chew and tenderness. Mix dry ingredients few seconds."
- "Add dry mix to wet in two parts. Fold or mix on low speed just till combined. Overmix and gluten builds — tough cookie."
- "Fold in chocolate chunks by hand. Keep chunk sizes if possible. Chips or chopped bars both work but size controls melting texture."
- "Cover bowl, refrigerate dough 1.5 to 3 hours max. Shorter chill keeps softer centers, longer solidifies fat contributing to less spread."
- "=== Baking ==="
- "Preheat oven 370°F. Line baking pan with parchment or silicone mat."
- "Scoop 1/2 cup dough balls — scalloped scoop or hands. Each ball about baseball size. Don’t pack too tight. Flatten slightly with palm to hockey puck, roughly 1/2 to 3/4 inch thick. Thickness critical to get proper chew versus crisp thin cookie."
- "Place cookies spaced 1.5 to 2 inches apart to allow gentle spreading."
- "Bake 16-18 minutes. Edges should bubble and turn golden brown. Surface dulls a bit and cracks appear on top. Smell caramelizing butter and sugar. If edges puff then soften with finger press, done. Too soft means underbaked; too firm equals overbaked crisp."
- "Remove pan, rest cookies 4-5 minutes on sheet before transferring to wire rack. Still too fragile hot, but rest firms texture while cooling."
- "=== Notes and tweaks ==="
- "Swapped almond extract adds nutty aroma; vanilla still fine for classic. Using baking soda instead of baking powder gives lighter rise but more chew - acid in brown sugar reacts to soda."
- "Cake flour substitutes all-purpose with cornstarch if needed: remove 2 tbsp AP flour per cup, add 2 tbsp cornstarch. Low gluten helps softness but not collapse. If no cake flour, sift cornstarch plus AP mix well before adding."
- "Refrigeration important. Too warm dough spreads too thin and crisp; too hard dough less melt."
- "For more crunchy edges, flatten thinner or add 1/4 cup chopped nuts/pretzels for texture; nuts add oil reducing spread, pretzels add salt crunch."
- "Overmixing dough creates tough, flat cookies. Mix slow, fold chocolates, and chill."
- "Use room temp eggs. Cold eggs chill dough unintentionally, slowing mixing."
- "If dough sticky, chill extra 15 minutes. If very firm, let soften slightly before shaping."
- "Thickness right in puck shape controls final texture: thicker equals softer middle, thinner crisp all over."
- "Listen for faint popping as edges bubble. That signals near doneness better than timer."
- "Cookie shape ideal with 1/2 cup scoop. Too small yields more crisp bite, too large risks raw center."
- "Watch cookie edges during bake. Browned edges with soft domed center signal just right."
