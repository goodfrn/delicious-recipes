ingredients:
- "1 can (20 ounces) crushed pineapple, very well drained"
- "3 tablespoons reserved pineapple juice"
- "3/4 cup granulated sugar"
- "1/3 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1 cup sharp cheddar cheese, shredded"
- "3/4 cup smoked gouda cheese, shredded"
- "1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted and browned"
- "1 1/2 cups crushed Ritz crackers"
instructions:
- "Set oven to 340 degrees Fahrenheit. Spray a 2-quart baking dish with nonstick cooking spray or lightly butter the edges. Drain pineapple thoroughly, reserving 3 tablespoons of juice. Excess moisture will wreck structure; dry is key."
- "In a large bowl, whisk sugar and flour with reserved juice. Avoid lumps by blending flour thoroughly before adding pineapple. Stir in pineapple chunks gently but evenly to avoid crushing fruit."
- "Spread pineapple mix into baking dish evenly—don’t pile up. Even surface means even bake. Scatter shredded cheddar and smoked gouda over pineapple. The combo adds sharpness and smoky creaminess; no bland cheese here."
- "In a small pan, melt butter and cook until it browns slightly, nutty aroma appears but don’t burn it. Toss crushed Ritz crackers in browned butter until all coated with shiny, golden butter. This creates crunch and amazing flavor boost."
- "Sprinkle buttered cracker crumbs evenly atop cheese layer. Press crumbs lightly with back of spoon to adhere and prevent slipping off when cutting."
- "Bake in preheated oven 25 minutes. Listen for bubbling edges, golden crumbs on top; cheese should look melted with slight browning. If top too pale, broil 1-2 minutes watching closely."
- "Remove casserole and let rest 8-12 minutes. This allows mixture to thicken and flavors to marry; cutting too soon causes runny mess. Edges will firm up, center still soft but holds shape."
- "Serve warm with fork or spoon. Piquant cheeses cut through pineapple sweetness. Crumb topping adds a wonderful buttery crunch. Leftovers reheat well, cover with foil to avoid drying."
- "If can’t find gouda, substitute with fontina or Monterey Jack for mild meltiness. No Ritz crackers? Use buttery panko crumbs or crushed buttery pretzels for unique salty contrast."
- "Common mistake: skimping on draining pineapple results in soggy bake. Always drain pineapple in fine mesh sieve or cheesecloth and press excess juice out if need be."
