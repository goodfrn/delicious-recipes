ingredients:
- "1 1/2 cups yellow cake mix"
- "1/2 cup finely ground almond flour"
- "1/3 cup packed light brown sugar"
- "1/4 cup vegetable oil"
- "1 large egg"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1/4 cup chopped pecans"
- "1/2 cup granulated sugar mixed with 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon"
instructions:
- "=== Mix Dry === Combine cake mix, almond flour, and brown sugar thoroughly in a roomy bowl. The finer almond flour creates tender crumb contrast; brown sugar deepens caramel notes."
- "=== Wet Addition === In a separate container, whisk oil, egg, and vanilla until homogenous. Pour into dry mix; stir gently but fully. Dough will be soft, almost tacky. Resist adding more flour—risk toughness."
- "=== Chill Down === Cover bowl with plastic wrap; refrigerate at least 15 minutes. Cold dough firms up sticky mess, makes rolling manageable. Essential skip to avoid melting fingers and excess stickiness."
- "=== Shape and Coat === Pinch and roll small walnut-sized balls. Roll each through cinnamon sugar blend, coat thickly. Coating adds crispy exterior, that satisfying crackle bite. No powdered sugar here; cinnamon sugar caramelizes better. If dough sticks, lightly flour fingers—not too much."
- "=== Arrange === Place spaced about two inches apart on ungreased or parchment-lined baking sheet. Cookies will spread slightly but hold shape well. Too close, they merge."
- "=== Bake === Oven at 320F (lowered from usual 325F). Bake 15 to 18 minutes, or until edges show just a hint of golden—not browned. The center remains soft but baked through. Listen for faint crackle and watch slight doming falling back."
- "=== Cool === Let rest on sheet 5 minutes before transferring to wire rack. Still hot, they’re fragile, easily misshapen. This brief rest lets structure firm up."
- "=== Storage Tip === Store airtight at room temp max 3 days or freeze. Nuts may lose crunch in fridge; keep sealed tightly to avoid moisture."
