ingredients:
- "1 package gnocchi"
- "3 tablespoons olive oil divided"
- "2 small onions finely chopped"
- "3 cloves garlic minced"
- "12 ounces spicy chorizo casing removed"
- "3 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "2 tablespoons cornstarch"
- "1 1/2 cups whole milk"
- "3/4 cup heavy cream"
- "1 teaspoon fresh thyme chopped"
- "1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan"
- "salt to taste"
- "fresh ground black pepper to taste"
instructions:
- "Cook gnocchi following package directions, drain well. Toss immediately in 1 tablespoon olive oil to keep from sticking; set aside off heat."
- "Heat 2 tablespoons olive oil in large deep skillet on medium. When oil ripples but not smoking, add chopped onions. Stir, listen for subtle sizzle. Cook until translucent, about 3 minutes. Season lightly with salt."
- "Add minced garlic, stir quickly till fragrant, less than 40 seconds. Avoid browning garlic here; burns easily, turns bitter."
- "Toss in chorizo; break apart with wooden spoon. Listen for crackle as fat renders. Cook 6 to 8 minutes until edges golden and bits crispy. Fat should release, smelling smoky and savory—not raw or greasy."
- "Slide butter in chunks around pan. Let melt quietly, no burning. Sprinkle cornstarch evenly over sausage fat. Stir briskly to form roux; paste thickens quickly but keep stirring to avoid lumps."
- "Gradually whisk in milk first, little by little. Stir constantly for 3-4 minutes. Sauce looks loose but will thicken as heat rises. Pour in cream last, stirring until sauce thickens, barely bubbling, coating back of spoon with slight resistance."
- "Season sauce with salt and pepper, taste for balance. Sprinkle thyme leaves in, toss lightly. Add Parmesan, stir till melted and glossy."
- "Fold in gnocchi gently, keep heat low. Sauce should cling, not pool watery. Warm through 1-2 minutes max. Watch for tiny bubbles on edges signaling readiness."
- "Serve immediately, hot with extra Parmesan on side. Sauces cool and thicken more but gnocchi best fresh."
