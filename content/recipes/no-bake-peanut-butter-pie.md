ingredients:
- "8 ounces cream cheese softened"
- "1 cup natural peanut butter chunky or smooth"
- "3/4 cup honey raw or mild flavor"
- "1 cup powdered sugar sifted"
- "1 1/2 cups frozen whipped topping thawed"
- "1 cup chopped peanut clusters plus extra for topping"
- "1 9-inch graham cracker pie crust pre-made or homemade"
- "1 cup heavy cream cold"
- "1/4 cup powdered sugar for whipped topping"
instructions:
- "Start by softening cream cheese till pliable but not melty. Use a stand mixer or hand mixer. Beat cream cheese alone until free of lumps, glossy and silky. This prevents grainy pockets in base."
- "Add peanut butter and honey. Mix on medium speed till fully combined. Scrape bowl sides to avoid unblended streaks of peanut butter or cream cheese. The honey replaces some sugar, reduces graininess, adds a subtle floral note."
- "Gradually add sifted powdered sugar to avoid clumps. Mix just until evenly distributed. Sugar’s crystals refine texture, vital for balance because the honey is wet."
- "Gently fold in the whipped topping. Use a rubber spatula just to combine; no vigorous stirring or you’ll deflate the air pockets. The topping is what makes this airy, not dense peanut goo."
- "Fold in chopped peanut clusters, reserve a quarter cup for finishing. Seamless distribution means every bite has crunch without overwhelming softness. These clusters hold their texture better after freezing than store-bought peanut butter cups."
- "Pour filling into pie crust. Level surface gently with spatula or back of spoon. Avoid shaking crust to prevent mess or broken crust edges."
- "Freeze pie on a flat surface for at least 8 hours. Overnight is better. You’ll know it’s ready when the filling resists finger pressure and the crust no longer feels fragile beneath."
- "For whipped topping: whip cold heavy cream with a small amount of powdered sugar until stiff peaks form. Soft peaks won’t hold shape on pie, leaving it sad and runny. Watch it carefully and stop as soon as peaks stand tall but tips don’t droop."
- "Remove pie from freezer about 10 minutes before slicing. This softens edges for cleaner cuts. Spread whipped cream on top smoothly or pipe with a star tip. Scatter reserved peanut clusters evenly."
- "Slice with a sharp serrated knife, wiping clean between cuts for neat presentation. Serve immediately or keep frozen but under 24 hours for best texture."
