ingredients:
- "1 pound fresh green beans trimmed and halved"
- "4 ounces pancetta diced (substitute cooked bacon)"
- "1 medium shallot finely sliced (sub onion)"
- "1/4 cup dry onion and mushroom soup mix"
- "1/3 cup mushroom broth (or vegetable broth)"
- "Nonstick cooking spray"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 405 degrees F. Spray an 8x8-inch baking dish lightly with nonstick spray; ensures easy cleanup and even cooking."
- "In large bowl, combine trimmed green beans, diced pancetta, and sliced shallot; the shallots add a subtle sweet punch and moisture as they soften."
- "In separate small bowl, whisk dry onion and mushroom soup mix into mushroom broth until no lumps remain. Mushrooms deepen flavor and add umami without extra fat."
- "Pour broth mixture over green beans, pancetta, shallots. Toss lightly but thoroughly with tongs or hands to coat evenly. Too much sauce pools and sogs beans; just enough to glaze."
- "Transfer everything to prepared baking dish, spread into even layer ensuring beans aren't crowded; crowding causes steaming, prevents crisp finish on pancetta and beans."
- "Bake uncovered on middle rack. Listen for bubbling, look for edges of pancetta crisping, shallots turning translucent, and green beans softening but still with slight snap. Usually 15 to 20 minutes, but check early and often."
- "If beans look pale or soggy after 18 minutes, broil 2 to 3 minutes to crisp pancetta and evaporate excess moisture. Watch carefully; broil burns fast."
- "Remove when bubbling hot, bacon fat golden, green beans are tender crisp. Let cool briefly before serving; sauce thickens as it rests."
- "Season with black pepper or red chili flakes if desired. Salt usually unnecessary due to pancetta and soup mix. Taste before adding anything."
- "Store leftovers in airtight container in fridge 3 days max. Reheat in oven rather than microwave to maintain texture."
