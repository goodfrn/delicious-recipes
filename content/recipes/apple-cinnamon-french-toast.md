ingredients:
- "3 medium firm pears peeled cored chopped"
- "1/3 cup packed light brown sugar"
- "1/4 cup water"
- "1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1 star anise pod"
- "4 large eggs"
- "2 tablespoons half-and-half cream"
- "1 teaspoon ground nutmeg"
- "3/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon"
- "8 slices thick rustic bread sourdough or French loaf"
- "Powdered sugar for dusting"
- "1/4 cup chopped toasted pecans"
- "Crème fraîche or whipped cream for serving"
- "Butter or cooking spray for frying"
instructions:
- "=== Pear Compote ==="
- "Place chopped pears, brown sugar, water, apple cider vinegar, vanilla, and star anise in a large heavy-bottomed saucepan."
- "Set over medium heat. Stir gently with a wooden spoon. Sugar dissolves, pears start to soften with a slight resistance still present after about 15-20 minutes."
- "Watch for pear edges turning translucent but not falling apart; star anise imparts aroma but don't overcook or flavor turns bitter."
- "Keep warm off heat once pears hold shape but yield to a gentle poke."
- "=== French Toast ==="
- "Turn oven on to 200°F to keep slices warm."
- "Beat eggs with half-and-half, nutmeg, and cinnamon in a shallow dish wide enough to dunk bread easily."
- "Heat griddle or heavy skillet just under 350°F; test by flick of water to sizzle softly but not spit aggressively."
- "Use butter or light spray as fat to prevent sticking and encourage eye-catching browning."
- "Dip bread slices quickly in egg bath; don't soak. Texture suffers if soggy."
- "Lay on hot pan. Cook undisturbed 3-5 minutes until golden and crisp underneath — listen for a faint crackle."
- "Flip carefully using spatula; crust forms gradually, tease edge to check color."
- "Move cooked slices to oven tray. Hold loosely covered to avoid condensation sogging crust."
- "Repeat until bread and egg mixture are done."
- "=== Assemble ==="
- "Stack slices on plates or trays."
- "Spoon warm pear compote over top, let juice drizzle down sides."
- "Dust with powdered sugar. Add chopped pecans for crunch and a cool creamy dollop on side."
- "Pecans toast extra flavor, pairs great with pear sweetness and aromatic spices."
- "Serve immediately or within 10 minutes of plating — preserves crust texture."
- "If pear compote thickens too much from standing, stir in splash of water or warm juice to loosen."
