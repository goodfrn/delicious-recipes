ingredients:
- "10 ounces pasta shells or rotini"
- "1 cup frozen corn, thawed"
- "1 1/2 cups cooked pinto beans, rinsed well"
- "1 1/4 cups grape or cherry tomatoes, quartered"
- "1 1/4 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese"
- "1/4 cup finely diced red onion"
- "1 cup diced red bell pepper"
- "1 seeded and chopped jalapeño (optional)"
- "1/2 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "1/4 teaspoon freshly cracked black pepper"
- "For dressing:"
- "1 cup plain Greek yogurt (can swap sour cream or mayo for tang or richness)"
- "1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar or fresh lime juice (tang)"
- "1 teaspoon ground cumin"
- "1 teaspoon smoked paprika"
- "1 teaspoon hot sauce (optional for heat)"
- "1/2 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "Garnish:"
- "1 medium ripe avocado, diced"
- "3 scallions, sliced thin"
- "Chopped fresh cilantro (optional)"
- "Tortilla chips or sturdy crackers for serving"
instructions:
- "Boil salted water, toss in pasta. Cook until just tender but still with bite; no mush. Rigorous stirring early to avoid clumps. Drain and run under cold water until pasta stops steaming, cool to touch. Prevent overcooking—pasta should snap, not flop."
- "Mix dressing by whisking yogurt, vinegar (or lime), cumin, smoked paprika, hot sauce if using, and garlic powder. Taste mid-whisk, adjust vinegar or hot sauce for balance between creaminess, tang, heat. Should coat, not drown."
- "Into a large bowl dump cooled pasta, corn, pinto beans, tomatoes, cheese, onion, red bell pepper, jalapeño if brave enough. Season with salt and pepper. Toss lightly, feel the texture: creamy cheese chunks, fresh crunch, soft beans."
- "Pour about three quarters of dressing into bowl. Reserve remaining for last-minute refresh. Toss. Coat everything evenly but gently. Mush the veggies less; want distinct bites. Taste again; fine-tune salt, acid. Dressing clings better chilled, flavors meld."
- "Cover bowl and refrigerate at least 45 minutes, up to 2 hours. You’ll smell the cumin deepen; pasta absorbs. Don’t skip this rest. Salad texture tightens, complex layers emerge, less one-note tang."
- "Before plating, fold in diced avocado and scallions. These fresh elements perk up the cold salad, creamy garlic richness meets sharp onion tang, contrast with soft—big forkfuls, layers of color, aromas of fresh garden."
- "Serve with crunchy tortilla chips—adds needed snap against soft pasta. Optional sprinkle of cilantro jazzes flavors further. Perfect for packed lunches, potlucks, or next-day barbecue leftovers. Keep leftover dressing handy; drizzle 15 minutes before serving to rehydrate and brighten."
- "Common pitfalls: overcook pasta making salad soggy, mash jalapeño too much (seeds mean fire—handle carefully), neglect seasoning frequently, especially after chilling. Use vinaigrette acid to cut creaminess or add smoky chipotle powder for a deep twist."
