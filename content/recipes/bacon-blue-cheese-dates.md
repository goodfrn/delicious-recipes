instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 380F. Aim high enough to crisp bacon, not smoke. Line a rimmed baking sheet with parchment—prevents sticking and simplifies cleanup."
- "Stuff each pitted date with about a teaspoon of blue cheese. Don’t overfill; cheese will melt and ooze. You want the creamy punch inside, not a mess outside."
- "Wrap each date with a half slice of bacon, overlapping edges slightly. Wrap snug but skip full rounds; too many layers means uneven cooking and uncooked fat pockets."
- "Thread two bacon-wrapped dates onto a toothpick. This secures the wrap, prevents bacon unraveling while baking. Skip this step? Bacon slips off, a total pain."
- "Arrange toothpicked dates spaced apart on the sheet. Crowding traps steam and softens bacon; airflow crisps better."
- "Bake for about 12 minutes, listen for the gentle sizzle and watch bacon edges darken to golden brown with crisp hints. Flip dates; bake another 15 minutes or until bacon crackles and browns evenly."
- "Rest on the sheet 5 minutes after baking. Handles hot cheese bubbling inside; cheese firms up slightly, easier serving and less molten threat."
- "Plate, drizzle with honey for counterbalance—sugary notes cut sharp cheese and smoky pork. Sprinkle with Maldon or flaky salt for bursts of crunch and depth."
- "Variations: swap blue cheese for creamy goat cheese for milder tang; use pancetta for leaner pork layer; add finely chopped almonds inside dates for crunch contrast."
- "Common glitches: bacon fatty or thick? Increase bake time; listen for popping fat. Cheese overflows too much? Less filling or firmer cheese helps. Dates too dry? Soften in warm water 10 minutes beforehand to avoid tough bite."
