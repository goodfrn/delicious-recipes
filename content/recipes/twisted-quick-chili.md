ingredients:
- "1 1/2 pounds ground pork"
- "1 medium leek thinly sliced"
- "1 cup green bell pepper diced"
- "10 ounces undiluted condensed tomato puree"
- "1 1/2 cups cooked black beans drained"
- "2 tablespoons chili powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika"
- "1 teaspoon ground cumin"
- "1 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper"
- "1/2 teaspoon garlic powder"
instructions:
- "=== Browning and Base ==="
- "Pump heat to medium-high before dumping pork, leek, and green pepper into Dutch oven. Listen for that steady sizzle, not frantic spit. Stir frequently enough to break meat apart, flip and move veggies around so nothing sticks. Pork should brown evenly with browned bits sticking to pan - where flavor lives. Leek should soften and start to caramelize visibly, little browning edges. Drain off excess fat cautiously but leave some moisture-cling for aroma and texture."
- "=== Building the Chili ==="
- "Pour in undiluted condensed tomato puree straight from the can for concentrated tang, no watering down here. Add beans—use black for creamier feel; soak and cook thoroughly if canned, rinse well to control saltiness. Sprinkle chili powder, smoked paprika, and cumin over the mixture. These three spice pillars bring depth and smoke alongside chili’s heat. Salt and pepper to taste. Garlic powder goes last for subtle background kick. Stir well; the pot should thicken slowly with vibrant reddish hues deepening."
- "=== Simmer and Meld ==="
- "Cut heat to low. Cover but leave lid slightly askew or use a loose tent to allow steam escape without drying out. Stir every 15 minutes or so, scrape browned bits off bottom to avoid bitter burn. Cook for approx 1 hour but note aroma bloom and texture telling when flavors are married enough and meat tender. Thick chili that stains the spoon is the goal, not runny slop."
- "=== Serving ==="
- "Serve hot. Grated sharp cheddar or smoky gouda on top cools heat; melts from residual warmth, adding richness and smooth mouthfeel. Garnish with chopped green onions or a dollop of sour cream if you like. Bread on the side to mop up thick sauce."
- "===== Tips and Troubleshooting ====="
- "Pork substitution ensures moist texture; beef can dry faster. Leeks give sweeter background compared to onions - caramelize well but don’t burn. Tomato puree shock - avoid watering down canned tomatoes; thicker base means less simmer time. If chili tastes flat, add a squeeze of lime or a splash of vinegar at the end to brighten. For quicker version, use pre-cooked beans, reduce simmer to 45 minutes but watch for texture and flavor meld. Too thick? Add small splashes of broth or water stepwise. Overly salty? More tomato or splash of cream counters that salty bite."
