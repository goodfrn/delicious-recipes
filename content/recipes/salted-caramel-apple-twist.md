ingredients:
- "6 medium apples, washed and completely dried"
- "1 package (14 oz) dark brown caramel pieces"
- "1/2 cup full fat coconut milk"
- "1 teaspoon almond extract"
- "1/2 teaspoon flaky sea salt, plus extra for topping"
- "Crushed pecans or cinnamon sugar for coating"
- "Butter for greasing parchment or silicone mat"
- "Optional: 1/4 cup dark chocolate chips for drizzling"
- "6 wooden sticks (caramel apple sticks or popsicle sticks)"
instructions:
- "Trim stems from apples. Dry well to remove waxy layer, better caramel cling. Spear sticks firmly at top center. Chill apples minimum 2 hours, ideally 3; cold apples hold caramel better, reduce sliding risk."
- "Line baking sheet with parchment or silicone mat. Generously rub with softened butter; traps air beneath and prevents caramel adhesion. No butter? Light vegetable oil smear alternative."
- "Place dark brown caramel chunks, coconut milk, and almond extract into small saucepan over medium-low heat. Stir gently but constantly. Caramel melts unevenly; looking for glossy, nearly fluid consistency. Avoid boiling; add up to tablespoon water if mixture stiffens or separates."
- "Once fully melted and smooth, stir in flaky sea salt. Remove saucepan from heat. Salt draws flavor forward and cuts cloying sweetness."
- "Load chilled apples on stick, dip approx 3/4 way into caramel; no full submerge so the top remains grip-friendly. Tap apple firmly once against pan edge to shed excess caramel. Immediately roll into your choice of crushed pecans or cinnamon sugar. If skipping toppings, just sit on greased sheet."
- "If drizzling chocolate, wait 45-60 minutes for caramel to firm. Melting chocolate chips on low heat or microwave pulses with stirring until just melted; drizzle with fork or spoon in thin ribbons."
- "Allow apples to set on counter at room temp until caramel is taut and slightly tacky but solid. Avoid refrigeration; condensation can cause caramel to weep or become sticky."
- "Serving tip: Let sit 10 minutes if pulled from fridge before eating. Rich, nutty, salty-sweet harmony with each bite."
- "Cleanup hint: Warm water soak loosens sticky caramel residue on pans."
