ingredients:
- "2¼ cups bittersweet chocolate chunks"
- "8 ounces mini marshmallows"
- "graham crackers or cinnamon cookies for dipping"
- "fresh strawberries or dried pineapple slices"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 340°F. Use an ovenproof 9-inch heavy skillet, cast iron preferred for even heat retention."
- "Scatter chocolate chunks evenly across the skillet bottom. No crowded patches; ensure air can circulate around pieces for uniform melt."
- "Top chocolate with mini marshmallows pressed close but not squished. Tight layering prevents marshmallow blowouts—important."
- "Bake 10–13 minutes. Watch marshmallows carefully. When puffed, slightly blistered, and glowing golden brown at edges, it’s ready. Hearing faint crackle from browning marshmallows? Spot on."
- "Remove skillet, let it rest 3–4 minutes. Chocolate should be molten underneath but marshmallows will set slightly on top, creating that mix of gooey and toasted."
- "Serve warm straight from the pan paired with cinnamon cookies or graham crackers, plus strawberries or dried pineapple for acid balance. Scoop or stab — either works."
- "Leftover tip: refrigerate, then gently reheat in a low oven; resist nuking or chocolate graininess emerges."
