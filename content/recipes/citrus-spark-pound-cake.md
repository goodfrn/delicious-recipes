ingredients:
- "1 1/3 cups unsalted butter softened"
- "1/4 cup vegetable oil"
- "2 1/2 cups granulated sugar"
- "5 large eggs room temperature"
- "2 2/3 cups all-purpose flour sifted"
- "1/2 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "3/4 teaspoon baking soda"
- "3/4 cup lemon-lime soda substitute (club soda mixed with 1 teaspoon lemon-lime extract)"
- "1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice"
- "1 tablespoon fresh lime juice"
- "3 cups powdered sugar sifted"
- "2 tablespoons lemon or lime juice"
- "1/4 cup lemon-lime soda substitute"
- "1/2 cup chopped Maraschino cherries drained"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 320°F. Grease and flour a 10-inch bundt pan thoroughly; excess sticking ruins the detail. Set aside."
- "In a stand mixer bowl fitted with paddle or large mixing bowl with electric mixer, cream butter and oil for 4-5 minutes. Stop when mixture looks pale, fluffy, not melting. This traps air for lift."
- "Add sugar in two or three additions, beating fully between each until light and fluffy. Scrape sides to avoid grainy sugar hiding at bottom."
- "Add eggs one at a time. Beat just until combined—overmixing breaks air pockets and causes dense crumb."
- "In a separate bowl sift or whisk flour, salt, and baking soda together three times. A finer sift results in better crumb."
- "Combine soda substitute, lemon juice, and lime juice in a small bowl. Mixing soda and acids separately activates soda fully. Do it right before adding to batter."
- "Alternate adding flour mixture and citrus-soda mix to butter mixture. Start and end with flour. Use low speed or fold by hand to avoid gluten development. Batter should feel light but thick, not runny."
- "Pour batter evenly into prepared bundt pan. Give pan a few light taps on counter to settle batter and release trapped air bubbles."
- "Bake uncovered for 1 hour 15 minutes. Stove temps vary; older ovens run hot or cold. Start watching at 1 hour. Surface should be golden with tiny cracks, not deep fissures."
- "Use toothpick test—insert in deepest part, comes out with few moist crumbs but not wet batter. Springs back with gentle press."
- "Remove cake from oven. Let rest in pan 12-15 minutes. Warm batter pulls away from pan sides at this stage, easing unmolding without breakage."
- "Invert on wire rack. If stuck, gently run thin spatula or butter knife around inside lip, warm pan slightly over low burner or in warm oven for a minute."
- "Cool fully before glazing. Warm glaze melts too much and runs off."
- "Whisk powdered sugar with lemon or lime juice and soda substitute. Aim for thick but pourable glaze that clings to cake."
- "Drizzle over cake while glaze is slightly tacky—glaze sets but is still sticky."
- "Press chopped Maraschino cherries gently onto glaze. Their juice stains glaze, adding character. Let sit to set completely."
- "Store loosely covered at room temp for 2 days. Refrigerate beyond that, wrapped to prevent drying out."
