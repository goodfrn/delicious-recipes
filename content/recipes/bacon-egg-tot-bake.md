ingredients:
- "1 package tater tots about 32 oz"
- "10 large eggs room temperature"
- "1 cup heavy cream"
- "6 oz pancetta diced small"
- "1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese divided"
- "1/2 cup finely diced red bell pepper"
- "1/2 cup chopped fresh chives divided"
- "1 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper"
- "Nonstick cooking spray"
- "Optional garnish salsa or sour cream"
instructions:
- "==="
- "Cook diced pancetta in a cold skillet over medium heat. It renders fat slowly, crisping up evenly without burning. Listen for gentle crackles and watch fat pool at edges. Once crisp and brown, transfer onto paper towels to drain excess grease. Crumble roughly, set aside."
- "Preheat oven to 345°F. Spray a 9x13-inch pan lightly with nonstick spray. Don’t skip the spray; tots will stick otherwise and ruin the crust. Spread tater tots evenly in a single layer on the bottom. They form a sturdy base; avoid crowding them or layering thick."
- "In a large bowl, whisk eggs thoroughly with heavy cream until uniform and glossy. Season with salt and pepper. Add half the pancetta, half the chives, diced bell pepper, and half the cheese. Stir gently but completely so veggies are dispersed, eggs tinted slightly orange from cheddar."
- "Pour the egg mixture over the tater tots. Use an offset spatula to spread evenly if needed. Gently tap pan on counter to settle the mixture, release air bubbles. Place uncovered in oven. Bake for roughly 40 minutes or until the edges puff slightly and the center jiggles just minutely when nudged. The eggs should be set but still moist to the touch."
- "Remove from oven. Sprinkle remaining pancetta, chives, and cheese over top. Return to heat for 4-6 minutes or until the cheese melts into a bubbly golden crust. Watch closely so it doesn’t scorch. Pull when the cheese is glossy and slightly browned at edges."
- "Serve warm. A scoop of salsa or dollop of sour cream cuts richness well. If leftover, cool completely before refrigerating. Reheat gently covered to avoid dry, rubbery eggs."
