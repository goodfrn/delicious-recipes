ingredients:
- "1.5 pounds red potatoes quartered"
- "1 medium sweet onion sliced"
- "2 tablespoons grainy mustard (sub yellow or whole grain mustard)"
- "3 tablespoons browned butter (or regular melted butter)"
- "2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil"
- "1 tablespoon fresh lime juice (sub lemon juice)"
- "1 teaspoon lime zest (or lemon zest)"
- "1 teaspoon dried thyme (replace with rosemary if unavailable)"
- "1 teaspoon kosher salt (fine sea salt works)"
- "1 teaspoon freshly cracked black pepper"
- "1 clove garlic finely minced (added twist)"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 435 degrees F. Line a large rimmed baking pan with foil. The foil helps with cleanup but avoid wrapping potatoes tightly—air circulation is key."
- "In a large bowl whisk grainy mustard, browned butter, olive oil, lime juice and zest, thyme, salt, pepper, and minced garlic together. Browned butter adds nuttiness; don’t rush this step or use cold butter—it must be melted and warm for emulsification."
- "Drop quartered potatoes and sliced onions into the bowl. Toss thoroughly to coat evenly. Avoid dripping excess sauce as it can pool and steam the potatoes; you want roasting, not soggy bits."
- "Using a slotted spoon, lift vegetables from the bowl, let excess sauce drip off back into the bowl. Spread potatoes and onions single layer on the pan. No crowding—crisp edges happen when air flows freely around each piece."
- "Start roasting for about 20 minutes until you hear light crackling and see edges turning golden. The smell shifts from raw starch to lightly toasted—this signals partial caramelization."
- "Remove pan. Use a spatula to gently flip and rotate pieces for even browning. Drizzle reserved sauce lightly back over veggies; discard what remains. Over-saucing impedes crispness. The timing here matters—don’t wait too long; oven cools quickly causing soggy texture."
- "Roast another 20–25 minutes until potatoes fork tender: soft inside but holding shape. The skin should feel crisp, almost like peel of roasted chestnuts. You want resistance but no crunch inside."
- "For the finishing touch, switch oven to high broil. Move pan close to broiler element. Watch closely—1 to 3 minutes max to avoid burning. Crispy, bubbly char spots appear, and aroma deepens into toasted sweet onion and buttery lime notes."
- "Pull pan out. Cool slightly—hot potatoes finish firming while cooling. Taste balance with indicated salt and acidity. Optional sprinkle fresh herbs or a dust of smoked paprika for bold finish."
- "Timing flexible. Smaller potatoes roast faster, bigger chunks need more time. Use these sensory cues over strict minutes. Use a fork test liberally. Too mushy? Cut even pieces next time. Too raw? Increase temp, reduce stirring."
