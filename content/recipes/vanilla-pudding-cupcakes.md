ingredients:
- "1 box yellow cake mix"
- "1 small package instant vanilla pudding mix (4 oz changed to 3 oz to adjust moisture)"
- "3 large eggs (originally 4, reduced for texture)"
- "1/3 cup vegetable oil (switched from 1/2 cup to reduce greasiness)"
- "3/4 cup whole milk instead of 1 cup milk"
- "1/2 cup sour cream (added for moisture, replaced some oil)"
- "For frosting: 1 cup unsalted butter softened (sub salted if needed)"
- "4 cups powdered sugar (decreased slightly for less sweetness)"
- "1 1/2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract (upgrade from imitation vanilla)"
- "Sprinkles for topping optional but recommended"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 350F. Line 24 muffin tins with cupcake liners."
- "Combine cake mix and instant pudding in a large bowl; whisk until fully blended—no lumps. Pudding mix thickens batter, crucial for moist crumb; don’t skip."
- "In a separate bowl crack in eggs, add oil, milk, and sour cream. Whisk until smooth but don’t overmix or bubbles form—it ruins texture."
- "Pour wet ingredients into dry mixture. Fold gently with spatula just until homogenous. Overworking leads to tough cupcakes."
- "Divide batter evenly among cupcake liners—about halfway full for rise without spillover. Tap tray lightly on counter to settle batter and release air bubbles."
- "Bake 18-22 minutes depending on your oven. Check doneness visually—tops spring back when pressed lightly, toothpick inserted in center comes out clean or with moist crumbs, not wet batter."
- "Remove from oven; let cool in pan 5 minutes before transferring to wire rack. Warm cupcakes bruise easily; handle gently to keep shape intact."
- "While cooling, prepare frosting. Beat room temp butter on low speed to soften. Add powdered sugar gradually to avoid sugar cloud. Mix vanilla in."
- "Increase speed to high and whip 40 seconds to incorporate air. Frosting will lighten in color and fluff up, signaling readiness."
- "Frost cooled cupcakes using a spatula or piping bag. If frosting too thick, add small splash milk for spreadability. Too thin, add more powdered sugar."
- "Sprinkle tops for texture and color pop. Store baked goods loosely covered at room temp up to 2 days or freeze unfrosted for longer shelf life."
