ingredients:
- "4 cups fresh baby spinach, roughly chopped"
- "2 cups cremini mushrooms, sliced thin"
- "8 large eggs"
- "1 1/4 cups shredded mozzarella cheese"
- "1/2 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "1/4 teaspoon black pepper"
- "1 tablespoon olive oil or butter"
- "2 tablespoons chopped scallions for garnish"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 340F. Spray a 12-cup muffin tin lightly with oil; ensures easy release without paper liners."
- "Warm skillet over medium heat. Add the olive oil and swirl to coat; hot but not smoking."
- "Toss in mushrooms. Stir often. Listen for gentle sputtering sounds. Watch edges brown slightly — signals flavor development without drying out."
- "Add chopped spinach once mushrooms are softened and reduced about 30%. Spinach wilts quickly; stir till leaves darken and shrink but still moist. Total cook time around 4 minutes; don’t let spinach turn mushy. Drain immediately on paper towels to remove excess liquid."
- "In a mixing bowl, crack eggs and whisk vigorously until yolks and whites blended frothy. Fold in cheese and drained veggie mix. Salt and pepper here—not before to avoid wilted texture or water release during cooking."
- "Divide mixture evenly into muffin cups. Do not overfill; fill about three-quarters up to allow eggs to puff without overflow."
- "Bake 15 to 20 minutes. Watch for firm edges and slight jiggle in the center when shaken gently. Overbake leads to rubbery texture; underbake leaves raw pockets. Visual cues matter higher than exact timer."
- "Remove from oven and let cups cool 5 minutes in tin for easier removal. Garnish with chopped scallions for freshness and subtle bite."
- "Serve warm or cool completely before freezing. Label properly. Reheat covered with foil to restore moisture and prevent drying."
