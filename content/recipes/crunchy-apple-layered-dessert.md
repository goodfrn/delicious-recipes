ingredients:
- "2 cups graham cracker crumbs"
- "1 cup walnuts chopped fine"
- "7 tablespoons unsalted butter melted"
- "1/3 cup packed brown sugar"
- "8 ounces cream cheese softened"
- "1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract"
- "1 cup Greek yogurt plain full-fat"
- "2 tablespoons honey"
- "1/2 cup powdered sugar"
- "1 can 20 ounces apple pie filling"
- "1/4 cup chopped walnuts for topping"
instructions:
- "=== Crust Preparation ==="
- "Heat oven to 345F. Noise of butter melting is key; listen for no sizzling to avoid burning."
- "Pulse graham crackers and walnuts in food processor till fine but not powder; slight texture adds interest."
- "Add melted butter and brown sugar. Pulse a few times, just enough to bind; avoid overprocessing or crust turns greasy."
- "Press firmly and evenly into 9x13 pan; test with fingers for compactness. Should hold together when pressed but not mushy."
- "Bake 13 minutes until edges turn golden and aroma of nuts fills kitchen. Butter smell subtle, not burned. Let cool completely on wire rack. Touch test on edges for firmness before next layer."
- "=== Cream Layer ==="
- "Beat cream cheese until soft and lighter in color. Add vanilla, powdered sugar, half the honeyed yogurt; mix til smooth but don’t overbeat — can get too runny."
- "Spread thickly over cooled crust. Should be opaque and creamy, no streaks of crust showing. Use offset spatula for even surface; rough spots cause uneven apple topping."
- "=== Apple Layer ==="
- "Spoon apple filling evenly over cream cheese. Avoid puddles; keep filling thick and not runny — excess juice will cause soggy crust."
- "Use spatula to gently smooth, press lightly so filling adheres but keep apple chunks intact for texture bursts."
- "=== Final Topping ==="
- "Whip remaining Greek yogurt with honey by hand until fluffy. Spread in gentle strokes atop apple layer. Should form soft peaks but remain spreadable, no sharp ridges."
- "Cover tightly with plastic wrap, chill at least 3.5 hours. Overnight better if possible; flavors meld, topping firms without freezing."
- "=== Before Serving ==="
- "Sprinkle remaining chopped walnuts right before slicing. Adds crunch and visual contrast. If nuts get soggy, unappetizing."
- "Cut with sharp knife, wipe blade between slices to keep layers clean. Serve cold but not ice cold; flavors brighten as temperature rises slightly."
