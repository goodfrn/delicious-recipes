ingredients:
- "3 cups rice Chex cereal"
- "3 cups corn Chex cereal"
- "1 cup wasabi peas (swap for Corn nuts)"
- "2 cups cheddar Cheez-Its"
- "2 cups thin pita crisps (replace bagel chips)"
- "1 1/2 cups mixed nuts (peanuts, almonds)"
- "2 cups pretzel twists"
- "1 tablespoon ranch dressing mix"
- "2 teaspoons dried dill weed"
- "1 1/2 teaspoons garlic salt"
- "3 tablespoons vegetable oil"
- "1/3 cup pickle juice (from dill pickles)"
instructions:
- "Set oven to 240°F, a bit lower than usual for slow drying. Line two sheet pans fully with parchment paper to catch drips and ease cleanup. Avoid foil; seasoning sticks."
- "Grab an oversized mixing bowl. Toss cereals, wasabi peas, Cheez-Its, pita crisps, nuts, and pretzels until well combined. Roughly mix but keep pieces intact."
- "In a small bowl, whisk ranch seasoning with dill and garlic salt. Pour in oil and pickle juice, whisk vigorously until emulsified. Liquid looks cloudy but aromatic with herb and tang."
- "Drizzle seasoning evenly over dry mix. Stir constantly. Distribute liquids thoroughly to coat most bits but avoid sogginess. Stir five minutes if needed. Use a large wooden spoon or hands for better control."
- "Scrape mix onto prepared pans. Spread out as thin and even as you can. Separate clumps to allow air and heat circulation, so every piece crisps and browns. Can divide over three pans for thinner layers."
- "Bake at 240°F for 35 to 40 minutes. Stir every 12-15 minutes to flip pieces. Listen for light crackle sounds as moisture leaves. Watch colors carefully takes precedence over clock. Look for edges browning golden, especially nuts and pretzels."
- "When mix is dry to touch but not scorched, pull pans out. Let cool completely on racks. Cooling solidifies crunch. Any residual steam disappears, flavor concentrates."
- "Store cooled mix airtight away from moisture. Going stale or soggy means your last batch was underbaked or sealed hot."
