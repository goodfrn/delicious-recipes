ingredients:
- "10-11 inch cast-iron skillet"
- "3 tbsp vegetable oil or lard for greasing"
- "2 tbsp all-purpose flour plus 2 tbsp whole wheat flour pastry type"
- "3/8 cup unsweetened cocoa powder (use Dutch-processed if available)"
- "¾ cup granulated sugar"
- "1 tsp baking soda"
- "1 cup whole milk"
- "5 tbsp melted coconut oil or unsalted butter"
- "2 tsp vanilla extract"
- "1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips"
- "½ cup packed light brown sugar mixed with 1 tsp ground cinnamon"
- "1 cup near-boiling water"
- "Caramel sauce to drizzle (optional)"
instructions:
- "=== Prepare fire and skillet ==="
- "Let campfire burn down to glowing coals, no flames licking. Place a sturdy grate over the coals for steady heat; keeps skillet from hotspots and burning underside."
- "Grease skillet heavily with vegetable oil or lard. Don’t skimp here or batter sticks. Dust skillet with combined flours, turning to coat every groove. Tap out excess."
- "=== Mix dry and wet ==="
- "In a bowl, whisk together all-purpose and whole wheat flour, cocoa powder, sugar, and baking soda. Whole wheat adds nuttiness and helps crumb structure—don’t overload or cake gets heavy."
- "In separate vessel, beat milk, melted coconut oil, and vanilla. Coconut oil adds complexity, butter is a safe swap if unavailable."
- "=== Combine and layer ==="
- "Fold wet into dry until no flour lumps remain—don’t overmix or gluten stiffens, crumb toughens."
- "Pour batter into skillet, smooth top gently."
- "Scatter chocolate chips evenly over batter. Chunky pockets of heat-melted chocolate, essential for texture contrast."
- "In small bowl, stir brown sugar and cinnamon. Sprinkle evenly on top—cinnamon cuts sweetness, adds warmth."
- "Pour boiling water slowly over brown sugar layer, creating a thin wet blanket that will transform into gooey sauce beneath cake during cooking."
- "=== Cook over fire ==="
- "Place skillet on grate directly over coals. Cover if you can, else ambient heat will still cook but slows the process."
- "Check after 50 minutes. Cake top should feel firm but springy; poke lightly with finger. Bottom sizzling and bubbling signals molten layer forming beneath. If top still wet, keep an eye in five-minute increments."
- "Remove when top dry to touch, glossy small bubbles on bottom visible when lifted slightly. Avoid overcooking or edges burn bitter."
- "=== Serve ==="
- "Let cake rest 10 minutes to settle. Drizzle with caramel sauce or sprinkle flaky sea salt for contrast."
- "Slice with spatula, serve warm. Pleasantly dense with pockets of molten chocolate and caramel sauce underneath."
