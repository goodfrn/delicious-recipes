ingredients:
- "1.5 pounds ground turkey"
- "1.5 tablespoons olive oil"
- "1 medium yellow onion diced"
- "3 cloves garlic minced"
- "2 tablespoons taco seasoning mix"
- "1 cup chunky medium-spiced salsa"
- "1 cup condensed cream of mushroom soup"
- "1.5 cups canned black beans rinsed and drained"
- "1 cup baked crunchy tortilla strips"
- "1.5 cups shredded monterey jack cheese"
instructions:
- "Heat large nonstick skillet over medium heat. Drop olive oil. Toss in diced onion. Sizzle a minute or two till translucent edges start to curl; onions should faintly sweeten without browning too much."
- "Add garlic, stir, cook 30 seconds more till fragrant but not burnt—smoke kills flavor fast here. Slide in ground turkey in chunks. Press down, resist stirring too often. Brown forms on edges. Look for meat color changing from pink to pale tan. When mostly cooked, break apart gently."
- "Will release juices. Watch carefully. Once no pink visible, tilt pan and drain excess liquid out. Steaming juices dilute seasoning and texture. Keep skillet fairly dry for best flavor punch."
- "Stir in taco seasoning and salsa immediately. Notice salsa thickens pan sauce, shifting from watery to sticky. Next, dollop in cream of mushroom soup instead of tomato soup for earthier depth. Mix briskly to combine and coat all meat evenly."
- "Drain and rinse black beans well to reduce canned saltiness and preserve bean texture. Fold gently into skillet. Beans warm through in the sauce, never mashed or mushy. Let mixture bubble up once, then drop heat to low."
- "Simmer uncovered about 7 to 12 minutes. Listen for quiet gentle bubbling—vigorous boil dries out mixture too fast. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking, scrape bottom clean to avoid bitter burnt bits."
- "While simmering, prep baked tortilla strips. Homemade preferred: cut corn tortillas into thin strips, brush lightly with oil, bake 10 to 12 minutes at 400F until crisp but not overbrowned. Store-bought works fine but loses texture if soggy folded in too early."
- "Right before plating, stir in half the tortilla strips. They soften slightly in sauce but maintain some crunch contrast."
- "Pile skillet servings on plates. Sprinkle generous shredded monterey jack cheese over each portion. Cheese melts from residual heat forming rich creamy pockets."
- "Serve extra tortilla strips on side for people to scoop or wrap. Lime wedges optional but fresh lime juice brightens flavors drastically. Cilantro chopped on top adds a fresh herb burst if handy."
- "Cleanup tip: after draining juices save a bit to deglaze pan if you want intensified sauce—just splash and scrape browned bits before adding rest of ingredients."
