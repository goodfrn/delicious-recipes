ingredients:
- "1 cup roasted red peppers, drained"
- "1/3 cup plain Greek yogurt (substitute for hummus)"
- "3 cups chicken stock or broth"
- "1/3 cup long grain or jasmine rice (can swap with quinoa or bulgur)"
- "1 cup cooked shredded chicken (rotisserie or leftovers work fine)"
- "2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley"
- "1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to heat preference)"
- "Salt to taste (usually about 1/2 teaspoon)"
- "1/4 cup sliced scallions (green parts only)"
- "1/3 cup shredded mozzarella cheese"
- "Olive oil or butter (optional finishing drizzle)"
- "Crusty bread for serving"
instructions:
- "Pulse roasted red peppers and Greek yogurt in small food processor until very finely blended and creamy. No processor: mince finely or blitz vigorously with stick blender in pan until almost sauce-like but still with some small texture left for body. Avoid overblending which can water down."
- "Pour pepper-yogurt mix into medium saucepan. Stir in chicken broth, rice, shredded chicken, parsley, cayenne, and salt."
- "Heat slowly stirring occasionally. Watch for bubbles forming gently around sides and then small simmer spots in center. As soon as simmer starts, cut heat immediately to avoid overcooking rice and curdling yogurt base."
- "Give a quick stir to loosen rice if sticking. Scoop into bowls while hot."
- "Sprinkle scallions and mozzarella over each portion. Cheese will melt warming from soup heat. Add a drizzle of olive oil or pat of butter if wanting richer mouthfeel."
- "Serve with warm crusty bread to mop up the last bits. Bread texture contrasts velvety soup well."
