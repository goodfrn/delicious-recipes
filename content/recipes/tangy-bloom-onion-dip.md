ingredients:
- "1 cup mayonnaise"
- "1/3 cup smoky chipotle sauce (substitute tomato ketchup plus 1 tsp smoked paprika)"
- "1 tbsp Dijon mustard (use yellow if unavailable)"
- "1/2 tsp onion powder"
- "1/2 tsp garlic powder"
- "1 tsp Worcestershire sauce"
- "1/4 tsp black pepper"
- "3-4 dashes hot sauce (adjust for heat tolerance)"
- "1 tbsp fresh lime juice (substitute white vinegar if needed)"
- "1/4 tsp smoked paprika (extra depth and color)"
instructions:
- "Start by measuring mayo into a medium bowl. Use a spatula to scrape every bit out—this is your creamy base."
- "Next add chipotle sauce and Dijon mustard. Stir vigorously to blend the thick textures into one homogenous mixture. Visual cue: no streaks of mustard or sauce visible—smooth and uniform."
- "Sprinkle in onion and garlic powders. These dry aromatics punch up savory notes; don't eyeball—accurate measurements matter. Fresh powders work best; old ones turn dull and flat."
- "Pour in Worcestershire sauce. It’s the umami anchor here—adds depth that cuts through the mayo’s richness."
- "Season with black pepper and a few dashes of hot sauce for heat. Hot sauce brand matters—taste and adjust slowly to hit the right balance."
- "Add fresh lime juice last. Citrus brightens flavor and balances fat. Watch for curdling; stir gently but thoroughly to prevent separation."
- "Finish with a dusting of smoked paprika for earthy complexity and a subtle red tint. Optional but worth it."
- "Cover and refrigerate at least 10 minutes to let flavors marry. Better if left 20–30 minutes. Sauce thickens as it chills—ideal dipping consistency should coat the back of a spoon smoothly without running off."
- "Taste before serving. Adjust salt or add more hot sauce if it falls flat after chilling. Re-whip briefly if oils separate during refrigeration."
- "Store leftover dip covered tightly in fridge up to one week. If it separates, a quick stir brings it back."
- "For no-mayo option, blend Greek yogurt with a little olive oil. Texture differs but still tasty for a lighter, tangy alternative."
