- "1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1/2 cup oat flour"
- "2 1/2 teaspoons baking powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "2 tablespoons all-purpose flour (for berries)"
- "1 1/4 sticks unsalted butter melted"
- "1/2 cup coconut sugar"
- "1/3 cup whole milk"
- "1 large egg"
- "1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract"
- "1 cup diced fresh strawberries"
- "Zest of 1 lemon"
- ""
- "=== Cheesecake Filling ==="
- "8 ounces cream cheese softened"
- "1/4 cup Greek yogurt"
- "1 large egg"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1/4 cup granulated sugar"
- "1 tablespoon all-purpose flour"
- ""
- "=== Streusel Topping ==="
- "5 tablespoons cold unsalted butter grated"
- "1/4 cup dark brown sugar packed"
- "2 tablespoons granulated sugar"
- "1/3 cup all-purpose flour"
- "Pinch of fine sea salt"
instructions:
- "=== Muffin Batter ==="
- "Preheat oven to 335°F or 340°F, depending on your oven quirks. Line muffin trays with paper liners; set aside."
- "Sift together 1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour, oat flour, baking powder, and salt. Important to combine dry ingredients thoroughly; this ensures even rise without pockets of baking powder."
- "Toss diced strawberries with 2 tablespoons flour. Coating stops berries from sinking and prevents blueish batter tinge from frozen (use fresh if possible). Add lemon zest here for a bright hit."
- "In mixing bowl, whisk melted butter with coconut sugar till fully mixed. Coconut sugar adds subtle molasses flavor, richer than plain white sugar."
- "Add milk, egg, and vanilla; blend well but avoid aeration which breaks crumb balance."
- "Gradually fold dry ingredients in using a spatula, barely mixing. Small lumps remain okay. Overworking gluten leads to dense muffins."
- "Finally fold in strawberries carefully by hand; keep batter spotty with bits of berry but no smeared pink. Batter will be thick and not perfectly smooth."
- ""
- "=== Cheesecake Filling ==="
- "Beat cream cheese and Greek yogurt till no lumps remain — don’t overmix or introduce too much air. Smooth texture is key."
- "Add egg and vanilla; mix briefly just to combine."
- "Incorporate sugar and flour; beat for about 2 minutes until filling thickens slightly, holds together but still spreadable."
- ""
- "=== Streusel Topping ==="
- "Using a box grater, cold butter grated into a medium bowl provides uneven, chunky texture to streusel."
- "Work in brown sugar, granulated sugar, flour, and salt with fingertips or two knives cut-in method until sandy and clumpy."
- "Stash in fridge if assembling later to keep butter cold, which equals crunch after baking."
- ""
- "=== Assembly and Baking ==="
- "Spoon a heaping tablespoon muffin batter into each liner — try judging by eye, not exact scoops; batter density matters."
- "Add slightly less than 1 tablespoon cheesecake filling — if it peaks, push down gently. Spread filling but avoid mingling too much with batter to keep layers distinct."
- "Top with more muffin batter, filling cups about two-thirds full. Too full? Muffins crack or spill over, too little — dry crumb. Find balance."
- "Sprinkle streusel evenly, press lightly so it adheres; too heavy — it’ll slide off when baking, too light and it won’t provide that audible crunch."
- "Bake 27 to 30 minutes. Look for edges turning golden, tops springy but not wet. Watch color; cookie sheet lining affects heat distribution."
- "If puffed tops are jiggly after 25 minutes, add 3-5 more minutes. Rotate pan halfway if your oven runs uneven."
- "Cool in tin 10 minutes then transfer to wire rack. Residual heat finishes baking and avoids soggy bottoms."
- ""
- "=== Notes and Substitutions ==="
- "Greek yogurt adds tang and moistness that cream cheese alone lacks. Oat flour lightens crumb and adds subtle nuttiness; can replace with more all-purpose if unavailable."
- "Coconut sugar swap: brown sugar or raw cane sugar, but watch flavor shifts."
- "Milk can be swapped with buttermilk or almond milk; adjust baking powder to baking soda ratio slightly if using acidic liquids."
- "Butter grated cold is non-negotiable for streusel texture. No solid chunks means no crunch."
- "Frozen berries introduce unwanted blueish batter and moisture; fresh preferred. Pat dry frozen berries if desperate."
- "Check muffins visually not by timer. Muffin tops should yield a little under finger press, bounce back immediately, with browned edges and a toasted scent."
- "Overmixing? Tunnels or tough crumb. Underfilled? Dry muffins with peaked tops, avoid too much batter at once."
- "If cheesecake filling bleeds into batter, mixing method or layering too thick likely culprit; keep portions manageable."
