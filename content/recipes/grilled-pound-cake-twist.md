ingredients:
- "1 loaf pound cake, cut into 1-inch-thick slices"
- "Olive oil or neutral oil for grill"
- "1 cup bourbon caramel sauce (sub maple syrup caramel for non-alcoholic)"
- "1 cup toasted walnuts"
- "1 cup grilled pineapple slices, cut thin"
- "Powdered sugar for dusting"
instructions:
- "Preheat grill to high heat. Oil grill grates well to prevent sticking. Use a brush or paper towel soaked in oil and rub over grates while hot. Direct heat crucial for proper sear and grill-mark development."
- "Arrange pound cake slices on grill so the long edge runs diagonally across the bars. This prevents falling through and creates angled grill lines. Cake crumb can stick; keep attention."
- "Grill 3-4 minutes per side. Listen for soft crackle as cake dries and chars, edges golden brown but not blackened. Use tongs gently to flip; cake fragile but needs firm grip. Internal texture should stay tender, slightly warm."
- "While grilling pineapple, slice rings 1/4-inch thick, grill 2 minutes per side until caramelized edges and smoky aroma fill air. This replaces peaches, adds tropical acidity."
- "Remove cake from grill, place immediately on warmed serving plate. Spoon bourbon caramel over slices so it melts slightly on heat. Sprinkle toasted walnuts providing crunch contrast."
- "Layer grilled pineapple on top. Dust lightly with powdered sugar, not thick or it masks flavors. Serve warm right away."
- "If cake sticks, soak slices briefly in milk before grilling to moisten crust; helps prevent breakage but expect longer grill time and softer grill marks."
- "Substitute walnuts with pecans or almonds for varied crunch texture. Caramel sauce can be homemade or store-bought—warm for easiest drizzle."
- "No grill? Use grill pan over stove high heat—watch carefully, fewer smoke signals but same crunch cues apply."
- "Check grill marks frequently; over-char burns flavor bitter. Aim for deep golden lines with crisp surface, soft interior."
