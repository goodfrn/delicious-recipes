ingredients:
- "1 medium ripe banana sliced"
- "1 cup unsweetened oat milk instead of yogurt"
- "2 tablespoons chia seeds swapped in for protein powder"
- "1 tablespoon pure maple syrup reduced from original amount"
- "1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon added twist"
- "A small handful of pecan nuts replaced original nuts"
- "1/2 cup crushed ice"
instructions:
- "Place sliced banana into blender first—preps blade for softer chunks."
- "Add oat milk and maple syrup, pour gently to avoid splashes."
- "Toss in chia seeds and pecans. They add a grit texture—expect it."
- "Ground cinnamon last. Mixes better without clumps, aromas waft after blend starts."
- "Add crushed ice; adjust by feel, less for creamier, more for icy chill."
- "Pulse blend initially, listen for smooth whirls, no large crunch sounds. Stop to scrape down sides if needed."
- "Blend on high 30-40 seconds until texture thickens but still liquid. Should coat the rim without clumping sticky bits."
- "Pour immediately; condensation forms on glass means proper chill."
- "Sip with straw, note nutty crunch, cinnamon warmth—balance banana sweetness."
- "If too thick, add more oat milk, but pour slowly. Over-thinning kills body."
- "Too thin? Add frozen banana chunks or more pecans, re-blend."
- "No blender? Mash banana thoroughly, whisk in remaining liquids then crush nuts by hand, fold in chia last."
