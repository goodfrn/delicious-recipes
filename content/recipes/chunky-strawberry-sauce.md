ingredients:
- "1 cup hulled strawberries divided"
- "1/4 cup agave syrup"
- "2 tsp arrowroot powder"
- "1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice"
- "1/3 cup water"
- "1/4 tsp fine sea salt"
instructions:
- "Start with 3/4 cup of hulled strawberries and mash in a bowl using the back of a fork or a potato masher. Rough texture desired; no puree."
- "Combine mashed berries, agave, arrowroot powder, lemon juice and water in a medium saucepan. Mix well so arrowroot dissolves fully before heat."
- "Place over low heat, gently bringing the mix to a simmer. Keep stirring constantly; slow bubbling will show initial thickening. Sounds change from watery splash to thicker simmer."
- "Cook for 5-12 minutes depending on heat, until sauce thickens to loose jam consistency and holds on a spoon with a slight coat. Avoid boiling hard; arrowroot can break down if overheated."
- "Remove from heat. Fold in remaining 1/4 cup diced strawberries plus salt. This adds fresh bursts and balances sweetness."
- "Let cool completely at room temp — sauce firms when chilled. Stir occasionally to prevent skin formation."
- "Use immediately or refrigerate up to a week in airtight container. Freeze for longer storage; thaw gently and stir before use."
- "If canning, fill hot sauce into sterilized jars immediately while hot to ensure safety and shelf stability."
