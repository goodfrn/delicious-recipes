ingredients:
- "8 ounces softened cream cheese"
- "1/3 cup Italian vinaigrette dressing"
- "1 teaspoon dried Italian herbs blend"
- "1/2 teaspoon granulated garlic powder"
- "Pinch coarse sea salt"
- "Pinch freshly ground black pepper"
- "3 chopped pepperoncini peppers"
- "1/4 cup finely diced roasted red bell peppers"
- "6 large flour tortillas"
- "6 ounces sliced Genoa salami"
- "6 ounces sliced soppressata (substituted for pepperoni)"
- "6 ounces thinly sliced prosciutto (replacing ham)"
- "7 ounces shredded provolone cheese"
- "1 cup shredded romaine lettuce"
instructions:
- "Whip softened cream cheese with Italian vinaigrette, Italian herbs, garlic powder, salt, and pepper in medium bowl until creamy and even. The acidity of vinaigrette softens the spread making it easier to spread smoothly later."
- "Fold in chopped pepperoncini and roasted red peppers. Both add moisture and a tangy bite. Taste and adjust seasoning—no need for extra salt if dressing is punchy."
- "Lay tortilla flat on clean surface. Spread about 3 tablespoons of cream cheese mixture covering almost entire surface but leaving a tiny border to avoid squeeze out when rolling."
- "Layer meats starting a little off center. Salami first, then soppressata, then prosciutto. Stack thin so layers roll evenly without bulky lumps that cause breaking."
- "Top meat with shredded provolone. Don’t overload cheese; weight can tear tortilla and wet edges."
- "Scatter shredded romaine lettuce last. Wash and spin dry lettuce very well. Wet lettuce wilts the tortilla and causes slipping when rolling."
- "Roll tortillas tightly from one edge into a spiral. This compression holds fillings in place and creates neat pinwheel pattern when sliced."
- "Wrap rolled tortillas in plastic wrap. Chill minimum 10 minutes to firm roll; helps slicing without squish."
- "Unwrap and cut into roughly 1 inch thick rounds with sharp serrated knife using sawing motion to reduce dragging."
- "Arrange on serving platter. Pinwheels keep up to 24 hours covered in refrigerator. Lettuce may dull crust crispness if stored too long—add fresh before serving if prepping way ahead."

Italian vinaigrette can be store-bought or homemade; essential for flavor and moisture. Dried herbs work better here than fresh, as they incorporate evenly without a mushy effect. The pepperoncini provide mild heat and acidity; swap with mild banana peppers if spice is unwanted. Roasted red peppers add sweetness and vibrancy, use jars or roast yourself if time permits. Tortillas preferably flour for softness and pliability; corn tortillas dry and brittle will crack when rolled. Meat substitutions should maintain similar thinness; prosciutto and soppressata can replace standard pepperoni and ham to vary flavor profile without drastic texture changes. Provolone preferred for mildness—mozzarella might make rolls too wet, harder to slice. Romaine lettuce chosen for crispness, iceberg works but less flavorful. instructionsNote: "Cream cheese spread mix done best with a sturdy spoon or small whisk to get truly smooth, avoid lumps. Proper seasoning at this stage prevents blandness — Italian dressing is acidic and salty so taste before adding salt. Spread evenly with a spatula or back of spoon, leaving a narrow edge free so cream cheese doesn’t squeeze out and messy up fingers. When layering meats, work methodically placing slices overlapping slightly. Avoid piling too much or thick edges; the goal is an even roll. Cheese shreds lightly sprinkled allow melting warmth to hold rolls together without sogginess. Lettuce added last preserves crisp texture. For slicing, a serrated knife with gentle sawing motion works best or a very sharp chef’s knife. Too much downward force crushes the tortilla.
