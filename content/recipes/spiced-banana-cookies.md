ingredients:
- "2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "2 tsp baking powder"
- "1/2 tsp salt"
- "1 tsp ground cinnamon"
- "1/2 tsp ground ginger"
- "3/4 cup unsalted butter softened"
- "1/2 cup granulated sugar"
- "3/4 cup packed light brown sugar"
- "1 large egg"
- "1 tsp vanilla extract"
- "1 cup mashed ripe banana (about 2 medium bananas)"
- "1/3 cup granulated sugar"
- "1 1/2 tbsp ground cinnamon"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 345 F. Line baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone mats."
- "Sift together flour, baking powder, salt, cinnamon, and ginger. Set aside dry mix."
- "In stand mixer or large bowl, beat butter and sugars until light and fluffy. Creaming needs to look aerated but not greasy. About 3-4 minutes on medium speed."
- "Add egg, vanilla, and mashed bananas. Mix just until combined. Do not overmix here or batter’ll get tough."
- "Add dry ingredients gradually, folding or on low speed until just incorporated. Dough will be soft and sticky. That’s right."
- "Cover tightly with plastic wrap. Chill minimum 2 hours ideally 3- This firms dough and keeps cookies from running too thin."
- "In small bowl combine sugar and cinnamon for coating."
- "Use hands to scoop and roll about 1 1/2 inch balls. If sticky, no fear. Just plop in cinnamon sugar and coat fully."
- "Space balls 2 inches apart on prepared baking sheets. No crowding or cookies glue together."
- "Bake 13-18 minutes. Watch for cracking tops, not browning. Crack pattern signals doneness. Pull off heat there. Overbaking means dry."
- "Cool on sheet 5 minutes minimum before transferring. Use thin spatula carefully or cookies break. Then cool completely on racks."
- "Store airtight at room temp up to 3 days. Wanna keep longer? Freeze dough balls or baked cookies wrapped tightly."
