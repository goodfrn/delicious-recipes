ingredients:
- "1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1 cup pastry flour"
- "1 teaspoon cream of tartar"
- "1 teaspoon cinnamon"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground cloves"
- "1/4 teaspoon salt"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking powder"
- "3/4 cup unsalted butter softened"
- "2/3 cup golden syrup"
- "1/3 cup apple butter"
- "1 large egg yolk"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1/2 cup granulated sugar"
- "1 1/2 teaspoons ground cinnamon"
- "For maple glaze:"
- "3 tablespoons unsalted butter melted"
- "3/4 cup powdered sugar"
- "1 tablespoon pure maple syrup"
- "Splash milk as needed to thin"
instructions:
- "Line two baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone mats; set aside for clean release and even heat distribution."
- "Whisk together both flours, cream of tartar, cinnamon, ground cloves, salt, baking soda, and baking powder in large bowl until homogenous. No lumps. Set dry mix aside."
- "Beat softened butter and golden syrup in stand mixer with paddle attachment starting slow. Once butter starts blending in, bump speed to medium for 3-4 minutes. Cream until light and fluffy but not greasy. Use room temp butter for proper air incorporation."
- "Add apple butter to creamed butter mix. Combine well—this adds moisture and flavor depth. Scrape sides to incorporate fully."
- "Throw in yolk and vanilla extract. Mix just until combined. Avoid overmixing here to keep dough tender."
- "Gradually add dry ingredients into wet bowl. Fold or slow mix to incorporate dough evenly, stopping once no flour streaks remain. Overmixing toughens cookies."
- "Using 2 tablespoon scoop, portion dough onto plate or tray. Chill scooped dough for 30 minutes up to overnight. The rest tightens gluten and solidifies butter. Makes thinner edges in oven without spreading out too fast."
- "Mix granulated sugar and cinnamon in small dish for rolling cookie dough later. The cinnamon sugar creates signature crackle crust after baking."
- "Heat oven to 340-355°F. Exact temp depends on oven hot spots; look for gentle oven springs on top, not quick aggressive browning."
- "Roll chilled dough balls in cinnamon sugar, coating every nook and cranny. Place on prepared sheet spaced comfortably apart to allow spreading without merging."
- "Bake 10-16 minutes depending on dough size, oven temp, bake rack placement. Cookies puff, soften, then develop tiny cracks on top. Edges lightly golden; centers puffy and soft. Remove before losing that delicate, tender crumb. Cooling on sheet for 10 minutes lets residual heat finish interior set, prevents breakage."
- "While cookies cool, prepare glaze: whisk melted butter, powdered sugar, and maple syrup until thick but pourable. Adjust with splash of milk if too stiff. No whisking endlessly; gloss and pourability matter more than fluff."
- "Drizzle glaze over cooled cookies. Sprinkle leftover cinnamon sugar on glaze if desired. Cool glaze sets to thin, shiny layer with light crisp snap."
- "Store airtight. If too soft next day, pop in oven for a few minutes or chill to firm back up. Freeze dough balls or cookies for later. Substitute ingredients work best if literal: brown sugar with golden syrup keeps chew unlike molasses or honey too acidic."
- "Avoid dough too warm or cookie plates stacking during chill — dough spreads unevenly otherwise. Watch oven temps accurately; convection bakes faster. Gentle dings and aroma of baked cinnamon apple signal readiness, not timer alone."
