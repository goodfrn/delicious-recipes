ingredients:
- "1 can pink salmon drained"
- "1/3 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1/2 cup panko breadcrumbs crushed"
- "1 teaspoon Dijon mustard"
- "1 large egg beaten"
- "1/3 cup evaporated milk"
- "2 scallions finely minced"
- "1/2 teaspoon salt"
- "1/4 teaspoon black pepper"
- "2 tablespoons vegetable oil"
- "1 tablespoon unsalted butter"
instructions:
- "Drain salmon thoroughly Remove skin and any large bones unless you want the soft ones to dissolve in cooking Break up salmon into chunks Place in mixing bowl"
- "Add flour, crushed panko, Dijon, beaten egg, evaporated milk, scallions, salt and pepper Stir all until just combined no overmix A rustic, sticky batter"
- "Rest in fridge 10-15 minutes Swells and firms up easier to shape Scoop 3 tablespoons at time Roll lightly into balls then flatten to patties Roughly 8 total"
- "Heat skillet medium with oil and butter Listen for gentle sizzle Test with scrap of batter Should brown steadily not burn"
- "Place patties gently in skillet Leave space Don’t overcrowd Cook 3-5 minutes each side Flip when edges turn golden and crisp Audible crackle, aroma freshens"
- "Drain on paper towels to retain crunch Serve warm"

Evaporated milk is a standby if buttermilk isn’t around; it adds creaminess without sourness. Salt and pepper essential but restrained—over-seasoning masks salmon’s natural flavor. Vegetable oil and butter combo in fry pan achieves ideal color and flavor balance—the oil raises smoke point while butter browns those edges. instructionsNote: "Draining the salmon well is the first step to avoid soggy mix. Removing large bones mainly for texture—the small ones dissolve and add calcium. Resting the batter chills fats and hydrates crumbs, firming mixture for easier shaping. Use a gentle hand shaping not compacting patties—too tight makes dry, too loose means falling apart.
