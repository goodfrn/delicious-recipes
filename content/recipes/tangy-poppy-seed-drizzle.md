ingredients:
- "3 tablespoons brown rice vinegar"
- "2 tablespoons dark brown sugar"
- "3/4 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric"
- "2 tablespoons finely minced raw shallot"
- "1/4 cup olive oil"
- "1/4 cup avocado oil"
- "2 teaspoons toasted sesame seeds"
- "freshly ground black pepper to taste"
instructions:
- "Add brown rice vinegar, dark brown sugar, salt, turmeric, and minced shallots to blender. Pulse until sugar dissolves and shallot gets finely dispersed. Look for a slightly grainy, golden base."
- "Slowly drizzle in olive and avocado oils while blender runs at medium speed. Watch mixture thicken and swirl into a pale, creamy emulsion with ribbons that hold their shape briefly. Avoid whisking too fast to prevent breaking."
- "Stop blender. Taste base adjust salt and add fresh cracked black pepper for bite. Remember acidity can be dialed by vinegar quantity, so balance carefully."
- "Transfer to a bowl. Stir in toasted sesame seeds by hand to retain crunch and avoid over-blending. Their nuttiness replaces poppy seeds but give a different texture crinkle in each bite."
- "Let sit 5 minutes minimum. Aromas bloom, flavors meld, and shallots soften just enough. Refrigerate if holding longer; let come close to room temp before serving."
- "Use over greens, slaws, or grilled veggies. If it separates later, shake or whisk gently before use."

Keep handy but don’t overdo sweetness or acidity—balance lives in adjusting by taste, not strict measures."
