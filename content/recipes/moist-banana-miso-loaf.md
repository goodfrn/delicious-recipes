ingredients:
- "3 ripe bananas mashed (about 1 1/4 cups)"
- "3 tbsp unsalted butter"
- "1/3 cup dark brown sugar"
- "1 tbsp white miso paste substitute tahini or soy sauce paste"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil substitute melted coconut oil"
- "1/2 cup buttermilk substitute plain yogurt thinned with milk"
- "2 tsp vanilla extract"
- "1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour substitute 1/4 cup whole wheat flour + 1 1/2 cups all-purpose"
- "1/3 cup granulated sugar"
- "1 tsp baking soda"
- "1 tsp baking powder"
- "1/2 tsp fine sea salt"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 345 F (drops from 350 for gentler rise). Grease 9×5 loaf pan evenly with butter then dust flour, tapping out excess. Set aside."
- "On medium heat, melt butter in skillet. Add mashed bananas, brown sugar, and miso. Whisk constantly to combine and break clumps. Mixture will bubble softly and thicken slightly in about 4 minutes. Smell deep caramel with salty hint. Lower heat to low, simmer gently, keep whisking to avoid scorching around edges for 3-4 minutes till glossy. Remove from heat. Let cool about 10 minutes until warm, not hot."
- "In large bowl, whisk eggs, olive oil, buttermilk, vanilla extract. Add banana mixture once warm. Whisk together quickly but off heat to avoid curdling eggs."
- "In separate bowl, whisk flour, sugar, baking soda, baking powder and salt until well combined for even lift."
- "Add dry ingredients in two parts to wet, fold gently using spatula with slow sweeping motions. Avoid overmixing to prevent tough bread. Few lumps okay; batter thick, holds shape but pourable."
- "Pour batter evenly into loaf pan. Smooth surface with spatula finger. Tap pan firmly on counter 2-3 times to release air bubbles."
- "Bake in center oven rack for 48-53 minutes. Watch edges pulling away from pan, golden brown crust with fissures. Test doneness with toothpick: insert in center; if it comes out with a few damp crumbs, done. Too wet batter? Bake 3-5 minutes longer. Under browning? Tent with foil halfway through."
- "Let bread cool undisturbed in pan 20-30 minutes. Warm loaf fragile and can crumble if unmolded too soon. Run thin knife around edge before turning loaf out onto wire rack to cool completely."
- "Slice after fully cool to avoid gummy texture inside."

Buttermilk adds acidity that reacts with baking soda for lift—it can be replaced with plain yogurt thinned with milk, same acidity level. Salt sharpens the flavors but use sparingly if you swap miso for soy sauce paste to avoid over-salting."
