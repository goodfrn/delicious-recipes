ingredients:
- "6 cups romaine lettuce leaves, torn bite-sized"
- "1½ cups cherry tomatoes, halved"
- "½ cup thinly sliced radishes"
- "1 cup cucumber, peeled and diced"
- "¼ cup thinly sliced red onion"
- "1 cup homemade croutons or store-bought"
- "½ cup grated carrots"
- "¼ cup toasted sunflower seeds"
- "¼ cup shaved fennel"
- "flaky sea salt to taste"
- "freshly cracked black pepper to taste"
- "⅓ cup vinaigrette (olive oil, red wine vinegar, Dijon mustard, salt, pepper shaken until emulsified)"
instructions:
- "Start with clean, dry lettuce. Tear into small, manageable pieces. Avoid using a knife to prevent browning edges—tearing gives better texture."
- "Divide lettuce equally across four small plates or shallow bowls. Layer evenly so no plate ends up overloaded or sparse."
- "Scatter halved cherry tomatoes over the greens. Add thin radish slices for peppery bite and crunch contrast."
- "Distribute cucumber and shredded carrot next, taking care not to squeeze or bruise the veggies to preserve snap and moisture."
- "Top with uniformly thin rings of red onion. The sharpness cuts through richness but use sparingly to avoid overpowering."
- "Sprinkle shaved fennel for aromatic, subtle anise flavor dimension. If unavailable, substitute with celery thinly sliced for crunch."
- "Add toasted sunflower seeds in place of pecans—less sweetness, more earthy nuttiness, crunch intact."
- "Scatter homemade or good-quality store-bought croutons last to prevent sogginess from dressing oversaturation."
- "Drizzle about 1 tablespoon of emulsified vinaigrette evenly on each salad. Toss gently but fully so every leaf shines with a light coat; avoid drenching."
- "Taste for seasoning. Finish with a few pinches flaky sea salt and freshly cracked black pepper—essential for punches of flavor and texture."
- "Serve immediately—salad wilts fast once dressed. If must wait, store components separately then combine last minute."
- "Troubleshooting: If lettuce soggy, salad time exceeded or dressing too heavy—lighter drizzle, faster serve time next round. No fennel? Celery or thin green apple slices work. No sunflower seeds? Toasted pumpkin seeds or chopped walnuts a good nutty swap."
