ingredients:
- "1 pound lean ground beef"
- "1/2 cup finely chopped onion"
- "3/4 cup panko breadcrumbs"
- "1/3 cup whole milk"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "1/2 teaspoon freshly cracked black pepper"
- "1 cup pineapple juice"
- "1/3 cup BBQ sauce"
- "1/4 cup sweet chili sauce"
- "1/3 cup brown sugar"
- "1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger"
- "2 tablespoons soy sauce"
- "1 1/2 teaspoons cornstarch"
- "1 1/2 cups drained pineapple chunks"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven 380 degrees Fahrenheit. Lightly grease 9×13 baking pan with nonstick spray. Set aside."
- "In large bowl, combine ground beef, onion, panko, milk, eggs, kosher salt, pepper. Mix gently but thoroughly -- avoid tough meatballs by not overhandling."
- "Use roughly 1½ tablespoons scoop for uniform size. Roll mixture into balls, place evenly spaced in baking dish. No crowding, so juices caramelize, not steam."
- "In separate bowl, whisk pineapple juice, BBQ sauce, sweet chili sauce, brown sugar, grated ginger, soy sauce, cornstarch till smooth. Cornstarch clumps mean bad sauce texture; whisk well."
- "Scatter pineapple chunks evenly over meatballs. Splash sauce atop carefully, trying not to move meatballs around too much."
- "Bake uncovered until sauce thickens and is bubbling actively, and meatballs feel firm but spring back slightly to touch, about 50 minutes but check after 45."
- "Hot. Serve over steamed jasmine rice or egg noodles. Sauce coats nicely, balance of sweetness and bite from ginger makes sharp contrast."
