ingredients:
- "3/4 cup full-fat coconut milk"
- "1/4 cup unsalted butter"
- "1 tsp kosher salt"
- "1 cup tapioca flour"
- "1 cup sharp white cheddar cheese, shredded"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1 tsp sugar"
- "2 tbsp coconut oil"
- "1/2 tsp smoked paprika"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 345 degrees Fahrenheit. Grease a mini muffin tin thoroughly with cooking spray or butter, so puffs release cleanly."
- "Combine butter, salt, smoked paprika, and coconut milk in a medium saucepan. Heat slowly over medium until mixture just reaches a rolling boil—listen for soft crackles, slight steam rises."
- "Remove from heat immediately. Transfer hot liquid into a mixing bowl. Gradually add tapioca flour, about 1/4 cup at a time. Mix with paddle or spatula initially to avoid splattering. Let dough cool slightly before electric mixer."
- "Turn mixer on low to medium speed. Incorporate eggs one at a time, followed by sugar and coconut oil. Mixture turns glossy, thick but tacky—like sticky bread dough. If too runny, add a sprinkle more tapioca but sparingly."
- "Spoon dough into each muffin cup, filling about 2/3 full. Don’t overfill or puffs spill over and deflate. Dough consistency causes this if rushed."
- "Pop into oven and bake about 23–25 minutes. Watch edges—looking for deep golden amber color, slight cracks on top. Puffs should spring slightly when touched. If underbaked, inside stays gummy."
- "Remove from oven. Let sit 5 minutes to firm up but serve while warm. Crisp outer crust with a tender, chewy interior. Cold sets a gummy texture—best fresh."
- "Tips: Tapioca flour varies brand to brand—if mixture too wet, add a tablespoon at a time. Coconut oil lends subtle aromatics but use neutral oil if disliking coconut flavor. Eggs bind and create rise; don’t skip or sub in egg replacer without adjusting wet ingredients."
- "Salty cheese is key. Cheddar swaps Parmesan well, for a sharper, more rustic profile. Experiment with herb additions like finely chopped rosemary or thyme for savory layers."
