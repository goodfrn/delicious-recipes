ingredients:
- "3 tablespoons balsamic vinegar"
- "1 tablespoon pure maple syrup"
- "1 teaspoon granulated sugar"
- "1/4 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice"
- "1 teaspoon lemon zest"
- "2 cloves garlic crushed"
- "1 teaspoon sea salt"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground sumac"
- "1 small English cucumber diced"
- "4 medium plum tomatoes chopped"
- "4 radishes thinly sliced"
- "1/2 small red onion finely diced"
- "1/2 cup fresh flat-leaf parsley chopped"
- "2 large pita breads torn into bite-sized pieces"
- "3 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil"
instructions:
- "Reduce balsamic, maple syrup, and sugar in a small saucepan over low heat. Stir occasionally. Will bubble softly then thicken, sticky when ready. About 12 minutes. Remove promptly to avoid burning."
- "Whisk in lemon juice, zest, crushed garlic, sea salt, sumac. Taste balance. Adjust salt or lemon if needed. Aromatics should be pungent but not harsh."
- "In a large glass or ceramic bowl toss cucumber, tomatoes, radishes, red onion, parsley with dressing. Toss gently but thoroughly so each vegetable glistens."
- "Cover and refrigerate minimum 45 minutes to let flavors marry and bitterness of onion soften; up to 20 hours is fine. Longer means punchier notes but pita softening risk."
- "While marinating, preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Spread torn pita on baking sheet. Drizzle olive oil. Toss quickly with hands until each piece lightly coated."
- "Bake pita 8–10 minutes. Listen for crackle and small popping sounds. Remove when golden brown but not toasted to charcoal. Cool on rack. Pita texture critical—crispy, not chewy."
- "Before serving, drain marinated veggies in fine mesh colander or tilt bowl carefully to pour off excess dressing. Prevent watery salad; wet pita ruins texture."
- "Add warmed or room temperature pita, toss carefully once. Serve immediately so bread maintains crunch."
- "Substitutions: Use apple cider vinegar for balsamic if unavailable but reduce sugar to compensate for acidity. Swap parsley with fresh mint or a combo for herbaceous lift. Sumac is key for authentic tartness; replace with lemon zest and a pinch of paprika if needed."
