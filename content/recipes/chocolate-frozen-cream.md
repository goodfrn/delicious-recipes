ingredients:
- "1 cup sweetened condensed milk, half replaced with evaporated milk"
- "1 1/2 cups whipped topping (use whipped aquafaba for dairy-free)"
- "1 cup oat milk chocolate drink (sub soy or almond chocolate milk)"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg (optional, for warmth and depth)"
- "Pinch salt"
instructions:
- "Start with the condensed and evaporated milk combination in a large bowl. Whisk aggressively until uniform and slightly thickened. The mixture should cling to the whisk but still pour slowly."
- "Add in whipped topping gently. Use a folding motion, not beating, to keep air bubbles intact—this impacts final texture. Over-mixing kills lightness."
- "Pour in the chocolate oat milk drink plus vanilla and nutmeg. Stir just until all elements are integrated. A few streaks are ok; overwork and it turns dense."
- "Transfer the blend into a freezer-safe container, spread evenly. Cover tightly with plastic wrap pressed to surface to prevent ice crystals."
- "Freeze immediately. Every 50 minutes, pull out and vigorously stir with a spatula scraping sides and bottom. The rattling sound of ice chunks breaking signals progress. Repeat 3 times."
- "Final texture reaches scoopable firmness in about 3 hours and 10 minutes. Surface will appear matte and less shiny, signaling proper partial freezing."
- "Spoon out into chilled glasses or bowls. Strong spoon pressure meets slight resistance without hardness. If ice crystals persist, stir a bit more or let sit 10 minutes at room temp before serving."
