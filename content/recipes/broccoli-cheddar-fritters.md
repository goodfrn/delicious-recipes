ingredients:
- "1 1/3 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1/3 cup cornmeal"
- "2/3 cup grated Parmesan cheese"
- "1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese"
- "1 large egg"
- "1/2 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika"
- "1/4 teaspoon black pepper"
- "1/2 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "2 cups chopped fresh broccoli florets, blanched and cooled"
- "3 scallions thinly sliced, whites and greens"
- "1 tablespoon olive oil or neutral oil for frying"
- "Maldon sea salt flakes for finishing"
instructions:
- "Whisk flour, cornmeal, cheeses, egg, garlic powder, smoked paprika, black pepper, and sea salt in a large bowl until just mixed. Over-mixing = tough bites."
- "Fold in broccoli and scallions by hand. Preserve texture; don’t pulverize greens. Batter thick but spoonable. Chill 10 minutes to hydrate flour and firm batter."
- "Heat oil in heavy skillet over medium heat. Oil should shimmer but not smoke. Hot enough when a pinch of batter sizzles instantly."
- "Spoon level tablespoons batter into pan, allow to spread naturally; don’t press or shape. Edges will start changing color and crisp up after 1–2 minutes."
- "Flip when bottom is golden-brown and you can lift easily without tearing. Another 1–2 minutes cook on second side till crisp and firm."
- "Transfer to wire rack over paper towels to drain excess oil. Maintain crunch; no sog at bottom."
- "Sprinkle with Maldon flakes as they rest. Salt crunch offsets creamy cheddar and earthy broccoli."
- "Serve warm for best texture. Store covered, reheat in skillet or toaster oven to revive crisp edges."
