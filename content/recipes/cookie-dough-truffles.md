ingredients:
- "1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour"
- "3/4 cup unsalted butter softened"
- "1/3 cup light brown sugar packed"
- "2/3 cup granulated sugar"
- "1 1/2 teaspoons vanilla extract"
- "1/2 cup coconut cream chilled"
- "1/2 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "1 cup mini white chocolate chips"
- "8 ounces bittersweet dark chocolate wafers"
- "5 ounces white chocolate wafers"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven 295 degrees Fahrenheit. Line two baking sheets with parchment paper. Set one aside, empty."
- "Spread flour thin, evenly over one baking sheet. Roast in oven 6 to 8 minutes. Watch flour closely, smell for toasted nutty aroma, no browning. Must kill bacteria intact yet keep color light."
- "Pull tray out, transfer flour to cool on a plate. Cool completely, important or dough texture will be off."
- "Cream butter, brown sugar, granulated sugar, and vanilla in mixer bowl on medium speed. Beat till pale, fluffy. You want airy texture, no gritty sugar chunks."
- "Slow mixer to low, stream in coconut cream. Mix 40 seconds till combined but mixture still thick."
- "Add toasted flour 1/3 cup at a time with salt. Keep mixer slow. Mix until just combined; overmix toughens dough. Scrape bowl sides to pull in all ingredients."
- "Stir white chocolate chips in by hand with wooden spoon. Fold evenly without breaking chips."
- "Cover dough tightly with plastic. Refrigerate 40 minutes minimum. Makes shaping easier."
- "Scoop dough with 1 tablespoon cookie scoop. Roll balls between palms quick—don’t warm dough too much. Place on reserved parchment-lined tray. Chill 15 minutes to firm balls."
- "Melt dark chocolate wafers in microwave-safe bowl. Heat 40 seconds at 50% power, stir, then 20 seconds increments. Should be fully melted, glossy, no graininess from overheating."
- "Use fork to roll dough balls in chocolate. Hold fork just over bowl edge, tap gently to drip off excess chocolate. Coat evenly with thin shell. Avoid puddles under balls or chocolate pools will crack hard."
- "Lay coated truffles on parchment sheet. Let set slightly at room temp if kitchen is cool or fridge if warm."
- "Melt white chocolate wafers same way: low power, short bursts, stir often. Should be light, creamy."
- "Transfer melted white chocolate into piping bag, clip tip. Drizzle over truffle tops in random pattern or lines."
- "Return truffles to refrigerator for 8–12 minutes to set chocolate layers fully before serving. Chocolate should snap when bitten, dough inside tender and creamy."
