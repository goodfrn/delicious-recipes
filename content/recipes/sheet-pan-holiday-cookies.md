ingredients:
- "1 cup Ghee or melted unsalted butter"
- "3/4 cup white granulated sugar"
- "1/2 cup packed dark brown sugar"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1/4 cup oat milk or regular milk"
- "2 tsp vanilla extract"
- "2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1 tsp baking powder"
- "1/2 tsp baking soda"
- "1/2 tsp salt"
- "1/2 cup chopped pecans (quadrant 1)"
- "1/2 cup dried cranberries (quadrant 2)"
- "1/2 cup white chocolate chunks (quadrant 3)"
- "1/2 cup mini semi-sweet chocolate chips (quadrant 4)"
- "Extra chocolate chunks for topping (optional)"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 345°F for a slightly lower bake to accommodate residual heat."
- "Spray a 13x18 inch half sheet pan lightly with nonstick spray; set aside."
- "Combine melted Ghee and both sugars in a large bowl. Whisk vigorously to break all clumps, especially brown sugar—you want no grainy pockets here."
- "Add eggs, oat milk, and vanilla. Whisk until batter looks uniform; pale yellow, light ribbons form when lifted."
- "Sift together flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt. Add to wet mix."
- "Fold dry ingredients in gently. Do not over mix; some flour streaks okay. Overworking pulls gluten—yields toughness, not chew."
- "Divide dough evenly into four portions; about 10-11 ounces each. Use a kitchen scale if possible. Place each in separate bowls."
- "Add suggested mix-ins: pecans, cranberries, white chocolate chunks, mini semi-sweet chips. Fold in just enough to combine—no over handling."
- "Scrape each flavored dough section onto one quadrant of sheet pan, sides touching. Work quickly to prevent butter softening too much."
- "Using a flexible rubber spatula, press each section flat and as even as possible to fill entire pan area. Don’t fret perfection; slight unevenness is normal."
- "Bake 16-21 minutes watching surface closely. Look for edges that are set with a slight golden tint, center still puffed but no wet batter. Cookie should feel springy to touch, not jiggly."
- "If very puffy after baking, set pan down on counter and tap gently, helps dough settle without deflating in a mess."
- "Scatter extra chocolate chunks on top immediately if desired. They’ll melt and add texture contrast."
- "Cool completely in pan on wire rack. Warm cookie squares cut poorly and crumble more."
- "Slice into 32 rectangles with a sharp knife or bench scraper. Store in airtight container at room temp. Cookies soften slightly over time but won’t stiffen unless chilled."
- "Substitutions: Ghee swaps butter for longer shelf life and subtle nuttiness. Oat milk nondairy and thicker; can adjust quantity slightly if batter too stiff. Honey replaces part of sugar adding moisture and caramel tones but watch bake time—it browns faster."
- "Common misstep: overmixing dough causes dense bake. Underbaking makes cutting clean easier but balance with chewiness. Rely on visual cues: matte-dull edges and no fountain of batter in middle."
- "Try folding in chopped toffee or toasted coconut for festive variations. Press toppings on top before baking for better distribution if mix-ins sink."
- "Store airtight; if stale, a few seconds in microwave softens centers again without losing integrity."
