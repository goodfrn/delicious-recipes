ingredients:
- "1 1/2 cup cake flour sifted"
- "1 cup brown butter cooled"
- "1 1/4 cup granulated sugar"
- "3 large eggs"
- "3/4 cup full-fat sour cream"
- "1 tablespoon lemon zest finely grated"
- "1 teaspoon baking powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "For glaze:"
- "4 ounces cream cheese softened"
- "1/4 cup unsalted butter softened"
- "1 cup powdered sugar sifted"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 345F; line 8x8 pan with parchment. Butter paper edges or spray; helps cake release cleanly."
- "Brown butter in saucepan over medium heat until foamy, nuts smell, and color deepens to amber. Watch carefully—can scorch fast. Remove from heat; cool slightly."
- "In large bowl, combine cooled brown butter and sugar. Beat with paddle attachment or hand mixer on medium speed until creamy and ribbon forms when lifted. Creates tender crumb by incorporating air."
- "Add eggs one at a time, beating well after each. Speed up? Batter can seize, coarse texture. Slow steady mixing keeps batter smooth. Add sour cream and lemon zest next; folds moisture and fresh acidity. Mix just to combine. Overmix drains volume."
- "Whisk together cake flour, baking powder, and salt separately. Add in thirds, folding gently with spatula after each addition. Peak in batter is shiny but thick; stop when no pockets of flour remain."
- "Transfer batter to pan; spread evenly but don’t press. Batter should jiggle slightly. Bake 48-52 minutes. Start checking at 45 mins by inserting toothpick about 1 inch from edge; moist crumbs without raw batter cling to stick when done. Top crust golden and shiny, edges pulling away."
- "Cool pan on wire rack 15 mins. Lift cake out with parchment. Room temp cooling prevents soggy crust. Completely cool before glazing, or glaze melts into cake unevenly."
- "Prepare glaze by beating cream cheese and butter till luscious and smooth. Gradually add powdered sugar to taste; too sweet dulls lemon punch. Add vanilla last; ensures bold aroma."
- "Spread glaze in thick layer using offset spatula or back of spoon for rustic look. Chill briefly if glaze too soft—firm but not rock hard. Cut with serrated knife; smooth cuts prevent crumbling."
- "Serve at room temp for best texture. Leftover can lose freshness; wrap tightly and keep refrigerated up to 3 days. Bring back to room temp or warm slightly before slicing."
