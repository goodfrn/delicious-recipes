ingredients:
- "1 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1/2 cup oat flour"
- "3/4 teaspoon baking powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "1/2 cup unsalted butter softened"
- "1/2 cup creamy peanut butter"
- "3/4 cup maple syrup"
- "1/2 cup light brown sugar packed"
- "2 large eggs room temperature"
- "1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract"
- "3/4 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips"
- "1/2 cup peanut butter chips"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to about 380°F. Line a 9x13 pan with parchment making sure paper hangs over edges for easy removal. Don't skip slipping the paper in or bars stick big time."
- "Whisk together the flours, baking powder, and salt until homogenous. Sifting optional but breaks up lumps; flour combo adds chew and slight nuttiness, not just plain white flour."
- "Cream butter and peanut butter till fluffy but not melted. Room temp key here to trap air; too soft and texture falls flat. Mixer paddle or hand mixer—whichever—works."
- "Add brown sugar and maple syrup gradually, beat till just mixed. You want uniform but not whipped or grainy. Add eggs one at a time, fully incorporate before next. Pour vanilla for fragrance mid-mix."
- "Slowly add dry mix; fold or mix on low speed until dough just comes together. Don't overwork or bars toughen."
- "Gently fold in chocolate and peanut butter chips by hand to avoid breaking chips or overworking dough. Texture bursts vs meltdown."
- "Press dough evenly into prepared pan using a spatula or lightly oiled fingers. Aim for thickness variance under 1/4 inch or bars bake unevenly."
- "Bake 25 to 30 minutes. Watch edges turn golden and surface set but center still soft. Knife inserted near center pulls out mostly clean with a few moist crumbs—not sticky batter. For chewier bars drop bake time by 4-5 minutes; underbake a touch. Overbaking dries bars and dulls flavor."
- "Cool completely on wire rack before slicing. Warm bars fall apart under knife pressure. Use parchment to lift entire slab out. Cut with sharp knife warmed briefly under hot water then dried for clean cuts."
- "Store airtight at room temperature up to 4 days or freeze individually wrapped for longer. Bars lose moisture fast if uncovered."
