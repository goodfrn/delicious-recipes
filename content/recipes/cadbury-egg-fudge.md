ingredients:
- "1 ½ cups milk chocolate chips or semi-sweet as fallback"
- "1 ½ cups white chocolate chunks or finely chopped white chocolate"
- "1 container smooth chocolate frosting approximately 14 oz"
- "1 container smooth vanilla frosting nearly 14 oz"
- "1 teaspoon almond extract or vanilla extract if preferred"
- "4 large chopped Cadbury Creme Eggs roughly in thirds"
- "½ cup crushed mini chocolate Easter eggs or any crunchy candy shell"
- "Generous spoonful of festive colored sprinkles – measure by eye"
instructions:
- "===Preparation==="
- "Use an 8×8 or 9×9 pan. Line with parchment leaving at least 2 inches overhang on sides. This prevents saucey fudge grabs and doubles as a sling for easy removal."
- "===Chocolate-Melt Steps==="
- "Put milk or semi-sweet chips in microwave-safe bowl. Set white chocolate chunks (or finely chopped bars) separately. Microwave one bowl at a time, 75 seconds initial, then stir every 20-25 seconds. Watch carefully. The chocolate should be shiny, thick yet fluid, no grainy lumps or scorched burnt smell. Stop when about 90% melted—residual heat finishes the job."
- "===Frosting Heat==="
- "Microwave the smooth vanilla and chocolate frostings separately, 50 seconds each on high. Stir to verify no clumps or cold pockets. You want fluid, flexible frosting, not scorching hot. This helps the final fudge texture stay soft but firm."
- "===Flavor Mix==="
- "Into the white frosting, stir in the almond extract. If using vanilla extract instead, same step. Almond counters sweetness and adds depth. No extract? Add a pinch of salt for balance."
- "===Combine Ingredients==="
- "Mix the melted milk chocolate with the chocolate frosting bowl. Stir thoroughly. Repeat with white chocolate and vanilla frosting bowl. Both should feel luxurious; if too stiff, microwave 10 seconds and stir."
- "===Assembly==="
- "Pour the darker chocolate mix evenly into the prepared pan. Tap sharply on counter to level and release any bubbles. While still tacky, scatter the large chopped Cadbury creme eggs evenly. These should sink in just a bit but remain visible and chunky."
- "Next, pour the white chocolate frosting blend over the dark layer. Tap pan again to spread and even out. Take a skewer or butter knife, swirl the two layers at random intervals. Don’t overmix or you lose distinct colors and textures."
- "===Final Touch==="
- "While the white layer still holds tackiness, sprinkle crushed mini candy eggs along with your choice of sprinkles. Quantity judged by eye and enthusiasm. Adds crunch contrast and springtime colors."
- "===Set==="
- "Place pan uncovered in fridge at least 3 hours or until firm but not rock-hard. Don’t cover—the fudge needs to breathe while chilling."
- "===Serving==="
- "Use the parchment sling to lift fudge block. Cut into 1-inch squares with a sharp knife. Clean the blade between cuts if needed to keep edges neat."
