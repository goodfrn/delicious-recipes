ingredients:
- "1 cup pancake mix, divided"
- "1/3 cup packed dark brown sugar"
- "1 teaspoon ground cinnamon, divided"
- "¼ teaspoon fine sea salt, divided"
- "3 tablespoons real maple syrup, divided"
- "3 tablespoons cold unsalted butter"
- "¾ cup whole milk"
- "1 large egg, lightly beaten"
- "¼ cup finely chopped pecans (twist ingredient)"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract (twist ingredient)"
instructions:
- "Set oven to 380 degrees F. Springform pan 9-inch. Line base with parchment paper. Spray pan and paper lightly with cooking spray. This prevents sticking and ensures smooth sides later."
- "Pecans toasting separately in dry pan till fragrant helps. Stir often so they don’t burn."
- "Streusel first. In small bowl, mix ½ cup pancake mix, brown sugar, ½ teaspoon cinnamon, pinch ⅛ tsp salt, 2 tablespoons maple syrup. Cut in cold butter with two knives or pastry cutter. Look for coarse crumbs – like rough brown sugar clusters, no large pats of butter visible."
- "Now coffee cake batter. In large bowl, add remaining pancake mix, milk, beaten egg, remaining maple syrup, vanilla extract, remaining cinnamon and salt. Whisk or stir just until combined; overmixing makes dense cake. Batter will feel somewhat thick but pourable."
- "Fold toasted pecans into batter—nuts give crunch and nutty aroma. Don’t skip to add texture layers."
- "Pour batter into prepared pan, spread evenly but don’t overwork. Dollop streusel evenly on top in chunks."
- "Use skewer or thin knife to swirl gently through streusel into batter. Swirl pattern prevents streusel from sinking completely and creates pockets of crunchy cinnamon-sweetness inside."
- "Bake center rack for 35–40 minutes. Watch color—golden top means done or nearly so. Toothpick inserted toward center should come out mostly clean but maybe few moist crumbs; cake should spring back when touched lightly."
- "Oven temperatures vary. If edges pull away or turn dark early, cover with foil halfway through to prevent burning."
- "Cool at least 20 minutes before removing ring to avoid breaking crumb. Warm is best but fully cooled cake slices neater. Store airtight upto 2 days or freeze."
- "If storing leftovers, warm gently in microwave or oven to revive maple aroma and soften streusel. Don't overheat or dry cake out."
