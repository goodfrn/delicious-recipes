ingredients:
- "2 pounds boneless skinless chicken thighs"
- "3 medium carrots diced"
- "1 large onion chopped"
- "2 stalks fennel sliced thin"
- "2 tablespoons fresh parsley chopped"
- "1 teaspoon Herbes de Provence"
- "1 1/2 teaspoons smoked paprika"
- "1/4 teaspoon chili flakes"
- "2 chicken bouillon cubes crushed"
- "4 cloves garlic minced"
- "5 cups low-sodium chicken broth"
- "1 teaspoon salt"
- "1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper"
- "1 1/2 cups half-and-half"
- "3 tablespoons cornstarch"
- "1 1/2 cups mini gnocchi"
- "3 cups fresh baby spinach"
- "6 ounces pancetta sliced"
- "Parmesan cheese freshly grated"
- "Warm crusty bread for serving"
instructions:
- "Dump chicken thighs, diced carrots, chopped onion, thinly sliced fennel, parsley, Herbes de Provence, smoked paprika, chili flakes, crushed bouillon, minced garlic, chicken broth, salt, and pepper into a 6-quart slow cooker. No need to fuss; spread everything evenly. Give it a gentle stir just to distribute flavors without tearing chicken pieces yet."
- "Put the lid on, set cooker to high. Cooking 4 hours plus 10 minutes is enough. Listen for steady bubbling, smell the sweeter savory meld after about 3 hours. Avoid lifting lid to keep temp steady and moisture in."
- "Check chicken at 4 hours 10 minutes. It should shred easily under two forks — if still resistant, few more minutes. Use the slow cooker itself to shred in place. Pull chicken aside on a board only if you want neater bits. Return shredded chicken immediately, stir gently."
- "Mix cornstarch with half-and-half until smooth slurry. Pour into soup swirling around to avoid lumps. Toss in mini gnocchi plus fresh spinach leaves piled on top. Cover again and crank on high for 35-55 minutes. Watch gnocchi swell, soup must thicken visibly, creamy sheen forms. Stir every 10 minutes to prevent gnocchi sticking and to gauge thickness. Add more slurry little by little if soup stays runny. Try to avoid soup turning gluey—more liquid can cure if over-thickened later."
- "While soup thickens, preheat oven to 410°F. Line baking sheet with parchment. Lay pancetta slices flat, no overlap. Bake 8-9 minutes until crispy, bubbly fat renders out, edges darken but not burnt. Let cool before breaking into chunks. Pancetta: saltier and fattier than prosciutto, gives intense crunch and porky flavor punch."
- "Dish soup into bowls. Generous sprinkle of grated Parmesan. Dollop of crispy pancetta pieces on top. Serve immediately with crusty bread warmed in oven or toasted. Crunchy bread works well to mop up creamy soup."
- "Cleanup tip: use leftover broth as base for risotto or stew. Gnocchi leftovers freeze well if kept separate from soup."

tips:
- "Use chicken thighs not breasts. Thighs keep moisture. Shredding easily tells you cooked enough. If using breasts, keep an eye—overcooking = dry meat."
- "Substitute fennel for celery. Fennel brings mild sweetness, anise notes without overpowering. Enhance broth flavor; can also use leeks. Adjust seasoning accordingly."
- "When mixing cornstarch with half-and-half, ensure no lumps form. Bit by bit; add to soup slowly. Watch for thickness. Stir regularly to check consistency."
- "For crispy pancetta, don’t overcrowd baking sheet. Space slices out; ensure even cooking. If using prosciutto, it crisps differently. Adjust timing to avoid burning."
- "If soup too thick, add broth gradually until desired texture. Cold broth to avoid sudden temperature drop. Adding too much could dilute flavor."
