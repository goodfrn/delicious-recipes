ingredients:
- "1 medium butternut squash, peeled, halved lengthwise, seeds scooped out"
- "1 tbsp unsalted butter"
- "1 cup diced shallots"
- "2 garlic cloves, minced"
- "4 cups vegetable broth (can substitute chicken broth)"
- "1 tsp ground cinnamon"
- "½ tsp ground nutmeg"
- "¼ cup heavy cream plus extra for garnish"
- "2 tsp maple syrup"
- "1 tsp salt"
- "Freshly ground black pepper to taste"
instructions:
- "===Preparation==="
- "Peel the butternut squash carefully; halves measured lengthwise. Use a sturdy spoon to scrape out seeds and stringy bits. Dice into roughly 1-inch cubes for even cooking; consistency is crucial here."
- "Mince garlic and dice shallots finely. Butter melts faster when cut into smaller chunks—so slice it beforehand if cold."
- "===Cooking==="
- "Heat a large pot over medium heat. Drop butter in; it should melt silently, no sputtering. Add shallots and garlic. Cook until translucent, about 5 minutes. Watch carefully; garlic browning too early = bitterness."
- "Toss in diced squash; it should sizzle softly as it hits the butter shallot base. Coat evenly to prevent sticking."
- "Pour vegetable broth — enough to just cover squash. Too much water dilutes flavor; keep it just submerged."
- "Crank heat to bring to gentle simmer. You want tiny bubbles rolling around edges—not a roaring boil. Cover pot loosely. Cook about 25 minutes, stirring occasionally. Squash should yield easily when poked with fork, tender but not mushy."
- "Remove from heat. Stir in cream, cinnamon, nutmeg, maple syrup, salt, and fresh black pepper. Spices wake up once heat returns, so mix thoroughly."
- "Use immersion blender straight in pot for convenience, blending until soup reaches uniform velvety texture. No chunks or stringy bits. If blender is used, do in small batches; takes less time and safer. Careful of hot splashes."
- "===Finishing==="
- "Ladle into bowls. Garnish each with a swirl of heavy cream for richness and visual contrast. Serve immediately."
- "If soup thickens too much on standing, stir in splash of broth or cream. Reheat low and slow."
- "Store leftovers in fridge, covered tightly; will keep up to 3 days."
