ingredients:
- "⅓ cup softened coconut oil"
- "⅓ cup light brown sugar"
- "1 teaspoon ground cinnamon"
- "½ teaspoon ground nutmeg"
- "½ teaspoon ground cardamom"
- "1 can (about 15 oz) sliced peaches drained"
- "1 box spice cake mix (8.5 oz)"
- "½ cup full-fat buttermilk"
- "3 large eggs"
- "Additional ⅓ cup softened coconut oil"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 355°F. Spray 9x13-inch pan generously with nonstick spray. The slight temperature bump promotes caramel texture without burning."
- "Melt ⅓ cup coconut oil and pour into the bottom of pan. Tilt pan slowly, ensure even layer covering entire base. This crucial for uniform caramelization."
- "Evenly sprinkle brown sugar over coconut oil. Resists clumping if sugar is slightly damp—light tap or funnel for spread."
- "Mix together cinnamon, nutmeg, and cardamom. Reserve half for batter. Sprinkle remaining half evenly atop sugar-oil layer. Spice oils start blooming now; aroma rises immediately."
- "Lay sliced peaches in neat rows, three per row, directly over sugar blend. Handle gently to avoid mixing layers. Very important — soft peaches bruise easily."
- "In a large bowl, combine spice cake mix, remaining spice blend, ⅓ cup softened coconut oil, buttermilk, and eggs. Use full-fat for moisture and crumb strength."
- "Beat mixture on medium-low speed 1 to 2 minutes until thick and velvety. Avoid high speed to keep batter fluffier, less dense."
- "Spoon batter carefully over peach rows. Use spatula to gently spread evenly without disturbing arrangement. Tops batter will hold. Not foolproof, but with practice works."
- "Bake 40 to 50 minutes, checking at 40. Deep golden edges and toothpick emerging clean with few crumbs mean done. Cake springs back lightly; top is dry but tender."
- "Rest cake 5 to 6 minutes in pan post bake. Tap edges lightly with heatproof spatula or butter knife to release sides. Flip onto platter quickly and smoothly to avoid stickiness tearing details."
- "Let cool slightly before slicing. Warm but not hot better for clean pieces. Serve plain or with spoonful of whipped cream or vanilla ice cream."
