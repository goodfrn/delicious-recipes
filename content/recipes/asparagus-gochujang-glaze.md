ingredients:
- "1 1/2 pounds fresh asparagus trimmed and cut into 2-inch segments"
- "3 tablespoons gochujang (Korean chili paste)"
- "2 tablespoons honey"
- "1 tablespoon tamari (gluten-free soy sauce alternative)"
- "1 small clove garlic, finely minced"
- "1 teaspoon toasted sesame oil"
- "1/2 teaspoon chipotle chili powder (replaces original heat source)"
- "1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar (adds brightness)"
- "1/4 cup toasted pecans, roughly chopped (replaces sesame seeds for crunch)"
instructions:
- "Bring a large pot filled with cold water to vigorous boil. Salty like sea water—this flavors the asparagus from inside as it cooks."
- "Toss asparagus segments into boiling water. Listen to that bubbling scream. Blanch for 2½ to 4 minutes—pierce the thickest spear with a fork. Should meet gentle resistance, bright green and pliable, never floppy."
- "Quickly drain asparagus in a sieve. Immediately plunge into ice water or run under cold tap. This shocking stop locks color, and halts cooking—no mush here."
- "While asparagus chills, whisk together gochujang, honey, tamari, garlic, sesame oil, chipotle powder, and apple cider vinegar in a medium bowl. Stir vigorous until glossy and combined. Sweet heat balanced by tang and smoky undertones."
- "Drain asparagus well from ice bath—pat dry if needed so glaze sticks and doesn’t slide off like grease down skin."
- "Return spears to empty warm pot off heat. This residual heat warms glaze without breaking down the chili paste. Pour glaze over asparagus and toss gently but thoroughly. Coating evenly—don't bruise the stalks."
- "Scatter toasted pecans on top for crunch, nutty contrast, and earthiness to cut through concentrated heat."
- "Serve warm. Texture should dance—soft but with bite, glaze sticky but not cloying, pecans adding that unexpected pop."
- "Alternative: If pecans unavailable, swap with dry toasted pumpkin seeds or lightly crushed peanuts for similar effect. No honey? Maple syrup works, though less floral. Tamari substitutes soy but use less salt overall if regular soy."
- "Pro tip: Don’t overcook asparagus to avoid bitter sulfur notes slipping out. Finish with ice bath always—hold color and snap, essential for visual and textural appeal."
