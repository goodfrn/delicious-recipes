ingredients:
- "6 tablespoons unsalted butter, divided"
- "3/4 cup light brown sugar"
- "1/4 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "10 slices canned pineapple in juice, drained"
- "Maraschino cherries for garnish"
- "2 cups yellow cake flour"
- "1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1/2 teaspoon salt"
- "1 cup buttermilk"
- "1/2 cup reserved pineapple juice"
- "3 large eggs"
- "1/3 cup vegetable oil"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven 350F. Grease 9x13 glass or metal pan thoroughly—bottom and sides slick with butter or oil spray."
- "Slice 4 tablespoons butter into pats. Place in pan, then straight into hot oven. Watch the butter bubble and melt evenly, about 5 minutes."
- "Mix light brown sugar with salt in a bowl. The salt cuts the sweetness and brings depth."
- "Pull pan from oven. Swirl melted butter to coat entire bottom surface, no pools."
- "Sprinkle sugar over butter, distribute evenly, but some grit expected. Resist mixing in; this forms the sticky base that browns while baking."
- "Arrange pineapple slices atop sugar layer. Center cherries in slices and scatter a few around edges. Firm placement ensures clean design once flipped."
- "In large bowl, whisk flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt together. These raise and lighten the batter."
- "Combine reserved pineapple juice (top off with water if less than 1/2 cup), buttermilk, eggs, oil, and vanilla in another bowl. Whisk until well blended."
- "Add wet ingredients to dry. Stir just until few lumps remain—overmixing toughens the crumb. Batter should be moderately thick but pourable."
- "Pour batter evenly over arranged fruit. Tap pan gently to settle and release air bubbles."
- "Bake 45-50 minutes. Look for golden edges pulling from pan and a cake top that springs back slightly under finger pressure."
- "Test with a toothpick inserted near center —! it should emerge mostly clean but a few moist crumbs okay. Avoid overbaking—dry cake kills the appeal."
- "Remove pan. Run a thin butter knife around edges immediately to loosen. Set a baking sheet or tray over top and, using oven mitts, invert swiftly but carefully. If edges char slightly, trim with serrated knife."
- "Serve warm or cooled to room temperature. The caramel topping should be glossy and sticky, fruit tender but intact."
