ingredients:
- "6 tablespoons unsalted butter, or coconut oil for dairy-free"
- "10 ounces mini marshmallows"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "4 cups kettle-cooked potato chips, coarsely crushed"
- "8 ounces semi-sweet chocolate chips or Candy Melts"
- "Sprinkles or mini candy-coated chocolates"
instructions:
- "===Preparation==="
- "Cut a sheet of parchment large enough to line a 9×9-inch baking pan with overhang on sides. This helps with removal later. Spray pan lightly with nonstick spray before lining to prevent slipping."
- "===Marshmallow Melt==="
- "Place butter or coconut oil in a large heavy pot over low heat. Melt fully but no bubbling; butter should shimmer and just liquefy. Toss in marshmallows and stir constantly, scraping bottom. Watch closely, marshmallows turn gooey and glossy when done; no browned bits allowed here."
- "Add vanilla extract off heat. Swirl gently until fully combined. Marshmallow mass should be thick, sticky but pourable. If clumpy or grainy, back on very low heat briefly stirring until uniform."
- "===Incorporate Chips==="
- "Remove from heat. Add crushed potato chips right away. Fold quickly—chips must coat evenly before marshmallow firms up. Silky marshmallow enveloping chips, sticky but not dripping is the goal. Work fast, or marshmallow hardens and chunks form."
- "Transfer mix to parchment-lined pan. Use a second parchment or wax paper on top to press down. Press evenly but not too hard—compress but keep volume to preserve crunch spots. No uneven lumps or gaps."
- "===Cooling & Setting==="
- "Let cool fully at room temperature. It will firm to a dense chewy slab with embedded crunchy bits. Avoid fridge; cold risks sugar condensation making surface sticky."
- "Once firm, lift slab from pan using parchment overhang. Place on cutting board."
- "===Cut & Decorate==="
- "Cut slab into 9 large squares or 18 triangles—whatever fits snack mood. For cleaner cuts, use a very sharp serrated knife warmed briefly with hot water, then dried. Wipe blade between cuts to avoid stickiness buildup."
- "Melt chocolate chips or Candy Melts in microwave in 20-second bursts, stirring to prevent burning. Should be glossy and free-flowing but not thin."
- "Dip one corner of each bar portion into melted chocolate. Let excess drip off. Place on parchment or wax paper to set. Before chocolate fully hardens (still tacky), sprinkle candy-coated mini chocolates or sprinkles to adhere."
- "===Final Notes==="
- "Chocolate adds shine, snap, and a smooth bitterness offsetting marshmallow sweetness and chip salt. Can swap potato chips for pretzels—saltier profile, crunchier texture. For variety, add crushed nuts or coconut flakes into marshmallow layer. Store airtight at room temperature. If chocolate softens too much in hot weather, refrigerate briefly but restore to room temp before serving."
