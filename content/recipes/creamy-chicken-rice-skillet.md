ingredients:
- "4 bone-in skin-on chicken thighs"
- "1½ tsp kosher salt divided"
- "1 tsp smoked paprika"
- "1 tsp garlic powder"
- "½ tsp black pepper"
- "1 tbsp olive oil"
- "1 cup long grain white rice"
- "2¾ cups chicken broth"
- "3 cloves garlic minced"
- "⅓ cup dry white cooking wine"
- "2 bay leaves"
- "1 tsp dried thyme"
- "½ cup sliced mushrooms"
- "½ cup chopped leeks (white and light green parts only)"
- "1 cup frozen peas"
- "1 cup diced sweet potatoes"
- "¾ cup canned coconut milk"
- "½ cup grated pecorino cheese"
- "2 tbsp chopped fresh parsley"
instructions:
- "Start salting chicken thighs 40-50 minutes before cooking. Salt is critical to moisture retention and flavor depth. Let thighs sit uncovered in fridge or on counter 10-15 minutes before cooking to take the chill off, ensuring even cooking."
- "Mix smoked paprika, garlic powder, 1 tsp salt, and pepper in a small bowl. Coat both sides of chicken evenly for that smoky, aromatic crust."
- "Heat cast iron skillet over medium-high heat until hot but not smoking. Add olive oil; oil should shimmer, not smoke. Lay thighs skin-side down. Listen for steady sizzle. Cook 3 to 4 minutes until skin crisps up, golden-brown and releases easily from pan. Flip, cook 4 minutes more until thigh firms but still juicy. Remove chicken, tent loosely with foil to rest. Resting traps juices; no poking or cutting here."
- "In same pan, reduce heat to medium. Toss in raw rice, mushrooms, garlic, leeks, bay leaves, and thyme. Stir quickly to toast rice slightly, fragrant and opaque. Pour in broth and white wine. The wine adds acid balance; if none, use extra broth with a teaspoon of lemon juice."
- "Cover pan with lid, lower heat to medium-low. Simmer gently for 18–22 minutes—don't open lid often or stir aggressively, rice needs steam to cook evenly without turning mushy. Check liquid; if too dry and rice still hard, add a splash of broth. At done: rice plump, tender, some grains may stick to pan edge but not burnt."
- "Remove bay leaves. Stir in coconut milk and pecorino cheese. Swap cream for coconut milk to add subtle sweetness and tropical complexity, plus tenderness. Pecorino adds salt and nuttiness in place of Parmesan."
- "Fold in frozen peas and diced sweet potatoes for texture contrast. Sweet potatoes need shorter cooking; smaller dice so they soften quickly during final simmer."
- "Nestle chicken thighs back into skillet, skin side up. Simmer uncovered on low for 5 to 8 minutes. This thickens sauce into creamy coating, melds flavors, heats chicken all the way through."
- "Finish with chopped fresh parsley. Taste, adjust salt and pepper carefully—pecorino is salty."
- "Serve straight from pan. Chicken skin stays crisp, rice stays creamy but not gluey. Taste notes of smoke, earthiness, sweet potato warmth."
- "If no cast iron, use heavy-bottomed nonstick or stainless pan but watch heat so oil doesn’t burn. Instead of leeks, yellow onion finely diced works fine."
