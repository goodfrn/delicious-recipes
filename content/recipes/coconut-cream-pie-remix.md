ingredients:
- "1 baked 9-inch pie crust"
- "4 large egg yolks"
- "3⁄4 cup granulated sugar"
- "3 tablespoons cornstarch"
- "1⁄4 teaspoon salt"
- "1 1⁄2 cups whole milk"
- "1 cup heavy cream or evaporated milk"
- "7 ounces canned coconut milk (use full fat for richness)"
- "4 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "1 teaspoon coconut extract"
- "1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract"
- "1 1⁄4 cups shredded coconut (reserve 1⁄3 cup for toasting)"
- "1 1⁄2 cups heavy whipping cream"
- "3 tablespoons powdered sugar"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract (for whipped cream)"
instructions:
- "=== Custard and Pie Crust ==="
- "Preheat oven, bake pie crust per package but shave a few minutes off original timing; remove when edges golden but still somewhat pale. Cool crust completely before filling or sogginess sets in."
- "In medium bowl whisk egg yolks and granulated sugar by hand until pale, slightly thickened – granules dissolve partially; creates base for custard smoothness."
- "Heavy saucepan now: combine cornstarch and salt dry. Slowly add whole milk, heavy cream, and coconut milk – whisk thoroughly to dissolve lumps before heat."
- "Place on medium heat, stir constantly with whisk. When mixture begins to steam, and just at first bubble pop, lower heat slightly. Let simmer 50 to 60 seconds – expect thickening, shiny, custardy glossiness. Remove one-third (about 1/3 cup)."
- "Slowly pour reserved milk into egg yolk mixture stirring constantly to temper eggs; prevents scrambled lumps. Once integrated, pour egg combo back to saucepan gradually while whisking nonstop."
- "Return to medium heat, heat to brisk boil, watch custard bubble up and crackle around edges like mini firework pops, boil exactly 80 to 90 seconds. Important: whisk continuously or custard will scorch and grain."
- "Off heat, stir in butter lumps to melt smoothly, add coconut and vanilla extracts, plus 1 cup shredded coconut for texture and bite."
- "Pour thick custard into cooled pie shell; cover surface tightly with plastic wrap pressed onto custard to prevent skin formation. Refrigerate at least 4 hours or overnight for proper set."
- "=== Whipped Cream ==="
- "Chill mixing bowl and beaters. Combine cold heavy cream, powdered sugar, vanilla. Whip at medium-high speed until stiff peaks. Avoid overwhipping which turns grainy."
- "Use piping bag with large star tip to swirl dollops over pie surface; neat decorative presentation."
- "=== Toasted Coconut ==="
- "Set oven to broil on low rack. Spread reserved 1/3 cup shredded coconut in thin, even layer on baking sheet."
- "Broil 2 to 3 minutes but watch continuously. When edges turn golden brown, remove immediately to cool. Burns fast; burnt coconut tastes bitter and ruins dessert."
- "Scatter toasted coconut over whipped cream topping."
- "Serve chilled."
- "=== Notes and Tweaks ==="
- "Whole milk substitution: 2% milk can be used but custard will be less rich and thick; compensate by adding extra cornstarch by 1 teaspoon."
- "Replace coconut extract with rum or almond extract for subtle flavor change but do not skip vanilla—important for depth."
- "If batter sweets feels too sharp, add pinch of salt to egg yolk-sugar mix to balance flavor."
- "If custard shows lumps, use fine mesh sieve to strain into crust; fiddly but yields smoother texture."
- "Leftover coconut milk can replace heavy cream for dairy-free version but custard won't thicken the same—add extra cornstarch if needed."
- "If pie crust bubbles during blind bake, prick with fork before baking or use pie weights."
- "Keep stirring when heating custard—lumps or burnt flavor happen fast and can ruin entire filling."
- "For faster serving chill minimum 3 hours but always check custard firmness with gentle press of finger on plastic wrap."
