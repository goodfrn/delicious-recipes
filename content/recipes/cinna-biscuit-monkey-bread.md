ingredients:
- "½ cup unsalted butter"
- "¾ cup light brown sugar"
- "1 tbsp ground cinnamon"
- "¼ tsp ground nutmeg"
- "1 can flaky biscuits (10 count), quartered"
- "½ cup chopped pecans"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 380 degrees Fahrenheit. Toss butter into an 8-inch bundt pan or circular pie dish and place inside oven as it comes to temperature. Butter must liquify fully—listen for that gentle sizzle, look for clear pools instead of milky blobs. Remove pan and swirl to coat every contour. That crust of butter sets the base for caramelizing sugar; don’t miss spots or you’ll have dry edges."
- "Mix brown sugar, cinnamon, and nutmeg well in a small bowl to distribute spices evenly; no clumps. Nutmeg adds background warmth, don’t skip."
- "Sprinkle half the sugar mixture evenly over melted butter base. Scatter chopped pecans next—nuts add crunch and stop bottom quarters from becoming soggy."
- "Toss biscuit quarters with remaining sugar blend in a bowl. Coat them completely. Arrange biscuits loosely atop nuts in pan. Don’t pack tight—leave air gaps so heat cooks dough evenly. Crowding leads to raw centers."
- "Bake for 18 to 23 minutes. Watch biscuit tops—they should swell, puff, and just turn golden brown. Smell thick cinnamon sugar caramel—start checking at 18 minutes by pressing the top lightly; dough should feel springy not sticky. If still pale or doughy, another few minutes needed."
- "Remove pan carefully (hot butter on bottom!). Invert pan onto serving plate right away—this avoids sticky mess and keeps the best crispy bits on top. Hold plate tightly and tap pan gently several times if needed. If stubborn, let cool 2 more minutes; sugar firms up as it cools but don’t wait too long or it sets hard."
- "Serve immediately or reheat gently without foil to restart that sticky melt-in-mouth goodness. Use a toothpick or butter knife to nudge stuck parts. Leftovers lose crispness fast—best eaten within hours."
