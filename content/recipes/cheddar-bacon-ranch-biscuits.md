ingredients:
- "2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1 tablespoon ranch salad dressing mix"
- "6 tablespoons cold unsalted butter"
- "1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese"
- "3/4 cup cooked bacon pieces or smoked ham cubes"
- "1 cup buttermilk or sour cream"
instructions:
- "=== Preparation ==="
- "Start with flour and ranch mix in a large bowl. Chill butter before cutting into flour; use a pastry cutter or two knives. The goal is butter bits roughly pea-sized—too large and biscuits won't rise evenly."
- "Add shredded cheese and meat (bacon bits or ham cubes). Stir to distribute evenly, but keep mixture loose."
- "Pour in cold buttermilk or sour cream—a thicker liquid gives richer texture. Stir just until dry ingredients moisten. Dough should look shaggy and sticky but not runny."
- "=== Shaping and Baking ==="
- "Spoon dough onto a parchment-lined baking sheet sprayed with nonstick spray or lightly greased. Drops about 2 tablespoons each, spaced an inch apart. Don't overwork dough; tough biscuits come from overmixing."
- "Preheat oven to 460°F. Place tray mid-oven; listen for sizzle as heat hits fat in dough."
- "Bake 12 to 14 minutes watching for golden brown tops and crisp edges. Surface should be slightly crackled, bottoms firm. Resist opening oven first 10 min to keep rise."
- "Remove; cool on rack a few minutes. Serve warm to taste rich butter and cheese. Refrigerate leftovers tightly wrapped."
