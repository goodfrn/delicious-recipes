ingredients:
- "2 medium apples chopped roughly"
- "1/3 cup brown sugar packed plus 1 tbsp extra"
- "1 teaspoon cinnamon ground"
- "1 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1/2 cup whole wheat flour"
- "1 teaspoon baking powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking soda"
- "3/4 teaspoon apple pie spice"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground cardamom"
- "1/2 cup unsalted butter softened"
- "1/2 cup granulated sugar"
- "2 large eggs room temperature"
- "3/4 cup buttermilk room temperature"
- "1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract"
- "1/2 cup powdered sugar"
- "1 tablespoon milk or cream"
- "1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract"
instructions:
- "Set oven to 345°F, lower than usual to avoid overbrowning. Prepare 9 by 5 inch loaf pan with parchment lining or non-stick spray. Paper helps release thick loaf better than spray for sticky sugars."
- "Toss chopped apples with 1/3 cup brown sugar, cinnamon, and cardamom thoroughly in medium bowl. Let sit 5 minutes to get juicy and syrupy — not soaking but glossy."
- "Whisk dry mix in separate bowl: combine both flours, apple pie spice, baking powder and baking soda — incorporation matters for even rising. No lumps. Set aside."
- "Cream butter with granulated sugar using stand mixer paddle or electric hand mixer on medium speed. About 2 minutes till pale and fluffy, not melting butter. Stops dense crumb later."
- "Add eggs one at a time, mixing low speed to keep emulsion stable. Scrape sides preventing overmixing, tough bread risk. Eggs add lift, binder."
- "Fold dry ingredients with sturdy rubber spatula not mixer — keeps batter lighter, less gluten formed. Stop mixing once flour streaks disappear."
- "Slowly add buttermilk and vanilla gently folding in. Buttermilk acidic, reacts with baking soda releasing bubbles for lift and moist tender crumb."
- "Pour half batter into pan, spread gently. Dollop half apple mixture evenly over batter. Careful not to push down apples, want pockets of fruit. Repeat with remaining batter and apples, layering."
- "Use butter knife to swirl apple layers using figure-eight motion — imperfect swirl is good looks, thin knife won’t deflate batter like spoon. Tap pan lightly on counter, releases air bubbles, settles batter."
- "Bake about 55 minutes. Oven varies. Smell of baked apples, cinnamon, and browning butter signals nearing done. Toothpick inserted in center comes out dry or with moist crumbs but no wet batter."
- "Cool in pan 45 minutes minimum. Loaf still hot can break on removal or crumble. After cooling, whisk powdered sugar with milk and vanilla for drizzling glaze. Thin enough to drizzle but hold shape on cooled bread."
- "Slice with serrated knife—serrated teeth saw through crust without squishing crumb. Leftover wrapped airtight. Refrigerate if warm kitchen or prefer firm slices."

- "Consider alternate flours; whole wheat adds depth but may require more baking powder for rise. For buttermilk, use milk with a splash of vinegar. Simple switch, solid results."
