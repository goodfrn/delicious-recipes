ingredients:
- "1 box 18.3-oz brownie mix"
- "3 large eggs"
- "3/4 cup melted unsalted butter instead of oil"
- "1/4 cup water"
- "8-oz mascarpone cheese in place of cream cheese"
- "1/2 cup powdered sugar"
- "1 tsp pure vanilla extract"
- "2 tbsp heavy cream"
- "2 tbsp all-purpose flour"
- "1 cup cherry pie filling swapped for cherry preserves"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 350F. Line 9x13 pan with parchment overhang. This ensures clean lift later. Set aside."
- "In big bowl, mix brownie mix, eggs, melted butter, and water. Stir until just combined. Avoid overmixing — stops tough gluten forming. Batter slightly lumpy is okay."
- "Pour batter into pan, spread evenly but gently—no air pockets."
- "In another bowl, beat mascarpone and powdered sugar on low speed with handheld mixer. 1-2 minutes till creamy but not runny. Scrape down sides."
- "Add vanilla, heavy cream, and flour to mascarpone mix. Whip 1-2 minutes more. Flour provides structure, prevents collapse during bake."
- "Drop generous spoonfuls of mascarpone mixture randomly on brownie batter. Leave gaps—means cherry layer."
- "Next, dollop cherry preserves in spaces between mascarpone. The preserves are thicker and less runny than pie filling—holds shape better on top."
- "Take a butter knife, swirl mascarpone and cherry preserves but don’t plunge deep into brownie layer. Aim for marbled top layer. Preserve some brownie showing on edges too."
- "Bake 50 minutes or till center is set and toothpick inserted into brownie (avoiding cream cheese bits) comes out almost clean with moist crumbs but no raw batter. Around edges should pull away slightly from pan and top lightly crack."
- "Cool fully on counter before refrigerating minimum 3 hours or overnight. Chilling firms layers so slices hold shape. Warmer slices crumble."
- "Serve cold or room temp. Clean knife between cuts for neat squares."
