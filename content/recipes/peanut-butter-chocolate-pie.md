ingredients:
- "4 ounces dark chocolate chunks"
- "1 cup heavy cream"
- "1 8-ounce block softened cream cheese divided"
- "3/4 cup almond butter"
- "3/4 cup powdered sugar divided"
- "1 cup Greek yogurt nonfat, divided"
- "1/4 cup dark cocoa powder"
- "1 9-inch graham cracker crust"
- "Zest of 1 orange"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
instructions:
- "=== Melt chocolate base ==="
- "Take chocolate chunks in heatproof bowl. Warm cream in microwave until just steaming, about 20-25 seconds; don’t scald. Pour cream over chocolate chunks. Let sit 5 minutes—no stirring till softening’s obvious. Stir slowly until uniform, glossy. Cool slightly while doing creams."
- "=== Almond Butter Cream Filling ==="
- "Beat 1/2 block softened cream cheese in large bowl until creamy but not whipped. Add almond butter and half powdered sugar. Mix moderate speed. Fold in half Greek yogurt. Mixture should be thick, spreadable, slightly tangy but rich."
- "=== Chocolate Cream Filling ==="
- "Same with remaining cream cheese: beat until lump-free but don’t aerate too much. Mix cocoa powder into melted chocolate cream; stir. Add remaining powdered sugar, vanilla. Stir until fully integrated. Fold in remaining Greek yogurt slowly. Aim for punchy chocolate flavor without graininess."
- "=== Assembly ==="
- "Spread half almond butter cream evenly and firmly into crust bottom. Pat gently to avoid air gaps. Spoon chocolate cream over almond layer. Smooth, but keep a few waves for later swirl. Reserve remaining almond butter cream for decoration."
- "Pipe almond butter cream around edge with star tip 1M or similar. Creates texture, prevents blending of layers during chill. Sprinkle orange zest over top for aromatic lift. Place pie in fridge minimum 3 1/2 hours—longer won’t hurt but not much less. Check filling firmness by lightly tapping; should hold shape without soft jiggling."
- "=== Storage and Tips ==="
- "Keep covered tightly in fridge for up to 4 days. Warm slightly on counter before serving for best texture. If crust soggy, blind bake prior to filling or use cookie crumb crust. Greek yogurt can substitute whipped cream for weight savings and tang; if allergic, use coconut cream but add coconut extract. Dark cocoa powder adds bitterness to offset almonds; swap for milk chocolate for sweeter profile. Don’t rush melting chocolate cream—grainy or broken if heated too fast. Be patient."
