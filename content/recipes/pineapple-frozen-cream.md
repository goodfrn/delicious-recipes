ingredients:
- "14 oz sweetened condensed milk"
- "1 tsp vanilla extract"
- "1/4 tsp fine salt"
- "2 cups heavy whipping cream"
- "1 1/2 cups crushed pineapple, well drained"
- "Optional replacement—coconut cream instead of heavy cream"
- "Alternative fruit—mango chunks or ripe peach pieces"
instructions:
- "Mix 14 oz sweetened condensed milk, 1 tsp vanilla extract, and 1/4 tsp salt in a large bowl until homogeneous. Keeps mix balanced and intensifies flavors."
- "Using cold bowl and whisk attachment, whip 2 cups heavy cream in stand mixer at medium-high speed. Aim for stiff peaks. Watch texture—stiff means peaks stand straight. Overwhip? Starts grainy, stop before that."
- "Fold whipped cream gently in thirds into milk mixture. Use rubber spatula, scoop from bottom and lift up. Don’t stir or lose aeration; lose volume = denser, not creamy texture."
- "Fold in 1 1/2 cups crushed pineapple slowly. Pineapple must be well drained or ice cream gets watery. Retain juice separately for later use in smoothies or cocktails."
- "Pour blend into a 9 by 5 by 3 inch bread pan. Scrape sides clean. Press lightly to even surface, seal with plastic wrap but leave uncovered in freezer for about 2 hours. Aim for mostly firm edges but slightly soft center. The ice cream is setting, chilly air causing slight crust to form on top for holding fruit."
- "After 2 hours, remove from freezer and press reserved pineapple chunks into top evenly. The fruit sinks less if ice cream is partially frozen—not rock solid. Provides lovely bursts of flavor and texture."
- "Cover pan tightly with foil or plastic wrap. Return to freezer for 7 to 13 hours, ideally overnight. Texture changes from soft cream to firm scoopable block. Do not disturb or refreeze once fully set."
- "When ready, let sit 3-5 minutes outside freezer to soften slightly for better scooping. Scoop using warm spoon or ice cream scoop dipped briefly in hot water. This prevents tearing and messy chunks."
- "Serve immediately. Store leftovers tightly sealed. If icy, allow brief soften or whisk before refreeze to regain smoothness."
