ingredients:
- "12 oz sharp cheddar cheese cut in 3/4 inch cubes"
- "1 can diced tomatoes with jalapeños, 10 oz including juice"
- "2 tbsp heavy cream or small splash of draft beer optional"
- "Freshly ground black pepper to taste"
- "Tortilla chips or assorted sliced veggies for serving"
instructions:
- "=== Preparation ==="
- "Cube cheddar, aim uniform sizes. Avoid shredding. Cubes melt more gradually & reduce risk of clumps. This yields patchy melts retaining some bite - good texture, especially if cheese is cold from fridge."
- "Drain half the juice from tomatoes but keep some in. Juice improves liquidity."
- "=== Cooking ==="
- "Place cheese cubes in a microwave-safe ceramic or glass bowl large enough for stirring. Pour in tomatoes with their chunky jalapeños plus leftover juices. Add cream or beer if using, stirs up smoothness and flavor layering."
- "Microwave on full power 50 seconds to 1 minute first. After that, stir thoroughly with a silicone spatula scraping edges and bottom to prevent hot spots and burning."
- "Repeat microwaving in 40 second bursts, stirring well each time, watch cheese guesses at melting stage. Once cheese is gooey but still has some chunks, stop. Over-doing makes rubbery, oily dip. Aroma switches from sharp cheese to mellow toasted notes, you’ll smell it."
- "=== Serving ==="
- "Serve immediately warm. Cool cheese will firm up and lose appeal fast. Pair with salty crunchy tortilla chips for contrast or crunchy bell pepper slices if you want freshness. If you wait too long dip thickens and becomes gluey."
- "Can be kept warm in a double boiler for short periods but reheating needs gentle low heat to avoid cheese clumping."
