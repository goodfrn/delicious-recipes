ingredients:
- "2 8-inch cake pans greased or lined"
- "1 13.5 oz box red velvet cake mix"
- "8 oz cream cheese softened"
- "4 tbsp unsalted butter softened"
- "2/3 cup granulated sugar"
- "1/3 cup crushed graham crackers"
- "1 tsp vanilla extract"
- "Alternate crumb coating: crushed chocolate wafer cookies"
- "Alternate binders: mascarpone instead of cream cheese"
instructions:
- "===Cake and crumbs==="
- "Heat oven to 350°F. Prep two 8-inch cake pans, grease or parchment."
- "Mix cake batter per package but cut liquid by 10% to avoid sogginess. Bake 30-35 mins. Look for springy top, clean toothpick."
- "Cool completely. Crumble by hand or fork, aiming for coarse sand, not paste."
- "If crumbs stick or feel wet, spread thin on parchment. Bake 5-7 mins at 350°F. Watch closely — dry crumbs should crisp but not brown or char. Remove and cool."
- "===Cheesecake ball base==="
- "Whip cream cheese, butter, and sugar in large bowl with electric mixer. Fluffy, aerated texture is key — no lumps or grainy sugar. ~2-3 mins medium speed."
- "Add graham cracker crumbs and vanilla. Mix till uniform with no streaks. Crushed cookies optional here too."
- "===Form balls and chill==="
- "Scoop about 1 tablespoon of mixture (roughly 2 tsp scoop) and roll gently between palms. Uniform sphere helps even texture inside."
- "Place on parchment-lined tray. Chill in fridge uncovered for 1 hour minimum. Firm surface makes coating easier."
- "===Coating==="
- "Roll chilled balls in red velvet crumbs. Press gently with fingers for an even, full crust. Re-round if edges get jagged."
- "If crumbs dry, tap off excess. Use chocolate wafers for deeper cocoa flavor coating as alternative."
- "===Storage and serving==="
- "Serve immediately or refrigerate up to 3 days. Return to fridge 15 mins before serving for best texture."
- "Watch for over drying in fridge—the balls should still feel creamy inside, not chalky."
- "Consume within 4 days to avoid souring or crumb moisture loss."
