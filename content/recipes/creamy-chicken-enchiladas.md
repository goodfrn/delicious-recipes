ingredients:
- "8 8-inch flour tortillas"
- "3 cups cooked shredded chicken (rotisserie works well)"
- "6 ounces cream cheese, softened"
- "3/4 cup plain Greek yogurt (substitutes sour cream for tang and creaminess)"
- "1 cup smoky chipotle salsa (replaces mild tomato salsa for deeper flavor)"
- "2 1/2 cups enchilada sauce (divided)"
- "1 1/2 cups shredded Monterey Jack cheese"
- "1/2 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese"
- "1 teaspoon ground cumin (replaces cumin seeds)"
- "Nonstick cooking spray"
- "Aluminum foil"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 355°F. Spray a 9×13-inch pan lightly with nonstick spray; this helps besides the foil to prevent sticking and promotes slight caramelization on edges."
- "Cut softened cream cheese into chunks, place in small microwave-safe bowl. Cover with damp paper towel to prevent drying out; microwave in 20-second intervals, stirring after each until creamy but not hot. This helps easier mixing without lumps."
- "In large bowl, fold softened cream cheese, Greek yogurt, chipotle salsa, and cumin until uniform. Add shredded chicken last, mixing gently to keep chicken flakes intact. The yogurt adds acidity to balance richness but avoid overstirring, or chicken will get too mushy."
- "Warm tortillas quickly on skillet or microwave until pliable but not soggy. Proper heating prevents tearing when rolling and avoids dry cracked edges. If tortillas crack easily, wrap warm towel around stack to keep soft while stuffing."
- "Pour half the enchilada sauce evenly on the bottom of prepared dish. This prevents sticking and infuses baked bottom tortillas. Makes cleanup easier."
- "Spoon about 1/3 cup chicken filling down center of each tortilla. Top with approximately 1 tablespoon blend of Monterey Jack and cheddar cheese. Roll each tortilla snugly so fillings stay inside. Arrange seam-side down in dish close but not touching; tight roll means less sauce leakage."
- "Ladle remaining enchilada sauce evenly over the top. Sprinkle remaining cheese mix liberally. The cheese shield locks in moisture and forms crispy golden crust on bake."
- "Cover tightly with foil to lock moisture in. Bake 30–35 minutes until bubbling around edges and cheese melted underneath foil. Remove foil last 5 minutes to allow tortilla edges to toast and slightly brown. Listen for gentle bubbling; avoid drying edges or hardening cheese crust."
- "Let rest 5 minutes out of oven so sauce thickens slightly. Top with fresh sliced jalapeños, chopped cilantro or diced avocado (optional) for contrast."
- "Serve hot, knife and fork recommended to keep filling intact."
