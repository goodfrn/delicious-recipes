ingredients:
- "1 cup unsalted butter softened"
- "1 3/4 cups granulated sugar"
- "4 large eggs"
- "1 1/2 cups crushed vanilla wafers"
- "1/2 cup almond milk"
- "1 cup shredded sweetened coconut"
- "2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract"
- "3/4 cup chopped macadamia nuts"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking powder (added)"
- "Pinch salt"
instructions:
- "Grease and flour a 10 to 12 cup Bundt pan thoroughly. The coating must fill ridges for even release. Tap out excess flour."
- "Preheat oven to 315°F for gentle, even baking. Too hot burns edges, leaves cold center raw."
- "In large bowl, beat butter and sugar on medium speed until pale and fluffy. Trap air here. Usually 3-4 minutes."
- "Add eggs one at a time, fully incorporated before next. Avoid curdling; slow addition."
- "Fold in crushed vanilla wafers with a rubber spatula. They bring texture not a flour substitute. No overmixing or wafers get gummy."
- "Whisk almond milk with vanilla and baking powder for added lift. Add slowly and mix just enough to combine."
- "Incorporate shredded coconut and chopped macadamia nuts gently. Even distribution keeps bites crunchy. Macadamias offer a buttery note unlike pecans."
- "Pour batter into Bundt pan. Smooth top with offset spatula but don't compress or lose air."
- "Bake 50-60 minutes. Start checking at 50. Insert toothpick: look for moist crumbs not wet batter. Surface should be golden, sides pulling slightly from pan."
- "Cool 15 minutes in pan on wire rack before unmolding. Hot cake sticks and tears. Use thin knife if needed around edges."
- "Serve slightly warm or room temp. Cozy crumb, crisp edges, nutty chew."
