ingredients:
- "1 cup browned butter cooled"
- "3/4 cup fine cane sugar"
- "1 teaspoon almond extract"
- "2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1 cup toasted chopped walnuts"
instructions:
- "===="
- "Start by melting butter over medium heat stirring constantly. You’ll see foam, then golden brown specks. Smell deep, nutty. Remove from heat; cool until just warm but not solid."
- "===="
- "In the bowl, mix sugar and browned butter. Beat until combined but not fluffy; gritty sugar sticks around adds texture."
- "Add almond extract. Stir in flour gradually, don’t dump it all at once or dough toughens. Mix until just incorporated. Avoid overmixing; the dough should be crumbly but hold when pressed."
- "===="
- "Fold in walnuts last. Nuts toasted before chopping releases oils, boosts flavor; toast small pan over medium, watch, not burn. Chips from knife can tear dough; use gentle folding to spread nuts evenly but keep chunky."
- "===="
- "Grease a 12 by 9-inch pan with butter or non-stick spray. Press dough firmly with fingers or wax paper to even thickness. Too sticky? Chill 10 minutes or oil hands lightly before pressing. Aim for rough flat surface, rustic edges okay."
- "===="
- "Slide into 325°F oven on middle rack. Listen for gentle crackle as edges set; color changes from pale to golden brown, especially corners. Start checking at 30 minutes but expect closer to 35-38 for correct doneness."
- "===="
- "Remove, cut warm with sharp knife or pizza cutter. Crust should snap with slight give underneath. Cool completely in pan to set texture before lifting pieces out. Store airtight."
- "===="
