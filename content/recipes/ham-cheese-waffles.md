ingredients:
- "1 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1 tsp baking soda"
- "1/2 tsp salt"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1 cup buttermilk"
- "3 tbsp olive oil, plus more for waffle iron"
- "3/4 cup diced smoked turkey, finely chopped"
- "3/4 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese"
instructions:
- "Start warming waffle iron. Olive oil the surfaces lightly—prevents sticking better than spray here."
- "In a mixing bowl, whisk flour, baking soda, and salt. Mixing these dry ingredients first ensures even distribution and proper lift from the soda."
- "Break eggs into a separate container, then mix with buttermilk and olive oil. Combining the wet parts separately before incorporating keeps batter from toughening."
- "Slowly fold wet mixture into dry until just moistened. Leave lumps in batter. Overwork, and waffles turn gummy. The batter will be slightly thick, easy to scoop but not runny."
- "Gently fold diced smoked turkey and shredded cheddar into batter. Even distribution is key so every bite bursts with flavor but doesn't weigh down the batter."
- "Splash more olive oil on the waffle iron. Pour a heaping 2/3 cup of batter (measurement flexibility depends on your waffle iron size) onto hot iron. Close lid immediately to trap steam—steam escaping prematurely steams the surface, dulling crispness."
- "Cook until the steam slows dramatically and edges look dry and slightly brownish—about 4 to 6 minutes. Sound changes: from hissing to a subtle settling hiss."
- "Remove carefully, set on cooling rack or wire rack to keep surface crisp—avoid stacking directly, or steam softens texture."
- "Repeat with remaining batter. If not serving right away, keep waffles warm on a baking sheet in 175°F oven, spacing them out to retain crunch."
- "Serve with hot honey drizzled over or top with a gently cooked fried egg for richness. Both add sweet-savory balance that plays well with smoky turkey and sharp cheddar."
