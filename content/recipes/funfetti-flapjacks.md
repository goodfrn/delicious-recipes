ingredients:
- "1 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1/3 cup almond flour"
- "1 1/4 teaspoons baking powder"
- "1/4 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1/3 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "3 tablespoons organic cane sugar"
- "3 tablespoons maple syrup oil or melted coconut oil"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1 cup buttermilk (or 1 cup milk plus 1 tablespoon lemon juice, rested 5 minutes)"
- "1 teaspoon almond extract (instead of vanilla bean extract)"
- "1/3 cup rainbow sprinkles, use vegan or natural for less bleed"
- "butter or oil for greasing skillet"
- "Vanilla Cream Cheese Glaze==="
- "1 cup powdered sugar"
- "2 tablespoons cream cheese softened"
- "2 tablespoons whole milk or almond milk"
- "1/2 teaspoon almond extract"
- "1 tablespoon unsalted butter softened; optional for silkiness"
instructions:
- "Dry Mix==="
- "Whisk together all-purpose flour, almond flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt and sugar in a large bowl. Sift if lumps or clumps appear. Almond flour adds tenderness, expect slightly coarse texture, normal."
- "Wet Mix==="
- "In separate bowl, beat maple syrup oil with eggs, buttermilk and almond extract until homogeneous but don’t overbeat—should be fluid, not foamy. Oil replaces butter for richer earthiness and moisture retention."
- "Combining==="
- "Pour wet into dry, fold gently with spatula just to combine. Some lumps okay, overmix develops gluten, tough pancakes. Last, fold in sprinkles carefully to avoid color run but don't skimp; they melt subtly in heat."
- "Resting Batter==="
- "Cover bowl loosely, set aside 5 minutes. Rest lets flours hydrate and powder activate for better rise. Bubbles start forming; batter thickens slightly. If too thick, add splash milk."
- "Cooking==="
- "Preheat non-stick skillet over medium heat (~350°F). Butter or oil skillet lightly. Drop 1/4 cup batter per pancake, use back of spoon to shape slightly if necessary. Wait for surface bubbles to appear and edges to set before flipping (listen for gentle sputtering). Flip gently, cook approximately 2-3 minutes until golden on second side. Pancakes firm to touch, springs back when pressed lightly with spatula."
- "Holding==="
- "Transfer cooked pancakes to wire rack set over baking sheet or cooling rack. Tent loosely with foil to stay warm without steaming (keeps edges from getting soggy). Bake sheet in 275°F oven if making large batches. Avoid stacking hot pancakes to prevent limpness."
- "Glaze==="
- "In small bowl, whisk powdered sugar with softened cream cheese until smooth. Add milk, almond extract, and butter if using. Whisk until thick but pourable. Cream cheese adds body and a subtle tang, cutting sugary sweetness. Adjust thickness by dribbling milk or sugar. Cover glaze to prevent crust."
- "Serving==="
- "Stack pancakes. Drizzle with glaze generously while warm. Sprinkle more confetti on top for visual pop and texture contrast. Optionally, add dollops of whipped cream, toasted nuts, or fresh berries for crunch and acidity."
- "Common Pitfalls==="
- "If batter bubbles excessively and collapses, reduce resting time or check leavening freshness. If pancakes stick, skillet likely too cool or residues remain; wipe skillet periodically. Too brown outside raw inside means heat too high, lower stove. Color loss of sprinkles means overmixing or high heat. For dairy-free, swap cream cheese for vegan soft cheese in glaze. For gluten-free, ensure almond flour is superfine and add 1 teaspoon xanthan gum to dry."
- "Cleanup Hack==="
- "Soak spatula and bowl in warm water with a splash of vinegar immediately after use to remove sticky sugar and oil residue easily."
