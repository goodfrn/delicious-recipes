ingredients:
- "3/4 cup granulated sugar"
- "1 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1/3 cup unsweetened cocoa powder"
- "1/4 cup packed light brown sugar"
- "1 cup almond milk or other non-dairy milk"
- "1/4 cup melted coconut oil"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1 teaspoon finely grated orange zest"
- "1/2 teaspoon instant espresso powder"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 345 degrees F. Grease an 8x8 inch pan thoroughly with coconut oil or nonstick spray. No shortcuts here for crust texture."
- "In a medium mixing bowl combine all dry ingredients except brown sugar – sugar, flour, cocoa powder, espresso powder. Whisk to avoid clumps."
- "Add brown sugar to the dry mix, stir lightly to distribute evenly, but don’t force lumps out yet. Brown sugar pockets boost chewy bits later."
- "In a separate bowl, whisk almond milk, melted coconut oil, vanilla, and orange zest until emulsified. The zest adds brightness offsetting cocoa bitterness."
- "Combine wet into dry in one go, mixing just until no flour pockets remain. Overmixing develops gluten and ruins tender crumb."
- "Scrape every bit of batter into prepared pan. Use an offset spatula to spread evenly but don’t press down too hard; air keeps it light."
- "Bake in lower temp for about 40 minutes. Watch the edges to pull away slightly and the surface to look dull but with a slight wobble in the center when jiggled gently."
- "Use visual wobble, not timer alone, as main doneness guide. Too firm means dry; too loose means raw in middle."
- "Remove from oven and let rest 10-15 minutes before scooping. Cooling helps set crumb without losing gooeyness. Serve warm alongside a scoop of vanilla or citrus gelato."
- "No ice cream? Dollop whipped cream with a sprinkle of salt to balance richness."
