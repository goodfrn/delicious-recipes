ingredients:
- "2 cans refrigerated cinnamon rolls with icing"
- "8 ounces cream cheese, cubed (or mascarpone)"
- "¾ cup unsalted butter"
- "1 cup packed light brown sugar"
- "½ cup granulated sugar"
- "1 cup chopped walnuts (or pecans)"
- "Nonstick cooking spray"
instructions:
- "Warm oven to 340 degrees Fahrenheit—slight adjustment ensures even caramelization without burning the sugars."
- "Spray large Bundt pan heavily with nonstick spray, focusing on every ridge and cavity; prevents stick and caramel damage later."
- "Combine butter, brown sugar, and white sugar in small saucepan over medium heat, simmer gently until mixture thickens and bubbles softly, about 7 minutes. Off heat, spoon a bit to test texture—should be syrupy but not too runny."
- "Scatter about ⅓ cup walnuts in the bottom of prepared pan; toasting nuts beforehand adds depth but optional. You want them toasted by bake’s end."
- "Open one can cinnamon rolls, press each gently flat—use palm or rolling pin, not too thin or dough breaks, just enough to envelop filling."
- "Place one cream cheese cube in center of roll, pinch edges tightly around cheese, roll into ball shape while holding it in your palm, roll gently but firmly to seal and distribute filling evenly. Important: no gaps, no cream cheese oozing out during bake or messy puddles result."
- "Arrange stuffed rolls snugly around pan bottom, space is close but not squished; too tight, they don’t rise well, too loose, gaps form."
- "Sprinkle half remaining walnuts over first layer, then drizzle half butter-sugar syrup across evenly; watch for syrup dripping down edges."
- "Repeat flattening, stuffing, sealing with remaining cinnamon rolls and cream cheese cubes. Layer second batch tightly atop first."
- "Top second layer with remaining walnuts; drizzle remaining syrup evenly. Excess syrup pools around edges, caramelizing edges nicely."
- "Place pan on oven’s center rack. Bake 35 minutes until dough rises, tops golden, edges bubbling, and center tests done with toothpick—moist crumbs, no raw dough visible."
- "Remove from oven, cool 7 minutes—not longer or sugar hardens to stick the bread to pan stubbornly."
- "Invert carefully onto plate. If stuck edges, run thin spatula along sides before flipping, or warm pan slightly on stovetop to loosen caramel."
- "For icing, remove metal lid from supplied tub, microwave 10-12 seconds just until pourable—test quickly. Drizzle over entire loaf or individual pieces. Icing adds sweetness, but not overpowering."
- "Let sit 5 minutes for icing to set slightly. Serve warm for gooey inside, chewy edges, crunchy walnuts. Store leftovers wrapped; reheat in microwave for gooey effect or oven to restore crust."
