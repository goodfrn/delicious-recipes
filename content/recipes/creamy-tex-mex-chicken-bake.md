ingredients:
- "1 ½ cups cooked brown rice"
- "3 cups shredded cooked chicken"
- "1 ½ cups salsa verde"
- "1 cup diced tomatoes with chilies"
- "1 cup diced bell peppers (mixed colors)"
- "1 cup cooked pinto beans (drained and rinsed)"
- "1 can (4 oz) diced green chiles"
- "½ cup diced white onion"
- "2 ½ tablespoons taco seasoning"
- "¾ cup sour cream"
- "2 cups shredded Monterey Jack cheese, divided"
- "Cooking spray"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 345°F. Spray a large (9x13-inch) casserole dish thoroughly. Set aside."
- "In a big bowl, toss brown rice with shredded chicken. Add salsa verde, diced tomatoes with chilies, bell peppers, beans, green chiles, onion, taco seasoning, sour cream, and 1 cup shredded cheese. Mix thoroughly but don’t overwork or mush the ingredients."
- "Spread mixture evenly in casserole dish. Sprinkle remaining 1 cup cheese on top. Cover tightly with foil, seal edges well to trap steam."
- "Bake about 25 minutes until edges bubble and steam escapes from under foil. Remove foil carefully—watch for hot steam hiss."
- "Continue baking 10 to 15 minutes uncovered, cheese should melt fully and turn golden with blisters. The internal temp should reach around 150°F (instead of 145) to ensure chicken is warmed through but not dried out."
- "Remove from oven and rest at least 10 minutes. The casserole will thicken slightly as it cools. Serve warm with toppings like sliced green onions, cilantro, or avocado."
- "If casserole seems too wet before baking, drain excess juices from canned ingredients. Using brown rice adds nuttiness and chew, but white rice can be swapped for a softer texture with shorter baking."
- "Substitute shredded rotisserie turkey or pork if no chicken on hand. Sour cream brings tang and creaminess; Greek yogurt is a good low-fat alternative but will thin the mixture more."
- "If you only have red enchilada sauce, it works fine but salsa verde cuts through richness and adds bright acidity."
- "To speed prep, use leftover rice and chicken or pre-cooked frozen rice. Avoid over-stirring so chicken stays in tender shreds, not a paste."
