ingredients:
- "12 Cadbury Eggs, unwrapped and frozen"
- "1 box chocolate cake mix or homemade equivalent (about 15 oz)"
- "1 teaspoon instant espresso powder, optional"
- "1/2 cup butter, softened"
- "1/4 cup cream cheese, softened, substitution for shortening"
- "3 cups powdered sugar sifted"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1/4 teaspoon salt"
- "3-4 tablespoons milk or cream"
- "6 additional Cadbury Eggs for filling and topping"
instructions:
- "Freeze unwrapped Cadbury Eggs at least 2 hours; easier to unwrap beforehand, prevents melting inside the batter."
- "Prepare cake batter adding espresso powder with dry mix. Preheat oven to 350F or package suggestion."
- "Line jumbo muffin tin with oversized liners; smaller ones risk batter leakage and uneven cooking."
- "Fill liners halfway with batter, place one frozen egg gently in center; cover with additional batter just enough to coat top."
- "Bake until cupcake tops firm to touch, lightly golden, and spring back. Skip toothpick test — solid center distorts results."
- "Cool completely on wire rack before frosting to avoid melting buttercream or slipping toppings."
- "For frosting, beat butter and cream cheese together until pale and airy. Gradually add powdered sugar, vanilla, salt."
- "Add milk slowly, 1 tablespoon at a time, beat till fluffy but not runny; frosting should hold shape without stiffness."
- "Scoop creme filling from 6 of the frozen eggs carefully; fold gently into frosting. Two or three turns max to streak, not blend."
- "Pipe or spread frosting generously onto cupcakes; offset spatula or piping bag with large round tip works best."
- "Slice remaining 6 eggs in half cleanly using sharp knife; press halves lightly on frosting for visual punch and texture contrast."
- "Serve at room temp. Leftovers refrigerate; bring to room temperature before serving for best texture."
