ingredients:
- "1 large bunch kale (about 8 cups packed)"
- "1 1/2 cups shredded smoked gouda cheese"
- "1 teaspoon herbes de Provence"
- "3/4 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "3/4 teaspoon onion powder"
- "1 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper"
- "1 cup canned full-fat coconut milk"
- "Powdered parmesan cheese for topping"
- "Cooking spray or a light brush of olive oil"
instructions:
- "Spray or oil a 5-6 quart casserole evenly. Preheat oven to 355°F to settle heat distribution; hotter ovens risk uneven cooking here."
- "Grab kale by hand, strip tough stems first. Massage leaves firmly. Fibers break down, kale softens remarkably. Don’t skip this step or risk stringy bites."
- "Pile massaged kale into casserole. Let it mound if it needs—undos during baking. Green will shrink dramatically, trust the shrink. No squishing required."
- "Sprinkle shredded smoked gouda. This cheese melts well, adds creamy smoke notes replacing Italian cheese blends. Dust with herbes de Provence, garlic, onion powders, salt, and pepper. Season boldly but balanced."
- "Gently pour coconut milk. Avoid pouring too fast; want even coverage without flooding. Coconut milk stabilizes creaminess with slight dairy-free twist."
- "Bake uncovered 18-23 minutes. Watch for bubbling edges, cheese melting into crisp edges, kale wilting but not soggy. Avoid overbaking which causes dryness and bitterness."
- "Pull casserole out hot. Flick powdered parmesan on top immediately. It melts slightly with residual heat creating a delicate crisp layer that brightens flavors."
- "Serve straight away. Letting sit cools cheese gloss and toughens kale texture. For leftovers, reheat gently under foil to restore moisture."

You can substitute gouda with fontina or even a sharp cheddar for a punchier flavor. Coconut milk stands in for heavy cream for dairy-free or lighter options, but full-fat is key to avoid thin, watery results. Herbs de Provence can be replaced with a mix of oregano, thyme, and rosemary—whatever’s on hand. Pay attention to quantity; less seasoning risks blandness. Use kosher salt, finer salts pack more punch so measure accordingly. Parmesan topping is optional but recommended for that extra sharp, crispy finish.
