ingredients:
- "1 cup softened unsalted butter"
- "2/3 cup light brown sugar"
- "1/3 cup granulated sugar"
- "1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1 teaspoon ground cinnamon"
- "1/4 teaspoon ground cloves"
- "1/4 teaspoon ground ginger"
- "1/2 teaspoon sea salt"
- "2 cups steel-cut oats"
- "1 cup dried cherries"
instructions:
- "=== Prep oven and baking surface ==="
- "Set oven to 345°F. Line cookie sheets with parchment or silicone mats to prevent burning; butter content means sticky edges otherwise."
- "=== Dry mix first ==="
- "Whisk flour, baking soda, cinnamon, cloves, ginger, and salt in a medium bowl until homogenous. Even spice distribution means no biting into a lone clove chunk."
- "=== Cream butter and sugars ==="
- "Use stand or hand mixer on medium speed. Butter soft but from fridge, not melted. Beat 3-4 minutes until light, fluffy, and pale. Granular sugar texture vanishes into creamy gloss—don’t rush or fingers catch gritty dough later."
- "=== Eggs and vanilla join ==="
- "Add eggs one at a time, fully integrating each before next. Pour vanilla in last. Mixer speed low; avoid splatter, dough slick but stable here."
- "=== Add dry to wet gradually ==="
- "Fold in thirds with rubber spatula or mixer low speed, just until no flour streaks. Overmix toughens cookies."
- "=== Sturdy oats and fruit ==="
- "Gently fold steel-cut oats and chopped dried cherries by hand. Avoid tearing fruit too much; spread them evenly. Steel-cut oats hold chew; will feel coarse in batter but soften during bake."
- "=== Scoop spacing and batch control ==="
- "Use 1 1/2 tablespoons per cookie. Drop dough balls at least 2 1/2 inches apart or they merge. Between scoops, chill dough 15-20 minutes if softening. Prevents premature spreading at bake time."
- "=== Bake cues ==="
- "8-13 minutes in center rack. Watch edges for light golden browning; centers should look moist and puffed but not raw. Insert toothpick near edge to confirm interior still soft. Bake too long—cookies dry, oats tough."
- "If wanting oat-topped look, sprinkle surface with reserved oats at 9 minutes. Crisp up top with visual contrast."
- "=== Cool and store ==="
- "Let cool on sheet 5 minutes, then transfer to wire rack to avoid soggy bottoms. Store airtight max one week at room temp; stale faster in fridge. Freeze dough balls up to 3 months; bake from frozen adding 2-3 min to time."
