ingredients:
- "4 tablespoons peanut butter, creamy or crunchy"
- "3 tablespoons light brown sugar, packed"
- "2 tablespoons powdered sugar"
- "1/4 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "70 mini pretzel rings"
- "---"
- "110 grams bittersweet chocolate, chopped"
- "2 teaspoons unsalted butter"
- "1 tablespoon salted caramel sauce (optional, for drizzling after chocolate sets)"
instructions:
- "Cover a baking tray with parchment paper or wax paper. Use a rimmed tray if possible; stops sliding around later."
- "Lay out every pretzel ring in neat rows on your tray; easiest to grab when assembling. Set aside for now."
- "In a medium bowl, combine peanut butter, light brown sugar, powdered sugar, salt, and vanilla. Use a hand mixer or sturdy spoon; beat until ingredients come together but mixture stays crumbly, kind of like damp sand. The salt cuts sweetness, balancing flavors."
- "Test the mix by pinching some between fingers; it should almost form a dough ball, sticking to itself but not hands. If too dry or crumbly, work in an extra tablespoon peanut butter, one at a time. Avoid overworking or it gets greasy."
- "Measure filling by teaspoon, scoop into hands, and roll into compact mini balls. Not sticky, but holds shape when pressed."
- "Sandwich each peanut butter ball between two pretzel rings, pressing gently but firmly to adhere without breaking the pretzels. Rows make this quicker; keep fingers clean to avoid residue."
- "Chill the assembled pretzel nuggets in fridge for roughly 1 hour or until firm but not frozen hard. You want filling set enough to handle while dipping."
- "Meanwhile, melt bittersweet chocolate and butter together. Use microwave in 20-second intervals, stirring each time. Watch closely; chocolate should be silky smooth, no lumps, glossy sheen forms when stirred. Alternatively, melt in a double boiler on low heat."
- "Remove nuggets from fridge; they should give slight resistance pressing but stay solid. Using two forks or toothpicks, dip each into melted chocolate, fully coating or half dipping for contrast."
- "Shake off excess chocolate gently and place nuggets back on parchment paper. This prevents sticking."
- "Optional: after chocolate sets, drizzle lightly with salted caramel sauce using a piping bag or spoon for an extra flavor layer."
- "Return tray to fridge for about 25-30 minutes until chocolate shell hardens completely, dulls visually, and snaps when tapped gently."
- "Once set, keep chilled until serving. Store leftovers tightly covered in cool place or fridge to avoid condensation."
- "Look for even, thin coats on chocolate. Lumps or clumps mean chocolate cooled before dunking—reheat gently in short bursts. Avoid water contact with chocolate; will seize or grain."
- "If pretzels break during assembly, use halves to patch with more filling or save for snacking; nothing wasted."
