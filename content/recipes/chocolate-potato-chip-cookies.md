ingredients:
- "4 oz kettle-cooked potato chips, divided"
- "6 oz dark chocolate wafers or chunks"
- "1 cup unsalted browned butter, cooled slightly"
- "1/2 cup granulated sugar"
- "3/4 cup coconut sugar"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1 tablespoon vanilla extract"
- "2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1 1/2 teaspoons cornstarch"
- "1/2 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "flaky sea salt for finishing"
instructions:
- "=== Prepare chocolate covered chips ==="
- "Crush 3 cups of potato chips roughly. Not dust, but small shards. Leave some bigger chunks for texture contrast."
- "Melt chocolate slowly in microwave stirring every 20 seconds, watching so it doesn’t seize or burn. Or double boiler at low simmer; patience here helps smooth melting."
- "Pour melted chocolate directly into bag with chips. Seal tight and shake aggressively yet gently — all chips should be evenly coated."
- "Spread chips out thinly on parchment-lined tray. Pop into fridge for 25 minutes until firm but not brittle. Watch for condensation; moisture dulls finish."
- "=== Make cookie dough and bake ==="
- "Lower oven to 320°F. Line baking sheets with parchment paper. Let browned butter cool enough to not cook eggs later but still fluid."
- "Beat browned butter with granulated and coconut sugar until creamy but no excessive fluffiness; you want structure without over-aeration."
- "Add eggs one at a time. Mix well after each addition. Fold in vanilla extract."
- "In separate bowl, whisk flour, baking soda, cornstarch, salt thoroughly to prevent clumps or uneven leavening."
- "Add dry mix gradually. Stir until just incorporated, no overmixing; overworking gluten makes tough cookies."
- "Gently fold in 2 cups of chilled chocolate covered chips. Save remaining for topping."
- "Measure dough with tablespoon, spacing balls 2 1/2 inches apart to allow spreading without merging."
- "Bake 13 to 16 minutes. Edges start turning light golden brown, centers still soft and look underdone. Tap tops lightly; should spring back slowly."
- "Immediately after removing, use round cutter or glass rim pressed gently around edges to shape perfectly round cookies before they harden."
- "While cookies still warm, press a few reserved chocolate chip clusters on top and sprinkle flaky sea salt. Adds crunchy finish and flavor pop."
- "Let cool on sheet 5 minutes before transferring to wire rack to finish setting completely."
- "Store airtight. Chips may soften slightly but will keep texture if sealed well."
