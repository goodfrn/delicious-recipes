ingredients:
- "10 ounces dried egg noodles"
- "3 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "4 cloves garlic minced"
- "1 small white onion diced"
- "2 stalks celery diced"
- "1 cup frozen peas"
- "1 cup diced carrots"
- "1 cup green beans cut into 1-inch pieces"
- "1 teaspoon dried thyme"
- "3/4 teaspoon salt"
- "1/2 teaspoon black pepper freshly ground"
- "3 tablespoons all-purpose flour"
- "2 cups low sodium chicken broth"
- "1 1/4 cups whole milk"
- "1 (10-ounce) can condensed cream of chicken soup"
- "1 (10-ounce) can condensed cream of celery soup"
- "4 cups shredded rotisserie chicken"
- "1 cup crushed buttery crackers or bread crumbs optional"
- "2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley for garnish"
instructions:
- "Heat oven 360 degrees Fahrenheit. Grab 9x13 pan. Butter or spray if unsure stuck."
- "Fill large deep pot with water, salt liberally. Bring to full boil. Drop in noodles. Stir immediately to prevent clumping. Taste noodles around 5-6 minutes, aiming for bite but not raw center. Drain, toss with tiny splash oil or butter so they don’t glue together. Set aside."
- "Pull skin off rotisserie bird, shred meat by hand or forks. Chunk size matters here, not too fine. Toss loosely in bowl."
- "Melt butter in wide skillet on medium heat. Listen for gentle sizzle, not burn. Add garlic, onion, celery. Stir often. Vegetables should soften, smell sweet and aromatic after 3-5 minutes. If onions start browning, lower heat. No char needed; just soften."
- "Add frozen peas, diced carrots, green beans. Stir in. Heat will wake them gently. Sprinkle thyme, salt, pepper. Toss so evenly coated."
- "Sprinkle flour over veggies. Stir to coat without lumps forming. This dusting is your thickening base; cook flour 2-3 minutes so raw taste cooks off but no color change. If clumps appear, press and stir."
- "Slowly pour broth into pan while stirring, let sauce blend smoothly. Add milk next. Bring mixture to soft simmer, small bubbles breaking on the surface. Stir frequently to prevent sticking or lumps. Simmer 4-6 minutes until sauce thickens and veggies look tender but firm."
- "Whisk in condensed cream of chicken and celery soups. Combine until velvety, no streaks. Remove from heat promptly to avoid curdling or scorching."
- "Add drained noodles into baking dish first. Layer shredded chicken on top. Pour vegetable cream sauce evenly over chicken and noodles. Stir all together gently but thoroughly. Scrape bottom to incorporate any flavorful bits stuck to pan sides."
- "Top with crushed crackers or breadcrumbs if you want crunch. Gives crusty texture contrast after baking. Skip this if soft preferred."
- "Bake uncovered 25-30 minutes. Look for bubbling edges, golden crumbs. Insert fork midway to check internal temperature and texture. The smell should fill kitchen with cozy warmth, and edges slightly crisp. Overbaking risks dryness; keep watch."
- "Sprinkle with fresh parsley before serving. Bright green color amping freshness in appearance and aroma."
- "For substitutions: if no cream of celery soup, use another cream soup like mushroom or make quick roux with extra milk and chicken bouillon. Frozen veg can swap for fresh, but add slightly earlier in sauce step. Rotisserie chicken can be replaced with leftover roasted or cooked chicken breast or thighs sliced or shredded."
