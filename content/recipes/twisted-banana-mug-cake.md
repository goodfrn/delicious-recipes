ingredients:
- "approximately 3/4 ripe banana mashed fine, no lumps"
- "2 teaspoons granulated sugar"
- "1 tablespoon melted coconut oil"
- "2 tablespoons milk (dairy or plant based)"
- "1/4 teaspoon almond extract"
- "3 tablespoons rolled oats ground roughly, substitute part flour"
- "1/4 teaspoon baking powder"
- "a pinch fine sea salt"
- "1 tablespoon chopped pecans"
- "extra pecans and peanut butter for topping"
instructions:
- "Roughly mash banana in a wide 12 to 14 oz mug until no clumps remain. Banana quantity matters: too much = soggy mess. Aim below 1/2 cup mashed volume."
- "Add sugar, coconut oil, milk, and almond extract directly to the mug. Stir with the same utensil used for mashing. Keeps cleanup easy and integrates flavors early."
- "Next, sprinkle the oats, baking powder, and salt on top. Stir gently, scraping bottom and sides with a spoon or fork. Avoid aggressive mixing to prevent tough texture—just enough to moisten dry bits."
- "Fold in pecans last. They add crunch and shift mouthfeel away from excess softness."
- "Microwave initial 60 seconds. Listen for soft bubbling sounds, look for fine bubbles rising around edges but top still wet."
- "Continue cooking in short spurts of 10 to 15 seconds, checking after each. Stop when top turns slightly shiny but still matte. Toothpick or skewer inserted should come out with moist crumbs, not wet batter."
- "Avoid microwaving past this stage. Overcooked mug cakes go rubbery fast."
- "Let cool 1-2 minutes before adding extra pecans and a drizzle of peanut butter. Warm mug releases nutty aroma, peanut butter melts just right."
- "Dive in immediately. Warm, slightly sticky, nutty, sweet but balanced with salt."
