ingredients:
- "3 tablespoons olive oil"
- "1 medium onion, diced fine"
- "4 cloves garlic, minced"
- "1 (28-ounce) can fire-roasted crushed tomatoes with juice"
- "1 (14-ounce) can diced tomatoes with juice"
- "1 teaspoon salt"
- "½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper"
- "1 teaspoon dried oregano (or 1 tablespoon fresh chopped)"
- "1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar"
instructions:
- "Heat olive oil in heavy bottomed pot over medium heat. Watch for shimmering surface – oil ready. Toss in onions evenly. Stir occasionally. After 8 to 12 minutes, onions will soften and become translucent. You want gentle sizzle not brown. If edges darken, lower heat. Onions turning glassy signals right stage. Saves raw bite but keeps body."
- "Add minced garlic and stir continuously. Garlic should smell pungent and aromatic within 45 seconds to 1 minute. Don’t let it brown, that bitterness kills the base here. Watch closely, smell is your guide."
- "Pour in fire-roasted crushed tomatoes with juice, then add diced tomatoes. The combo gives texture and smoky brightness. Add salt, pepper, and oregano now. Stir well, scraping bottom to mix in all browned bits from onions and garlic."
- "Bring the mixture up to a lively simmer – constant gentle bubbling around edges of pot. Not a rolling boil that evaporates too fast. Adjust heat lower if sauce boils vigorously. Simmer uncovered for 35 to 40 minutes. Watch sauce reduce and thicken. It will darken slightly and develop a complex aroma, richer and less sharp than when raw."
- "Five to ten minutes before finishing, taste for salt and acidity. If too acidic or sharp, splash in balsamic vinegar. It tames the bite without sweetness that would throw off balance. Stir and let blend briefly."
- "Use wooden spoon to check consistency. Sauce should coat back of spoon thickly but still pour slowly. If too thick, splash small water or broth, stir and simmer more. Too thin? Raise heat briefly to concentrate."
- "Take off heat. Let rest 5 minutes before tossing with pasta or layering into dishes."

Garlic varies: fresh is best - canned minced or paste shifts texture and flavor profile, use sparingly to avoid overpowering.
