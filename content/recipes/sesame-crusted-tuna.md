ingredients:
- "8 oz fresh tuna steaks"
- "3 tbsp hulled hemp seeds"
- "3 tbsp poppy seeds"
- "2 tbsp vegetable oil or grapeseed oil"
- "1/2 cup Greek yogurt"
- "1 tsp wasabi powder"
- "1/4 tsp fine sea salt"
instructions:
- "=== For the Tuna ==="
- "Scatter the hulled hemp seeds and poppy seeds on a wide, shallow plate. Press both sides of the tuna steaks firmly into the seeds; this builds your crust. The sheen from the tuna helps adherence. No oil needed on fish here."
- "Heat vegetable oil over medium heat in a skillet. It should shimmer but not smoke. Drop the tuna in. Listen for a steady sizzle, a sign the crust will crisp without burning. Sear 3-6 minutes total, flipping once gently with tongs. Thickness matters: 1-inch thick sticks to lower time around 3 minutes per side. Look for a golden crust with a tactile firm—but still giving—center. Avoid well done; dry, chalky."
- "Lift the tuna, place on a warm plate. Rest 4-5 minutes, juices redistribute. Cut into thick slices, crosswise, to reveal a moist pink center with the nutty crust hugging edges."
- "=== Wasabi Yogurt Sauce ==="
- "In a chilled bowl, stir Greek yogurt with wasabi powder and sea salt. Whisk briskly to blend but not turn runny. Chill till needed. This sauce has a tang and a kick without collapse, lighter than cream but still rich."
- "=== To Serve ==="
- "Dollop sauce atop tuna or on the side for dipping. Serve immediately. Fresh green garnish like sliced scallions or microgreens work well, adds brightness."
- "Cleanup note: If seeds stick to pan, soak immediately in hot water to loosen. Scrape residues carefully to preserve skillet surface."
