ingredients:
- "1 (21-ounce) blueberry pie filling or fresh blueberries with ⅓ cup sugar and 1 tablespoon lemon juice"
- "8 ounces cream cheese, softened"
- "⅓ cup Greek yogurt (sub sour cream for traditional tang)"
- "½ cup powdered sugar"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1 box (about 15.25 ounces) biscuit baking mix or yellow cake mix"
- "½ cup unsalted butter, very cold, sliced thin"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 340°F. Grease a 9×13-inch pan with non-stick spray."
- "Spread blueberry layer evenly across bottom. If using fresh, mix with sugar and lemon first."
- "In a medium bowl, beat cream cheese, powdered sugar, Greek yogurt, and vanilla, until lumps disappear but don’t overmix—overwhipping can heat mix and melt it."
- "Drop spoonfuls of the cream cheese mixture over the berry base. Not perfect dollops; rustic is fine."
- "Sprinkle the biscuit mix uniformly. Don’t clump. Work fast, or it’ll absorb moisture prematurely."
- "Slice cold butter into very thin pats, cover as much surface as possible on top of the biscuit mix. Thin pats melt evenly; thicker chunks cause greasy pockets."
- "Bake 40 to 50 minutes. Look for golden, bubbly edges and a browned, crackled crust. The top should feel firm but springy to touch, not soggy or doughy."
- "Let rest at least 15 minutes before cutting. The filling stiffens up; clean slices come from patience, not knives alone."
