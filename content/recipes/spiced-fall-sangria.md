- "2 cinnamon sticks"
- "2 pears sliced thin"
- "1 orange sliced"
- "1 cup fresh cranberries"
- "750ml dry white wine"
- "2 cups pear cider"
- "1/2 cup spiced rum"
- "1 cup pomegranate juice"
- "1/4 cup fresh lemon juice"
- "club soda for serving"
- "brown sugar"
- "ground cinnamon"
instructions:
- "Scatter cinnamon sticks, pear slices, orange rounds, and cranberries into a large pitcher. Notice the colors stacking and the cinnamon fragrance — it sets the tone."
- "Pour in white wine, pear cider, spiced rum, pomegranate juice, and lemon juice. Stir gently to blend; avoid mashing fruit, you want juices to seep slowly, not blend raw flavors abruptly."
- "Cover tightly. Refrigerate minimum 5 hours, up to 19 hours max. Too long and the fruit breaks down, turns mushy — watch for a slight change in aroma; it should smell spicy and bright, not fermented."
- "Before serving, stir lightly and taste for balance. Sweetness matters; if too sharp add a splash more juice or a teaspoon honey. If too flat, bump acidity with a splash lemon juice."
- "Rim glasses by moistening rims with lemon juice. Mix brown sugar and cinnamon on a small plate. Dip glass rims lightly and set aside."
- "Serve sangria poured over ice with some fruit pieces. Drop a cinnamon stick into each glass as garnish. Add a splash of club soda for bubbles. Listen carefully to fizz; it brings life."
- "Keep extra sangria chilled but don't add soda ahead — loses carbonation."
- "If stuck without spiced rum, use light rum plus 1/2 teaspoon pumpkin pie spice infuse briefly or steep cinnamon sticks longer."
