ingredients:
- "4 boneless skinless chicken cutlets"
- "3 medium zucchini, spiralized"
- "1/2 tsp kosher salt for zoodles"
- "1 tsp garlic powder"
- "3/4 tsp salt, divided"
- "1/2 tsp black pepper, divided"
- "1/3 cup almond meal (substitute for flour)"
- "4 Tbsp unsalted butter, divided"
- "1 Tbsp avocado oil (sub for olive oil)"
- "2 Tbsp capers, drained"
- "Juice of 2 lemons plus zest of 1 lemon"
- "1/3 cup dry vermouth (sub for white wine)"
- "2 garlic cloves minced"
- "1 Tbsp cornstarch"
- "1 Tbsp water"
- "1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes, drained"
- "1 Tbsp fresh basil chopped"
- "1 Tbsp extra virgin olive oil (for tomatoes)"
- "Fresh parsley and lemon slices for garnish"
instructions:
- "=== For the zucchini noodles ==="
- "Spiralize zucchini into long ribbons. Toss noodles with 1/2 tsp salt in a strainer over a bowl. Let sit 15-20 minutes. Squeeze gently to remove liquid; avoid crushing or noodles get mushy. Set aside dry zoodles."
- "=== For the chicken ==="
- "Season chicken cutlets both sides with garlic powder, 1/2 tsp salt, and 1/4 tsp pepper. Dredge evenly in almond meal, shaking off excess. Almond meal browns faster; watch closely."
- "Heat 2 Tbsp butter and 1 Tbsp avocado oil in large skillet over medium-high. Oil with high smoke point allows hotter sear. When pan shimmers, add chicken cutlets, no crowd. Sear 2.5-3 minutes per side until golden brown crust forms. Slight pink inside is fine here. Remove chicken, rest on warm plate."
- "Lower heat to medium-low. Into same pan add capers, lemon juice, lemon zest, vermouth, and garlic. Scrape browned bits with spatula. Whisk remaining 2 Tbsp butter into sauce. Slurry time: mix cornstarch and water well, whisk into simmering sauce until noticeably thickened with glossy sheen. Smells bright, tangy, buttery."
- "Return chicken to pan. Spoon sauce over each piece. Cover pan with lid, simmer gently on low heat 12-15 minutes. Use instant thermometer to check for internal temp 165°F. Sauce reduces lightly, coats chicken. Do not let boil too hard or butter separates."
- "=== For the zoodles ==="
- "Pat dry zoodles gently again if needed. Heat nonstick skillet over medium heat. Add zoodles, toss for 2-3 minutes to warm through without releasing water or softening too much. Look for just wilted, moist but firm. Overcooking = soggy mess."
- "=== For the tomatoes (optional) ==="
- "Empty diced tomatoes into small saucepan. Add basil, pinch salt and pepper, 1 Tbsp olive oil. Heat just until bubbles appear around edges, about 4 minutes. Not a boil; keep fresh bright flavor to spoon over zoodles and chicken."
- "=== To serve ==="
- "Plate zoodles first, top with chicken cutlets spooned with sauce. Add warm tomatoes if using. Garnish with fresh parsley and thin lemon slices for aroma and contrast."
- "=== Notes & tips ==="
- "Zoodles often soggy if excess water not released. Salt and strain key. Almond meal browns quicker than flour; watch crust closely. Avocado oil endures high heat better than olive oil. Vermouth adds subtle herbaceous note; white wine fine substitute. Don’t skip lemon zest, it lifts brightness in sauce. Sauce thickens with cornstarch slurry, never add slurry to cold sauce — lumps. Rest chicken after sear to avoid tough texture. Timings flexible, always check doneness visually and by feel."
