ingredients:
- "1 cup long-grain white rice"
- "1 ¾ cups water or low sodium chicken broth"
- "½ teaspoon fine salt"
- "1 teaspoon coconut oil or unsalted butter"
instructions:
- "===Rinse and Prep==="
- "Place dry rice in fine mesh strainer. Rinse under cold running water stirring gently with fingers for about 90 seconds until water runs mostly clear. This washes away surface starch which causes gummy texture later."
- "===Combine Ingredients==="
- "Transfer rice to medium-heavy saucepan. Add water or broth and salt along with chosen fat. Stir once just to combine everything evenly dispersed."
- "===Heat and Simmer==="
- "Set pan over high heat. Watch closely for rapid boil with bubbles aggressively breaking surface. Noise level increases. Once raging boil hits, immediately turn heat down low so surface barely shivers, not roiling. Cover tightly with lid. Steam trapped inside cooks evenly."
- "===Simmer Time==="
- "Leave undisturbed for 15-22 minutes. Pan thickness and heat variability make exact time float. No lid lifting or stirring during this phase—breaks seal and lets steam escape."
- "===Rest off Heat==="
- "After simmering, remove from burner but keep lid on. Rest rice for 7 minutes—time for trapped steam to finish cooking and plump individual grains without drying out."
- "===Fluff and Serve==="
- "Peel back lid carefully with a quick tilt to avoid hot steam burns. Gently separate grains using fork by light teasing motions. Avoid smashing or mixing vigorously, preserves fluffy texture and separates grains."
- "===Common Fixes and Tweaks==="
- "If rice's too sticky next time, lower water by ¼ cup or rinse longer. Too dry or undercooked add 2-3 tablespoons water and cover again for a few minutes. Use broth or coconut milk for extra flavor dimension when butter feels ordinary. Avoid stirring mid-cook to keep textures distinct."
