ingredients:
- "1.5 cups heavy cream"
- "1 cup whole milk (or oat milk for dairy-free)"
- "2 teaspoons vanilla bean paste or extract"
- "2/3 cup granulated sugar"
- "3 tablespoons arrowroot powder"
- "1/3 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "3 large egg yolks"
- "1 whole large egg"
- "1 tablespoon unsalted butter (or bourbon as alternative)"
instructions:
- "Mix cream and milk with vanilla bean paste in a medium heavy-bottom saucepan. Heat over low-medium flame. Watch edges carefully — gentle bubbles forming, not roaring boil. If steam rises and bubbles ripple near the sides, good; no bigger bubbles in center."
- "Whisk sugar, arrowroot powder, salt in a large bowl until homogeneous. Add egg yolks plus whole egg. Whisk hard until pale yellow, ribbons form; ensures smooth mix, no lumps."
- "Very slowly pour warm milk mixture into eggs while whisking low speed or by hand. Keeps eggs from scrambling. Avoid aerating too much — foam not wanted. Blend fully but gently."
- "Return combined liquid to saucepan. Medium-low heat now. Constant whisking mandatory. Listen for quiet simmer sounds, watch thickening around edges. Custard clings thickly to spoon back — the ‘nap’ stage. Takes 4-6 minutes, not rushed."
- "Pull from heat instantly when thick. Stir in butter or bourbon, melting all the way. Adds richness or smoky complexity. "
- "Transfer custard to clean bowl. Press plastic wrap directly on surface — crucial step. Skipping this? Skin forms, annoying and gritty."
- "Cool to room temperature briefly, then refrigerate min 4 hours. Overnight best for firmness. Bubbles vanish on chilling, custard sets solid but creamy."
- "To serve, whip lightly or fold air in for lighter texture. Perfect for tarts, parfaits, or spooning on fruit."
