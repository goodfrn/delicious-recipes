ingredients:
- "8 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "5 cups regular marshmallows"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1/2 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "1 cup creamy peanut butter"
- "5 cups rice crispy cereal"
- "2 cups mini marshmallows"
- "flaky sea salt for garnish"
- "optional melted dark chocolate for topping"
instructions:
- "Line or lightly grease a 9×9 pan with nonstick spray or parchment paper; set aside."
- "Melt butter in a large light-bottomed pan over medium heat. Foam will form; watch closely as foam fades and tiny brown specks appear. Stir constantly scraping bottom to prevent burning these solids. Smell nutty, chestnut brown? Stop. This browning is flavor gold; don’t rush or burn!"
- "Remove from heat. Toss in 5 cups marshmallows, stir until just melted and glossy. Remove pan from heat completely before next step."
- "Add vanilla and salt; stir well. Then mix in peanut butter until uniform. Peanut butter adds richness but don’t overheat or it’ll separate."
- "Fold in rice crispy cereal with a wooden spoon or spatula. Sticky and resistant—keep at it but don’t mash or compact."
- "Add 2 cups mini marshmallows last; stir gently to leave patches intact; those little clouds of sweetness pop in the bite."
- "Transfer mixture to prepared pan. Use the back of the butter wrapper or greased spatula to press gently. Avoid squeezing hard or treats turn dense and chewy rather than airy crisp."
- "Sprinkle flaky sea salt over the top while warm or drizzle melted dark chocolate for bitter balance and finesse."
- "Let cool uncovered at room temperature 30 to 60 minutes, if longer cover loosely to avoid drying out. Once set, lift bars using parchment edges and cut neatly."
- "Store airtight at room temperature up to 3 days or refrigerate if warmer kitchen. Mini marshmallow pockets keep texture alive; deficit means dense bars."
