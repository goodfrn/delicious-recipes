ingredients:
- "10 large eggs"
- "½ cup ricotta cheese, whole milk"
- "¼ cup plain Greek yogurt (substitute for cottage cheese)"
- "½ teaspoon kosher salt"
- "¼ teaspoon freshly cracked black pepper"
- "12 English muffin halves"
- "12 slices Swiss cheese (instead of American)"
- "1 pound cooked ham or turkey bacon, fully cooked"
- "Nonstick cooking spray or parchment paper for greasing pan"
instructions:
- "=== Prep the dish ==="
- "Set oven to 345F. Grease a 9x13 inch metal baking pan generously with nonstick spray; parchment lining optional but keep it tight for even heat. Metal pans speed heat through; reduce bake time slightly. Glass pans hold heat longer, increasing bake time."
- "=== Prepare egg mix ==="
- "Crack eggs into high-power blender. Add ricotta and Greek yogurt instead of cottage cheese; use these for creaminess and lasting moisture. Season salt and pepper. Blend on medium until combined but not foamy. Avoid over-blending; foam creates air bubbles leading to dry texture."
- "=== Bake the eggs ==="
- "Pour egg blend evenly into prepped pan. Place in oven center rack. Listen for quiet bubbling from edges, watch center—edges firm up first, center will join soon. Total bake time 20 to 23 minutes depending on pan. Check with instant thermometer; aim for 158F minimum internal. Remove before the surface browns and cracks; cover loosely with foil if top speeds darkening."
- "=== Cook meat separately ==="
- "While eggs cook, prep your protein. Use cooked ham slices warmed through or turkey bacon crisped on stovetop. These add less grease and salt than sausage patties. If using frozen sausage and planning to freeze the sandwiches, skip pre-cook—cook only before eating if assembling fresh."
- "=== Cool and cut ==="
- "Pull eggs from oven. Let them sit five minutes to finish setting; cutting hot eggs causes crumbles. Cut into 12 squares or use a 3 inch biscuit cutter for circles. Circles yield fewer portions but better English muffin fit. Save scraps—break into breakfast scrambles or burritos. Do not discard."
- "=== Assemble sandwiches ==="
- "Lightly toast English muffins; dry muffins soak up moisture better. Layer egg piece, then meat, top with Swiss cheese slice. Close sandwich. If eating immediately, heat in oven at 345F for 5 minutes to meld cheese and warm through. Avoid microwaving now; cheese might turn oily or rubbery."
- "=== Wrap and store ==="
- "Wrap each sandwich tightly in parchment paper or wax paper. For freezer storage, add tight plastic wrap outside then wrap again in foil to prevent freezer burn. Label with protein type and date. Refrigerate up to 5 days, freeze up to 8 weeks."
- "=== Reheat instructions ==="
- "Thaw overnight in fridge if frozen. No time? Let rest 30 minutes at room temp. To reheat quickly, microwave wrapped in damp paper towel 90 seconds to 2 minutes, check for heat. For crunch and flavor retention, reheat in toaster oven or conventional oven wrapped in foil at 345F for 15-18 minutes. Avoid over-toasting English muffin externally to maintain tenderness internally."
- "=== Serving suggestions ==="
- "Add avocado slices, hot sauce, or fresh microgreens for crunch and acid. Pickled red onions add brightness and cut grease from meat. Makes mornings less frantic, allows tweaks with whatever leftovers or ingredients on hand."
