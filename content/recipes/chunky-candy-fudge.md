ingredients:
- "1 1/4 cups brown sugar"
- "1/2 cup unsalted butter"
- "3/4 cup heavy cream"
- "3/4 cup dark cocoa powder"
- "1 cup peanut butter chips"
- "1 cup chopped walnuts"
- "1 tsp vanilla extract"
instructions:
- "Line a 9-inch square pan with parchment paper, overlap edges to form a sling for easy fudge removal."
- "Combine brown sugar, butter and heavy cream in a heavy-bottomed saucepan. Stir constantly over medium heat until boiling hard. Listen for the rolling roar of the boil, that’s your cue to start timing."
- "Simmer and stir for roughly 8-12 minutes, watching the thermometer hit about 235°F. If no thermometer, test by dropping a tiny bit into cold water—it should form a soft, pliable ball."
- "Remove from heat immediately to prevent scorching or grainy fudge. Add dark cocoa powder and peanut butter chips while still hot. Stir briskly until velvety and melted smooth (chips melt slower, don’t rush)."
- "Fold in walnuts and vanilla extract. Nuts provide texture; don’t skip unless allergic."
- "Pour the thick mixture into the prepared pan, spreading evenly with a spatula. Tap pan gently to settle and remove air bubbles."
- "Let cool on the counter until firm but not rock solid, about 3-4 hours, or refrigerate to speed up."
- "Use parchment sling to lift fudge out carefully. Cut into 1-inch squares with a sharp knife warmed in hot water and dried to clean cuts without smearing."
- "Store air-tight at room temperature for up to 2 weeks. Refrigerate if your kitchen’s warm to prevent melting."
