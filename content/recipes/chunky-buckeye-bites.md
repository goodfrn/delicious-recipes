ingredients:
- "1 cup smooth peanut butter"
- "5 tablespoons unsalted butter softened"
- "1/2 teaspoon pure vanilla extract"
- "1/4 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "1 cup powdered sugar sifted"
- "2 tablespoons honey"
- "10 ounces dark chocolate chips"
- "1 tablespoon unsalted butter"
- "1/2 teaspoon espresso powder optional"
instructions:
- "1. Combine peanut butter, honey, softened butter, vanilla, salt in a medium bowl. Beat on medium-high speed until creamy and light. Look for a fluffier texture — it should hold soft peaks but not be greasy or oily feeling. Helps dissolve sugar quicker later."
- "2. Gradually stir in powdered sugar, spoon by spoon. Don’t dump all at once — slows mixer, causes dust clouds, clogs blades. Mix until thick, dough-like, no longer sticky but pliable. The secret is slow addition for texture control. The dough should hold shape not stick to fingers."
- "3. Strap your hands with a little powdered sugar before rolling. Scoop out roughly 1 tablespoon portions. Roll into uneven little balls, a hint of irregularity adds homemade charm. Lay each on a parchment-lined tray. Slide tray into freezer for 25 minutes or until firm but not rock solid — cold but yielding to gentle poke."
- "4. While chilling, melt chocolate chips with butter in a double boiler on low heat. Stir slowly, constant gentle swirl. Don’t rush, overheating changes texture and robs gloss. Should look shiny and velvety. Add espresso powder here for complexity — optional but cuts sweetness."
- "5. Retrieve peanut butter balls. Insert toothpicks gently—avoid breaking edges. Dip halfway or just over center slowly, dragging off excess chocolate on edges of bowl. Hold over bowl to drip excess. Place each dipped ball chocolate side down onto parchment sheet. The chocolate should set matte, not glossy but firm to touch."
- "6. Refrigerate for 50-70 minutes or until chocolate is solid layered, not tacky. Check by lightly pressing—should snap softly, no smudge. If fridge is noisy or space tight, cover loosely to prevent condensation on chocolate. Best kept chilled until served."
- "7. Store leftovers in airtight container between parchment layers. Freezer-safe for quick fix but thaw in fridge to avoid sweat spots on chocolate."
