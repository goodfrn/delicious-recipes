ingredients:
- "1.5 pounds lean ground beef"
- "8 ounces Velveeta cheese, cut into cubes"
- "1 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "1 teaspoon dried thyme"
- "1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce"
- "20 slices rye crispbread or pumpernickel bread Alternative: naan bread or artisan crackers"
- "Salt optional"
instructions:
- "Heat skillet over medium-high. Crumble beef as it hits hot pan. No crowding; brown fully. Sear edges first, then break apart. When no longer pink, drain fat well — must be dry not greasy back in pan."
- "Return beef to pan. Add Velveeta cubes. Stir gently over low heat. Cheese melts slowly, coats beef. Add garlic powder, dried thyme, Worcestershire sauce. Stir to distribute evenly. Notice thickening, small bubbles signaling ready mix."
- "Preheat broiler or oven to 435°F (220°C) to compensate for added cheese. Arrange crispbreads on sheet tray. Spoon hot meat mixture evenly over each slice. Spread edge to edge but don’t overload; leaks burn."
- "Broil 4 to 6 minutes, watch hard. Bread should toast golden-brown, edges curled, cheese bubbly and slightly browned. Smell deep meld of toasted rye, cooked beef, melted cheese."
- "Remove, let sit minute to avoid burning tongues. Serve warm. Keeps crisp if eaten quickly; soggy if left too long."
- "Substitutions: Ground turkey or chicken for beef lowers fat but changes flavor profile and moisture—add 1 tablespoon olive oil if meat too dry. Swap Velveeta with sharp cheddar or gouda, melt time varies, watch carefully to avoid burning cheese."
- "Use garlic powder instead garlic salt if monitoring sodium. Worcestershire sauce has salt, adjust seasoning afterward by tasting."
- "For efficiency, brown meat while preparing crispbread. Drain and return to pan promptly to keep heat consistent. Dry pan well before adding cheese to prevent sticking."
- "Do not overbake; watch for bubbling cheese and just toasted bread for ideal texture."
