ingredients:
- "6 slices thick-cut bacon"
- "1 tablespoon reserved bacon fat"
- "5 ears fresh corn in husk"
- "1 cup halved cherry tomatoes"
- "1/4 cup chopped scallions"
- "1/3 cup chopped fresh cilantro"
- "1/3 cup mayonnaise"
- "1 tablespoon warm bacon fat reserved"
- "1 1/2 tablespoons fresh lime juice"
- "Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste"
instructions:
- "Cook bacon over medium heat in a heavy skillet. When edges start to crisp and fat renders with a faint sizzle, flip to cook evenly but not burn. When fully crispy but still pliable, scoop out. Drain on paper towels. Keep 1 tablespoon of fat dripping from the pan; rest of fat discarded or rendered down for other use."
- "Microwave corn in batches still husked. Use high power; about 3 to 4 1/2 minutes depending on ear size. Look for husks swelling, mild steam escaping. Let cool enough to handle but retain warmth. Slice off bottom about two rows of kernels above stem, grip silk top and slide ear free from husk and silks. Remove any stubborn silks with damp towel. Slice kernels off with sharp chef knife close to cob without scraping woodsy inner core. Yield about 4 1/2 cups kernels."
- "Whisk mayonnaise with warmed reserved bacon fat still liquid, and lime juice. Fat slightly warmed melds dressing. Lime punch wakes it up much better than lemon if you want sharper acidity."
- "In large bowl, mound corn kernels, cherry tomatoes, scallions, and cilantro. Toss in crumbled crispy bacon. Pour dressing over and toss gently but with confidence. Taste for salt and pepper; bacon and mayo handle much but still need seasoning boost."
- "Serve straight from fridge or allow to rest at room temp for 10 minutes. Corn texture changes with temperature, cooler keeps snap; warmer melds flavors faster. Both fine."
