ingredients:
- "8 tablespoons unsalted butter softened"
- "3/4 cup packed light brown sugar"
- "1 large egg"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1 teaspoon baking powder"
- "1/4 teaspoon salt"
- "1 teaspoon ground ginger"
- "1 cup peeled, diced tart apples"
- "1/2 cup chopped walnuts"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 345 F. Prepare 8x8-inch pan with nonstick spray or butter and flour; no shortcuts here or bottom sticks."
- "Start by creaming softened butter and brown sugar vigorously until blend is pale and loose. Use electric mixer if you want, but wooden spoon works fine."
- "Crack egg into bowl; add vanilla, beat thoroughly until mixture thickens, glossy sheen forms. This emulsifies fat and egg, critical for structure and moisture."
- "In separate bowl, stir together flour, baking powder, salt, and ground ginger until evenly distributed. Ginger hits differently than cinnamon; sharper, aromatic, watch doses."
- "Gradually add dry mix into wet bowl; fold gently with spatula to avoid gluten overdevelopment. You want tender chew, not tough."
- "Fold in diced apples and walnuts last. Apples add moisture pockets; use firm variety like Granny Smith to avoid mushiness. Nuts add crunch contrast."
- "Spread batter evenly into prepared pan. Smooth top with offset spatula; even layer cooks uniformly."
- "Slide into oven. Listen for quiet crackling edges, smell sweet caramelizing butter. Check at 40 minutes: edges should feel firm, center jiggle slightly but not wet. Gently press center — slight spring means done; too soft requires more time."
- "Let cool at least 20 minutes before slicing. Blondies continue to set as they cool. Cutting warm pieces? Use a sharp knife wiped clean between cuts to avoid crumb drag."
