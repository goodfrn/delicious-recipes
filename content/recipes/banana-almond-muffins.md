ingredients:
- "1 1/2 cups almond flour"
- "1/2 cup oat flour"
- "2 tablespoons ground flaxseed"
- "1 teaspoon baking powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1 teaspoon ground cinnamon"
- "1/4 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "2 ripe bananas, mashed well"
- "3 large eggs"
- "1/3 cup unsweetened almond milk"
- "1/4 cup melted coconut oil"
- "1 1/2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract"
- "3/4 cup dark chocolate chunks"
- "additional chocolate chips for topping (optional)"
instructions:
- "=== Prepare oven & tins ==="
- "Preheat oven to 435 degrees Fahrenheit. Use silicone liners or spray 12-cup muffin tin with baking spray; liners hold shape better, sprays speed release. Set aside."
- "=== Dry mix ==="
- "Combine almond flour, oat flour, flaxseed, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, salt in medium bowl. Whisk briefly to distribute leavening evenly — no clumps or uneven rise."
- "=== Wet mix ==="
- "In separate large bowl, whisk mashed banana, eggs, almond milk, melted coconut oil, vanilla until homogenous. Wins for consistent crumb and moisture distribution."
- "=== Combine ==="
- "Fold dry mixture into wet using spatula. Avoid overmixing — stops gluten development, keeps muffin crumb tender but stable. Then gently fold in chocolate chunks to scatter carefully without breaking pieces."
- "=== Divide & top ==="
- "Spoon batter evenly into liners, filling about 3/4 full for rise room. Scatter extra chocolate chips on top if bold on chocolate."
- "=== Bake phase one ==="
- "Slide into hot oven, bake 7-9 minutes until edges start puffing, surface mattifies, and you hear a faint crackling. High heat jump-starts oven spring, tightens crust early for better rise."
- "=== Reduce heat & bake phase two ==="
- "Lower oven to 345 degrees Fahrenheit. Continue baking 6-8 minutes until golden edges, slightly springy top, and toothpick test nearly clean with minimal crumbs sticking. Avoid overbake; almond flour burns easily."
- "=== Cool ==="
- "Rest muffins for 8 minutes outside oven to settle crumb and steam off excess moisture. Run thin knife along liner edges if sticking. Eats better warm but holds for 24 h in airtight container."
