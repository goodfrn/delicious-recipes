ingredients:
- "3 cups cooked shredded chicken"
- "1 cup creamy Alfredo sauce"
- "1 cup drained mixed vegetables (carrots, corn, green beans)"
- "½ cup diced red bell peppers"
- "1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves"
- "Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste"
- "2 refrigerated 9-inch pie crusts"
- "1 large egg"
- "1 tablespoon water"
- "2 tablespoons grated Parmesan cheese"
instructions:
- "Start by heating oven to 405°F. Toss shredded chicken with Alfredo and well-drained vegetables including red bell peppers. Fresh thyme folded in for earthy punch. Salt and black pepper adjusted last—remember chicken and Alfredo contain salt already; taste before assuming."
- "Take one pie crust out, unroll carefully. Press into a deep 10-inch pie plate making sure edges are smooth and no air bubbles trapped underneath; poke few holes with fork as a safeguard against soggy bottoms. Spoon filling evenly but don’t overfill; leave some margin at edges for sealing."
- "Unroll second crust, place atop gently. Seal edges tight by pinching together—do not leave gaps, or filling will leak during bake. Cut four 1-inch slits in top crust to vent steam; spacing matters to prevent cracks. If you skip this, top may bubble uncontrollably."
- "In small bowl whisk egg and water into wash. Brush thin layer across top crust surface—too much makes it soggy, too little loses color. Sprinkle Parmesan cheese evenly for a salty crusty finish. This extra layer crisps nicely, catching golden-brown hues, adding umami crunch."
- "Put pie on middle oven rack. Bake roughly 35 to 40 minutes but more importantly, watch color. Crust must turn rich golden, edges browning evenly. Filling bubbling at slits signals hot throughout. If crust browns too fast, tent foil to avoid burning while filling heats through."
- "Pull and let rest on rack for about 7 minutes. Filling firms with cooling steam, making clean slices easier without watery leaks. Too soon and filling runs; patience matters. Slice with serrated knife, lift gently. If crust feels soft, it means bake longer next time or check oven temperature accuracy."

tips:
- "Use shredded, not diced chicken. Holds together better. Mix well with Alfredo sauce. Diced can be dry, or too moist. Shredded incorporates evenly."
- "Don’t skip draining veggies. Any water ruins crust crunch. Squeeze extra moisture out with a paper towel, or let sit in a colander. Keep filling thick."
- "Adjust oven rack placement. Middle rack gives even heat, critical for crisp crust. Too low? The bottom burns. Too high? Tops may brown before inside cooks."
- "Venting is key. Cut slits in the top crust. Allow steam to escape; prevents top from bubbling over. Four cuts, 1-inch should do—keep it spaced evenly."
- "Rest post-bake. Wait before slicing. Filling needs time to set; hot pie spills everywhere. Let it cool for at least 7 minutes. Makes clean slices possible."
