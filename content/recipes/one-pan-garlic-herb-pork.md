ingredients:
- "1 1/2 lb pork tenderloin"
- "1 tsp garlic powder"
- "1 tsp onion powder"
- "1 tsp kosher salt"
- "3/4 tsp ground black pepper"
- "1 tsp dried Italian seasoning"
- "3 carrots chopped"
- "1 lb brussels sprouts halved"
- "15 fingerling potatoes halved"
- "2 tbsp red wine vinegar"
- "1 tsp smoked paprika"
- "1 tsp dried thyme"
- "1 tbsp olive oil"
- "cooking spray"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 410°F. Spray large rimmed baking sheet lightly with oil."
- "Stir together garlic powder, onion powder, kosher salt, pepper, Italian seasoning, smoked paprika, and thyme in small bowl."
- "Pat pork dry with paper towels. Rub all over with spice mixture until evenly coated. No clumps. Set aside."
- "Make a foil boat by folding long edges up at least 1 inch, leave open top. Helps trap pork juices. Place foil boat center of baking sheet."
- "In large bowl toss carrots, brussels, fingerlings with olive oil, red wine vinegar, pinch salt, and pepper until veggies glisten uniformly."
- "Nestle pork into foil boat center, arrange veggies evenly around pork but don’t crowd. Air circulation matters."
- "Slide pan into oven, immediately lower temperature to 355°F. Roast 24-28 minutes or until internal temp reads 145°F with instant read thermometer at thickest part."
- "Check veggies halfway: edges should blister and caramelize, tenderness tested by piercing with fork — slight resistance is perfect. If veggies dry out, mist with water quickly."
- "Remove from oven. Tent pork loosely with foil, let rest at least 5 minutes. Resting locks in juices so slices won’t dry out or stringy."
- "Spoon accumulated pan juices from foil boat over pork before slicing. Serve with your go-to starch or simply as is."
- "If pork finishes early, hold loosely covered out of oven - residual heat carries cook. Overcooking causes tough meat; rely on thermometer more than time."
- "Sub swaps: No fingerling potatoes? Use halved baby reds. No brussels sprouts? Broccoli florets or green beans work. No red wine vinegar? Apple cider vinegar or lemon juice adjusts acidity."
- "Keep spices ready in a small jar for quick rubs later — saves time and keeps measuring exact."
