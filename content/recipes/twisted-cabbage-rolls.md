ingredients:
- "1 medium head green cabbage"
- "1 lb ground pork"
- "1 egg"
- "3/4 cup cooked quinoa"
- "1/2 cup finely chopped yellow onion"
- "1 teaspoon kosher salt divided"
- "1/2 teaspoon freshly cracked black pepper"
- "1 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "1 14-oz can diced tomatoes"
- "1/2 cup tomato paste"
- "1 tablespoon brown sugar"
- "1 1/2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar"
- "1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce"
instructions:
- "Start by preheating oven to 355 degrees F and lightly grease a large baking dish with oil or nonstick spray; a glass or ceramic dish works best for even heat distribution."
- "Core the cabbage fully. Place the whole head into a large pot, cover with salted water (use about a tablespoon salt), bring to a boil. After 4 minutes the outer leaves should peel off easily— they will be softened but still hold shape. Remove and set aside. Keep boiling the remainder on a simmer, turning gently now and then for 12–18 minutes until next layers are pliable but not falling apart—test by bending leaf gently; it should not crack."
- "Trim out the thick central stalk from each leaf with a sharp paring knife. This prevents cracking when rolling and makes eating less fibrous."
- "In a mixing bowl combine ground pork, egg, cooked quinoa (cooled), diced onion, half teaspoon salt, pepper, and garlic powder. Mix just until uniform—overmixing packs meat and ruins texture."
- "Spoon about 2 to 3 tablespoons of filling onto the base of each leaf (near where stalk was removed). Roll tightly towards the edge, folding sides over to seal. Place seam-side down in the prepared dish—this keeps filling intact during bake."
- "In a separate bowl whisk together diced tomatoes, tomato paste, brown sugar, apple cider vinegar, Worcestershire sauce, and remaining salt and pepper. Pour evenly over the cabbage rolls—enough to cover but not drown."
- "Cover tightly with foil. Bake for roughly 90 minutes. Sauce should bubble gently without erupting, cabbage soft but holding form. You want faint caramelization at edges but no burning."
- "Remove foil last 10 minutes to allow sauce to thicken slightly and tops to dry if needed—watch closely, avoid drying out."
- "Pull from oven and let rest minimum 10 minutes. Juices redistribute; rolls firm to the touch yet tender throughout. If sauce looks too thin, swirl with a spoon and simmer on stove for a few minutes after baking."
- "Plate carefully; best eaten hot with the richness of the pork, tangy robust sauce, and the earthy chew of quinoa and cabbage visible in cross-section."
