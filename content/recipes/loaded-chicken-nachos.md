ingredients:
- "4 cups tortilla chips, spread in one layer"
- "1 cup diced bell pepper, fresh"
- "1/2 cup diced onion, raw"
- "1 cup diced tomato, seeded"
- "1 cup fire-roasted corn kernels, fresh or canned drained"
- "2 cups cooked shredded chicken, seasoned"
- "1 1/2 cups sharp cheddar cheese, shredded"
- "1/2 cup cream cheese, softened"
- "1/4 cup diced pickled jalapenos"
- "1/2 cup diced ripe mango"
- "1/2 cup sour cream for drizzling or dolloping"
- "Optional: chopped cilantro for garnish"
instructions:
- "==="
- "Step 1: Prep your baking sheet by pressing a piece of foil tightly onto the rimmed pan. Foil traps any molten cheese drips, saving cleanup grief later."
- "=== Step 2: Spread chips evenly in a single layer on the foil-lined sheet. Avoid pile-ups—overlapping chips mean uneven heating and soggy bottoms."
- "=== Step 3: Scatter diced bell pepper, onion, tomato, mango, and fire-roasted corn evenly over the chips. Sharp crunch and fruity brightness come from this layer."
- "=== Step 4: Spoon shredded chicken across, ensuring coverage. Dry chicken? Splash some lime juice or sprinkle smoky paprika for added moisture and punch."
- "=== Step 5: Dot softened cream cheese in small blobs randomly. It melts to creamy islands, replacing avocado’s richness and preventing sogginess."
- "=== Step 6: Cover liberally with shredded sharp cheddar—look for thick melting cheeses not overly processed to hold gooey texture without greasiness."
- "=== Step 7: Scatter pickled jalapenos sparingly for tang and heat contrast. Adjust to tolerance. Use gloves handling jalapeno to avoid skin irritation."
- "=== Step 8: Slide pan 4-6 inches below a preheated broiler on high. Listen for bubbling sounds, watch cheese transform from pale to golden with flecks of brown. Don’t walk away — cheese can burn within seconds."
- "=== Step 9: After 5-7 minutes under broil (depends on fridges), remove when cheese is molten and bubbly but before burning starts. Edges of chips crisp golden, cheese should pull in long strands when poked."
- "=== Step 10: Let cool 1-2 minutes to prevent burns. Dollop or drizzle sour cream across the top. Sour cream stands in for Greek yogurt here, similar tartness but richer texture."
- "=== Step 11: Garnish with cilantro if you like fresh herbal brightness. Serve immediately. Leftovers get soggier fast; refrigerate un-topped chips and toppings separately."
- "=== Step 12: Cleanup tip: foil lining means quick wipedown—no burnt cheese scrubbing. Better than baking dish for fast turnaround with repeat servings."

If fire-roasted corn unavailable, roast fresh corn kernels on stove or broil quickly—it adds smoky depth often missing from plain corn. Cream cheese replaces avocado to avoid excess moisture, which kills chip crunch; easy swap if you want richer texture or dairy alternate like mascarpone. Jalapeno pickles can be fresh sliced, but pickled adds complexity and mellows heat—handle carefully, gloves recommended if jalapeno juices ignite skin or eyes. Sharp cheddar preferred—lower quality shredded cheeses congeal greasy or fail to melt evenly. Tomato best seeded to prevent watery spills. Onion should be raw for crispness; caramelized onion would push sweetness in different direction, but not bad. Mango isn’t traditional but adds bright counterpoint to savory chicken and cheese. Fresh or frozen corn works; the key is drying off moisture before layering."
