ingredients:
- "12 large bay scallops or diver scallops"
- "flaky sea salt to taste"
- "fresh cracked black pepper to taste"
- "1 tablespoon almond oil or substitute high smoke point oil"
- "1/2 teaspoon lemon zest"
- "1/4 teaspoon red chili flakes"
instructions:
- "===Heat and prep==="
- "Heat a heavy cast iron or carbon steel pan over high flame. Wait till oil shimmers and just begins to smoke. Almond oil recommended for subtle aroma but grapeseed or avocado oil works. Avoid pooling oil; just enough to coat pan bottom thinly."
- "===Seasoning and spacing==="
- "Pat scallops dry with paper towels. Season each side with flaky sea salt and cracked pepper. Too wet and pan steams; no crust."
- "===Searing process==="
- "Arrange scallops spaced out in hot pan. Crowding traps steam and kills sear. Listen for lively sizzle immediately."
- "DO NOT flip or touch scallops for 1 1/2 to 2 1/2 minutes. The crust will form and release naturally. Peek underneath gently—mahogany crust is the goal, not dark burnt spots."
- "Flip scallops quickly but carefully with tongs or spatula. Cook opposite side same time till crust forms again. Total cook time varies but usually 3-4 minutes depending on scallop size."
- "===Finishing==="
- "Remove pan from burner. Scatter lemon zest and chili flakes over scallops while hot. Residual heat will release oils and flavors without overcooking the shellfish."
- "Let scallops rest in pan off heat for no more than 4 minutes if plating delay is needed. Make sure residual pan heat isn’t too high to avoid carryover cooking."
- "Serve immediately. Scallops toughen quickly when cooling or reheated."
- "===Tips & Troubleshooting==="
- "If scallops stick, pan or scallops not dry enough or pan wasn’t hot enough. Pat dry, preheat longer next time."
- "No almond oil? Use grapeseed, avocado, or light olive oil. Butter added post-sear adds richness but burns fast, so off heat."
- "Overcrowding leads to steaming, pale scallops; space them generously."
- "If crust forms too quickly and burns, lower heat slightly; key is balance of hot surface and oxidation timing."
- "Keep scallops the same size for even cooking and ease of timing."
