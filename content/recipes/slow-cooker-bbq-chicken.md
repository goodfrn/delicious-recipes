ingredients:
- "3 tablespoons barbecue sauce divided"
- "3 large boneless skinless chicken breasts"
- "1 loaf of slider rolls"
- "4 tablespoons unsalted butter melted"
- "2 teaspoons smoky barbecue spice rub"
- "1 tablespoon soy sauce"
- "1 1/2 teaspoons honey"
- "1/2 teaspoon Dijon mustard"
- "3 cups coleslaw mix"
- "1/2 cup dill pickle slices chopped"
- "1/3 cup Greek yogurt"
- "2 tablespoons pickle juice"
- "1 teaspoon honey"
- "1/4 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "1/4 teaspoon garlic powder"
instructions:
- "Smear 2 tablespoons barbecue sauce into the bottom of the slow cooker. Tuck chicken breasts in, douse with remaining sauce. Cover snug, cook on low. About 3 hours 55 minutes is average but poke meat—should yield easily when prodded with fork. Avoid drying out. Juice should bubble gently along edges."
- "Lift chicken out. Let cool enough to shred without burning fingers. Shred fine but not mushy—big clumps remain. Toss back into slow cooker. Stir well to coat all with sauce juices. This reabsorbs flavor—don’t skip."
- "Preheat oven to 355°F now. Spray baking dish lightly with nonstick. Cut slider rolls horizontally all the way through. Lay bottom half flat. Heap shredded chicken evenly atop. Cap with top half of rolls. Don’t press too hard—need space for glazing."
- "Whisk together melted butter, barbecue rub, soy sauce, honey, and Dijon until glossy. Pour glaze evenly over sandwich surface. Let marinate briefly or cover and refrigerate up to 20 hours—more than that, rolls get soggy. If refrigerated, bring back to room temp before baking."
- "Bake uncovered 18 to 23 minutes. Watch the top—deep golden brown with crisp edges is your cue they’re ready. Butter and honey create that shine and crunch on crust. Too pale? Bake more. Too dark? Pull immediately."
- "While baking, toss coleslaw mix and pickles in large bowl. Whisk Greek yogurt, pickle juice, honey, salt, and garlic powder until smooth. Pour over slaw, mix well. Chill till sandwiches come out. The tang and crunch cuts fattiness. Greek yogurt adds brightness, not just creaminess."
- "To serve, slice sandwiches if needed. Pile slaw generously on each slider or serve on side. Slaw should be cool, crisp with zing—not soggy or limp. If dressing separates, whisk again before serving."
- "Common pitfalls—don’t overcook chicken or it dries out. If sauce looks thin, thicken slightly with quick slurry before adding to cooker. Don’t douse rolls in sauce; glazing is key. For no slow cooker? Use Dutch oven and simmer chicken gently about 1 hour until shreddable."
