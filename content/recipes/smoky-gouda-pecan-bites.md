ingredients:
- "6 oz aged smoked Gouda cheese, shredded"
- "3 tablespoons crème fraîche"
- "1/4 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "24 large pecan halves, toasted"
- "smoked paprika for dusting"
- "Optional twist: 1 teaspoon honey mustard mixed into cheese spread"
instructions:
- "=== Mixing Spread ==="
- "Shred the smoked Gouda finely to avoid clumps. Combine in a bowl with crème fraîche and sea salt. Don’t use sour cream here — crème fraîche thickens better yet stays creamy and less tangy. Mix by hand or with paddle attachment. Texture should be creamy but hold shape, not runny. Chill in fridge 15-25 minutes. Pull out when firm yet still spreadable on your fingertip."
- "=== Preparing Pecans ==="
- "Toast pecan halves dry in a skillet over medium-low heat until fragrant. Should smell nutty with subtle smoke hints, about 3-5 minutes. Shake pan constantly to avoid burning. Let cool on parchment — warm nuts make spread too melty and fragile to build."
- "=== Assembly ==="
- "Use a scant teaspoon of the chilled Gouda spread. Dollop onto the flat side of a toasted pecan half. Top carefully with another pecan, placing bumpy textured sides outward to showcase the crunch contrast. Press slightly but don’t squeeze — keeps nuts crisp inside sandwich."
- "Dust with smoked paprika. This step is optional but elevates aroma and adds color. For a sweet-savory twist, drizzle honey mustard (mixed into spread earlier) right before serving instead of dusting."
- "=== Storage and Serving ==="
- "Chill assembled bites minimum 15 minutes to set spread. Keeps spread from sliding and pecans crisp. Serve cold or bring to room temperature 10 minutes before, which lets the cheese aroma bloom."
- "=== Troubleshooting and Tips ==="
- "If spread feels too loose after chilling, add more shredded cheese and chill again. Too thick? Stir in a bit more crème fraîche slowly. Toast pecans right before assembling to keep brittle crunch. For variety switch smoked Gouda with sharp aged cheddar, but omit smoked paprika then. Honey mustard can be swapped for fig jam for sweetness. Avoid overfilling pecans — makes handling awkward and leads to breakage."
