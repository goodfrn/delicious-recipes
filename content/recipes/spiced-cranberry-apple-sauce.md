ingredients:
- "4 cups peeled diced apples (Granny Smith or Braeburn)"
- "2 cups fresh cranberries"
- "1/4 cup pure maple syrup (sub honey or agave syrup)"
- "1 cup water"
- "1 tablespoon freshly grated ginger (sub 1/2 tsp ground ginger)"
- "1 teaspoon ground cinnamon (can swap with 1 cinnamon stick)"
- "1/2 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "Zest and juice of 1 lemon"
instructions:
- "Start medium-high heat with water, apples, cranberries, maple syrup, and ginger. Keep uncovered. You want bubbling sounds, not a roar, for evaporation; about 18-22 minutes. Watch apples swell and cranberries pop—color deepens, sauce thickens visibly."
- "Reduce heat to low. Add lemon zest, juice, cinnamon, and salt. Stir every 5 minutes so fruit breaks down evenly, no sticking to bottom. This slow simmer lets apples disintegrate gently—watch for softness, skin sloughing, no large chunks left. Total simmer now about 20-25 minutes."
- "Test texture by pressing a chunk between fingers or using a fork. If still firm, longer simmer needed. If sauce too runny, remove lid last 5 minutes for extra evaporation."
- "Mash with potato masher for chunky sauce or use immersion blender partway to create smooth base with bits left. Don’t overblend or sauce loses rustic grip and texture."
- "Serve warm with roast or chilled in airtight container. Keeps 5-7 days refrigerated. If sauce thickens too much, stir in small splash of water before reheating; regain pourability without thinning flavor."
- "Common issues: apples burning = too high heat or no stirring; add water or lower temp immediately. Cranberries too tart? Add extra maple syrup a teaspoon at a time."
- "Ingredient swaps: Pear instead of apples for juicier finish, wild blueberries or tart cherries for berry twist. Use fresh ginger for sharp bite, ground ginger for subtle warmth if raw unavailable."
