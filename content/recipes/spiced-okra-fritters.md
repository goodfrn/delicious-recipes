ingredients:
- "3 tablespoons chickpea flour"
- "1/2 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper"
- "1/4 teaspoon ground cumin"
- "1 cup fresh okra, coarsely chopped"
- "1/2 cup onion, diced"
- "1 large egg"
- "3 tablespoons plain yogurt"
- "Vegetable oil enough to cover skillet bottom"
instructions:
- "Heat oil in heavy skillet over medium heat. Enough to coat bottom thinly. Should shimmer but not smoke."
- "In a mixing bowl combine chickpea flour, salt, pepper, and cumin. Stir dry ingredients until evenly distributed."
- "Add chopped okra and diced onion into dry mixture. Toss well to coat pieces. This seals okra exterior, reducing slime."
- "In separate small bowl whisk egg and yogurt until smooth. Pour into okra mixture and stir gently until just combined. Batter will be chunky, not smooth or thin."
- "Drop about 1/4 cup scoops of batter carefully into hot oil. Flatten mound slightly with spatula to spread evenly. Don’t overcrowd skillet."
- "Listen for steady sizzle. Cook 4 to 6 minutes or until edges darken and bottoms develop rich golden-brown crust. If sizzling too aggressively and burning, lower heat slightly."
- "Flip only once using thin spatula. Press lightly to keep contact with pan. Watch for equal browning on second side, about same time as first."
- "Remove fritters to paper towels. Let drain briefly to remove excess oil. Spritz with pinch of fine salt while hot."
- "Serve warm with your choice of dipping sauce — cool yogurt raita or spicy chili mayo works well."
