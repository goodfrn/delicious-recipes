ingredients:
- "1 cup salsa verde sauce"
- "9 corn tortillas, cut in halves to fit snugly"
- "1 cup sour cream"
- "2 cups cooked shredded chicken"
- "1 cup black beans, rinsed and drained"
- "6 oz shredded pepper jack cheese"
- "1 diced poblano pepper, seeds removed"
- "1 tsp garlic powder"
- "1 tsp ground cumin"
- "1 tsp smoked paprika"
- "Optional toppings sour cream, diced avocado, green onions, hot sauce"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 450°F; hotter means faster crisp but watch carefully."
- "Spread half cup salsa verde evenly in the bottom of a 9x13 oven-safe dish."
- "Layer tortillas halves tightly, no gaps, press lightly to form stable base."
- "Mix sour cream and remaining half cup salsa verde in medium bowl; smooth, tangy mixture."
- "Combine shredded chicken, black beans, poblano, garlic powder, cumin, smoked paprika, 4 oz pepper jack cheese in large bowl. Taste—adjust seasoning with salt or chili flakes if dull."
- "Spoon half the sour cream salsa mix over tortilla layer; spread gently but fully."
- "Add chicken mixture in even 2-cup layer, press lightly to avoid gaps or floating cheese clumps."
- "Repeat layering: tortillas, remaining sour cream salsa mix, rest of chicken mixture, and final tortilla layer."
- "Finish with last 2 oz pepper jack cheese sprinkled thick over top tortillas, plus splash more salsa verde for moisture."
- "Seal loosely with foil. Bake 25 minutes covered. The hot oven bubbles sauce, smells deep toasted corn."
- "Remove foil for another 15 minutes uncovered. Cheese should bubble and edges slightly crisped; tortillas browned but not burnt."
- "If edges dull, switch to broil 4 minutes max; watch like hawk—your doorbell’s a distraction killer here."
- "Pull from oven when top is blistered, cheese molten with patches of deeply browned crust."
- "Set aside 20 minutes after baking. Rest time crucial. Slices hold shape better and layers set."
- "Slice with sharp serrated knife—clean cuts yield tidy portions, avoiding shredded mess."
- "Serve with diced avocado, green onions, sour cream dollops, and hot sauce on side."
- "Leftovers reheat well covered in microwave or oven; adds cozy flavors."
- "Common swap—rotisserie chicken saves shredding time, canned beans for speed. If too dry, splash broth between layers next time."
