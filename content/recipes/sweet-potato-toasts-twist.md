ingredients:
- "1 large sweet potato, washed"
- "1 tablespoon non-stick spray or light olive oil spray"
- "1/2 cup coconut yogurt (substitute for Greek yogurt)"
- "1/3 cup fresh blueberries"
- "1/2 kiwi, peeled and diced"
- "1/4 cup toasted pecans, chopped (substitute for granola)"
- "1 teaspoon maple syrup (substitute for honey)"
- "pinch of flaky sea salt"
instructions:
- "Wash large sweet potato thoroughly. Slice lengthwise into 3-5 mm thick pieces; aim for uniform thickness to ensure even cooking. Avoid slices too thin—they’ll crisp too fast and risk burning."
- "Preheat oven to 410 F. Lightly spray baking sheet with non-stick spray. No oil needed on slices; oil causes soggy spots instead of a dry toasty surface."
- "Arrange sweet potato slices in one layer, leaving slight gaps for air circulation. Bake 20-25 minutes, watching edges closely. Visual cue: edges start slight browning and surface looks dry but flesh remains soft to fork."
- "Remove baking sheet. Transfer sweet potato slices to toaster. Toast on medium-low 2-3 minutes to encourage caramelized edges and a crunch without burning. Keep ears open—snap or subtle crackle sounds indicate crisping."
- "Spread coconut yogurt evenly on each warm toast, bottom side up for better absorption."
- "Top with fresh blueberries and diced kiwi for juicy acidity and freshness. Blueberries burst gently, kiwi adds smooth texture contrast."
- "Sprinkle chopped toasted pecans over fruit to add crunch. Pecans have rich flavor; toasting beforehand releases oils and amplifies nuttiness. Skip pecans if nut allergy—substitute pumpkin seeds for crunch."
- "Drizzle light maple syrup over all. Sweetness balances tang, but less is more to avoid sogginess. Finish with a pinch of flaky sea salt to lift flavors and balance sweetness."
- "Serve immediately. Toast texture is key—soft inside, crisp edges; yogurt creamy, fruit juicy; nuts crunchy with maple-salt contrast. If sweet potato slices seem underdone after baking, increase oven time by 3-5 minutes, checking fork tenderness often."
