ingredients:
- "2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1/2 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "1 teaspoon baking powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1 cup unsalted butter softened"
- "1 3/4 cups granulated sugar"
- "4 large eggs at room temperature"
- "1 cup buttermilk"
- "1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract"
- "1/2 teaspoon almond extract"
- "1 teaspoon finely grated lemon zest"
- "Vegetable oil or solid vegetable shortening for pan"
- "2 tablespoons cornstarch for dusting pan"
- "1 tablespoon cinnamon sugar for dusting"
- "Fresh mixed berries for garnish optional"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven 320°F. Prepare a 10-inch fluted bundt pan: coat thoroughly with vegetable oil or solid shortening, then dust with cornstarch, tapping out excess. Cornstarch gives crisper crust than flour; helps release cleanly."
- "Whisk flour, salt, baking powder and baking soda together in a medium bowl. Baking powder added here for lift since buttermilk is less dense than sour cream. Set aside."
- "In a large mixing bowl — paddle attachment preferred. Beat butter on medium-high until it lightens pale yellow and turns fluffy like whipped cream, 4-5 minutes. This aeration is critical for structure, helps sugar dissolve."
- "Add sugar gradually, continue beating for 2 minutes. Mixture should feel lighter, almost silky. If grainy, keep beating or use fine sugar."
- "Add eggs one at a time, mixing until yolk color just disappears each addition. Scrape bowl sides every egg. Avoid overbeating eggs or batter will curdle."
- "Add buttermilk, vanilla, almond extract and lemon zest. Mix gently just to combine. Overmixing at this stage develops gluten, tough crumb."
- "Add dry ingredients in 3 additions, folding or mixing on low speed. Stop as soon as just incorporated. Some slight lumps OK. Batter will be thick but pourable."
- "Pour batter into prepared pan, smooth top with offset spatula."
- "Bake in center rack 60-70 minutes. Start checking at 60 minutes using toothpick inserted into thickest part. If it comes out with moist crumbs, done. Jiggle pan gently; middle should no longer wobble."
- "Let cake cool in pan on wire rack 15 minutes. Tap pan around edges firmly with wooden spoon: if cake sticks, repeat taps. Invert carefully onto rack, then flip right-side up to cool completely."
- "Once cooled, dust with cinnamon sugar. Garnish with fresh berries if desired to add tart contrast."
- "Store loosely covered at room temp up to 3 days or refrigerate up to 5. Warm slices briefly before serving if chilled."
