ingredients:
- "2 cups old fashioned rolled oats"
- "1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder"
- "1 teaspoon ground cinnamon"
- "1/2 teaspoon salt"
- "1/3 cup maple syrup"
- "1 1/4 cups whole milk"
- "1 cup pumpkin puree"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "2 large eggs"
- "3/4 cup chopped pecans plus reserved 2 tablespoons"
- "1/4 cup coconut sugar"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 345 F. Use a 8×8-inch dish; coat lightly with oil or butter. Avoid spray if curling edges bother you; direct fat helps browning."
- "In a large mixing bowl, stir together oats, baking powder, cinnamon, and salt. Make sure powder is evenly distributed to avoid rock pockets that mess up texture."
- "In a separate container, whisk eggs with syrup and vanilla until slightly frothy. Blend in pumpkin and milk until smooth. Thick but pourable."
- "Combine wet into dry, folding gently. Stop mixing as soon as oats moistened; overmixing toughens baked good. Fold in pecans leaving some back for topping."
- "Transfer to pan; spread level. Scatter reserved pecans evenly on top for crunch and visual appeal."
- "Bake 40 to 45 minutes. Oven temps vary so watch the edges browning, surface becoming firm with slight spring. Center should no longer jiggle but retain some softness. Insert knife - clean means done."
- "Cool 10 minutes; it continues to firm and flavors meld. Cut into squares; serve warm or room temp."
