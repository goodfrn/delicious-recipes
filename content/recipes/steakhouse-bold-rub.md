ingredients:
- "1 tablespoon kosher salt"
- "1 teaspoon smoked onion powder"
- "1 teaspoon smoked garlic powder"
- "1 teaspoon celery seed ground"
- "1/2 teaspoon black pepper freshly cracked"
- "1/2 teaspoon brown sugar packed"
- "1 teaspoon sweet paprika"
- "1/4 teaspoon ground coriander"
- "1/8 teaspoon ground cloves"
instructions:
- "Gather salts and spice powders in small bowl. Smoked powders hit scent profiles differently – swap if lacking with regular but scale back slightly to avoid burning."
- "Mix thoroughly. Break up clumps; aerate spices to release aroma."
- "Pat dry your steak. Too wet – rub slides off. Ideal: tacky surface after paper-towel dab."
- "Sprinkle rub evenly, about 3/4 tablespoon per 8 ounces beef. Press in firmly with fingertips. You want the spice to nestle in muscle fibers for flavor depth."
- "Rest rubbed steak 10 minutes at room temp for salt uptake. Rub should feel tacky, almost sticky. Not soaking or wet."
- "Store leftover rub airtight, dry, dark place. No fridge needed unless humidity very high."
- "Use tactile sense when rubbing—too light, rub won’t adhere; too heavy, sugar and salt risk oversalting or burning surface."
- "Cook steak as usual. Look for fine Maillard crust to signal rub binding. Avoid constant flipping."
- "If spices scorch during cooking, lower heat but extend time slightly—better flavor than bitter burn."
- "Adjust rub quantities if steak thicker or thinner. Less rub per thinner cut to avoid overwhelming meat."
