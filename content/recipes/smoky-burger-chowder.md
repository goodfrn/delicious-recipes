ingredients:
- "1.25 pounds ground beef or ground turkey for leaner option"
- "4 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "1 medium yellow onion diced"
- "2 medium russet potatoes peeled and diced small"
- "3 tablespoons all-purpose flour"
- "4 cups low-sodium chicken broth"
- "3 cups whole milk or swap half for half-and-half for creaminess"
- "1 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "¾ teaspoon black pepper freshly ground"
- "1 teaspoon granulated garlic or garlic powder"
- "2 cups sharp white cheddar shredded"
- "¾ cup sour cream full-fat or Greek yogurt to cut richness"
- "1 cup fresh chopped ripe tomatoes"
- "1 cup shredded iceberg lettuce"
- "8 strips bacon cooked crisp and crumbled"
- "2 large hot dog buns cut into 1-inch cubes toasted in butter until golden"
- "Optional twist fresh chopped jalapeño or smoked paprika for heat"
instructions:
- "Brown ground meat in skillet over medium-high heat. Break up with wooden spoon until no pink remains and juices start to brown. Drain excess fat. Meat should be crumbly and fragrant."
- "In heavy large pot or Dutch oven melt butter over medium heat. Toss in diced onions and potatoes. Stir often; cook until onions become translucent and potatoes take on light golden edges, about 4-6 minutes. Don’t let onions burn — medium heat, patience."
- "Sprinkle flour evenly over veggies while stirring quickly to coat all pieces. Work flour around to form a thick paste or roux. Should smell slightly nutty after 1-2 minutes — if raw flour lingers, heat longer but don’t brown roux too much or soup will taste bitter."
- "Gradually whisk in cold chicken broth and milk bit by bit. Stir to incorporate flour paste smoothly into liquid, no lumps. Bring mixture up to low simmer – bubbles should gently rise around pot edges, not a rolling boil."
- "Simmer soup uncovered stirring occasionally for about 15 minutes or until potatoes can be pierced easily with fork and consistency thickens. Adjust heat to keep gentle simmer; boiling breaks down texture."
- "Remove pot from heat. Stir shredded cheese in batches — let each handful melt fully before adding next to avoid clumping. Follow with dollops of sour cream or Greek yogurt, fold gently until creamy and uniform."
- "Fold in fresh tomato pieces and cooked ground meat, distributing evenly. These shouldn’t cook much further, just warmed. Tomatoes maintain fresh brightness, ground meat adds savory heft."
- "Serve by ladling into bowls topped with crisp shredded lettuce, crumbled bacon, and toasted bun croutons for textural contrast. Optionally sprinkle with smoked paprika or finely chopped jalapeños for smoky heat."
- "If soup thickens too much later, thin with splash of milk or broth reheating gently. Leftover croutons best stored separately to preserve crunch."
- "Cleanup tip: soak Dutch oven after use to prevent stuck roux residue from hardening."
