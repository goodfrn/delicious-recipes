ingredients:
- "=== For the crust ==="
- "1 cup rolled oats"
- "3 Tbsp brown sugar"
- "3 Tbsp melted unsalted butter"
- "Pinch sea salt"
- "=== For the filling ==="
- "14 oz canned pumpkin puree"
- "12 oz cream cheese softened"
- "3 large eggs room temperature"
- "1/2 cup Greek yogurt plain (substitute sour cream)"
- "1 1/2 tsp vanilla extract"
- "2 Tbsp arrowroot powder (substitute cornstarch)"
- "1/4 cup light brown sugar packed"
- "1/4 cup granulated sugar"
- "2 Tbsp all-purpose flour"
- "1 tsp ground cinnamon"
- "1/2 tsp ground nutmeg"
- "1/4 tsp ground ginger"
- "1/4 tsp ground allspice"
- "1/4 tsp pumpkin pie spice"
- "Pinch fine salt"
instructions:
- "=== Crust prep ==="
- "Pulse rolled oats in food processor until coarse crumbs form. No processor? Place oats in ziplock bag and crush gently with rolling pin until chunky but not powdery."
- "Add brown sugar, melted butter, sea salt. Mix until evenly moistened. Texture should hold when pressed but not greasy."
- "Line bottom of 7x3 inch springform pan with parchment paper. Spray lightly with non-stick oil to prevent sticking."
- "Press oat crust firmly into pan using bottom of glass or small cup, pack tightly and smooth edges. Chill in freezer to firm while making filling."
- "=== Filling assembly ==="
- "Combine softened cream cheese, pumpkin puree, eggs, Greek yogurt, and vanilla in mixing bowl. Beat until creamy but not whipped—avoid incorporating excess air to prevent cracks."
- "Sift together arrowroot, flour, both sugars, all spices, and salt. Add dry ingredients to wet mixture and fold gently until just combined. Smooth, homogenous batter critical for even texture."
- "Pull crust pan from freezer. Pour batter over crust evenly. Tap pan lightly to release any trapped air bubbles. This helps avoid cracks during cooking."
- "=== Instant Pot cooking ==="
- "Pour 1 cup water into bottom of Instant Pot (6 quart model)."
- "Wrap top of cheesecake pan tightly with heavy-duty aluminum foil to shield from excess moisture condensation."
- "Place trivet inside pot. Lower cheesecake on trivet carefully. Secure lid, set valve to sealing."
- "Cook on High Pressure for ~45 minutes. Avoid rushing. Internal temperature and batter set are more important than exact clock."
- "Allow natural pressure release for 45 minutes. Slow release lets cheesecake finish setting gently without sudden temperature swings."
- "Open valve to vent any residual steam manually. Remove cheesecake with trivet. Expect surface moisture—blot gently with paper towel, no vigorous wiping."
- "Set cheesecake on counter until lukewarm (about 20 minutes). Then refrigerate uncovered at least 6 hours or overnight for best structure."
- "Before serving, run thin knife edge around pan sides to loosen carefully. Remove springform ring. Garnish if desired—whipped mascarpone or toasted pecans work well."
- "=== Notes ==="
- "Oats offer nuttier flavor and more texture than graham crackers. Slight chew contrast useful here."
- "Arrowroot thickens delicately and doesn't cloud batter. Can swap back to cornstarch if unavailable."
- "Greek yogurt keeps filling tangy and less rich than sour cream, reduces risk of breakage under pressure."
- "Foil wrap is key — traps steam and avoids water droplets falling on surface, which ruins finish."
- "Loose batter air causes cracks. Beat just enough to combine ingredients smoothly but slow and steady mixing wins."
- "Watch for slight jiggle in center after cooking; if firm, done. Undercooked cheesecake collapses when chilled."
- "Instant Pot models vary — use internal doneness clues rather than hard timing. Adjust 5-7 minutes up or down if needed."
- "If top gets wet, blot and chill longer to firm up. Patience ensures clean slices later."
- "Substitute oat crust with gluten-free rolled oats for gluten-free option. Butter quantity can be adjusted to get proper bind without greasy crust."
- "If no springform, try loose-bottom tart pan lined with foil for easy removal."
- "Pressure release timing crucial. Always natural release to avoid sudden depressurization which can crack cheesecake."
- "Cheesecake is best cold and rested - don’t rush slicing. Clean sharp knife heated under warm water slices cleanly through dense pumpkin batter."
