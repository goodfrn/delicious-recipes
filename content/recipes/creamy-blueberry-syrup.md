ingredients:
- "1 cup fresh or frozen blueberries"
- "2/3 cup granulated sugar"
- "3/4 cup fresh orange juice"
- "1/3 cup full-fat coconut cream"
- "2 tablespoons ghee"
- "1/2 teaspoon pure vanilla extract"
- "1/8 teaspoon finely ground cardamom"
- "Pinch flaky sea salt"
instructions:
- "Heat blueberries, sugar and orange juice in a heavy-bottom saucepan over medium heat. Use wooden spoon to gently mangle berries as they heat, releasing juices. Watch closely: mixture turns from dull purple to bright, translucent blue with syrup running freely but not sticky. Bubbles should pop gently, not aggressively. This takes about 15 minutes but watch for visual and tactile cues over clock."
- "Lower heat to low and slowly drizzle in coconut cream while whisking briskly to avoid separation. Add ghee next in small chunks. The mixture will begin to thicken, shift from shiny to more velvety texture. Take care to keep heat steady and gentle—too hot, syrup breaks or cream curdles."
- "Stir in vanilla extract, cardamom and a pinch of flaky sea salt. The salt brightens flavors and balances fruity sweetness. Stir well but don’t overmix once salt goes in."
- "Remove from heat. Let syrup cool 7 to 12 minutes uncovered. Watch it thicken as it cools, texture moving from pourable to luscious spoonable. If too thick, whisk in splash of orange juice or coconut cream to loosen. Reheat gently if needed, never boiling after cream addition."
- "Serve warm or room temp over pancakes, ice cream or yogurt. Store leftovers covered in fridge; syrup thickens further and can be warmed to pour again."
