ingredients:
- "4 large Roma tomatoes, halved"
- "2 tablespoons olive oil divided"
- "1/2 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "1 teaspoon brown sugar"
- "1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper"
- "1 tablespoon vegetable oil"
- "1 medium yellow onion, thinly sliced"
- "4 ounces pancetta, diced"
- "3/4 cup shredded sharp white cheddar"
- "3/4 cup shredded smoked Gruyère"
- "1/2 cup crème fraîche"
- "1/4 cup fresh basil leaves, chopped"
- "1 cup cornmeal"
- "1 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1 tablespoon baking powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1/2 teaspoon salt"
- "1 cup buttermilk"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1/4 cup maple syrup"
- "1/3 cup unsalted butter, melted"
instructions:
- "===Roast Tomatoes==="
- "Start oven 475F. Line sheet pan with foil sparingly oiled. Place tomato halves cut side up evenly spaced. Brush tops with 1 tbsp olive oil, sprinkle salt, brown sugar, and black pepper. Toss sugar in deliberately; encourages caramelization. Roast 20-25 minutes. Watch skins curling, edges blistering and darkening. Smell sweet acidity rising. Pull when tomatoes start sticky and jammy but not burnt. Drop oven to 425F."
- "===Prepare Filling==="
- "Heat 1 tbsp vegetable oil in 10-inch cast iron preferably. Medium-low is key here so onions sweat, not scorch. Toss sliced onions, stir often to avoid uneven browning. After about 12 minutes, onions translucent with golden spots, add diced pancetta. Continue stirring till pancetta rendered fat, edges crisp. Remove from heat, allow to cool. Combine cheddar, Gruyère, crème fraîche, and chopped basil in bowl. Fold in caramelized onion pancetta mix gently. Resist overmixing to keep texture."
- "===Assemble Layers==="
- "Transfer half roasted tomatoes to skillet, cut side up, nestling close. Spread cheese mixture evenly over. Top with remaining tomatoes, again cut side up. The layering traps moisture underneath cheese, keeps filling plush without soggy bottom. Bake 18-22 minutes. Look for bubbling cheese, tomatoes holding shape but yielding to gentle probe."
- "===Make & Add Crust==="
- "Whisk dry crust ingredients: cornmeal, flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt. In separate bowl beat eggs with buttermilk, maple syrup, and melted butter. Combine wet and dry swiftly, mix until just incorporated. Pour batter evenly over tomato layer in skillet, covering gently. Bake 12-15 minutes or till crust edges turn golden brown, springs back lightly when poked. Avoid overbaking - crust should crackle slightly, not dry out. Remove, cool 5 minutes to set before slicing."
- "===Serving & Tips==="
- "Cut into wedges with serrated knife, crust fragile stored best warm. For crisp crust, use cast iron or heavy-duty ovenproof pan. No buttermilk? Stir 1 tbsp lemon juice into milk, rest 10 min. Swap pancetta with cooked diced kielbasa for extra smoky punch or turkey bacon for leaner twist. Brown sugar swap maple syrup but reduce added sweetness if tomatoes are super ripe. Adjust roasting times by eye - tomatoes should glisten with caramel but not collapse totally."
