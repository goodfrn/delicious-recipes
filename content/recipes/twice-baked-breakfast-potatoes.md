ingredients:
- "4 large cooked potatoes"
- "2 large eggs"
- "2 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "1/4 cup sour cream"
- "1/2 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese"
- "2 tablespoons finely chopped green onions"
- "1/4 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper"
- "4 slices cooked bacon, crumbled"
- "Additional cheddar cheese for topping"
- "Pinch smoked paprika"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 375 F; hotter temp shortens bake time, improves crust crisping on skins."
- "In a small pan, melt 2 teaspoons butter on medium. Whisk eggs lightly, cook stirring slowly till just set but still moist. Avoid dry curds; residual heat finishes gently. Remove from heat immediately."
- "Cut potatoes lengthwise. Scoop pulp with spoon, leave skins intact with minimal pulp for sturdy shell. Transfer pulp to bowl."
- "Mash pulp vigorously with sour cream and 2 tablespoons butter until creamy but not gluey. Overwork and they turn gluey and dense—less is more."
- "Add cheddar, green onions, garlic powder, black pepper, and a pinch of smoked paprika for subtle depth. Mix thoroughly but without beating air in."
- "Fold scrambled eggs into potatoes using two forks. Don’t overmix—just enough to combine. Overmixing breaks eggs into mush and ruins texture."
- "Fill potato skins generously with mixture, heaping for good measure. Press slightly to compact but don’t squash."
- "Transfer to baking sheet. Bake 15-18 minutes at 375 F. Look for bubbling cheese edges and skins crisped on bottom. This signals hot throughout."
- "Remove and immediately top with more cheddar, crumbled bacon, and extra green onions. The cheese on top should melt slightly from residual heat."
- "Serve right away. Leftovers reheat in oven for 5-7 minutes to regain crisp skin and melted cheese."
