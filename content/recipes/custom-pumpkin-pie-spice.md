- "1 tablespoon ground cinnamon"
- "1 teaspoon ground ginger"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground mace"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground star anise"
- "1/4 teaspoon ground cardamom"
- "1/4 teaspoon ground cloves"
- "1/4 teaspoon ground coriander"
- "1/8 teaspoon ground nutmeg"
instructions:
- "Sift the cinnamon, ginger, mace into a small mixing bowl. Look for lumps; break them up with the back of a spoon."
- "Add star anise, cardamom, cloves next. These bring subtle licorice and floral notes—important not to overload or spice will clash."
- "Stir gently to combine. Avoid overmixing to keep texture even. Feel for any gritty bits—if so, pulse a quick blitz in spice grinder or mortar."
- "Sprinkle coriander and nutmeg last. Coriander wakes up the warmth from other spices, nutmeg anchors."
- "Smell it well. Aroma should be fresh, vibrant, not flat or musty. If dull, check spices freshness before using."
- "Store in airtight container away from light, heat source. Use within 3 months. Shake container before each use to prevent settling."
- "Substitute: cinnamon for cassia if only have that—turn down quantity slightly; star anise can be swapped for a pinch of anise seed if pressed."
- "Common issue: moisture clumping mix. Dry spatula and bowl thoroughly before mixing. If humidity high, quick roast spices on dry pan for 30 seconds first to drive out moisture—watch closely to avoid burning."
- "Use this blend for baking pumpkin breads, pies, lattes, or sprinkle on roasted squash—aromatics transform textures and flavor layers."
