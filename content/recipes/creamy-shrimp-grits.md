ingredients:
- "4 cups water"
- "1 teaspoon salt plus more to taste"
- "1 cup stone-ground grits"
- "4 ounces cream cheese, cut into chunks"
- "3 tablespoons unsalted butter, cut into pieces"
- "6 slices bacon"
- "4 large shallots, thinly sliced (sub for green onions)"
- "3 cloves garlic, minced"
- "1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined"
- "1/4 teaspoon black pepper, plus more to taste"
- "2 tablespoons all-purpose flour"
- "1 1/2 cups low-sodium chicken broth (sub vegetable broth if preferred)"
- "1 tablespoon lemon juice"
- "1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (instead of hot sauce)"
- "Extra butter or bacon grease if needed"
instructions:
- "=== Make the Grits ==="
- "Boil 4 cups water in large pot; add 1 tsp salt. Gradually whisk in stone-ground grits to avoid clumps. Lower heat to low, cover loosely. Stir often, scraping sides and bottom; cook 25-30 minutes until tender but not mush. Test texture with a spoon–should be creamy but with slight bite."
- "Add chunks of cream cheese and butter to hot grits. Stir until fully melted; this creates a silky mouthfeel and balances the grits’ earthiness. Keep covered on low heat while prepping shrimp."
- "=== Cook the Bacon and Aromatics ==="
- "Fry bacon strips in large skillet over medium. Cook until crisp but not burnt; remove with slotted spoon to paper towels. Leave about 2 tablespoons of bacon grease in pan; drain or save the rest for another use. Bacon fat is flavor backbone but too much makes sauce greasy."
- "Add sliced shallots to hot bacon grease. Sauté gently 4-5 minutes until softened and lightly browned–releasing sweet aroma. Toss in minced garlic, stir 1 minute until fragrant but not scorched. Garlic burns easily, adjust heat as needed."
- "=== Cook the Shrimp and Sauce ==="
- "Season shrimp with salt and pepper. Add to skillet, tossing in bacon fat, adding a tablespoon extra butter if dry. Cook shrimp over medium-high until edges turn opaque and inner flesh still slightly translucent–about 2.5 to 3 minutes. Overcooked shrimp turn rubbery and lose sweetness."
- "Transfer shrimp to plate with bacon. Stir flour into broth, whisk vigorously; ensures no lumps. Pour slurry into skillet, deglazing browned bits off bottom. Reduce heat to simmer; stir until sauce thickens up to coating consistency–about 4 to 6 minutes. Sauce should cling to spoon but remain pourable."
- "Add lemon juice and cayenne pepper—start with less, adjust after tasting. Return shrimp to skillet. Warm through, stir gently to coat without breaking shrimp apart. Check seasoning, add salt and pepper incrementally."
- "=== Assemble and Serve ==="
- "Spoon creamy grits into shallow bowls. Ladle shrimp and sauce over top. Scatter crispy bacon pieces and extra sliced shallots if desired. Serve immediately to preserve contrast of textures and temperature."
- "=== Tips ==="
- "If grits clump or thicken too much, stir in splash of warm water or broth to loosen. For a dairy-free option, swap cream cheese and butter with full-fat coconut milk or vegan butter—the flavor profile shifts but texture holds."
- "Don’t rush bacon fat management. Too much leftover grease will make sauce greasy; too little and shrimp stick or dry out. Skillet temperature control is key for garlic and shrimp transitions–watch closely, smell cues help here."
- "Should shrimp seem close to overcooked but sauce needs more time, remove shrimp and keep warm; add back just before serving."
