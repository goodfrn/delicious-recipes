ingredients:
- "6 tablespoons unsalted butter (or 5 tablespoons coconut oil)"
- "3/4 cup packed dark brown sugar (level and broken)"
- "2/3 cup full-fat coconut milk (or heavy cream)"
- "1 teaspoon bourbon (optional, adds aroma)"
- "1/4 teaspoon sea salt (adjust to taste)"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla bean paste or scraped vanilla pod"
instructions:
- "Start with a heavy-bottomed pan. Melt butter and brown sugar together on medium-low. Stir nonstop—grainy sugar sits, burns fast on bottom—listen to soft crackle pops and watch sugar dissolve into paste."
- "Once uniform chocolatey gloss, slowly add coconut milk while whisking hard. A few lumps? Can't be helped, keep whisking till fluid and lightly thick."
- "Turn heat slightly up to medium. Sauce will bubble gently at edges, tiny simmer. Stir constantly to avoid scorching. Watch thickness develop; no roar, gentle hiss means right pace. About 3-4 minutes actually, not exactly 2-3, but use sight and feel."
- "Remove from heat. Stir in bourbon; watch it foam up wildly, then settle. Vanilla goes in last. Salt too—just a pinch brings depth, tone down sweetness."
- "Let cool on countertop. Thickens to luscious pourable fudge texture. Not fridge—too hard, loses silk. If sauce hardens too much, warm gently over water bath or low heat while stirring."
- "Serve slightly warm or room temp. Great over roasted pears or vanilla gelato. Store airtight max 5 days in fridge, always reheat cautiously."
