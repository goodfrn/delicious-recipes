ingredients:
- "8 ounces thick-cut bacon, diced"
- "1 large yellow onion, finely chopped"
- "3 cloves garlic, minced"
- "2 cans pork and beans (15 ounces each)"
- "1 can black beans (15 ounces), drained and rinsed"
- "1/2 cup ketchup"
- "1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce"
- "1 1/2 teaspoons spicy brown mustard"
- "1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar"
- "1/4 cup pure maple syrup"
- "Fresh cracked black pepper, to taste"
instructions:
- "===Prepping bacon and aromatics==="
- "Medium heat. Add bacon to a large oven-safe dutch oven. Listen to the staff sizzle, some pops. Cook until deep mahogany color, fat rendered but not burnt. Use a slotted spoon. Drain on paper towels. Leave about 2 1/2 tablespoons bacon fat in pot. Fat amount critical here; too little and onions stick, too much and beans get greasy."
- "===Sweat onions, build base==="
- "Add onions to bacon fat. Medium medium medium. Stir every minute or so. Watch them soften, edges turning translucent and just starting to color. About 6 minutes. Stir in garlic. Smell it, that sharp raw garlic scent softens fast. Cook one minute. Stir constantly now. Prevent garlic burning; that will ruin depth. Timing here is fluid; look for softened onions, bright aroma from garlic."
- "===Add beans sauce and flavorings==="
- "Dump in pork and beans, black beans, ketchup, Worcestershire, mustard, vinegar, and maple syrup. Stir well. Sauce will thicken a bit from sugar and mustard. Don't overmix at first or mash beans. Season black pepper now. Taste a spoonful. Adjust vinegar or syrup balance if too tart or sweet. Let it come together on stove for 2-3 minutes; sauce will thicken slightly, coating beans nicely."
- "===Top with bacon, bake==="
- "Sprinkle crisp bacon evenly on top. No stirring now; bacon needs to stay distinct and crispy-ish. Place uncovered in preheated 340°F oven (reduce temperature 10 degrees for slower cook, deeper flavor). Bake 50, but watch the bubbles. Bubbles at edges; sauce thick, glossy, and starting to caramelize? Good. Pull then."
- "Serve warm. Beans should be thick but saucy. Bacon delivers crunchy texture contrast. If sauce too thin, leave uncovered in oven 5-10 extra minutes, watching carefully. Too dry? Add splash water or broth next batch."
- "If you want more heat, add 1/4 teaspoon cayenne in step 3."
- "For stovetop-only version, simmer the assembled bean mixture on low, stirring every few minutes until thickened, about 20-25 minutes."
