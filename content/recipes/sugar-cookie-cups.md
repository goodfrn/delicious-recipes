ingredients:
- "Premade sugar cookie dough 16 ounces"
- "Nonstick baking spray"
- "1 cup unsalted butter softened"
- "4 cups powdered sugar sifted"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "⅛ teaspoon salt"
- "1-2 tablespoons milk whole preferred"
- "Gel food coloring optional"
- "72 small chocolate candy eggs or similar"
instructions:
- "==="
- "Cookies:"
- "Preheat oven to 345 degrees fahrenheit. Spray regular muffin tin generously with baking spray. Breaking dough into roughly 24 sections, place one piece into each muffin well, pressing no more than half an inch deep. Bake 8 to 12 minutes watching for edges just turning golden, tops are firm but centers remain slightly soft—a sign dough is still pliable for shaping. Listen for quiet baking, no crackling or excessive browning. Remove and immediately, while hot but safe to touch, use a small round object like bottom of a spice jar to slowly press center down. Do not force; slow, gentle pressure avoids cracking. This forms a small cup to hold frosting."
- "Let cookies rest in pan 4 to 6 minutes till cool enough to handle but centers not hardened. Use butter knife to gently pry edges from pan avoiding damage, then transfer to wire rack. Cool fully — can take 15 to 20 minutes depending on airflow and rack type. Full cooling critical so frosting doesn’t melt on contact."
- "==="
- "Frosting:"
- "In large bowl, beat softened butter at medium speed about 2-3 minutes till lightened in color. Scrape bowl sides frequently for even creaming."
- "Add powdered sugar one cup at a time on low speed to minimize sugar dust cloud. Once mixed, increase speed to medium and beat until fully incorporated and texture is dense but not crumbly."
- "Mix in vanilla and salt for balanced flavor. Texture should feel creamy but stiff. If too thick, drizzle milk one tablespoon at a time while beating until frosting loosens but stays holdable."
- "Continue whipping 2 to 4 minutes till light and slightly fluffy but still pipeable. Add gel food coloring dropwise stirring gently between drops until desired shade is achieved."
- "Fill piping bag fitted with medium round or star tip to ensure neat placement."
- "==="
- "Assembly:"
- "Confirm cookies fully cooled else frosting will melt or slide. Pipe frosting into hollow centers, filling generously to form soft dome. Work quickly before frosting sets but not while warm or cookie may soften and crack."
- "Top each with 3 small chocolate eggs arranged tightly for contrast in texture and contrast in sweet and mild chocolate flavor."
- "Store assembled cookies in airtight container at room temperature up to two days. For longer keep, refrigerate but bring to room temp before eating for best mouthfeel."
- "==="
- "Notes and troubleshooting:"
- "Swapping butter with margarine alters taste and firmness; margarine has more water and lowers rise. Milk alternatives work but beware changing frosting thickness markedly."
- "Avoid overbaking. Cookies go from chewy to dry fast. If dough cracks when pressing, cookie likely overdone. Smaller jar bottoms work better for smaller indentations. If cookie sticks to pan, let cool longer or spray more generously—using oil spray preferred over flour sprays to keep edges crisp."
- "Gel coloring creates more vivid colors without thinning frosting. If liquid color needed, increase powdered sugar to maintain stiffness."
- "If frosting too soft from milk excess, add sifted powdered sugar slowly to regain stiffness without graininess."
- "Chocolate candy eggs can be substituted for mini M&Ms, candy-coated nuts, or sprinkles for variety."
- "Use silicone muffin pan if available to ease cookie release but adjusting spray is key. If cracking occurs despite gentleness, chill cookie cups lightly before frosting to firm base."
