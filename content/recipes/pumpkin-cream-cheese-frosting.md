ingredients:
- "6 tablespoons unsalted butter softened"
- "8 ounces cream cheese softened"
- "1/4 cup pumpkin puree"
- "1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract"
- "1/4 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "3 1/2 cups powdered sugar sifted"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon"
- "1/8 teaspoon ground nutmeg"
instructions:
- "==="
- "Start by whipping butter with paddle attachment or hand mixer at medium speed. Beat until it loosens and turns pale, about 2 minutes. Soft, not greasy. Key for creamy base."
- "Add half the cream cheese next. Blend slowly first so it doesn’t splatter, then kick up to medium high. Incorporate fully until no chunks. Texture will start velvety but stiff. Watch for graininess, stop mixing to check often."
- "Pour in pumpkin puree with vanilla, salt, and all spices. Blend briefly. Don’t overbeat here — just enough to combine evenly. Pumpkin adds moisture and flavor; too much mixing breaks down structure."
- "Add powdered sugar gradually in two portions - start with 2 cups and mix on low speed until almost smooth. Raise speed slightly; add remaining sugar, fold in until lumps gone and no powdery bite remains. Frosting should hold peaks but still spreadable. If too soft, add sugar tablespoon by tablespoon."
- "Chill frosting at least 30 minutes before frosting cakes or cupcakes. This firms up texture for neater application. Keep refrigerated when not serving, especially with pumpkin."
- "Store any finished, frosted goods in fridge. Room temperature frostings with cream cheese get soft and lose form quickly. Serve within 2-3 days for best freshness."
- "=== End."
