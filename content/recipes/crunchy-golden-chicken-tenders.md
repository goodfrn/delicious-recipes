ingredients:
- "1 1/2 lb chicken tenders or strips"
- "3/4 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1 1/2 tsp smoked paprika"
- "1 tsp garlic powder powder"
- "1/2 tsp salt"
- "1/2 tsp black pepper"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1/4 cup milk (or cream for richer taste)"
- "2 cups crushed bran flakes, pulse gently in processor or bag"
- "Non-stick spray or oil"
- "For dipping sauce"
- "1/2 cup mayonnaise"
- "2 tbsp hot sauce (adjust to heat preference)"
- "1 tbsp honey"
- "1 tsp lemon juice"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 425°F instead of 450. High enough to crisp but less risk of burning the coating."
- "Line a rimmed sheet pan with foil. Place an oven-safe wire rack on foil. Spray the rack liberally with non-stick spray. Keeps chicken elevated, allows air circulation, crisps bottom."
- "Crush bran flakes coarsely using pulse on food processor or manual rolling pin method. Aim for flaky crumbs, not powder. Provides bite and structure. Bran flakes add nuttiness not found with cornflakes."
- "Setup three shallow dishes for dredging. Mix flour with paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper in first. Whisk eggs and milk in second. Bran flakes in third."
- "Pat chicken dry with paper towel to ensure coatings stick. One tender at a time: dredge thoroughly in flour, shake excess off but coat well to create base layer. Next, plunge into egg mixture fully submerged. Last, press into bran flakes firmly, coating both sides, pressing gently so flakes stick; loose coating peels off while baking."
- "Place coated tenders evenly on prepared rack. Don’t overcrowd. Enables air to crisp evenly. Spray tops lightly with oil spray or brush with a bit of melted butter if you want extra golden color."
- "Slide sheet pan into oven. Listen for crackling as coating hits heat. Bake about 18 minutes. Flip tenders halfway through only if coating feels loose or color uneven. Otherwise, leave alone. Visual guide: golden brown, firm edges, internal temp 165°F. Use instant-read thermometer inside thickest part."
- "While baking, prepare sauce by whisking mayo, hot sauce, honey, and lemon juice in bowl. Adjust sweetness or acidity if needed. Sauce adds cooling creaminess with quick heat punch."
- "Remove chicken carefully, rest 2 minutes before serving. Coating firms. Bite remains crunchy with juicy interior. Serve with sauce on side."
- "If coating falls off, likely too wet or flour not set first. Pat chicken as dry as possible and press flakes onto wet egg layer gently but thoroughly. For less mess, try dipping tenders one by one immediately before baking instead of prepping all at once."
- "Substitutions: If no bran flakes, crushed rice cereal or toasted panko work. Milk in egg wash can be water or buttermilk for tang. Spice mix is flexible; add cayenne or onion powder for variation. Swap mayo sauce base for Greek yogurt or sour cream for lighter dip."

- "Ensure chicken is dry before dredging. Moisture prevents adherence of flour and flakes. Pat with paper towels meticulously."
- "For crispy bottoms, using a wire rack is essential. It allows air circulation and minimizes steam that causes sogginess."
