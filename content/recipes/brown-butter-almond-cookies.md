ingredients:
- "1 cup unsalted butter"
- "1/2 cup coconut sugar"
- "1/4 cup pure maple syrup"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract"
- "1/4 cup milk (whole or almond milk works)"
- "1 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1/2 cup almond flour (blanched or raw)"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1/2 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon"
- "1 cup dark chocolate chunks, divided"
- "1/2 cup toasted sliced almonds"
instructions:
- "Start by preheating oven to 345°F. Prep two baking pans with parchment or silicone mats; heat evenly without sticking."
- "Brown butter slowly in a light-colored pan over medium heat. Swirl pan often; watch for frothy bubbles then nutty aroma, little brown specks on bottom. Remove from heat just as the sizzle softens; cool 10-15 minutes. Butter will be warm but not hot—too hot and it will cook eggs later."
- "Mix warm browned butter with coconut sugar and maple syrup using a hand mixer. Whip until grains begin to dissolve. This step controls cookie chew and moisture balance."
- "Add eggs one at a time, beating on medium speed to emulsify. Then stir in vanilla and milk. Stops dough from being dry. Milk type affects texture subtly—whole milk richer; almond milk nuttier."
- "Sift in all dry ingredients: all-purpose flour, almond flour, baking soda, salt, and cinnamon. Folding gently just to combine—over mixing develops gluten, toughens cookies. You will see sticky dough forming, shiny from brown butter fats."
- "Fold in toasted sliced almonds and three-quarters of chopped chocolate chunks. They add crunch amid chewy dough. If dough feels crumbly, add teaspoon milk increments."
- "Form dough into golf-ball size balls. Dunk each ball’s top into remaining chocolate chunks, pushing gently to embed. Press lightly to set. Flatten cookie balls just a bit to help chocolate stick and get uniform shape."
- "Arrange six cookies spaced on sheets—not too crowded. Bake 11-12 minutes. Edges will turn a soft golden brown, centers visibly softer, almost glossy but not wet. If unsure, jiggle pan gently—the cookies should wobble slightly in center but not be runny. Pull too soon, cookies stay too raw; too long, edges harden."
- "After pulling from the oven, swirl pan gently to round out edges if some spread unevenly. Rest cookies on pan 3 minutes—they’ll firm up more off heat. Transfer carefully to wire racks to cool fully."
- "Optional: sprinkle flaky sea salt atop warm cookies to offset sweetness and sharpen flavors."
