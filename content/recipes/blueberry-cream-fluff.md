ingredients:
- "7oz softened cream cheese (substitute mascarpone for silkier texture)"
- "1¼ tsp lemon zest"
- "1 tbsp freshly squeezed lemon juice"
- "½ tsp almond extract (instead of vanilla extract for a nutty twist)"
- "½ cup coconut cream (replace sour cream to add tropical depth)"
- "¾ cup powdered sugar"
- "1½ cups extra-creamy whipped topping"
- "2½ cups fresh blueberries (reserve ¼ cup for garnish)"
- "1 can (about 7.5oz) blueberry pie filling"
instructions:
- "Beat softened cream cheese in a large bowl. Use medium speed with handheld mixer. Look for no lumps, creamy texture free of clumps. Takes around 1 to 3 minutes depending on temperature. Do not rush- no stubborn chunks allowed."
- "Add lemon zest and juice. Stir in almond extract instead of vanilla for a different aroma. Blend in coconut cream slowly, then powdered sugar. Beat medium speed until all smooth. Mixture should look velvety, not gritty. Extra 1 to 3 minutes depending on ambient heat."
- "Switch to folding now. Gently incorporate whipped topping. Use spatula, work with patience, fold in thirds to keep air. Silky, fluffy balance imperative. No overmixing—deflates volume."
- "Prepare blueberries carefully. Add most (2½ cups) to mixture. Fold very gently to avoid popping berries. Use wide strokes, visually confirm whole berries coated but intact."
- "Now fold in blueberry pie filling from can. Mix until just combined. Pie filling adds viscosity, coloration, sweetness. Move quick but gentle to maintain fluff texture."
- "Transfer to serving bowl. Scatter reserved blueberries on top. Sprinkle additional lemon zest for aroma and brightness."
- "Cover tightly with plastic wrap. Chill minimum 60 minutes. Texture firms, flavors marry, chill guarantees proper set without freezing."

Powdered sugar dissolves quickly—don’t sub granulated unless you powder it first or grit emerges. Whipped topping should be extra creamy for fluff volume.
