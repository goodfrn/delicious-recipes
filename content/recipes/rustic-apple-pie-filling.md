ingredients:
- "9 cups peeled cored chopped tart apples"
- "2 tablespoons lime juice"
- "2 1/4 cup white sugar"
- "1 tablespoon brown sugar"
- "1/2 cup arrowroot powder"
- "1 teaspoon ground cardamom"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground star anise"
- "1/2 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "4 cups unsweetened apple cider"
instructions:
- "=== Prepare Apples ==="
- "Start with peeled cored chopped apples roughly bite-sized. Toss in lime juice immediately to arrest browning. No lemon? Use white vinegar sparingly but lime preferred for bright flavor."
- "Set aside while prepping sauce."
- "=== Make Thickening Base ==="
- "In a heavy-bottomed pot, whisk white and brown sugars, arrowroot powder, ground cardamom, star anise, and salt together dry. This prevents clumping. Pour in apple cider and mix smooth before turning heat on."
- "Bring to a gentle simmer over medium-low heat. Don’t rush; sudden heat will make arrowroot stringy or break down sugars unevenly."
- "=== Add Apples and Cook ==="
- "Once base simmers, stir in apples, cover pot loosely. Let cook 7-15 minutes depending on size chunks. Apples should pierce easily with a fork yet hold shape. Sauce thickened, glossy, subtly spiced with cardamom and star anise aroma faint but present."
- "Stir occasionally but gently. Poking apples with spoon can shred them—avoid."
- "=== Final Steps ==="
- "Remove from heat; let cool slightly—the filling thickens as it cools. Taste. Adjust salt or sugar to balance acidity or sweetness."
- "Use filling immediately or cool completely and store in fridge up to 4 days. Can freeze up to 6 months or process in jars for longer storage."
- "Always label and date if freezing or canning. When reheating, warm gently to avoid breaking down texture."
- "=== Cleanup Tip ==="
- "Arrowroot residue can stick—soak with warm water and a splash of vinegar before scrubbing."
