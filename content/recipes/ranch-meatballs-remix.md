ingredients:
- "1 pound ground pork"
- "1 pound ground turkey"
- "1 large egg"
- "1/3 cup plain Greek yogurt"
- "3/4 cup panko bread crumbs"
- "1 tsp garlic powder"
- "1 tsp smoked paprika"
- "1/2 tsp salt"
- "1/4 tsp black pepper"
- "cooking spray"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 340 degrees F. Lightly grease a shallow baking dish with spray; prevents sticking and uneven browning."
- "In a medium bowl toss together ground pork and turkey for balanced fat and flavor. Crack in egg, add Greek yogurt—acts as binder and moisture trap without thinning mix too much. Panko crumbs fold in last; adds crunch and absorbs juices."
- "Season mixture with garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, pepper. Mix gently but thoroughly till combined. Overmixing toughens meat. Hand mix or light fork strokes recommended."
- "Form the mixture into 1 1/4 inch balls. Smaller than usual for more even cooking and crisp exterior. If mixture feels too wet, add more panko in small increments."
- "Arrange meatballs in baking dish spaced evenly. Monitor spacing so hot air circulates around each ball ensuring browning on all sides."
- "Bake uncovered for 30 minutes. Look for golden crust forming and juices running clear when pierced. Meatballs should spring back to touch, not feel dense. Oven times vary; rely more on look and feel than clock."
- "Remove from oven; rest 5 minutes—lets juices redistribute. Hot plate, they might seem slightly underdone but carryover cooking finishes the job."
- "Serve with bold dips like chipotle mayo or blue cheese dressing. Dipping sauces add complexity and moisture balance."
- "Store leftovers in airtight container; reheat gently to prevent drying."
