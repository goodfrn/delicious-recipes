ingredients:
- "1 large head cauliflower cut into medium florets"
- "3 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil"
- "1/2 cup finely grated aged Pecorino Romano cheese"
- "3 cloves garlic minced"
- "1 teaspoon dried thyme"
- "1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika"
- "Salt to taste"
- "Freshly ground black pepper to taste"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 410 degrees Fahrenheit. Use convection if you have it; promotes more even browning."
- "Grab a large bowl. Whisk olive oil, minced garlic, Pecorino Romano, dried thyme, and smoked paprika thoroughly. Aromatic oils release their character here; don't rush."
- "Add cauliflower florets in batches. Toss well but gently; coat each piece without breaking. Too much oil will pool on pan causing steam not roast—avoid soggy results."
- "Line a rimmed sheet pan with parchment. Spread cauliflower evenly with space between pieces—crowding causes steaming."
- "Roast on middle rack for 18–23 minutes. Look for florets tender to gentle fork poking, edges gaining deep golden brown color, slight crunch on tips. Hot oven noises decrease as moisture evaporates."
- "Remove pan carefully—florets will look dry on surface but soft inside. Immediately season with salt and pepper. Adding salt post-roast prevents premature water draw, preserving crispness."
- "Taste one to verify seasoning balance. Adjust if needed, maybe more smoked paprika for smoky kick or a pinch of chile flakes for heat."
- "Serve hot or warm. Leftovers reheat well in skillet or oven to revive crisp texture; microwave tends to soften and lose browning."

Floret size, pan type, oven quirks all influence timing—trust texture, not the clock."
ingredientsNote: "Cauliflower selection matters. Firm heads with tight, unblemished florets roast best. Avoid overly wet or frozen; moisture hinders crispness. Substitute Pecorino Romano with Parmesan if sharper bite undesired. Olive oil must be good quality; bad oils produce off aromas when heated. Garlic can switch to granulated if fresh unavailable, adjust quantity to 1 teaspoon; flavor will be less robust but uniform. Thyme replaced common Italian seasoning bringing woodsy, less sweet notes; smoked paprika adds complexity missing in dried herb blends. Salt held back until roasting protects texture—adding prematurely draws moisture. If allergic to Pecorino or Parmesan, a mild mozzarella (low moisture) can work but expect less crisped topping.
