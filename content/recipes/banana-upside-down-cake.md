ingredients:
- "=== Topping ==="
- "1 cup light brown sugar"
- "8 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted"
- "3 ripe bananas, sliced thin"
- "=== Cake ==="
- "1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking soda"
- "3/4 teaspoon baking powder"
- "1/4 teaspoon salt"
- "1 teaspoon ground cinnamon"
- "1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg"
- "3/4 cup brown sugar (packed)"
- "1/4 cup white granulated sugar"
- "1 cup mashed banana (about 2 large bananas)"
- "2/3 cup buttermilk"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1/3 cup vegetable oil (can swap with melted coconut oil)"
- "2 large eggs"
instructions:
- "=== Preparation ==="
- "Preheat oven to 340°F (adjusted slightly down). Grease a 9-inch springform pan thoroughly—makes flipping cleaner. No parchment here; butter and sugar handle release."
- "=== Topping ==="
- "Mix brown sugar and melted butter until combined but not bubbly. Pour this into the pan and spread evenly with a silicone spatula—get coverage corner to corner."
- "Arrange banana slices in a single layer over butter-sugar mix. Overlap slightly but avoid thick clumps; even coverage caramelizes better."
- "=== Cake ==="
- "Whisk flour, baking soda, baking powder, salt, cinnamon, and nutmeg in a large bowl. Set aside."
- "In a separate bowl, beat brown sugar, white sugar, mashed banana, buttermilk, vanilla, oil, and eggs with a hand mixer. Beat just until homogeneous—don’t aerate excessively or cake gets dense."
- "Fold half the dry ingredients into wet. Use a spatula or wooden spoon with folding motions, not stirring. Add remaining dry ingredients and gently combine until no white streaks. Slight lumps okay. Scrape bowl edges."
- "Pour batter gently over the banana layer. Spread gently to edges. Don’t disturb the banana slices beneath. Batter consistency slightly thick but pourable."
- "Bake on center rack for 50 minutes or until toothpick inserted near center comes out with just a few moist crumbs. Crust should be golden, edges pulling slightly from sides. Aroma turns sweet and buttery."
- "After baking, remove from oven. Cool in pan uncovered for 7 minutes—warm but stable. Run a thin knife gently around edges to loosen sides if needed."
- "Place a large plate over the top of the springform pan; hold firmly and flip swiftly but carefully. Lift pan away once upside down; topping should glisten, bananas soft but intact."
- "Serve warm. If not immediately, gently reheat in low oven to restore gloss. Store leftovers tightly wrapped, best eaten day of."
- "=== Notes and substitutions ==="
- "Butter and brown sugar mix creates caramel base; use dark brown sugar for deeper flavor but risk bitter notes. Coconut oil in cake adds slight tropical hint, can swap for canola or light olive oil. Mashed bananas must be ripe but not too mushy; overripe turns cake gummy. Buttermilk acid reacts with baking soda for lift—whole milk plus 1 tsp vinegar can replace if needed."
- "Don’t overmix batter or cake turns rubbery. Folding technique critical. Baking time can vary; rely on color and toothpick test rather than clock. Springform pans can leak; wrap bottom with foil if pan is leaky or place on baking sheet."
- "Flipping while warm prevents topping sticking but cool cake is fragile. Use big plate and angle flip to maintain banana slices whole for presentation."
- "If bananas brown too quickly during prep, toss slices with lemon juice to prevent oxidation. For crunch twist, sprinkle chopped toasted pecans over the topping before baking."
