ingredients:
- "2 tablespoons avocado oil"
- "1 cup carrots, blanched and diced"
- "3/4 cup frozen peas"
- "3/4 cup frozen corn"
- "1 tablespoon fresh ginger, minced"
- "3 cloves garlic, minced"
- "1 tablespoon rice vinegar"
- "3 large eggs, lightly beaten"
- "4 cups riced broccoli"
- "2 tablespoons tamari"
- "1 teaspoon chili flakes"
- "1/4 cup scallions, chopped"
- "Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste"
- "1 tablespoon toasted sesame seeds for garnish"
instructions:
- "Heat avocado oil over medium-high heat until shimmering but not smoking. Careful, avocado oil holds heat well; watch closely."
- "Add blanched diced carrots; sauté about 3-4 minutes until edges just start to caramelize. Listen for soft sizzle — carrots should stay crisp-tender not mushy."
- "Stir in frozen peas and corn right from freezer; reduce heat to medium. Toss in minced ginger, garlic, and rice vinegar. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 4-5 minutes, until garlic aroma blossoms and veggies become bright."
- "Push vegetables to one side. Pour in beaten eggs on empty skillet spot. Let set slightly, then stir rapidly breaking eggs into curds. Keep moving to avoid big fried slabs. Cook ~2-3 minutes till eggs no longer runny."
- "Fold in riced broccoli, tamari, chili flakes, and scallions gently. Toss lightly — avoid heavy stirring which turns broccoli mushy. Heat through just until broccoli loses raw bite but retains snap, about 2-3 minutes."
- "Taste. Add salt and pepper sparingly; tamari adds saltiness. Final toss with care to distribute seasoning and avoid waterlogged texture."
- "Remove from heat, sprinkle toasted sesame seeds. Serve immediately to preserve texture and freshness."
