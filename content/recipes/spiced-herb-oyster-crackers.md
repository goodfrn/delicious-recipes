ingredients:
- "6 cups oyster crackers"
- "4 tablespoons ghee, melted"
- "1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil"
- "2 teaspoons smoked paprika"
- "1 teaspoon dried rosemary, crushed"
- "1 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "1 teaspoon onion powder"
- "1 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper"
- "1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 275 degrees Fahrenheit for even, slow crisping."
- "Combine crackers in a large bowl. Pour ghee and olive oil over. Use your hands or a spatula to coat every piece thoroughly; oil + butter fat mix gives a more nuanced mouthfeel and minimizes burning risks common with pure butter."
- "In a separate small bowl, mix smoked paprika, rosemary, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, pepper, and cayenne. Tasting the raw blend helps balance heat and aromatics—adjust cayenne if sensitive."
- "Sprinkle seasoning mix uniformly over the coated crackers. Stir gently but thoroughly to avoid clumping; each cracker must get spiced evenly, or bites turn unevenly bland or overkill salty."
- "Scatter crackers on a rimmed baking sheet, try to create a single layer with minimal overlap: crunch requires hot air circulation."
- "Bake for about 7 minutes then use a metal spatula or oven-safe tongs to turn crackers over. You want a faint golden hue on edges that sounds dry when tapped—not too dark or pale. Return to oven."
- "Bake for another 5 minutes. Much longer risks bitter burnt flavors, especially with paprika."
- "Remove and let cool uncovered on the baking sheet until crisp snaps are audible; residual heat completes the drying without making them tough or leathery."
- "Store fully cooled crackers in an airtight container at room temperature. Moisture kills crunch fast. If they soften, reheating briefly in a dry pan or low oven revives texture."
- "Optional twist: add a teaspoon of dried lemon zest with seasoning for a bright citrus kick, or swap rosemary for thyme for earthier notes."
- "If no ghee, unsalted butter plus a splash of vegetable oil works. Avoid pure olive oil; burns too quickly here."
- "Arrange all prep tools nearby—seasonings combined in advance. Tossing while crackers warm helps coating cling better for more even flavor."
