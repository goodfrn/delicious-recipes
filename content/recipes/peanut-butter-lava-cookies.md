ingredients:
- "2 refrigerated pie crusts (room temp)"
- "16 tablespoons almond butter (or peanut butter)"
- "1/4 teaspoon fine coconut sugar"
- "Extra granulated sugar for coating"
- "1 tablespoon grated dark chocolate (added twist)"
- "Optional pinch salt inside filling"
instructions:
- "=== Preparation ==="
- "Preheat oven to 395°F. Bring pie crusts to ambient temp to avoid cracking when rolling. Rolling softer dough aids sealing."
- "Unroll one crust on parchment. Using a lightly floured rolling pin, roll it down thinner than package thickness; aim for 1/12th inch. Thin enough for crispness but sturdy enough to hold filling."
- "Cut as many 3-inch rounds as possible with cookie cutter or jar rim, keeping scraps to reroll."
- "=== Filling & Assembly ==="
- "Place 1 tbsp almond butter center on each round. Sprinkle 1/8 teaspoon of coconut sugar and a tiny pinch of salt if preferred. The sugar helps caramelize and adds subtle sweetness without hindering seal."
- "Add a small sprinkle (around 1/4 tsp) grated dark chocolate on top – melts inside during bake, adds complexity."
- "Top with second crust round, gently seal edges by pressing firmly with fingers all around. Crimp edges using tines of a fork for stronger seal and fancier look. Seal prevents filling leak during baking."
- "=== Coating & Baking ==="
- "Lightly toss completed cookies in granulated sugar, coating all sides. Creates a crunchy crust on surface."
- "Arrange spaced on parchment-lined baking sheet. Reroll scraps and repeat until dough and filling used up."
- "Bake 14-17 minutes. Watch edges for light golden blush and watch for faint bubbling or slight puffing of crust top to judge doneness. Cookies feel firm but not hard when pressed lightly."
- "=== Cooling & Serving ==="
- "Let cool for at least 3 minutes on the pan. Filling inside will be molten lava temperature – watch out for burns. Cooling firms filling gradually but still warm, gooey center is goal."
- "Finish second pie crust batch while first bakes to speed process."
- "=== Tips & Troubleshooting ==="
- "If dough tears sealing, moist fingers help adhere edges better."
- "Too thick crust, filling won’t break through easily; too thin and they leak. Find balance."
- "Swap almond butter with cashew or sunflower seed butter for allergy-friendly options."
- "Sugar coating can be replaced with cinnamon sugar for aromatic notes."
- "Overbaking leads to dry, crumbly edges and hardened filling. Stop baking once light browning shows."
- "Watch oven hot spots; rotate baking sheet halfway through baking."
