ingredients:
- "5 cups water"
- "4 black tea bags"
- "1/3 cup honey"
- "2 cups cold water"
- "1/4 cup lime juice fresh squeezed"
- "2 cups water (for berry simmer)"
- "12 oz frozen raspberries (reserve 20 berries for garnish)"
- "1/4 cup fresh mint leaves"
instructions:
- "Bring 5 cups water just to boil. Not vigorous rolling but definitely hot enough to extract flavor without bitterness."
- "Remove from heat immediately. Add tea bags, cover loosely. Steep 6 minutes or until deep amber. Too long can turn bitter. Remove bags carefully—don’t squeeze, that adds harsh tannins."
- "While tea is hot, stir in honey until fully dissolved. Honey blends better warm, but adjust sweetness after chilling if needed."
- "Pour tea into large pitcher. Add 2 cups cold water and freshly squeezed lime juice. Lime cuts sharp and lifts flavors differently than lemon—no bitter pith here."
- "Bring remaining 2 cups water to gentle boil. Add frozen raspberries minus reserved berries for garnish. Simmer for about 6 minutes. Berries will burst, releasing deep magenta color and tart juices."
- "Strain berry liquid through fine mesh sieve into tea mixture. Press lightly on solids but avoid gritty bits in drink. Discard solids."
- "Add fresh mint leaves to the pitcher now. Let steep in tea while chilling. Releases cool menthol aroma, but avoid over-extracting or it will taste grassy."
- "Cover pitcher and refrigerate until well chilled, about 3 hours. Flavors meld; mint infuses subtly."
- "Serve over ice with reserved whole raspberries and sprigs of mint as garnish. Watch ice melt slowly to avoid diluting flavors too fast."
