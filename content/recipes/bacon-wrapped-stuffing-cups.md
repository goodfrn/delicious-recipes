ingredients:
- "12 strips thick-cut bacon"
- "6 cups dry bread cubes, stale or toasted"
- "1 cup diced parsnip"
- "1 cup diced celery"
- "3/4 cup diced shallot"
- "1/4 cup avocado oil, warmed"
- "1 1/4 cups mushroom stock, warmed"
- "2 tablespoons minced fresh parsley"
- "1/2 teaspoon dried sage"
- "1/2 teaspoon dried thyme"
- "Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste"
- "Cooking spray or softened butter for muffin tin"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 355°F. Grease 12-cup muffin tin liberally with softened butter. Butter prevents bacon from sticking, easier muffin release."
- "Wrap each muffin cup rim with a strip of bacon, overlapping edges slightly to form a snug shell. Bacon should cover about 3/4 of the way up—too high and it may burn, too low and it won’t hold stuffing shape."
- "Microwave mushroom stock in a microwave-safe container for about 75 seconds until steaming but not boiling. Aroma of mushrooms should be pronounced but delicate."
- "In a large bowl, whisk warmed avocado oil with dried sage and thyme. Toss parsnip, celery, and shallots to coat evenly. Add bread cubes and pour in mushroom stock gradually, stirring to evenly hydrate without saturation. Let sit 3 minutes to absorb fully."
- "Season stuffing mixture with salt and pepper. Taste raw stuffing for seasoning balance—won't be cooked yet."
- "Evenly pack stuffing into bacon-lined muffin cups, pressing lightly to compact but not smashing. Level tops with back of spoon."
- "Place muffin pan on middle rack. Bake 25 minutes or until bacon crisps sharply, edges curl and darken slightly, and tops become golden brown. Listen for bacon crackle and watch for bubbling juices around edges."
- "Remove from oven, let rest at least 8 minutes. Resting allows bacon fat to solidify and stuffing to firm up, easing removal."
- "Run a small knife around edges if sticky. Tap muffin tin base gently to loosen. Carefully lift muffins by bacon ends or with a small offset spatula."
- "Garnish tops with fresh minced parsley. Serve warm. These hold shape well without drying out if kept covered for up to 2 hours."
