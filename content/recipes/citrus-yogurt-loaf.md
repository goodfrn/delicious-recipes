ingredients:
- "3 large eggs"
- "1 cup granulated sugar"
- "3/4 cup Greek yogurt (sub for sour cream)"
- "1/3 cup vegetable oil"
- "2 tablespoons melted unsalted butter (adds richness)"
- "1 tablespoon finely grated lemon zest"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1 tablespoon fresh orange zest (sub for lemon extract)"
- "2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1/4 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "Powdered sugar glaze:"
- "1 cup powdered sugar"
- "2 to 3 tablespoons fresh lemon juice (adjust for thickness)"
- "1 tablespoon milk (optional, for thinning glaze)"
instructions:
- "=== Citrus Yogurt Loaf ==="
- "Preheat oven to 355°F. Lightly grease a 9x5-inch loaf pan with cooking spray or butter. Set aside."
- "Crack eggs into a large mixing bowl. Add sugar and Greek yogurt. Beat on medium speed until mixture looks uniform and a bit thickened. Scrape sides."
- "Slowly drizzle in oil and melted butter while mixer runs low. Incorporate gently to avoid deflating the air whipped into eggs."
- "Add lemon zest, orange zest, and vanilla extract. Pulse mix just to distribute evenly."
- "Whisk together flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt in a separate bowl. Add dry ingredients all at once to wet. Fold gently with spatula or mixer low speed until no big flour streaks remain. Some lumps fine. Overmixing gives dense texture—avoid."
- "Pour batter into prepared pan. Smooth top gently with spatula. Look for even spread but don't press hard."
- "Place on center rack in oven. Bake 40 to 48 minutes. Don’t rely on clock alone. Watch the edges—should start pulling slightly from pan. Top gets golden brown, with little cracks. Insert toothpick in center; it comes out with few moist crumbs, never wet batter."
- "Cool loaf fully in pan on wire rack before removing. Cooling inside pan lessens collapsing risk."
- "=== Citrus Glaze ==="
- "In small bowl, whisk powdered sugar with lemon juice and optional milk. Aim for pourable but not too thin consistency that slides off loaf fast."
- "When loaf is cool, drizzle glaze unevenly over top using spoon or small whisk. Let drip along sides."
- "Slice with serrated knife. Clean blade between cuts to maintain clean slices. Serve room temp or lightly warmed."
- "Store leftovers wrapped tightly or in container. Glaze may absorb into loaf, keeping moist."
