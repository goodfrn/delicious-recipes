ingredients:
- "1 large round sourdough bread loaf"
- "7 oz Fontina cheese shredded"
- "3 oz Monterey Jack shredded"
- "1 tbsp pickled serrano peppers diced"
- "1 jalapeño minced"
- "4 tbsp unsalted butter melted"
- "1 tsp garlic-infused olive oil"
- "1/2 tsp smoked paprika"
- "1/4 tsp cracked black pepper"
- "1/4 tsp kosher salt"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 345°F. Don’t start hotter, you want cheese melted but bread not scorched."
- "Score loaf crosswise and lengthwise about 1-inch apart. Use serrated knife, nearly cut through but leave the bottom intact. Bread needs to bloom evenly but hold together for pulling apart."
- "Mix Fontina, Monterey Jack, minced jalapeño and diced pickled serrano hacks into a bowl. Pickled peppers bring acidity, cut through fat."
- "Stuff big handfuls of cheese blend deep into every crevice. Push until cheese peeks out top. Don’t skimp; dense pockets means gooey bites and hotspots. Keep stuffing."
- "Whisk melted butter with garlic olive oil, smoked paprika, salt, pepper. This spiced fat seeps into bread, adding layers beyond cheese."
- "Drizzle sauce evenly between slits. Make sure every crevice gets some moisture so bread steams inside foil wrapping."
- "Wrap loaf tightly in heavy foil. Place on baking sheet for easy handling and catch drips."
- "Bake covered 15 to 18 minutes. Listen for faint crackle of butter starting to crisp. Cheese should start to melt but bread still soft."
- "Remove foil loose top just to expose bread surface but don’t unwrap fully. Bake another 7 to 10 minutes uncovered until top browns deeply, cheese bubbles aggressively, and crust edges crisp. Visual blaze sign that it’s done."
- "Pull loaf out, rest 5 minutes. Avoid molten cheese burns. Pull apart by hand, no utensils needed."
- "Serve warm to get the pull apart effect and maximize melty cheese stretch."
