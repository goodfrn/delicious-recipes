ingredients:
- "3⁄4 cup rolled oats"
- "1 medium ripe banana"
- "3⁄4 cup unsweetened almond milk"
- "1 large egg"
- "2 tablespoons almond butter"
- "2 teaspoons maple syrup"
- "1 teaspoon baking powder"
- "1⁄2 teaspoon cinnamon"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1⁄8 teaspoon salt"
- "1 tablespoon cocoa powder"
- "3 tablespoons chocolate chips"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 340F. Grease two 8-ounce ovenproof bowls or ramekins with neutral oil or butter. Set aside."
- "Place oats, banana chunks, almond milk, egg, almond butter, maple syrup, baking powder, cinnamon, vanilla, salt, and cocoa powder into blender pitcher. Pulse then blend about 45 seconds. Mixture should be mostly uniform but not watery. Stop before turning fully smooth."
- "Transfer to bowl, fold in 1 tablespoon chocolate chips by hand. Preserve some chip texture, not all melt."
- "Divide batter between ramekins evenly. Level tops with spatula. Press remaining chocolate chips atop each bowl, slightly embedding them."
- "Bake about 22 to 28 minutes. Edges firm when shaken gently but center keeps slight jiggle. Check with toothpick: slight crumbs but not wet batter is good. Overbaking makes dry, underbaking yields too liquid."
- "Remove from oven. Let cool 5 to 7 minutes. Rest time solidifies structure and lets flavors meld. Serve warm."
- "Notes: Use rolled oats, not instant. Instant can make runny batter. Almond butter chosen for mildness; peanut butter or sunflower seed butter works. Maple syrup adds nuanced sweetness; honey can replace but taste transforms."
- "If mixture too thick, splash a bit extra milk. Too thin: add a tablespoon more oats before blending again. Ramekins must be heatproof. No metal lids. Use glass or ceramic."
- "Swap chocolate chips for chopped nuts or dried fruit. Cocoa powder optional, adds depth and changes batter color."
- "Avoid overblending banana to keep slight oat bite. Smoother blends lose chew. Letting bake rest transfers heat internally completing cooking. Test each oven, times vary."
