ingredients:
- "4 cups frozen steak-cut fries (can use thick-cut or homemade, pat dry to avoid sogginess)"
- "1 ½ cups sharp cheddar cheese, shredded (substitute with Monterey Jack or smoked gouda)"
- "1 cup caramelized shallots (replace yellow onions with shallots for mild, sweet depth)"
- "½ cup spicy aioli (blend ⅓ cup mayonnaise, 1 tbsp sriracha, 1 tsp lemon juice; swap sriracha for chipotle mayo for smokiness)"
- "Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste"
- "Chopped fresh chives or parsley for garnish (optional)"
instructions:
- "Preheat broiler on high. Position oven rack so fries sit 5 inches from heat source. Line rimmed baking sheet with foil to catch drips and ease cleanup."
- "Arrange cooked fries in a thin, even layer directly on foil. Aim for at most two fries deep to prevent steam trapping and preserve crispness."
- "Scatter shredded cheese evenly over fries. Watch as cheese melts and bubbles, shifts from matte to glossy, signals perfect gooeyness. This usually takes about 3-4 minutes; listen for faint crackling sounds."
- "Remove tray carefully — intense heat just moments ago. Dollop spoonfuls of caramelized shallots across cheesy fries. Spread evenly but leave some fries peeking through to keep textural contrasts."
- "Drizzle spicy aioli atop everything. Pop back under broiler 1-2 minutes only, enough to warm aioli and meld flavors but not burn or over-soften cheese layer."
- "Remove and let rest 1-2 minutes — hot and dangerously tempting. Garnish with herbs if using. Serve immediately. Fork recommended; messy fingers won’t stand a chance."
- "If fries get soggy before broiling, spread on another sheet, back under broiler for 1-2 minutes to revive crispness before adding cheese."
- "Caramelizing shallots takes approx 15 mins on medium-low heat with butter. Stir often; aim for soft amber, sweet aroma. Avoid dark brown spots - bitter taste."
- "Aioli can be prepared hours ahead. Store chilled. Fresh lemon juice cuts mayo’s richness, balances heat. Adjust sriracha upwards for more punch, or tone down for mild."
- "Alternative cheese melts better stacked moderate layers - don’t overcrowd or cheese won’t brown well. If slow-melting cheeses like mozzarella used, extend broiling by a minute, watch closely."
- "Adapt this method for air fryer fries: shorter broiling times needed; watch cheese closely to prevent burning."
