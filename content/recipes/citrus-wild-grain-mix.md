ingredients:
- "1 cup dried wild rice or substitute with black rice"
- "2 1/2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil"
- "3/4 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "3 1/2 tablespoons fresh lime juice or grapefruit juice"
- "1/3 cup dried tart cherries or golden raisins"
- "1 cup diced celery"
- "1/3 cup chopped raw cashews instead of pistachios"
- "1/3 cup crumbled goat cheese swapped for feta"
instructions:
- "Rinse grains under cold running water until water runs clear. Long soak not required but aids fluffiness."
- "Bring three cups water to boil. Add grains. Cover, simmer on lowest heat. Listen for gentle simmer sound. Cook about 15-20 minutes until individual grains start to swell and separate but still firm. Not mushy."
- "Drain excess water immediately to avoid sogginess. Transfer to rimmed tray or sheet pan to cool quickly. Stir once or twice to release steam and prevent clumping. Cool completely before dressing."
- "In a small bowl, whisk olive oil, salt, and freshly squeezed lime juice. Citrus acidity bright enough to cut starch but subtle. Adjust salt carefully; grains absorb seasoning."
- "Transfer cooled grains to bowl, drizzle dressing. Toss carefully so grains don’t crush or clump. Gentle fold technique recommended."
- "Add dried tart cherries for chew and tartness. Celery diced finely provides crunch contrast to soft grain and cheese."
- "Stir in toasted cashew pieces for buttery bite—can swap for toasted almonds or walnuts depending on pantry. Roast nuts in pan 2-3 minutes until fragrant but not browned."
- "Sprinkle crumbled goat cheese over top—cheese should be cool and crumbled so it disperses evenly without melting into warm grains."
- "Serve at room temperature or chilled. If chilled, bring out 15 minutes prior to eating so flavors bloom and texture softens slightly."
- "Common problem: overcooked grains turn sticky. Rescue by fluffing gently and adding a teaspoon more oil or acid dressing to reintroduce separation and brightness."
- "If citrus too tart, balance with small pinch of sugar or honey to tame sharp edges."
- "Leftovers hold well covered in fridge 2 days. Grain may absorb more dressing, toss before serving again."
- "Substituting cheeses: feta, goat, or ricotta salata all give crumbly texture but vary salt intensity."
- "Nuts can be omitted for allergies but reduce crunch—consider toasted seeds like pumpkin or sunflower."
- "Use tactile feel to judge grain doneness—firm bite but no raw chew. Visual cue: swollen grains with intact shape, no broken bits."
- "Prep extra grains ahead and keep chilled for fast assembly salads or bowls later in week."
- "Adjust citrus and oil to balance dryness or sogginess depending on grain freshness and storage."
