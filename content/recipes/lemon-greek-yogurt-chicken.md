ingredients:
- "4 boneless skinless chicken breasts"
- "1 cup labneh or thick Greek yogurt (substitute: plain Greek yogurt or crème fraîche)"
- "3 tablespoons fresh lime juice (substitute: lemon juice or white vinegar)"
- "2 medium roasted shallots, finely minced (substitute: garlic cloves or onion powder)"
- "3 tablespoons olive oil, plus extra for greasing"
- "1 teaspoon fresh chopped rosemary (substitute: thyme or oregano)"
- "1 teaspoon smoked paprika"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground cumin"
- "Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste"
- "1 cup crushed cornflakes (substitute: Panko breadcrumbs or crushed crackers)"
- "4 thin lime or lemon slices (optional, for topping)"
instructions:
- "Start with room temperature chicken breasts for even cooking. Trim excess fat or silver skin to prevent toughness."
- "In a large mixing bowl, season chicken generously with salt and pepper. Add labneh, lime juice, roasted shallots, olive oil, rosemary, smoked paprika, and cumin. Mix thoroughly to coat every surface; the acid tenderizes while the spices penetrate."
- "Cover tightly with plastic wrap or lid. Marinate in refrigerator between 3 and 5 hours. Overnight risks too much acid breakdown resulting in mushy meat."
- "Remove chicken from fridge 20 minutes before baking to lose chill, ensuring even cook later."
- "Preheat oven to 380°F. Lightly grease baking dish with olive oil to prevent sticking and promote caramelization on bottom."
- "Place chicken breasts in single layer. Press crushed cornflakes firmly on top forming an even crust. If desired, add one thin lime or lemon slice per breast for extra citrus aroma."
- "Cover the dish loosely with foil to trap moisture and prevent breadcrumbs from burning. Bake 45-50 minutes. Start checking internal temperature after 40 minutes. Done at 165°F but ideally 160-165°F for juicy texture."
- "Remove foil last 7-10 minutes to allow crust to crisp and turn golden brown. Watch closely; cornflake crust burns quickly."
- "Once out of oven, transfer chicken breasts to plate. Rest loosely tented with foil 8-12 minutes; juices redistribute during this time — don't skip or juices run out on cutting."
- "Serve warm with lightly steamed or roasted vegetable sides. A simple tzatziki or yogurt-mint sauce perks this up if desired."
