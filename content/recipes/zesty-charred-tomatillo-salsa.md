ingredients:
- "1 cup chopped tomatillos, husked and rinsed"
- "1/4 cup diced white onion"
- "3 cloves roasted garlic, skin removed"
- "1 medium poblano pepper, charred and chopped"
- "1 handful cilantro leaves"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground coriander"
- "1/2 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "1/4 cup water"
- "3 tablespoons fresh lime juice"
instructions:
- "Char poblano pepper directly over flame or under broiler until blackened and blistered all around. Seal in bowl covered with plastic wrap to steam for 10 minutes, then peel skin, remove seeds, chop coarsely."
- "Combine chopped tomatillos, diced onion, roasted garlic cloves, chopped poblano, cilantro, ground coriander, salt, water and lime juice in heavy-bottom saucepan."
- "Bring mixture to a vigorous simmer—loud bubble and hiss, watch for edges softening and tomatillos losing firmness. Reduce heat to low; let gentle simmer continue for 20 minutes or until tomatillos feel very tender and starting to collapse under backside of the spoon."
- "Remove from heat, cool 10 minutes - mixture will thicken slightly and flavor settles."
- "Use immersion blender, pulse blend to roughly chunky texture - bits left visible; no puree."
- "Taste and adjust salt and lime juice needed; acidity should brighten but not overpower."
- "Transfer salsa to airtight container; chill minimum 2 hours to allow flavors to marry and thicken. Stir before serving."
