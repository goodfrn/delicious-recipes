ingredients:
- "1 stick unsalted butter softened"
- "3/4 cup packed brown sugar"
- "1 tablespoon instant espresso powder"
- "2 teaspoons vanilla extract"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1 cup whole wheat flour"
- "1 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1 teaspoon baking powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "3 tablespoons almond butter"
- "4 ounces dark chocolate for drizzling (optional)"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 355 degrees Fahrenheit. Line sheet with parchment or silpat. Helps with clean removal."
- "Cream softened butter, almond butter, and brown sugar with paddle attachment or by hand until grainy and just combined. Scrape bowl sidewalls often. Texture matters here; don't overbeat or get mousse."
- "Add espresso powder, vanilla, then eggs. Beat until mixture is uniform with a glossy sheen. If batter looks curdled, no panic; keep mixing gently until smooth but stop before airy."
- "Sift flours with baking powder and salt. Gradually fold into wet mix with a spatula to avoid overmixing gluten. Dough has to be firm, pliable, sugar-cookie like, not sticky or runny."
- "Divide dough roughly in half. Shape each half into a log about 3 by 8 inches, compact but not dense. Press slightly flat on parchment-lined tray. Logs should hold shape but still yield under fingertip."
- "Bake at 355 for about 28 minutes. Watch edges; they're golden but not burnt. Center may still be soft but should spring back slightly when pressed. Cool logs fully on wire rack 20 minutes - this firms them up before slicing."
- "Using serrated knife, cut logs into 1/2-inch thick slices crosswise. Lay slices on baking sheet cut sides down – gaps between slices important to ensure even crisping later."
- "Return to oven. Toast at 355 about 14 minutes, flip slices halfway through. Listen for audible crackle as biscotti crisp. Edges should blush light brown, surface dry to touch, not burn or char."
- "Cool completely on wire racks to stop carryover cooking and prevent sogginess. Crisps up as it cools."
- "Optional: Melt dark chocolate carefully (double boiler or microwave in short bursts). Drizzle or dip biscotti edges. Let chocolate set fully before storage."
- "Store in airtight container at room temp. Keeps crunch up to two weeks. If softened, re-toast briefly in warm oven 300 degrees, watch closely."
