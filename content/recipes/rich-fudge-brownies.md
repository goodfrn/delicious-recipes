ingredients:
- "1 cup unsalted butter browned and melted"
- "1 1/4 cup granulated sugar"
- "1/2 tsp fine sea salt"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1 tsp vanilla extract"
- "1 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder"
- "3/4 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips"
- "FOR GLAZE==="
- "3/4 cup heavy cream"
- "5 tbsp unsalted butter browned"
- "1 cup light brown sugar packed"
- "1/4 tsp fine sea salt"
- "3/4 cup bittersweet chocolate chips"
- "1 tsp instant espresso powder"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven 350F. Line 8x8 pan with parchment, hang edges for easy removal. Brown butter slowly till nutty aroma, golden brown flecks form but don’t scorch. Remove from heat, pour into mixing bowl. Whisk in sugar and salt until shiny and slightly grainy. Add eggs one at a time, mixing fully into smooth, glossy mass. Stir in vanilla."
- "Sift cocoa and flour together to remove lumps. Fold cocoa-flour mix gently into wet batter to avoid tough brownies. Fold in semi-sweet chips last, fold in carefully, batter thick but soft. Pour into pan, smooth surface with offset spatula for even layer."
- "Bake 22 to 25 minutes. Watch edges gently pulling away from sides, center still jiggles slightly — indicates soft fudgy texture. Overbake and edges dry or center cracks. Remove pan and prepare glaze while brownies still hot, crucial."
- "For glaze, combine heavy cream, browned butter, brown sugar, and salt in saucepan over medium heat. Stir regularly. Bring to full rolling boil — bubbles covering bottom, rapid steam rising. Boil exactly 30 seconds, no more, or sugar can crystallize. Remove from heat immediately. Stir in chocolate chips and espresso powder. Stir until chips melt completely, glossy and smooth. If grainy, put back on very low heat, stir gently till smooth."
- "Let glaze cool 4 to 6 minutes until thickened but still pourable. Pour over warm brownies immediately. Use spatula to spread if needed. The warm brownie surface traps glaze, prevents it from separating or sliding off."
- "Cool completely on wire rack. Glaze will set to soft, pliable finish with slight shine. Chill 20 minutes if needed to firm up before slicing clean squares using a hot knife—dip in hot water, wipe dry between cuts."
- "Store in airtight container at room temp up to 3 days. To refresh, warm briefly to revive glaze shine and softness. Brown butter and espresso deepen flavor but can be swapped: vanilla for espresso, regular butter for browned, whole milk for cream but texture and flavor altered."
- "Common mistake: pouring glaze on cooled brownies causes separation or dull matte glaze. Fix by reheating brownies or glaze gently. Avoid chilling glaze before pouring or it hardens too fast. Stir glaze gently to avoid air bubbles or grainy texture."
