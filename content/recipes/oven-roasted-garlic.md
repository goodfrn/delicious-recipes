ingredients:
- "1 whole head garlic"
- "1 tbsp unsalted butter melted"
- "1 tbsp avocado oil (sub with grapeseed oil)"
- "1 pinch flaky sea salt"
- "1 pinch cracked black pepper"
instructions:
- "=== Preparing the Garlic ==="
- "Slice off about ¼ inch from the top of the garlic head. Aim to expose the tips of the cloves but not too much to dry out during roasting."
- "=== Seasoning and Wrapping ==="
- "Place the garlic head upright on a large sheet of heavy-duty aluminum foil."
- "Combine the melted butter with avocado oil; drizzle thoroughly over the exposed cloves. Butter brings richness and makes cloves golden; avocado oil adds high smoke point and mild flavor. Can swap oils depending on what’s available."
- "Sprinkle flaky sea salt and cracked black pepper over the top to season and draw out moisture."
- "Wrap the garlic tightly but leave room for steam to circulate inside. Seal foil, folding edges down securely."
- "=== Roasting and Timing ==="
- "Set oven to 375°F for moderate heat - slightly higher than usual 350°F to pull out more caramelization but avoid burning."
- "Place wrapped garlic on middle rack. Listen for faint sizzling—steam developing inside foil."
- "Bake for 25 to 30 minutes. Exact time depends on head size and oven hotspots."
- "Use touch test: squeeze gently once cooled slightly. Should feel soft, cloves tender but keep shape. If hard, give 5 more minutes."
- "Remove from oven. Let cool wrapped at room temp for 10 minutes so steam finishes cooking internally."
- "=== Using Roasted Garlic ==="
- "Squeeze cloves out by pressing base of head downwards into bowl or onto bread."
- "Looks jammy, sweet with nutty aroma."
- "Mix into sauces, mash into potatoes, or blend for dips."
- "Store unused cloves in airtight container up to 3 days refrigerated."
