ingredients:
- "1 can refrigerated biscuit dough or pizza dough"
- "3 oz sliced spicy Italian sausage or soppressata, roughly chopped"
- "2 tablespoons provolone cheese, shredded"
- "3 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted"
- "1 teaspoon dried oregano"
- "½ teaspoon garlic salt"
- "2 tablespoons grated pecorino romano cheese"
- "1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped"
- "Marinara sauce for serving"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 380ºF. Grease a round baking dish lightly with olive oil or spray; set aside."
- "Unroll dough onto a floured surface. If thick, roll gently into a rectangle about ⅛ inch thick. Dough from can usually thin enough but feel. Cut width-wise into 10 equal strips using a pizza cutter or sharp knife."
- "Scatter spicy sausage evenly across each strip, press lightly to set. Top with provolone cheese, about 1½ tablespoon per strip. Avoid overfilling or risk leaking during bake."
- "Starting at one short end, roll each strip tightly like a jelly roll, seam side down into pan. Pack rolls snug but not smushed together to keep edges crisp."
- "Brush rolls generously with melted butter, sprinkle oregano, garlic salt evenly then scatter pecorino on top. Adds crunch and powerful punch. No heavy hand here."
- "Bake 25 minutes or until dough pulls back from pan edges, rolls show deep golden brown with crispy spots. Butter will bubble and scent will shift to roasted garlic and herbs."
- "Remove, sprinkle parsley instantly to avoid wilting in heat. Rest for 5 minutes to settle cheese inside before serving."
- "Serve hot with thick marinara or herbed tomato dip as a counterpoint. Rolls stand up well to messy dipping. Expect slight crunch, chewy dough, spicy heat softened by melted cheese."

- "Roll the dough thin but not tearing. Aim for about an eighth inch thick. Thinner bakes faster, but cracks and leaks ruin filling. Practice helps."
- "Brush that melted butter generously. It aids in browning and flavor - no skimping. Swap oil if needed, but butter adds the best aroma and crisp."
- "Watch the edges. Look for golden brown and slightly bubbling butter. Don't rely solely on time; every oven varies. Rely on visual cues."
- "Let those rolls rest before serving. Cheese sets up, reducing burns. Three to five minutes is good. Presentation matters too, align neatly."
