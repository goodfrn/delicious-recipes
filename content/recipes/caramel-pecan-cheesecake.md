ingredients:
- "1 1/2 cups crushed digestive biscuits"
- "1/2 cup chopped walnuts"
- "7 tablespoons unsalted butter melted"
- "24 ounces softened cream cheese"
- "2/3 cup coconut sugar"
- "1 1/2 teaspoons vanilla extract"
- "3 large eggs"
- "1 cup chopped hazelnuts"
- "5 tablespoons unsalted butter divided"
- "1/2 cup coconut sugar for topping"
- "1/4 cup honey"
- "1/3 cup heavy cream"
- "pinch sea salt"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 350 F. Grease 9-inch springform pan thoroughly with butter or use baking parchment to prevent sticking (nonstick sprays have oils that can interfere with crust bond)."
- "Mix crushed digestive biscuits, walnuts, and melted butter until mixture is damp and holds shape when pressed. Press firmly into bottom of pan, use bottom of a glass for even compact crust; no loose crumbs or gaps."
- "In stand mixer bowl, whip cream cheese with coconut sugar and vanilla until thick and silky but not overbeaten (avoid incorporating excessive air). Scrape down sides well to avoid lumps."
- "Add eggs one at a time; blend fully after each addition. Stop as soon as batter is uniform. Overmixing can cause cracks during baking due to excess air."
- "Pour filling onto crust, distribute evenly. Tap pan sharply on counter twice to release trapped air bubbles — cracks start here if skipped."
- "Bake for about 50 minutes. Look for edges set and center slightly wobbly but not liquid. A clean toothpick doesn’t work here. Visual and tactile check better than timer. Oven door wide open once. Let cool inside oven with door ajar for 50 minutes to avoid sudden temp drop which causes cracks."
- "Remove from oven, cool completely on wire rack. Refrigerate minimum 5 hours, overnight ideal to firm up and develop flavor."
- "For topping, lower oven to 320 F and toast chopped hazelnuts spread single layer on rimmed baking sheet 8 minutes until aromatic and golden—watch closely to avoid burning nuts which turn bitter fast."
- "Melt 3 tablespoons butter in saucepan over medium heat with coconut sugar and honey. Stir constantly until bubbling and sugar fully dissolved. Quickly add heavy cream and salt, stirring to combine. Bring back to gentle boil, then simmer 2 minutes until sauce thickens slightly. Remove from heat, stir in remaining 2 tablespoons butter for gloss and silky texture."
- "Let caramel cool to room temperature before folding in toasted hazelnuts."
- "Pour topping evenly over chilled cheesecake. Set 15 minutes in fridge to let caramel firm slightly."
- "Release cheesecake sides by sliding a sharp knife between cake and pan sides before removing ring to prevent sticking or tearing."
- "Slice with hot wet knife for clean cuts. Wipe blade between slices. Serve chilled."
