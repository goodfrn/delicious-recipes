ingredients:
- "1 bunch asparagus (20-24 spears), woody ends trimmed"
- "6 slices pancetta or thin-sliced bacon, each slice cut in half"
- "2 tablespoons maple syrup"
- "1 tablespoon tamari or soy sauce"
- "½ teaspoon garlic powder"
- "¼ teaspoon ground black pepper"
- "¼ teaspoon flaky sea salt"
- "1 teaspoon toasted sesame seeds"
- "¼ teaspoon chili flakes optional"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 410°F. Line a rimmed sheet pan with foil or parchment for easy cleanup. Spray lightly with oil. This stops bacon sticking and helps crisping."
- "Group 3-5 asparagus spears, depending on thickness. Wrap each bundle with half a slice of pancetta, overlap ends underneath so bacon won’t unravel when cooking. Lay bundles seam side down on the pan."
- "Slide pan into the oven. Roast for about 6 minutes, listen for subtle popping from bacon fat rendering, asparagus stalks begin softening but still firm. Pull pan out carefully."
- "Using tongs, gently flip bundles over so bacon gets even crisp all sides. Slide pan back in for another 6-7 minutes. Watch asparagus tips; when they start to brown and crisp, bacon edges caramelize dark golden, it’s done."
- "Meanwhile, whisk maple syrup, tamari, garlic powder, salt and black pepper in a small bowl. That syrupy mixture is your glaze."
- "Pull pan from oven when bacon is crisp and asparagus tender but not mushy. Brush glaze liberally over bundles, shiny and sticky."
- "Return pan to the oven for 3-4 minutes more or set to broil for 90 seconds but watch like a hawk. Look for bubbling syrup caramelizing and dark flecks forming without burning."
- "Remove and immediately sprinkle toasted sesame seeds and optional chili flakes on top for contrast and a kick."
- "Serve warm straight from the pan. Enjoy the crackle when cutting the bacon, the juicy snap of spears, sweet-salty punch from glaze."
- "If pancetta not available, use thick-cut smoky bacon or prosciutto but consider reducing salt in glaze. No maple? Substitute honey or agave syrup. Tamari can be swapped with regular soy sauce but expect saltier result. Toss chili flakes to taste or skip for mild."
- "Common fail: soggy bacon due to low heat or overcrowding bundles. Space apart, high temp, flip halfway. If overcooked, bacon turns leathery; remove before that and let rest as residual heat finishes cooking asparagus inside."
