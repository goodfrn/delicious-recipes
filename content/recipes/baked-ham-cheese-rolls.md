ingredients:
- "2 cans crescent roll dough"
- "1 ½ tbsp dijon mustard"
- "10 oz sharp white cheddar cheese slices"
- "10 oz smoked turkey breast slices, folded in half"
- "3 tbsp melted butter"
- "½ tsp smoked paprika"
- "1 tsp dried herb blend (oregano, basil)"
- "½ tsp garlic powder"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 350F. Unroll first crescent dough in baking dish. Press seams closed fully to avoid leaks. Dock lightly with fork. Bake about 12 minutes till dough slightly puffed and set but not browned."
- "Remove from oven. Spread dijon mustard evenly over warmed dough layer using a spatula or spoon."
- "Place cheddar slices in even single layer covering mustard completely—important for uniform melt."
- "Fold turkey slices in half to keep thickness manageable. Layer turkey over cheese evenly. Avoid clumping."
- "Add another cheddar slice layer directly on turkey, completely covering meat."
- "Place second crescent dough sheet over cheese. Seal all edges and seams tightly by pinching dough. Should be airtight to trap moisture."
- "In small bowl whisk melted butter with smoked paprika, dried herbs, and garlic powder."
- "Brush top dough layer generously with seasoned butter mixture for golden crisp finish."
- "Bake 18-22 minutes until top is deep golden brown and sounds hollow when tapped lightly. Excess bubbling juices indicate seams not sealed well."
- "Let rest 8 minutes after baking. Slice into 8 roughly 3 ½ inch squares. Firm but tender, hot cheesy layers inside."
