ingredients:
- "10 ounces rotini or corkscrew pasta, uncooked"
- "8 ounces cream cheese, softened"
- "3/4 cup whole milk or buttermilk"
- "1 package (1 ounce) ranch seasoning mix"
- "1 cup finely diced celery"
- "1/2 cup minced shallots (substitute for onion for milder taste)"
- "1/2 cup buffalo wing sauce (such as Frank’s) or chipotle hot sauce"
- "2 cups cooked shredded chicken (rotisserie is fine)"
- "1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese, divided"
- "1/2 cup crumbled blue cheese (swapped for half original mozzarella)"
- "1 tablespoon unsalted butter or olive oil for prepping pan"
- "Green onions sliced thin, for garnish"
- "Pinch of black pepper"
instructions:
- "Heat large pot salted water to a full rolling boil. Add pasta and cook until still slightly firm (al dente) but not crunchy. Drain immediately, rinse briefly under cool water to stop cooking. Set aside on a sheet pan to cool and prevent sticking."
- "Preheat oven to 380F. Generously coat a 9×13 casserole dish with softened butter or olive oil—spray can cause uneven browning and soggy edges."
- "In a large bowl, beat softened cream cheese with milk until smooth lumps are gone—cold milk can cause curdling if added too fast. Once silky, stir in ranch seasoning and a pinch of black pepper."
- "Add diced celery and minced shallots to sauce. These add crunch and bite, essential against creamy backdrop. Next stir in buffalo or chipotle sauce—taste, should be just spicy enough to tingle but not burn out flavors."
- "Incorporate shredded chicken and pasta carefully folding—don’t overmix or pasta will break apart. Half the cheddar and all the blue cheese go in now, folded evenly. Blue cheese adds tang and complexity instead of mozzarella's neutral melt."
- "Transfer mixture to prepared dish. Press lightly to compact but keep some air for bubbling during bake. Sprinkle remaining cheddar and a few extra crumbles of blue cheese on top for a crust that browns beautifully."
- "Bake uncovered at 380F for 30 minutes. Watch surface carefully. The edges should bubble aggressively, cheese top golden with small brown spots. If cheese looks pale after 30, broil very briefly—but watch closely or it burns."
- "Remove from oven and let stand for 8-10 minutes. This resting allows the casserole to set, making it easier to slice. Garnish with sliced green onions for a sharp fresh pop."
- "Serve warm. Leftovers reheat well in microwave with a splash of milk added, covered loosely to maintain moisture."
- "Common pitfalls—overcooked pasta turns mushy after baking. Drain and cool well. Using cold cream cheese straight from fridge without softening risks lumps in sauce. If missing fresh celery, finely diced jicama or zucchini can work but add after cooking for crunch."
