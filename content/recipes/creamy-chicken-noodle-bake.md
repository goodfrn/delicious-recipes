ingredients:
- "10 ounces wide egg noodles"
- "1 can cream of chicken soup (10.5 ounces)"
- "1 can cream of celery soup (10.5 ounces)"
- "1 cup Greek yogurt (full fat or 2%)"
- "1 ½ cups whole milk"
- "1 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "1 teaspoon onion powder"
- "½ teaspoon black pepper"
- "3 cups cooked shredded chicken"
- "1 cup crushed buttery crackers (e.g., Ritz)"
- "4 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted"
- "6 individual vegetable cups (Libby’s-style or any preferred brand), drained"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 355°F. Lightly grease a 9×13-inch casserole pan with nonstick spray to prevent sticking; avoids tricky cleanup."
- "Boil egg noodles in salted water until just shy of al dente—about 1-2 minutes less than package calls for. Drain thoroughly; any stray water dilutes the sauce. Set aside."
- "In a large bowl, whisk together cream of chicken and cream of celery soups with Greek yogurt for tang, whole milk for looseness, garlic powder, onion powder, and pepper. Achieve smooth consistency without lumps."
- "Add cooked chicken pieces and noodles to the bowl. Fold gently but completely; avoid breaking noodles or shredding chicken further. The dry noodles soak sauce during baking—don’t over mix."
- "Transfer mixture to prepared pan, spread evenly. This layering ensures even cooking and heat distribution."
- "In a small bowl, toss crushed crackers with melted butter till all crumbs absorb fat evenly. Sprinkle as an even blanket over casserole. This topping crisps golden with nutty aroma rather than soggy flour."
- "Slide casserole into oven. Bake about 30 minutes. Look for bubbling edges and golden cracker top that starts to darken but not burn. The aroma will shift—savory, rich, warming the kitchen."
- "Remove and let stand 5 minutes to let internal steam settle. Makes cutting cleaner and flavors mingle."
- "To serve, open vegetable cups, drain fully to avoid watering down casserole. Stir handful into individual plates for freshness, texture contrast, and color punch. Skip mixing upfront—keeps veggies crisp."
- "If lacking cracker crumbs, substitute with crushed crackers or panko breadcrumbs mixed with paprika or grated Parmesan for extra bite. For dairy-free swap, full-fat coconut yogurt and almond milk work with slightly less tang."
- "Leftovers reheat well in microwave with a splash of milk to restore creaminess; topping may soften but the core remains comforting."
