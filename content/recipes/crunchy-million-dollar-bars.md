ingredients:
- "1 cup light honey instead of light corn syrup"
- "1 cup granulated sugar"
- "3/4 cup creamy almond butter replacing peanut butter"
- "1 tsp pure vanilla extract"
- "5 cups puffed rice cereal"
- "3/4 cup semisweet chocolate chips"
- "3/4 cup butterscotch chips"
- "3 tbsp unsalted butter"
instructions:
- "Spray 9×13 pan lightly with oil, set aside. Honey and sugar into medium saucepan, stir constantly over medium heat. Watch closely — sugar dissolves, then tiny bubbles form, a low rolling boil starts."
- "Remove from heat immediately once boiling begins. Stir in almond butter and vanilla fast; almond butter’s oil can separate if overheated. Mix till silky but stop when just combined."
- "Fold in puffed rice cereal until every grain glistens with syrup. Press firmly into pan — pack it down with spatula or clean hands to avoid airy gaps that cause crumbly bars."
- "Ready for topping? Melt chocolate chips, butterscotch chips and butter in a double boiler set over simmering water. Avoid direct heat, stir gently, remove as soon as mixture is smooth and glossy — overheating dulls chocolate sheen."
- "Pour topping evenly over pressed cereal layer; tilting pan helps spread quickly before chocolate firms up. Tiny ripples on surface smoothing out means ideal thickness."
- "Chill in fridge for 25-30 minutes or until topping is hard but not brittle; tapping pan lightly sounds dull, not clink-clank. Slice while cool."
- "Store bars in airtight container at room temperature. If bars soften, pop in fridge just long enough to reset chocolate."
- "Substitute notes: Honey replaces corn syrup for natural sweetness but heats faster — watch boil carefully to prevent caramelization. Almond butter adds subtle nutty depth plus softer texture but slightly less binding. If unavailable, classic creamy peanut butter fine."
