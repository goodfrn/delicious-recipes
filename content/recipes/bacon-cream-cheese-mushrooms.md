ingredients:
- "1 lb fresh white mushrooms"
- "6 slices thick-cut bacon"
- "4 oz cream cheese softened"
- "2 tbsp sour cream"
- "1 tsp garlic powder"
- "1 tsp onion powder"
- "1/4 cup shredded aged sharp cheddar"
- "Salt and black pepper to taste"
- "2 tbsp bacon grease reserved"
- "Optional: 1 tsp smoked paprika"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 345 degrees Fahrenheit. Lightly grease a rimmed baking pan to prevent sticking and catching drippings."
- "Wipe mushrooms clean with a damp kitchen towel. Avoid washing under water, mushrooms soak up moisture and get soggy. Remove stems and finely chop, keep caps whole and placed cavity side up on pan."
- "In a large skillet over medium heat, cook bacon strips until crisp, about 8 to 10 minutes. Listen for steady sizzling; it means proper rendering, no burning. Remove bacon and drain on paper towels. Crumble once cooled."
- "Pour off all but roughly 2 tablespoons of bacon fat from skillet. Return skillet to medium heat, add chopped mushroom stems. Sauté slowly until stems visibly shrivel and soften, edges browned, about 4 to 6 minutes."
- "Transfer stems to a mixing bowl. Add cream cheese, sour cream, crumbled bacon, garlic powder, onion powder, shredded cheddar, and optional smoked paprika. Stir gently just to combine, avoid overmixing to keep chunk texture."
- "Season filling with salt and pepper cautiously; bacon is salty already."
- "Spoon a generous tablespoon of the mixture into each mushroom cap, pressing slightly to fill."
- "Bake uncovered for 18 to 23 minutes. Look for slight browning on cheese surface and bubbling near edges. Avoid overbaking; mushrooms release moisture and can get watery."
- "Remove when sizzling and golden. Let sit 5 minutes for filling to firm up slightly. Serve warm."
