ingredients:
- "6 cups mixed salad greens or romaine lettuce roughly torn"
- "2 ripe nectarines pitted and thinly sliced"
- "1 cup fresh blackberries"
- "3 ounces crumbled feta cheese"
- "1 small shallot thinly sliced"
- "3/4 cup candied pecans roughly chopped"
- "2 tablespoons apricot preserves"
- "3 tablespoons apple cider vinegar"
- "1 tablespoon Dijon mustard"
- "1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil"
- "1/2 teaspoon salt more to taste"
instructions:
- "Arrange greens in a shallow bowl or divide among four plates. Avoid overcrowding; leaves need room to breathe or they wilt fast."
- "Scatter nectarine slices evenly atop the greens. Fruit should glisten slightly with freshness; dull edges mean it’s past prime."
- "Add blackberries, then crumble feta cheese over everything. Feta adds a creamy tang but crumbles differently than blue cheese, cleaner bite here."
- "Sprinkle thinly sliced shallot and roughly chopped candied pecans last. Pecans offer crunch without overpowering sweetness walnuts sometimes do."
- "Combine apricot preserves, apple cider vinegar, Dijon mustard, salt, and olive oil in a jar with a tight lid or a small bowl. Vigorously shake or whisk until emulsified; dressing should thicken slightly and coat back of spoon."
- "Wait to dress salad until right before serving. Dressing too early makes greens limp and fruit soggy, colors fade."
- "Drizzle dressing over each plate or toss gently in the bowl just before plating. Toss carefully—fruit bruises easily."
- "Taste salad after dressing. Adjust salt or add extra drizzle of cider vinegar if needed to brighten flavor."
- "If pecans are unavailable, substitute toasted sliced almonds or walnuts but toast nuts lightly to avoid stale flavors."
- "For a no-shallot version, thinly sliced red onion or scallions work but rinse onions well to soften pungency."
