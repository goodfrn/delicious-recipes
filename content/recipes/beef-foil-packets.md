- "1 pound ground beef"
- "1 teaspoon salt"
- "1 teaspoon smoked paprika"
- "1 teaspoon onion powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon black pepper"
- "1 cup sliced bell peppers"
- "1 cup sliced zucchini"
- "4 large sheets heavy-duty aluminum foil"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven or grill to 345°F. That’s slightly under 350°F to compensate for heat variance. You want steady heat but no flare-ups."
- "Mix ground beef with salt, smoked paprika instead of garlic powder, onion powder replacing some common spices, and black pepper. Don’t overmix or patties get tough. Just enough to blend evenly."
- "Form into 4 patties, about thick enough to hold shape but not dense."
- "Place patty center on each foil sheet. Scatter bell peppers and zucchini on top. These vegetables swap potatoes or corn for something lighter and with bite."
- "Add a pinch of salt and pepper again over veggies for layers of seasoning. Don’t skip; tendency is to under-season the veg, fat and meat balance out saltiness."
- "Seal tight into foil packets, no steam should escape but leave some room inside for juices to circulate. Crimp edges well."
- "Set packets on rack or grill grate. Cook 55-65 minutes. Look for foil to puff up and sizzle starts inside. That sound is fat rendering, steam trapping. Don’t peek too early; let it finish cooking."
- "Remove from heat, let packets rest 5 minutes before opening. Steam blast is hot enough to burn."
- "Open carefully – release steam away from face – check patties for firm texture but still juicy inside. Veggies soften but should retain slight bite, not go mushy."
- "Serve directly in foil or transfer to plates, spooning juice from the packet. Adds flavor and moisture back."
