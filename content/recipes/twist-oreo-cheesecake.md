ingredients:
- "Crust"
- "12 double-stuffed sandwich cookies, filling reserved"
- "2 tablespoons granulated sugar"
- "4 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted"
- ""
- "Filling"
- "6 ounces white chocolate chips"
- "Reserved cookie filling from crust"
- "1 14-ounce block full-fat cream cheese, softened"
- "1 cup heavy cream"
- "10 crushed sandwich cookies, chopped roughly"
- ""
- "Topping"
- "1/2 cup heavy cream"
- "1 tablespoon powdered sugar"
- "Additional whole oreos for garnish"
instructions:
- "=== Crust (if baking) ==="
- "Preheat oven to 320°F, grease 9-inch springform pan with butter or PAM. Pulverize whole cookies in food processor until fine crumbs, scraping sides. Mix sugar and melted butter just enough to bind. Press crumb mix into bottom and halfway up sides of pan. Use back of spoon firmly, so crust holds. Bake 12–15 minutes or edges just start to darken slightly. Smells toasty. Cool thoroughly on wire rack; warm crust means filling sets poorly."
- ""
- "=== Filling ==="
- "Microwave reserved cookie filling and white chocolate chips in 20-second bursts, stirring mid-way. Melt completely but avoid burning or seizing; glossy, fluid but not grainy. Cool mixture to barely lukewarm. Meanwhile, whip cream on medium-high speed with hand mixer until soft-medium peaks, about 3-5 minutes. Peaks should hold shape but tip curls gently—not stiff. In large bowl, beat softened cream cheese with cooled chocolate mixture until combined and uniform. Mixture thickens, almost stubborn. Fold in one quarter whipped cream with spatula, breaking up thickness, scrapping sides well. Then gently fold in remaining cream, preserving air. Add chopped cookies last, fold by hand—no machines! Rough edges keep texture contrast."
- ""
- "Transfer filling into cooled crust. Smooth top with offset spatula. Cover lightly with plastic wrap and chill at least 5 hours, preferably overnight to fully set and flavor marry."
- ""
- "=== Topping ==="
- "Whip remaining 1/2 cup cream with powdered sugar to stiff peaks—trap air but don’t overbeat to butter. Fill piping bag fitted with large star tip (1M or equivalent) or ziplock with corner cut. Pipe rosettes or decorative lines atop cheesecake as desired. Press whole or halved cookies gently into whipped cream. Chill until serving. Holds shape well up to 5 days refrigerated. Cake benefits from resting; flavors deepen, texture tightens."
- ""
- "=== Notes & Tips ==="
- "If no food processor, place cookies in ziplock and crush with rolling pin but expect coarser crust. White chocolate can be swapped for milk or dark chocolate if preferred; adjust sugar accordingly. Use cream cheese at room temp for better mixing, no lumps. Whipping cream too cold or warm affects volume; fridge cool but not chilled. Folding is crucial—ripping or deflating ruins lightness; fold slow, turn bowl, use spatula’s edge to pull and fold from bottom. Cold fridge chills slow if cake still warm; plan ahead. Baking crust longer risks dry, crumbly; shorter risks soft crumble, unsturdy base."
- ""
- "Keep cheesecake covered to prevent fridge odors absorption. Cheesecake edges sink slightly during cooling? Normal. Use offset spatula to fix topping surface before piping. White chocolate sometimes grit? Beat well or melt longer in microwave in short bursts. If filling too runny, chill mixture briefly before adding whipped cream. For festive twist, add 1 tsp coffee or espresso powder to crust mix for depth or fold small chopped nuts into topping cream for crunch."
