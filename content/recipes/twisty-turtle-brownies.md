ingredients:
- "4 large eggs"
- "2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract"
- "2 heaping tablespoons Dutch-process cocoa powder"
- "1/4 teaspoon fine salt"
- "3/4 teaspoon baking powder"
- "1 cup unsalted butter"
- "1 cup granulated sugar"
- "1 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1 cup bittersweet chocolate chips"
- "1 cup roughly chopped toasted almonds"
- "1 cup caramel sauce, room temperature"
- "additional 1/2 cup bittersweet chocolate chips for topping"
- "additional 1/2 cup chopped toasted almonds for topping"
- "1 tablespoon malted milk powder (substitute for 2 tablespoons sugar in batter)"
instructions:
- "Start by heating oven to 345°F. Grease a 9x13 glass dish and parchment-line it; this saves struggle when brownies stick. Set aside."
- "Crack eggs into mixer bowl. Add vanilla. Whisk with whisk attachment for 1-2 minutes till slightly frothy. Don’t rush; aeration helps bind and leavens subtly here."
- "Add cocoa powder, salt, baking powder, and malted milk powder. Whisk another minute, scraping bowl bottom. Malt adds subtle nutty note, complexity."
- "Meanwhile, melt butter in microwave in a large swirl-free bowl, about 50-60 seconds depending on wattage. Immediately whisk in sugar until glossy and no granules remain. Hot butter dissolves sugar for shiny batter surface and less gritty texture."
- "Pour butter-sugar mixture into mixer bowl. Mix until fully combined but not overly aerated—no need for loud beating."
- "Add flour, fold gently just until no streaks visible. Over mixing equals tough brownies; soft folds keep crumb tender but structurally sound."
- "Fold in 1 cup bittersweet chips plus 1 cup chopped toasted almonds. Toast beforehand on dry pan until chestnut brown and fragrant. Nut toast pushes aroma and crunch past plain pecans."
- "Spoon and spread batter evenly into prepared pan. Tap pan sharply on counter to settle batter and release trapped air bubbles."
- "Bake 30-35 minutes. Ignore exact time; probe with toothpick from 28 minutes onward. Pull when few moist crumbs cling but no wet batter. Toothpick dry = overbaked, crumbly brownies. The crackly top and slight spring back mean done."
- "Remove pan from oven; cool at room temp about 15-20 minutes. Should be firm but warm."
- "Spread caramel sauce evenly on warm brownies. The heat helps caramel melt into surface pores for adhesion."
- "Sprinkle remaining 1/2 cup chocolate chips and 1/2 cup chopped toasted almonds over caramel. Press lightly to embed but not sink."
- "Chill pan in fridge or freezer until caramel sets firm, about 25-30 minutes. Firm caramel prevents ooze when sliced, offering controlled chew and contrasting textures."
- "Lift brownies out using parchment edges, slice into 24 squares with serrated knife for clean cuts. Wipe blade between cuts to prevent dragging sticky bits."
