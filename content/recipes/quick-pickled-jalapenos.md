ingredients:
- "4 cups jalapeños sliced thin, 3-5 mm thick"
- "4 large garlic cloves peeled"
- "1 tablespoon coriander seeds"
- "1 cup distilled white vinegar"
- "1 cup water"
- "1 ½ tablespoons dark brown sugar"
- "1 ¼ teaspoon kosher salt"
instructions:
- "Gather 2 clean 16 oz wide-mouth mason jars or a single 32 oz jar with lid. Wearing nitrile gloves, slice jalapeños evenly between 3 to 5 mm thick for balance of heat and structural integrity. No sloppiness here; inconsistent thickness leads to unpredictable texture."
- "Add garlic cloves and coriander seeds into the jar(s) with sliced peppers; coriander seeds replace black peppercorns, adding a subtle citrus note."
- "In a saucepan over medium heat, combine vinegar, water, brown sugar, and kosher salt. Stir frequently, listen for the slight hiss and bubbling as sugar and salt dissolve, about 5 to 6 minutes. No need to reach a full boil. Too hot—kills bright acids; too cold—sugar lingers undissolved, gritty finish."
- "Pour the warm brine carefully into the jar(s), pushing jalapeños gently down with a clean utensil to fully submerge. Uncovered air leads to mold; if peppers float, weight them with a small glass or fermentation weight. Leftover liquid acceptable but aim for minimal waste."
- "Seal the jar lid tightly. Set on counter at room temperature for roughly 25 to 35 minutes. Watch for condensation inside the glass and aroma beginning to evolve—sharp, slightly sweet vinegar mingling with garlic and coriander cues readiness for chilling."
- "Refrigerate immediately after room temp sitting. Minimum 20 hours before using; longer for deeper flavor or softer texture. Refrigeration slows fermentation, preserving crispness while taming raw heat."
- "Use within 4 weeks. If cloudiness or slime appears, discard. Clean jars and lids well to avoid contamination. Gloves mandatory during prep to avoid accidental eye or skin irritation."
