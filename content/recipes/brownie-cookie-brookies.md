instructions:
- "==="
- "Cookie dough dry blend salt baking powder baking soda flour; whisk to break lumps. Set aside."
- "In another bowl strike butter sugar mix on low till pasty and just blended not over whipped. Add egg vanilla, pulse blending fully."
- "Add dry mix gradually, low speed or fold. Scrape bottom pockets. Stir in chocolate chunks last; avoid overmix, keeps chew and dough integrity."
- "==="
- "Brownie dry mix salt flour cocoa powder sift then toss together well to avoid lumps. Reserve."
- "Butter melted warm, pour into bowl sugars; on medium beat until granules start dissolving, 2 minutes. Important: creaming sugars into fat gives lift and texture."
- "Add vanilla olive oil eggs; blend fully 2 minutes. Oil keeps batter moist and extends shelf life versus butter only. Do not rush mixing or risk toughness."
- "Fold in dry cocoa flour blend, no flour streaks but avoid over mixing. Quickly fold in chocolate chips evenly. Batter thick, glossy, dense but pliable."
- "==="
- "Heat oven 350F. Line 9×13 pan with parchment, edges extending for lifting out."
- "Drop rounded spoonfuls cookie dough and brownie batter side to side alternating over surface; full coverage no holes."
- "Use butter knife, swirl gently through surfaces, keep elements distinct but marring together for bite contrast. Less swirling better for layering effect."
- "Cover with foil, mid rack bake 19-22 min. Peek edges, they firm and set but center jiggles slightly. Remove foil, bake 17-20 minutes more till top golden and toothpick inserted shows moist crumbs not wet batter."
- ""
- "Cool on rack 15 minutes before slicing. Warm brookies offer soft chocolate chew and tender crumb. Store airtight for 3 days or freeze individually."
