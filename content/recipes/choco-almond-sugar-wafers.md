instructions:
- "Spread a silicone baking mat on a rimmed tray. Parchment can stick if chocolate is too hot or thin; silicone better for peel."
- "Slow melt white chocolate in a double boiler or microwave stirring every 20 seconds. Watch - overheating makes thick gritty chocolate. Smooth, glossy texture signals ready."
- "Hold each wafer by the edge. Dip only half or one-third into warm white chocolate. Let excess drip off. Avoid hefty coats that crack when cooled."
- "Place carefully on silicone mat. Immediately sprinkle crushed pistachios and a light pinch of flaky sea salt over the wet chocolate. The salt wakes up the white chocolate’s sweetness."
- "Keep wafers at room temp just until chocolate firms up. Gloss loses shine when too cold or refrigerated early. Rapid chilling causes condensation, dulling surface."
- "Once set—firm but not brittle—transfer wafers to airtight container between sheets of wax paper. Protects crunch and keeps the pistachios fresh."
- "If white chocolate thickens before all wafers are dipped, gently reheat. Stir slowly to maintain smooth melt without seizing."
- "Cracked chocolate? Stop dipping. Warm again. Cooling waves cause fractures."
- "Use pity chip flavor substitutions: milk chocolate or dark provide richer finish. Macadamia nuts swap for pistachios, but toast nuts first for best aroma and crunch."
