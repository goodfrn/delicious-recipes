ingredients:
- "1 lb lean ground beef"
- "1 medium shallot finely chopped"
- "1 tsp onion powder"
- "1 tsp garlic powder"
- "1 tsp salt"
- "½ tsp freshly ground black pepper"
- "2 tbsp ghee (or clarified butter)"
- "3 tbsp buckwheat flour (substitute all-purpose)"
- "2 cups beef stock"
- "1 tsp low sodium soy sauce (replace Worcestershire sauce)"
- "1 tbsp tomato paste (replace Better than Bouillon)"
- "1 cup unsweetened almond milk (or whole milk)"
- "Chopped fresh parsley for garnish"
instructions:
- "Start heating a heavy skillet on medium-high. Toss in ground beef, shallots, onion powder, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Break beef apart with spatula. Sizzle and hear the popping. Brown until no pink visible, beef edges crisping but not burnt, about 6 to 7 minutes. Shallots soften, fragrant onion aroma fills air."
- "Lift beef mixture onto a plate lined with paper towels. Rest a minute; let excess fat drain. Wipe skillet gently to remove residual fat but don’t wash."
- "Drop heat to medium. Melt ghee until shimmering but not smoking — that buttery aroma signals right heat level."
- "Sprinkle buckwheat flour evenly over melted ghee. Whisk fast to avoid clumps; the goal is a light golden roux with a faint nutty scent. Be patient; too dark and it tastes bitter."
- "Slowly drizzle beef stock into roux while whisking steady. Bubbles form; loosen browned bits stuck to pan without scraping too hard. This deglazing picks up flavor. No clumps. Simmer the gravy, stir often, until it thickens—think coat the back of a spoon—about 4 to 6 minutes."
- "Stir in soy sauce and tomato paste. The paste adds depth; dissolve it in gradually. Taste. Salt or acid may need adjusting here."
- "Pour almond milk in streams while whisking. Incorporates silky texture without curdling fear. Heat down to low now. Add reserved beef mixture back into skillet. Stir thoroughly. The gravy should bubble gently; no boil to prevent breaking the milk."
- "Simmer another 5 to 7 minutes. Sauce should thicken more, cling to beef morsels. Stir occasionally, smell rich beefy umami layered with toasted breadcrumb notes."
- "Off heat. Sprinkle chopped parsley for fresh color and herbal sharpness. Rest 2 minutes before serving to let flavors marry."
