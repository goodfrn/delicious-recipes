ingredients:
- "12 oz spaghetti or linguine"
- "2 cups spicy marinara sauce"
- "4 oz whipped goat cheese"
- "2 cloves garlic minced"
- "1 tablespoon olive oil"
- "Salt for pasta water and seasoning"
- "Optional garnish chopped fresh basil"
instructions:
- "Fill a large pot with heavily salted water, bring to a boil over high heat. Throw in pasta and cook, stirring frequently, until just under al dente; texture should still have a slight chew and firmness when bitten but no crunch. Do not drain."
- "In a wide skillet on medium heat, warm olive oil. Add minced garlic and stir until fragrant — 30 seconds or so, watch closely to not burn it. Garlic browns fast and turns bitter."
- "Pour marinara into skillet with garlic, stir to combine and bring the sauce to a low simmer. Look for small bubbles at edges, with aroma deepening, acidic tomato notes balancing sweetness."
- "Lower heat to a gentle simmer, stir in whipped goat cheese by spoonfuls. Break it up inside sauce with wooden spoon until fully melted and smooth. Heat residuals soften cheese smoothly but avoid boiling or sauce breaks into oily patches."
- "Using tongs or pasta fork, lift pasta straight from pot water into skillet—don’t drain. Some starchy water clings and helps sauce emulsify onto noodles. If sauce feels thick, add a splash of pasta water from pot and stir to loosen consistency."
- "Toss pasta in sauce vigorously until every strand coated, sauce thick but glossy and clings without pooling on plate. Taste for seasoning — salt brightens tomatoes; add gradually."
- "Transfer to warmed bowls or plates. Sprinkle chopped fresh basil if available for aromatic finish and color contrast."
- "Eat immediately — residual heat keeps sauce fluid, textures at peak."
- "Leftovers reheat gently in skillet with splash water; quick stir keeps sauce from drying out."
