ingredients:
- "1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking powder"
- "1 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1/4 teaspoon salt"
- "1 teaspoon cinnamon"
- "1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg"
- "3 medium ripe bananas mashed and measured at 1 cup"
- "3/4 cup packed light brown sugar"
- "1/3 cup melted unsalted butter"
- "1 large egg"
- "1/3 cup sour cream"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "3/4 cup bittersweet chocolate chunks"
- "Optional topping: coarse sugar and extra chunks"
instructions:
- "Start oven at 425 Fahrenheit; line 12-cup muffin tin with liners. Get setup before batter."
- "Mix dry stuff in bowl: flour, baking powder, soda, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg. Whisk until combined. No lumps. Set aside."
- "Mash bananas smooth; measure 1 cup exactly. Too much banana means dense muffins—measure well. No shortcut here."
- "In separate bowl, whisk brown sugar breaking all clumps, then add melted butter still warm, egg, sour cream, vanilla. Get mixture uniform but do not overbeat."
- "Fold dry ingredients into wet gently. Some flour pockets okay. Overworking batter tightens gluten—patchy batter better than tough muffins."
- "Add bittersweet chunks instead of semisweet chips. Fold just until flour streaks disappear. Stop mixing."
- "Cover with a kitchen towel, let rest at room temp for 20 minutes if time. Not mandatory—but resting relaxes batter, lets baking soda start reacting, leads to taller muffins with bakery loft."
- "Spoon batter into liners, fill nearly to top. Sprinkle extra chunks and optional coarse sugar atop for crunch and shine."
- "Place tin in oven. After popping in, bake 6 minutes at 425 Fahrenheit. This blast encourages oven spring—rise and crackly tops."
- "Without opening oven, reduce temp to 355 Fahrenheit and bake 13-16 minutes more. Muffins should look golden, tops firm to touch but slight bounce back. Test with toothpick—clean or few moist crumbs is done."
- "Cool in pan 4-6 minutes then transfer to wire rack. Cool fully to stop carryover cook and develop crumb. Warm muffins may fall apart; patience pays here."
