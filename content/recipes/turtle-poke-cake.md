ingredients:
- "1 box yellow cake mix (around 15.25 ounces)"
- "3 large eggs"
- "3/4 cup coconut milk (use dairy milk if preferred)"
- "1/4 cup avocado oil (neutral flavor oil substitution for canola oil)"
- "1 3/4 cups dulce de leche (replace caramel sauce plus sweetened condensed milk)"
- "1/2 cup full fat coconut milk condensed (homemade or store-bought)"
- "1 cup homemade or jarred chocolate ganache frosting"
- "1 cup whipped coconut cream (freeze coconut cream then whip until fluffy)"
- "1/2 cup toasted walnuts (instead of pecans, more aromatic)"
- "2 tablespoons espresso caramel sauce"
- "2 tablespoons dark chocolate sauce"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 345°F, rack in the middle position. Spray a 9x13 glass or ceramic dish with nonstick baking spray designed for sugar-rich bakes."
- "In a large bowl, use handheld mixer on medium speed to blend cake mix, eggs, coconut milk, and avocado oil. Mix thoroughly 2 1/2 minutes for full hydrating of dry mix, scraping down sides once or twice. Batter will be thick but pourable. Avoid overmixing; cake toughens if gluten overdevelops."
- "Pour batter into prepared dish, smooth top with offset spatula. Bake 30 to 33 minutes until edges begin to pull from sides and center springs back lightly when pressed with fingertip. Toothpick inserted near center should have moist crumbs but no raw batter. Expect slight doming."
- "While still warm, poke dense holes all over cake with the blunt end of a wooden spoon handle or chopstick. Size about 1/2 inch diameter, spaced evenly 2 inches apart. This allows caramel to seep deep, distributes moisture better."
- "Combine dulce de leche with condensed coconut milk in a small bowl; stir smooth. Slowly drizzle over cake ensuring caramel saturates the holes fully. The batter’s warmth helps liquid absorb. Leave cake on counter to cool at least 1 hour; caramel thickens and sinks without sogging edges."
- "Meanwhile, whip ganache frosting at medium speed for 1 minute until slightly lightened and aerated. Fold in whipped coconut cream in two portions, using rubber spatula gently to avoid deflating mixture. Folding creates lighter texture, integrating freshness without loss of chocolate richness. Once combined evenly without streaks, refrigerate until ready to frost."
- "Spread chilled frosting evenly over cooled cake with metal spatula or butter knife, applying gentle pressure to avoid digging into cake surface. Frosting will feel firm but creamy, spreadable. Drizzle espresso caramel and dark chocolate sauces in alternating thin zigzags across the surface. Use a toothpick or skewer for delicate swirls and swirl lines if desired."
- "Sprinkle toasted walnuts generously over top for contrast. Walnuts toasted til golden and aromatic, about 5 minutes at 350°F on a baking sheet, then cooled. Adds crunchy texture and nuttier note than standard pecans."
- "Cover loosely and refrigerate minimum 3 hours to firm frosting and set flavor. Up to overnight is fine. Serve chilled or bring to room temp 15 minutes for softer bite."
- "Troubleshooting: Use wooden spoon handle for even holes. Too much caramel can create soggy bottom—measure carefully. If frosting runs, place in fridge to rest 10 minutes then spread. If cake underbakes, extend by 3-minute increments, test doneness visually/tactilely."
