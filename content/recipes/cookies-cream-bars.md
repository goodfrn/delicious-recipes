ingredients:
- "1 cup all-purpose flour"
- "3/4 cup oat flour"
- "1/4 cup cornstarch"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1 teaspoon baking powder"
- "3/4 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "1 cup unsalted butter softened"
- "3/4 cup white granulated sugar"
- "1/2 cup light brown sugar packed"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1 large egg yolk"
- "1 1/2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract"
- "1 cup crushed Oreo cookies (about 10-12 cookies)"
- "3/4 cup chopped cookies and cream candy bars"
- "2/3 cup white chocolate chunks"
- "Extra Oreo crumbs for topping"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 355°F. Prep an 8×8 pan with parchment paper or non-stick spray remembering to leave extra hanging on edges for removal."
- "In big bowl, whisk flours, cornstarch, baking soda, baking powder, and salt. Combine dry evenly but don’t overmix; lumps are fine."
- "Grab stand mixer paddle; cream the softened butter, white sugar, and brown sugar for 3 to 4 minutes. Look for the light color and fluffy texture—this traps air for tenderness."
- "One at a time, add eggs and yolk mixing just until combined. Add vanilla after eggs fully integrated."
- "Pour in dry ingredients in 2 stages, slow to keep gluten from tightening. Fold in crushed Oreos, chopped candy bars, and white chocolate chunks carefully; chunks give unexpected creamy pockets."
- "Press batter evenly into the prepared pan with clean damp fingers or offset spatula—don’t press too hard. Top with more Oreo crumbs; they toast beautifully on surface."
- "Bake 18 to 25 minutes depending on oven variability. Look for medium golden edges, firm top but touch with finger a tiny bounce remains, and toothpick shows crumbs with no wet batter. Overbaking yields dryness; underbaking too soft."
- "Cool bars in pan on wire rack for 20 minutes minimum. Lift bars out using parchment edges—it’s key for clean cuts. Wait at least 15 minutes more before slicing or they crumble."
- "Cut bars into 16 squares. Store loosely covered or wrapped to keep edges tender without sogginess."
