ingredients:
- "6 large eggs"
- "3 tablespoons crema or whole milk"
- "1 tablespoon butter or vegetable oil"
- "1 cup crumbled cooked pork chorizo or breakfast sausage"
- "4 small corn or flour tortillas"
- "1 cup shredded sharp cheddar or Monterey Jack cheese"
- "1/2 cup fresh pico de gallo or chopped fresh salsa"
- "1 small ripe avocado, sliced"
- "1-2 fresh jalapeño peppers, thinly sliced"
- "Fresh chives or green onions, chopped"
- "Large flaky sea salt like Maldon"
- "Freshly cracked black pepper to taste"
instructions:
- "Crack eggs into a wide bowl. Add crema or milk. Whisk vigorously with fork or immersion blender. Look for fully blended mixture with some air bubbles on top; this helps fluffiness."
- "Heat skillet over medium, add butter or oil. Listen for slight sizzle when you add the fat—too hot means eggs will cook unevenly."
- "Pour eggs in, let them sit undisturbed for 10-15 seconds until edges just begin to set and puff slightly. Use a silicone spatula to fold eggs gently from the edges into the center. Avoid constant stirring; you want curds forming but still slightly wet."
- "Remove skillet from direct heat when eggs are mostly set but still look glossy and slightly runny in parts. The residual heat finishes cooking without drying eggs out. Overcooking here kills texture."
- "Quickly warm tortillas in another dry pan or wrapped in foil in oven to avoid sogginess. Keep them pliable but not brittle."
- "Divide eggs evenly into tortillas. Scatter cooked chorizo or sausage on top. Follow with shredded cheese, allowing residual heat to melt it slightly."
- "Add fresh pico de gallo, avocado slices, and jalapeño rings for heat and brightness. Add a sprinkle of chives or green onions."
- "Final touch: flaky salt and fresh cracked pepper give layers of texture and balance. Taste first, salt always last."
- "Troubleshooting tip: if eggs seem watery, reduce the milk or crema next time and cook on slightly lower heat. If tortillas get soggy, warm them separately but serve immediately."
