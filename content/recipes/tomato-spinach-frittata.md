ingredients:
- "1 tablespoon olive oil (or butter)"
- "3 tablespoons minced shallots"
- "5 large eggs"
- "1/8 cup ricotta cheese (can substitute with cream cheese)"
- "1 tablespoon half and half or milk"
- "1/2 cup sliced grape tomatoes"
- "1/2 cup chopped kale (tough ribs removed)"
- "1/8 cup shredded mozzarella cheese"
- "Salt and black pepper to taste"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 360F. Heat olive oil until shimmering but not smoking in a 10-inch ovenproof skillet over medium heat."
- "Add shallots, stir gently, watch closely. Cook 2-3 minutes until translucent, no browning yet; that bitter bite ruins the mood."
- "Meanwhile, crack eggs into a mixing bowl. Whisk with ricotta and half and half until smooth. Salt and pepper in here to layer seasoning."
- "Toss grape tomatoes and kale into skillet. Stir constantly; kale won’t wilt immediately but will soften. Look for color shift in tomatoes, slight sizzle."
- "Pour egg mixture over veggies. Without stirring, let cook on stovetop for 4-5 minutes until edges look set and center jiggles just slightly when nudged."
- "Sprinkle mozzarella cheese evenly on top. Transfer skillet to oven and bake for 6-7 minutes or until puffed and firm but not dry."
- "Finish with broiler on high, 45 seconds to 1 minute, watching carefully. You want golden spots, a hint of crunch on cheese, not blackened patches."
- "Remove from oven. Let sit 2 minutes before slicing. This rest sets texture so you avoid runny egg and terrible melt-off."
- "Serve hot or room temp. Pair with crusty bread or simple salad."
