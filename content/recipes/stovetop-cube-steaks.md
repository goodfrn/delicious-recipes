ingredients:
- "5 tablespoons all-purpose flour"
- "1 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "1 teaspoon onion powder"
- "3/4 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper"
- "4 cube steaks (about 1/2 inch thick)"
- "3 tablespoons vegetable oil"
- "2 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "1 medium white onion, thinly sliced"
- "6 ounces sliced cremini mushrooms"
- "1 1/4 cups low-sodium beef broth"
- "3 tablespoons dry beef gravy mix"
instructions:
- "Mix flour, garlic powder, onion powder, salt and pepper in a shallow bowl or plate. Combine thoroughly to get even seasoning."
- "Dredge cube steaks in flour mixture, pressing lightly to coat. Shake off excess but not too much or crust will be thin."
- "Heat a large heavy skillet over medium-high heat till hot enough to shimmer. Cast iron preferred for even browning."
- "Add vegetable oil, swirl for 30 seconds till it races across pan surface."
- "Place cube steaks carefully without crowding. You may need two batches. Overcrowding steams meat instead of browning."
- "Listen for a steady sizzle, let steaks cook undisturbed 3-4 minutes per side until deep golden crust forms. Resist flipping until crust is set; it’ll tear otherwise."
- "Transfer steaks to plate and tent loosely with foil to rest, juices redistribute."
- "Leave skillet on heat, reduce to medium. Add butter, onions, mushrooms. Stir often, transforming raw veggies into softened, aromatic mix, around 5 minutes. Watch mushrooms shrink and onions turn translucent, exuding sweetness."
- "Sprinkle dry gravy mix over vegetables; whisk vigorously to form thick paste while scraping browned bits from pan bottom — the flavor base."
- "Stream in beef broth slowly while stirring to dissolve paste into velvety sauce. Simmer gently, sauce thickens and glossy sheen appears. Taste and adjust salt, pepper here."
- "Nestle cube steaks back in skillet, spoon sauce lavishly over them. Heat for 2-3 minutes. This warms steaks through and lets sauce cling better."
- "Serve steaks and mushroom sauce immediately over buttered egg noodles, mashed potatoes, or steamed rice to catch all the juices."
