ingredients:
- "4 boneless skinless chicken breasts"
- "2 tablespoons olive oil"
- "1 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "1 teaspoon smoked paprika"
- "1 teaspoon salt"
- "½ teaspoon black pepper"
- "½ teaspoon onion powder"
- "Pinch cayenne pepper optional"
instructions:
- "=== Prep ==="
- "Set oven to 405 degrees Fahrenheit. Line a rimmed baking sheet with aluminum foil to catch drips, makes cleanup faster."
- "Place chicken breasts on a cutting board. Flatten thick sections with a meat mallet or heavy pan. Don't pulverize; just even thickness gets faster, even cooking."
- "=== Seasoning ==="
- "In a bowl, pour olive oil. Toss chicken in oil thoroughly, every side glossed, this seals moisture. In a smaller bowl, mix garlic powder, smoked paprika instead of cayenne, salt, black pepper, and onion powder."
- "=== Assembly ==="
- "Lay chicken breasts skin side down (or the smoother side down) on foil. Sprinkle half the spice mix over meat side. Flip, season the other side with remaining spices. Notice the reddish paprika dust coat; it will turn deeper with heat."
- "=== Cooking ==="
- "Slide tray in oven. Check around 17 minutes for juices running clear at thickest point, and a firm spring back when pressed. Use finger pressure test, finger is better than timer here."
- "If using a thermometer, 160°F near center means it’s time to broil quickly."
- "Switch oven to broil, top rack. Broil about 3-4 minutes watching closely; skin or surface should bubble and brown but not burn. Sizzle and smell signal caramelization of spices. You want browning, not char."
- "Remove from oven. Rest chicken 5 minutes loosely tented with foil—the juice redistributes. Cuts stay juicy, not dry."
- "=== Serving tips ==="
- "Slice and reveal opaque white with faint rosy tint at center. Texture firm but juicy, not rubbery."
- "Substitution advice: If no smoked paprika, cayenne plus a hint of cumin works; boosts warmth without smokiness. Use avocado oil if olive oil flavor too strong."
- "Troubleshooting: If chicken dries out, probably flattened too thin or overcooked; slicing thin before baking can help. Too pale? Skip foil, cook directly on rack for air flow."
- "Use parchment paper instead of foil if non-stick surfaces are an issue."
- "Trying nonstick pans? Keep oil and broil step for best texture."
- "Chicken pounded evenly = cook time consistency, stops tail end overcooked, center raw."
- "Clean cut knife is key for neat slices and juice retention."
- "Avoid crowding pans; space allows heat to circulate and brown chicken instead of steaming."
