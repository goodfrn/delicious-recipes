ingredients:
- "1 cup all-purpose flour"
- "3/4 cup yellow cornmeal"
- "1 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "½ teaspoon black pepper"
- "¾ teaspoon garlic powder"
- "1 cup diced roasted red bell pepper"
- "½ cup minced shallot"
- "1 large egg"
- "¾ cup cultured buttermilk"
- "1 cup pimento cheese spread"
- "Vegetable oil for frying"
- "Pepper jelly melted for serving"
instructions:
- "=== Mix Dry Ingredients ==="
- "Combine flour, cornmeal, salt, pepper, garlic powder in large bowl. Distribute uniformly; this ensures seasoning touches every fritter. Skip adding tomato until now to avoid moisture imbalance."
- ""
- "=== Prepare Wet Mix ==="
- "Lightly beat egg into buttermilk in a separate bowl. Add pimento cheese, stir slowly until combined. This blend introduces fat and acidity, binding protein and cheese flavor evenly into dry mix."
- ""
- "=== Incorporate Veggies ==="
- "Fold diced roasted red pepper and minced shallot into dry ingredients. No over-mixing. The veggies provide bursts of sweetness and slight pungency, countering heavy cheese notes."
- ""
- "=== Combine Wet and Dry ==="
- "Pour wet mixture over dry. Gentle stir just until batter appears cohesive but still lumpy. You want a batter thick enough to hold shape on spatula but not stiff. Add a splash more buttermilk if too thick, pourable but not runny."
- ""
- "=== Heat Oil ==="
- "Pour ½ inch vegetable oil in skillet, medium heat. Sound cues critical: wait for steady faint bubble when spoon dripped; oil too cold soaks up batter, too hot burns edges before cooking through."
- ""
- "=== Cook Fritters ==="
- "Drop heaping tablespoonfuls into oil. Don’t crowd pan; fry 4 to 6 minutes one side. Edges become golden and crisp. Flip carefully; press flat gently with spatula to ensure even cooking and thin live batter layer finalizes crisp crust."
- ""
- "Finish second side 4 to 6 minutes; internal texture should feel firm but yielding. Remove to paper towels to drain excess oil immediately, prevents sogginess."
- ""
- "=== Serve ==="
- "Keep fritters warm on rack in low oven while cooking batches. Serve hot with melted pepper jelly on top or dollops of additional pimento cheese spread. Contrasting textures—creamy, crisp, sweet-spicy—balance."
- ""
- "=== Tips and Substitutions ==="
- "Shallot can be swapped with finely diced green onion or mild yellow onion for less sharpness. Roasted red bell peppers add depth versus fresh tomato’s bright acidity but fresh finely chopped tomato works if drained well. Use regular milk mixed with a teaspoon vinegar or lemon juice as buttermilk substitute–let sit 5 mins for acidity."
- "Medium heat frying essential; too hot scorches outside raw inside. Oil temp control easier with thermometer around 350F or rely on steady bubble and aroma of frying cornmeal to judge."
- "If batter too thin, sprinkle in more flour or cornmeal sparingly; too thick, thin with liquid gradually. Batter texture controls final fritter mouthfeel."
- "To prevent sticking, oil surface before each batch and avoid overcrowding. Use slotted spoon or spatula for turnaround."
