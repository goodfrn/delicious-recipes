ingredients:
- "1 lb extra large shrimp shelled and deveined, patted dry"
- "½ tsp kosher salt"
- "¼ tsp freshly ground black pepper"
- "3 tbsp ghee or clarified butter"
- "2 tbsp avocado oil"
- "1 tbsp minced garlic"
- "⅛ to ¼ tsp Aleppo pepper"
- "1 tsp lemon zest"
- "3 tbsp fresh squeezed lemon juice"
- "1 ½ tbsp chopped fresh cilantro"
instructions:
- "Start with shrimp fully thawed and dried. This prevents steaming. Sprinkle salt and pepper evenly. Set aside and prep other ingredients."
- "Heat ghee and avocado oil in a 12-inch skillet over medium-high heat. Watch butter foam and loosen before dropping garlic in. Garlic must sizzle gently — listen for fragrant sizzle within 30-45 seconds. Watch closely to stop garlic burn. Add Aleppo pepper with garlic to infuse oil."
- "Lay shrimp flat in a single layer, don't crowd pan or they’ll steam. After about 3-4 minutes, shrimp color will shift from translucent gray to opaque pink with a sharp snap when poked gently. Flip all shrimp over once this happens."
- "Cook second side another 2-3 minutes. Shrimp should curl tight but still feel firm and springy, not rubbery or mushy."
- "Turn heat off. Toss shrimp gently with lemon zest, lemon juice, and cilantro. The residual heat wakes up bright citrus notes without cooking lemon flavor off."
- "Serve immediately. No resting or chilling here. Keeps texture bold and fresh."
- "If shrimp overcooked? Can feel tough or dry. Correct by lowering heat next time and watching color change obsessively rather than timing. Garlic burnt? Scramble in a couple drops more oil or fresh garlic to balance bitterness."
- "Substitutions: Ghee chosen for higher smoke point and nutty aroma, but butter can be used if watched carefully. Avocado oil neutral and healthy alternative for olive oil—no overpowering flavor."
- "Aleppo pepper adds moderate warmth with fruitiness—replace with red chili flakes for sharper heat."
- "Cilantro swaps parsley for a bright herbal note, reflects kitchen spontaneity—use parsley if preferred."
- "Allow pan to regain full heat between garlic and shrimp addition to avoid steaming shrimp."
