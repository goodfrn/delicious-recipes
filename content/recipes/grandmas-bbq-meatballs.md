ingredients:
- "=== BBQ Sauce ==="
- "2 tablespoons olive oil"
- "1 medium yellow onion, finely chopped"
- "3 garlic cloves, minced"
- "1 cup ketchup"
- "1/3 cup water"
- "1/4 cup dark brown sugar"
- "1/4 cup apple cider vinegar"
- "1 1/2 tablespoons Dijon mustard"
- "2 teaspoons Worcestershire sauce"
- "1 tablespoon molasses"
- "1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika"
- "1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper"
- "=== Meatballs ==="
- "3/4 cup evaporated milk"
- "3/4 cup panko bread crumbs"
- "1 small onion, finely diced"
- "2 garlic cloves, minced"
- "1 cup prepared BBQ sauce (from above)"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1 teaspoon salt"
- "1/2 teaspoon black pepper"
- "1/2 pound ground beef"
- "1/2 pound ground pork"
instructions:
- "=== Sauce first ==="
- "Heat olive oil in large Dutch oven over medium-high. Add chopped onion, stirring often. Listen for that gentle sizzle, onions soften and turn translucent in about 4 minutes. Hit softness before color, stop early to avoid bitter burnt notes."
- "Add garlic, toss quickly for 1 minute. Garlic’s aroma blooms but watch for any color change—burnt garlic turns bitter fast."
- "Next, add ketchup. Using cold water, fill ketchup bottle halfway. Shake well—critical for scraping thick residue. Pour water into pot. Stir vigorously to integrate every sticky bit, no waste here."
- "Add brown sugar, apple cider vinegar, Dijon mustard, Worcestershire sauce, molasses, smoked paprika, black pepper. Stir to combine. Reduce heat to medium-low. Simmer gently, uncovered. That’s the secret—evaporation thickens sauce, concentrates flavors."
- "Let it bubble softly for 15-20 minutes, stir occasionally, scrape bottom to prevent scorching. Sauce should thicken enough to coat back of spoon lightly."
- "=== Meatball assembly ==="
- "Preheat oven to 355°F. Line baking sheet with foil or parchment, lightly spray to prevent sticking. Slightly higher temp accounts for oven variability."
- "In large bowl, mix evaporated milk and panko. Let soak a couple minutes until breadcrumbs swell, bind better."
- "Add finely diced onion, garlic, 1 cup warm BBQ sauce, eggs, salt, pepper. Stir—introduce moisture and seasoning evenly."
- "Now drop ground beef and pork into bowl in small clumps. Use clean hands, or gloves, but don’t over-twist mixture—too much handling tightens texture, yields tough meatballs."
- "Combine gently until mixture comes together. Want uniform consistency but still tender."
- "Form into either 1-inch bite-sized or larger 2.5-3 inch meatballs, space evenly. Smaller meatballs cook faster, so adjust timing."
- "Arrange on sheet and bake meatballs 18-23 minutes. Look for golden edges, slight shrinking, juices clear when poked. Larger size may need extra 3-5 minutes. Avoid drying out—check after 15 minutes, then every few minutes."
- "Remove carefully. Drain any accumulated fat from baking sheet."
- "Transfer meatballs straight into simmering BBQ sauce. They soak up liquid, deepen flavor."
- "Maintain low simmer, uncovered, for 55-90 minutes. Long slow braise reduces sauce by roughly 1/4, thick, sticky coating clings to meatballs."
- "Stir occasionally, ladle sauce over meatballs to keep moist. Watch for bubbling but no vigorous boiling or it will toughen meat."
- "Serve hot atop creamy mashed potatoes, fluffy rice, or couscous. Potatoes especially catch sauce nice."
- "Leftovers? Refrigerate in sauce, reheat gently to avoid drying."
