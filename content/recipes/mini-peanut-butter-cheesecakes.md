ingredients:
- "1 package standard muffin pan with 12 slots"
- "12 Reese’s cups, standard size"
- "16 ounces cream cheese, softened slightly"
- "3/4 cup creamy peanut butter"
- "3/4 cup powdered sugar, sifted"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1/3 cup canned coconut cream, cold"
- "12 mini Reese’s Pieces candies"
- "Chocolate sauce for drizzling"
instructions:
- "Line muffin pan with 12 paper liners, no wrinkles. Makes release easier."
- "Press 1 Reese’s cup firmly into bottom of each lined muffin tin. Acts like crust base, adds flavor punch."
- "In large bowl, beat cream cheese and peanut butter on medium speed with handheld mixer. Look for light fluffiness, no lumps, about 2 minutes. Texture matters — no gritty bits."
- "Add sifted powdered sugar, blend until completely smooth. Scrape sides with spatula periodically. Avoid overmixing."
- "Pour in vanilla extract and cold coconut cream. Beat on high for around 2 ½ minutes. Mixture should double in volume, visibly light and airy."
- "Transfer filling to pastry bag or sturdy plastic zipper bag, cut small corner corner off. Pipe into muffin cups, fill right to top; no gaps or air pockets. Dense enough but airy."
- "Top each cheesecake with a Reese’s Pieces candy. Press gently just enough to sink base slightly, leave top showing. Visual contrast."
- "Cover pan, refrigerate at least 7 to 9 hours. The texture firms up completely, columns stay intact when unmolded."
- "Before serving, drizzle chocolate sauce over cheesecakes. Use thin, steady drizzle for fine lines rather than splats. Adds final touch, texture contrast."
