ingredients:
- "3/4 cup packed light brown sugar"
- "1/3 cup water"
- "3 tablespoons unsalted ghee (or butter)"
- "1/2 cup full-fat coconut milk (alternative to cream)"
- "1 teaspoon bourbon or whiskey"
- "1/4 teaspoon kosher salt"
instructions:
- "Heat sugar and water in a heavy-bottomed saucepan over medium-low heat. Stir only at start to dissolve sugar fully. Then stop stirring; swirl gently from time to time to keep even heating."
- "Watch carefully as the mixture bubbles gently. Tiny bubbles signal beginning of caramelization. Color will shift in stages; first pale gold, then a rich amber. Pull off heat just as it reaches deep golden-brown but before it smokes or smells burnt, about 6-12 minutes depending on your burner."
- "Remove pan from heat. Add ghee in chunks—expect vigorous sizzling. Whisk rapidly to emulsify butter fat into syrup. Follow immediately with coconut milk, add slowly, whisking the whole time to avoid separation and cool the caramel slightly."
- "Continue whisking until shiny, thick, and sticky. A few seconds until texture smooth but not runny. The sauce should coat the back of a spoon."
- "Finish by stirring in bourbon and kosher salt. The alcohol deepens aroma and salt brings out sweet flavors, while balancing richness."
- "Transfer to a heatproof container. Cool slightly before serving or refrigerate for up to two weeks. Warm gently before use to loosen if it thickens too much."
- "Troubleshooting: If sauce crystallizes or grainy, reheat gently with a splash of water and whisk until smooth. If burnt, best to start fresh but reduce heat next time."
