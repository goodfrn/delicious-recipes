ingredients:
- "1 teaspoon extra-virgin olive oil"
- "1 garlic clove minced"
- "2 large eggs beaten"
- "1/4 cup chopped kale"
- "1/4 cup cooked quinoa"
- "2 tablespoons crumbled feta cheese"
- "Salt to taste"
- "White pepper to taste"
- "Fresh chives chopped, optional"
instructions:
- "Warm oil in a small skillet over medium heat; wait for shimmer before garlic hits pan. Toss in minced garlic; stir with a wooden spoon until fragrant and just golden usually 1-1½ minutes. Watch closely—burnt garlic ruins everything."
- "Pour in beaten eggs in one smooth motion; tilt pan quickly to spread evenly. Listen for soft sputters as edges begin to set; no rush, patience here."
- "Before eggs firm completely but starting to thicken, sprinkle chopped kale and cooked quinoa over partially cooked eggs. Gentle fold—lift edges and slide under mixture to keep scramble fluffy. Kale is tougher than spinach. Needs a bit longer to wilt but shouldn’t stew—tender crisp is the goal."
- "Push in feta cheese last; it heats through but doesn’t fully melt. Salty, creamy contrast with bite from greens and nutty quinoa."
- "Season with salt and white pepper toward the end. White pepper less intrusive visually than black pepper but punchy. Taste at this point. Add more if necessary."
- "Turn off heat once eggs are JUST set, not dry. Residual pan heat carries cooking through gently preventing dryness."
- "Serve topped with chopped fresh chives or herbs you trust. Adds color and sharp freshness to balance creaminess."
- "If no kale handy, substitute baby spinach or Swiss chard but adjust cooking time down for delicate greens. For feta swap, try goat cheese or shredded mozzarella—different texture, same salty note."
- "If quinoa not cooked beforehand, steam or rinse then simmer with double water for about 15 minutes or until translucent and tender. Leftovers reheat well here."
- "Mistakes to avoid: too high heat scorching garlic or drying eggs by overcooking, overcrowding pan which steams ingredients rather than sautés."
