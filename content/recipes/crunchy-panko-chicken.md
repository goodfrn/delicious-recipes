ingredients:
- "1 ½ cups all-purpose flour"
- "1 ½ teaspoons smoked paprika"
- "1 teaspoon salt"
- "¾ teaspoon black pepper"
- "3 large eggs"
- "1 ½ cups plain panko breadcrumbs"
- "¾ cup finely grated sharp cheddar cheese"
- "3 tablespoons melted unsalted butter"
- "4 boneless skinless chicken cutlets (about 6 ounces each)"
- "Optional chopped fresh parsley"
- "Lemon wedges for serving"
instructions:
- "Set oven to 430°F. Line a baking sheet with foil, edges crimped up for easy cleanup. Organize three shallow bowls wide enough for chicken cutlets."
- "Mix flour, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper in one bowl. Crack eggs in second and whisk until fully smooth, no streaks. In last bowl, combine panko, cheddar cheese, and melted butter—coat crumbs well to help browning and flavor."
- "Dry chicken with paper towel. Dip cutlet first in flour mix, shake off excess. Then plunge in eggs, letting drip. Press into panko-cheddar mixture, coating firmly both sides. Don’t crowd—excess crumbs reused back in bowl."
- "Arrange coated cutlets spaced apart on baking sheet. Slide into hot oven. After about 20 minutes, check crust color—should be golden with crispy edges. Insert digital thermometer into thickest part; target 160-165°F. Oven times vary with cut thickness—trust color and feel too."
- "Remove from oven when done. Rest on a wire rack or plate 5 minutes. Resting keeps juices inside meat, avoiding dryness when slicing. Serve with fresh parsley sprinkled on top for color and lemon wedges squeezed over right before eating to brighten flavors."
