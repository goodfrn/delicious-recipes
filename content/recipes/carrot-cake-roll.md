ingredients:
- "3/4 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon"
- "1/4 teaspoon baking powder"
- "1/8 teaspoon salt"
- "1/8 teaspoon ground ginger"
- "1/8 teaspoon ground nutmeg"
- "4 large eggs"
- "2/3 cup granulated sugar"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1/3 cup coconut oil, melted"
- "3/4 cup finely shredded carrots"
- "Zest of 1 medium orange"
- "6 ounces cream cheese, softened"
- "1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened"
- "1 tablespoon Greek yogurt (optional, for tang)"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "3/4 cup powdered sugar (plus extra for dusting)"
- "1/3 cup powdered sugar"
- "2 tablespoons whole milk"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 385 F. Line a 15x10-inch jelly roll pan with parchment paper spray with nonstick spray good coverage. Set aside."
- "Whisk flour, cinnamon, baking powder, salt, ginger, nutmeg in a bowl. Orange zest folded in last for aroma. Set dry mix ready."
- "Using stand mixer or hand mixer beat eggs high 4 minutes not 5, just until frothy and pale. You want volume and lightness."
- "Add sugar, vanilla, and melted coconut oil while mixer runs medium speed. Beat until fully mixed but don’t overmix; oil should emulsify evenly."
- "Fold dry mix gently into wet with spatula. Cease stirring once no dry streaks remain. Then fold shredded carrots in evenly distributed."
- "Pour batter into pan. Use offset spatula to spread thin and level. Batter should touch all corners. No piles or runs."
- "Bake 8 to 10 minutes. Edges golden and cake springs back lightly to touch. Slight wobble centers okay. Don’t overbake or it will crack or dry when rolled."
- "Remove immediately from oven. Run sharp knife or thin spatula between cake and pan edges, focusing on sides without parchment."
- "Sprinkle generous powdered sugar over clean kitchen towel surface. This helps release cake and prevents sticking."
- "Invert cake carefully onto sugared towel. Peel off parchment slowly while cake is warm to avoid tearing."
- "Flip one short edge tea towel over cake end. Start rolling cake slowly with light pressure but firm grip so no cracking."
- "Roll whole cake up in towel and set aside to cool COMPLETELY before unrolling. Do not rush cooling or frosting will melt."
- "For filling beat cream cheese, butter, and Greek yogurt 1 to 1½ minutes until whipped but not airy. Reduces stiffness, adds flavor."
- "Add vanilla, powdered sugar gradually on low then medium speed blend until creamy with no lumps."
- "Mix powdered sugar and milk in small bowl until smooth and thin but not watery for the glaze. Set aside."
- "Unroll cooled cake gently. Use offset or silicone spatula spread cream cheese filling evenly over surface. Leave ½ inch margin edges dry to prevent seepage during reroll."
- "Roll cake back to original shape tightly but gently. Wrap in plastic wrap tightly to maintain shape and seal moisture."
- "Refrigerate at least 1 hour or overnight. Helps firm and set roll for cleaner slicing."
- "Before slicing drizzle glaze in zigzag pattern across top with spoon or fork. Don’t pour glaze; you want delicate coverage not pooling."
- "Use sharp serrated knife slice off small ends to neaten. Then cut 10 to 12 even slices using gentle sawing motion to avoid smashing."

- "Fold dry ingredients gently. Visibility of some flour is okay until mixed in completely. Don’t overwork. Strength is in lightness here; keep flour from clumping. The slower you fold, the better the texture."
- "Bake quickly, 8 to 10 minutes. Watch edges flirt with gold, center should wobble. Overbaking means dry crumbs. Set timer, but trust your hands too. Push lightly at the center when time's up."
- "Cool in towel for hours. Rolling hot could split, warm is flexible. Use powdered sugar generously on towel; mat is crucial. If stuck, sprinkle more sugar. Look for smoothness of roll."
- "Cream cheese filling needs to be creamy, not fully airy. Beat in butter first then add yogurt to loosen. This will set a good tone to balance sweetness. Check thickness while spreading; you want even distribution."
