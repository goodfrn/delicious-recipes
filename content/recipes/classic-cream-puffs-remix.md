ingredients:
- "4 tbsp browned butter pieces"
- "1 cup water"
- "1 cup half-and-half"
- "1/2 tsp salt"
- "2 tbsp sugar"
- "3/4 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1/4 cup arrowroot flour"
- "4 large eggs, room temperature, divided"
- "2 tsp water"
- "1/4 tsp dry mustard powder (for egg wash)"
- "1 cup heavy cream"
- "3 tbsp powdered sugar"
- "1 tsp almond extract"
- "powdered sugar for dusting"
instructions:
- "Oven preheated to 405°F – hot enough to jumpstart steam and get puffs rising fast."
- "In a medium saucepan, combine browned butter, water, half-and-half, salt, sugar. Heat over medium until bubbles cluster around edges and steam rises—watch, don’t boil hard."
- "Toss in all-purpose and arrowroot flour mix all at once. Stir vigorously with wooden spoon until dough sticks together, forms a ball, pulls from sides clean, and thick enough to leave paste-like smear on spoon. Keep dough in pan over low heat, press firmly against sides for 2-3 minutes. Smell should become less 'floury'—cooked flour reduces raw taste and stabilizes structure."
- "Dough off heat, transfer to bowl. Let cool until warm to touch — about 7 minutes. Too hot, eggs curdle; too cold, dough won't blend properly."
- "Lightly beat 3 eggs in separate bowl. Start mixer low speed; add eggs slowly, teaspoon by teaspoon. Dough will loosen and shine, thick ribbons falling slowly from beater. Reserve remaining egg for wash."
- "Fill sturdy Ziploc bag with dough, snip corner with 1/2 inch opening (approx Wilton 1A tip size). Pipe 2 inch mounds spaced 2-3 inches apart on parchment-lined tray. Smooth large peaks with damp finger to prevent cracking during rise."
- "Create egg wash: mix reserved egg with mustard powder and 2 tsp water. This salty tang increases color and promotes shiny crust."
- "Brush tops lightly with wash. Avoid soaking tops or pooling on tray—can cause soggy bottoms."
- "Bake at 405°F for 18 minutes. Oven noise should change—sharp crackles and expanding dough sound emerges. Do NOT open oven door that period, stop temp changes and collapse risk."
- "Without opening oven, reduce temperature to 355°F, continue baking 12 more minutes. Shell should be sturdy, deep golden—tap top, hollow sound confirms inside drying out properly."
- "Remove tray, instantly pierce each puff base with toothpick to release steam. Cooling on rack prevents soggy texture inside."
- "As puffs cool, whip cold heavy cream, sugar, and almond extract. Beat till stiff peaks form—stop before graininess, cream should hold shape without glistening."
- "Slice puffs in half horizontally. Using piping bag or Ziploc corner, fill generously with whipped cream. Replace top gently, keeping shape intact."
- "Right before serving, dust generously with powdered sugar. Visual contrast and restrained sweetness balances nutty cream and toasted dough notes."
