ingredients:
- "1 cup granulated sugar"
- "1 teaspoon finely grated orange zest"
- "1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract"
- "1/3 cup vegetable oil"
- "2/3 cup milk"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1 1/3 cups all-purpose flour"
- "2/3 cup whole wheat flour"
- "1/2 teaspoon salt"
- "1 1/4 teaspoons baking powder"
- "2 cups fresh rhubarb, diced 1/2 inch"
- "Glaze: 2 ounces cream cheese softened"
- "1/2 cup powdered sugar sifted"
- "1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 345F. Grease or line a 9 x 5 inch loaf pan with parchment, letting edges hang over for easy removal."
- "In largest mixing bowl, whisk sugar, orange zest, vanilla, oil, milk, eggs. Aim for even, slightly thick batter but don’t overdo."
- "Sift all-purpose flour, whole wheat flour, salt, baking powder in medium bowl. Important for even rise."
- "Rinse rhubarb, no need to peel. Pat dry with towel. Dice into 1/2 inch cubes; measure out exactly 2 cups. Too big or too small means uneven bites."
- "Fold dry flour mix into wet ingredients gently. Do not stir aggressively—some lumps remain okay. Overmix toughens crumb."
- "Fold in rhubarb pieces just until distributed. Overmix juice will leak and sogginess follows."
- "Pour batter into prepared pan. Tap pan lightly on counter to remove large bubbles; surface will be lumpy."
- "Bake 50 to 55 minutes. Look for cracked top, golden color, edges pulling away slightly. Insert toothpick near center—clean or few moist crumbs means done. If wet batter on toothpick, bake longer but watch so it doesn’t dry out."
- "Cool in pan 10 minutes. Use parchment edges to lift bread onto wire rack to cool completely. Avoid slicing warm bread; crumb will squeeze out and be gummy."
- "For glaze, beat softened cream cheese until creamy, whip in powdered sugar and lemon juice until smooth and loose enough to drizzle. If too thick, add splash of milk or water."
- "Drizzle glaze evenly over top. Let set 10-15 minutes before slicing to avoid messy topping."
