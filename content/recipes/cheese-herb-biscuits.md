ingredients:
- "3 cups all purpose flour"
- "1 1/2 cups sharp cheddar cheese shredded"
- "2 teaspoons fresh thyme, chopped"
- "1 teaspoon fresh chives minced"
- "1 teaspoon shallot powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon freshly cracked black pepper"
- "1 teaspoon baking powder (added for lift)"
- "3/4 cup frozen unsalted butter grated"
- "1 cup chilled buttermilk"
instructions:
- "Mix flour, cheddar, thyme, chives, pepper, shallot powder, and baking powder in a large bowl. Stir until evenly distributed. Baking powder adds extra lift because cheese weighs dough down."
- "Grate frozen butter on the largest holes of a box grater. Fold butter bits into flour mixture quickly. Avoid warming. Keep dough flaky. Cover bowl tightly and freeze for 15 minutes to firm butter back up."
- "Make a well in the center. Pour in the cold buttermilk all at once. Stir gently with a wooden spoon just to combine. Dough will look shaggy and sticky—that's right. Avoid overmixing. Few kneads in bowl to bring dough together; no aggressive work here."
- "Turn out dough on floured surface. Dust hands with flour. Pat into roughly 1-inch thick rectangle. Fold short edges together like a letter. Rotate 90 degrees and roll again. Repeat fold and roll two more times. Layering fat and flour creates flaky layers. You hear a gentle crackle when dough is cold and ready."
- "Flour a 2-inch biscuit cutter. Press straight down into dough, lift cleanly. Cut biscuits—no twisting or rocking. Twisting seals edges, bad for rise. Use a sharp cutter or dip in flour to avoid dragging or squeezing dough."
- "Place biscuits on parchment-lined sheet. Slam into freezer for 10 minutes solid. This freezes butter into chunks; when baked, creates steam pockets that produce layers and flakes."
- "Bake at 425°F for 18 to 23 minutes. Watch the color—deep golden, edges crisp, top domed and crackling. Should smell nutty cheddar and herb aroma filling air. Use visual and textural cues over strict timers."
- "Cool on rack 5 minutes before cutting or slathering butter. Interior crumb should be tender with visible layers from butter pockets."
- "If biscuits spread too thin, dough was warmed or improperly rolled. Chill dough longer next time or add a bit more flour."
- "Substitution notes: Swap cheddar with pepper jack for heat or gouda for smoky richness. Buttermilk can be replaced by 3/4 cup sour cream plus 1/4 cup milk to preserve tang and moisture."
