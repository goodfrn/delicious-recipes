ingredients:
- "7 ounces unsalted butter softened but still cool"
- "1 1/2 ounces solid coconut oil chilled but not rock hard"
- "1 1/4 cups powdered sugar sifted"
- "1 tablespoon vanilla bean paste"
- "2 tablespoons evaporated milk cold"
- "Additional evaporated milk as needed up to 2 tablespoons"
instructions:
- "==="
- "Start with chilled butter chunks and coconut oil in mixing bowl. Whip on medium speed until butter breaks down, blending fats to creamy paste; about 3 minutes. Look for pale, fluffy texture without melting into liquid. This initial step traps air for lightness but prevents greasy separation."
- "Drop in vanilla bean paste plus 1 tablespoon evaporated milk. Mix again briefly to combine, scraping bowl sides to gather sticky bits. The bean paste offers specks and aroma you’ll want evenly distributed; paste sticks if ignored."
- "Add sifted powdered sugar in two portions, mixing after each addition. Use low speed to avoid powder clouding your kitchen. After sugar’s mostly incorporated, swap to medium speed. Texture should thicken and stiffen, quiet tapping when bowl shakes."
- "Slowly dribble second tablespoon evaporated milk while whipping, adjusting to texture. Stop as soon as frosting spreads smoothly but holds shape when piped or spread. If frosting feels too stiff, add liquid by teaspoon increments; too soft then add a pinch more sugar."
- "Stop mixing ASAP after perfect texture reached — over-whipping can cause buttercream to melt or grain. Scrape bowl once for uniform consistency, check spreadability with offset spatula. Frosting must cling but not crumble or drip."
- "Store in fridge if not used immediately — bring to room temp and re-whip before piping; cold fats stiffen, working warm avoids greasy spots."
