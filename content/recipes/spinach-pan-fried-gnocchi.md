ingredients:
- "2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil"
- "24 ounces fresh gnocchi"
- "1 cup whole milk ricotta cheese"
- "Zest and juice from 1 large lemon"
- "3/4 cup water"
- "Kosher salt to taste"
- "Freshly ground black pepper to taste"
- "5 cups fresh baby spinach"
- "3 cloves garlic minced"
- "1/3 cup sundried tomatoes drained and patted dry"
- "1/3 cup grated Pecorino Romano cheese"
instructions:
- "Heat oil in a large heavy-bottomed skillet over medium-high until shimmering but not smoking. Swirl pan to coat evenly."
- "Add gnocchi in a single layer, shake the pan gently to prevent sticking. Cook undisturbed until bottom side turns golden brown—listen for faint crackle and watch for deepening color, about 4-7 minutes depending on humidity."
- "Flip gnocchi in batches using tongs, aiming to brown all sides without overturning constantly. Less flipping preserves crust."
- "While gnocchi browns, whisk ricotta with lemon zest, juice, water, salt, and pepper in a bowl until creamy but slightly loose. The added water adjusts sauce viscosity; too thick will clump when heated later."
- "Add about half the spinach to the skillet, toss quickly with tongs. Spinach should visibly shrink and become glossy without losing vibrant green—about 30 seconds definitely no more. Add rest of the spinach, toss again until just wilted."
- "Sprinkle minced garlic over wilted greens, stir constantly for about 40 seconds until fragrant but not browned—bitter garlic ruins dish here."
- "Reduce heat to medium-low and pour ricotta sauce over gnocchi-spinach mixture. Stir gently and continuously until sauce clings, no pooling liquid. If sauce appears too thick, add splash more water."
- "Add sundried tomatoes, fold evenly but gently so tomatoes don’t break down. Heat through for another minute."
- "Remove from heat and sprinkle Pecorino Romano over the top, let residual heat melt cheese slightly."
- "Serve immediately to preserve gnocchi crispness and fresh wilted spinach texture."
