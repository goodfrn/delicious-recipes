ingredients:
- "2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "2 tablespoons cold unsalted butter"
- "2 tablespoons vegetable shortening"
- "1 cup cold cultured buttermilk substitute (plain yogurt or kefir)"
- "1 teaspoon baking powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1/2 teaspoon salt"
- "non-stick spray or melted butter for baking sheet"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 475°F. Lightly grease baking sheet with spray or melted butter for browning control."
- "In large bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Important: sift dry ingredients if clumps show."
- "Cut butter and shortening into flour mixture using pastry blender or two forks. Aim for pea-size chunks with a few smaller crumbs mixed in; this creates layered texture."
- "Gradually pour cold cultured buttermilk substitute into flour-fat mix. Fold gently, no over-mixing. Dough will be sticky and loose but cohesive. Stop when all flour is just hydrated."
- "Flour hands well. Scoop out roughly 1/4 to 1/3 cup per biscuit. Form into tight balls, dusting lightly with flour to prevent sticking. Avoid filling cracks or overly dry balls."
- "Press each ball down gently with fingertips or base of a glass until about 3/4 inch thick. No rolling pin; pressing traps air pockets giving flaky rise."
- "Place on prepped baking sheet spaced to allow spreading."
- "Slide sheet into preheated oven. Listen for low crackle and faint popping, signals of steam expanding layers."
- "Bake roughly 13 to 17 minutes until tops turn golden brown and bottoms develop light tan crust. Edges should crisp and pull away slightly."
- "Remove and brush tops with melted butter immediately if desired for sheen and flavor boost. Cool 5 minutes on rack before serving."
- "Store leftovers wrapped in cloth to maintain soft crumb; refrigeration can dry quickly."
