ingredients:
- "1 box brownie mix (around 18 ounces)"
- "1/2 can sweetened condensed milk (approx 198 grams)"
- "3/4 cup chocolate chips (semi-sweet or dark)"
- "1/2 cup browned butter (substituted for softened butter)"
- "1 teaspoon espresso powder (substitute for vanilla extract)"
instructions:
- "===Prepare batter==="
- "Brown butter in a small pan. Swirl over medium heat until golden brown, nutty aroma rises, foam forms then dies down. Remove from heat; let cool slightly before mixing to avoid scrambling eggs."
- "Empty brownie mix into bowl. Add browned butter and espresso powder. Use a sturdy spoon or spatula to coarsely mix until just combined. Don’t overbeat. Batter will be thick, slightly greasy from butter."
- "===Bake and add condensed milk==="
- "Preheat oven to 335°F (170°C)—lower than box suggests to avoid overbaking edges. Pour batter into greased or parchment-lined 9x13 pan. Tap pan lightly to release air bubbles."
- "Bake 28–32 minutes until edges pull away, top is dry but center still jiggles slightly when nudged. Don’t wait for toothpick to come out perfectly clean; fudgy texture needs moisture."
- "Remove from oven. Immediately drizzle half the can of sweetened condensed milk evenly over hot surface. The milk sinks, creates sticky pockets. The sizzle and aroma change here—a sweet richness unfolding."
- "Sprinkle chocolate chips quickly before milk cools. Chips won’t melt fully but soften to glossy blobs."
- "===Cooling and slicing==="
- "Cool in pan completely, at least 40 minutes. The milk mash sets firm but sticky; slices hold better when fully chilled. Use non-serrated knife wiped clean between cuts to prevent smearing."
- "Store leftovers wrapped tightly at room temp or refrigerate for firmer texture."
- "===Troubleshooting==="
- "If edges overcook and crack too much, lower temp next time and reduce bake time slightly. If condensed milk runs off sides, check that brownies aren’t underbaked or too loose in batter. Brown butter can burn quickly; watch for toasted nuts aroma then immediate removal."
- "Espresso powder is subtle—adds chocolate enhancement without bitterness. Skip if sensitive; vanilla extract can substitute but dial back quantity to ½ teaspoon."
