ingredients:
- "12 large eggs"
- "3 tablespoons whole grain mustard"
- "2 teaspoons apple cider vinegar"
- "1/3 cup Greek yogurt"
- "1/2 teaspoon chipotle powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper"
- "10 small pickled jalapeños diced"
- "1 medium red bell pepper thinly shaved"
- "kitchen tweezers for decorating"
instructions:
- "=== Prepare eggs ==="
- "Place each egg on its side. Make two cuts—one very slim at bottom base to sit flat. Another cut at about 75% up the egg to remove top, creating a lid. Keep all lids intact. Repeat for all eggs."
- "=== Extract yolks ==="
- "Gently tap whites with a potato peeler edge or toothpick to loosen trapped yolks. Remove carefully. Mash yolks finely or grate for even texture. Avoid lumps—lumps show lack of smoothness and waste space when piping."
- "=== Mix yolk paste ==="
- "In a large bowl use a hand mixer or stand mixer with paddle attachment. Combine yolks, whole grain mustard, chipotle powder, and apple cider vinegar until a thick paste forms. Mustard graininess adds texture and sharp acidity."
- "Add Greek yogurt gradually, not mayo. Yogurt offers brightness; mayo is heavier, can swamp flavors. Beat on low till creamy consistency. Season with salt and freshly cracked black pepper. Taste here. Adjust acidity if needed with more vinegar, always balance spicy, tart, creamy."
- "=== Fill eggs ==="
- "Transfer yolk mixture into a sturdy piping bag or gallon-sized ziplock bag with corner snipped. Fill each white half generously. Don’t scrimp — let yolk mound slightly over rim without collapse. Place lids snug but loose. Lid keeps shape, not compression."
- "=== Decorate face ==="
- "Use kitchen shears to cut pickled jalapeños into tiny squares for eyes. Pickled jalapeños replace olives—offering acidic bite and heat rather than briny fat. With tweezers, carefully place jalapeño pieces. Create small noses with thin, shaved red bell pepper. It’s about precise placement. Tweezers prevent smushing filling."
- "=== Serving and storage ==="
- "Hold at room temperature up to 30 minutes before serving. Filling shines best not cold from fridge or warm. Yolks should smell faintly sulfuric but fresh. Peel eggs the same day boiled for easiest shell removal and least white damage."
- "=== Troubleshoot ==="
- "Yolks gritty? Overcooked eggs or insufficient mashing. Rebeat with a fork or fine sieve. Too runny filling? Add more grated yolk or Greek yogurt. Filling not piping well? Chill filling 5 minutes, firm up. Lid falls off? Trim underside edges."
- "=== Notes ==="
- "Fresh eggs peel worst. If possible, use eggs older than 7 days for peel ease. Hard boil eggs by placing in cold water, bring to boil, simmer for 10-12 mins, shock in ice bath till completely cool. Avoid overcooking: green rim signals sulfur overdevelopment. Perfect timing essential."
