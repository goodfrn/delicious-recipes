ingredients:
- "2 cups roasted red beets drained (skin removed)"
- "6 oz dark chocolate chopped"
- "1 1/4 cups granulated sugar"
- "2 large eggs room temperature"
- "1/2 cup avocado oil"
- "1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract"
- "3/4 cup all-purpose flour"
- "3/4 cup rolled oats finely ground"
- "1 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1/2 teaspoon fine salt"
- "1 tablespoon honey"
- "espresso powder for garnish (optional)"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 340 degrees Fahrenheit. Grease a 9x13-inch baking pan lightly with butter or vegetable shortening. Avoid spray oils containing lecithin to prevent uneven browning."
- "Rough-chop roasted beets and pulse in food processor until consistency resembles thick applesauce with small, visible bits. Avoid over-blending to retain some texture for subtle toothfeel."
- "Melt chopped chocolate gently in a microwave-safe bowl at 50 percent power. Stir every 20 seconds to prevent scorched spots. Combine chocolate with beet puree and pulse briefly to amalgamate flavors."
- "Transfer beet-chocolate mixture to a large mixing bowl or stand mixer fitted with paddle attachment."
- "Add sugar, eggs, avocado oil, vanilla, and honey. Blend on medium speed, just until mixture is homogenous. Overmixing creates dense cake, so watch texture closely."
- "Sift together all-purpose flour, finely ground rolled oats, baking soda, and salt. Add flour mixture in three parts to wet ingredients. Fold gently after each addition. Batter should be thick but spreadable, not pasty."
- "Scrape batter evenly into prepared pan, smoothing surface with a spatula or offset knife. Tap pan once on counter to settle batter, release trapped air bubbles."
- "Bake 30 to 35 minutes. Start checking at 28 minutes by inserting thin skewer near center. Expect moist crumbs adhering, not wet batter. Slight wobble in middle is acceptable; cake continues setting once out of oven."
- "Remove from oven when edges pull slightly away from pan and surface springs back when pressed. Cool completely in pan on wire rack before removing to prevent breakage."
- "Before serving, dust surface with espresso powder using fine mesh sieve. Adds bitter contrast and appealing aroma, cuts sweetness without altering texture."
- "If lacking stand mixer, use a sturdy whisk or wooden spoon but be prepared for a bit more arm work. Avoid substituting beet texture with puree canned because fresh or roasted yield aroma and moisture balance."
- "If beets seem watery, drain extra moisture by wrapping in cheesecloth and pressing gently before pulsing."
- "Honey substitute: maple syrup or agave works but may shift flavor notes slightly."
- "For crunch add chopped toasted walnuts on top before baking."
- "Keep cake covered at room temperature up to two days. Refrigeration drys it out quickly."
