ingredients:
- "4 cups fresh cherries pitted and chopped"
- "2 tablespoons fresh lime juice (sub for lemon juice)"
- "1 whole vanilla bean, split and scraped"
- "1.5 teaspoons powdered fruit pectin (low-methoxyl if possible)"
- "3 tablespoons granulated sugar for initial mix"
- "1/3 cup packed brown sugar"
- "3 tablespoons natural honey"
- "Optional twist Add 1 teaspoon cinnamon powder"
- "For canning: quart size canning jars with lids and seals"
instructions:
- "=== Jam preparation ==="
- "Rough chop cherries leaving some larger pieces for texture. Collect all juice released into a wide, heavy-bottomed pot. Add lime juice and scrape vanilla seeds along with pods into pot. Sprinkle pectin and initial sugar over mixture. Stir well to combine everything slowly."
- "Bring pot over high heat. Stir gently but frequently to avoid scorching but watch closely. Wait for a full rolling boil at surface that doesn’t stop when stirred. Boil just enough to use the pectin activation but not too long here or jam can get past stage."
- "Add brown sugar and honey mixture all at once. Stir gently to avoid splashes and maintain heat. Return pot immediately to full boil. This boil must be firm — no bubbles stop when stirring. Once rolling boil is steady again, begin timing exactly 1 minute and 5 seconds. Stir continuously so fruit doesn’t stick or burn on bottom."
- "After timing, remove pot from heat. Remove vanilla bean pods to avoid bitter flavors if jam will be stored long-term."
- "If skipping canning, cool slightly before ladling into clean jars or containers. Let come to room temp then seal and chill. Use within one month."
- "=== Canning instructions ==="
- "Before finishing cooling, fill canner to cover jars with 1 inch of water. Bring water to steady boil over high heat."
- "Submerge emptied, open jars into boiling water for at least 2 minutes to sterilize and warm glass. Use jar lifter with heavy gloves to avoid burns. Flip heated jars upside down on a clean towel. This also drains excess water."
- "Once jam is off heat, set jars upright. Using a funnel and ladle, fill jars leaving 1/4 inch headspace. Wipe rims clean with damp cloth then dry completely with paper towel. Moisture here prevents seal."
- "Put new seals on lids, screw rings on tightly but don’t over-tighten—allow air to escape during processing."
- "Lower filled jars gently into boiling canning water. Water should still cover them by at least 1 inch."
- "Process jars for 12 minutes. Start timing when water returns to full boil. Stir water occasionally to keep temp even, no thermometer needed if managed carefully."
- "Remove jars with lifter. Place upright on dry towel or rack. Do not disturb while cooling — lids must pop down tightly. Check lids after 12 hours; pressing center should be firm with no clicks."
- "Store sealed jars in a cool dark place up to 18 months. After opening refrigerate and consume within 1 month."
