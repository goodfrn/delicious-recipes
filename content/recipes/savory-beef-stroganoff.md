ingredients:
- "2 cups white rice uncooked"
- "1 medium onion chopped"
- "1 cup diced red bell pepper (replace celery)"
- "8 oz cremini mushrooms sliced"
- "2 tablespoons butter divided"
- "2 teaspoons minced garlic divided"
- "1 1/4 pounds ground beef"
- "2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce"
- "1 can (10.5 oz) cream of mushroom soup"
- "1/2 cup milk whole or 2%"
- "1/2 cup plain Greek yogurt (replace sour cream)"
- "1 teaspoon salt"
- "1/2 teaspoon black pepper"
- "2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese divided"
- "Butter for greasing casserole dish"
instructions:
- "===Prep rice and aromatics==="
- "Cook rice according to package instructions. Have fluffy grains, not sticky. Set aside to cool slightly."
- "Chop onion, dice bell pepper finely, slice mushrooms. Keep sizes even to cook uniformly."
- "Heat 1 tablespoon butter in large sauté pan over medium. Toss in onion, bell pepper, mushrooms, and 1 teaspoon garlic. Sizzle till veggies soften; bell pepper loses crunch but not mushy (about 6-8 minutes). Remove mixture from pan, set aside."
- "===Cook beef and build sauce==="
- "Increase heat to medium-high. Add ground beef to empty sauté pan. Break up with spoon, brown evenly until no pink remains, about 7-9 minutes. Drain fat carefully; too much makes sauce greasy, too little leaves gamey texture."
- "Lower heat to medium. Stir in remaining garlic and Worcestershire sauce. Smells rich and slightly smoky now."
- "Add cream of mushroom soup, milk, and Greek yogurt to pan. Stir gently. Avoid boiling hard to prevent yogurt curdling. Add salt, pepper."
- "Return sautéed veggies to skillet. Let sauce simmer gently, slight bubbles at edges, for about 6 minutes. Thickening should coat back of spoon, not run off too fast."
- "===Assemble and bake==="
- "Butter inside of 11×7 casserole dish lightly to prevent sticking and add flavor."
- "Spread cooked rice evenly on bottom. Sprinkle half the shredded cheese over rice; it melts into base layer, providing creamy pockets."
- "Spoon beef and sauce mixture on top, smoothing surface for even coverage."
- "Layer remaining cheese on top to form a melty crust."
- "Bake uncovered in preheated 395°F oven. Watch carefully for bubbling around edges and cheese beginning to turn golden, about 18-20 minutes."
- "Switch to broiler on high for last 2-3 minutes. Cheese should bubble vigorously, spots of deep brown but not burnt. Smell to time it right; burnt cheese smells acrid."
- "Remove immediately. Rest a few minutes before serving for sauce to firm slightly."
- "===Common twists and pointers==="
- "Bell pepper replaces celery for sweetness and color; keeps texture punch without bitterness."
- "Greek yogurt cuts fat and adds tang, less prone to breaking down when heat controlled carefully."
- "Fat removal critical with ground beef; excess causes greasy final dish and soggy rice."
- "Cheese variety matters: sharp cheddar preferred but Monterey Jack gives milder melt."
- "Rice should be fully cooked and cooled to prevent soggy bottom layer."
- "Use medium heat when simmering sauce; too high breaks dairy, too low prolongs cooking and thickening."
- "If sauce too thin after simmer, whisk small cornstarch slurry (1 tsp cornstarch with 1 tbsp cold water) at end and heat till thickened."
- "Broiler step not optional if you want crust; watch constantly to avoid rapid burning."
