ingredients:
- "1 cup cake flour"
- "1 ¼ cups all-purpose flour"
- "1 teaspoon baking powder"
- "½ teaspoon cream of tartar"
- "¾ cup powdered sugar"
- "½ cup unsalted butter, softened"
- "¼ cup vegetable oil"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "¼ teaspoon almond extract"
- "1 large whole egg"
- "1 egg white"
- "zest of half a lemon (optional)"
- "sugar for sprinkling"
instructions:
- "Combine cake flour, all-purpose flour, baking powder, cream of tartar in large bowl. Whisk thoroughly to remove lumps. Set aside."
- "In a different large bowl, beat powdered sugar, softened butter, and vegetable oil with hand mixer till light, fluffy, pale. Add vanilla, almond extract, lemon zest. Beat in whole egg until integrated. Add egg white, continue beating until fully combined. Stop mixer, scrape sides with rubber spatula to avoid dry pockets."
- "Set mixer on low speed, slowly incorporate dry ingredients into wet, mixing until no visible streaks or flour lumps. Dough will look stiff."
- "Cover bowl tightly with plastic wrap, refrigerate for at least 4 hours, ideally overnight. Chilling controls spread—don’t skip!"
- "Preheat oven to 370 F (choose slightly lower temp for better edge control). Line 2 baking sheets with parchment paper."
- "Scoop dough with about 2 tablespoons per ball. Place dough balls on sheets spaced 2 inches apart. Using flat-bottomed glass, gently press each ball to ½-inch thickness. Alternatively, roll dough on lightly floured surface to ½ inch thick; cut circles with cookie cutter."
- "Bake first sheet for 11-13 minutes. Watch edges carefully. Edges should firm up and slightly set; no browning."
- "Remove sheet from oven; immediately sprinkle sugar on tops—adds crunch and sparkle. Cool on pan for about 3 minutes before transferring to wire rack. Repeat with second sheet, bake 9-11 minutes, applying same sugar step."
- "Cool completely before storing or serving. Keeps for several days in airtight container."
- "Served with strong black coffee or tea—the subtle citrus twist and almond notes hold up well."
