ingredients:
- "4 tablespoons unsalted butter; divided"
- "2 cups diced yellow onions"
- "1 and 1/2 cups diced celery"
- "1 and 1/2 cups sliced cremini mushrooms"
- "1 tablespoon minced garlic (about 3 cloves)"
- "1 teaspoon poultry seasoning"
- "3/4 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "1/2 teaspoon freshly cracked black pepper"
- "1 cup wild rice blend (wild rice, brown rice, red rice)"
- "2 cups quartered baby new potatoes (red or gold)"
- "3 large carrots cut into 1-inch chunks"
- "6 cups low sodium vegetable broth (or chicken broth)"
- "1 dried bay leaf"
- "3 cups whole milk (warmed)"
- "3 tablespoons all-purpose flour"
- "Fresh thyme sprigs (2) added during simmer"
instructions:
- "Melt 2 tablespoons butter in a heavy stockpot over medium heat until foamy but not browned. Add onions, celery, carrots. Sauté 6 minutes stirring often. Look for onions turning translucent and carrots starting to soften but still firm. The smells should be sweet and earthy, not burnt."
- "Add mushrooms, garlic, poultry seasoning, salt, and pepper. Cook 4 minutes stirring occasionally. Mushrooms give off moisture then reabsorb it. You want soft mushrooms, browned spots but no soggy mess. Smell should turn rich and aromatic."
- "Toss in wild rice blend, quartered baby potatoes, bay leaf, and broth. Stir to combine. Crank heat to high until mixture reaches rolling boil; bubbles breaking surface rapidly. Then immediately drop heat to low to sustain gentle barely visible simmer. Cover tightly."
- "Add two sprigs fresh thyme tucked under lid. Simmer 35 to 45 minutes. Stir every 8 to 10 minutes scraping bottom so rice won’t stick or burn. Rice will plump, potatoes soften but still hold form. You’ll notice a nutty grain aroma and thicker broth consistency. Bay leaf infuses background herbal notes."
- "Five to ten minutes before done, start béchamel. Warm milk until just warm to touch—no scalding. Microwave can save time; avoid hot spots by stirring halfway."
- "In a separate small saucepan, melt remaining 2 tablespoons butter over medium-low heat. Sprinkle in flour all at once. Stir with wooden spoon continuously 3 to 5 minutes until fragrant and pale golden—not brown, that brings bitterness and ruins sauce texture."
- "Gradually whisk warm milk into roux in thirds, vigorously whisking after each addition to prevent lumps. Sauce will thicken to velvety smooth consistency. Adjust heat to medium-low; keep whisking gently until thickened but still pourable."
- "Pour béchamel directly into soup pot. Stir well. Turn heat to medium-low and simmer uncovered 5 minutes. Soup should thicken noticeably, take on creamy sheen. Taste and tweak salt or pepper. Remove bay leaf and thyme stems before serving."
- "Serve piping hot. Garnish with fresh parsley or chives if desired. Holds well reheated, just stir gently to reincorporate any settled creaminess."
