ingredients:
- "1 box German chocolate cake mix"
- "1/3 cup sweetened condensed milk"
- "3/4 cup clarified butter melted, cooled"
- "2 1/4 cups Snickers minis, chopped rough"
- "1 cup chopped walnuts"
- "1 cup semisweet dark chocolate chips"
- "12 unwrapped caramel squares"
- "1/4 cup plus 3 tablespoons sweetened condensed milk"
- "2 ½ cups milk chocolate chips"
instructions:
- "===Prepare base==="
- "Oven to 350°F. Spray 13x9 pan well — no naked brownie sticking here. Mix cake mix with ½ cup sweetened condensed milk and warmed clarified butter in 3-quart bowl. Hand mixer on low, just until no big lumps. Overmix, starting gluten — tough mess. Reserve one-third batter, cover with plastic, don’t dry it out."
- "Spread remaining two-thirds batter evenly in pan bottom. Bake until edges lift slightly and surface dulls — about 7 to 8 minutes. Look for firm but not browned crust forming."
- "===Caramel layer==="
- "Microwave caramels with ¼ cup sweetened condensed milk in a small bowl. Heat full power 1 min, stir with silicone spatula—sticky bubbles, no burn. Add 15 seconds if needed until spreadable but not hot lava."
- "===Assemble the madness==="
- "Pull pan. Snickers spread evenly, rough chopped better to avoid clumping. Immediately sprinkle dark chocolate chips, then chopped walnuts. Press gently so nothing falls out but keep texture intact. Drizzle that caramel all over the nuts and candy. Streaky drips good, not pool."
- "Remove plastic from saved brownie batter. Dollop by spoonfuls, staggered, over caramel. Don’t cover caramel completely; caramel patches peek out like golden lakes."
- "Return to oven. Bake 15 to 20 minutes. Watch edges set and tiny cracks form — they signal done. Test with toothpick at center; moist crumbs cling but no raw batter."
- "Cool to room temp completely. Don’t rush or chocolate topping melts too soon."
- "===Finishing==="
- "Microwave milk chocolate chips with ¼ cup plus 3 tablespoons sweetened condensed milk 40 seconds. Stir until glossy and pourable. Offset spatula to spread evenly. Set by resting in cool spot, fridge too cold causes bloom, wait instead."
- "Cut 3x6 slices. Each square has chewy base, gooey caramel, crisp nuts, and soft chocolate crown."
- "Serve or stash airtight. Holds moisture but not soggy."
- "Common issues: if caramel too thick, add teaspoon warm milk; if brownies too dry, shorten initial bake or add moisture (like sour cream) to batter next time."
