ingredients:
- "10 cups small pretzels"
- "1/4 cup honey"
- "1/3 cup Dijon mustard"
- "3 tablespoons pure maple syrup"
- "1 teaspoon smoked paprika"
- "1/2 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper"
- "Coarse sea salt for finishing"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 230°F (110°C). Line two 18x13 inch half-size baking sheets with parchment, set aside."
- "In a large mixing bowl, pour pretzels. Measure sauce ingredients: honey, Dijon mustard, maple syrup, smoked paprika, garlic powder, and cayenne pepper. Whisk until smooth and uniform."
- "Pour sauce over pretzels. Use a rubber spatula or wooden spoon to fold pretzels gently. Aim for total coating without smashing. Every kernel should glisten but not drown in sauce."
- "Distribute coated pretzels evenly over prepared baking sheets. Spread in a shallow single layer. A slight overlap is fine but no dense piles; they’ll stick on cooling if too close."
- "Slide trays into oven. Bake 45 minutes, stirring every 12 minutes. Rotate sheets halfway through baking for even heat exposure. Watch for deep golden color, subtle caramel aroma. Avoid dark spots, they burn fast at this temp."
- "Remove when crisp but not brittle. Let cool fully on sheets. They firm up as they cool. Avoid stacking warm pretzels; they’ll steam and lose crunch."
- "Transfer cooled pretzels to a large bowl or sealable baggie. Sprinkle with coarse sea salt and a touch more cayenne if you want a sharper finish. Toss to scatter evenly."
- "Store in airtight container after cooling. Flavor improves overnight. Crunch stays best away from humidity."
