ingredients:
- "2 1/2 cups cake flour"
- "1 small box instant vanilla pudding mix (replace original banana pudding)"
- "1 1/4 tsp baking powder"
- "3/4 tsp salt"
- "1 cup unsalted butter softened"
- "1 1/4 cups granulated sugar (reduce original sugar by 20%)"
- "3 large eggs"
- "1 tsp pure vanilla extract"
- "3/4 cup sour cream"
- "1/2 cup whole buttermilk (swap milk for tangier acidity)"
- "1 1/4 cups mini semi-sweet chocolate chips, tossed in 2 tbsp cake flour"
- "1/2 cup vegetable shortening"
- "Simple syrup:"
- "1/2 cup granulated sugar"
- "1/2 cup water"
- "1 tsp vanilla bean paste (sub vanilla extract)"
- "Vanilla bean frosting:"
- "1 cup powdered sugar"
- "1 1/2 tbsp unsalted butter melted"
- "1 tbsp whole milk"
- "1 tsp vanilla bean paste"
- "pinch salt"
instructions:
- "Set oven to 320 Fahrenheit. Slower bake equals more even crumb. No fan."
- "Grab bundt pan. Dab shortening on paper towel and rub inside nooks and crannies. Coat deeply so nothing clings later; dust lightly with cake flour sifted evenly. Tap excess over sink, not down pan walls."
- "Whisk cake flour, pudding mix, baking powder, salt in large bowl. Pudding mix packs flavor and moisture, don't skip."
- "Stand mixer, paddle on medium speed. Butter soft but not melted? Starts creamy, fluff up sugar in 4 minutes. Scrape. Don’t settle for gritty sugar lumps or you’ll miss that tender texture."
- "Meanwhile, combine sour cream and buttermilk; tang counters sweetness and keeps crumb lively."
- "Drop speed to low. Add eggs one by one, just long enough to vanish yolk – 25 to 30 seconds each. Longer beats air out and toughens crumb. Vanilla bean paste next, stir gently."
- "Start alternating dry and wet ingredients: flour mix, half sour cream combo, rest flour, then sour cream last. Stir / beat until combined, no more. Over mixing ruins moist crumb, be patient."
- "Half batter into pan. Sprinkle half floured chocolate chips evenly. Top with rest batter. Smooth surface with offset spatula, scatter remaining chips. Swirl chips slightly with toothpick to distribute but not mix."
- "Tap pan on counter twice to release air bubbles and settle batter into corners."
- "Bake at 320F for 50 to 55 minutes. Watch edges pull slightly away and surface crack small but firm. Test with skewer; moist crumbs okay but no wet batter."
- "Cool 15 minutes in pan. Don’t force cake out or it tears. Let gravity do work—cake loosens naturally."
- "Simple syrup: Combine sugar and water cold in saucepan. Heat to boil, stir few minutes till sugar dissolves. Off heat, stir vanilla bean paste. Syrup slightly warm, brush over cake using fork holes to soak in, repeat until absorbed."
- "Flip cake onto parchment-lined rack or board. Peel pan slowly away; if it sticks, run thin knife between edges carefully."
- "Fork poke holes all around cake for syrup soak. Brush syrup generously. Let it sit for 20 minutes minimum, syrup sinks better with patience."
- "Vanilla bean frosting: Whisk powdered sugar, melted butter, milk, vanilla bean paste, pinch salt until shiny and pourable. Pour evenly over cooled cake, letting it cascade naturally."
- "Optional mini chip garnish atop glaze for crunch contrast."
- "Cool again until glaze sets, then cut. Use serrated knife to slice clean without crumbling."
- "Store airtight. Cake gets better next day, syrup and glaze meld flavors. If you’re in a hurry, skip syrup but crumb will be drier."
