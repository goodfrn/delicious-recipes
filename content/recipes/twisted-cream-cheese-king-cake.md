ingredients:
- "3 cups hot roll mix or bread flour"
- "1 packet active dry yeast (about 2 1/4 tsp)"
- "3 tbsp raw sugar (or substitute brown sugar for deeper flavor)"
- "1 cup hot water (around 110°F to engage yeast properly)"
- "1 whole egg"
- "3 tbsp unsalted butter, melted; replace half with sour cream for richer texture"
- "8 oz cream cheese, softened"
- "3/4 cup powdered sugar"
- "1 tsp pure vanilla extract"
- "2 tsp ground cinnamon"
- "2 cups powdered sugar for icing"
- "3 tbsp unsalted butter, melted"
- "1 1/2 tsp lemon juice (fresh squeezed preferred)"
- "1 tsp vanilla extract"
- "3 tbsp milk, plus more to thin if needed"
- "Colored sanding sugar or festive sprinkles: green, purple, yellow"
instructions:
- "Mix yeast packet, hot roll mix, and raw sugar in a large bowl. Add hot water, egg, melted butter, and sour cream. Stir until the dough begins to pull away from the bowl edges and clumps together. If too sticky, sprinkle in flour by handfuls to a tacky but manageable state."
- "Dump dough onto a well-floured surface. Shape into a ball with floured hands. Knead vigorously for about 6-7 minutes. Dough should feel elastic; over-kneading makes crumb tight, under-kneading causes poor rise. Add more flour if the dough sticks excessively but avoid drying out."
- "Form the ball again and cover it loosely with an inverted bowl or a damp kitchen towel. Let rest 7-10 minutes. Resting relaxes gluten, easier rolling later."
- "While waiting, whip cream cheese, powdered sugar, vanilla, and caramelized sugar (subbing half powdered for brown caramel notes) in a separate bowl until creamy but thick enough to spread without sliding off dough."
- "Flour a large surface. Roll dough to roughly a 12x18 inch rectangle, slightly thinner on one side for even rolling."
- "Sprinkle cinnamon uniformly over dough surface. Spread cream cheese filling evenly atop, leaving a 1-inch bare border on the edges to seal the roll."
- "Start rolling from the longer side, jelly-roll style. Keep a firm but gentle grip to avoid squeezing filling out. Pinch seam and ends to seal completely to avoid leaks during bake."
- "Transfer to greased or parchment-lined baking sheet. Shape into a loose oval. Cover tightly with plastic wrap and a kitchen towel to keep moisture in. Let rise in a warm spot for about 35-40 minutes until puffed but not doubled."
- "Preheat oven to 375°F while dough proofs. Oven should be hot enough to brown quickly without drying interior."
- "Once risen, bake on middle rack 25 to 30 minutes. Look for glossy, golden crust with darker edges. Tap crust; it should sound hollow. Avoid opening oven early; cooling doesn't stall internal cooking."
- "Remove and cool completely on wire rack. Patience here is key. Warm cake will melt icing and sprinkles."
- "Make icing by mixing powdered sugar, melted butter, lemon juice, vanilla, and milk. Aim for pourable thick glaze. Adjust with extra sugar to thicken or milk to thin. Spoon or drizzle over cooled cake."
- "Finish with Mardi Gras colored sanding sugar or sprinkles immediately before icing sets. The colored sugars add crunch and traditional vibes."
- "Store wrapped at room temp up to 2 days or refrigerate wrapped tightly—warm slightly before serving for best texture."
