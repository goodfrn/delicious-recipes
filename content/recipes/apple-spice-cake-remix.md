ingredients:
- "2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1 teaspoon baking powder"
- "¾ teaspoon kosher salt"
- "2 teaspoons ground cardamom"
- "1 teaspoon ground ginger"
- "½ teaspoon freshly grated nutmeg"
- "1 cup granulated sugar"
- "¾ cup packed light brown sugar"
- "¾ cup vegetable oil (can substitute melted coconut oil)"
- "3 large eggs"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1 cup full-fat Greek yogurt (sub for sour cream or buttermilk)"
- "1½ cups peeled and grated Bartlett pears (can swap Granny Smith apples here)"
- "½ cup roughly chopped toasted walnuts (optional)"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 355°F. Light-colored 9×9-inch pan lined with parchment, excess hanging for easy removal. Butter the parchment edges to stick well."
- "Whisk dry elements in big bowl: flour, baking soda, powder, salt, cardamom, ginger, nutmeg. No lumps or clumps—spices evenly spread avoids bitter pockets."
- "Separate bowl: whisk granulated and brown sugar with oil just to blend, don’t aerate too much or batter gets dry. Add eggs one by one. Beat vanilla and yogurt in last."
- "Pour wet over dry. Fold gently only until flour disappears. Stop early—overmix blows the crumb, makes tough cake. You want streaks gone, slight rough texture."
- "Fold grated pears and walnuts in carefully. Pear moisture less than apple but still juicy, adds subtle sweetness and keeps crumb tender."
- "Spoon batter into pan. Level surface with spatula or back of spoon, don’t press down hard—air bubbles trapped underneath lead to dense spots."
- "Bake 30-38 minutes. Around 35, start checking from edges with toothpick. It should come out almost clean but with moist crumbs clinging little. Cake springs back lightly when pressed top center."
- "Cool in pan 12 minutes—not less. This rest sets cake, prevents cracking when you take it out. Use parchment edges like handles to transfer to wire rack. No grease stains or breakage here."
- "For brown sugar preserve glaze, medium saucepan, melt 3 tbsp butter, ⅔ cup dark brown sugar and ⁄3 cup whole milk over medium-low heat. Stir gently until bubbles appear around edges and mixture thickens slightly to light syrup texture, about 4-5 minutes."
- "Remove from heat. Whisk in 1 ounce cream cheese for tang, 1 tsp vanilla, then 1 cup powdered sugar gradually. Thick but pourable consistency—adjust sugar or milk to thin or thicken."
- "Icing on warm cake spreads and sinks into cracks, melding layers. Too hot cake melts glaze flat; too cool and glaze sits on top, less magic. Aim for cake just warm to touch."
- "Slice warm. Serve with coffee or chai. Wrapping leftovers tight keeps moisture locked for 2 days. Refrigerate glaze separately if made ahead, rewarm gently."
