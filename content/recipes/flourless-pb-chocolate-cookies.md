ingredients:
- "1 cup almond butter (smooth, unsweetened)"
- "1 cup coconut sugar"
- "1 large egg"
- "1 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "Pinch salt"
- "3/4 cup dark chocolate chunks"
- "Sea salt flakes for topping (optional)"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven 345 degrees F. Prepare cookie sheet with parchment paper; prevents sticking, makes cooling easier."
- "In mixing bowl, blend almond butter with coconut sugar first. Scrape sides. Add egg, vanilla, baking soda, and pinch salt. Mix until dough looks uniform with no sugar visible. Chunky bits not welcome here."
- "Fold in dark chocolate chunks gently. Don’t overmix, no batter whipping here. Chocolate pieces must stay intact."
- "Scoop dough about 1 rounded tablespoon, roll quickly between palms. Keep hands dry to avoid sticky mess."
- "Place balls spaced 1.5 to 2 inches apart on lined sheet. Press down gently with fork, form crosshatch pattern. Allows even bake, controls spread, and helps heat penetrate."
- "Sprinkle sea salt flakes on top now, if using. Salt heightens contrast, balances sweetness."
- "Bake 11 to 13 minutes, swapping rack positions halfway through to brown evenly. Watch closely past 10 minutes; edges get golden, centers look set but still soft with slight cracks. Oven behavior varies; trust visual cues over timer."
- "Remove from oven, leave cookies on sheet to cool 10 minutes. They firm up, peel off parchment easily, no sticking, no breakage."
- "Serve warm for melty chocolate or fully cooled for chewy, tender bite."
