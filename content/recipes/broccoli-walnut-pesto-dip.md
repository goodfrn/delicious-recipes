ingredients:
- "350g broccoli florets"
- "30g walnuts, toasted"
- "1 small shallot, peeled and roughly chopped"
- "50g aged parmesan, finely grated"
- "2 tbsp fresh lemon juice"
- "120ml extra virgin olive oil"
- "Salt to taste"
- "Fresh black pepper to taste"
- "Pinch chili flakes optional for heat"
instructions:
- "Steam broccoli in a metal colander over boiling water. Listen for the faint sizzle; watch the florets swell and brighten. Stop when stems pierce easily but still firm — roughly 5-7 minutes depending on size. Avoid floppy mush, no mush allowed."
- "Immediately plunge florets into ice water bath – a quick caffeine jolt for veggies. This shocks them, freezes that vibrant green. Drain thoroughly. Use paper towels or a clean tea towel to pat dry - excess water kills blender, turning sauce greasy and runny."
- "Toast walnuts in dry pan over medium-low heat until fragrant, just until golden edges appear. Cool slightly. Skipping toast? The flavor dulls, boring dip."
- "Into blender, add broccoli, toasted walnuts, chopped shallot, parmesan, lemon juice. Salt, pepper generously. Chili flakes optional - introduce some fire if you like. Pulse briefly to combine. Add olive oil slowly while blending to emulsify into creamy mixture. Scrape sides as needed - don’t skip. Texture should be thick and slightly coarse; no liquid soup."
- "Taste. Adjust salt, lemon juice, or oil balance. Too thick? Drizzle water or more oil a teaspoon at a time, keeping texture substantial."
- "Serve cold with crisp carrots, cucumber slices, pita wedges, or crunchy sturdy chips. Warn guests - dip clings like a boss. Store leftovers airtight in fridge for up to 4 days. If too thick later, stir in a splash olive oil or lemon."
- "Common mistakes: oversteamed broccoli loses grit, dulling flavor. Insufficient drying allows water separation. Too much oil ruins dip’s body. Swap walnuts for roasted almonds or pecans if on hand; swap parm for a nutty pecorino or omit for vegan twist, adding nutritional yeast. Shallots bring gentleness but garlic may be swapped for sharper punch if preferred."
