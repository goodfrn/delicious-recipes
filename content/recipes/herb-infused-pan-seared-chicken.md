ingredients:
- "1 teaspoon smoked paprika"
- "3/4 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper"
- "4 boneless skinless chicken cutlets"
- "2 tablespoons avocado oil"
- "3 cloves garlic minced"
- "1 teaspoon fresh oregano leaves chopped"
- "1 teaspoon fresh rosemary leaves finely chopped"
- "3/4 cup chicken stock"
- "2 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "1 teaspoon fresh lemon juice (optional twist)"
instructions:
- "Mix smoked paprika salt and black pepper in small bowl; toss to blend."
- "Pat dry chicken cutlets with paper towels; lay on plate."
- "Generously coat both sides of chicken with spice mix; press firmly so it sticks."
- "Heat heavy 12-inch skillet over medium heat; swirl in avocado oil."
- "Once oil shimmers and lightly ripples, add chicken cutlets spaced apart."
- "Listen for steady sizzle. Cook 6–7 minutes undisturbed; edges become opaque, underside deep golden brown."
- "Flip chicken, cook 5–7 minutes more or until thickest part reads 165°F meat thermometer. Flesh feels firm, juices clear."
- "Remove chicken; loosely tent with foil on warm plate to rest. Resting juices redistribute, keeps moist."
- "Lower heat to medium-low; add minced garlic chopped oregano and rosemary to skillet."
- "Stir constantly; watch garlic turn translucent and fragrant 1–3 minutes. Don’t brown or burn."
- "Pour chicken stock into skillet scrape brown bits from bottom. Simmer gentle bubbling not furious boil."
- "Reduce liquid by about half—thickens slightly, concentrates flavors; ~4-6 minutes."
- "Whisk in cold butter chunks off heat. Sauce emulsifies, thickens, glossy coat."
- "Optional: stir in lemon juice to cut richness, add brightness."
- "Return chicken back to skillet; spoon sauce over and gently warm 1–2 minutes."
- "Remove chicken onto cutting board; slice cutlets into strips against the grain to keep tender."
- "Plate sliced chicken; drizzle extra herb sauce over. Garnish with more oregano if desired."
introduction: "Start rough drying chicken cutlets off the bat—wet meat steams instead of sears. The dry surface is what gives that sharp crust when hitting hot oil, you want the smell of paprika and fat hitting heat first. Avocado oil here, higher smoke point than olive oil; fewer burnt flavors when you crank pan. Split the spice more toward smoked paprika instead of sweet; deeper notes, different animal. Use fresh herbs—oregano swaps out thyme for something earthier; rosemary stays for that pine background. Garlic softens mellowing the sauce; you do it low and slow to prevent harsh burnt garlic bittering your pan sauce. Reduce that broth to a glaze, keep heat controlled, whisk butter in last off flame—the magic that brings shine and silkiness. Lemon juice at the end; optional. It cuts through the butter like acid on rich food. Rest chicken cutlets tucked loosely or juices run out when slicing. Slice across grain, not with it — tender pieces instead of chewy strings. This technique isn’t fancy but gets chicken juicy, herbaceous, and layered in flavor. Small changes, big difference."
