ingredients:
- "1 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1/2 cup whole wheat flour"
- "1 tsp baking soda"
- "1 tsp ground cinnamon"
- "1/2 tsp ground nutmeg"
- "1/4 tsp salt"
- "1/2 cup vegetable oil"
- "2/3 cup honey"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1 tsp vanilla extract"
- "1 1/2 cups finely shredded carrots, packed"
- "1/2 cup chopped walnuts, optional"
- "For frosting"
- "4 oz cream cheese, softened"
- "4 tbsp unsalted butter, softened"
- "1 cup powdered sugar, sifted"
- "1/2 tsp vanilla extract"
- "1/4 tsp fine salt"
- "Zest of half a lemon"
- "Splash of milk or cream, as needed"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 345°F. Line 9x9 inch pan with parchment paper leaving 2-inch overhang on two sides; easy release later."
- "In large bowl, whisk flours, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, salt until uniform; break lumps. Sifting optional but helps even blend."
- "In second bowl, whisk oil and honey briskly until honey loosens and mixture looks glossy, no honey clumps. Helps incorporate better."
- "Add eggs one at a time. Whisk vigorously after each addition to emulsify fats and eggs; then add vanilla extract."
- "Fold dry ingredients into wet with a rubber spatula. Stop mixing when no streaks remain; batter thick, dense, slightly coarse. Overmixing toughens."
- "Fold shredded carrots and walnuts if using. Buckets of moisture here; coating carrot shreds lightens texture, walnuts add bite."
- "Scrape batter into pan. Thick, heavy batter tries to cling to parchment; use an offset spatula or wet hands to spread evenly. Pat down gently."
- "Slide pan into oven. Listen for ticking oven sounds, watch top color. Bars are done when edges pull slightly from sides, top turns lightly golden, and toothpick inserted near center comes out with moist crumbs but no raw batter."
- "Check at 22 minutes but trust visual and tactile cues more than clock. Underbakes handle better than dry bars; residual heat continues cooking outside oven."
- "Remove bars. Cool on wire rack at least 30 minutes. Fully cool before frosting or cream cheese melts into mess."
- "For frosting, beat softened cream cheese and butter until velvety smooth. Gradually add powdered sugar, lemon zest, vanilla, and salt. Whip until fluffy. If too thick, add small splashes of milk or cream; whisk between additions."
- "Spread frosting evenly on cooled bars, use offset spatula for clean edges. Sprinkle cinnamon or chopped walnuts on top if you like. Chill briefly to set."
- "Lift bars from pan using parchment overhang. Cut into 9 or smaller pieces with sharp knife wiped between slices to avoid crumbles."
- "Store leftover bars in airtight container in fridge up to 3-4 days. Bring to room temp before serving for tender crumb and flavor pop."
