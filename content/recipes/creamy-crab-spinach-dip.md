ingredients:
- "8 ounces cream cheese softened"
- "1/3 cup sour cream"
- "2 garlic cloves minced"
- "1 teaspoon prepared horseradish"
- "10 ounces imitation crab meat shredded"
- "1 cup fresh spinach chopped"
- "1/2 teaspoon salt"
- "1/4 teaspoon black pepper"
- "nonstick spray for dish"
instructions:
- "===Preheat and Prep==="
- "Oven to 345F. Not too hot, avoids burning cream cheese edges. Spray an 11x7 baking dish lightly with nonstick spray. Avoid too much oil—dip should stick slightly for crust formation."
- "===Mix Base==="
- "In large bowl, soften cream cheese first. Add sour cream, create loose, creamy base. Fold in minced garlic and horseradish. Horseradish sharpens, don’t overdo unless you want heat. Check texture: slightly thick with a loose slump."
- "===Add Main Ingredients==="
- "Fold in shredded imitation crab and chopped spinach. Spinach adds moisture and body. Season with salt and freshly cracked black pepper. Mix evenly but don’t over stir; chunks should be visible, bite intact."
- "===Bake==="
- "Spread evenly in dish. Thickness matters: 1-inch layer bakes best. Into oven, 25 minutes. Look for golden edges and bubbling center. The smell changes from raw dairy to nutty, roasted garlic. Slight wobble signals doneness; not rubbery or dried out."
- "===Serve==="
- "Hot out of oven is best. Use sturdy crackers, pita chips, sliced cucumbers or bell peppers. Holds heat but will cool and firm up—if reheating, add a splash of sour cream and stir gently before warming."
- "===Tips and Tricks==="
- "Softened cream cheese beats hotter versions to avoid lumps. If crab is frozen, thaw fully and drain moisture to prevent watery dip. Substitute spinach with kale or chard if desired, finely chopped to prevent toughness. Garlic substitution: roasted garlic for mellow flavor but bake time should increase 3-5 minutes. Horseradish can be replaced with Dijon mustard for tang that integrates well with sour cream."
