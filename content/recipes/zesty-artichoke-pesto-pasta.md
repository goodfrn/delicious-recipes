ingredients:
- "1 small shallot chopped"
- "2 cloves garlic smashed"
- "10 ounces canned artichoke hearts drained"
- "2 Meyer lemons zested and juiced"
- "1/2 teaspoon coarse sea salt"
- "1/4 teaspoon freshly ground white pepper"
- "1/3 cup extra virgin olive oil"
- "10 ounces cavatappi pasta"
- "2 tablespoons pine nuts, toasted"
- "2 tablespoons chopped sun-dried tomatoes (oil-packed preferred)"
- "2 tablespoons grated pecorino Romano cheese"
instructions:
- "Rough chop shallot and garlic. Pulse in a food processor just until minced evenly. Avoid over-processing; want texture, not paste."
- "Add artichokes, lemon zest, lemon juice, salt, and white pepper. Pulse twice more to combine ingredients without fully pureeing. Texture should be coarse chunks, not smooth sauce."
- "Slowly drizzle olive oil in and pulse 1-2 times more. The oil should just bind the mixture, not fully emulsify."
- "Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook cavatappi until just shy of firm bite (al dente). Drain promptly. Pasta will finish cooking slightly in the sauce."
- "Warm pesto briefly in a small saucepan over medium-low heat, stirring until aromatic steam rises and sauce loosens. Avoid high heat or boiling; citrus will turn bitter."
- "Toss pasta with warmed pesto off direct heat. Stir in garlic-scented pine nuts and sun-dried tomatoes for contrast in texture and flavor."
- "Divide pasta among bowls. Sprinkle with sharp pecorino Romano. Serve immediately to capture fresh lemon aroma and melted cheese richness."
- "Leftover pesto can be stored chilled up to 2 days. Rewarm gently with small splash of water or oil to loosen before tossing."
