ingredients:
- "3 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1 tablespoon active dry yeast"
- "1½ tablespoons granulated sugar"
- "1½ tablespoons honey"
- "1 teaspoon salt"
- "1 cup warm almond milk (about 110F)"
- "3 tablespoons olive oil (instead of butter)"
- "1 large egg"
- "Extra flour up to ¾ cup more, divided"
- "4 tablespoons melted garlic herb butter (for brushing post bake)"
instructions:
- "Mix 3 cups flour, yeast, sugar, honey, salt in stand mixer bowl. Stir to blend dry spices and yeast even."
- "Add almond milk, olive oil, egg. Attach dough hook. Start mixer low speed till flour and wet ingredients combine, scrape sides often."
- "Increase to medium speed. Beat 2 ½ minutes. Look for dough pulling clean from bowl but still tacky, sticky but manageable."
- "Add ½ cup flour, mix till folded in. Repeat with another ½ cup flour. Knead 1-2 minutes more at medium speed till dense ball forms."
- "Gradually add up to ½ cup more flour. Don’t go beyond if dough is sticky but holds shape, just slightly tacky and pliable."
- "Transfer to lightly oiled bowl. Cover with damp towel. Let rise about 35 minutes at room temp, until dough doubles and jiggles slightly when nudged."
- "Deflate by pressing dough with knuckles lightly, not too hard to retain some air pockets."
- "Pinch off equal portions, 20 balls total. Shape smooth, taut surfaces making rolls uniform for even baking."
- "Place balls in greased 9x13 pan, leave space to expand. Cover with towel. Rise another 35 minutes or until puffy, not quite doubled."
- "Preheat oven to 375F; bake rolls 17-20 minutes till tops golden brown and firm to touch."
- "If crust darkens too fast, tent foil loosely—keeps moisture in without sogginess."
- "Remove, brush immediately with warm garlic herb butter for aroma and sparkle."
- "Serve warm or cool completely before storing air-tight up to 3 days."
- "To refresh stale rolls, heat wrapped loosely with foil at 300F 10 min, restores softness."
