ingredients:
- "1 pound raw shrimp peeled and deveined"
- "¼ teaspoon salt"
- "¼ teaspoon black pepper"
- "1 large egg"
- "½ cup rice flour"
- "1 cup vegetable oil for frying"
- "¼ cup pure maple syrup"
- "1 cup pecans"
- "3 tablespoons water"
- "3 tablespoons Greek yogurt"
- "3 tablespoons honey"
- "4 tablespoons sweetened condensed milk"
- "2 green onions chopped for garnish"
instructions:
- "=== Prepare shrimp ==="
- "Pat shrimp dry then toss with salt and pepper in a large bowl. Sets flavor base and reduces moisture for better crispiness."
- "In one small bowl whisk egg until homogenous."
- "In another bowl place rice flour. Use rice flour for crisp-light texture; cornstarch often heavier and soggier after resting."
- "=== Coat shrimp ==="
- "One by one, dip shrimp in egg. Let excess drip off—too thick coating traps moisture and ruins crisp."
- "Then dredge shrimp in rice flour. Press lightly to adhere, but don’t clump. Place on plate; repeat for all shrimp."
- "=== Toast pecans ==="
- "In small saucepan combine maple syrup, pecans, and water. Simmer medium-low for 12 minutes. Syrup thickens around nuts. Remove and drain on paper towel; pecans should be sticky but not soggy."
- "=== Fry shrimp ==="
- "Heat oil in heavy skillet to shimmering but not smoking. Test by dropping tiny flour bit—should sizzle immediately."
- "Fry shrimp in batches; do not crowd. Cook shrimp about 1.5-2 minutes per side. Golden edges and firm bodies tell when done—overcook, shrimp toughen."
- "Drain fried shrimp on wire rack or paper towels. Keep warm."
- "=== Make sauce ==="
- "Whisk Greek yogurt, honey, and sweetened condensed milk in large bowl. Yogurt brighter than mayo, less heavy; adds tang and thinner mouthfeel."
- "=== Combine and serve ==="
- "Toss warm shrimp gently in sauce to coat evenly. Texture contrast crucial—don’t drown shrimp or sauce loses poise."
- "Serve over steamed rice. Sprinkle toasted pecans and chopped green onions atop for crunch and fresh bite."
- "=== Tips and troubleshooting ==="
- "Rice flour works best here; potato starch turns gummy. Oil temp steady around 350°F; too low, coating absorbs oil; too high, burns quickly."
- "Maple syrup gives nut caramelization a richer flavor than plain sugar. If syrup unavailable, try light molasses or agave."
- "If sauce looks watery, add a small dollop more condensed milk and whisk vigorously to emulsify."
- "If shrimp stick together in frying, check for oil temperature and try smaller batches. Drain shrimp thoroughly before frying."
- "Store leftover sauce separately; re-toss shrimp just before serving to keep crisp."
- "Green onions add freshness but can swap with cilantro or chives depending on preference."
