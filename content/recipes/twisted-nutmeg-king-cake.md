ingredients:
- "1 package active dry yeast"
- "2 teaspoons sugar (for yeast bloom)"
- "1 cup warm water about 110F"
- "3/4 cup scalded whole milk, cooled"
- "1/4 cup melted unsalted butter"
- "2 large eggs"
- "3/4 cup brown sugar (sub for white sugar)"
- "1/2 teaspoon fresh grated nutmeg"
- "1 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "3 1/2 cups bread flour, plus extra for adjustments"
- "Filling:"
- "1/2 cup softened unsalted butter"
- "3/4 cup packed light brown sugar"
- "2 teaspoons cinnamon (swapped from just nutmeg)"
- "1/4 cup chopped pecans (replaces some traditional nuts for better crunch)"
- "Glaze:"
- "1 cup powdered sugar"
- "2 tablespoons honey (instead of corn syrup)"
- "1/2 teaspoon granulated garlic powder (unexpected savory twist)"
- "1-2 tablespoons milk"
instructions:
- "Bloom yeast quick by stirring together yeast, 2 teaspoons sugar with 1 cup warm water, not hotter than 115F or it kills yeast. Look for foam and bready smell, 8-12 minutes max; no bubbles means dump and restart."
- "Pour in milk scalded and cooled (hot but safe to touch), melted butter and eggs into yeast mixture. Whisk in rest of brown sugar, fresh nutmeg and salt until smooth; brown sugar gives deeper molasses note but watch moisture."
- "Switch to dough hook or beaters if no hook. Gradually add flour 1/2 cup at a time, observing dough pulling cleanly from bowl sides, tacky but not sticky. Adjust with 1-2 teaspoons flour as needed; too dry equals tough bread."
- "Knead at low-medium speed or hand knead on floured surface for around 12 minutes, not less. Dough should resist stretch, elastic but springy. Too loose means under kneaded. Trap moisture with proper gluten development for rise."
- "Grease large bowl lightly with oil, place dough inside, turning once to coat inside and exterior lightly. Cover with damp tea towel or cloth, find draft-free, warm spot—30 to 90 minutes. Dough doubles roughly in volume, surface smooth with air pockets visible beneath."
- "Mix filling ingredients thoroughly in a small bowl until evenly spreadable with soft butter coating pecans and sugars. Cinnamon brings warmth, pecans add texture and subtle bitterness for balance. Avoid wet mixture to prevent soggy dough."
- "Flour counter; roll dough to 18 by 8 inches roughly rectangular. Use rolling pin pressure allowing even thickness but not paper thin or thick lumps."
- "Cut dough lengthwise down middle into two equal strips. Spread filling evenly over each strip; leave about 1/2 inch edge clear to ensure proper sealing during rolling."
- "Roll each long strip jelly-roll style tight enough to hold but avoid tearing. Pinch ends of both rolls together, gently twist ropes around each other creating a braid with visible spiral layers. Form into circle wreath shape; pinch ends tightly to seal to stop opening in oven."
- "Place twisted circle on baking tray lined with parchment. Cover loosely with dampened tea cloth, let rise again in warm spot 40 to 70 minutes or until puffy but not overproofed. Overproofed dough collapses and bakes flat."
- "Fire up oven to 380 degrees F, hot oven critical to set crust quickly and seal interior moisture. Bake for 25-35 minutes, watch for golden brown color all around crust and faint nutmeg-spiked scent. Bottom should sound hollow on tap."
- "Cool thoroughly on rack at least 45 minutes before glazing—warm glaze melts too fast and sugar crystal bleed. Whisk powdered sugar, honey, garlic powder, and milk to thick but pourable glaze consistency."
- "Drizzle glaze in stripes or zigzags while cake surface still slightly tacky but cool. Immediately scatter colorful sugars purple-green-gold or crushed candied citrus zest for festive crunch. Glaze sets tight but sticky."
- "Serve at room temperature. Store wrapped loosely in foil to maintain moist crumb but avoid sogginess. Reheat lightly if needed; avoid microwave to protect crumb structure."
