ingredients:
- "7 ounces cream cheese softened"
- "2/3 cup salted butter softened"
- "3/4 cup almond butter creamy"
- "3/4 cup coconut sugar packed"
- "1 1/2 cups powdered sugar sifted"
- "1/2 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1/2 cup rolled oats old fashioned or quick"
- "1/2 cup mini chocolate chips"
- "1/2 cup dried cranberries chopped if large"
- "1/2 cup roasted pecans roughly chopped"
instructions:
- "Throw cream cheese into stand mixer or big bowl. Whip at medium until creamy, no lumps visible. Stop, scrape sides. Almost no grainy bits or clumps left."
- "Add softened butter and almond butter. Beat again medium speed. Look for integration, creamy texture. Should hold air, but not too fluffy; fold in sugars next."
- "Pour in coconut sugar and powdered sugar. Salt and vanilla join the party. Whip 2-4 minutes, careful with speed. Notice the change: denser texture, light sheen on surface. Use spatula to scrape every corner. No gritty sugar pockets allowed."
- "Dump oats, chocolate chips, cranberries, and pecans. Fold gently by hand. Check distribution visually, pockets of mix-ins? Break 'em up. Should be even throughout. Avoid beating lest chips melt or pecans get pulverized."
- "Cover with plastic or airtight container. Chill minimum 1 hour. Texture changes from loose spread to scoopable but still creamy. Too cold? Let sit 5 minutes. Too soft? Back in fridge briefly."
- "Serve with crisp dippers: apple slices, crackers, or pretzels—nothing too flimsy. Scoop with sturdy spoon for neat serving. Leftovers chill well up to 5 days."
- "Problems? If too sweet, add extra pecans or oats next batch. Too sticky? Bench chill helps. Don't skip salt—balances fat and sugar, essential flavor layer."
- "Substitutions: Almond butter stands in for peanut—smoother, less salty. Pecans for pretzels adds crunch and earthiness; cranberries bring brightness instead of classic candy colors."
