ingredients:
- "1 serrano pepper, seeded and roughly chopped"
- "2 cloves garlic, peeled and chopped"
- "1 inch fresh turmeric root, peeled and chopped"
- "1/3 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice"
- "1/4 cup raw honey"
- "1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar"
- "1/4 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "1/3 cup cilantro leaves loosely packed"
- "1/4 cup fresh basil leaves loosely packed"
- "1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil"
instructions:
- "Toss chopped serrano, garlic, and turmeric into food processor. Pulse in quick bursts until you see tiny, evenly chopped bits. Look for moist, fragrant pieces – don’t puree to paste stage. Keeps texture clean, bites bright."
- "Add lemon juice, honey, apple cider vinegar, sea salt, cilantro, and basil leaves on top. Pulse again but watch consistency closely. You want a smooth drizzle, that holds but still moves freely. Stop early if oily sheen forms – emulsify longer only if separates."
- "With motor running on low, slowly stream olive oil through feeding chute. Notice how mixture thickens slightly, glossy finish. That’s your emulsion locking in flavor and texture. Avoid dumping oil too fast or it’ll break—pour slow, steady, watch texture change."
- "Serve over robust greens, chilled shrimp, or grilled chicken. Use immediately for freshest pop but store in airtight container in fridge up to 8 days. Bring to room temp, shake before each use. If it separates, whisk vigorously or pulse briefly in blender again."
- "Troubleshooting: If too tangy, add a teaspoon honey at a time. Too spicy? Remove seeds next time or use milder chili like poblano. No food processor? Use fine mince knife method; takes longer but works if patience intact. To extend shelf life beyond a week, add a teaspoon of Dijon mustard for stability and slight flavor lift."
