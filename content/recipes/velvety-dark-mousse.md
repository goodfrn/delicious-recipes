ingredients:
- "1/3 cup heavy cream ; plus 1 1/4 cups cold heavy cream"
- "5 oz bittersweet chocolate chips or chopped bar (at least 60% cacao)"
- "1/2 tsp good-quality instant espresso powder (optional, enhances chocolate)"
- "1/2 tsp pure vanilla extract"
- "2 tbsp powdered erythritol or confectioners sugar"
- "Pinch of salt"
- "Fresh raspberries or micro mint for garnish"
instructions:
- "Heat 1/3 cup heavy cream in microwave safe measuring cup. Watch for first steam wisps and tiny bubbles near edge — that heat level. About 50 seconds depending on microwave power. Hot to fingertip test but not boiling."
- "Put bittersweet chocolate and espresso powder in large heatproof bowl. Pour hot cream over chocolate. Let sit undisturbed for exactly 4 minutes. Chocolate melting, that glossy sheen emerging underneath. Don’t stir yet or risk seizing."
- "After 4 minutes, stir gently with a rubber spatula from center outwards. Should be velvety, no lumps visible, shiny melted chocolate pool. Set aside to cool on counter for 15-18 minutes until mixture thickens but still pourable. Warming bowl lightly with hands helps judge."
- "In chilled mixing bowl (preferably metal chilled 10 mins prior), combine 1 1/4 cups cold heavy cream with vanilla extract, erythritol, and a pinch of salt. Start whisking on medium speed."
- "Watch cream thicken into soft peaks stage—tips drooping slightly, still loose but airy. Then speed up to high until stiff peaks form. Peak tips stand firm without collapsing. Avoid over-whip and grainy butter forming."
- "Take one quarter of whipped cream and fold into chocolate base with careful circular folding motion. Lift from bottom and fold over to keep air bubbles intact. Mixture will lighten in color and texture."
- "Add another quarter whipped cream, fold gently again to maintain volume."
- "Add remaining whipped cream. Fold until homogenous in color but some light airiness remains. Stop before mixture fully deflates."
- "Transfer mousse to piping bag fitted with large round tip. Pipe into pre-chilled dessert glasses or ramekins. Alternatively, spoon carefully to avoid breaking mousse peaks."
- "Cover with plastic wrap pressed directly on mousse surface to prevent skin formation."
- "Chill for minimum 1 hour. Mousse sets from cool firmness and slows flavor melding."
- "Just before serving garnish with fresh raspberries or finely chopped micro mint leaves. Optional dust of cocoa powder."
- "Lick bowl is fine, but reheating or overmixing will spoil texture."
