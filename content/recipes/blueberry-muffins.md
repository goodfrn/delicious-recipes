ingredients:
- "2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "3/4 cup granulated sugar"
- "1/2 teaspoon salt"
- "2 teaspoons baking powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking soda"
- "2/3 cup vegetable oil"
- "1 cup plain Greek yogurt"
- "2 large eggs"
- "2 teaspoons vanilla extract"
- "1 teaspoon lemon zest"
- "1 1/2 cups fresh blueberries"
- "turbinado sugar for sprinkling"
instructions:
- "Set oven to 380°F, slightly hotter to encourage rise and golden crust. Line 12 muffin tin cups with liners or grease thoroughly. Essential to prevent sticking, especially with oil base."
- "Dry mix first. Whisk flour, sugar, salt, baking powder, baking soda vigorously. Break clumps early. Uniform blend essential for even rising."
- "Separate bowl: whisk oil, yogurt, eggs, vanilla, lemon zest until smooth. Yogurt adds moisture and tang; lemon zest brightens aroma keeping muffins from tasting flat."
- "Pour wet over dry; fold carefully just until barely moistened. Lumps good. Overmix = tough, leaden crumb. Use rubber spatula, scoop bottom up. Stop once streaks gone."
- "Gently fold in blueberries. Don't smash. For less sinkage, toss berries in flour first. Prevents bunching at bottom. Method critical for even fruit distribution."
- "Divide evenly into cups. Fill 3/4 full. Too full = spillover; too low = dry edges. Sprinkle tops generously with turbinado sugar. Crunch and sparkle after baking."
- "Bake 30 to 35 minutes. Watch muffins, golden tops, slight dome, edges pulling from liners. Toothpick test near center: Clean or crumbs attached ok. If wet batter, give a few more."
- "Cool 5 minutes in pan. Important for structure. Then move to rack. Cooling prevents soggy bottoms and keeps tops crisp."
- "Listen for faint crackling sugar on muffin surface in final minutes. Smell fruity, lemon-peppered aroma. Signals correct baking completion."
