ingredients:
- "3 boneless skinless chicken breasts"
- "1/3 cup homemade basil pesto or store-bought"
- "1/2 cup shredded mozzarella or provolone cheese"
- "1 cup cherry tomatoes"
- "2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice or white wine vinegar"
- "3 tablespoons olive oil"
- "salt to taste"
- "freshly ground black pepper to taste"
- "1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika (optional twist)"
- "non-stick cooking spray"
instructions:
- "Oven to 380°F, not too low or you'll dry the chicken; spray a shallow baking dish with a light coat of oil or non-stick spray; no excessive oil traps moisture properly."
- "Slice chicken side to side, horizontal slit nearly through but leave hinge uncut. Open like a book. Careful, don’t cut all the way or filling will leak out — easier to stuff and fold when the meat is even thickness inside."
- "Spread 3 Tbsp of pesto inside each breast, more or less depending on your taste; avoid watery pesto on meat – excess moisture ruins browning. Sprinkle 3 Tbsp shredded mozzarella or provolone over pesto."
- "Press chicken closed, pinch edges. Use 2-3 toothpicks per breast to hold stuffing tight. This avoids oozing filling and helps cook evenly."
- "Scatter cherry tomatoes around chicken breasts in pan for added moisture and flavor. Cherry tomatoes should be dry before roasting, rinse and pat gently to avoid excess water."
- "Drizzle lemon juice or white wine vinegar over chicken and tomatoes. Pour olive oil to coat everything; helps crisp the chicken skin surface and caramelizes tomatoes."
- "Season with sea salt, freshly ground black pepper, and smoked paprika if using. Paprika adds depth and a bit of warmth to counterbalance the acidity of lemon juice."
- "Cover pan loosely with foil — shields chicken from drying out before reaching 165°F internal temp but allows moisture to circulate."
- "Place in oven center rack. Listen for gentle crackle of tomatoes blistering and juices bubbling. Bake 50-55 minutes, sometimes less if breasts are thinner. Jiggle the pan lightly at 45 mins; juices should run clear with no pink."
- "Remove foil in final 10 minutes for slight browning on cheese and chicken surface. Browned cheese is your sign chicken's heating through well."
- "Let chicken rest 5-10 minutes after baking; carryover temp pushes inside to safe levels and juices redistribute. Toothpicks out before slicing or serving."
- "Serve alongside steamed broccoli, sautéed Brussels sprouts, or tossed salad with vinaigrette. Pasta or rustic bread to soak up flavorful pan drippings works great too."
