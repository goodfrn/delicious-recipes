ingredients:
- "1 cup ricotta cheese softened"
- "1/3 cup basil pesto"
- "40 crackers sturdy, buttery, with a hint of pepper"
- "1/2 large cucumber sliced paper-thin"
- "8 oz smoked salmon thin slices"
- "2 tablespoons green olives chopped fine (substitute for capers)"
- "Fresh thyme sprigs small pieces"
- "Flaky sea salt to finish"
- "Freshly cracked black pepper optional"
instructions:
- "===Mixing Pesto Ricotta==="
- "Spoon ricotta into a medium bowl. Add basil pesto. Fold gently—don't overmix or ricotta loses fluff. Look for uniform green streaks through creamy white, spreadable but not runny. If too thick add tiny splash olive oil to loosen."
- "===Assembly==="
- "Lay out crackers on tray. Use a small offset or butterknife spreading thin even layer ricotta-pesto mixture. Too thick masks delicate bite; too thin means dryness. Aim just to coat the surface."
- "===Layering==="
- "Place one fragile cucumber slice over half the crackers. Skip cucumber on others for texture contrast. Cucumber should be paper-thin to prevent cracking crackers or overwhelming flavors."
- "Top each cracker with a fold of smoked salmon bite-sized but not bulky. Tuck in chopped green olives instead of capers; note olives add smooth saltiness rather than sharp pop."
- "Sprinkle small thyme leaves rather than dill. Garlic or lemony thyme fits better here, brings subtle complexity under smoky richness."
- "Lightly tap flaky sea salt over all. Optional fresh cracked black pepper adds bite on top but avoid too much—salmon salty already."
- "===Serving Tips==="
- "Arrange on platter allowing air to circulate—prevents sogginess. Serve immediately or hold up to 30 minutes at room temp. Longer sits call for chilling and adding cracker base last minute to keep crunch."
- "Note moisture: cucumber recipes prone to sogginess. For longer holds, pat cucumber dry with paper towel before use. Consider serving cucumber slices on the side if dampness risk is high."
- "===Troubleshooting==="
- "If ricotta mix too firm and difficult to spread, soften by whisking briefly with olive oil. Too wet leads to crackers losing crunch fast—dry ingredients best kept dry until just before serving."
- "If smoked salmon overwhelming for less adventurous eaters, swap half with thinly sliced smoked trout or cooked shrimp chunks for variety."
- "Use sturdy crackers with some texture—plain water crackers too fragile, may crack under topping weight."
- "===Clean Up and Leftovers==="
- "Any leftover pesto ricotta makes excellent dip—serve with veggies or spread in sandwiches. Leftover salmon best used within a day. Store components separately to keep freshness."
- "Discard soggy crackers; homemade or store-bought with oils last longer in sealed container post-assembly."
