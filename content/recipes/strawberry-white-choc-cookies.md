ingredients:
- "1 box strawberry cake mix, about 15 ounces"
- "1/3 cup melted unsalted butter, room temperature"
- "2 large egg whites"
- "3/4 cup chopped dark chocolate or white chocolate, your choice"
- "Optional twist: 1/4 cup freeze-dried crushed strawberries for sharp tang"
instructions:
- "===Preparation==="
- "Cut parchment paper to size, line sheet pan completely. Preheat oven to 355°F for even heat penetration; hotter ovens brown edges too fast, cooler takes longer but preserves color."
- "===Mixing==="
- "Pour cake mix into bowl. Add melted butter—not oil. Butter adds richness and creates softer crumb. Follow with egg whites—whites reduce density and provide structure without extra fat. Toss in your chocolate chunks. Stir just until combined; overmixing engages gluten, makes cookies dense and dry. Dough should be thick but pliable."
- "===Forming Cookies==="
- "Using a spoon or cookie scoop, scoop balls about two tablespoons each. Place on parchment ensuring 2 inches of space between. Dough spreads slightly but not aggressively. Sprinkle crushed freeze-dried strawberries on top if using; adds bold texture and bite."
- "===Baking==="
- "Slide pan into center of oven. Listen for soft crackles as edges set. Watch color carefully—edges will firm first, center should remain pale, never brown. Pull cookies out around 14 minutes but start checking after 9. When edges resist gentle touch but centers yield slightly, done. No golden hues allowed, or heat was too high."
- "===Cooling==="
- "Let cookies rest on pan 7 minutes. This finishes baking via residual heat and firms up. Transfer with spatula to cooling rack to prevent sogginess underneath."
- "===Tips & Troubleshooting==="
- "No parchment? Lightly grease pan or use silicone mat. Using oil? Switch to butter if you crave deeper flavor or for more even baking. Oven hotter than 360°F? Crank baking time down; watch edges beforehand. Dough too runny or crumbly? Chill for 10 minutes; easier handling. Want softer cookies? Reduce stirring times, add a tsp of milk."
