ingredients:
- "3 1/4 tablespoons unsalted butter softened"
- "3 tablespoons vegetable oil"
- "180 grams granulated sugar"
- "85 grams dark brown sugar packed"
- "2 large eggs plus 1 large egg yolk"
- "3 tablespoons honey instead of corn syrup"
- "1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract"
- "28 grams cocoa powder unsweetened"
- "85 grams oat flour substitute for all-purpose flour"
- "1/4 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "140 milliliters heavy cream"
- "2 tablespoons honey"
- "1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "pinch of salt"
- "90 grams bittersweet chocolate chips"
- "candy coated chocolates for sprinkling"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 345 degrees Fahrenheit. Spray an 8-inch square pan thoroughly and line with parchment, leaving some overhang to lift brownies out later."
- "In a mixing bowl or stand mixer with paddle, cream butter and oil together with granulated and brown sugar until fluffy. About 3-4 minutes at medium speed. Pay attention—proper creaming traps air needed for texture."
- "Add whole eggs, yolk separately, honey, and vanilla. Whisk on medium speed till sugar dissolves fully and mixture becomes glossy. Should take 4 to 5 minutes. No visible sugar grains under tongue check."
- "In a different bowl sift cocoa powder with oat flour and salt, breaking any lumps. Oat flour brings tenderness but handle gently—it doesn’t hold gluten like wheat flour."
- "Fold dry ingredients into wet, mixing just until no dry streaks remain. Avoid stirring down or batter will toughen from gluten development. Batter will be thick, glossy, almost fudge-like."
- "Pour batter into pan and level using offset spatula. Tap pan sharply a few times on counter to release air bubbles. Ensures even baking and steady crumb."
- "Bake 25 to 30 minutes, start checking at 25. Use surface appearance to judge: tops shiny and set, edges pulling away. Insert toothpick near center; it should come out with moist crumbs, not wet batter."
- "Remove from oven. Let rest on wire rack 10 minutes but do not cool fully in pan. Run spatula or knife around edges to detach. Then invert brownies onto prepared rack above tray and peel off parchment from bottom. This prevents soggy crusts and promotes uniform cooling."
- "Prepare ganache: gently heat cream on medium-low till steam just rises but not boiling. Stir in honey, vanilla, and pinch of salt for depth."
- "Take cream off heat and add chocolate chips. Stir constantly till fully melted and shiny, incorporating a tablespoon of butter for richness and glossy finish."
- "Pour ganache evenly over cooled brownies using offset spatula to spread to edges. Hit pan lightly on counter to smooth out bubbles."
- "While still tacky, sprinkle candy coated chocolates liberally. Adds crunch and color; press lightly to secure but don’t embed them deep or ganache won’t set nicely."
- "Refrigerate uncovered for about 40 to 50 minutes to harden ganache. Use sharp knife cleaned between cuts and slice into 16 equal squares with confident, smooth strokes."
- "Store in airtight container at room temp or fridge depending on climate. For softer texture let reach room temp before eating. Quick tip—melt a little butter and flick over top for shine if ganache dulls during storage."
