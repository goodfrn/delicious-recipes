ingredients:
- "1 1/4 lbs ground pork"
- "4 cups chopped broccoli florets"
- "1/2 cup sliced mushrooms (shiitake or cremini)"
- "3 green onions, thinly sliced"
- "3 garlic cloves, minced"
- "1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated"
- "2 tbsp vegetable oil (divided)"
- "3 tbsp soy sauce"
- "1 tbsp oyster sauce"
- "1 tsp sesame oil"
- "1 1/2 tsp sugar"
- "1 tbsp rice vinegar"
- "1 tbsp cornstarch"
- "1/3 cup water"
- "1/2 tsp black pepper"
- "1 tsp kosher salt (divided)"
- "Optional garnish sesame seeds"
instructions:
- "=== Sauce ==="
- "Mix soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, sugar, vinegar, cornstarch, and water in a bowl. Whisk until cornstarch disappears. Set aside."
- "=== Aromatics ==="
- "Heat 1 tbsp oil over medium heat. Toss in minced garlic, grated ginger, and white parts of green onions. Sizzle for 40-50 seconds until fragrant. Watch garlic closely to avoid scorch—if it browns, discard and start fresh."
- "=== Pork ==="
- "Turn heat to medium-high. Add ground pork, season with half salt and black pepper. Break apart with wooden spoon, push meat around so it browns evenly. No stirring constantly. Let brown crust form on some pieces—that’s flavor, don’t rush. Cook 8-9 minutes until no pink remains. Remove pork to plate."
- "=== Veggies ==="
- "Add mushrooms first to the same pan, cook 2 minutes until moisture reduces. Then add broccoli. Season with remaining salt. Stir-fry without oil first to get charred edges and vibrant green color, about 3 minutes. Drizzle remaining oil now, toss to coat. Cook another 2-3 minutes until crisp-tender but not mushy. Test broccoli with fork; should be firm with slight bite."
- "=== Combine ==="
- "Return pork to pan. Pour sauce over meat and vegetables. Lower heat to medium. Stir continuously until sauce bubbles and thickens, about 3-4 minutes. Sauce should be shiny, cling to food. If sauce too thin, simmer longer; too thick, stir in splash of water."
- "=== Serve ==="
- "Splash over steamed rice or ramen noodles. Garnish with green onion tops and toasted sesame seeds if on hand. Eat immediately while hot, sauce and meat still juicy."
