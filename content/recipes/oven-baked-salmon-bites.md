ingredients:
- "1 1/2 pounds skinless salmon fillets, patted dry"
- "3/4 cup all-purpose flour"
- "2 large eggs, whisked"
- "1 1/2 cups panko breadcrumbs"
- "1 tbsp olive oil"
- "1 tsp garlic powder"
- "1 tsp onion powder"
- "3/4 tsp ground mustard (replace paprika)"
- "3/4 tsp salt, divided"
- "3/4 tsp ground black pepper, divided"
- "Green onions, sliced for garnish"
- "1 tsp sesame seeds for garnish"
- "FOR SAUCE"
- "1/2 cup mayo"
- "1/4 cup Greek yogurt (swap sour cream)"
- "3 tbsp sweet chili sauce"
- "1 tbsp Sriracha"
- "1 tsp lime juice"
- "1/2 tsp garlic powder"
instructions:
- "=== Bang bang sauce first"
- "Mix mayo, Greek yogurt, sweet chili sauce, Sriracha, lime juice, garlic powder in small bowl. Cover and chill — flavors meld better old school."
- "=== Prep salmon"
- "Dry fillets thoroughly with paper towels. Moisture is enemy number one for crisp coating."
- "Cube salmon into roughly 1-inch pieces. Don’t fuss perfect — rustic bites support crunchy crust and flaky center."
- "Season with half the salt and pepper. Set aside."
- "=== Toast breadcrumbs"
- "Heat olive oil in nonstick pan over medium-high heat. Add panko — hear that sizzle start? Keep tossing gently every 30 seconds, watch color closely — golden edges, not burnt. About 4 minutes."
- "Immediately transfer to wide shallow bowl."
- "=== Dredging station setup"
- "One bowl flour, one bowl whisked eggs, breadcrumb bowl now has toasted panko plus garlic powder, onion powder, ground mustard, rest salt and pepper—all tossed together thoroughly."
- "=== Coat salmon"
- "Use left hand dry (flour), right hand wet (egg) tip: avoids clumps and gloopy fingers."
- "Flour each piece lightly, shake off excess. Dip in egg, then coat evenly in seasoned panko. Lay bites spaced on parchment-lined pan."
- "Same goes for all remaining salmon — keep clean rhythm, avoid clumping."
- "=== Bake"
- "Nestle pan in 395°F oven (small dip to avoid overcooking). About 12–17 minutes. Look for opaque flesh, firm to touch yet give just at center."
- "Internal temp hits about 140°F – salmon carries heat gently past done."
- "=== Serve"
- "Sprinkle seafood bites with sesame and green onions right away — aroma wakes senses."
- "Dip in chilled bang bang sauce; piquant cream cuts richness."
- "Eat immediately while breadcrumb crunch is at its peak."
