ingredients:
- "8 ounces rigatoni pasta or penne pasta"
- "1 1/4 cups marinara sauce, preferably chunky"
- "3/4 cup crushed fire-roasted tomatoes, canned"
- "3 tablespoons grated Parmesan cheese, freshly grated preferred"
- "2 large chicken breasts, cooked and cut into 1-inch cubes"
- "1 1/2 cups shredded part-skim mozzarella cheese"
- "Nonstick cooking spray"
- "1 teaspoon dried oregano, optional twist"
- "Fresh basil leaves for garnish, optional"
instructions:
- "===Cleaning and prepping==="
- "Salt boiling water generously. Keep a rolling boil; this firms pasta better. Cook rigatoni 2 minutes less than package directs to keep al dente under bake heat. Drain well. Do not rinse; starch helps sauce cling."
- "===Sauce and pasta assembly==="
- "Preheat oven to 355 degrees Fahrenheit for a more consistent heat—ambient ovens vary. Spray a 9x13 baking dish with nonstick spray. Toss drained pasta with marinara and crushed tomatoes plus dried oregano. Stir until coated; chunky texture adds welcome contrast."
- "===Layering==="
- "Pour mixed pasta and sauce into dish, distribute evenly. Sprinkle Parmesan cheese over surface—fine grating works best to melt seamlessly. Scatter pre-cooked chicken pieces uniformly. Cut breasts formerly grilled or pan-seared; leftover rotisserie is an efficient swap."
- "Top with mozzarella cheese in even layer. Freshly shredded melts better than pre-shredded with anti-caking agents—those inhibit melt and cause clumps."
- "===Baking==="
- "Place in oven, uncovered. Expect 25 to 35 minutes bake time. Look for bubbling sauce at edges and cheese turning a light golden brown with bubbling spots. Firm texture of chicken also signals doneness; poke with fork to check warmth."
- "Rest five minutes out of oven. Allows cheese to set and aromas to concentrate. Garnish basil leaves if desired for color and fresh aroma."
