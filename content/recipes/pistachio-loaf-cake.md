ingredients:
- "2 cups shelled pistachios"
- "1 box (15 ounces) yellow cake mix"
- "1 box (3 ounces) instant pistachio pudding mix"
- "3 large eggs"
- "1/3 cup vegetable oil"
- "1 cup sour cream"
- "1 teaspoon almond extract"
- "3/4 cup chopped pistachios, divided"
- "2 loaf pans 9 by 5 inches, greased with vegetable shortening"
- "2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted"
- "2 cups powdered sugar"
- "2 tablespoons milk"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1/8 teaspoon fine sea salt"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 350F. Grease two 9x5 inch loaf pans well with vegetable shortening. Set pans aside to let fat firm up on surface."
- "Fill a small pot halfway with water. Bring to steady boil, not a rolling frenzy. Throw in the pistachios for 3 to 5 minutes—watch skins blister and loosen but avoid overboiling or nuts get soggy."
- "Drain immediately. Dump nuts onto a thick kitchen towel. Wrap and roll firmly to rub off those papery skins. Leaves cleaner crunch in final loaf. If stubborn bits remain, pluck manually. Skin-on bites taste bitter, no good."
- "In a mixing bowl or stand mixer with paddle attachment, add the yellow cake mix and pistachio pudding mix. Crack in the eggs next. Pour in vegetable oil and the sour cream instead of Greek yogurt—sour cream adds creaminess and slight tang but suits better here."
- "Add almond extract in place of vanilla to enhance nutty tones. Blend on medium speed until batter thickens, resembling banana bread batter—thick, not liquid. Help avoid flat loaves from thin batters."
- "Fold 1/2 cup of chopped pistachios in gently to distribute nuts evenly but keep some crunch intact. Avoid overmixing which develops gluten and toughens bread."
- "Divide batter evenly between pans. Smooth tops with spatula for even rise. Batter should be dense but spreadable."
- "Bake for 48 to 53 minutes. Do not rely solely on time—watch for golden cracks forming atop and test with toothpick inserted near center. It should come out mostly clean with a few moist crumbs, no wet batter present."
- "Remove loaves from oven and cool in pans on wire racks 15 minutes before turning out. Complete cooling before glazing is crucial; warm loaves melt glaze and create mess."
- "For glaze, whisk melted butter, powdered sugar, milk, vanilla extract, and salt into a glossy drizzle consistency. Let rest a couple minutes to thicken slightly—should hold shape but pour smoothly."
- "Drizzle glaze over fully cooled loaves in lines or circles. Sprinkle remaining chopped pistachios promptly before glaze sets to ensure nuts stick."
- "Cool glaze fully before slicing. Store wrapped airtight to maintain moist crumb. If pistachios turn soft, refresh by toasting lightly just before serving."
