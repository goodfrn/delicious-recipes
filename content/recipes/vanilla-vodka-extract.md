ingredients:
- "2 vanilla pods"
- "1 1/4 cups vodka"
- "1/2 teaspoon vanilla powder"
instructions:
- "=== Bean preparation ==="
- "Slice vanilla pods lengthwise with a sharp knife. Open the bean gently, exposing the black seeds inside without cutting fully in half; tearing ruins the structure and slows extraction."
- "=== Jar and liquid ==="
- "Place split pods into a jar. Use one sized for about 2 cups liquid so you have room to shake without spillage."
- "Pour vodka just over beans, about 1 1/4 cup. Seeds soak quicker if fully submerged. Top off with a pinch of vanilla powder for extra punch—adds texture and amplifies aroma."
- "=== Storage and timing ==="
- "Cap tightly. Store jar in cool, dry, dark spot. Avoid sunlight, which can degrade alcohol and flavor compounds."
- "Minimum steep 7 weeks. 8-10 weeks optimal for rich depth. Up to 12 months safe, intensity continues to evolve but won’t improve much past 10 months."
- "Every 5–7 days, shake vigorously to redistribute oils. Seeds sink; shaking wakes dormant flavors up."
- "=== Usage and finishing ==="
- "Strain through fine mesh or cheesecloth before use to remove bean husks and any powder grit."
- "Transfer to smaller bottle for daily use or leave in jar for slow oxidation and flavor maturation."
- "=== Troubleshooting and tips ==="
- "If extract tastes weak after full steep, ensure pods were split properly; unopened pods release next to nothing."
- "Bourbon or spiced rum can replace vodka for deeper notes—expect color shift and different aroma."
- "Vanilla powder is optional but useful if pods are old or flavor lacks intensity."
- "Avoid plastic containers; alcohol leaches chemicals and dulls flavor."
- "If mold forms, discard immediately—sign of moisture ingress or poor seal."
- "Keep spare vanilla pods on hand. Freshness radically affects outcome."
- "Patience pays; flavor is faint early but grows as alcohol pulls out essential oils."
- "Homemade extract strong enough for baking or cocktails, cheaper than store-bought, and personalized."
