ingredients:
- "1 pound rigatoni pasta"
- "1 tablespoon olive oil cooking spray"
- "1 tablespoon olive oil"
- "8 ounces ground beef"
- "8 ounces ground spicy Italian sausage"
- "1 medium yellow onion chopped"
- "3 cloves garlic minced"
- "1 teaspoon dried basil"
- "1 teaspoon dried oregano"
- "Salt to taste"
- "Fresh cracked black pepper to taste"
- "1 cup fire-roasted crushed tomatoes"
- "1 cup chipotle diced tomatoes drained"
- "1 cup plain marinara sauce"
- "3 cups shredded whole milk mozzarella cheese"
- "Fresh basil leaves chopped for garnish"
instructions:
- "===Prepping Rigatoni==="
- "Boil salted water vigorously for rigatoni. Pasta needs a firm bite because it’ll cook again in sauce in oven. 1-2 minutes shy of al dente. Drain well. No rinsing — starch keeps sauce clinging. Set aside in same pot."
- "===Making Meat Sauce==="
- "Heat olive oil in medium skillet over medium-high heat. Throw in ground beef and spicy Italian sausage. Let brown undisturbed 3-4 minutes until edges crisp up. Toss in onions, garlic, dried basil, oregano, salt, and plenty of cracked pepper. Cook until onions soften and meat is fully cooked, about 4 more minutes. Drain fat thoroughly — excess grease dulls sauce."
- "===Building Sauce==="
- "Mix browned meat with crushed tomatoes, chipotle diced tomatoes (dry well from juices), and marinara in a large bowl. Stir signals fully uniform sauce. The chipotle adds smokiness instead of sweetness. Adjust salt at this stage — taste is key here."
- "===Combining Pasta and Sauce==="
- "Pour entire sauce into rigatoni pot. Gently fold with wooden spoon to coat every crevice. The light saucing ensures no clumpy pasta, easier to bake evenly. Don’t over-stir or pasta mushes."
- "===Baking==="
- "Spray 9x13 inch baking dish with olive oil spray—prevents sticking without greasy buildup. Dump pasta and sauce in. Spread evenly, not sloppily. Layer whole milk shredded mozzarella evenly on top. Use a flat spatula to lightly press cheese into pasta surface. Oven at 380°F—slightly warmer than usual to encourage cheese browning in the middle without overcooking pasta. Bake about 25 minutes. Cheese should bubble vigorously and develop golden brown patches. Listen for soft crackle under cheese as it browns."
- "Optionally broil the top 2-3 minutes for crispier cheese crust. Watch carefully—cheese goes from perfect to burnt in seconds. Remove when cheese is blistered and pasta smells thick and hearty."
- "===Finishing==="
- "Rest 5 minutes before serving to let juices settle. Garnish generously with chopped fresh basil. Parsley is okay but basil gives aroma punch and brightness that cuts the fat. Serve straight from dish—plates just complicate this dish."
- "===Tips & Alternatives==="
- "Ground turkey or plant-based sausage are acceptable swaps but brown properly or moisture drains out. Whole milk mozzarella provides creaminess; you can use part-skim but results tighten up. Swap fire-roasted tomatoes for plain crushed if unavailable but lose smoky layer. You can replace chipotle with smoked paprika if chipotle’s too much heat."
- "Don’t skip draining excess water from canned tomatoes—too much moisture creates watery bake. Pasta quality matters. Cheap rigatoni turns mealy. Pick durable brands. Too much cheese? Holds moisture but can slide off when cutting."
- "If in a rush, use jarred sauce only but add sautéed meat and fresh garlic for depth. Don’t overcrowd skillet browning meat. Brown in batches if needed to get Maillard reaction—this is flavor foundation."
