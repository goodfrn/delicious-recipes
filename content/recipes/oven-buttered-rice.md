ingredients:
- "1 1/4 cup long grain white rice (avoid instant varieties)"
- "1 packet dry onion soup mix (or 1 tbsp garlic powder plus 1 tsp dried thyme)"
- "2 1/2 cups low sodium beef broth (or chicken broth for lighter flavor)"
- "1/2 cup unsalted butter, sliced thin into small pats"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 430F. Use a deep 2 quart baking dish; grease well with cooking spray to prevent sticking and ensure even heat circulation."
- "Combine rice, dry soup mix, and broth directly in the baking dish. Stir briefly to distribute seasoning but don’t overmix to avoid breaking grains."
- "Arrange butter slices evenly over the rice surface, allow them to float atop without pressing down; this encourages slow fat infusion during baking."
- "Cover dish tightly with foil or lid to trap steam. Place in oven and bake undisturbed for approximately 35 minutes. Look for bubbling broth edges and gentler steam release."
- "After initial baking, remove cover carefully to avoid steam burns. Continue baking uncovered for another 25-30 minutes. Surface should dry slightly, grains become tender yet firm."
- "Remove from oven. Fluff rice gently with fork; separate grains and avoid mashing. Butter should be mostly absorbed, leaving a glossy finish."
- "Serve immediately. Leftovers reheat well with a sprinkle of broth or butter to regain moistness."
