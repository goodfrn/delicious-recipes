ingredients:
- "4 boneless skinless chicken breasts about 3/4 inch thick"
- "16 ounces fresh green beans trimmed"
- "2 medium zucchini sliced into thin rounds"
- "Salt"
- "Black pepper"
- "Garlic powder"
- "6 tablespoons BBQ sauce divided"
- "2 tablespoons olive oil instead of butter"
- "Heavy duty aluminum foil or nonstick foil"
- "Nonstick cooking spray if using regular foil"
instructions:
- "Heat grill to medium high around 375 to 405 F. Look for humming heat and grill grates glowing faintly red, too hot means flare ups, too low means dry chicken."
- "Cut aluminum foil into four large squares. Spray or use nonstick foil to avoid sticking. Place one chicken breast center of each piece."
- "Divide green beans and zucchini evenly among chicken breasts. Scatter veggies on and around chicken, not piled thick."
- "Season each packet with salt, pepper, garlic powder. Sprinkle generously but don’t overdo garlic it can dominate."
- "Drizzle about 1 1/2 tablespoons BBQ sauce on each chicken portion. Top each with half tablespoon olive oil instead of butter for lighter finish."
- "Fold the longer foil edges over repeatedly then fold edges inward tightly to seal packets fully. Avoid gaps to trap steam but don’t crush chicken flat."
- "Place packets on grill over direct heat. Listen for steady sizzling, no heavy flare ups. Cook 12 minutes before flipping."
- "Flip carefully using tongs. Cook another 9 to 11 minutes depending on thickness. Chicken should firm up and juices run clear when pierced."
- "Use your finger or tongs to apply light pressure to packets. If contents feel springy but not mushy, near done."
- "Remove packets from grill and let rest 5 to 7 minutes before opening. Opening too soon blasts hot steam, risky."
- "Unfold foil edges carefully away from face. Brush chicken with more BBQ sauce if you like extra stick and gloss."
- "If you don’t have green beans, snap fresh broccoli florets. For butter swap, grapeseed or avocado oil work well. Add sliced red onion or cherry tomatoes for brightness."
- "Avoid opening packets early, keeps moisture locked in. Too long on grill and chicken dries. Thickness adjustment critical - measure, weigh if you can. Thinner cooks fast, thicker needs time but risk charring edges."
