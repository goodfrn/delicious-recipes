ingredients:
- "2 cans refrigerated crescent roll dough"
- "1-1 1/2 teaspoons cream cheese per roll"
- "Generous pinch shredded pepper jack cheese instead of cheddar"
- "Fresh jalapeño slices or pickled banana peppers to taste"
- "1 large egg beaten with 1 tablespoon half-and-half (for egg wash)"
- "Shortening or baking spray for sheet"
- "Optional: smoked paprika sprinkled inside for smoky twist"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 370 degrees F. Lightly grease or line sheet with parchment to prevent sticking. No pooling spray — coats thinly to avoid slipping."
- "Unroll first crescent sheet on clean, cool surface. Spread about 1 teaspoon cream cheese on the broad end of each triangle — too much makes dough soggy inside."
- "Add a sprinkle of shredded pepper jack cheese for a bit of sharpness with melty texture. Drop sliced fresh jalapeño or pickled banana peppers evenly over cheese. Smoking paprika optional for earthy note."
- "Roll dough tightly from wide end down to narrow point. Make sure edges are sealed firmly to avoid leaks."
- "Place roll-ups seam side down spaced evenly on the tray. Work quickly so dough doesn’t get too warm and sticky."
- "Whisk egg with half-and-half; brush a thin coat over tops. This ensures that signature golden color and slight crispness. Too thick and it can pool and mask flakes."
- "Into oven for roughly 16-20 minutes. Watch color — deep golden brown edges tell you crust is crisp, and dough has baked through. Bubbling cheese around edges is a good sign."
- "Remove and cool 3-5 minutes. Crisp sets better once resting but still warm enough to melt filling inside."
- "Serve warm. If spicy is too much, try pairing with a cooling ranch or sour cream dip."
- "Store leftovers wrapped in foil or airtight container. Reheat briefly in oven or toaster oven to keep crust crisp."
