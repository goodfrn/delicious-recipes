ingredients:
- "1 cup unsalted butter softened"
- "1 ½ cups packed dark brown sugar"
- "2 large eggs whole"
- "2 ⅓ cups all-purpose flour sifted"
- "1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract"
- "15 sandwich cookies roughly chopped"
- "½ cup cream cheese softened"
- "½ cup peanut butter chips"
- "sprinkles optional for topping"
instructions:
- "=== Preparing the pan ==="
- "Set oven to 310°F. Use a 13×9 inch light colored metal pan; prevents overbrowning. Spray evenly with nonstick oil. Avoid foil, sticks and leaks moisture."
- "=== Mixing the base ==="
- "In large bowl, blend butter, cream cheese, and dark brown sugar until grainy but combined—don’t overbeat. Adds moisture, tang. Add eggs one at a time. Stir until fully integrated, no streaks."
- "=== Dry to wet ==="
- "Sift flour separately to break lumps. Add to wet mixture gradually, folding carefully to avoid toughing dough. Mix until lumps gone but no overmixing; will make bars dense and dry."
- "Incorporate vanilla extract last to prevent evaporation during baking."
- "=== Add-ins and fold ==="
- "Fold in 2/3 of chopped sandwich cookies and peanut butter chips last to keep chunks intact. Prevents crumbs from coating batter too much."
- "=== Transfer and topping ==="
- "Spread batter evenly in pan. Use offset spatula for smooth top, no air pockets. Sprinkle remaining cookies and optional sprinkles in loose even layer to add crunch and festive vibe."
- "=== Baking ==="
- "Place in oven center rack. Bake 45-55 minutes. Watch edges pull away cleanly from pan; surface firm but center slightly soft to touch—springy gives chewiness. Color shifts to light golden brown, not dark."
- "Don’t rely solely on timer. Insert toothpick near edges, expect moist crumbs not raw batter. Avoid overbaking or bars dry out."
- "=== Cooling and storage ==="
- "Let cool completely in pan on wire rack before cutting. Bars firm up as they cool; warm bars crumble easily."
- "Store airtight at room temp up to 3 days, refrigerate up to a week. Bring to room temp before serving for best texture."
- "If separated or greasy, chill briefly then remix gently."
