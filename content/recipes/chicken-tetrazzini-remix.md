ingredients:
- "3 large chicken breasts"
- "1 teaspoon kosher salt divided"
- "1/2 teaspoon black pepper"
- "1/2 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "1 teaspoon smoked paprika"
- "4 tablespoons olive oil divided"
- "1 medium yellow onion sliced"
- "8 ounces cremini mushrooms sliced"
- "1 cup frozen peas"
- "3 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "3 tablespoons all-purpose flour"
- "1 cup heavy cream"
- "1/2 cup half-and-half"
- "2 cups whole milk warmed"
- "1 1/2 cups freshly grated parmesan cheese divided"
- "12 ounces egg noodles cooked al dente and drained"
- "3/4 cup breadcrumbs preferably panko"
- "1 tablespoon finely chopped fresh parsley for garnish"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 380°F. The extra 5 degrees helps crisp breadcrumbs faster without drying sauce too much."
- "Mix half the salt, pepper, garlic powder, and smoked paprika in bowl. Pat chicken dry then rub evenly on all sides. This spice swap adds subtle smokiness without overpowering."
- "Heat 2 tablespoons olive oil in large oven-safe skillet over medium-high. Oil should shimmer but not smoke."
- "Add chicken carefully. Sear 4 minutes per side. Flip when golden crust forms. Don’t crowd pan; cook in batches if needed. Use instant thermometer: 165°F internal signals just done. Remove and let rest 10 minutes. Shred using forks after cooled; shredding while warm makes fine strands, cooling firms meat for easier pulling."
- "Lower heat to medium-low. Add remaining 2 tablespoons oil if pan dry. Toss in onions, stir often till softened, about 3 minutes. They should turn translucent and slightly sweet, not browned."
- "Add mushrooms; cook until they release moisture and shrink, about 4 minutes longer. Watch closely to avoid soggy mush; goal is caramelized savory notes."
- "Stir in peas, remaining salt; cook 2 minutes until peas are tender but still bright green. Remove veggies to bowl."
- "Turn heat to low. Melt butter fully until it foams gently but does not brown. Sprinkle in flour. Stir constantly for 2 minutes forming a light blonde roux; this cooks raw flour taste out and thickens sauce."
- "Slowly add heavy cream while stirring. Follow with half-and-half. Pour warm milk gradually; sauce thickens visibly, should coat back of spoon with no lumps."
- "Mix in 1 cup parmesan cheese till just melted. Cheese creates umami depth and thickens sauce further but adding too hot milk or cheese too fast can cause clumping."
- "Return noodles, shredded chicken, and veggies to skillet. Fold gently so everything gets coated evenly in sauce. Avoid smashing noodles or chicken clumps for texture contrast."
- "Sprinkle remaining parmesan evenly over top, then breadcrumbs. This layering creates crispy crust in oven."
- "Bake uncovered around 22 minutes. Look for bubbling edges and golden toasted crumbs. Let rest 5 minutes before serving to thicken slightly and set portions."
- "Scatter fresh parsley just before plating for color and subtle herb freshness."

Smoked paprika swapped in for a little warmth that garlic powder alone can’t bring — totally optional and swap with cayenne if you want heat. Half-and-half cuts some heaviness of pure cream and stretches sauce without watering it down. Fresh mushrooms give cleaner flavor but button mushrooms will work. Panko breadcrumbs for crunch; if using regular breadcrumbs, toss with a little olive oil before baking to avoid drying out. Parmesan is best freshly grated—pre-grated can have anti-caking agents that ruin melting behavior. If you don’t have fresh, increase grated amount by 25% for similar texture."
instructionsNote: "Heat control is everything here — start with hot oil for a good sear but drop heat lower for vegetables to sweat not burn. Onions are your timing cue, translucent means ready. No browning or you shift flavor profile and risk bitter notes. Watch mushrooms closely; they’ll go from firm to watery in moments. Don’t rush the roux; flour needs to cook a couple minutes or sauce ends up floury; it should be nutty blonde.
