ingredients:
- "8 ounces ricotta cheese"
- "1 cup sour cream"
- "1 teaspoon dried onion flakes"
- "6 slices cooked bacon chopped"
- "1/2 cup grated aged cheddar cheese"
- "1/2 cup shredded mozzarella cheese"
- "1 1/4 cups fresh or thawed frozen corn kernels"
- "1 teaspoon smoked paprika"
- "Salt and black pepper to taste"
- "Assorted chips and crackers for serving"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 355 degrees Fahrenheit. Grease an 8-inch cast iron skillet or similarly sized oven-safe dish with butter or neutral oil to prevent sticking and help browning."
- "In a large bowl, combine ricotta, sour cream, dried onion flakes, and smoked paprika. Stir thoroughly to even out flavors and smooth out ricotta texture. Season lightly with salt and pepper but hold back slightly as cheeses add saltiness."
- "Fold in chopped bacon, cheddar, mozzarella, and fresh corn kernels. Mix until ingredients are well-distributed but avoid overmixing to keep some texture variety. Corn bites pop as they roast."
- "Scrape mixture evenly into prepared skillet, pressing lightly. Spread out to edges for uniform cooking. Should fill skillet but not mound high to avoid uneven baking."
- "Slide skillet into oven. Listen for gentle bubbling sounds from edges showing heat penetration. After about 18 minutes, check top—should be starting to bubble with edges golden but not burnt."
- "If bubbling strong but cheese still pale, broil for 2-3 minutes keeping a close eye—aim for golden patches and slight crisp on top for texture contrast."
- "Remove from oven when edges bubble actively with golden brown spots on surface, internal mixture creamy but set. Let sit 5 minutes before serving to avoid lava hot spots and let flavors settle."
- "Serve warm alongside chips or sturdy crackers. The key is contrasting softness with crunch. Reheat gently covered to keep texture intact for leftovers."
