- "4 large eggs"
- "1 tablespoon unsalted butter"
- "1 teaspoon olive oil"
- "1 tablespoon mayonnaise (optional substitution for cream)"
- "Salt to taste"
- "Smoked paprika for garnish"
instructions:
- "===Preparation==="
- "Break eggs into a medium bowl that leaves room to whisk without spilling. Break yolks fully. Use a fork or small whisk to beat vigorously, incorporating air for fluffiness. Avoid overbeating or eggs become tough. Optional: add mayonnaise now for richness and slight tang."
- "===Cooking==="
- "Heat butter and olive oil in a non-stick or well-seasoned cast iron pan over low heat. Butter should bubble gently, not brown. Test warmth by hovering hand a few inches above pan; warmth signals readiness."
- "Pour eggs gently into the pan, spreading evenly. Wait. Edges will start turning opaque and barely set. This is not scrambling yet, just setting the curds' foundation."
- "Using a wooden spoon or flexible spatula, start pulling edges inward toward center. No flipping or forceful stirring. Gentle folding pulls curds together. Lift pan slightly to let uncooked egg flow underneath. Keep pulling softly until most liquid is gone but eggs remain moist and fluffy. Watch texture constantly."
- "Transfer eggs from pan immediately to warm plate—carryover heat will finish cooking. Overcooking is the most common mistake and makes eggs dry."
- "Season only now with salt; adding earlier breaks down curds. Sprinkle smoked paprika if desired."
- "===Serving==="
- "Serve hot, fresh, and curdy. Toast or sautéed greens on side."
- "===Cleanup tip==="
- "Soak pan if eggs stick; low heat and fat prevent this. Avoid high heat transports eggs from creamy to rubbery in seconds."
