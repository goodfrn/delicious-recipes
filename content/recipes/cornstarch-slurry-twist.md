ingredients:
- "1 tablespoon potato starch or arrowroot powder"
- "3 tablespoons cold vegetable broth or water"
- "1 teaspoon light soy sauce (optional)"
instructions:
- "===Mixing Slurry==="
- "Combine starch with cold liquid, adding soy sauce last. Whisk vigorously until lump-free slurry forms. Using broth adds flavor dimension—better than plain water."
- "===Adding to Hot Liquid==="
- "Bring sauce or broth to gentle simmer. Gradually pour slurry in while whisking or stirring briskly. Listen for soft bubbling, watch as liquid thickens and gains sheen. Stop adding when sauce coats spoon, clings but doesn’t gel hard."
- "===Adjusting Thickness==="
- "Wait a few minutes after initial addition—it thickens as it cooks, not instantly. If too thin, add remaining slurry in small doses, stirring well each time. Too thick? Thin with small amounts of broth or water while stirring."
