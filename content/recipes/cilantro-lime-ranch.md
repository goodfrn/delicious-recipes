ingredients:
- "1 cup Greek yogurt (substitute for sour cream to cut richness without losing creaminess)"
- "1/2 cup mayonnaise"
- "1/3 cup avocado oil (swap from vegetable oil to add subtle buttery notes)"
- "1/4 cup fresh lime juice"
- "1 cup fresh cilantro, roughly chopped"
- "1/4 cup fresh parsley, roughly chopped (for brighter herbal depth)"
- "2 cloves garlic, smashed"
- "1 teaspoon smoked paprika (adds mild smoky warmth, unexpected twist)"
- "1 teaspoon onion powder"
- "1 teaspoon sea salt"
- "1/2 teaspoon black pepper"
- "1 tablespoon white vinegar"
- "1 teaspoon honey (balances acidity)"
instructions:
- "Blend garlic with chopped cilantro and parsley in a food processor. Notice how aromas hit first—the garlic sharp, herbs fresh and bright. Blur until finely minced but still vibrant green specks remain."
- "Slowly stream in avocado oil while pulsing. Watch the emulsion start to form, glistening and slightly thick."
- "Add Greek yogurt, mayonnaise, lime juice, vinegar, honey, paprika, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Pulse just until fully combined. Avoid overmixing; too much breaks the creamy consistency, making it runny."
- "Taste. Lift salt and lime as needed. Must be bright but balanced, with a whisper of smoke from paprika."
- "Transfer to a container. Cover tightly. Refrigerate at least 25–30 minutes to thicken properly. You want that chill to dull the sharp raw edges and coax thickening."
- "Before serving, whisk briskly. Texture should coat the back of a spoon, creamy and luxurious—not watery."
- "Use as a dip, drizzle, or salad dressing. Holds firmness better than sour cream base, great for grilling weather."
