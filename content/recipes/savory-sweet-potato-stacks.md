ingredients:
- "3 large sweet potatoes peeled and sliced into 1/8 inch discs"
- "3 tablespoons avocado oil"
- "2 teaspoons fresh thyme leaves, chopped"
- "1 teaspoon smoked paprika"
- "1 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "3/4 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "1/2 teaspoon cracked black pepper"
- "1 cup grated pecorino romano cheese"
- "For Maple Cinnamon Butter Topping (instead of honey butter):"
- "5 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "1 tablespoon pure maple syrup"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon"
instructions:
- "=== Sweet Potato Stack Preparation ==="
- "Spread peeled potatoes for uniform slicing. Use a mandolin or sharp chef's knife for 1/8 inch thick slices. Aim for even thickness to ensure stacks cook evenly; uneven slices will cause inconsistent textures."
- "Whisk together avocado oil, thyme, smoked paprika, garlic powder, salt, and cracked black pepper in a medium bowl. Watch for the oil's green tint and aroma — avocado oil holds up better under heat than olive oil, less bitterness risk."
- "Toss potato slices gently in the seasoning oil, get every slice coated but not dripping. Excess oil pools cause sogginess, dryness if too little."
- "Line a sheet pan with parchment paper instead of foil for better crisping. Use wider bases to support stacks 4-5 layers high. Press lightly as you layer pecorino between slices to bind layers — cheese melts into the pores of potato for glue effect."
- "=== Baking and Doneness ==="
- "Preheat oven to 405°F. Place tray right in the middle rack. The sound of gentle crackling edges indicates crisp start. Bake for about 35-40 minutes but start testing at 30 minutes."
- "Use a thin skewer or toothpick to pierce the center stack. No resistance means tender inside. Edges should be golden brown and firm to touch, not floppy or wet. If tops brown too fast, shield loosely with foil for last 10 minutes to prevent burning."
- "=== Maple Cinnamon Butter Sauce ==="
- "Melt butter in a small microwave safe bowl (about 25 seconds) until fully liquefied but not browned. Whisk in maple syrup and cinnamon. Heat releases spices’ oils; don’t skip this step to avoid gritty texture."
- "Drizzle over warm stacks immediately post-oven. Butter pools around base add luscious shine and cinnamon aroma floating in the kitchen."
- "Allow resting 5 minutes before serving. Stacks firm up and flavors meld. Cuts hold shape better; too hot they crumble."
- "Optional garnishes: fresh thyme sprigs or a light dusting of extra pecorino for contrast."
