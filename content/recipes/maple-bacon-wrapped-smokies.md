ingredients:
- "9 cups smoked cocktail sausages (little smokies) or 2 packages, approx 40 sausages"
- "18 slices thick-cut bacon, or center-cut bacon as leaner option"
- "1 cup dark brown sugar mixed with 2 tsp ground cinnamon (swap cinnamon with 1 tsp smoked paprika or 1 tsp chili powder for heat twist)"
- "1 cup pure maple syrup (replace half with honey for subtler sweetness)"
- "Nonstick cooking spray"
instructions:
- "==="
- "Prep a 9x13 inch baking dish. Lightly mist with nonstick spray. Avoid heavy greasing to prevent soggy bacon edges."
- "Wrap each little smokie snugly with bacon slice. Secure edges folded under sausage so bacon shrinks evenly without peeling off during bake."
- "Arrange wrapped sausages close but not overcrowded in pan for even heat circulation."
- "Mix ¾ cup brown sugar concentration with cinnamon (or chosen spice). Drizzle or sprinkle over sausages to coat lightly but evenly. This step starts the flavor absorption; sugar must touch bacon surface directly, no clumps. If sugar clumps, break apart with fingers or fork."
- "Cover pan tightly with foil, refrigerate minimum 45 minutes, maximum 1 hour. Marinate allows sugar to dissolve partially while bacon firms and derivatives meld. No longer or bacon gets gummy."
- "Countdown timer as fridge chills. Just before baking, preheat oven to 380°F. Slightly lower than typical 375°F to set sugar gradually without burning at first go."
- "Before oven, sprinkle remaining ¼ cup maple syrup over top for more syrupy gloss and sticky finish distinct from sugar crust."
- "Slide baking dish center rack into oven. After 27 minutes watch for bacon edges crisping, sugar evenly melting into dark caramel bubbles. Avoid premature charring on sugar—adjust rack down one if needed."
- "Boost oven to 410°F for last 12 minutes. This final blast crisps up bacon fat, accentuates crunch, melts remaining sugars to lightly crackle top."
- "On removal, don’t rush. Let rest uncovered 7 to 12 minutes at room temp. Cooling sets sugar crust, stabilizes fat rendering, makes pieces easier to pick up without sugar strands tearing."
- "Serve warm or just lukewarm, these hold shape, texture, and mouthfeel even if made ahead and reheated gently in 325°F oven for 10 minutes."
- "If smoke flavor lacking, consider adding 1 tsp smoked salt to brown sugar mix, or briefly crisp bacon first in skillet for a minute before wrapping sausages for an intense pork aroma punch."
- "Avoid freezing assembled product; bacon fat changes texture unpleasantly. Cooked leftovers freeze well, reheat slowly."
