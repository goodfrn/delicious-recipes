ingredients:
- "12 large hard-boiled eggs, halved lengthwise or widthwise"
- "Yolks removed and placed in bowl"
- "1/2 cup avocado oil mayonnaise or Greek yogurt blend"
- "2 teaspoons apple cider vinegar"
- "2 teaspoons spicy brown mustard"
- "1/4 teaspoon Kosher salt"
- "1/4 teaspoon ground white pepper"
- "1/4 teaspoon celery salt"
- "1/4 teaspoon ground turmeric"
- "Gel food coloring: beet red and turmeric yellow"
- "Cold water for dye baths, 1 to 1 1/2 cups per color"
- "Chopped fresh chives for garnish"
- "Smoked sea salt flakes"
instructions:
- "Halve eggs carefully either way, length or width—whatever gives clean halves. Scoop yolks out with small spoon or your fingers if you like gentle pressure. Avoid squeezing whites. Transfer yolks to a bowl."
- "Mash yolks very fine. Use fork, potato masher, or even ricer if you want ultra smooth. No clumps. Texture here impacts final mouthfeel. Stop once almost paste-like with no chunks."
- "Bring in stand mixer or hand mixer at low speed for better aeration. Slowly add mayo/yogurt mix, a few tablespoons at a time. Whip briefly between additions. This folds air in, lightens texture; skip if you want dense."
- "Add vinegar, mustard, salt, pepper, celery salt, and turmeric. Stir or pulse quickly until fully incorporated. Turmeric gives color and earthy flavor; celery salt adds subtle vegetal note, no harshness. Taste, adjust if needed."
- "Prepare dye baths separately: 2-3 drops of beet and turmeric gel color in cold water, stir until no lumps. Place whites gently into the baths, not overcrowding. Watch color deepen, about 9-14 minutes. More time means deeper tone; remove early for pastel. Tough to undo, so better to check often."
- "Drain dyed whites on paper towels thoroughly. Avoid residual water pooling; will thin out filling."
- "Fill a piping bag or resealable plastic bag with yolk mixture. Snip tip for control or use spoon if you’re rougher. Pipe or dollop mixture into whites. Don’t overload or filling spills out at first bite."
- "Sprinkle smoked sea salt flakes and finely diced chives on top for texture and fresh onion aroma. Skip paprika—that was swapped out for turmeric and celery salt inside the filling."
- "Refrigerate covered until serving. Filling firms and flavors meld. Don’t store more than 48 hours; egg whites get tougher, filling can dry out."
- "If yolks seem dry, add a teaspoon more mayo or yogurt to loosen. Overmixing can cause greasiness; stop once combined but still fluffy."
- "For milk allergy substitute Greek yogurt with sour cream or an additional 1/4 cup mayo. If avoiding eggshell staining, wear gloves when handling dyed whites."
