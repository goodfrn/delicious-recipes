ingredients:
- "8 ounces softened cream cheese at room temp"
- "½ cup sour cream"
- "1 teaspoon ground cumin (instead of chili powder for earthier flavor)"
- "½ teaspoon smoked paprika (switch for chili powder)"
- "½ teaspoon garlic powder (use fresh minced garlic for more punch)"
- "¼ teaspoon black pepper"
- "3 green onions sliced thin"
- "1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese"
- "2 cups cooked shredded chicken, cooled"
- "1 jalapeno finely diced, seeds removed for less heat"
- "1 cup canned black beans, rinsed and drained"
- "1 cup canned corn kernels, drained (replaces Rotel for sweetness and crunch)"
- "4 large flour tortillas"
- "Plastic wrap for rolling"
instructions:
- "Chop jalapeno fine, wear gloves or wash hands immediately after. Seeds add intense heat; optional to remove all seeds if sensitive."
- "Drain black beans and corn thoroughly, pat dry if needed to avoid soggy filling."
- "In a bowl, beat softened cream cheese and sour cream first. Helps achieve smooth base, prevents lumps."
- "Add cumin, smoked paprika, garlic powder, black pepper. Mix well with hand mixer or whisk. Spices wake the mix up."
- "Fold in green onions, cheddar, shredded chicken, jalapeno, black beans, and drained corn carefully. Avoid overmixing; want even distribution without breaking beans."
- "Lay out tortillas flat on plastic wrap squared off to tortilla size."
- "Spread about ¾ to 1 cup of filling evenly across each tortilla. Leave a small border to prevent overflow when rolling."
- "Tight roll each tortilla with help of plastic wrap underneath, sealing without air pockets. A snug roll ensures neat slices."
- "Wrap rolled tortillas fully in plastic, place in fridge. Minimum chill is one hour, but 1.5 hours cold ensures firm slices that hold shape."
- "When firm, unwrap and slice pinwheels ¾ to 1 inch thick. Use a sharp serrated knife, gentle sawing motion to avoid squishing."
- "Serve cold or bring to room temp briefly for softer feel. Holds well for party trays or meal prep."
- "If mixture seems too loose before chilling, add a few tablespoons of shredded cheese or scoop out excess moisture with paper towel. Soggy rolls won’t slice clean."
