ingredients:
- "2 boneless skinless chicken breasts seasoned with fine sea salt and freshly cracked black pepper"
- "2 tablespoons vegetable oil or avocado oil"
- "1/2 cup finely chopped shallots (substitute for red onion)"
- "2 cloves garlic minced"
- "1 serrano pepper finely diced (milder alternative: jalapeno)"
- "3 tablespoons fresh lime juice"
- "1 tablespoon peeled and grated fresh ginger"
- "2 teaspoons yellow curry powder, preferably fresh ground"
- "1 teaspoon cornstarch (optional, for thickening)"
- "1 cup full-fat coconut cream (substitute coconut milk for lighter result)"
- "1/2 cup fresh mint leaves roughly chopped (in place of basil)"
- "1/2 cup sweet peas (instead of corn)"
- "Coarse Maldon or fleur de sel flake salt to finish"
- "Cooked Cilantro-Lime Coconut Rice (see notes)"
instructions:
- "Heat oil in a skillet over medium heat—wait till it shimmers but doesn’t smoke. Pat chicken dry; season evenly with salt and cracked black pepper. Lay in skillet, no crowding. You want golden crusts, listen for hard sizzle. Cook approx 3-4 minutes per side depending on thickness. Flip carefully—don’t stab. When browned, remove chicken, tent loosely. Residual heat will finish cooking later; resting is key to keep juices."
- "Lower heat slightly. Add shallots, garlic, and serrano. Quick stir—soften but keep edges intact, about 1 min. Smell sharp, aromatic, pungent. Don’t rush."
- "Deglaze pan with lime juice. Scrape bottom with wooden spoon. Watch browned bits lift—that’s flavor gold. Allow the mix to soften into a paste, resembling sofrito. Stir occasionally, approx 2-3 minutes. Color shifts to translucent with bright spots."
- "Toss in ginger, curry powder, and sprinkle cornstarch last. Stir vigorously—mixture turns dry and pasty. Let it toast lightly 50-60 seconds till curry aroma pops. Don’t burn—bitterness kills."
- "Pour in coconut cream steady, stir to combine. Sauce will loosen, turn creamy and rich. Bring to low simmer, bubbles just breaking. Adjust heat—too hot will separate fat, too low stalls cooking."
- "Nestle chicken back into pan, submerge halfway in sauce. Add peas and mint—freshness cuts richness. Baste occasionally by spooning sauce over chicken. Simmer gently for 6 minutes, flipping halfway. Flesh should be opaque, sauce slightly thickened, herbs vibrant."
- "Plate chicken with generous spoonful of sauce. Sprinkle with coarse flakey salt to brighten flavors and add crunch."
- "Serve alongside cilantro-lime coconut rice (cooked jasmine rice mixed with toasted coconut flakes, chopped cilantro, lime zest, and juice). The acid and herbaceous tones lift the dish."
- "Watch for dry spots or undercooked chicken by checking texture; if needed, cover and cook an extra 1-2 minutes off direct heat. Avoid long simmering that breaks down herbs and dulls curry punch."
