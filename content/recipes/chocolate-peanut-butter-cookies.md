ingredients:
- "1 1/4 cups white whole wheat flour"
- "1/3 cup unsweetened dark cocoa powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking powder"
- "1 stick unsalted butter, room temp"
- "1/2 cup creamy peanut butter (natural or honey roasted)"
- "1/2 cup dark brown sugar, packed"
- "3/4 cup granulated sugar (reserve 1/2 cup for rolling)"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1 teaspoon almond extract"
- "turbinado sugar for rolling (or fine granulated sugar as backup)"
instructions:
- "Line baking sheet with parchment or silicone mat. No oil or spray needed, sugar coating prevents sticky bottoms."
- "Combine white whole wheat flour, dark cocoa, baking soda and baking powder in bowl. Sift together to avoid lumps, set aside."
- "Reserve 1/2 cup granulated sugar for rolling dough balls later."
- "Cream butter and peanut butter with stand mixer paddle or strong handheld while at medium speed. Should be smooth, starting to look shiny but still holding fluff."
- "Add dark brown sugar and 1/4 cup granulated sugar. Continue creaming until texture lightens — 2 to 3 minutes. Do not overbeat or cookie will flatten in oven."
- "Add eggs one by one, mixing just to combine. Look for smooth batter, no visible streaks. Overmixing toughens gluten."
- "Stir in almond extract, mixing briefly."
- "Gradually fold the sifted flour mixture into wet. Use low speed or spatula. Stop mixing as soon flour disappears but dough still a bit shaggy. Overworking makes dense cookies."
- "Cover dough tightly. Refrigerate at least 1 hour—1 hour 15 minutes if possible. Cooler dough holds shape better when baking, less spread."
- "Preheat oven to 345°F (hotter than typical 350°F to compensate for humidity)."
- "Scoop dough with small scoop or tablespoon, form balls about 1 1/4 inches diameter. Keep fingers cool / light touch to avoid melting dough."
- "Roll balls in reserved sugar; place in quart size Ziploc bag for less mess. Fully coated sugar makes the sugar shell and crunchy texture."
- "Arrange dough balls 2 1/2 inches apart on prepared sheet. Space matters—cookies spread more than you think because of peanut butter fat."
- "Dip fork in sugar before pressing each ball gently into a criss-cross shape. Do not press hard—just enough to flatten about half the height. The sugar on the fork prevents sticking, gives clean lines. If fork sticks, dipped sugar is the fix."
- "Bake 7 to 9 minutes. Edges should firm up, centers still soft and slightly shiny but no raw batter. Cookies firm more as they cool off tray. Use sound—cookie should stop making wet-sound when pressed lightly on edge with clean finger."
- "Let cookies cool on baking sheet for 3 to 4 minutes—longer than usual. Cooling lets edges solidify so cookies lift smoothly from parchment without breaking or sticking."
- "Transfer to wire rack to cool fully. Store airtight at room temp. Cookies develop tenderness overnight but best eaten within 3 days."
- "Substitute peanut butter with almond butter or sunflower seed butter for nut allergy. If cocoa powder unavailable, use instant espresso powder for unexpected mocha note but reduce sugar by 1/4 cup."
- "Don’t skip chilling; unchilled dough spreads too thin and dries."
- "Try turbinado sugar for crunch and rustic look instead of fine sugar."
