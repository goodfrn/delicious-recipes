ingredients:
- "1 box yellow cake mix"
- "4 cups fresh blueberries (can substitute with peaches, drained canned ones)"
- "2 tablespoons granulated sugar (adjust based on fruit sweetness)"
- "1 cup plus 2 tablespoons water (adjust depending on fruit juiciness)"
- "6 tablespoons cold unsalted butter, cut into pieces (can use margarine or coconut oil for dairy-free)"
instructions:
- "1. Heat oven to 355°F. Use convection if available, adds nice browning."
- "2. Spread cake mix evenly in 9x13 pan bottom. Important: don’t press down! Keep it loose so water percolates through."
- "3. Scatter blueberries (or peaches) on top. No stirring—just a light toss so berries coat mix. Keeping berries whole matters, mushy fruit ruins texture."
- "4. Sprinkle sugar over berries. Sweetness depends on fruit ripeness. Taste before deciding sugar amount."
- "5. Pour water gradually. Aim for enough to moisten cake mix without drowning. Watch for mix absorbing water but not disappearing."
- "6. Toss pan gently a few times to let water settle. Avoid vigorous mixing; that activates cake mix prematurely."
- "7. Dot butter pieces evenly on top. Butter moisture critical to golden brown crust and bubbling edges."
- "8. Bake 43 to 53 minutes. Peek at 40: edges should bubble, top turns tan, center set but slightly jiggly."
- "9. Remove when top is firm with slight spring and edges bubbling heavily. Let cool minimally—too hot means runny, too cold means hard to scoop."
- "10. Serve warm, with whipped cream or vanilla ice cream if available."
