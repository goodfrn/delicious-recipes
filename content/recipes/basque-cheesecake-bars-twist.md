ingredients:
- "1 3/4 cups almond meal"
- "2/3 cup coconut sugar"
- "1/4 teaspoon salt"
- "7 tablespoons melted butter"
- "24 ounces cream cheese softened"
- "1/2 cup granulated sugar"
- "4 large eggs"
- "2/3 cup heavy cream"
- "1 1/2 teaspoons almond extract"
- "1/4 teaspoon salt"
- "1/4 cup oat flour"
- "1 teaspoon lemon zest"
instructions:
- "Get a 9×13-inch pan ready. Line with parchment paper up and over edges—makes prying the bars out later less of a battle."
- "Toss almond meal, coconut sugar, and salt into a bowl. Pour melted butter over. Mix and press evenly into pan base. Freeze 30 minutes—no baking needed, will set hard for filling."
- "Crank up oven to 395°F. Give it time to reach heat, oven spring matters for that Basque top."
- "Cream softened cream cheese and sugar in mixer with paddle. Scrape bowl sides or risk lumps hiding. 3 minutes till satin smooth—patience."
- "Add eggs one by one. Medium low speed so they blend without whipping. You want creamy, not fluffy. Scrape again."
- "Drizzle in cream, almond extract, salt gently. Low speed. Scrape again. If mixer's roaring, you’re at risk of introducing air bubbles that crack crust."
- "Sift oat flour and toss lemon zest in. Mix low just to incorporate. Clumps will disappear in bake."
- "Pour batter over chilled crust. Use offset spatula to smooth but don’t overwork—it’s thick and sticky. Tap pan hard 3-4 times on countertop to dump trapped air."
- "Bake ~55 minutes. Top olive oil brown, blistered in spots, jiggly center shaking like gelatin but not sloshing. Edges pull slightly from pan. Don’t overbake or dry surface."
- "Let cool fully in pan on wire rack. Warm bars slice custardy, fridge overnight for denser texture. Lift from pan by parchment, then cut with sharp knife cleaned between each slice."
- "Store covered in fridge up to 3 days. Let warm 15 minutes before serving to soften flavors."
introduction: "Baking bars that hit the balance between creamy cheesecake and the burnished, caramelized drama of a Basque finish takes some discipline. Skip the blind bake on crust—freezing it firms almond meal, which brings a toasty, buttery nuance that's richer than graham crackers. Coconut sugar darkens sweetness gently without that harsh molasses hit, and swapping vanilla for almond extract with lemon zest ups complexity without stealing the show. Watch the jiggle. The dome rises proud then collapses; the jiggle signals just enough doneness without drying out. Bottom line? Don’t chase times blindly; rely on color, feel, and that wobble. It’s a learning curve but pays out."
ingredientsNote: "Almond meal over graham cracker crumbs gives a nuttier, moister crust. Can swap with finely ground hazelnuts if bored or nut allergies aren’t an issue. Coconut sugar adds subtle caramel notes and a bit less processed than white sugar; light brown sugar is the easy substitute but expect a brown tint. Oat flour replaces all-purpose for a tender crumb; if unavailable, use unbleached flour but tap down on mixing to keep aeration in check. Lemon zest cuts through fat and almond extract enhances without overpowering. Butter must be melted, warm to meld crumbs smoothly. Avoid cold butter chunks."
instructionsNote: "Prepping crust in the freezer avoids soggy bottoms and wasted oven time. Mix almond meal and sugar well to prevent clumps. Melted butter should be brushed or drizzled evenly, then pressed with clean flat-bottomed measuring cup for uniform density—don’t just smoosh by hand, uneven crust risks crumbling on slicing. Cream cheese at room temp is non-negotiable; lumpy batter ruins texture. Add eggs slowly on low to avoid curdling—speed kills. Minimal flour mixing preserves fluffiness yet enough to stabilize batter. Tap pan consistently to expel air bubbles, no shortcuts. Bake until surface cracks, deep gold with burnt speckles—those ugly brown spots are flavor gold. After baking, cool fully; warm cutting crumbles. When slicing, dip knife in hot water and wipe clean—cuts sharp, bars neat. Store airtight, but not frozen or will lose texture nuances."
