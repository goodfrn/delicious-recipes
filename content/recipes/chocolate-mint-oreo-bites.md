ingredients:
- "8 oz softened cream cheese"
- "1 package crushed Thin Mint cookies, about 30"
- "5 oz dark melting chocolate"
- "1 tbsp unsalted butter"
- "Crushed peppermint candies for decoration"
instructions:
- "Crush Thin Mint cookies finely but don’t turn to powder. Mix with softened cream cheese. Texture like damp sand. Stop when uniform but avoid over-kneading; too warm turns mix greasy."
- "Form 25 balls around 1.25 inch diameter. Slightly larger than usual to keep mint flavor intact. Place on parchment or wax paper-lined tray. Freeze 15 minutes until firm but not frozen solid. This firms up mix for easier dipping."
- "Double boiler melting chocolate with butter. Use barely simmering water, keep chocolate bowl dry. Stir until glossy and melted. Butter thins chocolate, prevents clumping, yields better coating snap when cooled."
- "Remove cookie balls from freezer. Using fork or dipping tool, dip gently into chocolate, turn to coat fully. Shake off excess slowly to avoid thin spots or drips. Return to lined sheet. If chocolate thickens, warm gently between dips."
- "Sprinkle crushed peppermint over dipped balls while chocolate still tacky. Crisp candy adds crunch and color contrast. If chocolate hardens, decorate with drizzle instead."
- "Refrigerate for minimum 55 minutes or until firm with snap on touch. Avoid condensation by covering loosely. Store chilled. Keep away from humidity to prevent melting or bloom."

instructionsNote: "Precise crushing controls texture—too fine means dense, too coarse means crumbly balls. Mix cream cheese and cookie crumbs until sandy and manageable, aim for smooth but not oily.
