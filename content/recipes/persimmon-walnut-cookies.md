ingredients:
- "2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1 teaspoon baking powder"
- "½ teaspoon salt"
- "1 teaspoon ground cinnamon"
- "¼ teaspoon ground cardamom"
- "½ cup unsalted butter softened"
- "1 cup light brown sugar packed"
- "¾ cup ripe persimmon pulp"
- "1 large egg"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "½ cup toasted walnuts chopped"
- "½ cup golden raisins"
- "½ cup confectioners sugar"
- "2 tablespoons fresh orange juice"
- "1 teaspoon orange zest"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 345 degrees F. Prep two baking sheets lined with parchment or silicone mats; set aside."
- "Toast walnuts lightly on dry skillet until fragrant, about 3 minutes. Cool completely."
- "In a medium bowl, sift together flour, baking powder, salt, cinnamon, and cardamom. Stir to combine evenly. Set aside."
- "In separate large bowl, cream butter and brown sugar until fluffy, light in color. Doesn’t need to be perfect but no big lumps."
- "Add persimmon pulp in chunks, then egg, vanilla. Scrape sides, mix until just blended—don’t over beat or batter toughens."
- "Slowly pour in dry ingredients, folding carefully to preserve moisture. Mix until no streaks but stop once combined."
- "Fold in cooled toasted walnuts and raisins evenly distributed. Dough will be sticky, thick but scoopable."
- "Scoop rounded tablespoons onto baking sheets spaced well apart. They spread slightly but keep shape. Don’t flatten."
- "Slide into oven. Around 15-17 minutes. Look for edges browned but centers still soft and springy. Jiggle is okay. Smell cinnamon warming and nuts roasting deeper."
- "Remove sheets, cool on sheet 5 minutes to firm up, then transfer to wire rack for full cooling. Delays cause breakage."
- "Whisk confectioners sugar, orange juice, and zest until glaze is thick but pourable. If too runny, add more sugar; too thick, a drop more juice."
- "Dip cookie tops quickly, twist off excess to prevent dripping mess. Place back on rack to let glaze harden, about 15 minutes. Avoid stacking till glaze sets."
- "Store airtight. Use ripe but firm persimmons; watery pulp dilutes texture. If no persimmons, try pureed pumpkin but reduce sugar slightly."
- "Butter swap tip: coconut oil imparts nuttier profile but softens dough faster. Keep chilled if needed."
- "If raisin-free, sub dried cranberries or chopped dates. Toast nuts fresh to keep crunch intact."
- "Watch baking sheets: too hot brown too fast, underbake leaves dough too soft inside. Rotate sheets halfway if uneven heat."
