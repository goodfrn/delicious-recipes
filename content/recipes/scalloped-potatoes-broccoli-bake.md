ingredients:
- "4 cups thinly sliced russet potatoes"
- "3 cups broccoli florets, lightly steamed"
- "1 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "1 teaspoon onion powder"
- "1 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "1 can 12 oz cream of mushroom soup"
- "1 1/2 cups evaporated milk"
- "2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese"
- "Nonstick cooking spray"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 350F. Spray a 9x13 baking dish lightly with nonstick spray."
- "In a large bowl, combine sliced potatoes, steamed broccoli, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, cream of mushroom soup, evaporated milk, and shredded cheddar. Use your hands or a spoon to mix thoroughly. Ensure broccoli is cooled to avoid wilting."
- "Pour mixture into prepared dish. Spread evenly. You want potato layers submerged but broccoli poking through here and there for texture contrast."
- "Bake uncovered on center rack for about 55 minutes. After 40 minutes peek for bubbles around edges and knife-tender potatoes. Insert thin knife - it should slide easily but with slight resistance; that starch bite means they're done."
- "Watch the top for golden brown spots. Cheese should be bubbling and edges slightly caramelized with a hint of crunch forming. If top browns too fast, tent loosely with foil."
- "Let stand 10 minutes before serving. The sauce thickens on cooling, holds everything together."
- "If broccoli turns mushy or overcooked, next batch reduce steaming time by half or add raw florets later in bake cycle."
- "Evaporated milk cuts fat but keeps creaminess. Heavy cream can replace if richer result desired, adjust salt accordingly."
- "For Dairy-free, swap cheese with nutritional yeast and coconut cream for soup base. Expect less browning."
