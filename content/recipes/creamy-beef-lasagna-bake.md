ingredients:
- "12 ounces egg noodles"
- "1 pound ground beef"
- "1 small shallot chopped"
- "1 tablespoon olive oil"
- "3 cloves garlic minced"
- "24 ounces marinara sauce"
- "1 1/2 cups evaporated milk"
- "6 ounces whipped cream cheese softened"
- "1 1/2 cups shredded mozzarella"
- "1 cup shredded white cheddar"
- "Salt to taste"
- "Nonstick cooking spray"
instructions:
- "Start with a large pot of salted water at a vigorous boil. Drop the noodles in. Cook until just shy of al dente—flesh still has some bite, maybe 8 minutes. Drain and set aside; tossing with a little olive oil stops sticking. Don't rinse or let sit too long; noodles stiffen and glue up."
- "While noodles cook, heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add shallot first, softening till translucent and sweet-smelling, about 2 minutes. Introduce ground beef; break apart with wooden spoon. Brown until no pink remains and fat renders, probably 7 to 8 minutes. Drain off excess fat thoroughly—wet meat kills sauce texture."
- "Return skillet to low heat with drained beef. Toss in garlic, stirring continuously. Watch the aroma pop and soften but don't scorch; fragile and fragrant in 2 to 3 minutes. Pour in marinara sauce, stir to combine. Let sauce warm through and thicken slightly, 5 minutes or so. Salt to balance acidity; sauce should taste lively but smooth."
- "In noodle pot, stir together evaporated milk and cream cheese over low heat. The cream cheese melts into a thick, velvet-like sauce that clings to noodles. If lumps form, keep stirring and slightly raise heat, gently coaxing smoothness. Pour this creamy noodle mix into a sprayed 9x13-inch baking dish. Level out with a spatula; even layer."
- "Spoon meat sauce evenly over noodles. Don't drown—spread thick enough that each bite gets meat but noodles peek through. Sprinkle mozzarella and cheddar on top. Cheese selection matters. Mozzarella melts silky; sharp cheddar adds tang and bite. No Parmesan? Use extra sharp cheddar or fontina for depth."
- "Bake at 345°F. Odd temp. Keeps edges from overbrowning too fast but melts cheese fully. Watch for bubbling sauce at edges, cheese golden with little brown spots. About 22 minutes. Let rest 10 minutes—crucial to firm up before cutting. Spoons of sauce settle, no sloppy run."
- "Serving tip: Slice with a sharp serrated knife. If sticking, run knife under hot water then dry. Add chopped fresh basil or oregano on top for freshness and color. Leftovers reheat well covered; avoid drying out by adding splash of milk."
- "Substitutions and common fixes: Swap ground turkey or pork for beef to lighten but keep cooking times similar. Cream cheese can be replaced with ricotta mixed with a tablespoon of sour cream, thicker but milder. If noodles glue, toss again with olive oil before layering or undercook by a minute during boiling. Overbaked cheese? Tent loosely with foil for remainder of cooking."
