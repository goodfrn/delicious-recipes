ingredients:
- "1 cup creamy peanut butter"
- "4 ounces softened cream cheese, room temp"
- "1 ½ cups powdered brown sugar, sifted"
- "1 cup crushed vanilla wafer cookies"
- "½ cup unsweetened shredded coconut"
- "Pinch of kosher salt"
- "1 pound dark chocolate almond bark or dark chocolate chips"
- "Coarse sea salt for garnish"
instructions:
- "Chill a large baking sheet or tray. Toss peanut butter, cream cheese, powdered brown sugar, crushed vanilla wafers, coconut, and salt into a big bowl. Use clean hands—not a mixer. You want a dense, moldable dough; too airy and it falls apart when dipped. Feel for a tacky but firm texture, not sticky enough to cling to fingers."
- "Scoop about 1 ½ tablespoons, roll firmly and tightly into balls. Aim for uniform size. Set on chilled tray. Pop them in the fridge for 25 minutes. You want them firm enough to handle, but not ice-cold; a slightly pliable center helps with fresh bite."
- "Melt the dark chocolate slowly in a double boiler or microwave in short bursts with stirring. Thick, shiny, fluid chocolate is key—too hot ruins temper; too cool won't coat properly. Use two forks per ball. Drop a ball in, roll and lift swiftly, tap forks on bowl side to shed excess. Place on parchment or silicone mat. Sprinkle sea salt before chocolate sets to lock the crystals onto the surface."
- "Work in small batches so chocolate stays temperate. Rest remaining balls in fridge. If chocolate hardens too fast, gently remelt. Avoid excess dipping time; chocolate won’t coat well if balls get too warm or soft. Let coated balls set at room temp or chill briefly just before serving."
- "Store airtight in fridge for up to 7 days or keep in a cool spot up to 3 days. Chocolate softens when warm; too cold makes it chalky. Let sit a few minutes before eating for max creamy texture."

Too cold and chocolate dulls, too warm and balls soften. Room temp is your friend just before serving. You’ll get a hold on tactile cues fast—use those, not timers."
