ingredients:
- "1/3 cup walnuts"
- "3 tablespoons Pecorino Romano cheese, finely grated"
- "4 garlic cloves, peeled"
- "1 1/2 cups fresh basil leaves, packed"
- "1/3 cup fresh arugula"
- "1/3 cup flat-leaf parsley, loosely packed"
- "4 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil"
- "1/3 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "1/3 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper"
instructions:
- "=== Toast nuts. ==="
- "Place walnuts in a dry skillet over medium heat. Toast until fragrant and just starting to brown, about 3 minutes. Listen for faint crackle, smell nutty warmth. Shake pan frequently to avoid burning. Cool slightly."
- "=== Process base ==="
- "Drop toasted walnuts into food processor. Pulse several times until coarse crumbs appear, not a puree. This step builds texture, prevents oily paste."
- "=== Add aromatics and greens ==="
- "Add garlic, basil, arugula, parsley. Pulse in short bursts, scraping sides once or twice. Watch color shift from bright green to slightly muted emerald, coarse grain still visible. Over-processing kills vibrancy and texture."
- "=== Cheese and seasoning ==="
- "Add Pecorino Romano, salt, pepper. Pulse 3-4 times. Cheese adds salty tang, balancing greens and nuttiness."
- "=== Oil incorporation ==="
- "Slowly drizzle olive oil through feed tube while pulsing. Stop when mixture is well combined but still grainy - not oily or emulsified smooth. Proper thickness clings to pasta without pooling."
- "=== Usage and storage ==="
- "Use immediately tossed with pasta or as spread on warm crusty bread. Pesto stiffens when refrigerated; bring to room temp and stir with fork before use. Keeps up to 5 days sealed airtight."
- "=== Troubleshooting and tips ==="
- "If nuts are oily, reduce oil added. Use pine nuts or almonds if walnuts unavailable, though flavor shifts. Arugula adds peppery bite; replace with spinach for milder taste. Garlic intensity can be dialed by quantity, raw always sharp. Over-processing causes bitter taste—pulse short bursts only."
- "=== Final notes ==="
- "Color is key: vibrant green signals fresh herbs and minimal oxidation. Texture should be coarse with tiny bits of nuts visible for mouthfeel. If paste clumps or seems dry, add more olive oil teaspoon by teaspoon until desired consistency."
