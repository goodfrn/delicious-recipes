ingredients:
- "1 1/4 pounds boneless skinless chicken breasts"
- "8 ounces uncooked spaghetti"
- "1 can (10 ounces) fire-roasted diced tomatoes with juice"
- "1 can (10 ounces) undiluted cream of celery soup"
- "8 ounces Velveeta cheese, cubed"
- "4 ounces cream cheese, softened"
- "1 1/2 teaspoons garlic powder"
- "1 teaspoon onion powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika"
- "2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese"
- "Nonstick cooking spray"
instructions:
- "Start with chicken in a large pot; just cover with cold water. Keep heat medium-low—no rolling boil allowed. When chicken’s firm and white inside, poke with fork to check; juices run clear not pink. Remove and cool enough to shred. Using two forks, pull apart fibers; avoid big clumps, shred fine—helps even mixing."
- "While chicken rests, heat oven to 355°F. Slightly grease a 13x9 baking dish with nonstick spray; too much oil pools, makes edges greasy."
- "Boil spaghetti in lots of salted water until al dente. Should still have bite, not floppy. Drain thoroughly; excess water ruins the sauce texture."
- "In the original cooking pot, combine fire-roasted tomatoes with juice, cream of celery soup, Velveeta, and cream cheese. Turn heat to low—cheese melts better slow. Stir constantly, scrape pot bottom to prevent scorching. Look for velvety smooth mixture, no lumps. Add garlic powder, onion powder, smoked paprika now; spices bloom in heat, don’t add after baking or flavor weakens."
- "Mix shredded chicken and cooked spaghetti into sauce, folding gently but thoroughly. Full coating on every strand important. Pour all into prepared dish. Cover top evenly with shredded cheddar cheese—freshly grated melts best, pre-shredded can leave waxy residue."
- "Bake uncovered 25 to 30 minutes until bubbling around edges and cheese melted with light golden spots. Visual cues more reliable than oven timer. Tent with foil if cheese browns too fast."
- "Remove and let rest 5 minutes; sets texture. Too hot and pasta keeps sliding apart, hard to serve."
- "Serve from same dish, scooping under the crusty, melty top."
