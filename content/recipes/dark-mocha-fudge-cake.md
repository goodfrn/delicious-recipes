ingredients:
- "2 ½ cups all-purpose flour"
- "¾ cup Dutch-processed unsweetened cocoa powder"
- "1 ½ tsp baking powder"
- "1 tsp baking soda"
- "¼ tsp salt"
- "1 ½ cups granulated sugar"
- "1 cup light brown sugar packed"
- "1 cup sour cream full-fat"
- "7 tbsp canola oil"
- "3 large eggs"
- "2 tsp vanilla extract"
- "1 cup hot water"
- "1 tbsp instant coffee granules"
- "½ cup unsalted butter softened"
- "3 ½ cups powdered sugar sifted"
- "½ cup unsweetened cocoa powder"
- "3 tbsp heavy cream"
- "1 tsp vanilla extract"
- "½ tsp ground brown mustard powder"
- "pinch sea salt"
instructions:
- "=== Cake ==="
- "Oven heat set to 345°F. Grease two 9-inch pans well with baking spray. Cut parchment to fit base snugly. Trust the paper not to wrinkle; helps cake release cleanly later."
- "In a large bowl, combine sugars, sour cream, oil, eggs, vanilla. Hand mixer on medium speed. Mix until just homogenous. Don’t overbeat – yolks and oils need gentle coaxing to blend. Look for glossy, no separate puddles."
- "In separate, whisk flour, Dutch cocoa, baking powder, soda, salt. Uniform dry blend prevents clumps mid-mix."
- "Dissolve coffee granules fully in hot water. Swirl; no grit leftover or bitterness sneaks in."
- "Add half the flour blend to wet mix; stir with mixer on low just until streaks disappear. Fold in coffee water mixture next. Again, just enough to meld. Pour remaining flour mix in last, give a 1-2 minute whip at medium speed. Batter thickens, velvety, ribbon falls slow off beater. That’s your cue."
- "Divide batter evenly between pans. Tap pans sharply on counter to level surface, pop out air bubbles."
- "Bake 30-40 minutes. Edges begin pulling from sides; top springs back with gentle touch. Toothpick test: no wet batter, small moist crumbs okay."
- "Let cakes rest in pans 12-15 minutes. Warm cakes release easier. Use offset spatula to loosen edge if stubborn. Invert onto wire rack. Peel parchment. Cool fully flat before frosting or frosting slips off."
- "=== Frosting ==="
- "Softened butter placed in large bowl or stand mixer with whisk. Whisk medium-high until uniformly creamy, pause to scrape bowl sides – no solid chunks means better structure."
- "Sift powder sugar and cocoa into butter. Start mixer slow to keep sugar cloud contained. Mix thoroughly, thick, paste-like. Pause. Add brown mustard powder and pinch sea salt here. Balances sweetness, adds complexity."
- "Slowly drizzle heavy cream and vanilla into running mixer. Whip on medium-high until fluffy, 3-4 minutes. Look for frosting light enough to hold shape but with spreadability."
- "Trim dome of cooled cakes with serrated knife for stable layers. Place bottom layer on serving plate. Spread approximately one-third frosting evenly, corner to corner, edges neat."
- "Add second layer. Cover top, then sides with remaining frosting. Use offset spatula for smooth finish. Stop when cake looks wrapped, not swamped."
- "Chill briefly if spreading gets difficult or to set frosting quicker. Remove 10 minutes prior to slicing for clean cuts without drag."
