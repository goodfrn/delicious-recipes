ingredients:
- "2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1 tsp baking powder"
- "1 tsp baking soda"
- "1/2 tsp salt"
- "1 tsp ground cinnamon"
- "3/4 tsp ground nutmeg"
- "1/2 tsp ground ginger"
- "1 1/2 cups granulated sugar"
- "3/4 cup packed light brown sugar"
- "4 large eggs"
- "1 1/3 cups pumpkin puree"
- "2/3 cup vegetable oil"
- "1 tsp pure vanilla extract"
- "3/4 cup sour cream"
- "1 1/2 cups semisweet chocolate chips"
- "For frosting:"
- "4 oz cream cheese softened"
- "1/2 cup unsalted butter softened"
- "2 cups powdered sugar sifted"
- "1 tsp vanilla extract"
- "2-4 tbsp whole milk"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 355°F. Butter and flour a 10-12 cup bundt pan well so cake releases clean. No corner left bare or you risk cake scraps sticking."
- "Sift or whisk flour, baking powder, soda, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger in a bowl. Even distribution avoids bitter pockets under flavors."
- "In mixer bowl with paddle, combine granulated and brown sugars plus eggs. Beat on medium for 3 1/2 minutes until pale and airy. Key step. Don't rush. Look for fluffy volume, pale yellow hue—not just mixed."
- "Add pumpkin puree, oil, vanilla. Mix until uniform without overbeating—overworked batter yields dense cake. Stop mixing the moment you can no longer see streaks."
- "Alternate dry ingredients and sour cream into batter, starting and ending with the flour mix. Use low speed, fold softly after each. Keeps gluten minimal. Overmixing toughens crumb."
- "Toss chocolate chips in a tablespoon of flour to keep them suspended and prevent sinking; fold gently into batter. Leave excess flour behind. Resist urge to stir vigorously."
- "Transfer batter to bundt pan. Tap pan firmly on counter 4-5 times. That burps big air bubbles that turn holes in crumb if left alone."
- "Bake 42-55 minutes. Look for cake pulling back from edges. Test center with toothpick: moist crumbs but no batter. Don't wait for fully dry pick—overbaking dries cake saying no."
- "Cool in pan 12 minutes max. Then invert onto wire rack over parchment to catch drips. Gently tease cake away from pan edges with thin paring knife if stuck. Let fully cool before frosting."
- "For frosting, hand beat cream cheese and butter till soft and smooth. Add powdered sugar and vanilla. Add milk spoon by spoon till thick but spreadable consistency. Thicker frosting holds shape on bundt ridges. Thin if you want glaze-like drip."
- "Spread or pipe frosting over cooled cake. Garnish if you want: toasted pecans, cinnamon dust, edible flowers."
- "Serve within 3 days. Store covered tightly in fridge to protect cream cheese topping. Bring slices to room temp before serving for best flavor."
