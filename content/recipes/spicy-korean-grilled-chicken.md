ingredients:
- "1 tablespoon chili garlic sauce"
- "1 tablespoon coarse sea salt"
- "2 tablespoons tamari or soy sauce as substitute"
- "1 tablespoon honey"
- "3 pounds bone-in chicken thighs"
- "2 tablespoons vegetable oil for grilling"
- "Quick stir-fry veggies (bell peppers, snap peas)"
- "Lemongrass lime rice (jasmine rice with fresh lemongrass and lime zest)"
instructions:
- "Mix chili garlic sauce with coarse sea salt, tamari, and honey in medium bowl. Reserve ⅓ cup for basting; chill it."
- "Toss chicken thighs in remaining sauce, massaging well. Marinate at least 2 hours, up to 18; longer won’t dry meat but check saltiness."
- "Preheat grill medium-high. Oil grates with paper towel dipped in oil—prevents sticking. Arrange chicken on cooler indirect side. Cover and cook about 30 minutes, flipping every 7–8 minutes to develop even color. Listen for steady sizzle, no flare-ups."
- "Move chicken direct heat. Apply reserved basting sauce with brush, repeat every 3 minutes while turning often for 12 minutes+. Skin will blister, darken, aroma bold and sweet-spicy. Use thermometer; pull at 165°F; texture firm, juices clear, skin crisp yet tender."
- "Serve immediately with quick stir-fried veggies—keep them crisp-tender by cooking in hot pan with minimal oil and salt after grilling. Lemongrass lime rice is zest and aroma in each bite; optional toasted sesame for nuttiness."
- "If flames flare, shift chicken away fast—avoid burning sugars in honey. Swap tamari for coconut aminos for soy-free. Vegan? Replace chicken with extra firm tofu, marinate less time, grill carefully to prevent crumble."
