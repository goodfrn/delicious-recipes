ingredients:
- "2 tablespoons cream cheese softened"
- "1 tablespoon basil pesto substitute for sun-dried tomato spread"
- "pinch salt"
- "pinch black pepper"
- "1 teaspoon butter"
- "1 cup fresh baby spinach rough chopped"
- "2 tablespoons diced roasted red peppers substitute for sun-dried tomatoes"
- "1/8 teaspoon smoked paprika"
- "1/8 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "3/4 cup egg whites"
- "1/4 cup crumbled goat cheese substitute for feta"
- "1 large lavash wrap or flour tortilla"
instructions:
- "=== Make the herb cream cheese spread ==="
- "Whip softened cream cheese with pesto until creamy and smooth. Add salt and pepper to taste. Set aside at room temp to keep soft. The pesto replaces original sun-dried tomato spread adding fresh basil notes, balancing richness and herbaceousness."
- ""
- "=== Wilt spinach and prepare mix-ins ==="
- "Heat butter in an 8-inch nonstick skillet over medium. Toss in spinach, stir just until leaves lose volume and become limp. Should hear immediate sizzle, wilt within about 1-2 minutes. Add diced roasted red peppers, sprinkle smoked paprika and garlic powder. Stir briefly to combine and wake up spices without burning. The peppers bring bright sweetness while paprika adds smoky warmth."
- ""
- "=== Cook egg whites gently ==="
- "Pour egg whites evenly over spinach mixture without stirring. Let spread naturally covering spinach chunks. Cover pan with a tight-fitting lid immediately. Lower heat to medium low. Cook slowly–listen for quiet bubbling–so whites set softly without toughening edges. About 4-7 minutes, depending on your stove; watch for opaque surface and no glossy areas. If parts remain runny near edges, swirl pan gently to redistribute. Remove from heat but keep lid on. Rest 5 minutes to let residual heat finish cooking and firm up the custard-like texture."
- ""
- "=== Assemble the wrap ==="
- "On clean surface, open lavash or tortilla. Spread even layer of pesto cream cheese, covering fully but not too thick. Spoon cooled egg whites with spinach mixture over spread. Scatter crumbled goat cheese across the top so each bite gets that tangy creaminess. Goat cheese is softer and tangier than feta, adding a fresh twist and cream dream. Avoid overfilling to prevent tearing during rolling."
- ""
- "=== Roll and seal ==="
- "Fold bottom edge up over filling, fold in sides snugly, then roll tightly but gently. Thin lavash can tear; if fragile, roll loosely and handle with care. Keeps filling contained without squeezing out. Wrapping technique important–seal seams well to avoid oozing and help with crispness later."
- ""
- "=== Crisp the wrap ==="
- "Wipe out skillet clean with paper towel to remove moisture and burned bits. Place pan over medium heat, no oil. Place wrap seam side down first for seals to stick. Toast 2-3 minutes until underside develops golden crust. Flip carefully, toast other sides for same duration until evenly browned and crispy. Listen for faint crackle as surface firms crisp. If heat too high, wrap scorches or tears."
- ""
- "=== Serve ==="
- "Cut wrap in half diagonally for easy handling. Eat as is or customized with hot sauce or tangy mustard. Keeps well briefly; crispness fades so eat shortly after crisping. Can wrap in foil and reheat in pan for crispness revival."
