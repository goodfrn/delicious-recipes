ingredients:
- "1 lb top sirloin steak"
- "4 tablespoons unsalted butter divided"
- "1 tablespoon vegetable oil"
- "3 cloves garlic minced"
- "1 tablespoon fresh ginger minced"
- "3 tablespoons brown sugar"
- "1/3 cup soy sauce"
- "3 tablespoons rice vinegar"
- "1 teaspoon chili paste (substitute for pineapple juice)"
- "1 teaspoon toasted sesame seeds replaced with 1 teaspoon black sesame seeds"
- "1 tablespoon arrowroot starch (substitute for cornstarch)"
- "Water or rice vinegar to dissolve arrowroot"
- "Sesame seeds for garnish"
- "Chopped scallions for garnish"
instructions:
- "Cut steak into bite-sized chunks. Let sit at room temperature for 40 minutes; takes edge off cold meat and helps sear evenly."
- "Heat 2 tablespoons butter with oil in well-seasoned cast iron on medium-high. Should shimmer, not smoke immediately."
- "Add some steak batchwise. Overcrowding kills crust formation. Brown on all sides until you hear steady sizzle and meat releases easily from pan; about 4-6 minutes total per batch. Transfer to resting plate."
- "Lower heat to medium, melt remaining 2 tablespoons butter. Stir in garlic and ginger. Cook until aromatic, about 90 seconds. Don’t let garlic burn; watch closely."
- "Whisk brown sugar, soy sauce, rice vinegar, and chili paste in a bowl; pour into pan. Sauce will bubble gently. Simmer 2-3 minutes until sugar dissolves and flavors meld."
- "Mix arrowroot with small amount of water or rice vinegar to slurry. Stream into simmering sauce while stirring vigorously. Sauce thickens quickly; remove from heat at first glossy coat—takes around 1-2 minutes."
- "Return steak bites, toss vigorously to coat and warm through. Heat only until steak glistens with sauce but stays tender."
- "Plate. Sprinkle with black sesame seeds and scallions for color and crunch."
- "Serve immediately. Crunch of sear contrasted by sticky, spicy sauce."
- "If garlic threatens to burn, toss pan off heat briefly. Arrowroot must never boil hard or sauce breaks down."
- "Chunks can vary—trim fat for leaner bites or marbled for richness. Substituting chili adds heat and complexity; pineapple juice swap keeps acidity but cuts sweetness."
