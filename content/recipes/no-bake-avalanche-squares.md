ingredients:
- "3 cups crispy rice cereal"
- "1 cup marshmallow bits"
- "9 oz white chocolate chips"
- "¾ cup almond butter"
- "½ cup dried cherries"
- "½ cup chopped pecans"
- "Optional sea salt for topping"
instructions:
- "==="
- "Line two large trays with parchment. Flat, no wrinkles. Set aside."
- "Mix rice cereal, marshmallow bits, cherries, pecans. Use rubber spatula. Stir gently—don’t crush cereals."
- "In microwave-safe bowl, melt white chocolate in 25-second bursts. Stir between each. Look for glossiness, thick but pourable. Avoid grainy or burnt smell—stop early if needed."
- "Add almond butter while chocolate still warm. Stir until fully blended. Should be creamy but not too thin. If too stiff, nuke 10 seconds, stir. Repeat carefully."
- "Pour chocolate-almond blend over cereal mix. Stir swiftly to coat every piece. Texture sticky, glossy. Immediate work, do not wait or marshmallows harden separately."
- "Scoop mixture with 1 tablespoon spoon onto trays. No need to shape perfectly. It’s sticky, messy, that’s expected."
- "Sprinkle light pinch sea salt atop each mound if preferred. Adds savory lift against white chocolate’s sweetness."
- "Refrigerate 25-35 minutes. Not longer or texture veers dry and crumbly. Blocks should be firm yet yielding. Tap surface—if tacky, chill longer."
- "Remove from parchment using offset spatula or gently lift edges. Store airtight in cool place momentarily, but best fresh within 2 days."
- "Feedback, tweaks, or war stories welcome. Adapt nuts, dried fruits, or nut butters as pantry dictates."
