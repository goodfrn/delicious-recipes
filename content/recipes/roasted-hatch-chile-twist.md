ingredients:
- "10 large Hatch green chiles or 10 Poblano peppers"
- "2 tablespoons avocado oil or grapeseed oil"
- "Plastic sandwich bags large enough for all chiles"
- "Salt to taste"
- "Optional twist: 2 garlic cloves, whole"
- "Optional twist: 1 teaspoon smoked paprika"
instructions:
- "Throw oil and garlic cloves into a bowl, set aside."
- "Heat grill or oven broiler on high. If stove top available, flames ok but slow. Grill recommended for quick blistering."
- "Place chiles directly on grill grates or baking sheet under broiler. Arrange with any green chile or poblano."
- "Wait for skin to bubble, pop, and blacken here and there. Listen for soft crackles like fireworks; the chiles are bursting their skin."
- "Turn every 3-4 minutes to get all sides evenly blistered. Total roast time about 15–20 minutes, depending on heat source intensity."
- "Remove chiles, toss into plastic bag with garlic cloves, seal tightly. Let steam sit for 12 to 18 minutes—don’t skip this. Steam loosens that tough skin bond."
- "Put on nitrile gloves or any plastic gloves before handling hot chiles to avoid burns and staining."
- "Peel skin by hand; it should slide off without scraping if blistered enough. Use paring knife gently only if stubborn parts remain."
- "Slice chile lengthwise, pull out seeds and discard stems. Scrape seeds out carefully to avoid ruining chile flesh. Seeds scorch if left on during roasting."
- "Optional: sprinkle smoked paprika over peeled chiles for a smoky aroma, fold in oil and garlic for infused flavor punch."
- "Lay chiles flat on paper towels, pat dry gently. Too wet, and chiles steam further—undesired sogginess."
- "Ready for any green chile recipe or use them as charred toppings or sauces base. Keep refrigerated if not used immediately."
- "Common mistake: rushing peeling. If skin sticks, bag for another 5 minutes or reheat briefly."
- "Glow of roasted chile not just color but aroma. Woodsy, vegetal, creeping heat, subtle garlic sweetness if added. Use your nose to judge readiness."

ingredientsNote: "Choose fresh large Hatch green chiles or substitute with poblano or Anaheim varieties if unavailable; poblano offers milder but earthy flavor, Anaheim brings more gentle heat. Avoid small, thin-skinned chilies—they blister differently and dry out fast. Oils with higher smoke points like avocado or grapeseed help form crisp skins without burning. Garlic is an optional partner, roasted in the bag to infuse mild pungency, not overpower. Smoked paprika adds depth and an earthy shadow post-peeling without adding heat—great for balancing vibrant chile zing. Plastic bags must seal well to trap steam; avoid paper towels for this steaming phase. Gloves protect hands from burning and stubborn chile oil stains—rinse hand oils before cooking for better grip peeling. If you dislike seeds, pre-roast splitting is optional but risks moisture loss and uneven blistering."
instructionsNote: "Key is blistering skin uniformly—don’t rush turning or the chiles will char unevenly, risking burnt patches stuck to flesh. Listen for muffled pops and cracks while roasting; when skin blacks sporadically and bubbles, it’s blistered. Over-roasting shrinks flesh, under-roasting means stubborn peeling. Transfer immediately to sealed plastic bag for 12-18 minutes—steam loosens that tough, fibrous skin making peeling near fail-proof. Peeling soon after steaming while chiles still warm is easier; cold chili skins tighten. Use plastic gloves to peel without burning or oils spreading to eyes later. Remove seeds with paring knife, scraping gently—seeds are bitter and tough after roasting, can alter texture. Pat dried chiles before storage to prevent soggy flesh. Use residual oil and garlic for flavoring or discard garlic if desired. Reheat briefly for stuck skins or double-bag steaming if necessary."
