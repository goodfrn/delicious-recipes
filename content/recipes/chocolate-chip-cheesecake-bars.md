ingredients:
- "9 ounces softened cream cheese"
- "3/4 cup granulated sugar"
- "1 large egg"
- "1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract"
- "1 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1/4 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1/4 teaspoon salt"
- "1/2 cup coconut oil softened (instead of butter)"
- "1/3 cup brown sugar"
- "1/2 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips"
instructions:
- "Rise oven to 345°F, give it time to stabilize. Line a 9x9 inch pan with parchment to avoid sticky messes later."
- "Mix dry stuff: flour, baking soda, salt—whisk to blend evenly. Separate half the chips."
- "In bowl, cream softened coconut oil with brown sugar until grainy yet coming together. Fold half the dry mix in with half the chocolate chips. Should be pliable but dense."
- "Press that cookie dough mix firmly into pan bottom. Press hard to avoid air pockets or crumbling after baking."
- "In mixer bowl, whip cream cheese on medium-high until fluffy, nearly pale. This step crucial to avoid dense filling. Slowly add sugar, then egg and vanilla, beating until just smooth—don’t overmix or trap too much air or filling cracks."
- "Pour filling evenly over cookie base layer. Level with spatula to avoid thin spots that brown unevenly."
- "Crumble remaining cookie dough on top in chunks; random sizes okay. Helps bake evenly, melts in spots, adds texture."
- "Bake 28 to 38 minutes. Watch edges for golden brown start, center should be almost set but slight jiggle remains. Sounds of bubbles slowing, cheesecake firms. Not too done or drying on edges."
- "Pull out, set on wire rack to cool untouched. Don’t cut hot—wait full cool or chill if you want cleaner bars."
- "Cut into 16 bars; expect edges sturdier, center soft but set. Store airtight to keep edges from hardening."
