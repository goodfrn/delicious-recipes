ingredients:
- "2 pounds russet potatoes peeled and sliced 3 mm thick"
- "1 medium shallot finely diced"
- "3 tablespoons vegetable oil"
- "1 tablespoon rendered pork fat or lard"
- "1 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper"
- "¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper optional"
instructions:
- "=== Preparation ==="
- "Wash potatoes thoroughly. Peel using a sharp peeler, aim for uniform 3 mm slices—too thick slows cooking; thin shards crisp better."
- "Dice shallot finely. Keep ready."
- "=== Cooking ==="
- "Heat large cast iron skillet over medium heat. Add vegetable oil and pork fat together; wait for shimmering surface but no smoking."
- "Place potatoes evenly in a single layer. Should hear a gentle sizzle, not a roar. Scatter diced shallots on top."
- "Cover with lid. Let steam-fry undisturbed around 9 minutes. Look for bottoms turning translucent, edges curling slightly."
- "Remove lid. Sprinkle salt, pepper, and cayenne over top. Flip potatoes carefully with spatula. Some slices might stick; loosen gently."
- "Continue cooking uncovered around 12 minutes. Flip once or twice more. Watch for golden crust, potatoes tender when pierced."
- "Drain briefly on paper towels if oil pools. Serve immediately while crisp."
- "=== Tips and Notes ==="
- "Cast iron retains heat evenly—helps with even browning and prevents soggy spots."
- "Rendered pork fat ramps flavor; substitute with butter for richness, or garlic-infused oil for aroma twist."
- "Shallots cook quicker than onions; avoid browning too early or can burn—add during initial cooking."
- "Too thick slices mean raw middles; too thin risk bruising. Aim for consistent slices."
- "If potatoes stick, lower heat slightly and use metal spatula to scrape gently."
- "Use russet or Yukon gold—starchy types brown and crisp better than waxy."
- "Cayenne optional, adds heat but jump in cautiously if unsure."
- "Keep pan moderately hot. Flames too high darken edges too fast; too low means sogginess."
