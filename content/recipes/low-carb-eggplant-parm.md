ingredients:
- "1 large eggplant sliced into 1 3/4 - 2 inch thick rounds"
- "1 1/2 tsp fine sea salt (for salting eggplant)"
- "3/4 cup unsweetened almond milk"
- "3 large flax eggs (3 tbsp flaxseed meal + 9 tbsp warm water, mixed and set 10 minutes)"
- "3/4 cup almond meal (sub for breadcrumbs to cut carbs and add nuttiness)"
- "1/2 cup grated parmesan cheese (divided)"
- "1 1/4 cups shredded mozzarella cheese"
- "1 tbsp olive oil (replace vegetable oil for flavor)"
- "1 1/2 cups low-sugar marinara sauce (homemade or store brand with no added sugar)"
- "4 medium zucchinis spiralized into zoodles"
- "2 tbsp chopped fresh basil or parsley for garnish"
instructions:
- "=== Salting Eggplant ==="
- "Slice eggplant about 1 3/4 to 2 inches thick. Salt each side evenly with fine sea salt, about three-quarters teaspoon each side. Use a rack or colander set over the sink or bowl to catch moisture. Let sit for 35 minutes, tops."
- "Visual check: eggplant will release beads of water; flesh should feel firmer but slightly pliable. Salting pulls bitterness, reduces sogginess later. Don’t skip or rinse off salt—brush off dry excess with paper towel. Keeps seasoning but removes grit."
- ""
- "=== Preparing Coating and Sauce ==="
- "Mix flax eggs thoroughly and set aside at least 10 minutes to thicken, resembling a gelatinous binder. Combine almond meal and half parmesan in a shallow plate. Stir well."
- "Whisk almond milk and flax eggs together until smooth. This replaces traditional egg and milk. Almond meal gains flavor, less crumbly than plain breadcrumbs."
- ""
- "=== Frying Eggplant ==="
- "Heat a skillet on medium heat. Add 1 tablespoon olive oil—light but enough to create a shallow sizzle. Dip both sides of eggplant slices into the almond/flax mixture. Allow excess to drip off, then dredge firmly in almond meal/parmesan mix. Press lightly to adhere."
- "Place coated eggplants on wax paper or parchment. Don’t crowd your pan. Fry 2 slices at a time. Place gently and listen for sizzle—should be moderate, not a roar. Flip carefully every 2-3 minutes, look for deep golden brown edges, slight crispiness. Adjust heat down if browning too fast or burning."
- "After frying, place slices on a wire rack over a sheet pan to avoid steam build-up underneath. Repeat, using olive oil as needed but sparingly. Too much oil makes soggy edges, too little and crust won’t form well."
- ""
- "=== Assembling ==="
- "Preheat oven to 380 degrees F (up 5 degrees to compensate for slight frying time reduction). Spread 1 1/4 cups marinara evenly on bottom of casserole dish. Stack or overlap eggplant slices slightly across sauce."
- "Spoon more marinara over each slice, don’t drown it. Generous but balanced. Top with mozzarella and remaining parmesan. Layer a bit more cheese if slices are stacked. Cheese should blanket slices but not slide off."
- ""
- "=== Baking ==="
- "Slide dish into oven for about 23-28 minutes. Watch for bubbling cheese with patches of golden brown. No need to brown strongly or burn. You want gooey, melty cheese that sings a little."
- "If crust edges crisped up too fast, tent loosely with foil mid-bake to keep cheese from burning."
- ""
- "=== Zoodles ==="
- "While baking, spiralize zucchinis and give them a quick salt toss in a colander for 5-7 minutes to release moisture. Pat dry."
- "Sauté zoodles in a hot pan with a splash of olive oil just until warmed through, 2-3 minutes. They should remain slightly firm; mushy defeats purpose."
- ""
- "=== Serving ==="
- "Plate zoodles, nestle eggplant parm slices on top. Sprinkle fresh basil or parsley and a touch more parmesan, if desired. Serve immediately."
- ""
- "Any leftovers: cool completely and refrigerate within 2 hours. Reheat gently covered to keep moisture."
