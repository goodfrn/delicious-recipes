instructions:
- "=== Deep-Dish Pizza ==="
- "Start by lightly spraying the air fryer basket with your chosen nonstick spray. Avoid aerosols to prevent sticky buildup."
- "Place the frozen deep-dish pizza unwrapped in the center of the basket. No foil or microwave disk inserts—they block air circulation and cause sogginess."
- "Mist the pizza’s top sparingly with oil spray. It protects toppings from charring while promoting mild browning."
- "Set temperature to 355°F and air fry for 6 minutes. Listen for the subtle crackle and watch edges rise. Check toppings, rearrange any wayward slices with tongs if needed."
- "Drop temp to 295°F and continue cooking for 3 minutes, checking surface gloss and crust firmness. Cook until crust edge is golden but firm to touch—not burnt or doughy beneath."
- "=== Other Brand Deep-Dish ==="
- "Preheat air fryer basket to 355°F for 3 minutes for even heat."
- "Spray basket lightly, place unwrapped pizza inside."
- "Cook 7 to 9 minutes watching for bubbling cheese and golden edge. Inspect often; pull at first sign of sharp burning smell."
- "Use flat spatula to free pizza gently, avoid scraping bottom."
- "=== Thin-Crust Pizza ==="
- "Spray basket lightly."
- "Center the frozen thin crust pizza without wrapping."
- "Oil spray top lightly to protect toppings from drying out."
- "Cook at 360°F for 4 minutes. Smell toasted crust aroma but not burnt."
- "Rearrange toppings carefully if slid around. Lower temp to 305°F and cook 2 more minutes. Crisp edges should slightly lift—test with tongs."
- "=== Other Brand Thin-Crust ==="
- "Bake at 360°F for 5–7 minutes, watching for dry edges or topping burn."
- "=== French Bread Pizza ==="
- "Spray basket. Center pizza."
- "Light oil spray on top to keep cheese melty but not scorching."
- "Set air fryer to 350°F, cook 5 minutes to meld cheese and crust."
- "Check texture of bread surface, softness on top; continue cooking 2–3 more minutes at 300°F until edges are just crispy and cheese bubbles."
- "=== Other Brand French Bread ==="
- "Cook 7–9 minutes at 350°F. Smell and sight guide doneness."
- "=== Hand-Tossed Pizza ==="
- "Spray basket generously—hand-tossed crust takes slightly longer for crisping."
- "Place pizza in center, spray the top lightly as always."
- "Cook at 310°F for 7 minutes; listen for crackling sounds of crust crisping."
- "If cheese isn’t melty enough, raise temperature to 320°F and cook for 2 additional minutes."
- "=== Other Brand Hand-Tossed ==="
- "Cook at 315°F for 9 minutes. Avoid overbrowning mid-crust by rotating halfway if possible."
- "Remove using a spatula. Sharp edges can shred toppings; patience here pays off."
- "== Notes on Techniques =="
- "Spraying with oil prevents toppings from baking dry or burning under hot air. Non-aerosol sprays reduce buildup. Olive oil or avocado oil sprays tolerated well, grapeseed oil offers higher smoke point if working at higher temps."
- "Adjust times based on pizza size and air fryer brand. Smaller air fryers cook unevenly; small pizzas may need flipping midway."
- "Visual queues trump timers. Look for bubbling cheese, crust edges golden not blackened, toppings visibly warmed but not shrunken into leather."
- "Rearrange toppings mid-cycle when possible. Sometimes pepperoni or mushrooms slide; reset for even cooking."
- "For soggy crust issues, spray basket well and avoid overcrowding. Allow air space underneath."
- "If frozen pizza has extra cheese or thick sauce, add 1–2 minutes at lower temp to avoid burnt top."
- "For crispier crust, finish with 1-minute boost at higher temp after cooking but watch closely."
- "Using spatula to remove pizza avoids scratches and crumb damage. Gentle pries better than brute force."
- "If pizza starts spitting oil, reduce temperature 5–10 degrees to prevent flare ups."
- "Frozen pizza sizes vary. Small single servings faster, family size pizzas just over 9 inches benefit from lower and longer heat cycles."
- "Remove plastic wrap fully before cooking; microwave disks included in packaging often lead to undercooked crust or molten mess inside fryer."
- "Air Fryer models vary. Cosori runs hot–reduce temp 5 degrees; others run cooler."
- "Alternative toppings: swap pepperoni for mushroom slices or roasted peppers for fresher, less oily results."
- "Use timer only as backup. Your nose, eyes, and finger pressure are better judges than blindly relying on minutes."
- "For thicker crusts, add 1–2 minutes at lower temp to ensure thorough cooking inside. Thin crusts rarely need more than 7 minutes total."
- "When reheating leftovers, lower temp and cook 2 to 3 minutes longer for good reheated texture."
