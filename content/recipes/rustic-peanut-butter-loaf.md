ingredients:
- "1 cup maple syrup"
- "3 large whole eggs"
- "1 large egg white"
- "1 cup oat milk"
- "1 1/2 cups natural peanut butter"
- "1 cup all-purpose flour"
- "3/4 cup rolled oats"
- "1 tbsp baking powder"
- "1/2 tsp sea salt"
- "nonstick spray"
- "parchment paper"
instructions:
- "===Preparation==="
- "Slide rack to center. Preheat oven to 345°F. Let it fully come to temp. Nonstick spray on 8×4 loaf pan. Cut parchment to size; loosely fit pan to prevent sticking and crisp edges."
- "===Mix Wet==="
- "In a sturdy blender or food processor, combine oat milk, maple syrup, peanut butter, whole eggs, plus egg white for volume. Pulse 1-2 minutes until thick, glossy, and homogeneous. Listen for motor sound to lighten; this step whips in air, critical for texture."
- "===Add Dry==="
- "Dump in flour, rolled oats, baking powder, salt gradually. Short blasts to integrate fully but do not overmix; stops gluten bumps, keeps crumb tender. For low-powered machines, add dry ingredients in small increments."
- "===Bake==="
- "Scrape batter carefully into prepared pan. Surface wet, lumpy is fine; don’t smooth aggressively. Slide into oven. Expect around 1 hour 15 minutes, but start checking at 1 hour - test center with toothpick: few moist crumbs are fine, raw batter not. Should spring back lightly to touch."
- "===Rest & Cool==="
- "Remove from oven. Leave loaf in pan on cooling rack for 10 minutes. This resting period firms structure preventing collapse or cracking when unmolded. After, gently lift out using parchment edges. Cool completely on wire rack before slicing — warm loaf can gum up knife."
- "===Serving==="
- "Toast slices for extra crunch and aroma. Consider toppings like creamed honey, sliced pears, or a smear of creamy peanut butter. Store wrapped tightly at room temp up to 3 days or freeze slices individually."
- "===Troubleshooting & Tips==="
- "If batter runs thin, add a tablespoon extra flour or oats to thicken. Low oven temps cause gummy centers; use oven thermometer to verify heat. Over-beating leads to tough crumb; pulse carefully. Peanut butter natural oils can separate in fridge – bring loaf to room temperature before serving to restore moisture and flavor."
- "===Variations==="
- "Try almond butter instead of peanut butter for a milder tone. Substitute oat milk with coconut milk for subtle sweetness. Add 1/4 cup chopped roasted peanuts or dark chocolate chunks into batter for texture contrast."
