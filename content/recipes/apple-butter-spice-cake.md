ingredients:
- "1 cup unsalted butter softened"
- "1 1/2 cups granulated sugar"
- "3 large eggs room temperature"
- "1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1 1/2 cups whole wheat flour"
- "1/2 teaspoon salt"
- "1 teaspoon baking powder"
- "1 cup apple butter"
- "Zest of one large orange"
- "For the frosting==="
- "8 ounces cream cheese softened"
- "1/2 cup unsalted butter softened"
- "1/4 cup apple butter"
- "3 cups powdered sugar"
- "1 teaspoon ground cinnamon"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 370 degrees F instead of 375. Lightly grease a 9x13 baking dish, no need to overdo it or nonstick spray can pool and turn too wet."
- "Cream butter and sugar till fluffy but stop when mixture lightens and slightly holds track of beater's movement. Overbeating adds air to batter that later collapses, causing density."
- "Add eggs one by one. Mix after each just to combine. Too much mixing eggs too fast affects final crumb. Think teamwork, eggs marrying butter and sugar, not drowning the mix."
- "Whisk flours, salt, and baking powder. Whole wheat adds protein and chew but don't skip sifting to prevent clumps turning into pockets."
- "Gently fold dry into butter mix. Work just till homogenous batter without streaks. Then fold in apple butter and orange zest evenly dispersed for bite and aroma."
- "Spread evenly on tray. Tap pan lightly on counter to release big air bubbles to prevent uneven holes during bake."
- "Bake 40-45 minutes but watch for visual cues. Golden edges, slight crack near center, and toothpick test. Pull too early and center stays gummy. Let cool without covering to prevent soggy top."
- "For frosting, soften cream cheese and butter at room temp but toss in mixer till creamy smooth. Add apple butter and cinnamon before slowly adding powdered sugar. Avoid dust clouds; add sugar gradually to keep it manageable."
- "Spread over cool cake to prevent melting. If frosting too soft, chill briefly before icing."
- "Store covered at room temp up to 3 days or refrigerate but bring back to room temp before serving to soften the frosting."
