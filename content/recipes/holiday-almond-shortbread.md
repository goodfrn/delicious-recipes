ingredients:
- "1 cup unsalted butter softened"
- "3/4 cup powdered sugar"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1/2 teaspoon coconut extract"
- "1/2 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1/2 cup oat flour"
- "1/4 cup arrowroot powder"
- "1/4 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "Holiday sprinkles"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 320F. Prep 2 pans with parchment. Set aside."
- "Into mixer bowl, toss butter and powdered sugar. Paddle attachment on medium. Whip 2 1/2 minutes till pale and fluffy. Scrape bowl sides."
- "Add vanilla and coconut extracts. Mix 1 minute at medium speed. Aroma shifts, richer notes come out."
- "In separate bowl, blend flours, arrowroot, salt thoroughly. No lumps, dry mixture even."
- "Mixer low speed, add flour mix bit by bit. After each addition, ensure dough smooth, no flour streaks. Slow blending stops gluten overdevelopment."
- "Raise speed medium, beat 35 seconds more. Dough light, pulls from bowl clean."
- "Swap paddle for spatula. Cover bowl, chill 20 minutes. Cooler dough's easier to handle. Too warm equals spreading cookies."
- "Scoop 1 1/2 tablespoon dough with cookie scoop. Roll gently, not too firm. Place on sheets 2 inches apart. Flatten each ball with fork tines crosswise. Around 1 1/2 inches wide. Finger pressure matters—too hard, cookies tough."
- "Sprinkle season’s color on tops. Press lightly so sprinkles stick but keep texture."
- "Bake 12 to 17 minutes. Watch color; cookies stay pale except faint golden edges. Don’t wait for browning! Listen for soft cracking as they set."
- "Cool 10 minutes on sheets. Firm to touch but soft inside. Transfer to rack until fully cooled. Avoid stacking warm cookies — they stick."
- "Store airtight. Refrigerate to preserve freshness. Can freeze in layers separated by parchment."
