ingredients:
- "2 cups unsalted butter softened"
- "1 1/4 cups packed light brown sugar"
- "3/4 cup granulated sugar"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1 tablespoon pure vanilla extract"
- "3 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "1 teaspoon baking soda"
- "2 cups semi-sweet chocolate chips"
- "1 1/2 cups chopped toasted pecans"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 380 degrees F. Line baking sheet with silicon mat or parchment well oiled or greased to prevent sticking."
- "In large bowl cream butter at room temp with sugars until mixture light and fluffy. Avoid overmixing after adding eggs."
- "Add eggs one at a time with vanilla, blend until homogeneous but don't aerate too much. Too much air means cookie will flatten."
- "In separate bowl, sift flour combine salt and baking soda. Gradually add this dry mix into wet batter, scraping bowl edges often. Mixing just until flour disappears."
- "Stir in chips and nuts by hand. Don't overmix - that'll develop gluten, toughening cookies."
- "For denser, chewier texture chill dough minimum 40 minutes or up to 2 hours. Skip and bake at once for thinner cookies with crisp edges."
- "Scoop dough with heaping tablespoon measure. Space cookies with at least 2.5 inches apart - they spread."
- "Bake on middle rack 12 to 14 minutes. Look for edges turning golden brown, centers set but not dry, slight jiggle still visible."
- "Cool cookies on sheet 7 minutes to firm. Then transfer carefully to wire rack to cool completely. Warm cookies more fragile."
- "Store airtight at room temperature. Crisp loses after day two, reheat briefly to restore texture. No fridge needed or texture changes."
