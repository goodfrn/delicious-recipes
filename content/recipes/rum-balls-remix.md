ingredients:
- "6 ounces semi-sweet chocolate chips"
- "¾ cup powdered sugar"
- "3 tablespoons honey"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "3 tablespoons dark rum"
- "½ teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "1½ cups graham cracker crumbs"
- "½ cup unsweetened shredded coconut"
- "½ cup cocoa powder"
- "½ cup crushed freeze-dried strawberries"
instructions:
- "Gently melt chocolate. Use 25-second bursts in microwave, stirring between. Watch closely—chocolate will stiffen if overheated. Melt until shiny, silky. No lumps when stirred—if thick, add a teaspoon water or rum to loosen up."
- "Right after melting, dump chocolate in a large bowl. Add powdered sugar, honey, vanilla, rum, and salt. Stir vigorously. Mix until evenly combined with no clumps—texture slightly thick, shiny, sticky."
- "Next, fold in graham cracker crumbs and shredded coconut slowly. Don't whip or overwork dough. Mix just long enough so crumbs are moistened but still crumbly. Too much mixing = gummy."
- "Cover bowl with plastic wrap. Chill in fridge for 1 to 1 ½ hours. You're after firmness—firm enough for rolling but not rock solid. Cold dough will stiffen but remain pliable."
- "Prep toppings: half cocoa powder, half crushed freeze-dried strawberries. Use separate shallow bowls, about ½ cup each. Chill these too if possible—it helps toppings stick to balls better."
- "Once chilled, scoop dough with tablespoon measure. Roll dough quickly between palms until smooth spheres form. Don’t over-handle—heat from hands melts chocolate, makes rolling messy."
- "Roll each ball immediately in one topping, press gently to coat. Coatings stick better while dough is tacky, not dry or too cold. If balls stick to fingers, chill dough again briefly."
- "Place finished rum balls on parchment-lined tray. Once all rolled and coated, cover loosely and refrigerate in airtight container. Keep chilled until serving."
- "Removing from fridge: pull out 10–15 minutes before eating. Cold rum balls are dense and flavor-muted. Letting them soften a bit releases aromas and makes chewing easier."
- "Tips: Honey adds moisture yet sets candies slower than corn syrup—patience needed. Graham cracker crumbs give coarse crunch compared to cookie crumbs. Coconut adds chew and subtle nuttiness. Freeze-dried strawberries add tartness and color without watering down coating like fresh fruit would."
- "Troubleshooting—chocolate seizes? Add a spoon of vegetable oil before melting next batch. Dough crumbly? Add a splash more rum or honey. Too wet? Add more crumbs. If toppings fail to stick, dampen balls slightly with water or rum before rolling."
