ingredients:
- "1 lb broccolini stalks ends trimmed"
- "1 teaspoon toasted sesame oil"
- "1/3 cup white miso paste"
- "2 tablespoons apricot jam"
- "1 tablespoon low sodium soy sauce"
- "1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger"
- "1/4 teaspoon smoked paprika powder"
- "1 tablespoon toasted sesame seeds"
- "Zest of 1 lime"
- "Salt and black pepper to taste"
- "Cooking spray or neutral oil spray"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 425 degrees F. Use rimmed sheet pan, spray lightly with oil so broccolini doesn’t stick or steam. Spread stalks out in one layer. Sap dries better with spacing. Spray tops lightly, season with smoked paprika, salt, and fresh ground pepper."
- "Roast for about 6-7 minutes. Listen—the ticking sizzle of water evaporating signals moisture loss. Pull pan, check color. Tips should crisp slightly, stalks flex without snapping rigid. If dull green and floppy, needs more time. Flip each bunch carefully."
- "Return pan to oven 5-6 minutes more, roast until you hear quiet crackle as sugars caramelize. Watch edges—tiny char means flavor. Too black burnt tastes bitter. Don’t rush this stage."
- "While roasting, whisk miso paste, apricot jam, soy sauce, ginger, and lime zest in bowl. It should be thick, glossy. If jam sticks too hard, warm gently to loosen. Fresh ginger adds spicy brightness replacing garlic—won’t mask delicate broccolini."
- "Toast sesame seeds separately in dry skillet over medium heat until nutty scent rises and color deepens, 2-3 minutes. Stir constantly to avoid burning. Sets apart from cold, raw sprinkle flavor."
- "Remove broccolini, transfer to serving dish. Immediately drizzle miso glaze over hot vegetables and toss gently but thoroughly; heat softens glaze for full coverage. Taste for salt adjustment, add more lime zest or soy if needed."
- "Serve right away—textures shift as glaze cools and absorbs moisture. Crunchy edges, tender centers, tangy-sweet ginger notes with warm umami. If made ahead, reheat briefly in skillet or oven to refresh glaze snap."
