ingredients:
- "1/2 cup unsalted butter softened"
- "3/4 cup creamy peanut butter"
- "1 1/2 cups finely crushed pretzels substituted for graham cracker crumbs"
- "1 cup brown sugar firmly packed replacing powdered sugar"
- "1/4 tsp fine sea salt reduced from original"
- "1/2 tsp smoked paprika addition for subtle heat"
- "1 1/2 cups semi-sweet chocolate chips"
- "2 tbsp natural peanut butter divided"
- "Nonstick cooking spray to grease pan"
- "Parchment paper or aluminum foil for lining"
instructions:
- "Pre-chill the butter and peanut butter in the bowl for 5 minutes so easier to blend smooth without melting."
- "Line an 8x8 inch baking pan tightly with parchment or foil. Spray lightly with cooking spray. This prevents sticking and mess later."
- "Using a stand mixer or hand mixer on medium speed, cream chilled butter and peanut butter until completely creamy. No lumps."
- "Add crushed pretzels first. Mix so evenly combined but don't over beat or pretzels lose crunch. Texture should feel coarse."
- "Add brown sugar slowly with salt and smoked paprika. Fold gently to just combine. Dough will be crumbly but sticks when pressed."
- "Press dough firmly into bottom of lined pan with your fingers or flat-bottomed glass. Chill in fridge for 30-40 minutes to set base. This keeps crumb from sliding."
- "Meanwhile, melt chocolate chips gently in microwave. Use 20 second bursts instead of 30 for more control. Stir after every interval. Stop when chips nearly melted, then stir till smooth. Avoid overheating which causes grainy or burnt taste."
- "Mix in remaining 2 tbsp peanut butter into melted chocolate for glossiness and richer mouthfeel. Stir well."
- "Spread chocolate layer evenly over chilled peanut dough. Tap pan lightly to settle chocolate and remove bubbles."
- "Chill in fridge minimum 3 hours until firm to touch. Bars should snap cleanly when cut. If still soft or sticky, additional 30 minutes refrigeration needed."
- "Use very sharp serrated knife scored into 1 inch squares. Warm knife briefly under hot water and dry between cuts for cleaner edges."
- "Store bars airtight in fridge. If kept at room temp, chocolate softens and pretzels absorb moisture, losing crunch."
- "To rescue crumbly dough that won't come together, add a teaspoon of milk or cream gradually. If chocolate seizes while melting, stir in tablespoon of vegetable oil or butter to smooth out."
