ingredients:
- "1/3 cup vegetable oil"
- "1/2 cup brown sugar"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1 cup ripe mashed banana (about 2 medium bananas)"
- "1/4 cup Biscoff spread"
- "1/4 cup natural peanut butter"
- "1/3 cup Greek yogurt (plain, full-fat)"
- "1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract"
- "1/4 cup milk (whole or 2%)"
- "1 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1/2 cup whole wheat flour"
- "1 teaspoon baking powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1/4 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "For glaze: 1 cup powdered sugar"
- "2 tablespoons almond milk"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon"
- "Crushed Biscoff cookies for garnish"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven 340°F. Lower-middle rack position. Lower than usual to preserve crust color and slow browning."
- "Grease light-colored 9×5 inch pan thoroughly with softened butter. Dust lightly with flour; tap out excess. Parchment alternative, cut with overhang for easy bread lift. Avoid dark pans - burn risk."
- "In large bowl, combine oil, brown sugar, eggs, mashed banana, peanut butter, Biscoff spread, Greek yogurt, vanilla, and milk. Whisk vigorously until just smooth but not overmixed. Air bubbles help rise."
- "In separate bowl, sift together flours, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Dry mix distributes leavening evenly; no clumps allowed."
- "Add dry ingredients to wet in 2 stages. Fold with rubber spatula—gentle, just until streaks vanish. Overfolding toughens crumb."
- "Pour batter into prepared pan. Level top with spatula. Slightly glossy, thick texture. Should smell sweet—banana-vanilla notes apparent."
- "Bake 50-60 minutes. Watch for golden brown edges and firm top springing back when pressed lightly. Toothpick should come out with few moist crumbs but no raw batter."
- "Cool at least 30 minutes in pan on wire rack. Bread shrinks slightly when cooled, making release easier."
- "Whisk powdered sugar, almond milk, and cinnamon until thick but spreadable. Adjust milk quantity to control consistency."
- "Spread glaze evenly over cooled bread. Sprinkle crushed Biscoff cookies on top while glaze still wet for extra crunch and spice notes."
- "Slice crosswise using serrated knife. Clean cuts maintain shape, minimize crumbs."
