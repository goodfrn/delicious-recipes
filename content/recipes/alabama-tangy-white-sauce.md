ingredients:
- "1 1/2 cup mayonnaise"
- "3/4 cup apple cider vinegar"
- "1 tablespoon horseradish freshly grated"
- "1 teaspoon dry mustard seed crushed"
- "1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika ground"
- "1/4 teaspoon granulated garlic fresh grated garlic substitute"
- "1 teaspoon white pepper freshly ground"
- "1/2 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "1 teaspoon honey optional sweetener"
instructions:
- "Start with mayonnaise in medium bowl. Not too cold from fridge so it mixes easier."
- "Whisk in apple cider vinegar slow, see sauce thin out but keep creamy. Stop before it separates—aim for velvety texture."
- "Add grated horseradish. Fresh but not overwhelming. Smell sharp, sting on nose but balanced with mayo."
- "Crush mustard seeds finely in mortar or spice grinder; add now. Adds tickle texture; skip if no tools, use dry mustard powder instead but no crunch."
- "Stir in smoked paprika last, dusty aroma rounds the sharpness. Garlic powder replaced by very finely grated fresh garlic—use sparingly or sauce turns bitter."
- "Season with kosher salt and white pepper gradually, tasting after each addition. White pepper benefits those who want subtle heat without black flecks."
- "Optional drizzle honey if sauce tastes too acidic; balances but don't overdo or flavor swings sweet."
- "Cover airtight. Refrigerate minimum 50 minutes, up to 8 hours max. Flavors meld, sauce thickens but can thin on sitting—stir before using."
- "Check texture by dipping spoon; sauce should coat but drip slowly, neither gluey nor watery."
- "If sauce separates, re-whisk with dash warm water slowly, then refrigerate again. Avoid overheating mayo base as it breaks down."
- "Serve cold or room temp on grilled chicken, pork, or as dipping sauce. Watch for palate fatigue—acidity can overwhelm if left long uncovered."
- "Store up to 7 days in sealed jar. If off smell or texture—discard."
