ingredients:
- "1 cup reserved pear juice or fill to 1 cup with water"
- "1 small box lime flavored gelatin (3 ounces)"
- "4 ounces mascarpone cheese at room temperature"
- "2 tablespoons heavy cream divided"
- "2 cups canned pears drained and mashed"
- "1 cup cold heavy cream"
- "1 teaspoon fresh lemon zest"
- "Non-stick spray for mold"
- "Whipped topping and maraschino cherries for garnish (optional)"
instructions:
- "=== Prepare gelatin base ==="
- "Reserve pear juice, measure about ¾ cup, top with water to make 1 cup total liquid. Warm slowly in a small saucepan over medium heat. Not boiling—just a gentle simmer, look for little bubbles forming, steam rising is good."
- "Whisk in lime gelatin powder completely until dissolved; no grains visible. Smell sharp citrus notes, color bright green. Let cool on counter 6-7 minutes until warm but not hot. Surface cools, no steam, watch for jelly starting to thicken around edges—ready to mix."
- "=== Cream cheese and gelatin mixture ==="
- "In medium bowl, beat mascarpone with 1 tbsp heavy cream on medium speed until fluffy and consistent. No lumps—if texture grainy, beat longer, room temp cheese crucial here."
- "Turn mixer to low. Very slowly stream gelatin into cheese mixture. Why slow? To avoid lumps and incorporate evenly. Mix just until uniform. Cover tight, refrigerate 35 minutes or until thicker but not jiggly or set solid. Slight jiggle means perfect stage."
- "=== Pear and cream assembly ==="
- "Mash pears thoroughly with fork or ricer; no big chunks. Stir in lemon zest—adds fresh brightness against sweetness. In chilled bowl, whip remaining 1 tbsp heavy cream on high speed to stiff peaks. Peak test: lift whisk, cream stands straight up, no droop."
- "Gently fold pears into whipped cream. Folding = light, deliberate strokes lifting from bottom over top to keep aeration. No heavy stirring or deflate."
- "Once gelatin mixture ready, fold in pear-whipped cream blend also gently. Aim for uniform color but light and airy texture maintained."
- "=== Mold and chill ==="
- "Spray mold well with non-stick spray—critical to ensure clean release later. Spoon mixture carefully, avoid trapping air bubbles. Cover tightly with plastic wrap, chill 3 hours minimum. Longer you chill, firmer it is; overnight fine too. Don’t unmold too soon—too soft will break."
- "=== Serving ==="
- "To release, dip mold base in warm water 10 seconds max. Warm enough to soften edges but not melt gelatin interior. Quickly invert onto serving dish, gentle taps if stubborn. Decorate with whipped topping dollops and maraschino cherries as desired."
- "Store leftovers refrigerated covered. Best eaten within 24 hours for texture integrity."
