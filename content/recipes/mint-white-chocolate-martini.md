instructions:
- "Start with cold glasses. Dip rims into melted white chocolate until coated but not dripping. Then immediately press into crushed peppermint or sugar until evenly covered. Chill briefly so chocolate firms. If rimmed glass isn't your thing, skip this or use plain sugar."
- "Load a shaker with plenty of ice—enough to keep things chilled but not overwhelmed. Pour vodka, mint white chocolate liqueur, white crème de menthe, and whole milk. The milk swap adds creaminess but stays lighter than half-and-half."
- "Seal tightly. Shake hard for about 25 seconds until frost forms on shaker surface and a faint minty aroma escapes. Proper chilling and dilution happen here. Over-shaking dilutes too much—be alert to tactile chill, not just time."
- "Strain carefully into prepped glass using fine mesh if available to catch any ice shards. Serve immediately while surface slightly silky but cold."
- "If you felt it leaned too sweet, add a tiny splash of fresh lime juice next time to brighten. Or swap milk for coconut milk if you want a tropical twist."
- "Leftover liqueur stores fine, but shake well before using again as some separation can occur."
