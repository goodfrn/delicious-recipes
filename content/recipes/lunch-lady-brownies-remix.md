ingredients:
- "1 cup coconut oil melted instead of butter"
- "3/4 cup cocoa powder (reduce 10%)"
- "1 cup all-purpose flour plus 1/4 cup rolled oats (for chew)"
- "1/4 tsp salt"
- "3/4 cup maple syrup exchanged for granulated sugar"
- "3 large eggs"
- "1 tsp vanilla extract"
- "For frosting:"
- "1/2 cup unsalted butter softened"
- "2 tbsp almond milk substituted for cow milk"
- "2 cups powdered sugar"
- "2 tbsp cocoa powder"
- "1/2 tsp vanilla extract"
- "1/2 tsp espresso powder (optional but recommended)"
instructions:
- "=== Brownie Base ==="
- "Use a medium bowl. Whisk melted coconut oil and 3/4 cup cocoa powder together. Look for uniform sheen, no clumps. Important—oil binds plus cocoa activates chocolate flavor."
- "Add 1 cup flour, 1/4 cup oats, salt next. Mix until crumbly but evenly distributed. Don’t overmix here; dry crumbly is the key stage to know you’re not breaking gluten yet."
- "Crack eggs one at a time adding vanilla extract. Fold gently stirring until batter glistens and smooths out from clumps. It should hold together but not be runny."
- "Preheat oven to 350F, line 9x13 pan with parchment. Pour batter in. Spread evenly with a spatula. Surface should be rustic but no massive lumps."
- "=== Bake ==="
- "Slide into oven and watch. Crackly top forms at roughly 18 minutes. After 20-22 minutes, test center toothpick: moist crumbs sticking means done. Wet batter means needs 2 more min; dry toothpick too far, next time reduce time a smidge."
- "Pull out and let cool on wire rack 30 minutes minimum before frosting."
- "=== Frosting ==="
- "In chilled bowl, whisk butter soft to creamy. Slowly add powdered sugar, cocoa powder, espresso powder stirring continuously. Add almond milk a tablespoon at a time to reach spreadable thick fudge consistency. Avoid runny bowl."
- "Smell aroma change—espresso cuts sweetness, cocoa deepens rich scent."
- "If frosting too sweet, a pinch of salt balances flavor."
- "=== Final Touch ==="
- "Spread frosting evenly over brownies after they cool. Frosting should hold shape and stay thick. Don’t apply hot brownies or frosting will melt into goo."
- "Allow frosted brownies to rest 1 to 2 hours at room temp or overnight in fridge. This sets flavors, firms frosting."
- "Slice with serrated knife in warm water to avoid drag and crumb mess."
