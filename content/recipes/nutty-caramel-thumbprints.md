ingredients:
- "1¾ cups all-purpose flour"
- "1 teaspoon baking powder"
- "½ teaspoon salt"
- "¼ cup unsweetened cocoa powder"
- "1 cup unsalted butter softened"
- "½ cup granulated sugar"
- "¾ cup packed light brown sugar"
- "1 large egg"
- "3 tablespoons Greek yogurt plain substitute for extra moisture"
- "2 teaspoons vanilla extract"
- "1 cup chopped almonds toasted instead of pecans"
- "1 large egg white reserved for coating"
- "30 caramel candies unwrapped"
- "2 tablespoons heavy cream"
- "½ teaspoon flaky sea salt added to caramel"
- "1 tablespoon bourbon optional twist for caramel"
- "7 ounces semisweet chocolate chips"
- "¼ cup shredded toasted coconut flakes sprinkled last"
instructions:
- "Set oven to 355 degrees Fahrenheit. Slightly above standard for crisper edges without overbaking center."
- "Line baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone mats. Helps clean release no mess."
- "Whisk together flour baking powder salt and cocoa powder in a bowl until combined evenly to avoid clumps."
- "In a large bowl, beat butter granulated sugar brown sugar at medium-high speed until pale fluffy about 2 ½ minutes. Butter must be softened but not melting for aeration."
- "Add egg plus Greek yogurt and vanilla beat until incorporated and smooth. Yogurt replaces yolk for gentler crumb, adds subtle tang."
- "Fold dry ingredients into wet with a spatula or mixer on low just till combined. Avoid overbeating or dough toughens."
- "Wrap dough tightly in plastic. Chill minimum 1 hour but up to 2 preferred for easier scooping and less spreading during baking."
- "Combine toasted chopped almonds in a shallow bowl. Toasting intensifies flavor and crunch. Reserve."
- "Whisk reserved egg white until frothy but not stiff peaks. Use quickly for coating dough balls, helps almonds stick and creates thin shell texture."
- "Use a 1 ½ tablespoon cookie scoop or measure level spoon, roll balls gently between palms to form smooth spheres. Dip each into egg white then press lightly into almonds covering fully."
- "Arrange balls 2 ½ inches apart on sheets. Proper spacing prevents merging as they expand."
- "Press center of each ball with back of teaspoon, creating deep indentation nearly halfway through dough height. Critical for holding caramel later, no shallow dives allowed."
- "Bake 12 to 14 minutes. Look for edges to turn dry and set with faint darkening but center still soft and puffy. Don’t overbake or caramel melts unpredictably and cookie dries out."
- "Immediately press centers again with teaspoon to re-establish wells if they puffed up too much while hot."
- "Meanwhile heat caramel candies with heavy cream sea salt and bourbon in microwave safe bowl stirring every 20 seconds until melted and smooth. Watch closely prevents burning.
"
- "Spoon caramel into each pressed center. The sea salt cuts sweetness and bourbon adds depth. Let cookies cool fully at room temperature until caramel firms slightly and dough sets completely."
- "Melt chocolate chips in microwave 30 seconds, stir, repeat until melted and velvety. Drizzle thin streams back and forth over cooled cookies. Immediately sprinkle toasted coconut flakes on top for texture contrast and brightness."
- "Let set uncovered or refrigerate briefly to firm chocolate drizzle. Store in airtight container between layers of parchment."
