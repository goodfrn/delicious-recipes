ingredients:
- "2 large boneless skinless chicken breasts cut horizontally to 4 thinner fillets"
- "1 teaspoon fine kosher salt plus more to taste"
- "½ teaspoon ground black pepper"
- "2 tablespoons olive oil divided"
- "2 tablespoons unsalted butter divided"
- "10 ounces crimini mushrooms thinly sliced"
- "¼ teaspoon kosher salt for mushrooms"
- "4 cloves garlic minced"
- "1 ½ cups Marsala wine dry or semi-dry"
- "⅔ cup low sodium chicken broth"
- "¼ cup heavy cream"
- "2 teaspoons cornstarch"
- "1 teaspoon cold water"
- "1 teaspoon fresh lemon juice (twist addition)"
instructions:
- "Halve chicken breasts horizontally creating thinner cutlets. Pat dry thoroughly to ensure searing not steaming. Season both sides with ¾ teaspoon kosher salt and ½ teaspoon black pepper."
- "Heat large rimmed skillet over medium heat until shimmering but not smoking. Add 1 tablespoon olive oil plus 1 tablespoon butter. Butter should foam quickly."
- "Toss in mushrooms, sprinkle ¼ teaspoon kosher salt. Mushrooms should sizzle immediately. Stir occasionally for even browning. After about 5 minutes, edges caramelized, moisture evaporated, remove mushrooms to bowl. Set aside."
- "Increase heat to medium-high. Add remaining 1 tablespoon olive oil and 1 tablespoon butter to pan. Wait until butter is melted and foaming."
- "Add chicken cutlets. Sear undisturbed for about 3-5 minutes per side until golden with crisp edges. Thickest part should resist slight press but not too firm. Transfer chicken to plate and tent with foil to rest."
- "Drop heat to low. Add minced garlic, stirring often for about 30 seconds until aromatic but not browned—burning garlic imparts bitterness."
- "Pour in Marsala wine gradually. Scrape brown bits stuck to pan using wooden spoon—those carry deeper flavor. Once all wine is in, add chicken broth."
- "Turn heat back to medium. Let simmer vigorously until liquid reduces by roughly a third, around 3-4 minutes. Sauce will thicken slightly."
- "Whisk heavy cream with lemon juice, pour slowly into pan while stirring. Add the last tablespoon of butter to enrich sauce and give it that glossy finish. Taste here, adjust salt sparingly—Marsala and broth contribute salty notes."
- "Mix cornstarch with cold water until no lumps. Slowly drizzle slurry into simmering sauce while constantly stirring. Sauce should thicken and cling to spoon. Avoid clumps; if thickens too fast, add splash broth."
- "Return mushrooms and chicken to pan nestling them in sauce. Warm through 1-2 minutes. Chicken should remain juicy with sauce coating every crevice."
- "Serve hot with sauce spooned over generously. A grind of black pepper on top if desired. No resting longer or sauce tightens and loses shine."
