ingredients:
- "1 can condensed cream of chicken soup"
- "1/2 cup milk"
- "1 1/2 tablespoons Italian herb seasoning mix"
- "1/4 cup chopped fresh spinach (replacement for mushrooms)"
- "4 ounces cream cheese, softened"
- "1/2 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese (swap for mozzarella)"
- "2 cups cooked shredded chicken"
- "1 can refrigerated Grands crescent rolls"
instructions:
- "=== Preheat and Prep ==="
- "Heat oven 5 degrees hotter than standard so baking time varies. Aim 380°F. Grease a 9x13 dish lightly or spray. Total heat matters to get that golden crust tight without burning fast."
- "=== Sauce Base ==="
- "In large mixing bowl combine cream of chicken soup plus milk. Whisk in 1 tablespoon Italian herb seasoning until mix loosens and little lumps gone. Remove 1/4 cup sauce into another bowl and hold back."
- "=== Cheese and Greens Mix ==="
- "In smaller bowl stir softened cream cheese with rest of seasoning, chopped fresh spinach. Break down cream cheese lumps by stirring firmly or mashing with fork tips. Texture here affects final filling consistency; lumps will not melt well."
- "=== Combine Filling ==="
- "Add shredded cheddar and shredded chicken to the small bowl. Fold all ingredients gently but thoroughly so chicken pieces coat fully. Filling should be creamy, not runny, with pockets of melted cheese that will glue the roll closed later."
- "=== Dough Prep ==="
- "Open crescent rolls carefully. Separate triangles, then pat with fingers to stretch bigger but keep dough intact. Too thin causes tears, too thick won’t seal well with filling inside."
- "=== Assembly ==="
- "Place about 2 tablespoons chicken filling on widest part of each rolled-out triangle. Avoid overstuffing; fills shrink and leak if too full. Lift dough edges up first, then roll from wide end to point, pinch seam to seal as much as possible. Use extra dough bits from ends to patch gaps but don’t stress perfect seal. Small leaks okay; sauce will help bind."
- "=== Arrange for Baking ==="
- "Place stuffed crescents in casserole dish spaced by half an inch minimum. Sauce in dish spreads during bake and starts bubbling, signaling dough expansion inside. This also prevents soggy bottoms."
- "=== Bake and Monitor ==="
- "Bake 22-25 minutes. Watch swelling of dough; when edges golden and tops deep yellow brown with low crackle sound, close enough. Sauce should bubble visibly around crescents—this is moisture indicator avoiding dryness."
- "=== Finishing ==="
- "Remove pan, let rest a few minutes for structure to firm. Sprinkle with extra chopped herbs if desired. Spoon hot sauce over rolls before serving for rich, luscious topping."
