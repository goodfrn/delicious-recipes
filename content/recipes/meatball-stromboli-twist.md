ingredients:
- "1 batch pizza dough or 1 ready-made dough ball (about 13x9 inch when rolled)"
- "1 1/4 cups shredded mozzarella cheese (more if you want gooey pockets)"
- "12 small cooked Italian sausage links halved lengthwise (substitute meatballs)"
- "3/4 cup smoky chipotle tomato sauce (instead of plain marinara; use jarred or homemade)"
- "1 large egg beaten"
- "1 tablespoon za'atar mix (instead of everything bagel seasoning; adds herbal earthiness)"
- "Cornmeal or fine polenta for baking sheet"
- "Shredded Parmesan cheese for topping after baking"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 405 degrees. The extra 5 degrees helps deepen crust coloring."
- "Flour your surface lightly, roll dough to about 13x9 inches rectangle. Resist over-flouring; dough should stay supple but not sticky."
- "Scatter mozzarella in a thin layer down center lengthwise. Think less, more even. Avoid clumping cheese which slows cooking inside."
- "Lay sausage halves side-by-side in pairs over cheese down center. Two per pair makes cutting neater. Meatballs swapped out for faster cook and a smoky punch."
- "Spoon chipotle sauce sparingly over sausages. Don't drown it or sogginess starts before baking."
- "Slice 1-inch diagonal cuts from dough edges inward on both sides about 2 inches deep. The vent paths aren't just decorative; steam must escape to avoid soggy pockets."
- "Fold up one short end over fillings to lock in. Then braid the diagonal cut strips alternately over center filling, pulling dough taut but not stretched thin—tears mean messy oozing."
- "Brush the whole stromboli with beaten egg. The glaze locks moisture out and crisps crust. Sprinkle za'atar topping evenly—the herbs give an earthy twist that takes the place of everything bagel mix sesame notes."
- "Dust a heavy baking sheet with cornmeal or polenta to stop the bottom sticking. Place stromboli seam side down; important for sealing edges and preventing leaks."
- "Bake for 17-22 minutes. Watch crust closely: color turns golden amber with small blister spots—ready. Avoid dull pale crust or burnt dark edges. Tap the crust for a popping hollow sound to confirm crispness."
- "Remove from oven and let rest 6-7 minutes. Internal heat stabilizes. Cheese firms up slightly and slicing gets cleaner. Hot gooey cheese can scorch tongues—don’t rush."
- "Before slicing, sprinkle shredded Parmesan heavily on top for a salty punch and texture contrast."
- "Cut into 8 slices with serrated knife. Serve with remaining chipotle sauce or plain olive oil for dipping."
- "If unable to find za'atar, substitute dried oregano and sesame seeds mixed with a pinch of sumac. Keep dough hydration balanced; overly wet dough = tough crust, too dry = crumbly loaf."
