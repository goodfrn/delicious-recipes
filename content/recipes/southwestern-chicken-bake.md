ingredients:
- "1 cup long grain white rice"
- "1 15-ounce can pinto beans drained and rinsed"
- "1 cup frozen diced bell peppers thawed slightly"
- "1 cup corn kernels fresh or frozen"
- "1 teaspoon chili powder"
- "1 teaspoon ground cumin"
- "1 teaspoon smoked paprika"
- "½ teaspoon garlic powder"
- "½ teaspoon onion powder"
- "½ teaspoon dried oregano"
- "1 ½ pounds chicken tenders or strips"
- "1 ½ cups chicken broth"
- "Salt and black pepper to taste"
- "Avocado slices for topping"
- "Sour cream for topping"
- "Mexican cheese blend for topping"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 345°F. Spray a 9×13 baking pan lightly with cooking spray; ensures no sticking and easier cleanup."
- "Combine all spices in a small bowl: chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, oregano. Mix well so flavors evenly distribute."
- "In a large bowl toss rice, pinto beans, corn, and diced bell peppers. Salt and pepper now, but go light—broth often salty. Add spice mix. Stir thoroughly to coat everything with seasoning. The spices cling better when mixed dry first."
- "Lay chicken strips in one even layer on bottom of pan. This placement right against pan ensures meat cooks thoroughly—no half-done chicken. Pour rice-vegetable-spice mix evenly over chicken. Don’t press down; loose layers let steam circulate."
- "Pour chicken broth gently over filling. Liquid should just barely cover rice, so no soggy mess later."
- "Cover pan tightly with heavy-duty foil; seal edges well. Makes a mini-steamer. Hold in moisture for fluffy rice and tender chicken."
- "Bake for about 55 minutes. Look for bubbling broth at edge and chicken turning opaque through foil. Smell will shift from dry spices to cooked savory bird and sweet corn."
- "Take pan out, carefully remove foil (watch hot steam). Fluff rice gently with a fork, separating grains. This step is critical—no mushy clumps. Reseal with foil and bake 5-10 more minutes to dry surface slightly and finish cooking chicken."
- "Remove from oven. Let rest 5 minutes uncovered; juices settle. Serve topped with creamy sour cream, sliced avocado, and shredded Mexican cheese. Melts slightly from residual heat—balance of cool and warm."
- "If pressed for time or no oven, cook chicken and rice mix in a large skillet covered on stovetop over low heat, stirring halfway through. Keep lid tight to trap steam."
