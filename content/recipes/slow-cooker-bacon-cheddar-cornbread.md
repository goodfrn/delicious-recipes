ingredients:
- "1 1/2 cups yellow cornmeal mix"
- "1 1/4 cups cooked and chopped smoked turkey bacon"
- "1 1/2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese"
- "1 cup drained whole kernel corn"
- "1 cup creamed corn"
- "1/2 cup plain Greek yogurt"
- "3 tbsp unsalted butter, melted"
- "3 large eggs"
- "1/4 cup packed dark brown sugar"
instructions:
- "Spray inside of a 5-7 quart slow cooker with nonstick cooking spray, covering all sides to prevent sticking. A little patience helps here—mistakes lead to a mess later."
- "In a large bowl combine cornmeal mix, diced smoked turkey bacon (instead of pork for less fat), shredded sharp cheddar (or swap for gouda for smoky sweetness), drained whole kernel corn, creamed corn, Greek yogurt replacing sour cream for tang and protein punch, melted butter, eggs, and dark brown sugar."
- "Mix gently but thoroughly; avoid overmixing or the cornbread gets gummy. Aim for uniform batter with no dry streaks."
- "Pour batter evenly into the prepared slow cooker. Level the surface with a spatula or back of a spoon."
- "Cover lid tightly and set slow cooker on high. Let cook undisturbed for 3 hours, then check for wobble. Edges should be a deep golden brown, almost pulling away from sides. Centers will firm but still slightly jiggle—avoid waiting for complete solidity or it dries out."
- "If jiggle too much, cook an additional 20-30 minutes but check every 10. Overcooked slow cooker cornbread turns rubbery, so trust your eyes, not the clock."
- "Remove lid and let sit uncovered for 10 minutes. This drying period crisps edges slightly and sets internal crumb."
- "Use a wide spatula to loosen edges before slicing to avoid breaking."
- "Serve warm. Crusty outside, tender crumb inside. Sweet hits from brown sugar, cheesy pockets, smoky bacon ribbons contrast with corn bursts."
- "Leftovers reheat well wrapped in foil in moderate oven; resist microwave which collapses texture."
- "Variation tip: Fold in a handful of diced jalapeños or roasted peppers before cooking for heat and color contrast."
