ingredients:
- "3/4 cup all-purpose flour"
- "2/3 cup stone-ground yellow cornmeal"
- "2 tablespoons coconut sugar"
- "3/4 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1/2 teaspoon cream of tartar"
- "1/2 teaspoon kosher salt"
instructions:
- "Whisk together flour and coarse cornmeal in a wide bowl till evenly mixed; dry ingredients need uniformity for even rise and crumb texture."
- "Add sugar, baking soda and cream of tartar; sift or fine whisk again to prevent pockets of uneven leavening agents. Salt sprinkled last to blend consistently; prevents flat flavor."
- "Store immediately in airtight container to keep moisture out and powder clump-free; stashing in fridge prolongs shelf life but room temp fine short term."
- "Use mix for muffins or quick bread loaves; hydrate with buttermilk or yogurt—those acids react with the soda and tartar for lift and moistness."
- "Baking temperature set at 375°F. Watch muffin tops; golden, crackled surfaces should spring back when pressed lightly, not leave indentation."
- "If baking loaf, insert toothpick near center; moist crumbs adhere but no wet batter clings—that’s your done signal."
- "Common swap: oat flour or spelt for all-purpose to cut gluten load, but crumb texture shifts. Sugar swap maple or honey, reduce liquids slightly if liquid used."
- "Avoid stirring batter long after adding liquid; agitates gluten, makes dense final crumb."
- "Keep an eye on crust color—too dark means oven too hot or sugar burnt; tent loosely with foil if crust browns too fast."
- "Store leftovers wrapped; reheat briefly to bring back slight crust crunch and warmth."
