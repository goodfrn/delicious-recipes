ingredients:
- "4 cloves garlic, diced and rested 10-15 min"
- "4 tablespoons olive oil"
- "1 large yellow onion, diced"
- "2 cans petite diced tomatoes, juices included"
- "1/2 cup dry white vermouth (sub: dry sherry)"
- "6 cups vegetable stock (sub: chicken stock)"
- "1 teaspoon dried oregano"
- "1 teaspoon dried thyme"
- "1 tablespoon tomato paste"
- "1 parmesan rind piece (~1 inch thick)"
- "2 large carrots, peeled and diced"
- "2 cups dried Ditalini pasta (sub: small shells)"
- "2 medium zucchini, diced with skin"
- "2 cans cannellini beans, drained and rinsed (sub: navy beans)"
- "1 cup water"
- "2 bay leaves"
- "Salt to taste"
- "Freshly ground black pepper"
- "Fresh parmesan wedge, shaved for serving"
instructions:
- "Dice garlic first, spread on cutting board, let sit 10-15 minutes to activate enzymes for better flavor and immune benefits. Rushing this step wastes garlic’s power."
- "Heat olive oil in large heavy-bottom pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Wait till oil shimmers and moves fluidly across pan before adding onion and garlic. Sauté stirring often until onions turn translucent and smell sweet, about 7-9 minutes. Don’t brown garlic or onions here, burnt garlic gives bitterness. Slow is key."
- "Add diced tomatoes in juice, then pour in vermouth. Stir to combine. Raise heat to medium-low and let simmer uncovered. Watch liquid reduce and thicken to sauce consistency. About 18-22 minutes. The scent darkens, acidity mellows. No lids to avoid bland stewing."
- "Add stock, tomato paste, oregano, thyme, parmesan rind, carrots, and bay leaves. Bring mixture to full boil, stir constantly first 2 minutes to prevent sticking and layer flavors quickly. Boil breaks down carrot fibers better but don’t overdo."
- "Lower heat to gentle simmer. Salt lightly now to control seasoning going forward. Stir in dry pasta and zucchini pieces (skin on for color, nutrients, texture). The skin holds shape and adds subtle bitterness balancing sweet carrot and tomato. No peeling needed, just dice evenly."
- "In a separate colander rinse canned beans to wash packing liquid that dulls taste and adds unwanted salt. Set aside drained beans until last step. Rinsing crucial for fresh flavor and softer soup texture."
- "Pour in 1 cup water, stir well. Simmer with pot partially covered for 10-15 minutes or until pasta al dente and zucchini tender but not mushy. Visual cues: pasta swells and softens, zucchini brightens and yields to fork easily but still holds shape."
- "Turn off heat. Gently fold in beans, adjust salt and pepper seasoning. Leave bay leaves and parmesan rind visible to fish out before serving. The rind will have softened but remains firm enough to remove in one piece."
- "Serve hot with fresh parmesan shaved on top and cracked black pepper. Cheese melts over warm soup, adds complexity and depth."
- "Optional twist: swap vermouth for dry sherry or dry white wine if unavailable. Use small shells if no Ditalini; adjust simmer time slightly. If parmesan rind missing, add a handful of grated aged parm during cooking instead but lose that umami infusion punch."
