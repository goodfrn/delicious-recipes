ingredients:
- "27 Ritz crackers or salted round crackers"
- "1 cup creamy almond butter instead of peanut butter"
- "3/4 cup powdered sugar"
- "2 1/2 cups semi-sweet chocolate chips divided"
- "2 tablespoons cacao nibs optional twist for crunch"
instructions:
- "Line a 17x12 sheet pan with parchment paper. Avoid wax paper if possible; it clings."
- "In a small bowl, mix almond butter with powdered sugar stirring until thick but spreadable. Thick filler keeps shape; too loose and it oozes out."
- "Place 27 crackers face down on pan spaced evenly like a grid but leave room to handle."
- "Using a tablespoon measure, dollop almond filling smack in the middle. Don’t smush to edges—gives room for pressing without mess."
- "Top each with a cracker, face up. Press gently but firmly. Feel for filling near edges but no squeezing out visible. Stop pressing if filling starts to leak."
- "Pop the tray into the fridge for 30 minutes minimum. Texture should transition to firm and slightly dense to avoid breakdown when dipping."
- "Meanwhile, in a medium microwave-safe bowl, heat 2 cups of chocolate chips for 20 seconds at a time. Stir after each burst. Watch carefully; chocolate scorched smells acrid and grainy, useless."
- "Add remaining 1/2 cup of chips off heat and stir gently until melted chocolate turns shiny and silky. This off-heat addition lowers temperature preventing thick clumps. This method simulates tempering to control setting texture."
- "Right before dipping, sprinkle cacao nibs on parchment. They add a bitter crunch if you want to tap cookies in or press nibs on chocolate after coating for texture contrast."
- "Take chilled sandwiches out. Using two forks or a chocolate dipping fork, one lifts cookie into chocolate and flips it over to coat fully."
- "Tap the fork on bowl edge gently to drip excess chocolate. Too thick coat loses snap and looks clumpy."
- "Place dipped cookies back on parchment. While still wet, optionally press cacao nibs onto surface."
- "Chill for 35 minutes or until chocolate coating feels firm but not brittle. Visual cue: chocolate surface should be matte and resist finger indentation."
- "Store cookies in airtight container between parchment layers in fridge or cool pantry if humidity low; moisture ruins texture."
