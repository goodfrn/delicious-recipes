ingredients:
- "=== Crust ==="
- "1 1/4 cups graham cracker crumbs"
- "1/3 cup melted unsalted butter"
- "2 tablespoons granulated sugar"
- "1/4 cup finely chopped almonds (replace half graham crumbs for texture twist)"
- ""
- "=== Filling ==="
- "32 ounces cream cheese softened"
- "3/4 cup granulated sugar"
- "1 cup Greek yogurt (replace sour cream)"
- "2 tablespoons cornstarch"
- "Zest of 1 lemon"
- "3 large eggs"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1 cup fresh blueberries tossed in 2 tablespoons flour"
- ""
- "=== Blueberry Topping ==="
- "2 cups frozen blueberries"
- "1/3 cup granulated sugar"
- "Zest of 1 lemon"
- "1 tablespoon cornstarch mixed with 2 tablespoons cold water"
instructions:
- "=== Crust ==="
- "Preheat oven 340F. Line bottom of 9-inch springform pan with parchment circle sprayed with nonstick spray; prevents sticking, clean release. Mix graham crumbs, chopped almonds, melted butter, and sugar thoroughly until moistened, no dry spots. Press crumbs firmly evenly on bottom and halfway up sides—can use flat measuring cup or bottom of glass. Crust texture key; firm base but not rock hard. Bake 7 minutes until aromatic and golden edges appear along sides. Cool on rack; residual warmth helps set but don't skip resting."
- "Lower oven to 320F (important to prevent cracking during slow cook of filling)."
- "=== Filling ==="
- "Beat softened cream cheese, sugar, Greek yogurt, and cornstarch on medium-high until creamy and no lumps, about 1 to 1.5 minutes. Stop once you see homogeneous texture; over-beating incorporates too much air, leads to cracks. Drop speed to low, add lemon zest for subtle brightness without overmixing. Add eggs one at a time on low speed, fully incorporated before next; keeps batter stable. Last, stir in vanilla just to combine. Toss blueberries in flour—prevents sinking and helps distribute from bottom. Fold blueberries gently by hand with rubber spatula; breaking berries will tint batter blue, keep swirled but not smeared. Pour filling gently on crust. Tap pan lightly to release large air bubbles and smooth top."
- "Wrap pan sides twice with aluminum foil to prevent water seeping inside during water bath baking. Set wrapped pan inside larger roasting pan. Pour hot water carefully to come 1.5 inches up sides but not splash into cake."
- "Bake 1 hour 45 minutes. Cake should be just set with slight wobble in center; jiggle test critical—too firm means dry; too wobbly means underdone. Turn off oven, crack door open slightly, let cheesecake rest inside water bath for 20 minutes—slow cooling prevents cracks and shrinks. Remove from water bath, cool on wire rack 25-30 minutes to near room temp. Refrigerate in pan uncovered 7-8 hours or overnight. Patience here matters for proper texture."
- "Run thin sharp knife along edge before releasing springform ring to avoid uneven breaking. Pull ring gently. To invert cake onto flat plate or cake board, place plate over cake, flip carefully so crust side is down, remove parchment and pan bottom, then flip cake again for final serving presentation. This double flip sets smooth crust side up."
- "=== Blueberry Topping ==="
- "While chilling, combine frozen berries, sugar, lemon zest in heavy saucepan over medium-high. Stir until berries thaw, sugar melts—listen to gentle crackle and feel jammy syrup forming. Bring to low boil then reduce heat to medium-low; simmer 5 minutes to soften berries further and draw out juices. Mix cornstarch with cold water to slurry; pour into berries, stir continuously until thickened to jam consistency. Remove from heat, cool completely; warm topping seeps moisture into cake, ruins crust crispness. Once cooled, spread evenly over cheesecake. Keep chilled until serving."
- "Serve cold. Leftovers tightly covered will hold 3-4 days in fridge. Freeze slices with wax paper between layers for longer storage."
- ""
- "=== Substitutions and Tips ==="
- "If no graham crackers, digestive biscuits or vanilla wafers work well. Replace sour cream with equal parts full-fat plain Greek yogurt for tangy richness. Almonds in crust add crunch and nutty flavor—optional but recommended for texture contrast. If blueberries are scarce, try blackberries or raspberries for tart balance."
- "If crust shrinks during bake, press crumbs firmly before baking and chill crust briefly before adding filling. Baking with water bath prevents cracks but ensure foil layers are tight to stop leaks and water spots."
- "Do not overmix filling once eggs are added; aeration traps air, causing cracks and holes after baking. Fold blueberries carefully so batter remains light but consistent."
- "Knife test: slight wobble center means set but not overcooked. Cool slowly so surface doesn’t crack; rapid temperature change dries and cracks cheesecake."
- "Let topping cool fully. Warm topping will make crust soggy and ruin texture. The cornstarch slurry thickens topping, adds shine without clumps."
- "Inversion technique—flip twice—is to get neat edges and remove parchment without damaging sides."
