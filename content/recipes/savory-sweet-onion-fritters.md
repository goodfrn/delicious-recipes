ingredients:
- "1 finely chopped sweet onion replaced with shallots 1 cup"
- "fine polenta 3/4 cup"
- "all-purpose flour 2/3 cup"
- "honey 1 tablespoon no refined sugar"
- "fine sea salt 1 teaspoon"
- "large egg 1"
- "buttermilk 2/3 cup"
- "vegetable oil 3 inches depth"
instructions:
- "Add vegetable oil deep enough to reach half the side of a heavy bottom skillet or Dutch oven about 3 inches deep. Heat oil slowly stirring thermometer until oil reaches about 345°F aiming slightly lower to prevent burning and maintain crispness."
- "Mix shallots with polenta, flour, honey, and salt in a medium bowl. Honey gives subtle sweetness missing in white sugar recipes. Stir to combine evenly. Aroma builds as dry mixes."
- "In separate small bowl, whisk egg briefly into buttermilk then pour into dry mix. Fold carefully until just combined; overmixing toughens fritters. Batter’s texture gritty but cohesive."
- "Rest batter 7 minutes minimum; hydrates polenta for better structure and moisture retention during frying. Watch for batter thickening slightly without letting it separate."
- "Drop heaping spoonfuls slowly into hot oil. Avoid overcrowding. Too many drops cool oil drastically; fries greasy and soggy. Fry 5 to 7 minutes total turning once — wait for edges to bubble fiercely and turn amber brown."
- "Flip carefully with spider or slotted spoon; crispy crust should hold without crumbling. Listen: consistent fry sizzle changes tone over time."
- "Drain fritters on wire rack layered over paper towels to keep crisp crust intact. Avoid stacking hot fritters; steam ruins texture."
- "Adjust heat throughout frying to keep oil between 340°F and 350°F. Thermometer essential, but watch bubbles around batter edges. Large coarse bubbles signal nearly done."
- "If you lack buttermilk use 2/3 cup milk plus 1 teaspoon lemon juice or vinegar. Honey replaced with maple syrup or malt syrup for variation. Onion swapped with leeks or scallions changes flavor profile."
- "Use thermometer; guessing oil temperature is risky. Oil below 330°F equals greasy, oil above 360°F burns crust before cooking inside. Small batch frying steadies temp and finish."
- "Keep cooled fritters warm in oven at 200°F on rack, uncovered. Moisture escapes rather than concentrates steam inside crust."
