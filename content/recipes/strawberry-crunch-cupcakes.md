ingredients:
- "1 cup unsalted butter melted"
- "2 1/2 tablespoons strawberry gelatin powder substituted with raspberry gelatin for a twist"
- "1 3/4 cups crushed golden Oreo cookies replaced with crushed vanilla wafers for texture variation"
- "1 box strawberry cake mix"
- "1 cup whole milk"
- "3/4 cup canola oil reduced from original 1 cup for moistness without greasiness"
- "3 large eggs beaten"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1/4 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "3 1/2 cups sifted powdered sugar"
- "24 small fresh strawberries halved"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 345°F like a pro knowing the slower rise really aids crumb structure."
- "Line 2 muffin tins with paper liners and prep a large rimmed sheet with parchment; the crunch bakes separately."
- "Start crust: mix melted butter with gelatin powder in medium bowl blending gently so not all crumbs are coated; you want some stark, dry golden spots for crunch contrast."
- "Toss in cookie crumbs uniquely crushed not pulverized fine; stirred gently, not smooshed in butter."
- "Spread the crunchy mix evenly over prepared sheet; bake around 10 minutes till edges slightly bronzed, not burnt."
- "Cool on counter while batter preps; aroma hints sweet strawberry-powdered sugar melding."
- "Grab a large bowl, beat together cake mix, milk, oil, eggs, and extra gelatin powder for precisely 2 1/2 minutes medium speed; do not overmix or crumb will toughen."
- "Evenly portion batter into paper-lined cups; no lumpy tops."
- "Bake 18-20 minutes checking for toothpick dry but moist crumbs inside; tapping top yields slight bounce, not firm or wet."
- "Rest cupcakes 7 minutes in pan before transferring to wire racks; hot bottoms hold heat and continue baking internally."
- "Make frosting while cooling: beat softened butter high speed 3 minutes ‘til clouds of creamy lightness form; don’t rush—air whipping is key."
- "Add vanilla extract and salt to balance sweetness; fold in sifted powdered sugar gradually by cup, blending fully after each addition."
- "Frosting is right when it’s glossy but stiff enough to hold shape piped with large decorative tip."
- "Pipe generous swirls atop cooled cupcakes. The cold cake keeps frosting stable without melting or sliding."
- "Top each with ambient temperature strawberry crunch and half fresh strawberry; garnish calls for neatness to highlight texture contrast."
- "Serve fresh; cupcakes should feel moist with crisp top contrast and rich airy frosting."
