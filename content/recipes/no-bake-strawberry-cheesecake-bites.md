ingredients:
- "8 ounces cream cheese softened"
- "1/4 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract"
- "3 cups dry pound cake crumbs pulverized fine"
- "12 ounces white chocolate chips or bark"
- "Sprinkles optional"
instructions:
- "===Mixing the base==="
- "Start softening cream cheese just to barely smooth with electric handheld or stand mixer paddle. No lumps. Fold in salt and vanilla evenly. Adds dimension, cuts cheesiness."
- "Pulverize pound cake into coarse crumbs until dry but pliable. Add slowly to cream cheese mixture, keep beating medium speed till dough forms. It’ll feel crumbly at first, eventually thick dense mass. If too wet, add more crumbs, too dry, a splash cream or milk."
- "===Rest and firm up==="
- "Cover bowl. Refrigerate minimum two hours. Overnight better. Dough firms, flavors meld, easier to shape."
- "===Prepare coating==="
- "Line baking sheet with parchment paper. Melt white chocolate chips in microwave or double boiler. Use short bursts 20 seconds stirring vigorously each time. Look for thick shiny melted chips, no lumps. Too hot? Chocolate grainy, retemper gently with cool metal bowl over ice water."
- "===Scoop and dip==="
- "Use tablespoon scoop to create uniform balls from chilled dough. Toothpick or fork picks. Dip fully into melted chocolate, allow extra coating to drip off by tapping fork on edge. Avoid thick globs that crack when drying."
- "Place balls on parchment. If sprinkles, sprinkle immediately before chocolate sets but after initial drip tap — coating should be tacky, not runny."
- "===Finishing==="
- "Let coating set naturally at room temp — look for matte finish replacing shine. No fingerprints pressed. For quicker finish, chill 10-15 minutes but avoid condensation forming."
- "Optional: drizzle extra melted white chocolate over set bites for contrast lines. Use back of spoon for thin ribbons, scrape excess off edges for cleaner lines."
- "===Storage==="
- "Store airtight in fridge. Keeps 3-4 days, no harder than firm fudge consistency. Let rest 5-10 minutes at room temp before serving for best mouthfeel."
- "===Troubleshooting==="
- "If coating cracks on bites, chocolate too cold when dipped — warm slightly and re-dip quickly. Dough too soft? Add handful extra cake crumbs, chill longer."
- "Substitutions: cream cheese can be full-fat ricotta, but less firm. For coating, white chocolate more stable than candy melts but thicker, melt carefully to avoid seizing."
- "Remember: visual and tactile cues weigh more than any clock. Dough should hold shape when scooped but not be dry crumbling. Chocolate glossy, well-melted but not cooked or grainy."
