ingredients:
- "20 garlic cloves peeled"
- "3 sprigs fresh rosemary"
- "2 sprigs fresh oregano"
- "1 teaspoon sea salt"
- "1 1/2 cups extra virgin olive oil"
- "Optional twist 1 tablespoon unsalted butter"
- "Optional twist pinch crushed red pepper flakes"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 290F. Not a hair over. Slow and steady turns these cloves golden, nutty, mellow."
- "Decant garlic cloves into a shallow medium baking pan. Nestle rosemary and oregano among them; strips or whole sprigs both okay. Herbs should infuse but not burn."
- "Sprinkle salt evenly over everything. A measured shake. Keep the salt lowish so it draws out moisture but won’t dry or harden garlic."
- "Cover all with olive oil. At least one inch above garlic level. Olive oil is your slow cooker here, no shortcuts. Optional add butter chunks on top for richness; it will melt and add silkiness. Red pepper flakes for kick if fancy."
- "Bake for roughly 40-45 minutes. Watch closely after 35. Garlic softens, edges get lightly toasted, color shifts from raw white to warm beige or pale amber. Aroma deepens—must fill kitchen with sweet garlic scents, herbaceous and slightly savory."
- "Careful not to spike heat or overbrown. Too dark means bitter, nope. Tap garlic with your finger or fork—soft, tender, breaks apart without mushiness is the goal."
- "Remove pan from oven. Let cool undisturbed until safe to touch. Cooling solidifies textures, oils absorb flavors further. Avoid air contact by storing garlic and oil submerged in airtight jar."
- "Use garlic softened in this way for spreads, sauces, or roasting veggies. Oil doubles as flavor base or drizzle. Keep refrigerated; use within 2 weeks."
- "Struggles with peeling fresh garlic? Smash cloves under blade to loosen skin before peeling. No fresh? Jarred peeled garlic works but reduces brightness."
- "If olive oil is missing, substitute quality vegetable oil plus a few teaspoons of melted butter for flavor depth. Skip herbs if unavailable but consider dried crushed garlic for punch."
- "Keep an eye on oil bubbles during cooking. Tiny bubbles signal gentle simmer; silence could mean oven temp dropped; real sizzle means too hot."
- "If garlic cooks unevenly, spread cloves single layer, avoid crowding. Stir with tongs mid-bake for uniformity but gently so you don’t bruise soft cloves."
- "Waxiness or graininess in garlic signals under or over roast. Trust your nose and touch more than timer."
- "Stored properly, confit garlic lasts weeks. If oil tastes rancid or garlic smells off, toss without hesitation."
