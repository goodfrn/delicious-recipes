ingredients:
- "1 can (10.5 oz) undiluted cream of mushroom soup instead of chicken soup"
- "2 1/2 cups frozen mixed vegetables or use frozen pearl onions, carrots"
- "1 teaspoon freshly cracked black pepper"
- "1 tablespoon low sodium soy sauce"
- "3 cups cooked chicken, shredded or diced"
- "2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1/2 cup vegetable shortening"
- "1 cup whole milk"
- "1 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "1 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "2 tablespoons fresh chives, minced"
- "1 1/2 cups sharp white cheddar cheese, shredded"
- "Optional twist: 1/4 cup grated Parmesan mixed into biscuit dough"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 460 degrees F to counter heat loss when adding filling"
- "Warm a large 10-inch oven-safe skillet over medium, swirl in cream of mushroom soup and add frozen pearl onions mix; cook stirring often until veggies are heated through and softened—look for slight translucency and small bubbling sounds under spoon"
- "Add cracked pepper and soy sauce, stir thoroughly, then gently fold in shredded chicken; filling will look thick and shiny, save room for biscuit dough on top"
- "Pull skillet off heat promptly to prevent curdling or separation of dairy base; set aside"
- "In a big bowl, mix flour and cut in shortening using forks or pastry blender until crumb resembles coarse peas—avoid overwork to keep dough tender"
- "Add milk, salt, garlic powder, chives, shredded cheddar, and grated Parmesan; mix with spatula until just combined; dough should feel soft but not sticky, add tiny splashes milk if stiff—stop before wet"
- "Portion dough by heaping spoonfuls and drop carefully onto chicken mixture, cover as evenly as possible but gaps are fine because biscuits will spread a bit"
- "Set skillet on rimmed baking sheet to catch drips and avoid smoke"
- "Bake 12 to 17 minutes; watch for biscuit tops turning golden with browned edges and filling bubbling aggressively, jiggle pan gently to check consistency—thick and hot, not soupy"
- "Remove from oven, let rest 5 minutes to set filling and cool biscuit surface slightly before serving"
