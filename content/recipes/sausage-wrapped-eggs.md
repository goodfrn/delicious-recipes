ingredients:
- "12 large eggs, hard boiled"
- "1 lb ground pork sausage"
- "1 tbsp whole grain mustard instead of Dijon"
- "1 tsp fine sea salt"
- "1 tsp freshly cracked black pepper"
- "3/4 cup all purpose flour"
- "1 1/4 cups Italian seasoned breadcrumbs mixed with 1/3 cup finely grated Pecorino Romano"
- "2 large eggs, lightly beaten"
- "Vegetable oil for frying, enough for 2 inch depth"
instructions:
- "=== Blending and wrapping ==="
- "In a sturdy bowl, mix pork sausage, mustard, salt, and pepper until homogenous but not overworked. Overmixing toughens the texture."
- "Set up station with flour in one shallow dish, beaten eggs in another, and seasoned breadcrumbs mixed with Pecorino Romano in a third. Pecorino adds sharper tang compared to Parmesan; timing shifts slightly due to crumb moisture absorption."
- "Shell the hard boiled eggs carefully, pat dry to remove moisture which hampers coating adhesion. Divide sausage meat into 6 portions roughly the size of a golf ball. Flatten each into a thin patty."
- "Dust each egg lightly with flour first to give the sausage something to grip. Encase each egg by wrapping flattened sausage around it, sealing edges as needed. If the sausage is sticky, wet your hands slightly to smooth seams."
- "After wrapping, roll each egg once more in flour (some falls off, but this primes the egg for the egg wash), dunk in the beaten eggs ensuring full coverage, then roll thoroughly in the Pecorino breadcrumb mixture pressing gently to adhere crust."
- "=== Frying fundamentals ==="
- "Use a heavy sided frying pan; heat 2 inches of vegetable oil to a controlled 290 to 310 degrees Fahrenheit. This range prevents rapid burning and helps cook evenly. Always verify with a reliable deep fry thermometer. Too hot, crust scorches; too cool, greasy and greasy eggs."
- "Drop 2 to 3 eggs at a time carefully to avoid splatter. Use tongs or slotted spoon gently lowering; listen for steady gentle crackle of oil indicating correct temp."
- "Roll and turn eggs consistently every 90 seconds or so. The crust will transition from pale to rich golden brown—this visual cue governs readiness. Total fry time around 8 to 10 minutes but don’t rush by color and oil shimmering."
- "Drain on paper towels briefly to catch oil but don’t let sit long or crust will soften. Transfer to a warm oven set to about 200 degrees Fahrenheit to keep tempered without further cooking."
- "=== Serving tips and alternatives ==="
- "Slice on a sharp serrated knife. Look for firm white and creamy soft yolk. If yolk is chalky or firm, eggs were overcooked initially."
- "Substitute chicken or turkey sausage for pork to lighten flavor profile; double check seasoning levels as poultry is often blander. For vegetarian options, try herbed plant-based sausage alternative, but fry times and temperatures may differ."
- "Storage: cooled Scotch eggs store wrapped in fridge up to 3 days; reheat in oven to regain crispness—not microwave, or crust muddles."
- "If breadcrumb coating slips or falls off during frying, likely due to moisture on sausage or egg surface. Pat dry and handle minimally."
