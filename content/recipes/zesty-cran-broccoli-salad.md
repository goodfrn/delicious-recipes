ingredients:
- "4 cups broccoli florets, chopped uneven to bite size"
- "1 cup dried cherries, roughly chopped"
- "1 medium green apple, diced small"
- "½ cup thinly sliced red onion"
- "1 cup celery, sliced thin at angle"
- "¾ cup toasted almonds, coarsely chopped"
- "½ cup crumbled goat cheese"
- "½ cup Greek yogurt, full fat"
- "¼ cup light mayonnaise"
- "2 tablespoons maple syrup"
- "2 tablespoons fresh lime juice"
- "Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste"
instructions:
- "Toast almonds in dry skillet over medium heat, stirring often until fragrant and lightly golden; smells nutty, about 5 minutes. Pull off heat to cool."
- "Chop broccoli unevenly into bite-sized clusters for mouthfeel variation. Add to large bowl with dried cherries, diced green apple, sliced red onion, thin celery slices, cooled almonds, and goat cheese crumbles. Toss lightly just to mix."
- "Whisk Greek yogurt, mayo, maple syrup, and lime juice in smaller bowl. Maple syrup adds deeper sweetness than honey. Adjust with more lime or syrup for brightness or softness. Sauces should coat but not drown broccoli."
- "Pour dressing over broccoli mixture. Stir gently but thoroughly so dressing clings evenly to broccoli surfaces. Season with salt and cracked pepper last. Taste often to avoid over-salting."
- "Cover bowl. Chill minimum 25 minutes to let flavors mingle and soften raw bite without losing snap. Broccoli should still hold crunch—not soggy. Serve cold or near cool room temperature."
- "If salad gets watery (common pitfall), drain excess liquid and re-toss. Crunch is non-negotiable here; soggy ruins texture. Add more nuts for extra crunch if needed before serving."
