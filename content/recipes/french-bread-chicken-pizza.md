ingredients:
- "1 loaf rustic French bread"
- "3/4 cup spicy jalapeño Alfredo sauce"
- "1 1/2 cups cooked diced chicken breast"
- "1 small sweet onion thinly sliced"
- "1/2 cup fire-roasted poblano pepper strips"
- "1 1/2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese"
- "crushed red pepper flakes optional"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 400°F. Cut the loaf into quarters lengthwise like a long sandwich bun."
- "Arrange bread pieces cut side up on a baking sheet rimmed to catch drips. Toast 7-8 minutes till surface firms and slightly golden; crust should offer firm bite but not burn."
- "Heat sliced onion in a hot dry skillet until soft and lightly browned; enhances sweetness but tread carefully to avoid bitterness."
- "Spread Alfredo sauce evenly over each bread slice. Sprinkle diced chicken on top. Distribute roasted poblano strips and caramelized onions over all."
- "Cover with shredded mozzarella—don’t overload or cheese won’t melt properly; careful distribution matters."
- "Bake 10-12 minutes until cheese bubbles with golden brown spots and edges get crisp. Visual cues over clocks here."
- "Remove, let rest about 2 minutes. Sprinkle crushed red pepper flakes if you want heat. Serves warm, texture crisp but chewy beneath toppings."
