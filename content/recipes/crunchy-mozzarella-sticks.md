ingredients:
- "1 cup panko breadcrumbs"
- "½ cup all-purpose flour"
- "1 cup Italian-seasoned dry breadcrumbs"
- "1 teaspoon smoked paprika"
- "1 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "2 large eggs"
- "½ cup heavy cream"
- "16 ounces block mozzarella cheese"
- "Vegetable oil or peanut oil for frying"
- "Marinara or spicy arrabbiata sauce for serving"
instructions:
- "Slice mozzarella block into ¼ inch sticks, uniform thickness critical for even frying. Thinner sticks cook faster but risk cheese leakage; thicker require longer fry time."
- "Mix together panko, flour, Italian breadcrumbs, smoked paprika, and garlic powder in a shallow bowl. This blend adds depth and crunch uncommon in plain breadcrumb mixes."
- "In a separate bowl, whisk eggs together with heavy cream, not milk. Cream thickens mix improving coating adherence and frying stability."
- "Coat each cheese stick by dipping first into egg-cream mixture, then into breadcrumb mix. Repeat dipping and coating once more. This double breading forms a protective shell preventing cheese escape. Press coating firmly into cheese to avoid gaps."
- "Arrange coated sticks in a single layer on a large tray or plate. Cover loosely and freeze for about 2½ hours until firm to touch but not rock solid. Freezing time critical to stop cheese from oozing during frying."
- "Heat oil in a deep pan over medium heat, aiming for 350–355 degrees F. Use peanut oil if possible; it has a higher smoke point than vegetable oil, delivering cleaner fry without burnt aftertaste."
- "Drop frozen sticks into oil carefully, in batches to prevent temperature drop. Shhhh. Listen for steady sizzle . Oil should shimmer with tiny bubbles. Fry until golden brown, about 3–4 minutes per side. Flip once gently using tongs to avoid breaking crust."
- "Remove with slotted spoon and transfer to paper towels. Let rest 1–2 minutes; crust hardens slightly, cheese inside softens but not runny."
- "Serve immediately with spicy arrabbiata or ranch; dipping sauces cut richness. Warm, crunchy, mild mozzarella with savory crust—a classic."
- "If coating falls off or cheese leaks, probable causes: inadequate freeze time or uneven breading pressure. Press crumbs in firmly. Also avoid overly thin sticks—better firing known thickness. If oil too cool crust will soak oil; too hot crust will burn before cheese melts."
