ingredients:
- "1 1/3 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1 teaspoon baking powder"
- "3/4 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1 tablespoon pumpkin pie spice"
- "1/2 teaspoon salt"
- "2 large eggs"
- "3/4 cup cane sugar"
- "3/4 cup pumpkin puree (or mashed cooked sweet potato)"
- "3/4 cup half & half (milk substitute for creaminess)"
- "5 tablespoons melted unsalted butter, warm"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "cooking spray or neutral oil for waffle iron"
instructions:
- "Measure all dry ingredients—flour, baking powder, soda, spice, salt—into a medium bowl. Whisk or sift to remove lumps and activate leaveners, set aside."
- "In a large bowl, crack eggs and whisk briskly. Add cane sugar, pumpkin puree, half & half, vanilla. Beat to combine thoroughly, smooth consistency."
- "Pour warm melted butter gradually into wet mix while stirring gently. Avoid hot butter—it'll cook eggs. Butter adds richness, emulsifies batter."
- "Fold dry mixture into wet using a spatula; don't overmix. Lumps are fine. Overworking develops gluten; waffles turn tough. Folding helps trap air—key for lift."
- "Preheat waffle iron fully, hear sizzle when sprayed or lightly oiled. Spray cooking surface liberally every batch to prevent stubborn sticking."
- "Scoop batter per manufacturer’s recommendations. Surface should bubble and steam softly during cooking. When steam largely stops, edges appear dry and golden, waffles ready."
- "Remove waffles gently with plastic or wooden tool; avoid metal scraping. Stack loosely to keep crisp, cover lightly if storing briefly."
- "Serve immediately with maple syrup, toasted pecans or candied walnuts, and a knob of whipped cream. Garnishes add contrast, texture, and richness."
- "If batter thickens while waiting, stir gently, add splash of half & half to loosen; batter should flow but not be runny."
- "Waffles too pale? Check iron heats high enough or extend cook time by a minute; too dark? Lower temp next batch or reduce cook time."
