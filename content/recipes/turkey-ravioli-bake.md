ingredients:
- "1 medium yellow onion chopped"
- "2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil"
- "freshly cracked black pepper pinch"
- "fine sea salt pinch"
- "3 garlic cloves minced"
- "1 lb ground chicken (sub for turkey)"
- "1 tsp Italian seasoning blend replaced by 1 tsp oregano plus 1 tsp dried thyme"
- "2 cups smoky chipotle tomato sauce (swap for marinara)"
- "1 package (13 oz) cheese ravioli"
- "1-2 tbsp olive oil extra for tossing"
- "15 oz whole milk ricotta cheese"
- "1 large egg"
- "2 tbsp fresh parsley chopped"
- "1 tbsp fresh basil chopped"
- "2 cups shredded fontina cheese (replace mozzarella)"
- "Fresh Parmesan for serving optional"
instructions:
- "Heat olive oil over medium low flame in heavy-bottom skillet. Add chopped onions with salt and pepper. Stir occasionally watching for translucid edges and sweet aroma; about 6 minutes this time. Avoid browning—sweat only."
- "Toss in minced garlic once onions softened. Garlic crackle should be quick, around 45 seconds. Smell sharpness, not burnt. Immediately add ground chicken. Break up meat with wooden spoon. Season with oregano and thyme now. Cook stirring steadily till no pink remains, roughly 7 minutes. Meat should lose raw sheen and brown slightly in spots, not mushy."
- "Pour smoky chipotle tomato sauce over meat. Stir to combine thoroughly. Let simmer off heat for 5 minutes; thicker texture will form, flavor deepens."
- "Fill large pot with water and bring to rapid boil. Salt generously to flavor pasta. Add ravioli gently in batches if needed. Cook according to package (usually 4 minutes). Watch ravioli float and swell; that’s the sign to fish out promptly with slotted spoon. Drain and rinse under cold water — this stops carryover cooking and keeps shape intact. Drizzle 1-2 tbsp olive oil and toss careful to coat. Prevents sticking — critical mistake is skipping oil here. Set aside."
- "In mixing bowl combine ricotta, egg, chopped parsley and basil. Stir till smooth and fluffy-ish. Herbs add freshness against smoky sauce. This layer adds moisture plus creamy contrast."
- "Preheat oven to 380°F — notch higher than standard for better caramelization during broil stage. Assemble in 9×13 baking dish layering as follows: start with half cup smoky tomato sauce, create thin even bed; pile half the cooked ravioli gently into a single layer; spoon all savory meat mixture over ravioli and spread evenly; layer remaining ravioli atop; dollop ricotta mixture then smooth out; add leftover tomato sauce, spread; finish with shredded fontina evenly covering top. Fontina offers meltiness with subtle nuttiness, different from mozzarella's milky flavor."
- "Tent pan loosely with aluminum foil, avoid foil touching cheese; steam trapped will help meld layers without burning top. Bake covered for 28 minutes. Look for bubbling along edges and sauce reduction signs. Remove foil, continue baking uncovered 24 minutes. Top should be golden, slightly blistered here and there."
- "Optional broil 3 minutes max. Watch carefully — cheese should bubble and brown quickly, avoid black spots. Remove instantly once bubbly and fragrant."
- "Let rest out of oven 15 minutes min. Resist cutting while hot; structure firm enough only after; flavors meld during cooling. Serve with grated Parmesan and extra fresh herbs sprinkled atop for brightness."
