ingredients:
- "3 tablespoons vegetable oil"
- "1 1/4 cups finely diced yellow onion"
- "3/4 cup finely diced celery"
- "3/4 cup sliced scallions white and green parts"
- "1 cup diced poblano peppers (substitute for bell pepper)"
- "5 crushed garlic cloves"
- "12 ounces smoked kielbasa, sliced (substitute for andouille sausage)"
- "1 1/4 pounds boneless chicken thighs, cut into bite-size pieces"
- "3 tablespoons clarified butter or ghee"
- "4 tablespoons all-purpose flour"
- "1/2 cup dry white wine"
- "4 cups chicken stock (preferably low sodium)"
- "1 cup water"
- "1 (14-ounce) can diced tomatoes"
- "1 1/2 teaspoons Creole seasoning blend"
- "1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper"
- "1 teaspoon hot sauce, or to taste"
- "Coarse kosher salt, to taste"
- "6 cups cooked white rice"
- "Chopped fresh parsley, for garnish"
instructions:
- "Heat 2 tablespoons vegetable oil in heavy-bottomed Dutch oven over medium heat. Add onion, celery, scallions, poblano, garlic. Sauté 6-7 minutes. Look for softened veggies, onions losing translucence, smell sweet and aromatic. No browning yet. Remove veggies with slotted spoon, set aside. Leave pot hot but empty, don't wipe."
- "Crank heat to medium-high. Add kielbasa slices straight to fat rendered by veggies. Brown on both sides, about 4 minutes total. You want color, crisp edges but no burnt spots. Remove with slotted spoon. Set aside."
- "Add remaining 1 tablespoon oil. Toss in chicken chunks. Brown all sides, about 6-9 minutes. Chicken will firm up, release juices. Pull chicken out. Pot now loaded with browned bits — do NOT rinse. These sticky bits hold all the flavor; key to gumbo depth."
- "In separate saucepan, warm ghee on medium-low. Watch carefully until it foams and just begins to brown, nutty smell rising. Remove from heat. Whisk in flour swiftly to make roux. Keep whisking constantly; adjust heat if browns too fast. Aim for deep brown, like milk chocolate, 12-17 minutes. Stop as soon as aromatic — keeps cooking off residual heat. Whisk a minute more to prevent scorching bottom."
- "Return Dutch oven to medium-high. Splash in white wine to deglaze. Scrape bottom vigorously to scrape up fond — noisy, bubbling. Wait until reduced by a quarter and almost syrupy. Pour in chicken stock, water. Toss in reserved veggies, chicken, kielbasa, tomatoes, Creole seasoning, cayenne, and hot sauce. Stir well. Simmer gently stirring occasionally, about 15-20 minutes – flavors marry, sauce thickens. If too thin, simmer longer; too thick, add more stock or water."
- "Lower heat to gentle simmer. Begin whisking in half the roux slowly. Watch gumbo thicken gradually — glossy, velvety coating backs spoon thick like gravy. Add more roux if needed, but stop once ideal thickness reached. Scrape and discard leftover roux to avoid bitterness. Taste after 5 minutes warm-through. Adjust salt carefully. That restrained seasoning cut through the spices and richness."
- "Serve gumbo ladled directly over steaming rice bowls. Sprinkle parsley for brightness. Crusty rustic bread optional but highly recommended for sopping browned bits left behind."
- "Troubleshooting: Too much roux makes gumbo gummy — thin with stock. Roux burned? Start fresh, no rescue. If chicken undercooked, shred and finish cooking in pot. No andouille? Smoked kielbasa or chorizo work well. No poblano? Bell pepper standard but poblano adds slight smoky heat. Use clarified butter/ghee for nuttiest roux; vegetable oil more neutral."
- "Storage: Holds in fridge 3 days, flavors deepen. Reheat slowly, stirring to prevent gumbo settling or roux separating."
