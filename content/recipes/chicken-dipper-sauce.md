ingredients:
- "3 tablespoons yellow mustard"
- "2 tablespoons hickory barbecue sauce"
- "4 tablespoons mayonnaise"
- "2 teaspoons wildflower honey"
- "1/2 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika"
instructions:
- "Get a small bowl, dump mustard then mayo in. Swirl together with a whisk or fork until you reach a creamy texture without streaks."
- "Add in barbecue sauce next, dark and thick. Notice its texture—thicker sauces bind better so adjust if needed, thin with a drop of water or more honey if too sharp."
- "Sprinkle garlic powder and smoked paprika evenly over the surface. Paprika adds a hint of smoky warmth as a twist—don’t skip it."
- "Whisk all ingredients briskly. Look for a velvety consistency, no clumps. Taste now: should hit sweet, tang, and mild smoke. Adjust honey if too tart, or mustard for bite."
- "Serve immediately or chill 5 minutes. Chilling melds flavors but don’t overdo or sauce thickens too much, loses its punch."
- "Use with oily fries, grilled or fried chicken, or as a tangy dip for seafood. Texture contrast from crispy to creamy. If sauce separates, a quick whisk revives it."
