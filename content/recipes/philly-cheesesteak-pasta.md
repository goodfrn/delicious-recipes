ingredients:
- "1 tablespoon olive oil"
- "1 pound ground turkey"
- "1 medium yellow onion, diced"
- "1 teaspoon salt"
- "½ teaspoon black pepper"
- "1 tablespoon soy sauce"
- "1 cup sweet mini bell peppers, sliced"
- "1 cup uncooked fusilli pasta"
- "2 cups water"
- "1 cup low sodium chicken broth"
- "½ cup milk"
- "4 ounces ricotta cheese, cubed"
- "½ cup shredded mozzarella cheese"
- "½ cup shredded provolone cheese, divided"
- "2 tablespoons chopped fresh basil"
instructions:
- "Heat olive oil in 12-inch skillet on medium. Add diced onions and ground turkey. Brown until no pink remains and onions turn translucent, stirring to break meat into small pieces. Meat should have light brown crust. Season with salt and pepper midway to layer flavor."
- "Add soy sauce and sliced mini bell peppers. Stir and cook for about 2 minutes until peppers soften slightly but still crisp—texture contrast is key here."
- "Pour in fusilli, water, and chicken broth. Raise heat to high. Stir occasionally to prevent pasta from sticking. When liquid hits a rolling boil—bubbles pounding the pan—reduce heat to low immediately, cover with lid."
- "Simmer gently for 12–17 minutes. Pasta must be tender but not mushy. Most liquid should be absorbed or evaporated. Peek occasionally, no stirring too roughly or noodles will break down."
- "Off heat, pour in cold milk and add ricotta cubes. Using spoon, fold gently till creamy sauce forms. Do this slowly to avoid curdling milk proteins. Ricotta adds smoothness and a hint of sweetness unlike sharp cream cheese."
- "Sprinkle mozzarella and half the provolone into skillet. Stir once just to combine evenly but don’t overwork cheese or risk stringy mess."
- "Top with remaining provolone slices. Replace lid for 3 minutes on very low heat. Cheese should melt into glistening blanket without browning. If you want golden edges, switch to broiler for 1 minute but watch close."
- "Finish with chopped fresh basil. Brightens dish with herby fragrance and freshness. Basil preferred here over parsley for its aromatic oils that lift the rich sauce."

instructionsNote: "Olive oil temperature important; too hot burns onions, too cool leaves them raw. Brown meat thoroughly to build Maillard flavor—don’t rush. Finishing with herbs adds bright flavor layers, cutting richness. Avoid overcooking cheese to prevent rubbery texture. Taste frequently and adjust salt near end, broth and soy add sodium so beware."
