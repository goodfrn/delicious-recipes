ingredients:
- "6 oz California prunes, chopped"
- "3 tbsp orange juice, freshly squeezed"
- "1 tsp ground cinnamon"
- "½ tsp ground nutmeg"
- "¼ tsp ground ginger"
- "3 tbsp maple syrup"
- "1 tsp vanilla extract"
- "1 cup rolled oats, old-fashioned"
- "¾ cup almond flour"
- "1⁄3 cup white sugar"
- "½ tsp baking soda"
- "Pinch salt"
- "7 tbsp unsalted butter, softened"
- "Nonstick baking spray"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 355°F. Spray an 8x8 pan thoroughly. Even coating—avoid sticky edges later."
- "Chop prunes smaller chunks if sticky. Process prunes, orange juice, cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, maple syrup, and vanilla into smooth but slightly coarse paste. About 1-2 minutes on medium speed. You want paste not puree—texture matters."
- "In medium bowl, toss oats, almond flour, sugar, baking soda, salt thoroughly. Dry mix prevents clumps."
- "Work softened butter into oat mix with fingers until coarse crumbs form. Don’t overmix or it gets greasy. Tactile feel: crumbly, no big chunks of butter left."
- "Press half the crumb into pan firmly to create bottom crust. Pack dense so prune filling won’t soak through."
- "Spread prune paste evenly over crust. Use spatula, smooth layer but avoid smashing crumbs down too hard."
- "Top prune layer with remaining crumbs. Gently press crumbs, just enough to stick without compacting paste out."
- "Bake 30-33 minutes until top rim and edges visibly golden, surface crackling, and aroma toasty with spice notes."
- "Cool fully in pan on wire rack. Bars must set or crumble when cut. Run knife edges around pan for clean cuts."
