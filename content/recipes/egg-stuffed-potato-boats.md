ingredients:
- "2 large baking potatoes"
- "1/4 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese"
- "1/4 cup cooked breakfast sausage crumbles"
- "1/2 tablespoon diced bell peppers"
- "Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste"
- "2 large eggs"
- "Butter or oil for greasing (optional)"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 425 degrees Fahrenheit. Higher temp gives crisper edges and faster cooking. Prepping potatoes first."
- "Wash potatoes well; dry thoroughly so skins roast well. Cut off tops about 1 inch from stem end. Spoon out insides, leaving about 3 mm thickness all around to keep structure firm but not bulky."
- "Reserve potato flesh and tops for hash or roasting later. Mix them with a little oil and seasoning for a quick side dish; no waste."
- "Lightly grease a rimmed baking sheet or line with foil for easy cleanup. Place hollowed potato shells upright. Important: Avoid cracks by handling gently during hollowing."
- "Distribute cheese evenly into each shell bottom, followed by sausage crumbles and diced peppers. Season with a few pinches of salt and pepper. Layering the cheese first seals the shell and stops sogginess."
- "Carefully crack one egg directly into each potato shell on top of fillings. Avoid breaking yolks for best presentation and texture contrast."
- "Slide sheet into oven. Bubbles and aromas will start around 10 minutes. Whites should look opaque and set but yolks still jiggly and rich around 13-15 minutes; adjust timing if you prefer firmer yolks. Visual is key."
- "Remove when white is firm and not shiny or runny. Let cool on rack 3-4 minutes to finish cooking gently, prevent burns, and let flavors settle."
- "Serve immediately. Crisp edges, creamy yolks, melty cheese, savory sausage with sweet pepper finish. Bacon replaced by sausage adds deeper notes, peppers bring crunch and pop."
- "If cracks appear or shells tend to fold, use larger potatoes or double thickness hollowing next time. Medium-large russets work best for crispness; waxy potatoes get soggy faster."
- "Eggs can be swapped for quail eggs for smaller versions or poached separately if you want runnier textures without risk of spillover."
- "Veggie options: use finely diced mushrooms or spinach; just reduce moisture to avoid sogginess. For spice, add a pinch of smoked paprika or cayenne in cheese layer."
