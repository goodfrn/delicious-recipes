ingredients:
- "1 cup cold lager or pale ale beer or substitute with chilled sparkling water for lighter batter"
- "1 cup all-purpose flour sifted"
- "1 large egg lightly beaten"
- "1 tablespoon fresh chopped parsley or swap with fresh thyme"
- "1 teaspoon granulated garlic powder"
- "3/4 teaspoon dried oregano"
- "1 teaspoon cornstarch to enhance crispness"
- "12 oz frozen cheese curds use firm, white cheddar if unavailable, freeze well beforehand"
- "About 4 cups vegetable oil for frying sunflower or canola works well"
- "Sea salt for finishing"
- "For sauce: 1/2 cup mayonnaise"
- "2 tablespoons ketchup"
- "1 tablespoon horseradish for punch or sub Dijon mustard"
- "1 teaspoon smoked paprika"
- "1 teaspoon lemon juice"
- "1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper adjust spice level to taste"
- "1 garlic clove minced finely"
instructions:
- "Freeze cheese curds solid before starting batter. This prevents cheese leaks during frying."
- "In a medium bowl whisk flour, cornstarch, egg, parsley, garlic powder, oregano together. Thick and pasty, that’s right. Whisk in cold beer gradually; batter thins but still holds."
- "Chill this batter 10-20 minutes; resting hydrates flour fully for cohesive crust. Use up to 20 hours in fridge to develop flavor if needed."
- "Heat oil in deep heavy pan over medium-high; aim for 320° to 350°F. Use a fry thermometer, watch oil shimmer and ripple rather than smoke."
- "Test oil ready by dropping small batter dollop; it should float immediately with lively bubbles but not burn black fast."
- "Working in small batches, dredge frozen curds in cold batter fully coated. Avoid clumps; shake excess gently."
- "Carefully place battered curds in hot oil. Listen for that satisfying sizzle and float. Fry 3 to 5 minutes turning once or twice for even browning. Deep golden brown exterior is done. Cheese should remain intact, not leaking."
- "Transfer fried curds to cooling rack over paper towels immediately after frying to drain oil. Sprinkle with sea salt while crust still tacky."
- "Keep fried curds in warm oven (about 200°F) on wire rack if holding for several batches; prevents soggy bottoms."
- "Whisk sauce ingredients together until smooth. Adjust horseradish and cayenne for heat. Sauce should be tangy, creamy, a bit smoky."
- "Serve immediately with sauce on side; clean bites of crispy crust with molten cheese inside."
- "Common pitfalls: Oil too hot = burnt batter, too low oil temp = greasy soggy crust. Frozen curds warm up fast, monitor frying or cheese will leak. Don�t overcrowd oil; temperature dive ruins crispness."
