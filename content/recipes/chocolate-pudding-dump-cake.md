ingredients:
- "Nonstick spray for pan"
- "1 cup dry Devil's Food cake mix (adjusted from 1 standard box around 15.25 oz)"
- "3/4 cup instant chocolate pudding mix (replace instant with homemade instant pudding powder for cleaner ingredient list)"
- "1 cup whole milk (can swap for half-and-half or strong brewed coffee for depth)"
- "6 tbsp unsalted butter softened, not melted or hot (better for even mixing with milk; swapping salted butter changes sodium levels)"
- "1 small bag semisweet chocolate chips, approx 6 oz (replace with chunks for more melty pockets or white chocolate for twist)"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 355°F (explanation: slight bump helps browning without burning chocolate). Spray 9x13 baking dish with nonstick spray, cover bottom evenly."
- "Scatter dry cake mix evenly across bottom, don’t press or cover; gives structure to crust."
- "Evenly distribute instant pudding mix over cake layer; no stirring here needed—dry layers must remain separate for texture differentiation."
- "Combine room-temp milk with softened butter. Avoid hot butter to prevent curdling or separating; mix gently with a whisk until homogenous but don’t overbeat—too much air means quicker drying while baking."
- "Pour milk-butter mixture evenly over dry layers. Try to cover entire surface; use a whisk to swirl gently, just enough to slightly incorporate but maintain distinct patches so cake bakes with light/dense zones."
- "Top everything with chocolate chips, scatter in a single uniform layer. This melts into dense spots but prevents clumping."
- "Place dish in oven, aim for center rack. Bake 35-40 minutes until edges bubble intensely, top forms a set crust with golden tones. Insert toothpick in center—sticky crumbs but no liquid batter signals done. You want the crust to spring back lightly when pressed."
- "Remove from oven and let stand 10-15 minutes. Hot is good, but steaming too long can collapse crust. Serve warm with cold ice cream or whipped cream to contrast temperatures."
