ingredients:
- "1 pound ground beef"
- "1 medium yellow onion diced"
- "1 cup spicy brown mustard replacing ketchup"
- "1 cup roasted red pepper sauce replacing canned mushroom soup"
- "1 teaspoon Cajun spice blend replacing Tony Chachere’s"
- "1 14-ounce can fire-roasted diced tomatoes drained"
- "1 tablespoon pickled jalapeños diced added"
- "2 cups shredded pepper jack cheese"
- "1 32-ounce bag frozen tater tots"
- "Nonstick cooking spray"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven 390°F instead of 375°F for better tot crisping. Spray a 9×13-inch pan evenly. Avoid too much spray—tot crispiness depends on dry heat."
- "Brown ground beef and onions in a wide skillet over medium-high. Listen. Meat will sizzle aggressively when hot enough. Stir frequently to break lumps. Cook until no pink remains and onions are translucent but have some edges browned for flavor. Drain grease completely; too much fat will make bake soggy."
- "Lower heat to low. Stir in mustard, roasted red pepper sauce, Cajun seasoning, drained tomatoes, and jalapeños. Mix well. Allow slight bubbling. Not a rolling boil—just enough so flavors meld and sauce thickens. About 7-8 minutes. Smell shifts from raw to well-seasoned meat-pot aroma."
- "Spread mixture evenly in prepared baking dish. Sprinkle pepper jack evenly on top. Cheese layer important for binding tots later."
- "Cover cheese with tater tots in neat single layer. No overcrowding. Gaps help hot air circulate which crisps edges. Lightly dust with extra Cajun spice. Adds visual flair and boosts flavor punch."
- "Bake uncovered 35 minutes or until tots are deep golden and bubbling juices peek around edges. Visual. Texture of tots firm but not burnt. If tots lack crunch, broil 2-3 minutes watching constantly. Burn happens fast. Broiler trick rescues soft tops."
- "Remove from oven. Wait 5 minutes before serving. Rest lets juices settle; prevents burns and sogginess."
- "Serve hot. Expect crunch then chewy layers beneath."
- "To save time: brown meat and onions day ahead; store covered in fridge. Layer and bake next day."
- "Troubleshooting: oily bake means insufficient grease drain. Fix by draining better or blotting with paper towels after browning."
