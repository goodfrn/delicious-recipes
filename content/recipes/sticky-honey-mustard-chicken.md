ingredients:
- "2.5 pounds bone-in chicken thighs, skin-on"
- "1 cup barbecue sauce, your choice (smoky or spicy works best)"
- "3 tablespoons maple syrup (alternate honey for mild sweetness)"
- "2 tablespoons Dijon mustard (substitute yellow mustard for milder tang)"
- "1.5 tablespoons apple cider vinegar (white wine vinegar also fine)"
- "1 teaspoon smoked paprika (can replace garlic powder for warmth)"
- "1.5 teaspoons garlic powder"
instructions:
- "=== Mix Marinade ==="
- "In a small bowl, whisk together barbecue sauce, maple syrup, Dijon mustard, apple cider vinegar, smoked paprika, and garlic powder until blended. Smell that smoky aroma? That’s the paprika kicking in."
- "=== Prepare Chicken ==="
- "Pat chicken dry with paper towels; moisture traps steam, prevents browning. Add chicken pieces to a large gallon-sized zip-top bag. Pour marinade over chicken. Seal tightly. Massage bag gently to coat, making sure every piece is slick with sauce."
- "=== Marinate ==="
- "Refrigerate for at least 2 hours, ideally up to 3 hours. Not mandatory, but flavor seeps in deeper, melting into fibers. If pressed for time, 45 minutes still adds a punch."
- "=== Preheat Oven and Dutch Oven Prep ==="
- "Set oven to 355°F. Slightly above 350 to balance cooking time and moisture retention. Lightly spray inside of a large Dutch oven with nonstick spray or brush with oil to prevent sticking and burning."
- "=== Bake Chicken ==="
- "Transfer chicken and marinade from bag into Dutch oven in a single layer. Makes cleanup easier. Bake uncovered for about 65 minutes. Listen for quiet bubbling of sauce around edges; sauce thickens, chicken skin tightens and crisps subtly around bone edges."
- "=== Visual and Tactile Cues ==="
- "Check at 60 minutes: skin should be sticky and caramelized, not wet. Use meat thermometer - 165°F internal temp. If below, cook in 5-minute increments. Skin texture and juices running clear tell story too."
- "=== Rest and Serve ==="
- "Remove chicken from oven; let rest 5-10 minutes in pot, thickened sauce will coat chicken. Resting locks juices, gives sauce time to cling. Serve warm but not piping hot; cooling just enough improves balance and texture."
