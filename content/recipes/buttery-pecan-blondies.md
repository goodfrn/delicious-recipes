ingredients:
- "1 cup unsalted butter melted"
- "1 cup dark brown sugar"
- "1⁄2 cup coconut sugar"
- "3 large eggs beaten"
- "1 1⁄8 cups all-purpose flour sifted"
- "1 cup chopped toasted pecans"
- "2 teaspoons vanilla extract replaced with almond extract"
- "1⁄2 teaspoon salt added"
- "Optional 1⁄4 cup mini chocolate chips twist"
instructions:
- "===Prep==="
- "Preheat oven 295°F. Use light metal pan 13x9 sprayed well with nonstick. Dark pans scorch fast, skip."
- "Toast pecans in dry skillet, medium heat, tossing until fragrant, 3-5 mins. Cool before mixing."
- "===Mix==="
- "Whisk melted butter with both sugars thoroughly. No sugar lumps allowed—texture suffers. Add eggs, stir vigorously until glossy. Fold in sifted flour and salt, blend until no streaks visible, no overmixing or batter toughens."
- "Stir in toasted pecans, vanilla swap almond, optional mini chips for unexpected bursts."
- "===Bake==="
- "Pour batter evenly in pan. Tap pan gently on counter to settle air bubbles—no leapfrogs."
- "Bake 45 to 55 minutes but rely mainly on texture: edges golden brown and set, center wiggles slightly under finger press but doesn’t collapse."
- "Don’t rush. Overbake, and chewiness turns cakey. Underbake, gooey and falls apart."
- "===Cool & Store==="
- "Crucial: cool completely in pan on wire rack. Warm is fragile, slices crumble."
- "Slice using serrated knife with gentle sawing motions."
- "Store airtight at room temp. Refrigeration risks drying out and hardening."
- "Freeze for months—wrap tight, thaw before slicing."
- "===Tips==="
- "Butter temp matters. Too hot spills egg curdles; too cool clumps sugar."
- "To save if pecans missing, swap with toasted walnuts or almonds, not raw nuts—you lose flavor."
- "Overly sweet? Cut sugar by 10%, balance rich butter."
- "Dark brown sugar for molasses flavor; coconut sugar adds subtle caramel and chew. Replace but one sugar at a time to keep moisture balance."
- "Watch batter thickness: thick means flour over measured, add splash milk or cream to loosen slowly."
- "Use light pan for even bake; if you only have dark, reduce oven by 10-15 degrees."
- "When cutting, warm knife, wipe between cuts—prevents crumbling and sticky edges."
