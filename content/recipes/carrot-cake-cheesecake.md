ingredients:
- "=== Cheesecake ==="
- "24 oz full fat cream cheese, softened"
- "3/4 cup granulated sugar"
- "1/4 tsp kosher salt"
- "3 tbsp all-purpose flour"
- "1 tsp ground cinnamon"
- "1/2 tsp ground nutmeg"
- "1 tbsp pure vanilla extract"
- "3 large eggs"
- "3/4 cup full fat sour cream"
- "=== Cake ==="
- "2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1 1/2 tsp baking powder"
- "1/2 tsp baking soda"
- "1 tsp ground cinnamon"
- "1/2 tsp ground ginger"
- "1/4 tsp fine salt"
- "1 cup brown sugar, packed"
- "1/2 cup granulated sugar"
- "1 cup vegetable oil (can substitute for melted butter or coconut oil)"
- "4 large eggs"
- "2 tsp vanilla extract"
- "1 1/2 cups finely grated carrots (about 3 medium carrots)"
- "1 cup chopped pecans, plus more for frosting"
- "=== Cream Cheese Frosting ==="
- "8 oz cream cheese, room temp"
- "1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened"
- "1 tsp vanilla extract"
- "3 1/2 cups powdered sugar, sifted"
- "3-4 tbsp heavy cream"
- "1/2 cup chopped pecans"
instructions:
- "=== Cheesecake Preparation ==="
- "Set a shallow roasting pan with warm water, 1 inch depth. Oven rack middle position. Wrap base of 9-inch springform pan in 2 layers heavy foil to prevent leaks; important. Line bottom with parchment paper."
- "Preheat oven to 320°F. Slightly lower than usual to avoid cracking cheesecake surface."
- "Beat cream cheese and sugars on medium speed until fluffy and combined. No lumps."
- "Add salt, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg. Mix just enough to integrate, avoid overmixing—can cause toughness."
- "Fold vanilla extract. Add eggs one at a time mixing slowly with spatula—no mixer—keeping air and smooth batter. Stir in sour cream gently until just combined. Batter will be thick but silky."
- "Pour batter into prepared pan, smooth top gently with offset spatula."
- "Place pan into foil-covered water bath carefully to avoid splashing into batter."
- "Bake for 50 minutes, jiggle test after 45 minutes. Center should quake slightly when nudged but not liquid. Don’t overbake or dry edges form."
- "Turn oven off, leave cheesecake inside with door closed 25 minutes to settle and prevent shrinkage cracks."
- "Crack oven door slightly open, cool another 20 minutes. Then room temperature on rack until fully cooled, about 1 hour."
- "Wrap tightly in plastic wrap; freeze min 2 hours for firm cutting and easier stacking."
- "=== Cake Layers ==="
- "Increase oven temp to 355°F to accommodate cake's rise."
- "Whisk together flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, ginger, salt in medium bowl."
- "In large bowl, beat brown sugar, granulated sugar, eggs on medium-high until fluffy and paler, 2-3 minutes. Add vegetable oil and vanilla, beat till emulsified."
- "Gradually add dry ingredients, mixing medium speed until combined. Batter thick and smooth."
- "Fold in grated carrots and chopped pecans by hand. Uniformly dispersed but don’t overmix batter or it stiffens."
- "Prepare two 9-inch round pans with parchment and spritz oil. Divide batter evenly using scale or by eye."
- "Bake 20-22 minutes, test doneness with toothpick; few moist crumbs but no wet batter."
- "Cool in pans 20 minutes minimum; then remove and transfer to racks until cool to touch."
- "Wrap tight and freeze 2 hours to firm layers, easier to slice level."
- "=== Cream Cheese Frosting ==="
- "Beat cream cheese and butter on high till light and fluffy, 2-3 minutes."
- "Add vanilla, continue mixing for smooth flavor blend."
- "Add powdered sugar in increments, beating thoroughly after each addition. Thick and sweet."
- "Drizzle heavy cream to adjust consistency. Should be spreadable but hold shape. Too thin? Add more powdered sugar."
- "Reserve 1/2 cup frosting separately in piping bag fitted with star tip or Ziploc with snipped corner for decoration."
- "Fold pecans into remaining frosting for textured crumb coat layer."
- "=== Assembly ==="
- "Trim dome from cake layers using serrated knife to level. Flat layers stack better."
- "Place first cake layer on platter. Spread thin even coat of pecan-frosting to anchor cheesecake layer."
- "Transfer cheesecake layer from freezer to cake, place gently. Thin layer of frosting atop cheesecake to stick next layer."
- "Place second cake layer on top, press lightly, spread thin frosting coat. Freeze entire stacked layers 1 hour minimum to set before final frosting."
- "Remove cake from freezer. Mound remaining frosting on top and sides. Use offset spatula; smooth over entire cake. Work quickly so frosting stays spreadable, but no sliding."
- "Pipe swirls around top edge about 1 inch apart. Each swirl gets a pecan half if using garnish."
- "Keep refrigerated until serving, bring to room temp 30 minutes before slicing for best texture."
- "=== Tips and Variations ==="
- "Substitute mascarpone for half cream cheese in cheesecake for silkier texture. Watch baking time reduction by 5 minutes."
- "Applesauce or crushed pineapple (drained) may replace oil in cake for less fat but expect denser crumb."
- "Water bath essential to avoid cracked cheesecake; aluminum foil seals bottom prevent water intrusion. Tin foil tears lose water bath benefit."
- "Chilling layers is not optional; firm layers reduce crumbs and slippage during frosting and stacking."
- "If cheesecake cracks appear, gently cover with a thin layer of frosting on sides, or fill crack with chopped pecans before stacking cake."
- "Use fresh grated carrots; shredded pre-packaged carrots often wet and affect batter consistency."
- "If frosting too thick, add cream one teaspoon at a time; too thin? More powdered sugar patiently."
- "To soften frozen layers quickly, unwrap and leave at room temp 20-30 minutes before assembling or serving."
