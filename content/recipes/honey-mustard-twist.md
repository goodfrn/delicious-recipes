ingredients:
- "1/3 cup mayonnaise"
- "2 tbsp Dijon mustard"
- "2 tbsp maple syrup"
- "1 tbsp white wine vinegar"
- "1/2 tsp smoked paprika"
- "1/2 tsp garlic powder"
- "1/4 tsp salt"
instructions:
- "Break mayo and Dijon mustard into a small mixing bowl first. Start combining with a fork — thick, sticky, wanting to separate. The fat in mayo stabilizes the mixture."
- "Add maple syrup in a slow drizzle, not a flood. Sweetness can overwhelm if rushed. Stir gently to fold in evenly but keep voluminous texture."
- "Pour white wine vinegar next. Sharp aroma hits the nose. Stir briskly, breaking up any clumps of garlic powder and paprika that may land too dry."
- "Sprinkle smoked paprika evenly across. Bitter edges round out sweet layers. Garlic powder next—dust it on the sides too, to avoid clumping."
- "Salt last. Helps everything sing, but too much kills the balance here. Start small, then taste, adjusting as you go."
- "Whisk vigorously now. Sharp circular motions. Look for sauce to thicken noticeably and shimmer under the light with oil droplets embracing powders — emulsion forming."
- "If mixture feels too thick, add tiny splashes of cold water or vinegar, stir carefully. Texture should coat the back of a spoon, not runnny nor caked."
- "Cover bowl and let chill 5-10 minutes to marry flavors. You’ll notice depth evolving — sharp acidity settling into sweet notes."
- "Store leftover sauce in airtight container in fridge. Stir before reuse if separation occurs."

White wine vinegar replaces apple cider for a cleaner, sharper acidic punch—distilled vinegar too harsh and masks other flavors.
