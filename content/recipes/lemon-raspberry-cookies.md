ingredients:
- "1 cup unsalted butter softened"
- "2/3 cup granulated sugar"
- "1 tablespoon lemon zest finely grated"
- "2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "2 large eggs"
- "2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1/2 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking powder"
- "1/4 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1/4 teaspoon cream of tartar"
- "3/4 cup frozen raspberries gently chopped"
- "1 cup powdered sugar"
- "2 to 3 tablespoons lemon juice or heavy cream for glaze"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 345°F for a slight slow bake. Line baking sheets with parchment; keeps bottoms even and clean. Butter dough can stick and burn without it."
- "In large bowl, cream butter and sugar with paddle attachment or whisk vigorously for 1 to 2 minutes. Should be pale, fluffy - this traps pockets of air that lighten crumb."
- "Add lemon zest, lemon juice, vanilla, and eggs one at a time. Beat after each addition until fluffy consistency. You'll feel dough get lighter and slightly puffy; key for texture."
- "In separate bowl, whisk flour, salt, baking powder, baking soda, and cream of tartar thoroughly. Sifting optional but recommended to avoid lumps."
- "Slowly add dry ingredients to wet mixture. Mix on low until just combined. Over-mixing develops gluten making cookies tough. Stop when flour streaks disappear."
- "Remove frozen raspberries from freezer. Roughly chop — do not puree or fully mash. Gentle chopping avoids total bleeding but enough to have those vibrant red streaks."
- "Fold raspberries carefully into dough. Use a spatula, not a whisk, to prevent berry juices from breaking down butter prematurely. Expect some red bleed;"
- "Scooping dough: use a 1-tablespoon scoop for smaller bites, spacing cookies about 2 inches apart. For chunkier cookies, use 3 tablespoons scoop; space at least 3 inches."
- "Bake at 345°F between 10 and 16 minutes depending on size. Look for soft edges that start to color golden and tops that are puffy but not cracking. Should spring back slightly on touch."
- "Remove sheets immediately after baking; let rest 5 to 8 minutes to firm up before transferring to wire rack. Warm cookies fragile, too soft to move without breaking."
- "While cooling, whisk together powdered sugar and 2 tablespoons lemon juice to form glaze. Add lemon juice or heavy cream slowly to reach drizzle stage, thin but not too runny."
- "Drizzle glaze over fully cooled cookies. Let set at room temp about 20 minutes until glaze hardens slightly but remains tender. Store in airtight container, separate layers with parchment."
