ingredients:
- "1 cup pumpkin puree"
- "3/4 cup half and half (substitute evaporated milk)"
- "2 tablespoons arrowroot powder (instead of cornstarch)"
- "3/4 cup granulated sugar"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1 teaspoon ground cinnamon"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg"
- "1/4 teaspoon salt"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1 tablespoon bourbon (optional twist)"
- "Non-stick cooking spray"
instructions:
- "Set oven to 320 degrees Fahrenheit. Not too hot; slow and low avoids cracking the custard surface."
- "Lightly grease a 9-inch pie pan. Too much oil pools, too little pie will stick; a thin even layer works best."
- "In a small bowl, mix the half and half with arrowroot powder. Stir until no lumps remain. Arrowroot swells better than cornstarch, thicker at lower temps; prevents gritty texture."
- "In a large bowl, crack the eggs; whisk vigorously. Gradually add sugar, salt, spices, vanilla, and bourbon. Then fold in pumpkin puree until homogeneous."
- "Add arrowroot mixture into pumpkin blend. Whisk again until perfectly smooth; lumps mean uneven bake."
- "Pour into prepared pie pan. Tap gently to release air bubbles; bubbles cause holes in final texture."
- "Bake 1 hour 10 minutes. Around 1 hour check with clean knife. Insert gently near center; if knife comes out clean and custard wobbles slightly but doesn’t run, it’s done. Overbaked pie cracks and becomes dry, underbaked stays liquid in center."
- "Cool on wire rack until room temperature. Cooling allows structure to set; warm pie isn’t slice-ready."
- "Chill in fridge if desired for firmer slices. Serve topped with whipped cream or chopped toasted pecans for crunch."
