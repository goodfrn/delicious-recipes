- "1 box vanilla cake mix ; altered batter with almond extract"
- "3 large eggs ; substitute 2 large eggs for fluffier texture"
- "1 cup water"
- "1/3 cup vegetable oil"
- "24 sugar cones"

- "24 Fudge Stripe cookies ; swap with ginger snap cookies for spice"
- "Green gel food coloring"
- "Optional sprinkle decorations like colored sugar or edible glitter"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 355F for slightly faster browning effects ; rack in lower third position."
- "Prepare cake batter following box directions mixing eggs, oil, water, plus a teaspoon almond extract to add flavor depth."
- "Transfer batter to a piping bag or plastic bag with corner cut off. Fill cones about two thirds full. Too full risks overflow and rough edges."
- "Place cones upright on a parchment-lined baking sheet. Bake 12 to 17 minutes watch for top springs back when touched lightly with fingertip. Insert toothpick if unsure to avoid drying out."
- "Cool cones completely on wire rack before trimming flat tops to avoid crumbling. Use a serrated knife, sawing gently around edge to level."
- "Frost flat tops with vanilla buttercream. Cap with cookie, press slightly to secure."
- "Allow frosting to set fully — chilling a few minutes helps firm the surface to avoid smears later."
- "Flip cones upside down for decorating base. Apply thin crumb coat of frosting and chill to lock crumbs in place."
- "Fill piping bag with green buttercream dyed with gel color. Using star tip, pipe tight swirls forming Christmas tree shape."
- "Use assorted decorative sprinkles or edible glitter as tree ornaments before frosting sets."
- "Rest finished cones chilled or cool environment to stabilize frosting structure before serving."
- "Store leftovers covered loosely at room temperature to preserve cake moisture but keep cone crisp. Avoid refrigeration, frosting may harden excessively."
