ingredients:
- "1 box chocolate cake mix"
- "3 large eggs"
- "½ cup melted butter instead vegetable oil"
- "2 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa powder replace part of cake mix"
- "½ cup semi-sweet chocolate chunks"
- "cooking spray"
- "parchment paper"
instructions:
- "=== Preparation ==="
- "Oven set 345°F. An 8×8 pan. Spray well. Line with parchment. Don't skip lining; it stops sticking nightmares. Trim parchment edges to fit snugly—no folds."
- "=== Mixing ==="
- "In big bowl: Crack eggs, pour melted butter not vegetable oil—better flavor and chew. Dump cake mix minus 2 tablespoons replaced by cocoa powder (fold cocoa in dry before mixing with wet). Stir together but don’t overwork. Folding chocolate chunks last, gently. Keep batter thick, shiny, clumpy. If it’s too runny, you've added too much liquid or not enough cocoa; add a pinch of cocoa powder or more cake mix."
- "=== Baking ==="
- "Spread batter evenly. Tap pan on counter to settle air bubbles. Place mid-oven rack. Listen for quiet crackle as brownies bake. Timer: 18–23 minutes, but check sooner. Toothpick must come out with some moist crumbs, not wet batter. Top should be matte, cracked, not shiny or wet. Edges will pull from pan sides, a golden-brown signal. If you rush, brownies dry; too slow and they fudge will be missing."
- "=== Cooling and Serving ==="
- "Cool pan on wire rack 10–15 minutes minimum. Warm brownies cut easier, retain fudginess. Use parchment edges to lift. For cleaner cuts, chill for 20 minutes then use a sharp knife heated under hot water and dried between slices. Scoring top first helps control breaks."

ingredientsNote: "Cake mix shortcuts many ingredients but can taste artificial or overly sweet. Replacing oil with melted butter adds depth—don’t just dump vegetable oils for nothing. Cocoa powder added intensifies chocolate tones; use unsweetened to avoid mistakenly sweetening too much. If no chocolate chunks, chopped nuts or dried cherries also work for texture and flavor shift. Parchment lining—don’t just spray; that’s corner-cutting trouble later when brownies stick and tear. Eggs act as binder and determine brownie structure: bigger eggs = less dryness. Use room-temp eggs for best mixing and texture until batter’s even. Don’t overmix or develop gluten and you’ll avoid cake-like rubbery instead of fudgy. For vegan or allergy swaps, applesauce or flax eggs can replace some binding but expect texture shifts and less rise. Watch sugar content—cake mix often high; adding cocoa powder dulls sweet edge balance, so you might want fewer add-ins than typical brownies. Better to adjust sweetness than end bitter or too cloying."
