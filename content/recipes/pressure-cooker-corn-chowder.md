ingredients:
- "2 tbsp unsalted butter"
- "1 cup diced carrot"
- "1 cup diced celery"
- "1 medium white onion chopped"
- "1 1/2 cups diced sweet potato"
- "3 cups low sodium chicken broth"
- "1 tsp smoked paprika"
- "1/2 tsp dried thyme"
- "1 1/4 cups frozen corn kernels divided"
- "3/4 cup half and half"
- "1 tbsp cornstarch"
- "1/4 cup cold water"
- "1 tsp kosher salt"
- "1/2 tsp freshly ground black pepper"
- "Roasted red pepper strips for garnish"
- "Chopped chives for garnish"
- "Sour cream for garnish"
- "Flakey sea salt for garnish"
instructions:
- "Set Instant Pot to sauté. Melt butter until it froths and smells nutty but not brown. Watch carefully — if it darkens, toss and start again."
- "Toss in carrot, celery, onion, and diced sweet potato. Stir. Sauté 3-4 minutes until edges soften and aromatic notes blossom. Veggies should still have bite; no color change yet but softness creeping in."
- "Pour in chicken broth. Add smoked paprika and thyme. Stir well. Then fold in 1 cup frozen corn — no thawing, let kernels start softening in hot liquid."
- "Seal lid, set vent to sealing. Pressure cook high for 6 minutes. Slightly longer than usual—sweet potatoes tend to need a tad more time than regular spuds."
- "Release pressure naturally for 8 minutes. Quick release any remaining. Lid off carefully."
- "Mix cornstarch into cold water until dissolved, no lumps. Add half and half and slurry to pot."
- "Use immersion blender directly in pot. Pulse several times — don’t overblend, leave some texture for body and bite. Creaminess but not pudding."
- "Salt and pepper in. Add remaining 1/4 cup frozen corn. Switch Instant Pot to 'Keep Warm'. Stir gently, let sit 3-4 minutes. Corn cooks through, soft yet slightly crunchy."
- "Serve in warm bowls. Top with strips of roasted red pepper, chopped chives, dollop sour cream, and a pinch of flakey salt."
