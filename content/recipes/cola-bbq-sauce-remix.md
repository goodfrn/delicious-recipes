ingredients:
- "1 cup cola"
- "1 cup tomato ketchup"
- "3 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce"
- "1 tablespoon finely minced fresh onion"
- "1 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "1 teaspoon chipotle powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper"
- "1 tablespoon brown sugar"
- "1 tablespoon honey"
instructions:
- "Start with a medium saucepan over medium heat. Add cola, ketchup, Worcestershire sauce. Stir until uniform—watch how the ketchup loosens and the cola foams slightly."
- "Add minced onion, garlic powder, chipotle powder, ground black pepper, brown sugar, honey. Stir to integrate everything smoothly, no lumps."
- "Turn heat up just enough for a steady gentle boil — not a full rolling boil. Tiny bubbles will rise consistently around the edges and across the surface. This is the cue to watch closely."
- "Simmer for about 8 minutes. Sauce thickens, darkens; clings when you dip a spoon and run finger down it. Too thin? Let it go a minute or two longer but avoid scorching edges."
- "Ready when you see those bubbles slow and the sauce holds shape briefly on the spoon. It’ll coat but still pour. Remove from heat."
- "Brush on grilled proteins or veggies. Holds well as a glaze, balanced with smoky heat and subtle sweetness. Cool sauce thickens in fridge—warm it slightly to loosen."
- "If fresh onion is too pungent, sweat it first in a teaspoon of oil until translucent before adding liquids. Avoid dry powder overload by sticking to spices listed."
- "Can substitute cola with root beer or Dr Pepper for flavor twists; adjust sugar accordingly as some sodas are sweeter."
- "Use honey alternatives like maple syrup or agave nectar if unavailable. Chipotle powder adds smoky depth; smoked paprika can replace but less heat."
- "Constant stirring during simmer avoids burning—a common error. Watch texture over clock time."
- "Serve with confidence. Comments welcome if variations tried."
