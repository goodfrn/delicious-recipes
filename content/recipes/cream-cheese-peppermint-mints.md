ingredients:
- "8 ounces softened cream cheese"
- "⅔ cup softened unsalted butter"
- "1 teaspoon peppermint extract"
- "½ teaspoon maple extract"
- "4 cups powdered sugar, divided and sifted"
- "food coloring gels in desired shades"
instructions:
- "Beat softened cream cheese and butter on medium-high speed about 1 minute or until creamy without lumps; should glide easily on the beaters."
- "Add peppermint and maple extract; blend at medium speed for 1 minute, until evenly fragrant but not overmixed to avoid breaking structure."
- "Slow mixer to low; add powdered sugar gradually about ¾ cup at a time, scraping sides between additions. The texture must thicken up yet remain pliable, not dry or crumbly."
- "Raise speed to medium-high, whip 45 seconds to fully incorporate and make the mixture glossy and smooth."
- "Divide the cream cheese base evenly into 4 bowls."
- "Add 4-6 drops of each gel color to separate bowls; fold in gently until no streaks remain, uniform tones without overbeating to avoid fading colors."
- "Cover bowls tightly with plastic wrap; chill in fridge for 45 minutes until firm but still pliable—not frozen. This makes shaping easier and prevents melting."
- "Put ¾ cup powdered sugar into a medium bowl for rolling."
- "Line a baking sheet with parchment paper; dust lightly with powdered sugar to prevent sticking as mints rest."
- "Use a 1 tablespoon scoop to portion colored mixture. Roll into balls between palms. Slice each ball cleanly in half; then roll halves smaller balls again until smooth."
- "Roll each small ball in powdered sugar; shaking off excess prevents a gummy finish. Place on parchment."
- "Press fork tines gently on top of each ball for surface texture and a subtle grip. Dip fork into powdered sugar between impressions to avoid sticking."
- "Leave rolled mints on parchment at room temperature to dry for 4-5 hours; more time needed in humid climates. The touch test: firm outer shell, no tackiness, but soft-center preserved."
- "If sticky underneath, flip mints carefully onto a drying rack to finish. Do not cover while drying; air circulation is key."
- "For substitutions: cream cheese can be half Neufchâtel for lower fat; butter can be swapped with coconut oil but expect softer texture and coconut aroma."
- "If mixture too wet when adding powdered sugar, add small pinch more sugar; if too dry and crumbly, beat in a few drops milk or cream. Color fading means you overmixed or used too much extract."
- "Store finished mints in airtight container with parchment layers between. Refrigerate in warm weather to avoid melting."
- "Common mistakes: overmixing causes stiffness and color loss. Under chilling results in sticky, tough mints. Rolling immediately after chilling feels pleasant; avoid leftover greasy hands."
- "A quick aroma check after extracts blend: pure peppermint with subtle maple background, no bitter edge."
- "Adjust times based on your kitchen humidity and temperature. Use feel, not watch, to judge progress."
- "Expect some sheen and slight stickiness before rolling; smooth to touch when sugar added correctly."
- "Press fork impression too deep, and mints might break. Light, shallow pressure is enough."
- "When working with food colors, gels hold better than liquids; avoid too much for runniness."
- "Roll balls evenly sized to ensure consistent drying. Smaller mints dry faster but risk being brittle."
- "Always sift powdered sugar to remove lumps for consistent texture."
