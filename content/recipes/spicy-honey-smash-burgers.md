ingredients:
- "4 frozen beef burger patties (about 4-5 oz each)"
- "4 slices sharp white cheddar cheese"
- "4 burger buns (brioche recommended but any soft bun works)"
- "1 cup romaine lettuce leaves, chopped (sub leaf lettuce or kale for crunch)"
- "3 tbsp smoked chili honey (sub regular hot honey with a pinch of smoked paprika)"
- "4 tbsp garlic aioli (replace chipotle mayo for mellow garlic flavor)"
- "2 tbsp harissa paste (swap hot pepper spread for harissa for layered heat)"
- "Butter for toasting buns (optional)"
- "Salt and pepper for seasoning"
instructions:
- "Heat large skillet over medium heat; wait for surface to shimmer — no smoke yet."
- "Add frozen patties directly; listen for sharper sizzle and watch juices start to surface mostly edges then top after 5-7 minutes. Avoid pressing down; juices mean moisture retention."
- "Season tops with salt and pepper once juices appear but before flipping."
- "Flip patties with spatula, sear opposite side until browned with faint crust edges, about 3-5 minutes. Look for firm but springy touch to gauge doneness."
- "Lay cheese slices on burgers; cover skillet tightly with lid or foil to trap steam so cheese melts without drying around edges, about 1-2 minutes. Avoid prolonged covered heat, can make cheese rubbery."
- "Meanwhile, optionally toast buns in separate pan with butter; but keep it quick to preserve softness."
- "Remove burgers from pan to plate; let rest 3 minutes at room temp — juices reintegrate avoiding dry bite."
- "Spread garlic aioli on bottom halves, drizzle smoked chili honey directly over lettuce, dot harissa paste on bun tops for layered spice hit."
- "Assemble: lettuce, honey drizzle, burger patty with melted cheddar, then bun cap with harissa."
- "Eat promptly; melty cheese and spicy-sweet layers best warm."
- "If burgers cook unevenly indoors, rotate pan or adjust heat. Frozen patties allow more moisture retention but need patience for juices to appear as doneness guide."
