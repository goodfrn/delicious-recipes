ingredients:
- "1 lb ground turkey sausage"
- "1 refrigerated pizza dough, roughly 11x14 inches when unrolled"
- "4 large eggs"
- "4 oz ricotta cheese"
- "2 tablespoons half-and-half"
- "1 cup marinara sauce"
- "1 1/2 cups shredded smoked mozzarella cheese"
- "Salt and black pepper, to taste"
- "Nonstick spray or parchment paper"
instructions:
- "===Prep==='"
- "Heat oven to 395° F. Use parchment or spray baking sheet well to stop crust sticking. Ground turkey sausage cooks differently—don’t rush, break up meat with fork, listen for sizzling and smell perspiring fat—indicates it's done. Drain on paper towel to avoid soggy base."
- "===Crust==='"
- "Stretch dough on pan, aim for rectangle close to 11x14 inches. Par-bake about 10 minutes. Look for edges turning gold but center still soft and pliable. If bubble forms in crust, prick with fork. Avoid overbaking here; flatbread must finish baking later, crisp but not cracker hard."
- "===Eggs==='"
- "Whisk eggs, salt, and pepper. Use medium-low heat, slow scramble encourages tender curds—no rubbery lumps. Take off stove just before fully done since they’ll finish cooking later. Keep warm in bowl."
- "===Ricotta Base==='"
- "Heat ricotta and half-and-half in microwave or stovetop just to loosen—don’t overheat or it separates. Stir till creamy but slightly loose for easy spreading. Smear evenly over par-baked crust, skip heavy ladles—thin layer works best beneath toppings."
- "===Assembly & Finish==='"
- "Spoon sauce lightly atop ricotta layer. Scatter cooked turkey sausage evenly so every bite has meat. Sprinkle eggs next in random clumps, patchy looks better than uniform – adds texture contrast. Lastly, cover with shredded smoked mozzarella. Place flatbread back in oven—about 4 to 7 minutes. Watch cheese carefully—bubbles and just beginning to brown indicate done. Can broil for 1 minute to finish but keep eye on it, strings burn fast."
- "Let rest a few minutes for flavors to settle and cheese to firm slightly before slicing. Avoid cutting hot flatbread—it can tear and the juices run off."
