ingredients:
- "12 ounces bowtie pasta (farfalle) or fusilli, for better sauce cling"
- "8 cups chopped romaine lettuce, coarse pieces for crunch"
- "3 cups diced grilled turkey breast, skin removed, cooled"
- "1 cup shaved pecorino romano cheese, crumbly texture"
- "1 1/2 cups garlicky croutons, homemade or store-bought"
- "6 ounces crisp pancetta, diced and rendered"
- "1 cup creamy Caesar-style dressing with Dijon mustard and Worcestershire"
- "1 lemon, zested and juiced"
- "Freshly cracked black pepper, to taste"
- "Kosher salt for pasta water"
instructions:
- "Bring large pot of salted water to a roiling boil, about 2 tablespoons salt per quart. Toss in bowtie pasta. Stir occasionally. Cook until just tender, test after 8 minutes for bite but no chew. Drain in colander. Run cold water over pasta until completely cooled; stops cooking, firms texture, prevents stickiness."
- "Dry pasta well with paper towels or let drain thoroughly; soggy pasta dilutes dressing, soggy salad."
- "In a large bowl, combine chopped romaine lettuce and cooled pasta. Add grilled turkey pieces and rendered pancetta."
- "Add shaved pecorino and croutons last, toss gently but thoroughly. Pecorino brings saltiness; croutons add crunch and garlic aroma."
- "Pour creamy Caesar dressing over salad in increments. Toss and fold, not mush. Adjust amount to your desired creaminess but avoid overload; dressing should coat, not drown."
- "Finish with cracked black pepper and a generous squeeze of fresh lemon juice. Lemon adds bright acidity, cuts through richness."
- "Taste test. If salad tastes flat, add a pinch more salt, another splash of lemon, or extra cracked pepper. Fresh cracked pepper is key."
- "Serve immediately or refrigerate briefly but no longer than 2 hours to preserve crunch. Before serving after chill, toss lightly again to redistribute dressing."
- "Common swap if pancetta not available: use smoked turkey bacon or omit for lighter. Pecorino can replace Parmesan but expect sharper bite. If croutons unavailable, dry toast cubed bread with olive oil and garlic powder."
- "If cooked pasta sticks together, toss with a spoonful of olive oil after rinsing. Keep salad crisp by draining any excess moisture from rinsing or dressing excess."
- "Leftovers separate quickly; re-toss before serving to restore texture."
