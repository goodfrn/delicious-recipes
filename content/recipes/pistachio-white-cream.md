ingredients:
- "1 cup raw pistachios shelled"
- "1 cup blanched almonds shelled"
- "1/3 cup heavy cream divided"
- "4 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "1/2 cup white chocolate chips"
- "3 tablespoons powdered sugar"
- "1/4 teaspoon flaky sea salt"
- "1 small fresh basil leaf minced (optional)"
instructions:
- "Fill medium saucepan halfway with water; bring to gentle boil. Throw in pistachios and almonds. Stir and simmer for 5 minutes until skins loosen. Listen for faint crackles and water movement changing from aggressive boil to gentler bubble."
- "Drain nuts quickly in colander. Spread out on towel; cover with another towel and roll nuts between them, pressing firmly. Skins should break free easily. Remove as much papery skin as possible. Bits left behind alter texture; nutty oils become dull if skipped."
- "Into high-speed blender, toss skinned nuts with half the cream (about 1/6 cup). Pulse short bursts until coarse paste. Avoid over-blending or paste becomes gluey. Stop when you see clumps start binding but texture still grainy."
- "Melt butter and remaining cream (about 1/6 cup) in small saucepan over low heat. Heat till steam forms and sides shimmer. Add white chocolate chips off direct heat. Stir gently till glossy and melted. No lumps, no overheating – chocolate burns fast and smells bitter."
- "Add melted chocolate mixture, powdered sugar, salt, and basil leaf into blender with nut paste. Blend for about 60 seconds. Stop often and scrape sides with silicone spatula to avoid uneven mixing. Goal: creamy texture with visible flecks, not pureed."
- "Transfer to airtight container. Chill minimum 1 hour to let flavors meld and body firm up. Skip chilling and sauce stays loose, less rich mouthfeel. Serve cold or just off-cold for a soft, luscious coating on desserts."
- "If nut skins prove stubborn, briefly soak in warm water before blanching again. Substitute salted butter for unsalted but omit salt later. White chocolate can be swapped for milk chocolate for a caramel tone; reduce sugar by 1 tablespoon then."
- "Stray bits of skins or browned nuts create speckles visible on pale cream. Not a flaw but affects presentation. Grind to medium-fine, never powder. Work fast to prevent nut oils from overheating and turning bitter."
- "Keep blender cool by pulsing in short bursts, using a damp towel to hold base if needed. Overworking heats ingredients resulting in graininess or separation."
