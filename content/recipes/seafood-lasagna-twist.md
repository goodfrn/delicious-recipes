ingredients:
- "12 lasagna noodles"
- "6 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "4 cloves garlic minced"
- "4 tablespoons all-purpose flour"
- "1 teaspoon kosher salt divided"
- "1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper divided"
- "1 cup dry white vermouth or dry sherry"
- "3 cups whole milk at room temperature"
- "15 ounces ricotta cheese"
- "1 large egg"
- "1 teaspoon dried oregano"
- "1 tablespoon freshly squeezed lemon juice"
- "¾ cup finely grated pecorino romano cheese"
- "8 ounces cooked shrimp peeled and deveined"
- "8 ounces lump crab meat picked over for shells"
- "4 cups shredded mozzarella cheese"
- "2 tablespoons chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 345°F. The slightly reduced temp helps gradual bubbling without burning cheese edges."
- "Bring a large pot of heavily salted water to a rolling boil. Add noodles carefully to prevent sticking. Cook until just shy of tender—firm bite still. Drain and rinse under cold water to halt cooking and remove excess starch. Set aside flat on baking sheet to avoid clumping."
- "In a heavy-bottomed saucepan, melt butter over medium heat until foaming subsides. Add minced garlic, sauté until it begins to turn golden, about 90 seconds. Be wary—garlic burns fast; color is your guide."
- "Sprinkle flour gradually, stirring constantly with a whisk to prevent lumps. Add half the salt and pepper. Cook for 2 to 3 minutes, stirring often until roux smells nutty and just starts to brown; this step tightens your sauce’s body and depth."
- "Slowly pour in vermouth first, whisking vigorously to combine and lift any browned bits from the pan's base. Gradually add milk while continuing to whisk. The gradual addition prevents clumps and promotes a glossy sauce finish."
- "Crank heat to medium-high, whisk constantly. You’ll hear low bubbling and see the sauce thicken, swirling smoothly around your spoon. Once simmering, reduce to medium and cook another 2 minutes. Sauce should coat the back of a spoon cleanly but not glue to it—aim for fluid thickness."
- "In a bowl, blend ricotta, egg, remaining salt and pepper, oregano, lemon juice, and pecorino romano. The acid brightens flavors and keeps cheese mix vibrant. Incorporate fully but don’t over-mix; keep texture a bit chunky not paste-like."
- "Spread ¼ cup of sauce over the bottom of a 9x13 inch casserole. Lay three noodles across to cover. Layer half the ricotta mixture over noodles, spread evenly but gently to preserve some texture."
- "Distribute half of the shrimp and crab atop ricotta. Seafood placement matters for even bites—avoid pile-ups that slow internal cooking."
- "Drizzle about one cup of sauce over seafood layer, spreading evenly. Sprinkle one cup mozzarella on top; use fingers or fork tines to fluff cheese for better melt and browning."
- "Repeat layering noodles, remaining ricotta, remaining seafood. Cover with another cup of sauce and mozzarella layer. Press slight but don’t compact—air pockets help melt cheese evenly."
- "Final layer: three noodles to cover last cheese layer snugly. Top with rest of white sauce, spreading evenly. Finish with two cups mozzarella. The top must be thick enough for golden browning but not overloaded to prevent sogginess."
- "Bake uncovered for 30 to 45 minutes. Look for bubbling sauce at edges and cheese turning golden with spots of deep tan—avoid burnt black spots. The smell will sharpen; seafood aroma mingled with garlic-butter richness signals readiness."
- "Let rest at least 15 minutes after removal. Resting firms layers, allowing cleaner slices and better flavor integration. Resist temptation to cut too soon; messier, runnier layers result otherwise."
- "Garnish with fresh parsley for visual pop and herbal brightness just before serving."
