ingredients:
- "1 can refrigerated pizza dough"
- "½ cup tomato basil sauce"
- "1 cup cooked chorizo, crumbled"
- "20 slices sliced salami"
- "1½ cups shredded provolone cheese"
- "Parchment paper"
- "Garlic aioli for dipping (optional)"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 395 degrees Fahrenheit. Line large baking sheet with parchment paper; helps with crisp bottom, easy cleanup."
- "Unroll dough onto parchment. Press and stretch dough paper-thin but not tearing; uniform thickness ensures even bake. Cold dough tightens; let sit 5 minutes if hard to stretch."
- "Spread tomato basil sauce thinly, leaving a perimeter free to seal edges properly. Over-saucing leads to soggy base."
- "Layer crumbled chorizo evenly for spice kick. Lay sliced salami overlapped—salty, fatty contrast to chorizo heat."
- "Sprinkle shredded provolone. Provolone melts distinct, sharper than mozzarella; watch for browning."
- "Starting from long edge, roll dough tightly like jelly roll. Tucking edges as you go avoids leaks; sloppy rolling leads to filling seeping out during bake."
- "Move roll to center of sheet, seam side down to prevent unraveling. Gently pinch seams closed—weak seal equals filling mess."
- "Use sharp knife to cut 5-7 diagonal slits across top for steam venting. Too few? Filling steams; crust soggy. Too many? Filling escapes, dry roll."
- "Slide baking sheet into oven. Listen to faint crackle as crust firms up—about 20-25 minutes. Crust turns deep golden with tiny darker speckles; bubbling cheese peeking through slits."
- "Remove when crust feels firm but not rock hard. Let rest 5 minutes—cutting too hot, cheese slides out messy, burns fingers."
- "Slice into 6 generous portions. Serve warm, with garlic aioli on side if you want dip complexity; acidic, creamy contrast."
- "If dough sticks, use bench scraper, dust surface lightly with flour next time. Avoid overfilling to maintain roll integrity."
- "Oven hot spots? Rotate pan halfway for even browning."
