ingredients:
- "3 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "4 cloves garlic minced fine"
- "2 1/2 tablespoons all-purpose flour"
- "1 cup heavy cream"
- "1/2 cup whole milk"
- "4 ounces cream cheese softened"
- "1 teaspoon onion powder"
- "1 teaspoon kosher salt adjust to taste"
- "1 teaspoon herbes de Provence substituted for Italian seasoning"
- "3/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese"
- "1 pound shrimp peeled and deveined"
- "8 ounces fettuccine cooked al dente"
- "2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley"
- "1 teaspoon smoked paprika new twist replacing some salt"
instructions:
- "Heat a 10-inch deep skillet over medium-high. Melt butter till bubbly and fragrant but not browned yet."
- "Add minced garlic. Stir continuously, listen for that soft sizzling, and smell when garlic hits its aromatic peak—roughly 45-50 seconds. Don’t scorch."
- "Sprinkle in the flour evenly. Whisk nonstop to blend with butter and garlic. Look for a pale roux bubbling gently but no color change yet, about 1 to 1 1/4 minutes."
- "Pour in heavy cream and milk slowly while whisking aggressively to prevent lumps. The mixture will thin at first. Keep whisking firmly until it thickens slightly and coats the back of the spoon. Should take about 2 to 2 1/2 minutes on medium heat. Patience here builds body."
- "Cube cream cheese and whisk into the sauce off-heat to melt smoothly. Avoid direct heat to prevent curdling or graininess. Stir till fully incorporated and velvety."
- "Season with onion powder, herbes de Provence, smoked paprika, and kosher salt. Adjust salt carefully here—it can amplify or flatten flavors."
- "Remove the sauce base from heat and fold in grated Parmesan cheese gradually. Stir until melted and integrated, sauce should thicken more and look glossy."
- "In a separate nonstick skillet over medium-high heat, add a splash of oil. Sauté shrimp for 2 to 4 minutes, turning once. Watch the color jump from grey to pink with slight opacity and firm but springy texture. Overcook and they toughen instantly."
- "Drain pasta al dente, reserving a little pasta water. Toss fettuccine into Alfredo sauce while still warm to coat evenly. If too thick, add teaspoon increments of pasta water to loosen."
- "Top pasta with shrimp gently, fold through once to coat but don’t break them up. Sprinkle parsley on top for fresh bite and color. Serve piping hot."
