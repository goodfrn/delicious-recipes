ingredients:
- "=== Pecan Pie Crust ==="
- "1 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1 cup whole wheat flour"
- "1/2 tsp salt"
- "1/2 cup chilled coconut oil, grated or cut into chunks"
- "1/4 cup chilled vegetable shortening, grated or cut into chunks"
- "5-6 tbsp ice cold water"
- "=== Pecan Pie Filling ==="
- "3/4 cup muscovado sugar"
- "2/3 cup pure maple syrup"
- "4 tbsp unsalted butter"
- "1 tbsp all-purpose flour"
- "1 tsp vanilla extract"
- "1/2 tsp ground cinnamon"
- "1/4 tsp ground cardamom"
- "3 large eggs"
- "1 1/2 cups halves or chopped pecans"
instructions:
- "=== Pecan Pie Crust ==="
- "Start by whisking the all-purpose and whole wheat flours with salt ; must be evenly distributed. Grate or cut chilled coconut oil and shortening into flour mix. Goal : coarse crumbs visible, some pea-sized bits for flaky layers. Use pastry cutter or finger tips, avoid warming fats."
- "Sprinkle ice water a tablespoon at a time while folding dough gently ; stop as soon as it holds together without being sticky. Overwork and you lose flakiness ; underwork and it falls apart."
- "Pat dough into thick disk ; wrap in plastic and chill minimum 45 minutes. Crucial to rest fats to prevent shrinking and toughness."
- "Prepare 8x8 pan by lining with parchment paper, stabilize edges by dabbing water underneath — stops slipping when rolling crust."
- "On lightly floured surface, roll dough evenly to about 3mm thick ; dust rolling pin too so no sticking. Transfer carefully by draping over rolling pin or folding over twice if needed ; aim for even thickness."
- "Patch surface with fork tines all over crust ; lets steam escape and stops bubbling in oven."
- "Return crust to fridge for 15 minutes to firm back up before baking."
- "Oven at 435°F now ; bake crust 12-17 minutes till edges just start to brown and surface looks matte, slightly crisp to touch but not hard. You want raw dough cooked through but not overbaked. Remove and decrease oven temp to 345°F"
- "=== Pecan Pie Filling ==="
- "In small pan, combine muscovado sugar, maple syrup, butter, 1 tbsp flour, vanilla, cinnamon and cardamom. Heat gently, stir constantly until sugar dissolves and mixture thickens slightly ; watch for bubbling edges but no boiling over. Aroma should bloom with spices and butter richness."
- "Whisk eggs separately while tempering ; add a spoonful of hot mixture to eggs while whisking briskly to raise egg temp gradually. Pour eggs back into pot ; whisk over low heat till fully combined and slightly glossy. Don’t let egg scramble — remove from heat at first hint of clumps."
- "Scatter pecans evenly over baked crust, press down lightly to embed. Pour filling evenly over pecans, spread gently with spatula if needed to fill corners. Pecans float up slightly, creating uneven but attractive texture."
- "Bake bars 43-48 minutes ; edges should caramelize darkly without burning, center set but with slight jiggle — a toothpick pulled should come out with moist crumbs but no raw batter. If edges brown too fast, loosely tent foil."
- "Cool fully on rack before cutting ; bars firm up and slice cleaner. Warm bars tend to sag and spill."
- "Store leftovers covered in fridge ; coconut oil may harden so bring to room temp before serving for softer bite."

- "Watch the crust rolling. 3mm thick, even all around. Dusting flour prevents sticking, no pressure. Patch with fork tines to prevent bubbles or explosions."
- "Resting dough is key. Chill after mixing, again before baking. Avoid shrinking. If it cracks, that’s a sign — too warm. Dough better firm."
- "Filling needs tempering. Hot mixture slowly into eggs, then mix back. No scrambling. Quick whisking, watch for clumps. Texture is essential — creamy not curdled."
