ingredients:
- "2 cups granulated sugar"
- "2/3 cup honey (sub for corn syrup)"
- "1/2 cup water"
- "3 large egg whites"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1/4 teaspoon salt"
- "1/2 cup chopped pecans (optional, for twist)"
instructions:
- "==="
- "Set heavy-bottomed saucepan on medium. Combine sugar honey water. Stir gently till sugar dissolves. Insert candy thermometer. Watch temp carefully, no stirring past 250°F. Stop heating when hitting 265°F—the soft ball stage gives just right texture; too little and candy falls apart, too much and it turns grainy."
- "==="
- "Meanwhile, crack egg whites into dry, grease-free bowl. Whisk using electric mixer on medium-high until frothy then stiff peaks form. Peaks should hold shape, not droop. Any yolk traces ruin whip—clean equipment vital."
- "==="
- "Immediately switch mixer to paddle attachment or beaters mode. With mixer running low speed pour in hot syrup slowly. Use a measuring cup with spout for control. Pour in thin stream so whites absorb heat evenly without scrambling. Listen and watch: mixture thickens, gloss fades from bright white to dull satin."
- "==="
- "Continue mixing for 7-10 minutes. Mixture becomes dense but still pliable. Test by dropping spoonful on parchment—holds shape but feels soft. If it slumps or puddles, mix longer. Overmix and it hardens like taffy. Under—melts with warmth."
- "==="
- "Fold in salt vanilla nut chunks. Nuts add crunch, deepen flavor. Ground almonds or chopped hazelnuts work too. Skip nuts for pure chewy candy."
- "==="
- "Spray two spoons lightly with cooking spray. Drop mounds roughly 1 tablespoon each onto parchment-lined tray. Aim for 35-40 pieces. Leave space—they won't spread much."
- "==="
- "Set aside at room temperature, no cover. Let dry for 10-12 hours. Candy firms by losing moisture not chilling. If humid or sticky, dry longer or place near fan. Store in airtight container once set. Refrigeration ruins texture."
- "==="
- "Notes: Syrup temperature is key. Use candy thermometer and trust sight and feel. Honey adds subtle floral notes versus corn syrup’s neutral lump-preventing. Nuts toasted for nuttier aroma but raw are fine. Whites whisked separate then combined slowly trap air and moisture creating the cloudlike structure of divinity."
