ingredients:
- "4 sturdy deli rolls, crusty"
- "3 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened"
- "1.5 lb boneless chicken thighs, sliced thin"
- "2 teaspoons dried Italian seasoning"
- "1 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "1/2 teaspoon freshly cracked black pepper"
- "2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil"
- "1 medium green bell pepper, sliced"
- "1 medium yellow bell pepper, sliced"
- "1 medium red onion, thinly sliced"
- "4 ounces cremini mushrooms, sliced"
- "3 cloves garlic, minced"
- "6 ounces provolone cheese, sliced"
instructions:
- "Preheat broiler. Line baking sheet with parchment or foil to catch drips and for easy cleanup."
- "Using a sharp knife, carefully cut a narrow V into top and center of each roll. Avoid slicing all the way through the base; keep roll intact for fillings."
- "Spread softened butter generously inside each roll. Place rolls butter-side up on baking sheet. Broil 2-4 minutes, eyes on the bread color—toast until edges glisten golden and slightly crisp. Pull out immediately; over-toasting turns dry fast."
- "In a medium bowl, toss sliced chicken thighs with Italian seasoning, kosher salt, and black pepper until all pieces show even coating."
- "Heat 1 tablespoon olive oil in skillet over medium-high heat until it shimmers, not smoking. Add chicken in one layer, cook 5-7 minutes, turning halfway. Cook until chicken pieces no longer show pink internally and juices run clear. Remove chicken, set aside in warm bowl."
- "Return skillet to medium heat. Add remaining tablespoon olive oil and butter; once butter melts and foams, add all bell peppers and onion. Stir frequently to avoid scorch spots. After 4 minutes peppers should soften slightly, onions translucent but not browned heavily."
- "Add mushrooms and garlic; continue sautéing 3-4 minutes. Mushrooms shrink, soften, and release earthy aroma; garlic turns fragrant but not bitter—watch closely to prevent burning."
- "Return chicken to skillet; toss gently to combine, coat meat and veggies evenly with any pan juices."
- "Divide chicken-vegetable mixture equally into buttered rolls. Layer provolone slices between two layers of filling to trap melty cheese pockets."
- "Place filled sandwiches back under broiler for 1-3 minutes. Watch for cheese bubbles and gentle browning. Remove as soon as cheese melts; carryover heat finishes the melt without drying bread."
- "Serve immediately while warm, savory aromas still steam and flavors are vibrant."
