ingredients:
- "24 large white mushrooms, cleaned with damp towel, stems removed and chopped"
- "1 cup herbed cornbread stuffing, crumbled"
- "1 cup shredded fontina cheese (can substitute Monterey jack for milder melt)"
- "1/4 cup finely chopped celery"
- "1/4 cup finely chopped yellow onion"
- "2 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "1 teaspoon fresh sage, minced (use 1/2 tsp dried if fresh unavailable)"
- "1/4 teaspoon black pepper"
- "1/2 teaspoon salt"
- "1/2 cup leftover turkey gravy (replace dressing if none)"
- "1/2 cup cranberry sauce, microwaved until just warm (see instructions)"
- "Nonstick cooking spray"
instructions:
- "=== Prepare Filling ==="
- "Melt butter in a skillet over medium heat. Add onions and celery. Sauté until translucent, about 4 minutes. Toss in chopped mushroom stems. Cook until moisture evaporates and mixture smells earthy, roughly 3-4 minutes. Transfer to bowl. Fold in cornbread stuffing, fontina, sage, salt, and pepper. Stir in turkey gravy slowly to keep mixture moist but not soggy."
- "=== Preheat and Prepare Pan ==="
- "Heat oven to 345°F for gentler roasting. Spray rimmed baking sheet with nonstick spray to prevent mushroom juice from sticking. Use a rimmed sheet – juices drip fast and cause burning. No plate or foil – avoid soggy bottoms."
- "=== Stuff Mushrooms ==="
- "Pat mushrooms dry with a kitchen towel. If too wet, filling slides off. Use spoon or small scoop. Heap one tablespoon of filling into each cap. Don’t underfill; risk dry edges. Pack firmly but gently."
- "Place stuffed caps on pan, spacing out for airflow. Crowding traps steam, ruins texture. Roast in middle rack."
- "=== Bake and Watch ==="
- "Bake 22 to 25 minutes. Midway through (around 12-15 minutes) listen for subtle sizzling. Juices bubbling but edges firming. Topping should start browning lightly—pale golden patchwork. Longer baking pulls out moisture but burns if left unattended. If edges crisp too fast, tent with foil."
- "=== Cranberry Sauce Finish ==="
- "Microwave cranberry sauce 20-30 seconds until runny but not hot enough to burn lips. Drizzle over hot mushrooms immediately. The warm sauce melts slightly into cheese crust; sweet-tart balance cuts richness. Serve right away. Cold sauce will make filling gummy and clumpy."
- "=== Tips to Avoid Common Issues ==="
- "Don’t wash mushrooms under running water. Pat dry only—wet mushrooms steam not roast. Dry filling ingredients mean better oven caramelization. Use leftover cornbread or bread crumbs with fresh herbs; stale bread hardens filling. No gravy? Use cream cheese or chicken broth for moisture. If filling separates, hold back liquid next batch. Watch oven heat closely; ovens vary. Use visual and tactile cues over timer. Mushrooms should shrivel minimally but not collapse."
