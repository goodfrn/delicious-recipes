ingredients:
- "2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "3 tablespoons brown sugar"
- "1/2 cup cold margarine or unsalted butter, cut into chunks"
- "1 cup fresh strawberries, diced"
- "3/4 cup half-and-half or heavy cream"
- "2 tablespoons cooking oil for brushing"
- "2 tablespoons granulated sugar for topping"
instructions:
- "Start with flour and sugar in a large bowl. Use fingers or pastry cutter to rub in butter or margarine until mixture looks sandy, like course cornmeal. Take time here; fat coats flour to keep crumbly texture from turning tough."
- "Fold in diced strawberries gently. Pour half-and-half or cream over. Mix with wooden spoon or spatula just until dough clumps up and feels sticky but not soupy. Don’t overmix or develop gluten."
- "Flour your work surface well, scrape dough out. Sprinkle some flour on top. Gently fold and knead the dough no more than 6-8 times. Stop when dough stops sticking to fingers but still soft and pliable. Add more flour sparingly — too much dries scones out."
- "Roll dough slab evenly about 1 inch thick. Cut with 2 1/2-inch round cutter dipped in flour every time before pressing to avoid sticking. Place rounds on oiled or silicone-lined baking sheet spaced apart."
- "Brush biscuit tops lightly with cooking oil for a golden, crisp shell. Sprinkle granulated sugar on top to add crunch and gloss as it bakes."
- "Bake in oven preheated to 425°F. Bake for roughly 20 minutes, check after 15. Look for golden brown tops, flaky edges pulling away slightly. Internal crumb should be moist but set; use toothpick to confirm no wet dough."
- "Cool on rack a bit before serving. Serve warm with butter or jam if you want. Store leftovers in airtight container but best fresh."
