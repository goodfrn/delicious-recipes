ingredients:
- "1 1⁄2 cups graham cracker crumbs"
- "1⁄4 cup packed light brown sugar"
- "7 tablespoons unsalted butter melted"
- "1⁄2 teaspoon ground cinnamon"
- "3 tablespoons granulated sugar"
- "3 tablespoons unsweetened Dutch-process cocoa powder"
- "2 1⁄2 tablespoons cornstarch"
- "1⁄4 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "2 1⁄2 cups whole milk divided"
- "1 teaspoon instant espresso powder"
- "3 tablespoons unsalted butter cut into pieces"
- "1 1⁄2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract"
- "For garnish: whipped cream, fresh berries, dark chocolate shavings"
instructions:
- "=== Make the Crust ==="
- "Turn oven on to 345°F. Gently grease 9 inch pie pan if dry spots. Mix graham cracker crumbs with brown sugar and cinnamon first. Melt butter separately. Pour butter in and stir fast, make sure crumbs are moist but not drenched. Press with flat bottom of glass. Pack it tight, edges included. It’s the base; don’t skimp. Pop in oven, watch for golden edges and dry feel on surface. Bake 18-22 minutes. Slightly cool on rack, crust will firm up."
- "=== Prepare the Chocolate Pudding ==="
- "Combine sugars, cocoa, cornstarch, salt, and espresso powder dry in mid-sized saucepan. Whisk cold milk (2 cups) into dry slowly, avoid lumps. Switch burner on medium-low — don’t rush, patience here. Stir nonstop. Eventually aroma thick and chocolate rich, light bubbles form at edges, steam rising steadily. Pudding will start to cling to whisk. Slowly add remaining milk (½ cup), whisk constantly as it nears boil. Hard boil not needed but do watch thickness. It must coat the back of spoon thickly, leaves clear line when run finger through. Off heat, add butter cubes and vanilla—fold until butter melts in silky sheen. Leave in pan, no hot steam left before next step."
- "=== Assemble ==="
- "Pour pudding into crust immediately. Smooth with offset spatula—try to avoid air pockets, do not tap pan hard, crust delicate when hot. Cover top directly with plastic wrap. Press gently to remove air bubbles; this stops pudding skin forming and keeps top super creamy. Fridge for minimum 2.5 hours, 3 to 4 better. Firm set desired, jiggle free. Garnish with softly whipped cream (hint of sugar), seasonal berries for slight acidity, shards of dark (70%+) chocolate, finish bright, complex flavors."
- "=== Tips and Tweaks ==="
- "Brown sugar and cinnamon swapped for vanilla sugar or maple syrup for alternate crust flavor. Butter can be replaced with coconut oil, but crust might be crumblier. Espresso powder optional but multiply chocolate flavor enormously; don't skip if you want bite. Use Dutch-process cocoa over natural for darker, more balanced flavor. If pudding chunks or lumps appear, strain before adding butter. Need to speed chilling? Freeze for 45 minutes but not beyond or texture suffers."
- "=== Troubleshooting ==="
- "Crust falling apart? Press harder or add a pinch of salt; buttery flavor sharpens and helps set. Pudding too thin? Keep cooking, whisk longer; cornstarch activation crucial. Too thick? Stir in more milk off heat cautiously. Skin forms even with wrap? Wrap must touch surface fully; avoid wrinkles. Wrinkled wrap means trapped air."
- "=== Serving ==="
- "Cut with sharp, warm knife for clean slices. Best eaten same day. Leftovers keep well refrigerated 2 days; texture softens under chill."
