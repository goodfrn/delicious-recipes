ingredients:
- "1 can black refried beans drained and smashed"
- "4 tablespoons water"
- "1 cup premade guacamole"
- "3/4 cup Mexican crema or sour cream substitute"
- "1 tablespoon lime juice"
- "1 teaspoon chipotle chili powder"
- "1 cup chunky medium salsa drained well"
- "1 1/4 cups shredded Monterey Jack and cheddar blend"
- "1 cup shredded romaine lettuce"
- "1/3 cup drained sliced black olives"
- "3/4 cup chopped roma tomatoes patted dry"
- "1/2 cup thinly sliced jicama instead of green onions"
- "Tortilla chips for serving"
instructions:
- "Drain beans if liquid visible; mix with water until creamy but not runny. Overly thick beans make spreading tough; add drops of water. Spread evenly in 9×13 dish with offset spatula pressing gently to prevent air pockets."
- "Spread guacamole layer over beans. Not too thick or it overwhelms next layers. Smooth with silicone spatula, you'll see slight sheen when evenly coated."
- "In bowl, whisk crema with lime juice and chipotle powder until blended. Chipotle adds smoky heat, adjust carefully so it doesn’t overpower mild layers."
- "Carefully spoon crema mix over guacamole layer. Use back of spoon to avoid piercing guacamole."
- "Drain salsa through fine mesh strainer, tap to remove excess moisture. Directly spreading wet salsa brings soggy dip. Spread salsa evenly over crema layer. Small chunks should remain intact but no excess juice pools."
- "Sprinkle shredded cheese immediately over salsa to adhere layer, preventing cheese from sliding. Cheese melt not required here; raw adds textural contrast."
- "Add shredded romaine lettuce, spread loosely but cover all cheese. Romaine stays crisp unlike iceberg which wilts fast."
- "Scatter black olives evenly, pitted and drained. Their briny bite cuts richness but too many make it salty."
- "Top with chopped tomatoes, gently pat with paper towel to remove juice so bottom layers stay firm, no dripping."
- "Finish with thin jicama slices. Crunchy, slightly sweet, clean bite replaces usual green onion sharpness, refreshing twist."
- "Cover dish tightly with plastic wrap to prevent drying. Chill in fridge minimum one hour to let layers settle and flavors come together. Serve cold with sturdy, thick tortilla chips to scoop through layers without breakage."
