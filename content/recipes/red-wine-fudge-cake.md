ingredients:
- "1 cup unsalted butter softened (sub coconut oil same amount)"
- "3/4 cup granulated sugar"
- "3/4 cup packed dark brown sugar"
- "1 tablespoon pure vanilla extract"
- "3 large eggs"
- "2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1/3 cup unsweetened cocoa powder"
- "1 1/2 teaspoons baking soda"
- "1/2 teaspoon fine salt"
- "3/4 cup red wine (dry red or substitute red grape juice with 1 tsp apple cider vinegar)"
- "1 cup bittersweet chocolate chips"
- "1/4 cup powdered sugar"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 350°F. Grease a bundt pan generously with nonstick spray or butter plus flour—prevents sticking without scraping later."
- "Cream butter with both sugars and vanilla extract in stand mixer bowl using paddle attachment. Beat on medium till light and fluffy, about 3-4 minutes. Proper creaming traps air for cake rise—don’t rush or underbeat or heavy."
- "Add eggs 1 at a time, beating just until combined and creamy after each addition. Overbeat now can cause curdling or dense crumb."
- "In separate bowl sieve flour, cocoa powder, baking soda, and salt together. Sifting breaks up lumps, helps even texture."
- "Slowly add dry mix to wet in 2 batches on low speed to avoid flour clouds and overmixing gluten which toughens texture."
- "Pour red wine gradually into bowl. Mix on medium until batter looks glossy, uniform, no streaks. Batter should drip thickly from spatula but be pourable."
- "Scrape batter into prepared bundt pan; smooth top with spatula. Tap pan lightly on counter to release air bubbles."
- "Bake in center rack; listen for subtle hissing from oven as moisture evaporates. Check near 45 minutes with toothpick. Comes out clean or with few moist crumbs is done. Cake springs lightly when pressed, edges pull away from pan slightly."
- "Cool on wire rack 10 mins before invert onto plate. If cake sticks, warm pan slightly or loosen edges gently with small knife to prevent crumbling."
- "For glaze: Microwave butter and chocolate chips in short bursts 20 seconds each, stirring frequently to avoid scorching. Add powdered sugar and 2 tablespoons red wine. Whisk briskly until shiny and spreadable. If too thick, whisk in more wine slowly."
- "Pour glaze over cooled cake. Let set 15 minutes before slicing to allow glaze to slightly firm but remain soft. Store leftovers lightly covered to keep moist."
