ingredients:
- "3 cups Rice Chex cereal"
- "1 cup mini pretzels"
- "2 cups oat clusters cereal"
- "1 cup roasted almonds"
- "1/2 cup pumpkin seeds (pepitas)"
- "5 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "1/4 cup honey"
- "1/4 cup dark brown sugar packed"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1/2 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "1 cup Reese's Pieces"
- "1 cup autumn candy mix (candy corn, candy pumpkins)"
instructions:
- "Oven at 195 degrees Fahrenheit. Low temp needed to slowly crisp without burning sugars. Line baking tray with parchment paper to catch sticky glaze and avoid cleanup headaches."
- "In large bowl mix Chex, pretzels, oat clusters, almonds, pumpkin seeds. Combine dry components. You want even distribution so glaze coats uniformly."
- "Small saucepan over low heat. Butter, honey, brown sugar, vanilla, salt. Stir constantly. Watch melting happen — glossy, smooth, no graininess. If you see sugar crystallizing, pull off heat briefly before continuing."
- "Pour hot glaze onto dry mix all at once. Toss vigorously but gently. Use two spoons or hands if cooled slightly. Coat thoroughly. Crucial. Uncoated bits go stale or bland."
- "Spread mixture evenly on sheet in a single layer for uniform baking. Too crowded? Takes longer to crisp."
- "Bake 55 minutes. Every 12 minutes, pull tray out, flip pieces thoroughly with spatula or toss gently. Smell deepening aroma honey and caramel coming to life? Check color—look for golden sheen without burnt edges."
- "Cool completely on tray at room temperature. Mix will harden and crisp as it cools. Clumps form naturally. Break apart carefully by hand — fingers warm to soften glue but don’t crush pieces."
- "Transfer to bowl. Fold in Reese's Pieces and autumn candy. Do this last so no melting or stickiness."
- "Storage: Airtight container keeps texture crisp at room temp for days. Refrigerate if longer but candies might get firm. If mix soggy next day? Happens if glaze undercooked or exposed to humidity—re-bake briefly at low temp."
- "Substitutions: Almonds switch for peanuts when allergy or preference. Oat clusters for Cheerios add heft and chew. Pumpkin seeds bring earthy bite compared to plain nuts. Swap brown sugar for coconut sugar for deeper caramel notes and less moisture."
- "Avoid loading tray too thickly. Stirring frequently prevents burnt spots and undercooked clumps. Don’t skip parchment—cleaning sticky sugar residue is a nightmare."
- "Glaze timing critical. If you walk away too long, glaze cools and won’t coat well. Work fast but steady. Heating on low prevents scorching sugar which tastes bitter."
