ingredients:
- "2 lbs boneless skinless chicken thighs"
- "7 cups low-sodium chicken broth"
- "1 tbsp mushroom broth powder substitute for chicken bouillon"
- "1 medium yellow onion diced"
- "3 medium carrots diced"
- "3 stalks celery diced"
- "4 cloves garlic minced"
- "1 tsp dried thyme"
- "1/2 tsp dried rosemary crushed"
- "3/4 tsp kosher salt"
- "1/2 tsp freshly ground black pepper"
- "1/4 tsp red pepper flakes optional"
- "2 bay leaves"
- "1/2 cup fresh lemon juice"
- "1 tbsp lemon zest finely grated"
- "1 cup small orzo pasta uncooked"
- "1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley, dill, or cilantro"
instructions:
- "=== Assemble base ==="
- "Large 6-quart slow cooker. Add chicken thighs straight from fridge, no need to brown—save time. Pour broth and sprinkle mushroom powder evenly. Toss in diced onion, carrots, celery, garlic, thyme, rosemary, salt, pepper, chili flakes if using, and bay leaves. Push everything down gently but don’t pack too tight. Lid on."
- "=== Cook low and slow ==="
- "Set cooker to low. Cook for roughly 6 hours 50 minutes. Watch for broth reducing slightly, veggies soft with bite, chicken tender but still firm — perfect stage. High heat cuts time but risks drying chicken or uneven cooking. Low retains moistness, extracts flavors better."
- "=== Shred chicken ==="
- "Pull out chicken carefully, let cool a bit or handle cautiously. Use two forks, shred against grain for tender, bite-size pieces. Don’t shred in pot—avoids cloudy broth and mush. Return chicken to pot, stir gently into the broth with softened veggies."
- "=== Add lemon and orzo ==="
- "Stir in fresh lemon juice and zest, stir for aroma lift. Add orzo directly; it’ll cook in the residual broth, absorbing flavors. Cover and set cooker on high for 25 minutes. Keep an eye near end— when orzo swells, tender but not mushy, it’s done. Orzo cooks faster than couscous, adjust if needed."
- "=== Final touches and serve ==="
- "Discard bay leaves; they’re bitter if left too long. Fold in chopped parsley, dill, or cilantro. Taste broth for seasoning—balance salt or acidity. A splash of olive oil can add silkiness. Ladle out while hot, fresh herb scent upfront, acidity bright on tongue."
