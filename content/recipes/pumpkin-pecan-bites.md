ingredients:
- "1 cup canned pumpkin puree"
- "3/4 cup rolled oats"
- "1/2 cup finely chopped pecans"
- "1/4 cup desiccated coconut"
- "1/3 cup Greek yogurt"
- "3 tablespoons honey"
- "1 teaspoon pumpkin pie spice"
- "1/4 teaspoon sea salt"
- "1/2 cup dark chocolate chips"
- "2 teaspoons coconut oil"
instructions:
- "Pulse oats, pecans, and coconut in a food processor until uniformly chopped. Avoid big lumps but keep some texture; think coarse gravel to balance chew and crunch."
- "Add pumpkin puree, Greek yogurt, honey, pumpkin pie spice, and sea salt to processor bowl. Blend just until mixture comes together and forms thick dough. Overmixing breaks down oats too much, losing bite."
- "Line baking tray with parchment paper. Using a large spoon, scoop about 2.5 Tbsp per ball. Roll roughly between palms. Flatten slightly—cakes not blobs. Place pecan halves on top, gently pressing them in so they stick when frozen."
- "Freeze cookies for 40-50 minutes. This sets their shape; surface feels firm and dry to touch but still soft inside."
- "Melt dark chocolate chips and coconut oil in microwave safe bowl. Heat 40 seconds, stir well; if not melted, heat 20 more seconds. Avoid overheat—burning smells, grainy texture, rescue: add small coconut oil splash and stir."
- "Use fork or spoon to drizzle chocolate over frozen cookies in thin ribbons. Work quickly before chocolate firms. The glossy sheen indicates proper melting."
- "Return cookies to freezer in airtight container. Freeze minimum 2 hours before eating for best bite—fudgy edges, chewy center, crunchy pecan contrast."
- "Store frozen—if softened, pop in freezer to reset before serving. For softer bites, thaw 10 minutes at room temp."
