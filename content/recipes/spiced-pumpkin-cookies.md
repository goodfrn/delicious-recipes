ingredients:
- "1 cup unsalted butter softened"
- "3/4 cup demerara sugar"
- "1/2 cup granulated sugar"
- "1 package instant cream cheese pudding powder"
- "2 teaspoons vanilla extract"
- "2 large eggs"
- "2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking powder"
- "1 teaspoon ground cinnamon"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground ginger"
- "1/4 teaspoon salt"
- "3/4 cup dried cranberries"
- "1/2 cup chopped pecans"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 345°F. Prepping pan with parchment avoids sticking; no greasing needed."
- "Cream butter with demerara and granulated sugar until mixture looks fluffy but with grainy texture from demerara, about 3 minutes using hand mixer on medium. Cream cheese pudding adds moisture and a tangy depth; beat it in along with vanilla extract and eggs. Don't rush, get it homogenous but don't aerate excessively."
- "In another bowl combine flour, baking powder, cinnamon, ginger, and salt. The baking powder gives a slight lift unlike baking soda so it’s lighter bite with spices in fresh focus."
- "Fold dry stuff into wet carefully. Stir with wooden spoon until just coming together. Overmix dries cookies. The dough should feel slightly sticky but hold shape."
- "Add cranberries and pecans last. They bring bursts of tart and crunch, switching up texture from the usual chip sweetness."
- "Scoop out approx 1.5 tablespoon rounds onto sheet spacing 2.5 inches apart. Gently press down just a bit. Toss a few cranberries and pecan bits on top for looks and sharp flavor boost baked right on surface."
- "Bake 7 to 10 minutes. Watch closely. Edges firm, bottoms barely golden, tops pale. Don’t wait to see cracks on top or browning past light tan—will dry out. Cool 3 minutes on sheet to set, then transfer to rack. Underbaking yields a tender, chewy center. Expect slight wobble when fresh from oven."
- "Store airtight once fully cooled or freeze half for later. Pecans add a bit of hold against sogginess but freeze carefully to protect texture."
