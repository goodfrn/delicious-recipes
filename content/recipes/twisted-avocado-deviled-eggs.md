ingredients:
- "6 large eggs"
- "1 ripe avocado"
- "1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice"
- "2 tablespoons finely diced red onion"
- "1 teaspoon Dijon mustard"
- "1 tablespoon chopped pickled jalapeños"
- "1 tablespoon finely shredded sharp cheddar cheese"
- "1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika"
- "1/4 teaspoon celery seeds"
- "1/4 teaspoon garlic salt"
- "Salt and black pepper to taste"
instructions:
- "Bring eggs to rolling boil over medium-high heat, stirring gently occasionally so yolks stay centered. Once boiling, remove from heat and let rest in hot water for 15-20 minutes depending on size, shells getting firm but not chalky. Immediately plunge eggs into ice water bath; cool with a sharp snap. Crack shells carefully, peel under cool running water to avoid white tearing. Halve eggs lengthwise on a wooden board, then use a teaspoon to scoop yolks into a bowl. Set whites aside on a platter, face up; track them carefully – pairs matter."
- "Slice peeled avocado near pit, scoop flesh into the yolk bowl. Use a fork, stir and press but keep texture, some lumpy bits give character and prevent slime. Avoid over-mashing – that means mush and dull flavor, the bite disappears. Add lemon juice immediately to cut browning and brighten taste. The acid balances richness here, no one wants dull green mush."
- "Stir in diced red onion for crunchy brightness, sharp cheddar cheese for unexpected depth, and minced pickled jalapeños instead of horseradish—provides heat with a vinegar tang that plays differently on the palate. Add Dijon mustard for smooth sharp undertone. Season with garlic salt, celery seeds for earthy pop. Salt and freshly cracked black pepper to taste. Remember, celery seeds aren't overpowering if measured; too much kills the balance."
- "Spoon mixture generously back into whites’ cavities. Don’t overload; a neat mound holds better. Press firmly but gently so filling doesn’t spill out when plated. Sprinkle evenly with smoked paprika—far better for color than plain cayenne—and celery seeds on top for rustic crunch. Watch out: paprika can burn and turn bitter if over-toasted, so no direct flame or heat here. Chill 10 minutes to meld flavors and firm texture. Serve cold or room temp. If leftovers linger, keep covered tightly or avocado may darken—squeeze extra lemon juice on filling if prepping ahead."
- "Pro tips: If eggs slip apart or whites have cracks, swap for smaller or fresher batch—fresh eggs peel better but older eggs are easier to peel; find middle ground by boiling them correctly. Jalapeño option is forgiving—mild heat level customizable by chopping size and seed retention. When mixing, small batches avoid overworking the avocado. For a tang tweak, swap lemon for grapefruit juice or a pinch of sumac powder."
