ingredients:
- "4 cups broccoli florets fresh or frozen"
- "1 cup sour cream full-fat"
- "8 ounces cream cheese softened (substitute for mushroom soup)"
- "1 tablespoon soy sauce (replaces Worcestershire)"
- "1 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "1 cup sharp cheddar cheese shredded"
- "1 cup smoked Gouda shredded"
- "1 cup Ritz crackers crushed"
- "3 tablespoons unsalted butter melted and browned"
- "Additional ½ cup shredded sharp cheddar for topping"
- "Cooking spray"
instructions:
- "=== Prepare broccoli ==="
- "Broccoli: fresh or frozen. If fresh, cut into even florets for uniform cooking. Blanch in boiling salted water until bright green and just tender, 2-3 minutes. Immediately drain and shock in ice water to stop cooking, keep crisp. If frozen, thaw and drain excess water well. Excess moisture kills casserole texture. Shake or pat dry thoroughly before mixing. Cooking broccoli just right keeps bite, prevents mushy outcome."
- "=== Mix base ==="
- "In large bowl, combine sour cream, softened cream cheese — beaten smooth beforehand to avoid lumps, soy sauce, garlic powder. Blend well. Add shredded sharp cheddar and smoked Gouda blend until evenly dispersed. The cheese mix balances sharpness with creamy smokiness, adds complexity beyond standard cheddar alone."
- "=== Assemble ==="
- "Fold in prepared broccoli gently, preserving floret integrity to avoid puree. Spray 2-quart dish liberally to prevent sticking. Spread mixture evenly. Avoid overfilling to ensure heat circulation."
- "=== Topping ==="
- "In small bowl, toss crushed Ritz crackers with browned butter. Browned butter adds nutty, deep flavor compared to plain melted butter. Evenly sprinkle crumbs atop casserole. Press lightly to help topping adhere."
- "=== Bake ==="
- "Place casserole in preheated 350°F oven. Bake 40 to 45 minutes. Watch for bubbling along edges, crackers turning golden with audible sizzling sounds. These cues tell you the casserole is heated through, topping toasted but not burnt."
- "=== Final cheese topping ==="
- "Remove carefully. Sprinkle remaining cheddar over hot surface. Return to oven 5 minutes or until cheese melts and just begins to bubble. Watch closely to avoid browning – aim for glossy finish."
- "=== Rest and serve ==="
- "Let stand 5 minutes after baking to settle structure. Textural contrast shines now: crunchy topping, creamy layer beneath, tender but firm broccoli pieces."
- "=== Pro tips ==="
- "If mixture seems watery, add ¼ cup breadcrumbs or more cheese to bind. Can swap cream cheese for plain Greek yogurt for lighter tang and protein boost but expect looser texture. Oven temps vary; if topping browns early, loosely tent with foil. For gluten-free, substitute crackers for crushed gluten-free crackers or toasted nuts mixed with herbs for crunch. Watch closely on final cheese melt step – it happens fast, key to glossy, melty finish, not burnt edges."
