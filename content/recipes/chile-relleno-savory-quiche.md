ingredients:
- "1 store-bought refrigerated pie crust"
- "1 cup diced roasted poblano peppers (or canned mild green chilies)"
- "1 cup shredded pepper jack cheese"
- "1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese"
- "4 large eggs"
- "3 tablespoons all-purpose flour"
- "1 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika"
- "1 1/4 cups whole milk"
instructions:
- "=== Prepare crust ==="
- "Preheat oven to 345 F to start gently. Unroll pie crust into 8 or 9-inch pie plate. Prick bottom and sides thoroughly with fork to vent steam and prevent bubbles. Use pie weights or dried beans—don’t skip this step or crust puffs unevenly. Bake blind for 13-17 minutes. Edges should turn a light golden tan with just a hint of deeper brown at tip of ridges. If edges brown too fast, tent loosely with foil to avoid burning."
- "=== Mix filling ==="
- "In large bowl, vigorously whisk together eggs, milk, flour, salt, and smoked paprika until completely smooth. Stir in diced poblano peppers, shredded pepper jack and mozzarella cheese until evenly combined. Flour firms custard, preventing cracked quiche and aiding slice cleanup. Smoked paprika adds subtle warmth; can replace with mild chili powder or cumin for different profile."
- "=== Assemble and bake ==="
- "Pour filling gently into prebaked crust. Avoid overfilling; filling rises slightly but shouldn’t spill. Place in center of oven. Bake for about 43-48 minutes. Look for custard setting: center jiggles slightly only when nudged; edges firm and pull just away from pan sides. If it’s still liquidy, bake in 3-5 minute increments. Don’t rely blindly on clock—oven variability is a fact."
- "=== Final cheese topping ==="
- "Remove quiche from oven; sprinkle remaining 1/4 cup each pepper jack and mozzarella evenly over surface. Return to oven for 4-6 minutes or until cheese melts fully and bubbles with light golden spots. That sizzling, crackling sound means fats rendering nicely. Visually check bubbles—burst cheese bubbles reveal melting complete; no raw coating left."
- "=== Rest and serve ==="
- "Cool on wire rack for 12 minutes: a must for slicing neat wedges. Residual heat finishes cooking and custard firms. Serve warm or near room temp. If reheating, cover with foil to prevent cheesy top drying out."
- "=== Troubleshooting and tips ==="
- "Puffed crust edges? Shield early with foil. Soggy bottom? Ensure crust fully baked and pierced well to let moisture escape. No pie weights? Use uncooked rice or sugar. No poblano peppers? Mild canned chiles or diced roasted jalapeños on low heat work. Eggs should be room temp for even custard. Use whole milk or cream—for richer custard swap 1/4 cup milk for cream. Keep an eye on cheese melt; burnt cheese tastes bitter."
