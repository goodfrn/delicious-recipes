ingredients:
- "1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1 teaspoon baking powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1/4 teaspoon salt"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground ginger"
- "1/3 cup packed brown sugar"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1/4 cup unsalted butter melted and cooled"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "2 ripe bananas mashed"
- "1 small apple peeled diced small"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 355°F and line 12 tin cups with paper liners. Butter can replace liners but grease well to avoid sticking."
- "Mix dry ingredients thoroughly in a large bowl: flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, nutmeg, and ginger."
- "In a separate bowl whisk brown sugar and eggs just until combined;"
- "Add cooled melted butter, vanilla, mashed bananas, and diced apple. Hand mixer on low to blend; stop before completely smooth, small lumps ok."
- "Pour wet into dry bowl; fold gently with spatula until no white streaks remain but batter slightly lumpy. Overmix here equals tough texture later."
- "Divide batter evenly among liners filling about 2/3 full. Batter thick but scoopable."
- "Bake on middle rack for about 20-22 minutes. Watch muffin tops—they’ll puff up and edges get golden. Toothpick test crucial; clean or few crumbs stuck says done."
- "Rest in pan 5 minutes; muffins firm but still hot. Transfer to wire rack to crisp bottom edges. Hot muffins fragile, cooling firm them just right."

ingredientsNote: "Flour forms structure—use all-purpose, bread flour makes dense bread and cake flour too fragile. Brown sugar provides more moisture and flavor than white. Vanilla is flavor anchor, don’t skip; for stronger aroma use real extract not imitation. Apples add a textural pop, small cubes to avoid sogginess, peeled to prevent bitter skins.
