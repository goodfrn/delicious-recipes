ingredients:
- "6 thinly sliced ham rounds, enough to line muffin cups with overlap"
- "2 tablespoons sharp cheddar cheese, grated"
- "2 tablespoons vegan cheddar for dairy-free option"
- "1 tablespoon diced pickled jalapeños, drained"
- "6 large eggs"
- "1 teaspoon smoked paprika, plus more to sprinkle"
- "Salt, to taste"
- "Light cooking oil spray or butter, for grease"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 365 degrees Fahrenheit. Use oil spray or melted butter to grease 6 muffin tin cups thoroughly — keeps ham from sticking, easier removal later."
- "Carefully press each ham slice into the muffin cups. It must cover the sides completely with some overlap. If slices are uneven, trim thinner parts or double layer for sturdiness. The ham acts as a shell to hold the filling; gaps risk leaks."
- "Mix sharp cheddar cheese with diced jalapeños and smoked paprika in a small bowl. This replaces the sweet pimento cheese with sharper, spicy notes. Spoon roughly 1.5 tablespoons into each ham-lined cup. Press down slightly to create an even base for eggs."
- "Crack eggs one at a time into a small bowl to inspect for shell bits and freshness. Pour each egg gently over the cheese layer in the muffin cups. If yolk runniness bothers you, scramble eggs lightly before pouring."
- "Sprinkle a pinch of salt over each cup. Skip black pepper — smoked paprika adds earthiness. Optional: a dusting of paprika on top for color and aroma once baking is complete."
- "Pop the tray in the hot oven. Listen for gentle sizzle from ham edges curling and crisping. Whites should set fully but yolks remain wobbly—around 18 to 22 minutes. Egg size and pan material can push bake time. Prodding whites gently with a finger can tell you doneness; no runny film, slight spring."
- "Remove from oven as soon as whites are set to avoid overcooked dry yolks. Let rest a few minutes for carryover heat to firm the yolk edges just slightly. Ham edges will crisp and bring out smoky aroma."
- "Run a thin knife around each cup’s edge before carefully lifting out the ham shell with egg filling. If sticking occurs, a thin offset spatula or butter knife helps gently pry free without tearing ham or messing presentation."
- "Serve immediately. Great on its own or tucked into toasted English muffin halves for layered breakfast sandwiches."
- "Common mistakes: not oiling tin enough, ham tears, or baking at too low temperature causing rubbery edges rather than crispy. Swap dairy cheddar with vegan shredded cheese blends if avoiding dairy but mind melt qualities—some will be less gooey. Jalapeños optional but give nice contrast; pickled adds acidity and a textural pop."
- "If you want firmer yolks, increase baking to about 25 min and check frequently from 20 min. For those nervous about runny, scramble eggs lightly before pouring but know you lose rich yolk texture."
