ingredients:
- "3 pounds sweet potatoes peeled and sliced"
- "1/2 cup coconut milk full fat"
- "1/4 cup honey"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract"
- "1/2 teaspoon salt"
- "Streusel topping==="
- "3/4 cup chopped pecans"
- "1/2 cup oat flour"
- "1/3 cup brown sugar packed"
- "1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon"
- "1/4 teaspoon sea salt"
- "4 tablespoons unsalted butter browned and slightly cooled"
instructions:
- "1 Heat oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit. Grease a 1 1/2-quart casserole dish with butter or spray. Don't skip greasing; topping may stick like a bear trap otherwise."
- "2 Place peeled and sliced sweet potatoes in medium pot. Just cover with cold water so they cook evenly. Boil then reduce to simmer uncovered for about 20 minutes. Check by stabbing with fork. Should slide through with little resistance but still hold shape. Drain potatoes in colander but leave steam escaping for 5 minutes to let excess moisture fade. Otherwise casserole turns soupy—potatoes hold water like a sponge."
- "3 Return potatoes to pot or big bowl. Mash with hand masher or mixer on low. Avoid whipping too long or you get glue-like paste. Add coconut milk, honey, eggs, vanilla extract, and salt. Mix thoroughly until consistent but don't overdo. It’ll get glossy, not sticky. Taste for balance—sweet potatoes vary, honey can be adjusted."
- "4 Prepare the streusel. Brown the butter over medium heat until it foams and smells nutty, watch carefully or it burns. In separate bowl, combine pecans, oat flour, brown sugar, cinnamon, and sea salt. Pour in browned butter, mix until crumbly clumps form with sticky patches from melted sugar and butter. If flaky, add a pinch more butter. Topping should feel like damp sand."
- "5 Spread mashed sweet potato mixture evenly into casserole dish. Crumble streusel topping over the surface in an even layer but don’t pack it down. Bake 35 to 40 minutes or until edges bubble around the dish and topping is golden with darker spots from the pecans roasting. Listen for gentle crackle from sugar caramelizing. Test doneness by inserting knife at center; should come out mostly clean but moist. Let sit at least 10 minutes before serving. The topping crisps more as it cools."
- "6 Substitutions and fixes: No coconut milk? Use regular milk with a splash of cream for richness. No oat flour? Swap with almond meal or all-purpose flour—almond meal adds nuttier crunch, AP flour binds more. Honey can be subbed with maple syrup but expect lighter color and thinner texture. If topping soggy, bake uncovered another 5-7 minutes or broil briefly but watch closely to avoid burning pecans. For chunkier mash, hold back one sliced potato to stir in last for texture contrast."
