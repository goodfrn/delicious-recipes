ingredients:
- "1 3/4 cups almond flour blend"
- "1 tsp baking soda"
- "1/2 tsp fine sea salt"
- "2 tsp ground cinnamon"
- "1/2 tsp freshly grated nutmeg"
- "1/2 tsp ground ginger"
- "1/3 cup vegetable oil"
- "1/4 cup pure maple syrup"
- "1/4 cup honey"
- "1 tbsp unsweetened applesauce"
- "1 tsp vanilla extract"
- "3 large eggs"
- "1 1/2 cups finely grated carrots"
- "8 oz cream cheese, softened"
- "1/2 cup Greek yogurt, instead of sour cream"
- "3/4 cup granulated sugar"
- "1 tsp vanilla extract"
- "2 large eggs"
instructions:
- "=== Prepare and Set Up ==="
- "Preheat oven to 345F for precise caramelization without overbaking. Line a 9x13-inch pan with parchment, excess hanging over edges for easy removal. No spray here; parchment is key for clean edges."
- "=== Carrot Cake Batter ==="
- "Combine almond flour blend, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, and ginger in a bowl. Whisk together until evenly mixed. Almond flour requires gentle handling, don’t over whisk or risk density."
- "In another bowl, mix oil with maple syrup, honey, applesauce, vanilla, and eggs thoroughly but don’t aerate. Fat and sugar mix forms the base; important for moistness and chew."
- "Add wet mixture to dry. Fold gently until mostly combined. Over mixing spells toughness here. Then fold in grated carrots. Watch for uniform distribution without batter looking dull or thick."
- "=== Cheesecake Filling ==="
- "Beat cream cheese and Greek yogurt on medium speed until creamy and no lumps remain (about 1–2 minutes). Add sugar and vanilla, mix just until incorporated."
- "Add eggs one at a time on low speed—this prevents curdling. Cheat and scrape bowl sides between eggs to keep smooth texture. Too fast or skipping this can cause cracks or grainy filling."
- "=== Layering and Swirling ==="
- "Pour half the carrot batter into pan. Spread carefully with offset spatula; batter is loose and thin layers make swirls visible."
- "Dollop cheesecake mix across carrot cake layer in uneven spoonfuls. Doesn’t need to cover fully, rough edges give more swirl depth."
- "Spoon remaining carrot batter over cheesecake in blobs. Resistant to smooth spreading here. Use a butter knife to swirl gently with wide sweeping motions—less is more. Too much swirling breaks the layers and ruins rise."
- "=== Baking and Cooling ==="
- "Bake 35 to 40 minutes. Look for edges pulling from pan and cheesecake mostly set but with slight jiggle center. The sinking top after cooling is normal due to lighter cheesecake. It looks rustic once cut; no problem."
- "Cool fully at room temp to stop steam from softening bars further. Then chill in fridge 3+ hours or overnight. This firms cheesecake and makes clean slicing possible."
- "=== Cutting and Serving ==="
- "Use a sharp knife wiped between cuts. Running under warm water or quick spritz of oil will prevent dragging, especially those sticky carrot bits. Clean cuts show off distinctive layers and swirls."
- "Serve cold or room temp, depending on preference. Bars keep well refrigerated for 4-5 days in airtight container. Freezing works but texture softens slightly."
