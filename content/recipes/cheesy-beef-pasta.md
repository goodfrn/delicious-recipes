ingredients:
- "1 pound lean ground beef"
- "1 medium onion diced"
- "2 cloves garlic minced"
- "1 packet brown gravy mix"
- "1 can (10 1/2 oz) cream of mushroom soup"
- "1 1/2 cups water"
- "2 1/2 cups uncooked medium shell pasta"
- "1 1/2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese"
- "1 teaspoon dried parsley flakes"
- "1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika"
- "Salt and pepper to taste"
instructions:
- "Heat large skillet over medium; toss in ground beef and diced onions. Break meat apart with spatula. Listen to steady sizzle, onions turning translucent, edges curling slightly. Cook until no pink remains. Drain grease well, return skillet to stove over medium-low heat; dry bottom a bit by stirring for 1 minute."
- "Add minced garlic; stir constantly about 90 seconds till fragrance punches through. Avoid burning—once aroma hits, move to next."
- "Dump in brown gravy powder, cream of mushroom soup, water. Stir vigorously until combined and smooth. If substituting dried herbs for fresh, add parsley flakes and smoked paprika now. This mix thickens noticeably as it heats, coats the back of spoon when right."
- "Scatter uncooked pasta in skillet; stir to submerge all pieces. Bring to a rolling boil, then immediately reduce heat to low. Cover skillet snugly. Wait—bubbling reduces, hear occasional slight steam hissing. Cook 12 to 15 minutes, stirring every few minutes to prevent sticking. Pasta should be al dente; check a piece by bite—not mushy, no raw hardness."
- "Turn off heat; layer shredded cheddar cheese over pasta. Cover and let rest 3 to 4 minutes off heat. Cheese melts into gooey finish with heat trapped underneath. Stir partially or fully before serving for consistency."

Brown gravy mix adds depth but can be replaced by a spoonful of beef bouillon granules.
