ingredients:
- "1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour"
- "2/3 cup packed coconut sugar"
- "1/2 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "8 tablespoons frozen unsalted butter, grated"
- "1 cup rolled oats"
- "3/4 cup unsweetened shredded coconut"
- "1 (14 ounce) can sweetened condensed milk"
- "1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract"
- "20 soft caramel candies"
- "2 tablespoons whole milk"
- "1/2 cup bittersweet chocolate chips"
- "flaky sea salt for sprinkling"
instructions:
- "Set oven to 345°F. Line 9x13 pan with parchment or grease lightly."
- "Mix flour, coconut sugar, salt in medium bowl. Grate butter frozen; combine quickly. Break big chunks with fingers to pea-sized bits for even baking. Mixture looks coarse, like wet sand before pressing."
- "Firmly press crumbs evenly on bottom of pan. Don’t fuss—push edges tight, prevent runoff."
- "Bake about 18 minutes. Should glow light golden, firm but not dry. Partial bake crucial. Oats and coconut will go on hot crust."
- "Scatter oats and coconut over warm crust, spread evenly. No clumps."
- "Whisk condensed milk with vanilla until silky. Pour gently over oats & coconut, tracing edges so all covered. Pour too fast will mix layers. Aim for a glossy, smooth pour."
- "Back into oven 28-30 minutes. Filling sets when center jiggles slightly but no wet spots. Look for light golden around edges."
- "Remove, cool 15 minutes. Meanwhile, chop caramel candies roughly, melt with milk over low heat in small saucepan. Stir constantly or risk grainy caramel, low and slow wins here."
- "Pour warm caramel layer atop bars. Immediately scatter chocolate chips; they’ll melt just enough if bars still warm, creating swirls. Sprinkle flaky sea salt on top. Wait to cool completely. Chill if impatient."
- "Cut with sharp knife; wipe blade between cuts to keep edges neat."
- "Use chopped pecans or toasted pumpkin seeds for coconut swap—texture variation. Tapioca starch added to flour (1 tablespoon) can help with extra crumbly crust."
- "If caramels stick together, add bit more milk to thin, or microwave in short bursts stirring frequently."
- "Avoid butter melting too soon—keep grated frozen for crumb structure. Don’t overmix crumb or it stiffens."
- "Watch oven temps since variation textures depend on baking depth. Visual cues beat clocks here."
