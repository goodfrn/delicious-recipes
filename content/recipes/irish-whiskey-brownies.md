ingredients:
- "1 box brownie mix, about 18 ounces"
- "3/4 cup Irish whiskey"
- "1/4 cup coffee liqueur (like Kahlúa), substitute dark rum or omit"
- "1/2 cup vegetable oil, can replace with melted unsalted butter for richness"
- "3 large eggs"
- "1 cup semisweet chocolate chips"
- "2/3 cup heavy cream"
- "1/4 cup Irish whiskey (extra, for ganache)"
- "2 ounces mascarpone cheese, room temperature"
- "2 tablespoons granulated sugar"
- "Optional melted bittersweet chocolate for drizzling"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 340°F . Line a 9 x 13-inch pan with parchment paper, edges overhanging for lifting later."
- "In a large bowl, whack together brownie mix, eggs, oil, 3/4 cup whiskey, and coffee liqueur. Use a sturdy spatula or wooden spoon. Mix just until blended — don't overwork or batter gets tough."
- "Scrape batter into pan. Smooth top with offset spatula, but no obsessing. Look for even thickness about 1-inch deep. Bake 25 to 30 minutes. Check by inserting toothpick 1.5 to 2 inches from edge. Should come out with just a few moist crumbs, not raw batter."
- "Remove from oven. Crackle aroma of whiskey and chocolate will rise. Cool completely in pan on wire rack. Ripping out too soon wastes ganache smoothness."
- "For ganache, plop chocolate chips in medium heatproof bowl. Heat cream with 1/4 cup whiskey over low heat in small saucepan until just before simmer — bubbles appear at edges but do not boil. Pour directly over chocolate. Cover bowl immediately. Wait 7 minutes — watch for melting and glossy shine."
- "Whisk ganache until velvety and uniform. Once cool to lukewarm, fold in mascarpone cheese and sugar. The mascarpone adds lightness and a slight tang that cuts richness. Place mixture in large bowl and whip with hand mixer on medium-high until fluffy, 3-4 minutes. Should hold soft peaks – not stiff or grainy."
- "Pull cooled brownies out by overhanging parchment. Spread whipped ganache thick and even. If ganache too runny, chill 15 minutes and rewhip."
- "Optional: Drizzle with melted bittersweet chocolate for bite and drama. Wiggle spoon or piping bag for thin ribbons."
- "Cut into squares with sharp knife wiped between cuts for clean edges. Serve at room temp for best flavor and texture."
- "If you’ve tried it, remember to note texture, doneness, and ganache whip consistency. Let kitchen experience guide future tweaks."
